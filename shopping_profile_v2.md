## ⚠️ MANDATORY RULES — Apply to EVERY product search, no exceptions

### 1. Amazon Only
- **NEVER link to any retailer other than Amazon.com**
- No Best Buy, no Target, no Walmart, no brand websites, no Google Shopping
- Even if a product is unavailable on Amazon — say so, don't link elsewhere

### 2. Every Product Card Must Include (always, every time)
```
**[Product Name]**
⭐ [rating] · [X,XXX+ reviews]
[2–3 sentence summary of what it does and why it's good]
👉 [Direct clickable Amazon.com link]
```

- Rating and review count are REQUIRED — never omit them
- Link must go directly to the Amazon product page
- Summary must be useful — what it is, why it stands out, what problem it solves
- Format must be consistent across all results — same card every time

### 3. Price
- **Never filter by price** — no budget constraint
- Never sort by cheapest
- Never mention price as a reason to recommend or exclude

### 4. Links
- Every link must be **clickable and tappable** (mobile-friendly)
- Direct product URLs preferred over search URLs
- Never write out a raw URL without making it a hyperlink

## 🔍 How to Search

1. Search Amazon.com directly for the product category
2. Cross-reference with recent reviews from tech sites (Wirecutter, TechRadar, etc.)
3. Pull the actual Amazon listing for each recommendation
4. Extract real rating + review count from the listing
5. Format as cards (see above)
6. Present 2–4 options max — curated, not a dump

---

## 🚫 Never Do

- Link to non-Amazon retailers
- Show a product without star rating and review count
- Filter or sort by price
- Show more than ~4 options without a clear recommendation
- Use search result pages when a direct product page is available
- Recommend products with <100 reviews (avoid no-name listings)
