Pipeline
North Shore, Oahu
The most famous wave in the world. A shallow lava reef produces massive, perfectly hollow barrels that break just 30 yards from shore. Hosts the WSL Pipe Masters each winter. For expert surfers only.

A long, shifting peak that produces powerful walls on big swells. Conditions change rapidly as currents shift the sandbar. A proving ground for big-wave surfers and host of the Sunset Open since 1965.

Jaws (Pe'ahi)
North Shore, Maui
One of the biggest rideable waves on Earth. A deep-water reef that only breaks in the most powerful winter swells, producing 60–120 ft faces. Pioneered by tow-in surfers Laird Hamilton and Buzzy Kerbox. Waves reach speeds of 30 mph.
📅 Nov–Mar🌊 30–60+ ft🔥 Expert (tow-in)
