Best UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Europe
Historic Centre of Rome
Rome, Italy
The Colosseum, Pantheon, Roman Forum, and Trevi Fountain within a single walkable zone. Twenty-seven centuries of continuously layered history — ancient ruins sit beneath medieval churches beneath Baroque palazzos. The densest concentration of heritage landmarks in the world.
📅 Inscribed 1980🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Rome Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Historic Centre of Florence
Florence, Italy
Birthplace of the Renaissance. Brunelleschi's dome, the Uffizi Gallery, Ponte Vecchio, and Michelangelo's David within a compact medieval street grid. Six centuries of art and architecture virtually unchanged since the Medici era.
📅 Inscribed 1982🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Florence Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Venice and its Lagoon
Venice, Italy
A city built on 118 islands connected by 400 bridges across a saltwater lagoon. St. Mark's Basilica, the Doge's Palace, and the Grand Canal lined with Gothic and Renaissance palaces. No cars — all movement by boat or on foot across a thousand years of maritime engineering.
📅 Inscribed 1987🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Venice Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Cinque Terre
Liguria, Italy
Five pastel-coloured fishing villages clinging to steep terraced cliffs above the Ligurian Sea. Connected by coastal hiking trails through centuries-old vineyards. No cars reach the villages — access by train, boat, or foot only.
📅 Inscribed 1997🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Cinque Terre Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Amalfi Coast
Campania, Italy
A 50 km stretch of dramatic coastline where pastel villages cascade down steep cliffs to the Mediterranean. Positano, Amalfi, and Ravello connected by a single winding road carved into the rock. Terraced lemon groves, medieval watchtowers, and deep blue water.
📅 Inscribed 1997🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Amalfi Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Alhambra, Generalife and Albaicín
Granada, Spain
A Moorish palace complex of unmatched geometric ornamentation set against the Sierra Nevada mountains. The Nasrid Palaces contain the finest Islamic architecture in Western Europe. The Generalife gardens cascade down the hillside with fountains and reflecting pools.
📅 Inscribed 1984🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Official Site
Works of Antoni Gaudí
Barcelona, Spain
Seven buildings by Gaudí inscribed collectively — including the Sagrada Família, Park Güell, Casa Batlló, and Casa Milà (La Pedrera). Organic forms inspired by nature, with no straight lines. The Sagrada Família has been under construction since 1882.
📅 Inscribed 1984🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Barcelona Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Historic Centre of Prague
Prague, Czech Republic
A thousand years of architecture virtually undamaged by war — Romanesque rotundas, Gothic spires, Baroque palaces, and Art Nouveau facades. The Charles Bridge, Prague Castle, and the Old Town astronomical clock within a compact, walkable medieval core.
📅 Inscribed 1992🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Prague Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Budapest: Banks of the Danube & Buda Castle
Budapest, Hungary
The Danube divides Buda's medieval hilltop castle quarter from Pest's grand 19th-century boulevards. The Parliament Building, Fisherman's Bastion, Chain Bridge, and thermal baths fed by 120 natural hot springs beneath the city.
📅 Inscribed 1987🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Budapest Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Old City of Dubrovnik
Dubrovnik, Croatia
A walled medieval city on a rocky promontory jutting into the Adriatic. Nearly 2 km of intact fortifications with towers and gates encircle limestone streets, Baroque churches, and the Rector's Palace. A walk around the walls gives panoramic views of terracotta rooftops and deep blue sea.
📅 Inscribed 1979🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Dubrovnik Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Acropolis of Athens
Athens, Greece
The Parthenon and its companion temples crowning a limestone plateau 156 metres above modern Athens. Built in the 5th century BC under Pericles — the highest expression of Classical Greek architecture. The new Acropolis Museum at the base houses the original caryatids and friezes.
📅 Inscribed 1987🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Athens Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Hallstatt–Dachstein Salzkammergut
Hallstatt, Austria
An Alpine lakeside village with 7,000 years of salt-mining history. Pastel houses reflected in the mirror-still lake beneath towering Dachstein peaks. The world's oldest known salt mine is still operational. One of the most photographed villages in Europe.
📅 Inscribed 1997🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Official Site
Historic Centre of Bruges
Bruges, Belgium
A medieval trading city laced with canals, with one of the best-preserved Gothic cityscapes in Europe. The Belfry tower, Market Square, and the Basilica of the Holy Blood. Michelangelo's marble Madonna and Child resides in the Church of Our Lady.
📅 Inscribed 2000🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Bruges Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Palace of Versailles
Versailles, France
The ultimate expression of French royal power. The Hall of Mirrors stretches 73 metres with 357 mirrors reflecting the gardens. 800 hectares of formal gardens with 1,400 fountains. Built by Louis XIV to outshine every palace in Europe — and it did.
📅 Inscribed 1979🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Paris Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Mont-Saint-Michel and its Bay
Normandy, France
A granite island topped by a Gothic abbey rising from vast tidal flats on the Normandy coast. At high tide the island is surrounded by water; at low tide you can walk across the sand. The abbey sits 80 metres above the bay. One of France's most visited landmarks outside Paris.
📅 Inscribed 1979🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Official Site
Historic Areas of Istanbul
Istanbul, Turkey
Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque, Topkapi Palace, and the Grand Bazaar at the crossroads of Europe and Asia. The Bosphorus strait divides the city between two continents. Fifteen centuries of Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman layers compressed into one skyline.
📅 Inscribed 1985🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Istanbul Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Old Town of Edinburgh
Edinburgh, Scotland
The Royal Mile descends from Edinburgh Castle on its volcanic rock to the Palace of Holyroodhouse. Medieval closes and wynds branch off into hidden courtyards. The Georgian New Town across the valley is a masterpiece of 18th-century urban planning.
📅 Inscribed 1995🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Edinburgh Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Cultural Landscape of Sintra
Sintra, Portugal
Romantic-era palaces scattered across forested hilltops 30 km from Lisbon. The Pena Palace combines Neo-Gothic, Neo-Moorish, and Neo-Renaissance styles in a kaleidoscope of colour. The Moorish Castle ruins offer panoramic Atlantic views. Lord Byron called Sintra "glorious Eden."
📅 Inscribed 1995🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Sintra Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Asia
Angkor
Siem Reap, Cambodia
The largest religious complex ever built. Angkor Wat, Bayon's stone faces, and the jungle-consumed ruins of Ta Prohm spread across 400 km². Built by the Khmer Empire between the 9th and 15th centuries. Sunrise over the main temple's five towers is one of the most iconic moments in travel.
📅 Inscribed 1992🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Book Tour
Great Wall of China
China
Over 21,000 km of fortifications spanning mountains, deserts, and plateaus across northern China. The best-preserved sections — Badaling, Mutianyu, and Jinshanling — date to the Ming dynasty (1368–1644). Visible from nearby hilltops but not, despite the myth, from space.
📅 Inscribed 1987🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Beijing Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Taj Mahal
Agra, India
A white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan for his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653 after 22 years of construction by 20,000 artisans. Inlaid with 28 types of semi-precious stones. The marble shifts colour with the light — pink at dawn, white at noon, golden at sunset.
📅 Inscribed 1983🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Book Tour
Historic Monuments of Ancient Kyoto
Kyoto, Japan
Seventeen temples, shrines, and gardens collectively inscribed — including the golden Kinkaku-ji, the zen rock garden at Ryoan-ji, and the bamboo groves of Arashiyama. Japan's imperial capital for over a thousand years, spared from WWII bombing. Over 2,000 temples and shrines remain.
📅 Inscribed 1994🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Kyoto Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Bagan
Mandalay Region, Myanmar
Over 3,500 Buddhist temples, pagodas, and monasteries built between the 9th and 13th centuries across a 104 km² plain along the Irrawaddy River. Sunrise hot-air balloon flights over the temple-studded landscape are among the most spectacular aerial views on Earth.
📅 Inscribed 2019🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Ha Long Bay
Quảng Ninh, Vietnam
Nearly 2,000 limestone karst islands and pillars rising from emerald-green water in the Gulf of Tonkin. Caves, floating fishing villages, and hidden lagoons accessible by kayak. Overnight junk-boat cruises navigate between the towers. One of the most dramatic seascapes on Earth.
📅 Inscribed 1994🌿 Natural
🌐 Hanoi Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Borobudur Temple
Central Java, Indonesia
The world's largest Buddhist temple — a 9th-century step pyramid of volcanic stone with 2,672 relief panels and 504 Buddha statues. Nine stacked platforms represent the Buddhist path to enlightenment. Sunrise visits begin in darkness, climbing to the top as the temple emerges from morning mist.
📅 Inscribed 1991🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Book Tour
Historic City of Ayutthaya
Ayutthaya, Thailand
The ruined capital of the Siamese kingdom, founded in 1350 and destroyed by the Burmese in 1767. Brick prangs (towers), headless Buddha statues, and crumbling monastery walls spread across an island where three rivers converge. The famous Buddha head entwined in tree roots is at Wat Mahathat.
📅 Inscribed 1991🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Bangkok Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Middle East & North Africa
Petra
Ma'an, Jordan
A Nabataean city carved into rose-red sandstone cliffs over 2,000 years ago. The Treasury (Al-Khazneh) emerges at the end of a narrow 1.2 km canyon called the Siq. Over 800 individual monuments including temples, tombs, and a Roman-style theatre. One of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
📅 Inscribed 1985🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Petra Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Memphis and its Necropolis — Pyramids of Giza
Giza, Egypt
The Great Pyramid of Khufu — the only surviving Ancient Wonder — stands 146 metres tall alongside the pyramids of Khafre and Menkaure and the Great Sphinx. Built around 2560 BC with 2.3 million stone blocks. The oldest and largest of the three pyramids held the record as tallest structure on Earth for 3,800 years.
📅 Inscribed 1979🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Cairo Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Old City of Jerusalem
Jerusalem
Sacred to Judaism, Christianity, and Islam within one square kilometre. The Western Wall, Church of the Holy Sepulchre, Dome of the Rock, and Al-Aqsa Mosque. Four quarters — Jewish, Christian, Muslim, Armenian — separated by ancient stone alleys. Over 3,000 years of continuous spiritual significance.
📅 Inscribed 1981🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Medina of Marrakesh
Marrakesh, Morocco
A labyrinthine walled city founded in 1070 with over 600 hectares of souks, riads, and palaces. Jemaa el-Fnaa square transforms from market by day to open-air food court and performance space by night. The Koutoubia Mosque's 77-metre minaret is visible across the entire medina.
📅 Inscribed 1985🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Marrakech Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Africa
Victoria Falls (Mosi-oa-Tunya)
Zambia / Zimbabwe
The world's largest sheet of falling water — 1,708 metres wide and up to 108 metres tall. The Zambezi River plunges into a narrow gorge creating a spray cloud visible from 50 km away. Known locally as "The Smoke That Thunders." Peak flow in April sends 500 million litres per minute over the edge.
📅 Inscribed 1989🌿 Natural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Rock-Hewn Churches of Lalibela
Amhara, Ethiopia
Eleven medieval churches carved downward from solid volcanic rock in the 12th–13th centuries, connected by tunnels and trenches. The cruciform Church of St. George (Bete Giyorgis) was carved from a single block of stone. Still an active pilgrimage site for Ethiopian Orthodox Christians.
📅 Inscribed 1978🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Ngorongoro Conservation Area
Arusha, Tanzania
A 260 km² volcanic caldera sheltering the densest concentration of wildlife in Africa. Lions, elephants, rhinos, flamingos, and wildebeest on the crater floor, with 600-metre walls rising on all sides. The only place where the Big Five can be seen in a single day within a contained ecosystem.
📅 Inscribed 1979🏛️🌿 Mixed
Wikipedia UNESCO
Robben Island
Cape Town, South Africa
The island prison where Nelson Mandela was held for 18 of his 27 years of imprisonment. Guided tours are led by former political prisoners who describe daily life under apartheid. The ferry ride from the V&A Waterfront takes 30 minutes across Table Bay.
📅 Inscribed 1999🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Official Site
Americas
Historic Sanctuary of Machu Picchu
Cusco, Peru
A 15th-century Inca citadel perched on a 2,430-metre ridge between two peaks in the Andes. Dry-stone walls fitted without mortar, terraced agricultural platforms, and temples aligned with astronomical events. Abandoned before the Spanish conquest and unknown to the outside world until 1911.
📅 Inscribed 1983🏛️🌿 Mixed
🌐 Machu Picchu Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Chichén Itzá
Yucatán, Mexico
The most visited archaeological site in Mexico. El Castillo (Temple of Kukulcán) is a 30-metre step pyramid engineered so that equinox sunlight creates a serpent shadow descending the staircase. The Great Ball Court is the largest in Mesoamerica. One of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
📅 Inscribed 1988🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO Book Tour
Statue of Liberty
New York, United States
A 93-metre neoclassical copper statue on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, a gift from France dedicated in 1886. The crown observation deck offers panoramic views of Manhattan. Ellis Island, the adjacent immigration station that processed 12 million arrivals, is part of the same inscription.
📅 Inscribed 1984🏛️ Cultural
🌐 New York Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Galápagos Islands
Ecuador
The volcanic archipelago that inspired Darwin's theory of evolution. Giant tortoises, marine iguanas, blue-footed boobies, and flightless cormorants found nowhere else on Earth. Animals show virtually no fear of humans. The first UNESCO World Heritage Site ever inscribed.
📅 Inscribed 1978🌿 Natural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Iguazu National Park
Argentina / Brazil
275 individual waterfalls spanning nearly 3 km where the Iguazu River plunges into a basalt gorge. The Devil's Throat — a U-shaped, 82-metre-tall curtain of water — is the centrepiece. Eleanor Roosevelt reportedly said "Poor Niagara" upon seeing it. Inscribed on both the Argentine and Brazilian sides.
📅 Inscribed 1984🌿 Natural
🌐 Iguaçu Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Historic Centre of Lima
Lima, Peru
Founded by Pizarro in 1535 as the capital of Spanish South America. The Plaza Mayor, the Cathedral, and the San Francisco Monastery with its underground catacombs containing an estimated 25,000 bones. Baroque and colonial architecture painted in vivid ochre and terracotta.
📅 Inscribed 1988🏛️ Cultural
🌐 Lima Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Rapa Nui (Easter Island)
Chile
Nearly 900 monolithic moai statues carved by the Rapa Nui people between the 13th and 16th centuries. The tallest erected moai stands 10 metres high and weighs 82 tonnes. The most remote inhabited island on Earth — 3,700 km from the Chilean mainland. How the statues were moved remains debated.
📅 Inscribed 1995🏛️ Cultural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Natural Wonders
Grand Canyon National Park
Arizona, United States
A 446 km gorge carved by the Colorado River over six million years, exposing nearly two billion years of geological history in its layered rock walls. Up to 1,857 metres deep and 29 km wide. The South Rim is open year-round; the quieter North Rim sits 300 metres higher.
📅 Inscribed 1979🌿 Natural
Wikipedia UNESCO NPS
Great Barrier Reef
Queensland, Australia
The world's largest coral reef system — 2,300 km of reef stretching along Australia's northeast coast, visible from space. Over 1,500 species of fish, 400 types of coral, and six of the world's seven sea turtle species. Snorkeling, diving, and glass-bottom boat tours operate from Cairns and the Whitsundays.
📅 Inscribed 1981🌿 Natural
Wikipedia UNESCO Foundation
Yellowstone National Park
Wyoming, United States
The world's first national park, established in 1872. Sits atop one of the world's largest active volcanic systems. Old Faithful geyser erupts roughly every 90 minutes. Grand Prismatic Spring is the largest hot spring in the US at 112 metres across, with vivid bands of orange, yellow, and green created by heat-loving bacteria.
📅 Inscribed 1978🌿 Natural
🌐 Yellowstone Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Plitvice Lakes National Park
Croatia
Sixteen terraced lakes connected by 90 waterfalls and cascades, with colours ranging from azure to green to grey depending on mineral content and light. Boardwalks cross the lakes at water level. The travertine barriers between lakes grow by about 1 cm per year through calcium carbonate deposition.
📅 Inscribed 1979🌿 Natural
Wikipedia UNESCO Official Site
Serengeti National Park
Tanzania
Home to the Great Migration — over 1.5 million wildebeest and 250,000 zebras circuit 800 km between the Serengeti and Kenya's Masai Mara. The largest terrestrial animal migration on Earth. Year-round Big Five viewing across 14,763 km² of savanna, woodland, and kopje rock formations.
📅 Inscribed 1981🌿 Natural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Jiuzhaigou Valley
Sichuan, China
A system of multi-coloured lakes, waterfalls, and snow-capped peaks in the Min Mountains. The lakes are famous for their extraordinary clarity and kaleidoscopic blue, green, and turquoise colours caused by calcium carbonate deposits and hydrophyte growth. Autumn foliage turns the surrounding forests brilliant red and gold.
📅 Inscribed 1992🌿 Natural
Wikipedia UNESCO
Te Wahipounamu
South Island, New Zealand
A vast wilderness covering southwest New Zealand — Fiordland, Mount Aspiring, Aoraki/Mount Cook, and Westland Tai Poutini national parks. Milford Sound, Franz Josef Glacier, and the Routeburn Track within a single inscription. Temperate rainforest, glacial valleys, and coastal fiords largely unchanged since Gondwana.
📅 Inscribed 1990🌿 Natural
🌐 Queenstown Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
Wadi Rum
Aqaba, Jordan
A desert landscape of massive sandstone and granite rock formations, natural arches, and narrow canyons. Red sand dunes and towering cliffs used as the filming location for "The Martian" and "Lawrence of Arabia." Bedouin-run desert camps offer overnight stays under some of the darkest skies in the Middle East.
📅 Inscribed 2011🏛️🌿 Mixed
Wikipedia UNESCO Book Tour
Zhangjiajie (China Danxia extension)
Hunan, China
Over 3,000 sandstone pillar formations rising up to 200 metres from a sea of subtropical forest. The inspiration for the floating Hallelujah Mountains in "Avatar." The Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge spans 430 metres at 300 metres above the canyon floor — the world's highest and longest glass-bottomed bridge.
📅 Inscribed 1992🌿 Natural
🌐 Zhangjiajie Guide Wikipedia UNESCO
