Valais, 4 Vallées
The freeride capital of Europe. Mont Fort (3,330 m) anchors a vast off-piste playground. Vibrant international crowd, excellent restaurants, and the Xtreme Verbier freeride competition each March.

St. Moritz
Engadin, Graubünden
Twice Olympic host (1928, 1948) and the resort that invented winter tourism. Corviglia and Corvatsch offer wide, sunny pistes. Famous for 320 days of sunshine a year.

United States

Aspen Snowmass
Colorado
Four mountains, one town. Aspen Mountain for experts, Snowmass for families, Highlands for locals, Buttermilk for beginners. World-class dining and cultural scene in a historic mining town.

Vail
Colorado
America's largest single-mountain ski area. The legendary Back Bowls offer 12 km² of wide-open alpine terrain. Consistent dry Colorado powder. Purpose-built Tyrolean-style village.
📅 Nov–Apr🏔️ 2,476 m base⛷️ 234 km pistes

Wyoming
The steepest inbounds terrain in North America. Corbet's Couloir is skiing's most famous expert chute. 1,262 m vertical drop. Wild West town with the Teton range as a backdrop.
📅 Dec–Apr🏔️ 1,924 m base⛷️ 104 km pistes

The largest ski resort in the United States. Utah's dry, light powder averages 890 cm annually. Just 35 minutes from Salt Lake City airport — the most accessible major resort in North America.

Whistler Blackcomb
British Columbia
North America's largest ski resort by area. Two massive mountains linked by the Peak 2 Peak gondola. Over 1,600 m vertical. Hosted 2010 Winter Olympics alpine events. Vibrant pedestrian village.

Lake Louise
Banff National Park, Alberta
Set inside a national park with views of the turquoise lake and Victoria Glacier. Cold, dry powder and a long season. Part of the SkiBig3 pass with Sunshine Village and Mt Norquay.

Mont Tremblant
Quebec
Eastern Canada's premier resort. Colourful pedestrian village modelled on Quebec City. Excellent for families. Just 90 minutes from Montreal — viable as a long-weekend trip from US East Coast cities.

Japan
🌨️
Niseko
Hokkaido
Japan's most international resort. Averages 15+ metres of snowfall annually — the lightest, driest powder on Earth. Four interconnected resorts. English widely spoken. Outstanding food scene.
📅 Dec–Apr🏔️ 260 m base⛷️ 80 km pistes
Official Site Skiresort.info
⛰️
Hakuba Valley
Nagano
Eleven resorts sharing a lift pass. More terrain variety and steeper mountains than Niseko. Happo-One hosted the 1998 Olympics. Backcountry access unmatched in Japan.
📅 Dec–Apr🏔️ 760 m base⛷️ 137 km pistes
Official Site Skiresort.info
♨️
Nozawa Onsen
Nagano
A centuries-old hot-spring village with ski runs woven through it. Thirteen free public onsens. Less international, more authentically Japanese — the cultural pick for travellers.
📅 Dec–Apr🏔️ 565 m base⛷️ 50 km pistes
Official Site Skiresort.info
🌸
Furano
Hokkaido
Central Hokkaido's hidden gem. The same legendary powder as Niseko without the crowds. Perfectly groomed runs, excellent ski school, and former FIS World Cup host.
📅 Nov–May🏔️ 245 m base⛷️ 25 km pistes
Official Site Skiresort.info
