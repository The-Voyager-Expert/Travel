Three Types of Coverage
✈️
Trip Cancellation & Interruption
Reimburses non-refundable prepaid costs if you have to cancel or cut a trip short for a covered reason — illness, death of a family member, natural disaster, jury duty.
Typical cost4–10% of trip value
CFAR upgrade+50% of premium · reimburses 50–75%
Buy byWithin 14–21 days of first deposit
🏥
Emergency Medical
Covers hospital stays, ER visits, prescriptions, and imaging abroad. Critical because US health insurance (including Medicare) typically doesn't cover international care.
RecommendedAt least $100,000
Credit cards$0–$2,500 (not enough)
Real costA hospitalization abroad: $10,000–$50,000+
🚁
Medical Evacuation
Pays for emergency transport from a remote area or inadequate facility to a proper hospital — or home. The most expensive travel emergency by far if uninsured.
Recommended$250,000–$500,000
Real cost$100,000–$200,000+ uninsured
Best cardCSR/Preferred: $100,000 · Amex: uncapped
🧳
Baggage & Delay
Covers lost, stolen, or damaged luggage and reimbursement for essentials if bags are delayed. Trip delay coverage pays for hotel and meals when a flight delay strands you overnight.
Baggage$500–$3,000 per person
Trip delayKicks in after 6–12 hrs · $150–$500/day
CardsMost premium cards cover this well

When to Buy Standalone vs. Use Your Card
Situation Recommendation Why
International trip, any length
 Buy standalone Credit cards cap emergency medical at $0–$2,500. A hospitalization abroad easily runs $20,000+. Medicare doesn't cover outside the US.
Pre-existing medical condition
 Buy standalone Buy within 14–21 days of first deposit to qualify for a pre-existing condition waiver. Cards rarely cover pre-existing conditions at all.
Trip over $5,000 non-refundable
 Buy standalone Cards cap at $10,000/person but that excludes hotels booked direct, tour packages, and cruises on some cards. A policy covers the full trip cost.
Adventure / remote destination
 Buy standalone Skiing, diving, trekking — many cards exclude "hazardous activities." Evacuation from a remote location can top $150,000.
Cruise
 Buy standalone Medical care on a ship is expensive; medevac to a port hospital is even more so. Cruise line insurance is often overpriced — compare independently.
Domestic US trip, flexible bookings
 Card coverage likely enough Your US health insurance works domestically. If flights and hotels are refundable, trip cancellation risk is low. A premium card handles delay and bags.
Short domestic, under $1,500 total
 Skip or card only Insurance at 4–10% of trip cost isn't worth it for a cheap, mostly-refundable trip where your own health insurance is valid.

Key Things to Know
⏱️
Buy within 14–21 days of first deposit to qualify for a pre-existing condition waiver and the widest CFAR eligibility window. The clock starts on the day you pay your first deposit, not when you book flights.
🔍
Read what "covered reason" means. Basic trip cancellation covers a specific list: illness, death, jury duty, severe weather. It does not cover changing your mind, work conflicts, or fear of flying. CFAR covers anything — at a higher premium.
💳
You must pay with the card to activate card coverage. Charge at least the flights (or the full trip cost) to your premium card. Paying with points or a different card voids the protection.
🏥
Medicare and most US health plans stop at the border. If you're on Medicare, you have effectively zero international medical coverage. A standalone travel medical policy ($30–$60 for a short trip) fills that gap entirely.
🎿
Check the activity exclusions list. Skiing, diving, motorcycling, and trekking above certain altitudes are excluded from most standard plans. World Nomads covers these; most others don't without a rider.
🚢
Don't buy the cruise line's insurance. It's almost always more expensive and covers less than a comparable third-party plan. The one exception: cruise credit (vs. cash back) — some cruise line policies let you cancel for credit rather than cash.
⏰
CFAR has a hard cancel deadline. Most Cancel For Any Reason policies require you to cancel at least 48 hours before departure — some require 72 hours. If you wait until the night before to cancel, CFAR won't cover you. Check your exact window when you buy.
