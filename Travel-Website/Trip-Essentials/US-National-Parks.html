US National Parks

Updated July 2026
West
🏔️
Yellowstone National Park
Wyoming / Montana / Idaho
The world's first national park. Geothermal features including Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic Spring, and over 10,000 hot springs. Bison, elk, grizzly bears, and wolves roam freely.
📅 May–Sep🏞️ Geysers, wildlife📍 3,472 sq mi
🦌
Grand Teton National Park
Wyoming
Jagged peaks rising abruptly 7,000 feet above the valley floor. Pristine alpine lakes, abundant moose and bear sightings, and world-class mountaineering.
📅 Jun–Sep🏞️ Mountains, lakes📍 310,000 acres
🌲
Glacier National Park
Montana
Over 700 miles of trails through alpine meadows, glacial-carved valleys, and turquoise lakes. Going-to-the-Sun Road is one of the most scenic drives in North America.
📅 Jul–Sep🏞️ Glaciers, alpine lakes📍 1,013,322 acres
🌊
Olympic National Park
Washington
Three ecosystems in one park: temperate rainforest, alpine peaks with glaciers, and 73 miles of wild Pacific coastline. Hoh Rain Forest receives 12 feet of rain per year.
📅 Jun–Sep🏞️ Rainforest, coast, mountains📍 922,650 acres
🌋
Mount Rainier National Park
Washington
A 14,411-foot active stratovolcano draped in 25 glaciers. Wildflower meadows at Paradise, old-growth forests, and challenging summit climbs.
📅 Jul–Sep🏞️ Volcano, glaciers, meadows📍 236,381 acres
🌲
Yosemite National Park
California
Granite cliffs, giant sequoia groves, and iconic waterfalls. El Capitan and Half Dome are world-renowned climbing destinations. Yosemite Falls drops 2,425 feet.
📅 May–Oct🏞️ Waterfalls, granite cliffs📍 747,956 acres
🌵
Joshua Tree National Park
California
Where the Mojave and Colorado deserts meet. Surreal rock formations, twisted Joshua trees, and some of the darkest night skies in Southern California.
📅 Oct–Apr🏞️ Desert, stargazing📍 795,156 acres
🌿
Redwood National Park
California
Home to the tallest trees on Earth, reaching over 370 feet. Foggy coastal groves, fern-carpeted floors, and Roosevelt elk grazing in coastal prairies.
📅 May–Sep🏞️ Old-growth redwoods📍 139,000 acres
🏜️
Death Valley National Park
California / Nevada
The hottest, driest, and lowest national park. Badwater Basin sits 282 feet below sea level. Sand dunes, salt flats, colorful badlands, and dark-sky stargazing.
📅 Nov–Mar🏞️ Extremes, geology📍 3.4 million acres
Southwest
🏜️
Grand Canyon National Park
Arizona
A mile-deep chasm carved by the Colorado River exposing 2 billion years of geological history. South Rim open year-round; North Rim offers solitude.
📅 Mar–May, Sep–Nov🏞️ Canyon, geology📍 1,218,375 acres
🪨
Zion National Park
Utah
Towering red and cream sandstone cliffs, narrow slot canyons, and the Virgin River. Angels Landing and The Narrows are two of the most iconic hikes in the country.
📅 Mar–May, Sep–Nov🏞️ Canyons, hiking📍 147,242 acres
🪨
Bryce Canyon National Park
Utah
Thousands of crimson hoodoos — tall, thin rock spires — packed into natural amphitheaters. One of the darkest night skies in North America.
📅 May–Sep🏞️ Hoodoos, stargazing📍 35,835 acres
🏜️
Arches National Park
Utah
Over 2,000 natural sandstone arches including Delicate Arch, the state icon of Utah. Short hikes lead to dramatic formations against a red-rock desert.
📅 Mar–May, Sep–Nov🏞️ Arches, desert📍 76,679 acres
🏜️
Canyonlands National Park
Utah
Vast wilderness of canyons, mesas, and buttes carved by the Colorado and Green rivers. Island in the Sky offers sweeping views; the Needles district rewards backcountry hikers.
📅 Mar–May, Sep–Oct🏞️ Canyons, wilderness📍 337,598 acres
🌵
Saguaro National Park
Arizona
Iconic saguaro cacti standing up to 50 feet tall across the Sonoran Desert. Two districts flanking Tucson with petroglyphs, desert wildlife, and vivid sunsets.
📅 Nov–Mar🏞️ Saguaro cacti, desert📍 92,000 acres
Rockies
🏔️
Rocky Mountain National Park
Colorado
Trail Ridge Road climbs above 12,000 feet with tundra landscapes. Over 300 miles of trails, alpine lakes, elk herds, and peaks exceeding 14,000 feet.
📅 Jun–Sep🏞️ Alpine tundra, elk📍 265,807 acres
🏜️
Great Sand Dunes National Park
Colorado
North America's tallest sand dunes rising 750 feet against the Sangre de Cristo Mountains. Sand sledding, seasonal Medano Creek, and alpine hiking above the dune field.
📅 May–Sep🏞️ Sand dunes, mountains📍 107,342 acres
Southeast
🌄
Great Smoky Mountains National Park
Tennessee / North Carolina
America's most visited national park. Ancient mountains draped in blue mist, synchronous fireflies, over 100 species of native trees, and 800 miles of trails.
📅 Apr–Jun, Sep–Nov🏞️ Mountains, biodiversity📍 522,427 acres
🐊
Everglades National Park
Florida
The largest subtropical wilderness in the United States. Sawgrass prairies, mangrove forests, and coastal wetlands home to manatees, alligators, and the endangered Florida panther.
📅 Dec–Apr🏞️ Wetlands, wildlife📍 1.5 million acres
East
🍂
Acadia National Park
Maine
Rocky Atlantic coastline, Cadillac Mountain (first place to see sunrise in the US from October to March), carriage roads, and dramatic tidal pools on Mount Desert Island.
📅 May–Oct🏞️ Coast, fall foliage📍 49,075 acres
🌲
Shenandoah National Park
Virginia
Skyline Drive runs 105 miles along the Blue Ridge with 75 overlooks. Waterfalls, Appalachian Trail access, and one of the best fall foliage drives on the East Coast.
📅 Apr–Jun, Sep–Nov🏞️ Blue Ridge, fall color📍 199,224 acres
Alaska & Hawaii
🐻
Denali National Park
Alaska
Six million acres of wild Alaska centered on Denali (20,310 ft), North America's highest peak. Grizzlies, caribou, wolves, and Dall sheep across unbroken tundra.
📅 Jun–mid Sep🏞️ Denali, big wildlife📍 6 million acres
🌋
Hawaii Volcanoes National Park
Hawaii
Two active volcanoes — Kilauea and Mauna Loa. Lava flows, steam vents, volcanic craters, lava tubes, and rainforest on the Big Island. Eruption activity varies.
📅 Year-round🏞️ Active volcanoes📍 333,308 acres
