Himalayas & Karakoram
🏔️
Mount Everest (Sagarmatha / Chomolungma)
Nepal / Tibet, China
The highest point on Earth at 8,849 metres. The classic Everest Base Camp trek on the Nepal side takes 12–14 days through Sherpa villages and rhododendron forests, ending with a view of the Khumbu Icefall. The north face in Tibet is more remote and less trafficked.
⛰️ 8,849 m📍 Solukhumbu, Nepal🥾 EBC trek: 12–14 days
Nepal Tourism
🔺
K2 (Mount Godwin-Austen)
Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan / Xinjiang, China
The world's second-highest peak and the most technically difficult of the 8,000ers. A near-perfect pyramid of rock and ice rising 8,611 metres above the Karakoram. The Concordia base camp trek passes through the greatest concentration of high peaks on Earth — four 8,000-metre summits visible at once.
⛰️ 8,611 m📍 Karakoram Range🥾 K2 BC trek: 14–18 days
Pakistan Tourism
🐟
Machhapuchhre (Fishtail Peak)
Annapurna, Nepal
A sacred double-summit peak that resembles a fish tail when seen from Pokhara. Climbing is forbidden — no one has ever stood on its 6,993-metre summit. The mountain dominates the Annapurna Sanctuary and reflects perfectly in Phewa Lake at dawn. One of the most recognizable mountain silhouettes in the world.
⛰️ 6,993 m📍 Pokhara, Nepal🚫 Climbing prohibited
Nepal Tourism
🗻
Ama Dablam
Khumbu, Nepal
Called the "Matterhorn of the Himalayas" for its striking ridgeline. A steep, ice-encrusted pyramid at 6,812 metres that dominates the Everest Base Camp approach. The hanging glacier on its southwest face resembles a mother's necklace — "ama" means mother, "dablam" means necklace in Sherpa.
⛰️ 6,812 m📍 Sagarmatha NP🥾 Visible from EBC trek
Nepal Tourism
European Alps
🔻
Matterhorn (Cervino)
Zermatt, Switzerland / Cervinia, Italy
The most iconic mountain silhouette on Earth — a near-perfect pyramid of rock rising in isolation above Zermatt. The Hörnli Ridge is the classic climbing route (first ascent 1865, one of mountaineering's most storied events). The Gornergrat railway offers the definitive reflection view across Riffelsee.
⛰️ 4,478 m📍 Zermatt, Switzerland🚂 Gornergrat Railway
Zermatt Tourism
👑
Mont Blanc
Chamonix, France / Courmayeur, Italy
Western Europe's highest peak at 4,808 metres, a massive snow-dome visible from across the Alps. The Tour du Mont Blanc is one of the world's great long-distance treks — 170 km through France, Italy, and Switzerland. The Aiguille du Midi cable car from Chamonix reaches 3,842 metres.
⛰️ 4,808 m📍 Chamonix, France🥾 TMB: 11 days / 170 km
Chamonix Tourism
🧊
Eiger North Face
Bernese Oberland, Switzerland
A 1,800-metre vertical limestone wall — the "Mordwand" (Murder Wall) — that defined the golden age of alpine climbing. The Jungfrau Railway tunnels through the mountain's interior, stopping at the Eismeer window cut into the north face itself. Grindelwald below offers direct views of the wall.
⛰️ 3,967 m📍 Grindelwald, Switzerland🚂 Jungfrau Railway
Jungfrau Region Lucerne Guide
🏔️
Tre Cime di Lavaredo
Dolomites, Italy
Three massive vertical towers of pale dolomite limestone, each rising 500 metres from a high alpine plateau. The circuit hike (9.5 km, 3 hours) is one of the most photographed trails in the Alps. A UNESCO World Heritage site. The north faces glow orange-pink at sunset — the famous enrosadira effect.
⛰️ 2,999 m📍 Sexten Dolomites🥾 Circuit: 3 hrs / 9.5 km
Dolomites Tourism
🌿
Seceda Ridgeline
Val Gardena, Dolomites, Italy
A dramatic sawtooth ridgeline of vertical rock fins rising above rolling green meadows at 2,500 metres. The cable car from Ortisei reaches the plateau in minutes. The contrast between the razor-sharp grey peaks and the soft alpine grassland below is one of the most surreal landscapes in Europe.
⛰️ 2,519 m📍 Ortisei, South Tyrol🚡 Cable car access
Val Gardena Tourism
Scandinavia & Iceland
👅
Trolltunga
Odda, Norway
A flat tongue of rock jutting horizontally out over a 700-metre drop above Lake Ringedalsvatnet. The 28 km round-trip hike takes 10–12 hours through high-plateau terrain. One of Norway's most famous rock formations and one of the most dramatic viewpoints in Scandinavia.
⛰️ 1,180 m📍 Hordaland, Norway🥾 28 km / 10–12 hrs
Visit Norway
⛪
Kirkjufell
Snæfellsnes, Iceland
A perfectly symmetrical arrowhead-shaped mountain on Iceland's Snæfellsnes Peninsula, with a three-tiered waterfall (Kirkjufellsfoss) in the foreground. Featured as "Arrowhead Mountain" in Game of Thrones. At 463 metres it is modest in height but extraordinary in shape — the most photographed mountain in Iceland.
⛰️ 463 m📍 Grundarfjörður📷 Most photographed in Iceland
Visit Iceland Reykjavik Guide
🪨
Preikestolen (Pulpit Rock)
Forsand, Norway
A flat-topped cliff platform hanging 604 metres above Lysefjord with no railings or barriers. The 8 km round-trip hike from the trailhead takes 4–5 hours. The vertical drop and the open edge make it one of the most vertiginous viewpoints on Earth.
⛰️ 604 m above fjord📍 Ryfylke, Norway🥾 8 km / 4–5 hrs
Visit Norway
Patagonia & Andes
🗼
Torres del Paine
Magallanes, Chile
Three granite towers rising vertically to nearly 2,850 metres above the Patagonian steppe, with a glacial lake at their base. The W Trek (5 days) and O Circuit (8–10 days) are among the world's greatest multi-day hikes. Guanacos graze in the foreground of one of the most dramatic mountain panoramas on Earth.
⛰️ 2,850 m📍 Torres del Paine NP🥾 W Trek: 5 days / 80 km
Chile Tourism Santiago Guide
🏔️
Fitz Roy (Cerro Chaltén)
El Chaltén, Argentina
A jagged granite spire piercing the sky above the Patagonian ice field. The Tehuelche people called it Chaltén — "smoking mountain" — because clouds perpetually stream from its summit. The Laguna de los Tres trail (20 km, 8–10 hours) ends at a turquoise glacial lake directly beneath the 3,405-metre needle.
⛰️ 3,405 m📍 Los Glaciares NP🥾 Laguna de los Tres: 20 km
Argentina Tourism Buenos Aires Guide
🌈
Vinicunca (Rainbow Mountain)
Cusco, Peru
A mountain striped in bands of red, yellow, green, and turquoise caused by mineral deposits — iron oxide (red), sulfur (yellow), chlorite (green). Hidden under snow until recent glacial retreat exposed the colours. The hike from the trailhead reaches 5,200 metres. Altitude sickness is common — acclimatize in Cusco first.
⛰️ 5,200 m📍 Ausangate region🥾 Day hike from Cusco
Visit Peru Cusco Guide
🌋
Osorno Volcano
Los Lagos, Chile
A near-perfect snow-capped stratovolcano rising 2,652 metres above Lago Llanquihue in the Chilean Lake District. Often compared to Mount Fuji for its symmetry. Ski resort on the upper slopes in winter; hiking and a chair lift to the snowline in summer. Petrohué Falls and waterfalls sit at its base.
⛰️ 2,652 m📍 Vicente Pérez Rosales NP🎿 Year-round access
Chile Tourism
Africa
🌍
Mount Kilimanjaro
Kilimanjaro Region, Tanzania
Africa's highest peak and the world's tallest free-standing mountain — rising 5,895 metres directly from the surrounding plains. Five distinct climate zones from tropical rainforest to arctic summit glaciers. The Machame Route (6–7 days) is the most scenic. No technical climbing required — the most accessible of the Seven Summits.
⛰️ 5,895 m📍 Northern Tanzania🥾 Machame: 6–7 days
Tanzania Tourism
🏞️
Table Mountain
Cape Town, South Africa
A 3 km flat-topped sandstone plateau rising 1,085 metres above Cape Town, often draped in its signature "tablecloth" of orographic cloud. One of the oldest mountains on Earth — over 600 million years. The cable car rotates 360° during the ascent. Part of a national park with over 1,470 plant species.
⛰️ 1,085 m📍 Cape Town🚡 Rotating cable car
Table Mountain
🦍
Virunga Volcanoes
Rwanda / Uganda / DR Congo
A chain of eight volcanoes straddling three countries, home to the world's last mountain gorillas. Karisimbi (4,507 m) is the highest. Volcanoes National Park in Rwanda is the primary gorilla trekking base — a 1–4 hour hike through bamboo forest to sit with a gorilla family. Nyiragongo in DR Congo holds the world's largest lava lake.
⛰️ 4,507 m (Karisimbi)📍 Albertine Rift🦍 Mountain gorilla habitat
Visit Rwanda
Iconic Volcanoes
🗻
Mount Fuji
Shizuoka / Yamanashi, Japan
Japan's highest peak and most sacred mountain — a near-perfect symmetrical volcanic cone at 3,776 metres. The official climbing season runs July–September. The view from Lake Kawaguchi with cherry blossoms or autumn maples is Japan's most iconic image. A UNESCO World Heritage cultural site.
⛰️ 3,776 m📍 Fuji-Hakone-Izu🥾 Summit: 5–8 hrs up
Mt Fuji Climbing Tokyo Guide
🌋
Mount Etna
Catania, Sicily, Italy
Europe's highest and most active volcano at 3,357 metres, in near-constant eruption. A cable car and 4x4 reach the upper craters; guided summit treks walk along the rim. Vineyards and citrus groves grow on the fertile lower slopes. The volcanic soils produce some of Sicily's finest wines.
⛰️ 3,357 m📍 Eastern Sicily🚡 Cable car + 4x4 access
Italia.it Sicily Guide
🌊
Piton de la Fournaise
Réunion, France
One of the most active volcanoes on Earth, erupting roughly once a year. The lunar landscape of the Plaine des Sables leads to the crater rim, where lava flows sometimes reach the Indian Ocean. The 2,632-metre shield volcano is part of a UNESCO World Heritage site. Hiking the caldera takes 5–6 hours round-trip.
⛰️ 2,632 m📍 Réunion Island🌋 Erupts ~annually
Réunion Tourism
North America
🦅
Grand Teton
Wyoming, United States
The highest peak in the Teton Range, rising 2,100 metres above the valley floor with no foothills — one of the most dramatic vertical rises in North America. The Cathedral Group (Grand, Middle, and South Teton) forms a jagged skyline above Jenny Lake. The Snake River foreground with the Tetons behind is one of the most photographed scenes in the American West.
⛰️ 4,199 m📍 Grand Teton NP, Wyoming🥾 Many trailheads
NPS Info Yellowstone Guide
🏜️
Monument Valley
Arizona / Utah, United States
Isolated sandstone buttes rising 300 metres from a flat red desert floor on the Navajo Nation. The Mittens and Merrick Butte define the American West — featured in dozens of John Ford westerns. A 27 km Valley Drive loops past the formations. The Navajo manage all access; guided tours reach restricted areas.
⛰️ 1,822 m (West Mitten)📍 Navajo Nation🚗 27 km Valley Drive
Navajo Parks
🪨
Devils Tower
Wyoming, United States
A 264-metre monolithic igneous rock column rising above the Belle Fourche River — the remains of a volcanic intrusion exposed after surrounding sedimentary rock eroded away. The fluted columns of phonolite porphyry are a world-class rock-climbing destination. Sacred to over 20 Native American tribes. America's first national monument (1906).
⛰️ 1,558 m (summit)📍 Crook County, Wyoming🪨 264 m above river
NPS Info
🌲
Half Dome
Yosemite, California, United States
A sheer granite dome sliced in half by glacial action, rising 1,444 metres above the Yosemite Valley floor. The cables route to the summit (14–16 hours round-trip) is one of the most famous day hikes in the world. Permits required. El Capitan's 900-metre vertical wall faces it across the valley.
⛰️ 2,693 m📍 Yosemite NP, California🥾 Cables route: 14–16 hrs
NPS Info
🌨️
Denali (Mount McKinley)
Alaska, United States
North America's highest peak at 6,190 metres and the tallest mountain on Earth measured base-to-summit (5,500 m vertical rise from the surrounding lowlands). Only visible on roughly 30% of days due to weather. The park road extends 148 km into the wilderness with grizzly bears, caribou, and wolves.
⛰️ 6,190 m📍 Denali NP, Alaska🐻 Largest base-to-summit rise
NPS Info Alaska Guide
🌄
Mount Rainier
Washington, United States
A 4,392-metre glaciated stratovolcano dominating the Pacific Northwest skyline. The most glaciated peak in the contiguous United States — 25 named glaciers cover its slopes. Wildflower meadows at Paradise are legendary in late July. The Wonderland Trail (150 km) circumnavigates the entire mountain.
⛰️ 4,392 m📍 Mount Rainier NP, Washington🥾 Wonderland Trail: 150 km
NPS Info Seattle Guide
Canadian Rockies
🏔️
Mount Robson
British Columbia, Canada
The highest peak in the Canadian Rockies at 3,954 metres, with a massive north face permanently sheathed in ice. The Berg Lake Trail (23 km one way) passes the Emperor Falls and ends at a glacial lake where icebergs calve from the mountain's toe glacier. Clear views of the summit are rare — it generates its own weather.
⛰️ 3,954 m📍 Mount Robson Provincial Park🥾 Berg Lake: 23 km one way
BC Parks
🗻
Mount Assiniboine
British Columbia / Alberta, Canada
The "Matterhorn of the Canadian Rockies" — a near-perfect pyramid rising to 3,618 metres, visible only after a 27 km hike or a helicopter ride. Lake Magog at its base reflects the peak. The surrounding provincial park is roadless wilderness. One of the most remote and rewarding viewpoints in North America.
⛰️ 3,618 m📍 Mount Assiniboine PP🥾 27 km hike or heli access
BC Parks
Table Mountains & Tepuis
🌧️
Mount Roraima
Venezuela / Brazil / Guyana
A 2,810-metre flat-topped tepui — a tabletop mountain — where three countries meet at its summit. The 31 km² plateau is a lost world of endemic species, quartz crystal valleys, and ancient black rock. The trek takes 5–6 days from the Venezuelan side. Inspired Arthur Conan Doyle's "The Lost World."
⛰️ 2,810 m📍 Gran Sabana, Venezuela🥾 Trek: 5–6 days
Venezuela Tourism
💧
Auyán-tepui (Angel Falls)
Canaima, Venezuela
A massive table mountain with a 700 km² summit plateau, from which Angel Falls — the world's highest uninterrupted waterfall — plunges 979 metres. The falls are so high that water atomizes into mist before reaching the base. Access is by small plane to Canaima and then motorized canoe up the river.
⛰️ 2,535 m📍 Canaima NP (UNESCO)💧 979 m waterfall
Venezuela Tourism
Oceania
🌿
Milford Sound (Mitre Peak)
Fiordland, New Zealand
A 1,692-metre granite peak rising directly from the waters of Milford Sound — one of the most iconic mountain-water scenes on Earth. Rudyard Kipling called Milford Sound the "eighth wonder of the world." Cruise boats pass beneath the peak and through mist from permanent waterfalls. The Milford Track (53.5 km, 4 days) ends here.
⛰️ 1,692 m📍 Fiordland NP🥾 Milford Track: 53.5 km
DOC Info Queenstown Guide
🏔️
Aoraki / Mount Cook
Canterbury, New Zealand
New Zealand's highest peak at 3,724 metres, surrounded by 19 other peaks over 3,000 metres and the largest glaciers outside the Himalayas and polar regions. The Hooker Valley Track (10 km return) crosses three swing bridges to a glacier lake at the mountain's base. Named Aoraki ("Cloud Piercer") in Māori.
⛰️ 3,724 m📍 Aoraki/Mount Cook NP🥾 Hooker Valley: 10 km
DOC Info Queenstown Guide
East & Southeast Asia
🌅
Zhangjiajie Pillars
Hunan, China
Thousands of narrow sandstone pillars rising over 200 metres from subtropical forest, wreathed in mist. The 150-metre-tall Southern Sky Column was renamed "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" after inspiring the floating mountains in the film. Glass-bottomed skywalks and the world's longest glass bridge span the chasms between pillars.
⛰️ 1,262 m (highest pillar)📍 Zhangjiajie NP, Hunan🌫️ "Avatar Mountains"
China Tourism Zhangjiajie Guide
🐉
Huangshan (Yellow Mountains)
Anhui, China
Granite peaks emerging from a sea of clouds, twisted Huangshan pines clinging to cliff edges, and hot springs at the base. The landscape that inspired centuries of Chinese ink-wash painting. Cable cars reach the upper ridges; sunrise from Bright Summit Peak (1,864 m) is one of China's great spectacles. A UNESCO World Heritage site.
⛰️ 1,864 m📍 Anhui Province🚡 Cable car access
China Tourism
🌿
Mount Kinabalu
Sabah, Malaysian Borneo
Southeast Asia's highest peak at 4,095 metres, rising from tropical rainforest to bare granite. The two-day climb (summit at dawn) crosses six vegetation zones and ends on a moonscape of smooth granite slabs. The Via Ferrata on the upper slopes is the world's highest. Over 5,000 plant species on its slopes, including carnivorous pitcher plants.
⛰️ 4,095 m📍 Kinabalu NP (UNESCO)🥾 2-day summit climb
Sabah Tourism
Australia
🟠
Uluru (Ayers Rock)
Northern Territory, Australia
A 348-metre sandstone monolith — the world's largest — rising from the flat red desert of central Australia. Sacred to the Aṉangu people for over 30,000 years. The rock changes colour through the day, glowing deep orange-red at sunset. A 10.6 km base walk circles the formation. Climbing has been permanently closed since 2019.
⛰️ 863 m (summit)📍 Uluṟu-Kata Tjuṯa NP🥾 Base walk: 10.6 km
Parks Australia
🔴
Kata Tjuta (The Olgas)
Northern Territory, Australia
A group of 36 domed rock formations — the tallest (Mount Olga) higher than Uluru at 546 metres above the plain. The Valley of the Winds walk (7.4 km) threads between the massive domes. Less visited than Uluru but considered more sacred by the Aṉangu people. The domes glow purple and red at sunset.
⛰️ 1,066 m (Mount Olga summit)📍 Uluṟu-Kata Tjuṯa NP🥾 Valley of the Winds: 7.4 km
Parks Australia
Middle East
🏜️
Wadi Rum
Aqaba, Jordan
Massive sandstone and granite rock formations rising from a red sand desert — the landscape where T.E. Lawrence operated during the Arab Revolt. The Seven Pillars of Wisdom, Jebel Khazali with its ancient inscriptions, and natural rock bridges define the terrain. Jeep tours, camel rides, and Bedouin desert camps offer overnight access.
⛰️ 1,754 m (Jebel Rum)📍 Southern Jordan🏕️ Bedouin camps

📜
Mount Sinai (Jebel Musa)
South Sinai, Egypt
A 2,285-metre granite peak in the Sinai Peninsula, revered in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam as the mountain where Moses received the Ten Commandments. The pre-dawn climb (2–3 hours via the camel path) rewards with sunrise over a sea of jagged desert peaks. St. Catherine's Monastery (6th century) sits at the base.
⛰️ 2,285 m📍 St. Catherine, Sinai🥾 Summit: 2–3 hrs
