China
Mandarin Oriental Qianmen
Qianmen, Beijing · From $700/night
Not a hotel in any conventional sense — 42 standalone heritage courtyard houses spread across eight 600-year-old hutong lanes. An 8-year restoration preserved 83% of original Ming and Qing Dynasty structures. No. 14 on World's 50 Best Hotels, Forbes Five-Star, and 2025 Prix Versailles winner for the world's most beautiful hotel.

France
Ritz Paris
Place Vendôme, Paris · From $2,200/night
The hotel that defined modern luxury, opened 1898 by César Ritz on Place Vendôme. Coco Chanel lived here 35 years; Hemingway "liberated" the bar in 1944. Reopened in 2016 after a four-year, €400M restoration. The world's only Chanel spa, a 142-room palace with Michelin-starred L'Espadon.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Hôtel Plaza Athénée
Avenue Montaigne, Paris · From $1,800/night
A 1913 palace hotel on Avenue Montaigne with the red-geranium balconies that legend ties to Jean Gabin decorating for Marlene Dietrich. Palace distinction, Dior Spa (Dior's first worldwide), and a roster from Grace Kelly to Anna Wintour's Fashion Week base.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Four Seasons Hotel George V
Avenue George V, Paris · From $1,400/night
A 1928 Art Deco palace holding 6 Michelin stars — the most of any hotel in Europe. Le Cinq (3 stars), L'Orangerie (2 stars), Le George (1 star + Green Star). Jeff Leatham's 15,000-flower-per-week installations have made the lobby a cultural attraction in its own right.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Cheval Blanc Paris
Quai du Louvre, Paris · From $2,100/night
LVMH's crown jewel — 72 rooms in the restored La Samaritaine building on the Seine, the result of a 16-year, €750M renovation. Plenitude restaurant holds 3 Michelin stars (earned in its first full year), the 30-metre pool is Paris's largest indoor hotel pool, and the Dior Spa is the only one of its kind.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Le Bristol Paris
Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré, Paris · From $1,500/night
An Oetker Collection palace open since 1925 with 4 Michelin stars across two restaurants — Epicure (3 stars, MOF chef Arnaud Faye) and 114 Faubourg (1 star). A penthouse rooftop pool designed by Caesar Pinnau and a 1,200 sqm interior courtyard garden.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Hôtel de Crillon, A Rosewood Hotel
Place de la Concorde, Paris · From $1,500/night
A 1758 neoclassical palace commissioned by Louis XV, reimagined with interiors by Karl Lagerfeld and reopened in 2017 after a €200M restoration. L'Ecrin holds 2 Michelin stars; the pool is lined with 17,600 hand-applied gold-leaf fish-scale mosaic tiles.

Hong Kong
Rosewood Hong Kong
Victoria Dockside, Tsim Sha Tsui · From $700/night
No. 1 hotel in the world (World's 50 Best Hotels 2025). The Rosewood brand's global flagship — 3 Michelin Keys, two Michelin-starred restaurants, Tony Chi interiors and a 400-piece art collection. Harbour House penthouse commands up to $100,000/night.

India
Rambagh Palace
Bhawani Singh Road, Jaipur · From $400/night
India's first palace hotel (1957), the former residence of the Maharaja of Jaipur. 47 acres of Mughal gardens, hand-painted ceilings, a polo bar and peacock-filled lawns. You are sleeping in a maharaja's bedroom — not a room decorated to look like one.
Official Site Wikipedia Map
Taj Lake Palace
Lake Pichola, Udaipur · From $350/night
A white marble palace built in 1746 floating on Lake Pichola, reached only by boat. 83 rooms of carved stone and semi-precious inlays. 3 Michelin Keys (highest tier). Featured as Octopussy's lair in the 1983 James Bond film. There is nothing else like it anywhere.
Official Site Wikipedia Map
Italy
Passalacqua
Moltrasio, Lake Como · From $1,500/night
No. 1 hotel in the world (2023), No. 2 (2024), No. 4 (2025). An 18th-century villa with 24 rooms where Napoleon and Bellini once stayed. No lobby, no check-in desk — guests are greeted at the gate of a private home. The reason Lake Como is back on the map.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Lake Como Guide Map
Aman Venice
Grand Canal, Venice · From $1,500/night
A 16th-century Palazzo Papadopoli with 24 suites, original Tiepolo frescoes and Brustolon carvings. The only Grand Canal palazzo hotel with two private gardens on the water. 3 Michelin Keys, Forbes Five-Star. The aristocratic Arrivabene family still lives above the hotel.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Venice Guide Map
Belmond Hotel Cipriani
Giudecca, Venice · From $1,200/night
Founded in 1958 by Giuseppe Cipriani (of Harry's Bar fame), reached by private launch from San Marco. The only hotel pool in central Venice. One-Michelin-star Oro restaurant in collaboration with Massimo Bottura. Currently undergoing a multi-phase Peter Marino renovation.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Venice Guide Map
Four Seasons Hotel Firenze
Borgo Pinti, Florence · From $1,200/night
Two Medici-era palaces — the 1473 Palazzo della Gherardesca and a former convent — set in Florence's largest private garden (11 acres). Original Renaissance frescoes uncovered during restoration. One-Michelin-star Il Palagio and a garden pool ringed by specimen sequoia and ginkgo trees.

Japan
Aman Tokyo
Otemachi Tower, Tokyo · From $1,200/night
Aman's first urban hotel — 84 rooms on the top six floors of the Otemachi Tower, designed by Kerry Hill. A six-storey washi-paper lobby, black basalt furo baths, Imperial Palace Garden views. No. 25 on World's 50 Best Hotels, 2 Michelin Keys, Forbes Five-Star.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Tokyo Guide Map
Bulgari Hotel Tokyo
Yaesu, Tokyo · From $1,200/night
No. 15 on World's 50 Best Hotels (highest-ranked hotel in Japan). 3 Michelin Keys. 98 rooms on floors 40–45 of Tokyo Midtown Yaesu, fusing Italian marble and Venetian glass with Japanese elm and bamboo. One-Michelin-star dining, a sky pool and a traditional onsen at altitude.

Monaco
Hôtel de Paris Monte-Carlo
Place du Casino, Monte Carlo · From $800/night
A 160-year-old Belle Époque monument on the Place du Casino, reopened in 2019 after a €280M renovation. Le Louis XV — Alain Ducasse was the first hotel restaurant in the world to earn 3 Michelin stars (1990). A wine cellar of 400,000 bottles. 3 Michelin Keys, Forbes Five-Star.

Morocco
Mandarin Oriental, Marrakech
Route du Golf Royal, Marrakech · From $800/night
A 20-hectare estate of olive groves and rose gardens with 63 private villas, each with its own garden, pool and hot tub. Atlas Mountain views, Michelin-starred dining, a spa spanning three treatment rooms and two hammams. The benchmark for this list.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Marrakech Guide Map
Royal Mansour Marrakech
Rue Abou Abbas El Sebti, Marrakech · From $1,500/night
Commissioned by King Mohammed VI — a private medina of 53 three-storey riads, each with a rooftop terrace and plunge pool, connected by underground tunnels for invisible service. The only 3-Michelin-starred restaurant on the African continent.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Marrakech Guide Map
La Mamounia
Avenue Bab Jdid, Marrakech · From $700/night
A palace hotel on grounds gifted by an 18th-century sultan, open since 1929. Winston Churchill called it "the most lovely spot in the whole world" and painted here repeatedly. 17 acres of gardens within the old city walls, Art Deco interiors and Moroccan zellij tilework.

United Arab Emirates
Burj Al Arab Jumeirah
Jumeirah Beach Road, Dubai · From $1,500/night
A sail-shaped tower on its own artificial island, open since 1999. All-duplex suites, a restaurant reached by simulated submarine ride, a helipad and floor-to-ceiling Arabian Gulf views from every room. The building that became a global symbol of modern luxury.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Dubai Guide Map
Mandarin Oriental Emirates Palace
West Corniche, Abu Dhabi · From $500/night
A 394-room palace on a 1.3-kilometre private beach, built at a reported cost of three billion dollars. 114 domes, a marina, two pools and interiors finished in gold leaf and mother-of-pearl. The sheer scale of the construction has no equivalent in modern hospitality.

United States
The Beverly Hills Hotel
Sunset Boulevard, Beverly Hills · From $1,000/night
The Pink Palace on Sunset Boulevard, open since 1912, set among 12 acres of tropical gardens. 23 private bungalows with their own gardens and pools — Marilyn Monroe, Elizabeth Taylor and Howard Hughes each kept one. The hotel that gave Beverly Hills its name and its identity.
