British Virgin Islands
Rosewood Little Dix Bay
Virgin Gorda, British Virgin Islands
Laurance Rockefeller built this bay into a low-impact nature retreat in 1964 rather than a conventional resort, and that founding idea — buildings kept below the tree line, nothing between the room and the beach but a screen door — still shapes the 42 rooms, 25 suites and four hillside villas today. Queen Elizabeth II and Prince Philip stayed here for the privacy as much as the view. Rosewood took over management in 1993 and rebuilt the property after the 2017 hurricanes without changing that founding restraint.

Fiji
Laucala Island
Laucala Island, Fiji
The entire 3,500-acre island is the resort — 25 villas, each with a private pool, and roughly five staff for every guest. The rate covers everything: meals cooked from vegetables and livestock raised on the island's own farm, a David McLay Kidd golf course carved through the hills, and unlimited diving and water sports across the surrounding reef. It's closed for a full renovation now, set to reopen in 2027 under COMO instead of its original owners.

France
Cheval Blanc Paris
Quai du Louvre, Paris
LVMH spent years turning the old La Samaritaine department store into its Paris flagship, and it shows in details like a butler assigned to every room and Seine views from nearly all of them. Arnaud Donckele runs the rooftop restaurant, Le Tout-Paris, which holds three Michelin stars, and the Dior Spa two floors down has a 30-metre pool lit entirely from below.
Official Site 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Hôtel du Cap-Eden-Roc
Cap d'Antibes, French Riviera
A private villa turned hotel in 1889 on 22 acres of pine-shaded gardens at the tip of Cap d'Antibes, with a seawater pool blasted into the rocks at Eden-Roc and no printed price list anywhere on the property — a Riviera institution since the 1920s, when Gerald and Sara Murphy's summer-long stay here inspired F. Scott Fitzgerald's Tender Is the Night. Rooms and suites are spread across the main Villa Soleil, the Eden-Roc pavilion, and a handful of private garden villas.

La Réserve Paris
Avenue Gabriel, Paris
Behind an unmarked courtyard gate near the Champs-Élysées, this 40-room Haussmann mansion has no public lobby and no sign — you're either expected or you're not. Jérôme Banctel's restaurant Le Gabriel holds two Michelin stars, and the Nescens spa in the basement runs Swiss medical-grade longevity programs rather than standard spa treatments.

French Polynesia
The Brando
Tetiaroa Atoll, Tahiti
Marlon Brando bought this atoll in the 1960s after filming Mutiny on the Bounty nearby, and the resort that eventually opened on it — 35 villas, each with its own pool, reachable only by a 20-minute private flight from Tahiti — runs on solar power and coconut-oil biofuel with a marine research station on site. The rate includes everything: three restaurants, spa treatments, and excursions across a reef that's been under UNESCO protection since before the hotel existed.

Indonesia
Bvlgari Resort Bali
Uluwatu, Bali
59 villas and five mansions strung along a 160-metre limestone cliff on Bali's Bukit Peninsula, every one with its own private infinity pool facing the Indian Ocean. The flagship Bvlgari Villa alone runs 1,300 square metres with a 20-metre heated pool that cascades into a second pool below it. Milan-based Antonio Citterio designed the interiors, pairing Italian furnishings with traditional Balinese materials and craftsmanship.

Italy
Aman Venice
Grand Canal, Venice
Aman's Venice outpost occupies one of only eight monumental palazzos still standing on the Grand Canal, with 24 rooms wrapped around original 18th-century Tiepolo ceiling frescoes. Two private gardens back onto the water — genuinely rare in a city built wall-to-wall — and the kitchen will serve any dish, in any room, at any hour, rather than running a fixed restaurant schedule.
Official Site 🌐 Venice Guide Map
Belmond Hotel Cipriani
Giudecca, Venice
79 rooms and suites on the quiet island of Giudecca, reached from St Mark's Square by the hotel's own launch across five minutes of lagoon — the water crossing alone does most of the work of leaving Venice's crowds behind. The hotel's pool, filled with heated water, is the only one of its size in the city, and Peter Marino's interiors mix 18th-century detail with contemporary design across gardens that face directly onto the lagoon.

Passalacqua
Moltrasio, Lake Como
An 18th-century villa on Lake Como, restored from a private residence into just 24 rooms, with terraced gardens that step straight down to the water and George Clooney's villa in view across the lake. Breakfast, afternoon tea and evening aperitivo all come with the room rate, and the infinity pool sits at the edge of the property looking back at the water.

Japan
Aman Tokyo
Otemachi, Tokyo
84 suites fill the top six floors of the Otemachi office tower, arranged so every one is a suite — the size difference between the smallest and largest room here is unusually narrow for a luxury hotel, so no one's stay feels like an afterthought. The spa spans two full floors with onsen-style hot baths and a 30-metre pool, and Musashi, the in-house sushi counter, serves a 21-course omakase.

Maldives
Cheval Blanc Randheli
Noonu Atoll, Maldives
LVMH built this one from scratch across a private cluster of islands in Noonu Atoll, connected by raised wooden walkways, with 46 villas and a dedicated "Ambassadeur" assigned to each. The spa, run by Guerlain, wraps around a 30-metre infinity pool, and the four-bedroom Private Island villa gets its own seaplane arrival and private chef, entirely separate from the main resort.

Kudadoo Maldives Private Island
Lhaviyani Atoll, Maldives
Kudadoo built its entire pitch around one idea — "Anything, Anytime, Anywhere" — meaning the rate genuinely includes everything: a personal chef on call, unlimited spa treatments, a private infinity pool at each of the 15 overwater residences, and no printed menu you're expected to choose from. The island caps out at 30 guests total, so it never feels like a resort so much as a very large, very private house.

Soneva Jani
Noonu Atoll, Maldives
59 overwater and island villas spread across a 5.6km lagoon, the smallest of them still larger than most hotels' presidential suites. Every villa has a private pool and a dedicated "barefoot butler," plus a retractable bedroom roof for sleeping under the stars and a water slide straight from the upper deck into the lagoon below.

Velaa Private Island
Noonu Atoll, Maldives
47 villas in Noonu Atoll, each with a private pool, built around the only golf course in the Maldives — a 9-hole José María Olazábal design — and a snow room for contrast therapy that feels almost absurd this close to the equator. Michelin-trained chef Adeline Grattard runs the kitchens, and the wine cellar is one of the most serious in the Indian Ocean.

Morocco
Amanjena
Route de Ouarzazate, Marrakech
40 pavilions and two-storey maisons wrap around a reflecting pool the size of a small lake, set in a walled oasis of centuries-old olive trees just outside Marrakech. The domes and rose-colored walls draw on traditional Moroccan palace architecture rather than a generic resort look, and the hammam spa runs full traditional rituals rather than a Westernized version of one.

Kasbah Tamadot
Asni, Atlas Mountains
Richard Branson spotted this kasbah from a hot air balloon over the Atlas Mountains and opened it as a hotel in 2005; after the 2023 earthquake, it was rebuilt larger, with 42 rooms across the main kasbah, three-bedroom riads and traditional Berber tents pitched in the gardens. It sits an hour from Marrakech in the foothills near Asni, a working Berber market village, with hiking straight into the mountains from the property and a spa built around a hammam and heated pool.

Mandarin Oriental, Marrakech
Route du Golf Royal, Marrakech
There isn't a single hotel room on this 20-hectare estate — all 61 stays are freestanding villas, each with its own garden and pool, scattered across grounds planted with olive trees and roses with the Atlas Mountains visible in the distance. Multiple restaurants sit apart from each other across the property, so dinner never feels like walking into a shared dining room.

The Oberoi, Marrakech
Route de Ouarzazate, Marrakech
84 rooms, suites and villas spread across 28 acres of olive and citrus groves that predate the hotel by centuries, with private pools attached to 76 of them. The central courtyard was modeled directly on the 14th-century Medersa Ben Youssef across town, and Rohit Ghai, a Michelin-starred chef, runs the resort's Indian restaurant, Rivayat.

Royal Mansour Marrakech
Rue Abou Abbas El Sebti, Marrakech
King Mohammed VI commissioned this one personally — 53 private riads, each three stories with its own rooftop terrace and plunge pool, connected by underground tunnels so staff can appear and disappear without ever being seen crossing a courtyard. La Grande Table Marocaine, the in-house restaurant, holds two Michelin stars, and the 32,300-square-foot spa runs on Sisley products throughout.

Saint Barthélemy
Eden Rock
St Jean Bay, Saint Barthélemy
37 rooms, suites and villas built directly onto a rock promontory splitting St Jean Bay in two, founded in the 1950s by the island's own mayor and rebuilt from the ground up after Hurricane Irma. Howard Hughes, Greta Garbo and generations of the Rockefeller family have stayed here — Garbo checked in under a false name for three days and ended up staying three weeks. The rooms are still filled with antiques, four-poster beds and steamer trunks rather than minimalist design.

Saint Lucia
Jade Mountain
Soufrière, Saint Lucia
24 sanctuaries with a wall deliberately left off each one, so the infinity pool inside your room runs straight out toward the twin volcanic peaks of the Pitons with nothing in between. Every pool is surfaced in one-of-a-kind glass tile, sized and colored differently suite to suite, and ceilings run 15 feet high to keep the open side from ever feeling exposed. Five smaller Sky suites skip the pool for a soaking tub instead, but the sanctuaries are the reason people come.

54 villas and 10 suites carved into the cliffs of AlUla, near Hegra, Saudi Arabia's first UNESCO World Heritage Site. Cliff-hanging villas and cave suites come with cliffside plunge pools, and the Michelin-starred Nyra serves the property's fine dining.

The Red Sea, Saudi Arabia
63 villas on a private island in Saudi Arabia's Red Sea development, accessible only by seaplane or yacht, built around a coral reef that was completely uninhabited before construction began. Every villa has its own pool and a dedicated butler — the Ritz-Carlton Reserve tier is the brand's quietest, most private version of itself.

The Red Sea, Saudi Arabia
90 villas on the same private Red Sea archipelago, every one with its own pool and direct coral reef access, run with the full St. Regis ritual — a dedicated butler for every guest, evening champagne sabering, and a butler-drawn bath on request. Several restaurants sit along the private beach rather than centralized in one dining room.

Seychelles
North Island
North Island, Seychelles
11 villas on an entire private island, each with its own plunge pool and a staff-to-guest ratio north of 2:1. The rate covers essentially everything — meals built around what you actually want to eat, all drinks, diving, kayaking, laundry — and giant Aldabra tortoises wander the grounds freely. You can also buy out the whole island, capped at 22 guests total.

South Africa
Royal Malewane
Greater Kruger, Thornybush Private Game Reserve
Four lodges and three private villas sit on their own concession inside Greater Kruger, unfenced and contiguous with the national park itself, so wildlife moves through freely rather than being contained. The two Royal Suites run 5,490 square feet each with a private chef and a dedicated game vehicle; the rate covers all meals, premium wine and spirits, and two game drives a day for a property that never holds more than about 50 guests.

Singita Boulders Lodge
Sabi Sand Game Reserve, Kruger
13 suites on the Sand River inside the Sabi Sand reserve, each with a private heated plunge pool, built around a wine cellar constructed literally around a boulder that couldn't be moved during construction. Everything is included — meals, premium wine and spirits, twice-daily game drives and walking safaris — with a dedicated tracker and a resident sommelier on staff.

Tanzania
andBeyond Mnemba Island
Mnemba Island, Zanzibar
12 open-sided Bandas shaped like the spiral of a nautilus shell on an island just 1.5km wide, ringed by one of the healthiest coral reefs in the Indian Ocean. There's no wall between the room and the sea — waves are close enough to be part of the experience — and the entire island holds only a small handful of guests at a time, sharing the beach with green turtles that nest on it. Diving and snorkeling start a few steps from the door; a Wellness Banda runs breathwork and sound-therapy sessions on a raised deck over the sand.

Thailand
Capella Bangkok
Chao Phraya River, Bangkok
101 rooms, suites and riverfront villas along the Chao Phraya, each with a dedicated "Culturist" — a concierge who curates the entire stay rather than just booking restaurants — and a private balcony over the water. Mauro Colagreco's Côte holds two Michelin stars, and ground-floor villas add a private jacuzzi plunge pool on top of everything else.

Amanpuri
Pansea Beach, Phuket
The first Aman ever built, opened in 1988 on a private headland above Pansea Beach, with 83 pavilions and villas that reopened in late 2026 after a full renovation. The larger villas — some with up to six bedrooms — come with a private pool, a live-in housekeeper and a personal chef, closer to renting a very well-staffed private house than checking into a hotel room.

United States
Aman New York
Fifth Avenue, Manhattan
83 suites fill the Crown Building on Fifth Avenue, and there isn't a standard room in the building — the smallest suite still runs a soaking tub and Aman's signature minimalist design. The three-floor, 25,000-square-foot spa has two indoor pools and a jazz lounge, and it's deliberately built to feel nothing like Manhattan — hushed, residential, no street noise.

Amangiri
Canyon Point, Utah
Thirty-four suites, plus ten tented pavilions at the adjoining Camp Sarika, cut into 900 acres of red-rock desert on the Colorado Plateau, where the concrete architecture is built to disappear against the mesa rather than stand out from it. The Aman Spa is carved into the rock itself, and the pool wraps around a stone outcrop so the water seems to grow out of the landscape. Some suites and every Camp Sarika pavilion have a private pool or plunge pool; days are built around canyon hikes, stargazing and total quiet.

Post Ranch Inn
Big Sur, California
40 rooms perched 1,200 feet above the Pacific on a former cattle ranch homesteaded in the 1860s, with no televisions and construction designed to disturb as few trees as possible when it opened in 1992. Peak Houses have curved living roofs planted with grass and wildflowers so they read as part of the hillside from above; Ocean Houses and the Cliff House face Highway 1's coastline directly. Every room has a private deck, fireplace and soaking tub.

Zimbabwe
Singita Pamushana
Malilangwe Wildlife Reserve
8 suites on a private 130,000-acre reserve in southeastern Zimbabwe, one of the region's most biodiverse conservation areas, where black rhino, elephant and roan antelope move freely across the property. Everything's included — premium South African wine, twice-daily game drives, walking safaris — on a reserve so remote and quiet it barely resembles the safari-lodge template most people picture.
