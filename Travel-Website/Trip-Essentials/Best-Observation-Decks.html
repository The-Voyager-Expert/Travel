Tokyo Skytree Tembo Deck & Tembo Galleria
Tokyo, Japan
The tallest tower in the world at 634 metres. The Tembo Deck at 350 metres features floor-to-ceiling glass panels and a glass floor section with views extending 70 kilometres on clear days. The upper Tembo Galleria is a spiral glass-enclosed skywalk ascending from 445 to 451 metres — the highest accessible point.
📏 350 m / 451 m🏗️ Opened 2012🗼 634 m tower

The observation deck on the 118th floor of the Shanghai Tower — the tallest building in China at 632 metres. Floor-to-ceiling windows offer 360-degree views of the Pudong skyline. The world's fastest elevators reach the deck in 55 seconds. The 119th floor has a 1.7-metre-wide glass floor strip.

Taipei 101 Observatory
Taipei, Taiwan
The former tallest building in the world (2004–2010) at 508 metres. The indoor observatory on the 89th floor sits at 382 metres with floor-to-ceiling windows. The outdoor deck on the 91st floor at 391 metres is open weather permitting. The exposed 730-tonne tuned mass damper — the world's largest — is visible between the two levels.

CN Tower
Toronto, Canada
A 553-metre communications tower that held the world's tallest free-standing structure title for 32 years (1975–2007). The main LookOut at 346 metres has a glass floor section; the SkyPod at 447 metres is a higher enclosed level. The EdgeWalk — an outdoor, hands-free walk on a 1.5-metre-wide ledge circling the tower's roof at 356 metres — is the world's highest full-circle hands-free walk.
📏 346 m / 447 m🏗️ Opened 1976🗼 553 m tower

Open-air viewing on the outer ring.
