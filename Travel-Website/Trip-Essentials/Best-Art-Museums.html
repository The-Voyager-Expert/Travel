Best Art Museums

France
🏛️
Louvre Museum
Paris · Encyclopedic · All eras
The most-visited art museum in the world, housed in a 12th-century fortress turned royal palace. Collection of roughly 500,000 objects with 35,000 on display, including the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and Winged Victory of Samothrace. I.M. Pei's glass pyramid entrance (1989) is a landmark modern addition.
📅 Founded 1793👥 8.7M visitors (2024)
Official Site Paris Guide
🎨
Musée d'Orsay
Paris · Impressionism · Post-Impressionism
Housed in a grand Beaux-Arts railway station built 1898–1900, holds the world's largest collection of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist works. Masterpieces by Monet, Degas, Renoir, Cézanne, and Van Gogh fill the soaring former train hall.
📅 Opened 1986👥 3.2M+ visitors/year
Official Site Paris Guide
🔲
Centre Pompidou
Paris · Modern · Contemporary
Iconic high-tech building by Renzo Piano and Richard Rogers with its structure and mechanical systems on the exterior. Houses the Musée National d'Art Moderne — Europe's largest collection of modern and contemporary art.
📅 Opened 1977👥 ~8M visitors/year
Official Site Paris Guide
Italy
🖼️
Uffizi Gallery
Florence · Renaissance
Built starting 1560 by Giorgio Vasari for Cosimo I de' Medici, the Uffizi holds the world's foremost collection of Botticelli — including "Primavera" and "The Birth of Venus" — alongside works by Giotto, Leonardo, Raphael, and Michelangelo.
📅 Public since 1769👥 4–5M visitors/year
Official Site Florence Guide
⛪
Vatican Museums
Vatican City · Renaissance · Classical Antiquities
Founded 1506 under Pope Julius II, the Vatican Museums culminate in the Sistine Chapel — Michelangelo's ceiling (completed 1512) and "The Last Judgment." Also houses the Raphael Rooms and one of the world's great collections of classical sculpture.
📅 Founded 1506👥 6.8M visitors (2024)
Official Site Rome Guide
🗿
Galleria dell'Accademia
Florence · Renaissance Sculpture
Home to Michelangelo's original "David" since 1873. Founded in 1784 by Grand Duke Pietro Leopoldo of Tuscany, it is the second most-visited art museum in Italy after the Uffizi.
📅 Founded 1784👥 ~1.5M visitors/year
Official Site Florence Guide
Spain
🖼️
Prado Museum
Madrid · Spanish Old Masters · Baroque
Opened 1819 as the Royal Museum of Paintings. Holds roughly 8,600 paintings and 700 sculptures, with the world's largest collections of Velázquez ("Las Meninas") and Goya. Together with the Reina Sofía and Thyssen-Bornemisza, it forms Madrid's "Golden Triangle of Art."
📅 Opened 1819👥 3.3M visitors (2023)
Official Site Madrid Guide
🎭
Reina Sofía Museum
Madrid · Modern · Contemporary
Spain's national museum of 20th-century art, home to Picasso's "Guernica" since 1992. Also holds major works by Dalí, Miró, and Juan Gris. Housed in a former 18th-century hospital with a striking Jean Nouvel expansion.
📅 Opened 1992
Official Site Madrid Guide
🏛️
Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum
Madrid · Encyclopedic · Renaissance to 20th Century
Opened 1992 in a renovated 18th-century palace, completing Madrid's Golden Triangle. The collection spans seven centuries — from early Italian Renaissance through Impressionism to Pop Art — filling gaps left by the Prado and Reina Sofía.
📅 Opened 1992👥 ~1M visitors/year
Official Site Madrid Guide
United Kingdom
🏛️
British Museum
London · Ancient · Classical Antiquities
The world's first public national museum, established 1753. Holds roughly 8 million objects including the Rosetta Stone and the Parthenon Marbles. Spans human history from ancient Egypt and Mesopotamia through to the present. Free admission.
📅 Founded 1753👥 6.4M visitors (2025)
Official Site London Guide
🖼️
National Gallery
London · Renaissance · Old Masters
Founded 1824, occupying a grand Trafalgar Square building since 1838. Over 2,300 Western European paintings from the mid-13th century to 1900, including works by Van Eyck, Leonardo, Turner, and Constable. Free admission.
📅 Founded 1824👥 2M+ visitors/year
Official Site London Guide
🔲
Tate Modern
London · Modern · Contemporary
Opened 2000 inside a converted power station on the Thames, redesigned by Herzog & de Meuron. Houses the Tate's international modern and contemporary art collection in one of London's most striking architectural conversions. Free admission.
📅 Opened 2000👥 ~4.7M visitors/year
Official Site London Guide
Netherlands
🖼️
Rijksmuseum
Amsterdam · Dutch Golden Age
Home to Rembrandt's "The Night Watch" and the world's largest collection of Dutch Golden Age art. The grand Pierre Cuypers building (1885) was fully renovated 2003–2013 by Cruz y Ortiz architects.
📅 Founded 1798👥 2.3–2.7M visitors/year
Official Site Amsterdam Guide
🌻
Van Gogh Museum
Amsterdam · Post-Impressionism
The world's largest Van Gogh collection — about 200 paintings, 400 drawings, and 700 letters. Housed in a Gerrit Rietveld building (1973) with a later wing by Kisho Kurokawa. Traces the artist's life from early Dutch work through his final years in France.
📅 Opened 1973👥 1.8–2.3M visitors/year
Official Site Amsterdam Guide
Austria
👑
Kunsthistorisches Museum
Vienna · Old Masters · Habsburg Collections
Built 1871–1891 to house the Habsburg imperial art collections. Works by Titian, Vermeer, Velázquez, Rubens, and the world's largest Bruegel collection. The building itself — designed by Semper and Hasenauer — is a masterpiece of historicist architecture.
📅 Opened 1891👥 1M+ visitors/year
Official Site Vienna Guide
Russia
🏛️
Hermitage Museum
St. Petersburg · Encyclopedic · European Old Masters
Founded 1764 by Catherine the Great. Spans six historic buildings including the Winter Palace and holds over 3 million items — one of the world's largest painting collections, with masterworks from Leonardo, Rembrandt, Matisse, and Picasso.
📅 Founded 1764👥 4M+ visitors/year
Official Site
United States
🏛️
The Metropolitan Museum of Art
New York City · Encyclopedic · All eras
The most-visited museum in the United States. Permanent collection of roughly 1.5 million works spanning ancient Egypt through contemporary art. The main Fifth Avenue building (opened 1880) and The Met Cloisters together cover 5,000 years of art.
📅 Founded 1870👥 5.7M visitors (2025)
Official Site New York Guide
🔲
Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)
New York City · Modern · Contemporary
Founded 1929 by Abby Aldrich Rockefeller and two co-founders. Collection of over 200,000 works spanning the late 19th century to the present, including Van Gogh's "The Starry Night" and Picasso's "Les Demoiselles d'Avignon."
📅 Founded 1929👥 2.8M visitors (2023)
Official Site New York Guide
🌀
Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum
New York City · Modern · Abstract
Frank Lloyd Wright's iconic spiral rotunda (opened 1959) is as celebrated as the art it houses. The collection focuses on modern and contemporary art, with particular strength in early-20th-century abstraction, including a major Kandinsky collection.
📅 Opened 1959👥 ~1.2M visitors/year
Official Site New York Guide
🎨
Art Institute of Chicago
Chicago · Impressionism · American Art
Founded 1879, one of the oldest large art museums in the United States. Nearly 300,000 works including Grant Wood's "American Gothic" and Seurat's "A Sunday on La Grande Jatte." Renowned for its Impressionist collection.
📅 Founded 1879👥 ~1.5M visitors/year
Official Site Chicago Guide
🏛️
National Gallery of Art
Washington, D.C. · Encyclopedic · Western Art
Established 1937 with Andrew Mellon's founding donation, housed in a neoclassical West Building and I.M. Pei's modernist East Building. Traces Western art from the Middle Ages to the present. Free admission — one of the few major art museums worldwide with no entrance fee.
📅 Opened 1941👥 ~4M visitors (2024)
Official Site Washington DC Guide
🏔️
Getty Center
Los Angeles · European Old Masters · Photography
Richard Meier's hilltop campus (opened 1997) overlooks Los Angeles from the Santa Monica Mountains. Pre-20th-century European paintings, drawings, illuminated manuscripts, and one of the world's leading photography collections. Free admission.
📅 Opened 1997👥 ~1.8M visitors/year
Official Site Los Angeles Guide
🏛️
Philadelphia Museum of Art
Philadelphia · Encyclopedic · European & American Art
Greek Revival building on Fairmount (completed 1928), famous for the "Rocky Steps." Over 240,000 objects spanning 4,000 years, with strengths in European, American, and Asian art, including outstanding Impressionist and Post-Impressionist holdings.
📅 Chartered 1876👥 ~800K visitors/year
Official Site Philadelphia Guide
Canada
🍁
National Gallery of Canada
Ottawa · Canadian Art · European Old Masters
Founded 1880, housed since 1988 in a striking granite-and-glass building by Moshe Safdie. Over 87,000 works with a strong emphasis on Canadian art — the largest such collection — alongside European masterworks from the 14th century onward.
📅 Founded 1880
Official Site
Asia
🏯
Tokyo National Museum
Tokyo, Japan · Japanese & Asian Art
Japan's oldest and largest museum, established 1872. Over 120,000 objects spanning Japanese art, archaeology, and Asian antiquities, including 89 designated National Treasures. Located in Ueno Park.
📅 Founded 1872
Official Site Tokyo Guide
🏛️
National Palace Museum
Taipei, Taiwan · Chinese Imperial Art
Houses nearly 700,000 pieces of ancient Chinese imperial art and artifacts spanning 8,000 years — one of the largest collections of Chinese art in the world. Highlights include the famed Jadeite Cabbage and Meat-Shaped Stone.
📅 Founded 1925
Official Site Taipei Guide
🏛️
National Museum of China
Beijing, China · Chinese History & Art
Located on Tiananmen Square, one of the largest museums in the world by floor area. Over 1 million objects covering Chinese art and history from ancient times to the modern era. Among the most-visited museums globally.
📅 Founded 2003 (merged)
Official Site Beijing Guide
🎨
National Museum of Korea
Seoul, South Korea · Korean Art & History
The flagship museum of Korean history and art, with over 420,000 objects spanning from the Paleolithic era to modern times. One of the most-visited museums in the world by attendance, with free admission to permanent collections.
📅 Founded 1945
Official Site Seoul Guide
Middle East
🕌
Louvre Abu Dhabi
Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates · Encyclopedic
Jean Nouvel's stunning domed museum on Saadiyat Island, opened 2017 as part of a 30-year agreement with France. A "universal museum" presenting art from ancient civilizations to the contemporary era, organized thematically rather than by origin.
📅 Opened 2017
Official Site Abu Dhabi Guide
🏛️
Museum of Islamic Art
Doha, Qatar · Islamic Art
I.M. Pei's geometric masterpiece sits on a purpose-built island in Doha Bay. Spans 1,400 years of Islamic art from three continents — ceramics, metalwork, jewelry, textiles, and manuscripts from Spain to Central Asia.
📅 Opened 2008
Official Site
📜
Israel Museum
Jerusalem, Israel · Encyclopedic · Archaeology
Israel's largest cultural institution, holding nearly 500,000 objects. Houses the Dead Sea Scrolls in the iconic Shrine of the Book and an extensive archaeology wing spanning the Holy Land's ancient history alongside fine arts and Judaica.
📅 Founded 1965
Official Site
South America
🎨
MASP — São Paulo Museum of Art
São Paulo, Brazil · European & Brazilian Art
Latin America's most important art museum, housed in Lina Bo Bardi's brutalist icon (1968) — a glass box suspended above a public plaza on Avenida Paulista. The collection of over 11,000 works includes the Southern Hemisphere's finest European Old Masters alongside Brazilian art.
📅 Founded 1947
Official Site
Oceania
🦘
National Gallery of Australia
Canberra, Australia · Australian & International Art
Australia's national art museum, holding over 155,000 works. The largest collection of Australian art — including a major Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander art collection — alongside international works from Pollock, Monet, and Warhol. Free admission.
📅 Opened 1982
Official Site
