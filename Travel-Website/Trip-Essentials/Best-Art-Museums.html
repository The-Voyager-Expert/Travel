Best Art Museums

Updated July 2026
Argentina
Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes
Buenos Aires · Argentine & European Art
Argentina's national fine arts museum, holding the country's most important public art collection, spanning Argentine artists and European masters from the Renaissance to the modern era.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Buenos Aires Guide Map
Australia
National Gallery of Australia
Canberra · Australian & International Art
Australia's national art museum, holding the country's largest art collection. It is especially strong in Australian and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander art, alongside international works.
Official Site Wikipedia Map
Austria
Kunsthistorisches Museum
Vienna · Old Masters · Habsburg Collections
Built to house the imperial Habsburg collections, it holds major Old Master paintings, including the world's largest group of works by Pieter Bruegel the Elder.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Vienna Guide Map
Belvedere
Vienna · Austrian Art · Klimt's The Kiss
Set in a Baroque palace complex, it holds the largest collection of Gustav Klimt paintings, centered on The Kiss, alongside Austrian art from the medieval era onward.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Vienna Guide Map
Albertina
Vienna · Graphic Arts · Monet to Picasso
Housed in a former Habsburg residence, it holds one of the world's largest and most important collections of prints and drawings, including works by Dürer alongside modern painting.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Vienna Guide Map
Brazil
MASP — São Paulo Museum of Art
São Paulo · European & Brazilian Art
Since 1947
A landmark of Brazilian modernism by Lina Bo Bardi, its main volume raised on red pillars over an open plaza. It holds the most significant collection of European art in the Southern Hemisphere.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 São Paulo Guide Map
Canada
National Gallery of Canada
Ottawa · Canadian Art · European Old Masters
Canada's national art museum, housed in a glass-and-granite building by Moshe Safdie. It holds the country's largest collection of Canadian art, including works by the Group of Seven.
Official Site Wikipedia Map
China
National Museum of China
Beijing · Chinese History & Art
Founded 1912/19591.4M+ Items
One of the world's largest museums by floor area, on the east side of Tiananmen Square. Its permanent galleries trace Chinese history and civilization from prehistory to the present.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Beijing Guide Map
France
Louvre Museum
Paris · Encyclopedic · All eras
World's Most-Visited MuseumFormer Royal Palace
The world's most visited museum, housed in a former royal palace on the Seine. Its collection spans antiquity to the mid-19th century, anchored by the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Musée d'Orsay
Paris · Impressionism · Post-Impressionism
Former Railway StationWorld's Largest Impressionist Collection
Set in a former Beaux-Arts railway station, it holds the world's largest collection of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist masterpieces, including works by Monet, Renoir, Degas, and Van Gogh.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Centre Pompidou
Paris · Modern · Contemporary
Opened 1977120,000+ Works
Known for its radical inside-out high-tech architecture, it houses Europe's largest collection of modern and contemporary art, spanning the 20th century to today.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Musée de l'Orangerie
Paris · Impressionism · Monet's Water Lilies
Monet's Water Lilies Murals
A former royal greenhouse in the Tuileries, best known for two oval rooms displaying Monet's monumental Water Lilies murals, alongside an Impressionist and Post-Impressionist collection.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Musée Rodin
Paris · Sculpture · Auguste Rodin
18th-Century Hôtel Biron
Dedicated to the sculptor Auguste Rodin, set in the 18th-century Hôtel Biron and its gardens. Highlights include The Thinker, The Kiss, and The Gates of Hell.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Paris Guide Map
Germany
Pergamon Museum
Berlin · Ancient Near Eastern · Classical Antiquities
Opened 1930Museum Island
Part of Berlin's Museum Island, it is renowned for monumental reconstructed structures, including the Pergamon Altar and the Ishtar Gate of ancient Babylon.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Berlin Guide Map
Alte Nationalgalerie
Berlin · 19th Century · Impressionism & Romanticism
UNESCO World Heritage Site
A temple-like building on Museum Island housing 19th-century art, from German Romanticism and the works of Caspar David Friedrich to French and German Impressionism.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Berlin Guide Map
Israel
Israel Museum
Jerusalem · Encyclopedic · Archaeology
Founded 1965Dead Sea Scrolls
Israel's largest cultural institution, spanning archaeology and fine art. Its Shrine of the Book displays the Dead Sea Scrolls, and the grounds include a model of Second Temple-era Jerusalem.
Official Site Wikipedia Map
Italy
Uffizi Gallery
Florence · Renaissance
Housed in a 16th-century Medici administrative building, it holds one of the finest collections of Italian Renaissance painting, including Botticelli's Birth of Venus and Primavera.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Florence Guide Map
Vatican Museums
Vatican City · Renaissance · Classical Antiquities
A vast complex displaying art amassed by the popes over centuries. The route culminates in the Raphael Rooms and Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel ceiling.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Rome Guide Map
Galleria dell'Accademia
Florence · Renaissance Sculpture
Since 1873
Best known as the home of Michelangelo's original David, moved here in 1873. It also holds his unfinished Prisoners and a collection of Florentine paintings.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Florence Guide Map
Galleria Borghese
Rome · Baroque · Bernini & Caravaggio
Timed Entry
A former cardinal's villa set in landscaped gardens, renowned for its Bernini sculptures and Caravaggio paintings. Timed entry limits visitor numbers to preserve the intimate galleries.
Wikipedia 🌐 Rome Guide Map
Pinacoteca di Brera
Milan · Renaissance · Italian Old Masters
Milan's main public gallery, occupying a grand palazzo shared with an art academy. It is celebrated for northern Italian painting, including works by Raphael, Mantegna, and Piero della Francesca.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Milan Guide Map
Japan
Tokyo National Museum
Tokyo · Japanese & Asian Art
Founded 1872Oldest in Japan
Japan's oldest and largest museum, set in Ueno Park. It holds the country's most comprehensive collection of Japanese art and antiquities, including numerous designated National Treasures.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Tokyo Guide Map
National Museum of Western Art
Tokyo · Western Art · UNESCO World Heritage
UNESCO World Heritage Site
Japan's principal museum of Western art, built around the Matsukata collection. Its Le Corbusier-designed main building is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Tokyo Guide Map
Netherlands
Rijksmuseum
Amsterdam · Dutch Golden Age
The Netherlands' national museum, devoted to Dutch art and history. Its Gallery of Honour centers on Rembrandt's The Night Watch and paintings by Vermeer and Frans Hals.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Amsterdam Guide Map
Van Gogh Museum
Amsterdam · Post-Impressionism
Largest Van Gogh Collection
Holds the world's largest collection of works by Vincent van Gogh, including paintings, drawings, and letters, tracing his career alongside works by his contemporaries.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Amsterdam Guide Map
Qatar
Museum of Islamic Art
Doha · Islamic Art
Designed by I. M. Pei
Designed by I. M. Pei on its own island in Doha Bay, it holds one of the most comprehensive collections of Islamic art, spanning manuscripts, ceramics, metalwork, and textiles across continents.
Official Site Wikipedia Map
Russia
Hermitage Museum
St. Petersburg · Encyclopedic · European Old Masters
One of World's Largest Museums
One of the world's largest museums, spread across the former Winter Palace and adjoining buildings. Its holdings span antiquity to modern art, including works by Rembrandt and da Vinci.
Official Site Wikipedia Map
South Korea
National Museum of Korea
Seoul · Korean Art & History
Pensive Bodhisattva
The flagship museum of Korean history and art, and one of the largest in Asia. Its collection ranges from prehistoric artifacts to Buddhist sculpture, celadon ceramics, and calligraphy.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Seoul Guide Map
Spain
Prado Museum
Madrid · Spanish Old Masters · Baroque
Spain's National Art Museum
Spain's national art museum, holding the finest collection of Spanish painting anywhere. It is anchored by masterworks from Velázquez, Goya, and El Greco.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Madrid Guide Map
Reina Sofía Museum
Madrid · Modern · Contemporary
Home of Picasso's GuernicaFormer Hospital
Spain's national museum of 20th-century art, set in a former hospital. Its centerpiece is Picasso's Guernica, alongside significant holdings of Dalí and Miró.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Madrid Guide Map
Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum
Madrid · Encyclopedic · Renaissance to 20th Century
Private Collection
Built around a private collection assembled by the Thyssen-Bornemisza family, it traces Western painting from the medieval period through the 20th century, filling gaps left by the nearby Prado.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Madrid Guide Map
Picasso Museum
Barcelona · Pablo Picasso · Early Works
Five Medieval Mansions
Spread across five medieval mansions, it holds one of the most extensive collections of Picasso's work, with a particular focus on his formative years and Barcelona period.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 Barcelona Guide Map

Taipei · Chinese Imperial Art
Jadeite CabbageMeat-shaped Stone

Louvre Abu Dhabi
Abu Dhabi · Encyclopedic
An encyclopedic museum created through an agreement with France, under a vast latticed dome by Jean Nouvel that filters light as a "rain of light" over galleries linking world cultures.

United Kingdom
British Museum
London · Ancient · Classical Antiquities
Founded 17538M+ Objects
A vast survey of human history and culture, holding some eight million objects. Highlights include the Rosetta Stone, the Parthenon (Elgin) Marbles, and Egyptian mummies.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 London Guide Map
National Gallery
London · Renaissance · Old Masters
13th–20th Century Collection
Overlooking Trafalgar Square, it holds a comprehensive collection of Western European painting from the 13th to the early 20th century, with works by Van Eyck, Turner, and Van Gogh.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 London Guide Map
Tate Modern
London · Modern · Contemporary
Former Power Station
Britain's national museum of modern and contemporary art, converted from the former Bankside Power Station on the Thames. The vast Turbine Hall hosts large-scale commissioned installations.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 London Guide Map
United States
The Metropolitan Museum of Art
New York City · Encyclopedic · All eras
Largest in the Americas
The largest art museum in the Americas, lining Central Park's Museum Mile. Its encyclopedic collection ranges from Egyptian antiquities and European paintings to American and modern art.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 New York Guide Map
Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)
New York City · Modern · Contemporary
A defining institution of modern art in Midtown Manhattan, holding landmark works such as Van Gogh's The Starry Night and Picasso's Les Demoiselles d'Avignon.
Official Site Wikipedia 🌐 New York Guide Map
Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum
New York City · Modern · Abstract
Opened 1959Frank Lloyd Wright
Famous for its spiraling Frank Lloyd Wright rotunda, it holds a collection of modern and contemporary art, with strengths in Impressionism, Post-Impressionism, and abstract work.

Art Institute of Chicago
Chicago · Impressionism · American Art
One of the Largest Art Museums in the USHome of American Gothic
One of the largest US art museums, known for its French Impressionist holdings and iconic American works, including Grant Wood's American Gothic and Hopper's Nighthawks.

National Gallery of Art
Washington, D.C. · Encyclopedic · Western Art
Free EntryOnly da Vinci Painting in the Americas
On the National Mall, it surveys Western art from the Middle Ages to the present and holds the only Leonardo da Vinci painting in the Americas. Admission is free.

Getty Center
Los Angeles · European Old Masters · Photography
Free Entry
A hilltop campus of Richard Meier travertine pavilions and gardens, holding European paintings, drawings, sculpture, and photography. Admission is free.

Philadelphia Museum of Art
Philadelphia · Encyclopedic · European & American Art
Featured in Rocky
Set in a Greek Revival building whose entrance steps were made famous by the film Rocky. Its collection spans European, American, and Asian art across two millennia.

Museum of Fine Arts
Boston · Encyclopedic · Impressionism & Asian Art
One of the Largest Museums in the US
One of the largest museums in the United States, with encyclopedic holdings notable for French Impressionism, ancient Egyptian works, and one of the finest Asian art collections in the West.

Whitney Museum of American Art
New York City · American Art · Contemporary
Home of the Whitney BiennialRenzo Piano Building
Focused on 20th- and 21st-century American art, now housed in a Renzo Piano building in the Meatpacking District. It is the home of the recurring Whitney Biennial.

Los Angeles · Encyclopedic · All eras
Largest Art Museum in the Western US
The largest art museum in the western United States, with encyclopedic holdings spanning global cultures and eras. Chris Burden's Urban Light installation stands at its entrance.
