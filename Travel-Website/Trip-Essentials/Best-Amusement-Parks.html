Best Amusement Parks

United States
🏰
Magic Kingdom
Walt Disney World, Orlando, Florida
The most visited theme park on Earth for 19 consecutive years. Six themed lands radiate from Cinderella Castle — Adventureland, Frontierland, Liberty Square, Fantasyland, Tomorrowland, and Main Street U.S.A. Classic rides include Space Mountain, Pirates of the Caribbean, and the Haunted Mansion.
🎟️ 17.8M visitors/year🎢 Over 40 attractions📅 Year-round

🌋
Universal Islands of Adventure
Universal Orlando, Florida
Consistently rated the top thrill park in the US. Eight themed islands include the Wizarding World of Harry Potter — Hogsmeade, Jurassic World, and Marvel Super Hero Island. Home to VelociCoaster, one of the world's highest-rated roller coasters, and the Incredible Hulk Coaster.
🎟️ 11.0M visitors/year🎢 Over 30 attractions📅 Year-round
Official Site Orlando Guide
🌌
Universal Epic Universe
Universal Orlando, Florida
Universal's newest and largest park, opened May 2025. Five immersive worlds: Super Nintendo World, How to Train Your Dragon — Isle of Berk, Dark Universe, The Wizarding World of Harry Potter — Ministry of Magic, and the Celestial Park hub. The most ambitious theme park opening in decades.
🎢 Over 50 experiences📅 Year-round🆕 Opened 2025

🏰
Disneyland Park
Anaheim, California
Walt Disney's original park, opened in 1955 — the one that started it all. Nine themed lands including Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge and the recently expanded Avengers Campus next door. The Matterhorn Bobsleds, Indiana Jones Adventure, and the original Pirates of the Caribbean ride.
🎟️ 17.3M visitors/year🎢 Over 50 attractions📅 Year-round

🎢
Cedar Point
Sandusky, Ohio
Known as "America's Roller Coast." Tied for the most roller coasters in North America with 18, including five exceeding 200 feet. Steel Vengeance, Millennium Force, and Top Thrill 2 anchor the lineup. Won the Golden Ticket Award for Best Amusement Park for 16 consecutive years. Set on a Lake Erie peninsula since 1870.
🎟️ 3.6M visitors/year🎢 18 roller coasters📅 May–Oct

🦸
Six Flags Magic Mountain
Valencia, California
Tied for the most roller coasters in North America. Known for extreme thrill rides including X2 (a 4D coaster), Twisted Colossus (hybrid), and Full Throttle (world's tallest vertical loop at opening). A bucket-list park for coaster enthusiasts.
🎢 18 roller coasters📅 Year-round (weekends in winter)

Japan
🌊
Tokyo DisneySea
Urayasu, Chiba (Tokyo)
Widely considered the most beautifully designed theme park ever built. Eight themed ports of call explore maritime myths — from the erupting volcano of Mysterious Island to the canals of Mediterranean Harbor. Fantasy Springs (2024) added Frozen, Tangled, and Peter Pan areas in a landmark expansion.
🎟️ 14.8M visitors/year🎢 35 attractions📅 Year-round
Official Site Tokyo Guide
🎬
Universal Studios Japan
Osaka
The most visited theme park in Asia, drawing 16 million guests annually. Home to the original Super Nintendo World (opened 2021) where visitors wear Power-Up Bands to interact with the environment. Also features the Wizarding World of Harry Potter, Jurassic World, and Japan-exclusive attractions.
🎟️ 16.0M visitors/year🎢 Over 30 attractions📅 Year-round
Official Site Osaka Guide

Shanghai Disneyland
Shanghai
Disney's newest castle park, featuring the tallest and most interactive Disney castle in the world — the Enchanted Storybook Castle. TRON Lightcycle Power Run and Pirates of the Caribbean: Battle for the Sunken Treasure are considered among Disney's best-ever attractions.
🎟️ 11.5M visitors/year🎢 Over 40 attractions📅 Year-round

Universal Beijing Resort
Beijing
The largest Universal Studios park in the world. Seven themed lands include a Kung Fu Panda Land and a Transformers experience unique to Beijing. WaterWorld and the Jurassic World attractions are among the most technologically advanced in the Universal portfolio.
🎟️ 12.0M visitors/year🎢 Over 35 attractions📅 Year-round

South Korea
🐻
Everland
Yongin, Gyeonggi (near Seoul)
South Korea's largest theme park, spanning 99 hectares with five themed zones. Home to T Express, Korea's first wooden roller coaster, and Zootopia, housing over 2,000 animals. Lost Valley offers an open-air safari experience with amphibious vehicles through African and Asian wildlife habitats.
🎟️ 6.6M visitors/year🎢 Over 40 attractions📅 Year-round
Official Site Seoul Guide
🏙️
Lotte World
Seoul
The world's largest indoor theme park. Divided into the indoor Adventure section (under a massive glass dome) and the outdoor Magic Island on a lake. Part of a complex including the Lotte World Tower with its Seoul Sky Observatory at 555 metres. Open regardless of weather or season.
🎟️ 5.8M visitors/year🎢 Over 40 attractions📅 Year-round
Official Site Seoul Guide
France
🏰
Disneyland Paris
Marne-la-Vallée (Paris)
Europe's most visited theme park with over 10 million annual visitors. Two parks — Disneyland Park and Walt Disney Studios Park — sit 35 minutes east of Paris by train. Sleeping Beauty Castle anchors a park that feels distinctly European. Avengers Campus and a Frozen-themed area are among recent additions.
🎟️ 10.4M visitors/year🎢 Over 50 attractions📅 Year-round
Official Site Paris Guide
⏱️
Puy du Fou
Les Epesses, Vendée
A live-action historical spectacle park with no traditional rides. Massive shows recreate Roman gladiator battles, Viking raids, and medieval jousting with hundreds of actors, live animals, and cinematic special effects. The evening Cinéscénie show uses 2,400 volunteer actors on a 23-hectare stage — the largest night show in the world.
🎟️ 2.3M visitors/year🎭 Over 30 live shows📅 Apr–Nov
Official Site
Germany
🇪🇺
Europa-Park
Rust, Baden-Württemberg
Named "Best Theme Park Worldwide" by the Golden Ticket Awards ten times — more than any other park. Twenty themed areas represent European countries, with 100+ attractions including 14 roller coasters. Also features six on-site hotels, a water park (Rulantica), and Eatrenalin, a Michelin-starred dining experience.
🎟️ 6.0M visitors/year🎢 14 roller coasters📅 Apr–Jan (seasonal)

Netherlands
🧚
Efteling
Kaatsheuvel, North Brabant
A fairy-tale theme park open since 1952, drawing on European myths, legends, and folklore. The Fairytale Forest (Sprookjesbos) brings 30 classic stories to life across 15 acres. Six roller coasters, five dark rides, and Aquanura — Europe's largest water show. Open every day of the year. Over 5 million annual visitors.
🎟️ 5.4M visitors/year🎢 6 roller coasters📅 Year-round
Official Site
Denmark
🎡
Tivoli Gardens
Copenhagen
The world's second-oldest amusement park, opened in 1843 in the heart of Copenhagen. Famously inspired Walt Disney before he built Disneyland. Lantern-lit gardens, wooden roller coasters, a 1914 carousel, and seasonal festivals — Halloween and Christmas are spectacular. A Michelin-starred restaurant sits inside the park.
🎟️ 4.0M visitors/year🎢 30+ rides📅 Apr–Dec (seasonal)
Official Site Copenhagen Guide
Spain
🎢
PortAventura World
Salou, Tarragona (near Barcelona)
Southern Europe's premier theme park destination on the Costa Dorada. Three parks in one resort — PortAventura Park, Ferrari Land, and Caribe Aquatic Park. Home to Shambhala, one of Europe's tallest hypercoasters (76 m), and Dragon Khan's eight inversions. Warm Mediterranean climate extends the season.
🎟️ 3.8M visitors/year🎢 Over 40 attractions📅 Mar–Jan (seasonal)
Official Site Barcelona Guide
Sweden
🎠
Liseberg
Gothenburg
Scandinavia's largest amusement park, located in the centre of Gothenburg. Known for Helix, one of Europe's best steel coasters, and Valkyria, a dive coaster plunging 50 metres. The park transforms for Halloween (one of Europe's best) and Christmas, with millions of lights and a traditional Swedish market.
🎟️ 3.0M visitors/year🎢 8 roller coasters📅 Apr–Dec (seasonal)

United Arab Emirates
🏎️
Ferrari World Abu Dhabi
Yas Island, Abu Dhabi
The world's first Ferrari-branded theme park, mostly indoors under a massive 200,000 sq m roof. Home to Formula Rossa, which held the record as the world's fastest roller coaster (240 km/h in under 5 seconds) from 2010 until 2024. Over 40 attractions including racing simulators and Italian-themed experiences. Open 365 days a year in full climate control.
🎢 Over 40 attractions🏎️ 240 km/h top speed📅 Year-round
Official Site Abu Dhabi Guide

Universal Studios Singapore
Sentosa Island
Southeast Asia's only Universal Studios park, set within the Resorts World Sentosa complex. Seven themed zones include a Battlestar Galactica duelling coaster and Transformers: The Ride 3D. Compact enough to experience in a single day. A major expansion is underway with a Minions Land addition.
🎟️ 4.5M visitors/year🎢 Over 20 attractions📅 Year-round

United Kingdom
🎢
Alton Towers
Staffordshire, England
The UK's most visited theme park, set in the grounds of a ruined Gothic mansion. Home to The Smiler (world-record 14 inversions), Nemesis Reborn (reimagined inverted coaster), and Wicker Man (UK's first wooden coaster in 20 years). The Gothic Towers estate and surrounding gardens add atmosphere unlike any other park.
🎟️ 2.1M visitors/year🎢 10 roller coasters📅 Mar–Nov

Australia
🎬
Warner Bros. Movie World
Gold Coast, Queensland
Australia's premier movie-themed park on the Gold Coast. DC Rivals HyperCoaster is the tallest, fastest, and longest hypercoaster in the Southern Hemisphere. Themed areas cover DC Comics, Looney Tunes, and classic Warner Bros. films. Part of a cluster with Wet'n'Wild and Sea World nearby.
🎢 6 roller coasters📅 Year-round

Mexico
🦅
Xcaret Park
Riviera Maya, Quintana Roo
A unique eco-archaeological park on the Caribbean coast near Playa del Carmen. Underground rivers for snorkeling, a coral reef aquarium, a butterfly pavilion, and a jaguar island. The evening México Espectacular show celebrates Mexican culture through music, dance, and a pre-Hispanic ball game. More nature reserve than theme park.
🎟️ 2.0M+ visitors/year🌿 Eco-park + cultural shows📅 Year-round

Saudi Arabia
🦅
Six Flags Qiddiya
Qiddiya, near Riyadh
Home to Falcon's Flight, the world's fastest and longest roller coaster — reaching 250 km/h and spanning over 4 km of track along a cliff edge. Part of the massive Qiddiya entertainment megaproject. The most ambitious new theme park development in the world, set to redefine the Middle East's amusement landscape.
🎢 250 km/h top speed🆕 Opened 2025
