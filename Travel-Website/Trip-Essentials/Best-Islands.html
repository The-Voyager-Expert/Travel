Caribbean
Barbados
Barbados
The easternmost Caribbean island, with a west coast of calm turquoise water and an east coast of Atlantic surf. Bridgetown's colonial architecture is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The island's rum distilleries date back to the 1600s, making it one of the oldest rum-producing regions in the world.

St. Lucia
Saint Lucia
A volcanic island defined by the twin Piton peaks (739 m and 743 m), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The island has a drive-in volcano at Soufrière, the only one of its kind in the Caribbean. Rainforest covers much of the interior, with volcanic hot springs along the southwestern coast.
☀️ Dec–Apr📐 617 km²🌋 2 Pitons (UNESCO)

Curaçao
Curaçao
A Dutch Caribbean island with rows of pastel-colored colonial buildings along the Handelskade waterfront in Willemstad, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The island sits outside the hurricane belt. Over 35 sheltered cove beaches line the leeward coast, many accessible only by foot.
☀️ Year-round📐 444 km²🏛️ Willemstad (UNESCO)
Tourism Board View Guide
Puerto Rico
United States
A US territory with no passport required for American travelers. Old San Juan's 16th-century fortifications (El Morro, San Cristóbal) are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. El Yunque is the only tropical rainforest in the US National Forest System. Mosquito Bay in Vieques is the brightest bioluminescent bay in the world.
☀️ Dec–Apr📐 9,104 km²🏛️ 500+ years of history

Aruba
Aruba
A small, flat island off the Venezuelan coast, outside the hurricane belt with consistent trade winds and an average of 300+ sunny days per year. The interior is arid with cacti and the Arikok National Park covers nearly 20% of the island. Eagle Beach is consistently ranked among the top beaches in the world.
☀️ Year-round📐 180 km²☀️ 300+ sunny days/year
Tourism Board View Guide
Antigua
Antigua & Barbuda
Known for having 365 beaches — one for every day of the year. Nelson's Dockyard in English Harbour is a restored 18th-century British naval facility and UNESCO World Heritage Site, the only continuously working Georgian dockyard in the world. Antigua Sailing Week is one of the Caribbean's premier regattas.
☀️ Dec–Apr📐 281 km²🏖️ 365 beaches

Mediterranean
Santorini
Greece
A crescent-shaped volcanic island formed by one of the largest eruptions in recorded history (~1600 BCE). The caldera cliffs rise 300 m from the sea, lined with white-washed villages. Oia and Fira sit on the rim with direct views into the submerged volcanic crater. Red, white, and black sand beaches reflect the island's volcanic geology.
☀️ Apr–Oct📐 76 km²🌋 Caldera rim villages

Capri
Italy
A limestone island in the Bay of Naples with sea cliffs rising up to 589 m at Monte Solaro. The Blue Grotto (Grotta Azzurra) is a 60-meter-long sea cave where sunlight enters through an underwater opening, turning the water electric blue. The island has been a retreat since Roman Emperor Tiberius built 12 villas here in 27 CE.
☀️ May–Oct📐 10 km²🔵 Blue Grotto
Capri Tourism View Guide
Crete
Greece
The largest Greek island and birthplace of the Minoan civilization, Europe's first advanced society (~2700 BCE). The Palace of Knossos is the most important Bronze Age archaeological site in the eastern Mediterranean. The Samariá Gorge runs 16 km through the White Mountains, one of the longest gorges in Europe.
☀️ Apr–Oct📐 8,450 km²🏛️ Minoan civilization origin
Visit Crete
Sicily
Italy
The largest island in the Mediterranean at 25,711 km², with Mount Etna (3,357 m) — Europe's tallest and most active volcano — on its eastern coast. The Valley of the Temples near Agrigento holds some of the best-preserved Greek temples outside of Greece. Sicilian cuisine is distinct from mainland Italian food, shaped by Greek, Arab, and Norman influences.
☀️ Apr–Oct📐 25,711 km²🌋 Mt. Etna (3,357 m)
Visit Sicily View Guide
Mallorca
Spain
The largest of Spain's Balearic Islands, with the Serra de Tramuntana mountain range (UNESCO World Heritage cultural landscape) running along the northwest coast. Palma's Gothic cathedral (La Seu) sits directly on the waterfront. The island has over 200 beaches and coves, from long sandy stretches to narrow calas carved into limestone cliffs.
☀️ May–Oct📐 3,640 km²⛰️ Serra de Tramuntana (UNESCO)

Corsica
France
A mountainous French island in the western Mediterranean with peaks reaching 2,706 m (Monte Cinto). The GR20 trail is considered one of the most challenging long-distance hiking routes in Europe, traversing 180 km of rugged terrain. The island's interior is covered in dense maquis shrubland, and Corsican is still widely spoken alongside French.
☀️ May–Sep📐 8,680 km²🥾 GR20 trail (180 km)

Southeast Asia
Bali
Indonesia
A Hindu-majority island in predominantly Muslim Indonesia, with over 20,000 temples. The interior is defined by terraced rice paddies (the Jatiluwih system is UNESCO-listed) and volcanic peaks — Mount Agung reaches 3,031 m. Ubud is the cultural center, with traditional dance performances held nightly. The island's southern coast has consistent surf breaks.
☀️ Apr–Oct📐 5,780 km²🛕 20,000+ temples
Indonesia Tourism View Guide
Phuket
Thailand
Thailand's largest island, connected to the mainland by two bridges. The west coast has a series of sand beaches (Patong, Kata, Karon), while the east coast is quieter with mangroves and fishing villages. Phuket Old Town retains Sino-Portuguese architecture from the tin-mining era. The island serves as a hub for day trips to Phang Nga Bay's limestone karsts.
☀️ Nov–Apr📐 576 km²🌊 Phang Nga Bay access
Thailand Tourism View Guide
Palawan
Philippines
A narrow, 450-km-long island in the western Philippines. El Nido and Coron are the primary destinations, with limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, and coral reefs. The Puerto Princesa Underground River is a UNESCO World Heritage Site — an 8.2-km navigable subterranean river, one of the longest in the world. The Tubbataha Reef is a UNESCO-listed marine sanctuary.
☀️ Nov–May📐 14,649 km²🏛️ 2 UNESCO sites

Komodo Island
Indonesia
Home to the Komodo dragon, the world's largest living lizard (up to 3 m long, ~70 kg). The island is part of Komodo National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The surrounding waters are in the Coral Triangle, the most biodiverse marine region on Earth. Padar Island nearby has a tri-colored beach viewpoint — pink, white, and black sand.
☀️ Apr–Nov📐 390 km²🦎 ~3,000 Komodo dragons
Indonesia Tourism
Langkawi
Malaysia
An archipelago of 99 islands off Malaysia's northwest coast, with Langkawi as the main island. It holds UNESCO Global Geopark status for its 550-million-year-old geological formations. The island is duty-free. The Langkawi Sky Bridge, suspended at 660 m altitude, offers aerial views of the Andaman Sea and the rainforest canopy.
☀️ Nov–Mar📐 478 km²🌍 UNESCO Geopark

Indian Ocean
Maldives
Maldives
A chain of 1,192 coral islands grouped into 26 atolls, with an average ground-level elevation of just 1.5 m — the lowest-lying country on Earth. The islands sit on a submarine ridge in the Indian Ocean. Over 80% of the land area is less than 1 m above sea level. The house reef system makes shore-accessible snorkeling possible at most resorts.
☀️ Nov–Apr📐 298 km² (land)🏝️ 1,192 islands
Tourism Board View Guide
Zanzibar
Tanzania
A semi-autonomous archipelago off the Tanzanian coast. Stone Town's labyrinthine alleyways, carved doorways, and mix of Arab, Indian, and Swahili architecture earned it UNESCO World Heritage status. The island was historically the center of the East African spice trade — cloves, nutmeg, cinnamon, and black pepper are still cultivated. The east coast has white sand beaches with tidal flats extending hundreds of meters.
☀️ Jun–Oct📐 1,651 km²🏛️ Stone Town (UNESCO)

Seychelles
Seychelles
An archipelago of 115 islands split between granite inner islands and coral outer islands. The granite islands are the only mid-oceanic granite islands in the world. The Vallée de Mai on Praslin is a UNESCO site with the endemic coco de mer palm, which produces the world's largest seed (up to 25 kg). Anse Source d'Argent on La Digue is one of the most photographed beaches in the world.
☀️ Apr–May, Oct–Nov📐 459 km²🌴 Coco de mer (endemic)
Tourism Board
Mauritius
Mauritius
A volcanic island in the southwestern Indian Ocean, surrounded by one of the world's largest coral reef systems. The "underwater waterfall" illusion off Le Morne is caused by sand and silt runoff along the ocean shelf. The island was the only known habitat of the dodo (extinct ~1681). Le Morne Brabant, a basalt monolith, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site linked to the island's history of escaped slaves.
☀️ May–Dec📐 2,040 km²🏛️ Le Morne (UNESCO)
Tourism Board
Sri Lanka
Sri Lanka
An island nation off India's southeastern coast with eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the ancient cities of Sigiriya (a 5th-century fortress built atop a 200 m rock column) and Polonnaruwa. The central highlands produce Ceylon tea at elevations above 1,200 m. The island's coastline stretches 1,340 km, with distinct surf seasons on each coast — southwest (Nov–Apr) and east (May–Oct).
☀️ Dec–Mar (west), May–Sep (east)📐 65,610 km²🏛️ 8 UNESCO sites

South Pacific
Bora Bora
French Polynesia
A volcanic island surrounded by a barrier reef and a turquoise lagoon. Mount Otemanu (727 m) is the remnant volcanic peak at the island's center. The overwater bungalow concept originated here in the 1960s. The lagoon is shallow enough to stand in across large sections, with manta rays and reef sharks present year-round.
☀️ May–Oct📐 30 km²🌋 Mt. Otemanu (727 m)
Tahiti Tourism
Fiji
Fiji
An archipelago of 333 islands (about 110 inhabited) in the South Pacific. The two main islands, Viti Levu and Vanua Levu, are volcanic with mountainous interiors and sugarcane-covered lowlands. The Yasawa and Mamanuca island chains are the primary resort areas. Fiji sits on the 180th meridian — the international date line bends around it.
☀️ May–Oct📐 18,274 km²🏝️ 333 islands

Moorea
French Polynesia
A volcanic island 17 km northwest of Tahiti, shaped like a heart when viewed from above. Eight mountain peaks ring the interior, with the tallest (Mount Tohiea) at 1,207 m. Cook's Bay and Opunohu Bay are deep inlets carved by erosion, bordered by vertical green ridges. The barrier reef creates a calm lagoon used for research by UC Berkeley's Gump Station.
☀️ May–Oct📐 134 km²⛰️ 8 volcanic peaks

Cook Islands
Cook Islands
A self-governing territory in free association with New Zealand, comprising 15 islands spread across 2 million km² of ocean. Rarotonga, the main island, is a volcanic peak ringed by a coral reef and lagoon. Aitutaki's lagoon is considered one of the most beautiful in the Pacific. The total population across all islands is approximately 15,000.
☀️ May–Oct📐 237 km²👥 ~15,000 total

New Zealand (South Island)
New Zealand
The 12th-largest island in the world, split by the Southern Alps with Aoraki / Mount Cook at 3,724 m. Fiordland National Park contains Milford Sound and Doubtful Sound — glacially carved inlets with vertical walls rising over 1,200 m from the water. The west coast glaciers (Franz Josef, Fox) descend from alpine snowfields to near sea level through temperate rainforest.
☀️ Nov–Mar📐 150,437 km²⛰️ Aoraki / Mt. Cook (3,724 m)

Atlantic & Northern Europe
Iceland
Iceland
A volcanic island on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge where the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates diverge. The island has over 30 active volcanic systems, 10,000+ waterfalls, and 13% glacial coverage. The Golden Circle route connects Þingvellir (UNESCO — site of the world's oldest parliament, est. 930 CE), Geysir (the original geyser), and Gullfoss waterfall. The northern lights are visible September through March.
☀️ Jun–Aug (summer), Sep–Mar (aurora)📐 103,000 km²🌋 30+ active volcanic systems
Tourism Board
Faroe Islands
Denmark
An archipelago of 18 volcanic islands between Norway, Iceland, and Scotland. The landscape is treeless — steep sea cliffs, grass-roofed villages, and narrow fjords. Múlafossur waterfall on Vágar drops directly into the ocean from a cliff edge. The islands have more sheep (~80,000) than people (~54,000). Inter-island tunnels, including a sub-sea tunnel, connect the main islands.
☀️ May–Sep📐 1,399 km²🐑 80,000 sheep
Tourism Board
Madeira
Portugal
A Portuguese volcanic island 1,000 km southwest of Lisbon in the Atlantic. The interior is mountainous (Pico Ruivo, 1,862 m) and laced with levadas — 16th-century irrigation channels now used as walking trails totaling over 2,500 km. The island's subtropical climate supports year-round flowers, earning it the nickname "floating garden." Funchal's New Year's Eve fireworks display holds a Guinness record.
☀️ Year-round📐 801 km²🥾 2,500+ km of levada trails

Canary Islands
Spain
A Spanish archipelago of seven main islands off the northwest coast of Africa. Tenerife's Mount Teide (3,718 m) is Spain's highest peak and the third-tallest volcanic structure in the world. Lanzarote's Timanfaya National Park is a volcanic landscape of lava fields and craters, and the entire island is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. Average temperatures stay between 18–24 °C year-round.
☀️ Year-round📐 7,447 km²🌋 Mt. Teide (3,718 m)

Americas
Galápagos Islands
Ecuador
A volcanic archipelago 1,000 km off Ecuador's coast, where Charles Darwin's 1835 observations contributed to his theory of evolution. The islands are home to species found nowhere else — giant tortoises (up to 250 kg), marine iguanas, and flightless cormorants. 97% of the land area is a national park, and visitor numbers are regulated. The surrounding marine reserve covers 133,000 km².
☀️ Jun–Nov (dry), Dec–May (warm)📐 7,880 km²🐢 Endemic species
Galápagos Conservancy
Maui
Hawaii, United States
The second-largest Hawaiian island, formed by two volcanoes. Haleakalā (3,055 m) dominates the eastern half — sunrise from its summit, above the cloud layer, is a major draw. The Road to Hana covers 84 km of coastline with 620 curves and 59 bridges. The waters between Maui, Molokai, and Lanai are a humpback whale sanctuary (peak season: January–March).
☀️ Apr–Nov📐 1,883 km²🌋 Haleakalā (3,055 m)
Go Hawaii View Guide
Vancouver Island
British Columbia, Canada
The largest Pacific island south of Alaska, at 31,285 km². The west coast (Pacific Rim National Park) receives up to 3,300 mm of rain annually, supporting old-growth temperate rainforest. Tofino is the primary surf destination in Canada. The island is home to black bears, cougars, wolves, and resident orca pods. Victoria, the provincial capital, sits on the southern tip.
☀️ Jun–Sep📐 31,285 km²🐻 Old-growth rainforest

Easter Island (Rapa Nui)
Chile
One of the most isolated inhabited islands on Earth, 3,700 km west of Chile in the southeastern Pacific. The island is known for 887 monolithic moai statues carved by the Rapa Nui people between 1250–1500 CE, the largest standing at 10 m and weighing 82 tonnes. The entire island is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Rapa Nui language is still spoken by the island's ~8,000 residents.
☀️ Nov–Mar📐 164 km²🗿 887 moai statues
Chile Tourism
