📋 ATM Tips
Choose local currency
Dynamic currency conversion (DCC)
When an ATM or card terminal asks "Pay in USD or local currency?" — always choose local currency. Choosing USD activates dynamic currency conversion, which applies a bad exchange rate set by the merchant, not your bank.
Use bank ATMs only
Avoid independent kiosks
Stick to ATMs attached to a physical bank branch. Independent kiosks in airports, tourist areas, and convenience stores charge high fees and sometimes offer poor rates. Look for ATMs from national banks or major international networks (Cirrus, Plus).
Withdraw larger amounts
Reduce per-transaction costs
Per-transaction ATM fees add up fast if you withdraw small amounts frequently. Take out enough for 2–4 days at once. Keep excess in a hotel safe if needed.
4-digit PIN required
PIN format
Most ATMs outside the US only accept 4-digit PINs. If your card has a longer PIN, set a 4-digit one before departure. If your PIN starts with 0, some older European ATMs may not accept it — check with your bank.
Notify your bank before travel
Prevent card freezes
Many banks flag out-of-country transactions and freeze the card automatically. Call or set a travel notice in your bank's app before departure. Carry two cards from different networks in case one is blocked.
ATM skimming
Security
Skimming devices are more common in tourist areas and older ATMs. Wiggle the card slot before inserting — skimmers are loose. Cover the keypad with your hand when entering your PIN. Prefer tap-to-pay (contactless) for purchases to avoid card data exposure entirely.
💵 Cash by Region
Japan
High cash dependency
Cash is still widely used. Many restaurants, temples, vending machines, and smaller shops are cash-only. 7-Eleven and Japan Post ATMs reliably accept foreign cards. Withdraw cash upon arrival.
Morocco · Vietnam · Peru
Partial cash dependency
Cash needed for markets, street food, smaller vendors, and tips. Cards accepted at hotels and larger restaurants. ATMs available in cities but limited in rural areas.
Western Europe · Australia · Singapore
Mostly cashless
Card or contactless payment accepted almost everywhere. Tap-to-pay (Visa/Mastercard contactless, Apple Pay, Google Pay) is standard. Carrying small amounts of cash for taxis and emergencies is still useful.
Contactless & mobile pay abroad
Apple Pay · Google Pay
Apple Pay and Google Pay work anywhere that accepts contactless Visa or Mastercard — which is most of Western Europe, UK, Australia, Japan, Singapore, and Canada. Your stored card's foreign transaction fee still applies. No separate setup needed.
