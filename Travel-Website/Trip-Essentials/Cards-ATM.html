Wise / Revolut
Multi-currency prepaid
Wise uses the mid-market exchange rate with a small transparent fee — usually the best rate available. Revolut offers fee-free exchange up to a monthly limit. Both are useful for currencies where ATM access is limited or rates are poor. Not a substitute for a primary bank card.
Cards to avoid abroad
High-fee cards
Most standard bank debit cards (Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo checking) charge 1–3% foreign transaction fees plus $2–$5 ATM fees per withdrawal. Check your card's terms before relying on it internationally.
ATM tips
General guidance
Use bank ATMs (not independent kiosks in airports/tourist areas — they charge high fees). Always decline "dynamic currency conversion" (DCC) when asked — choose to be charged in the local currency, not USD. Withdraw larger amounts less frequently to minimize per-transaction fees. Notify your bank before departure to avoid card freezes.
Cash by region
When to carry cash
Japan: cash is still widely used — many restaurants, temples, and smaller shops are cash-only. Morocco, Vietnam, Peru: cash needed for markets and small vendors. Western Europe, Australia, Singapore: card or contactless payment accepted almost everywhere. Always carry some local currency for taxis, tips, and emergencies.
