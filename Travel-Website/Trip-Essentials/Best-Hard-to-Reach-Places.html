Antarctica
Antarctica
Antarctica
Earth's southernmost continent, governed by international treaty and accessible only by expedition cruise from Ushuaia or Punta Arenas. No permanent civilian population, no hotels — visitors observe penguin colonies, calving glaciers, and the Weddell Sea pack ice from ships or zodiacs.

Argentina
Patagonia — Los Glaciares
Santa Cruz, Argentina
A national park in Argentine Patagonia anchored by the Perito Moreno Glacier — one of the world's few advancing glaciers — and the granite towers above El Chaltén, the trekking capital of Argentina. Reached by flights to El Calafate followed by long road transfers.

Bhutan
Bhutan
Bhutan
A landlocked Himalayan kingdom with a controlled-tourism policy requiring visitors to book through licensed operators and pay a daily Sustainable Development Fee. Known for Tiger's Nest Monastery (Paro Taktsang) perched on a 900-meter cliff, pristine dzong fortresses, and one of the world's lowest-traffic trekking networks.
Wikipedia Tourism Council of Bhutan Map
Bolivia
Salar de Uyuni
Bolivia
The world's largest salt flat, sitting at 3,650 meters on the Bolivian altiplano, stretching over 10,000 square kilometers. In the rainy season a thin layer of water creates a mirror effect that reflects the sky. Reached by bus or 4WD from Uyuni town after flights to La Paz or Sucre.
Wikipedia Bolivia Tourism Map
Brazil
Amazon Rainforest Interior
Amazonas, Brazil
The deep interior of the world's largest tropical rainforest, accessible only by small river boat from Manaus after a multi-day upstream journey. Rare wildlife including pink river dolphins, jaguar, and hundreds of bird species inhabit a flooded forest landscape where there are no roads.
Wikipedia Visit Brasil Map
Chile
Atacama Desert
Chile
The driest non-polar desert on Earth, in northern Chile at 2,400 meters elevation, centered on the town of San Pedro de Atacama. Landscapes include the Valle de la Luna, El Tatio geysers, flamingo-filled salt lakes, and one of the world's best stargazing skies. Reached by flight to Calama followed by road transfer.
Wikipedia Chile Tourism Map
Patagonia — Torres del Paine
Magallanes, Chile
A national park in Chilean Patagonia built around three granite towers rising over 2,800 meters, with the W Trek and O Circuit among the world's most celebrated multi-day hikes. Reached by flight to Punta Arenas then a four-hour bus to Puerto Natales, followed by another transfer into the park.
Wikipedia Chile Tourism Map
China
Tibet
China
An autonomous region on the Tibetan Plateau averaging over 4,500 meters above sea level, home to the Potala Palace in Lhasa, Namtso Lake, and the base camp route to Mount Everest. Foreign visitors require a Tibet Travel Permit in addition to a Chinese visa, and independent travel is not permitted — all visits must be through an authorized tour operator.
Wikipedia Map
Denmark
Greenland
Greenland (Denmark)
The world's largest island, an autonomous Danish territory covered 80 percent by an ice sheet up to 3 kilometers thick. The small coastal settlements — including Ilulissat near a UNESCO-listed icefjord — are connected only by air or boat. No roads link towns. The northern lights, dog-sledding, and glacier hiking are the main draws.
Wikipedia Visit Greenland Map
Falkland Islands
Falkland Islands
United Kingdom
A British Overseas Territory in the South Atlantic roughly 500 kilometers east of Patagonia, with a small permanent population in Stanley. Home to large penguin colonies, elephant seals, black-browed albatrosses, and a rugged moorland interior. Reached by a twice-weekly flight from Brize Norton (UK) or a weekly charter from Punta Arenas, or as a stop on Antarctic expedition cruises.

Mongolia
Mongolian Steppe & Gobi Desert
Mongolia
A vast landlocked country between Russia and China where nomadic herder culture survives largely unchanged, centered on ger camps across open grassland and the Gobi's sand dunes and saxaul forests. The Flaming Cliffs yielded the first confirmed dinosaur eggs. Ulaanbaatar is one of the world's most isolated capitals, and most interior destinations require long overland drives on unpaved tracks.

Namibia
Namib Desert
Namibia
Considered the world's oldest desert at around 55 million years old, running 2,000 kilometers along Africa's Atlantic coast. The Sossusvlei dunes — some reaching 300 meters — and the eerie dead trees of Deadvlei are the visual centerpiece. Reached by fly-in from Windhoek or long self-drive on unsealed roads through one of the world's least densely populated countries.
Wikipedia Namibia Tourism Map
Nepal
Everest Base Camp
Nepal
A 12-to-14-day round trek from Lukla (accessed by a famously short mountain airstrip) to the 5,364-meter South Base Camp at the foot of Mount Everest, passing through Sherpa villages, Buddhist monasteries, and the high-altitude hub of Namche Bazaar. The trek requires a Sagarmatha National Park permit and crosses two high passes at altitude.
Wikipedia Nepal Tourism Map
Norway
Svalbard
Norway
A Norwegian Arctic archipelago at 78° North, between the Norwegian mainland and the North Pole. Longyearbyen is the world's northernmost settlement with a commercial airport. Polar bears outnumber people; leaving town without a rifle guide is prohibited. The midnight sun runs from April to August, polar night from October to February. Attractions include glacier hikes, snowmobile expeditions, and Northern Lights from November.

Papua New Guinea
Papua New Guinea Highlands
Papua New Guinea
A mountainous interior home to hundreds of distinct tribal groups, many maintaining traditional dress, face paint, and cultural practices little changed for centuries. The annual Goroka and Hagen cultural shows bring dozens of tribes together. Infrastructure is limited — most highland communities are accessible only by small plane or multi-day foot trek through dense jungle and steep ridgelines.

Russia
Kamchatka Peninsula
Russia
A remote peninsula in Russia's Far East projecting into the Pacific, with over 300 volcanoes including 29 active ones, extensive geothermal fields, and some of the world's densest brown bear populations. Accessed by flight to Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky (about 8 hours from Moscow), then helicopter or boat to the wilderness areas. Foreign visitor numbers remain extremely low.

South Georgia
South Georgia
United Kingdom
A sub-Antarctic British Overseas Territory in the South Atlantic, accessible only by expedition cruise — there are no flights and no permanent civilian residents. The island hosts the largest king penguin colony on Earth, vast elephant seal harems, and the grave of Ernest Shackleton at Grytviken, where his 1914 Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition ended.

United Kingdom
Pitcairn Islands
United Kingdom
The world's most remote inhabited territory — a British Overseas Territory of four islands in the South Pacific, with a permanent population of around 40 people descended from Bounty mutineers and their Tahitian companions. There is no airport; the only way to arrive is by a cargo/passenger supply ship from Mangareva, French Polynesia, a journey of roughly 32 hours each way with departures once or twice a month.

Yemen
Socotra Island
Yemen
A UNESCO World Heritage island in the Arabian Sea so isolated that a third of its plant life exists nowhere else on Earth, including the unmistakable Dragon Blood Tree with its umbrella-shaped canopy. Often called the Galápagos of the Indian Ocean. Direct flights from Abu Dhabi and Dubai currently operate, though access depends on the ongoing security situation in Yemen.
Wikipedia Map
Zambia / Zimbabwe
Victoria Falls Backcountry
Zambia / Zimbabwe
While Victoria Falls itself is accessible, the vast surrounding wilderness — Mana Pools National Park on the Zimbabwe side and the Lower Zambezi on the Zambian side — is reached only by small charter plane or long bush road. Canoe safaris on the Zambezi pass within meters of hippos and crocodiles; Mana Pools is one of the few African parks where walking safaris alongside elephant and lion are permitted without a vehicle.
