🚰 Tap Water
Western Europe Safe
Austria · Belgium · Denmark · Finland · France · Germany · Iceland · Ireland · Luxembourg · Netherlands · Norway · Sweden · Switzerland · UK
Tap water meets EU drinking water standards and is safe to drink in all major cities. Iceland tap water is glacier-fed and among the cleanest in the world.
Southern Europe Varies
Croatia · Greece · Italy · Portugal · Spain
Technically safe in major cities but often heavily chlorinated and unpleasant-tasting. Most locals drink bottled water. Stick to bottled in rural areas and islands (Croatia, Greek islands). Spain and Portugal tap water is safe in cities.
Eastern Europe Varies
Czech Republic · Hungary · Poland · Slovenia · Slovakia
Prague, Budapest, Warsaw, and Ljubljana all have safe, clean tap water. Bottled water is widely used by locals in smaller cities and rural areas as a habit, not a necessity.
USA & Canada Safe
All major cities
Tap water is regulated and safe to drink in all US and Canadian cities covered in the guides. Older buildings in some US cities may have lead pipes — use a filter or bottled water if the building is pre-1986.
Australia & New Zealand Safe
Tap water is safe and well-regulated across all major cities. No precautions needed.
Japan & South Korea Safe
Tap water in Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, and Seoul is safe to drink and routinely rated among the best in Asia. Filtered water dispensers are common in hotels.
Southeast Asia Avoid
Thailand · Vietnam · Indonesia · Philippines
Do not drink tap water. Use bottled or filtered water for drinking and brushing teeth. Ice in restaurants is typically made from purified water but verify at street stalls.
Singapore & Hong Kong Safe
Tap water meets WHO standards and is safe to drink directly from the tap. Singapore's water is among the most rigorously treated in Asia.
China Avoid
Beijing · Shanghai · Chongqing · Zhangjiajie
Tap water is not safe to drink. Hotels provide bottled water. Boiled water from hotel kettles is generally safe if the kettle is clean.
Middle East Varies
UAE · Israel
Technically meets WHO standards in Dubai and Abu Dhabi but tastes strongly of desalination. Bottled water is universally used. Israel tap water is safe to drink.
Morocco & Africa Avoid
Morocco · Nigeria · Lagos
Do not drink tap water. Use sealed bottled water. Avoid ice, salads washed in tap water, and fruit you haven't peeled yourself at street stalls.
Latin America Varies
Mexico · Peru · Brazil · Argentina · Colombia · Chile
Avoid tap water in Mexico, Peru, Colombia, and Brazil. Chile (Santiago) and Argentina (Buenos Aires) have generally safe tap water but most travelers use bottled. Lima tap water is not safe.
Caribbean Varies
Aruba · Barbados · Cayman Islands · Puerto Rico · USVI · Sint Maarten · Bahamas · Curaçao
Aruba, Barbados, Cayman Islands, Puerto Rico, USVI, and Curaçao have safe tap water. Bahamas and Sint Maarten tap water is technically treated but most visitors use bottled. Follow hotel guidance.
Turkey Avoid
Do not drink tap water in Istanbul or anywhere in Turkey. Bottled water is cheap and widely available. Use bottled for teeth brushing too.
💉 Health & Vaccines
Routine vaccines
All destinations
Be up to date on MMR (measles-mumps-rubella), flu, COVID-19, Tdap (tetanus-diphtheria-pertussis), and chickenpox before any international travel. These are recommended regardless of destination.
Hepatitis A
Most non-Western destinations
Recommended for travel to Mexico, Central and South America, the Caribbean, Africa, Asia, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East. Spread through contaminated food and water. Two doses provide long-term protection.
Typhoid
South/Southeast Asia · Africa · Latin America
Recommended for India, Nepal, Southeast Asia, sub-Saharan Africa, and parts of Latin America — particularly if eating outside tourist restaurants. Available as an oral vaccine (4 doses) or a single injection.
Hepatitis B
Most international travel
Recommended for most international travel, especially longer trips or destinations with lower medical standards. Spread through blood and bodily fluids. 3-dose series; faster 2-dose schedule (Heplisav-B) is available.
Yellow Fever
Sub-Saharan Africa · South America
Required for entry to some countries in Africa and South America if arriving from an endemic country. Some countries require proof of vaccination on arrival — check requirements for your specific route. The vaccine is given at certified travel clinics and lasts for life.
Malaria (medication)
Sub-Saharan Africa · Southeast Asia · Latin America
No vaccine. Prescription antimalarial medication (atovaquone-proguanil, doxycycline, or mefloquine) is recommended for travel to risk areas. Consult a travel doctor at least 4–6 weeks before departure. Use DEET repellent and long sleeves at dusk.
Japanese Encephalitis
Rural Southeast Asia · China · Japan
Recommended for travel to rural Asia lasting more than a month, or for travelers spending significant time outdoors. Risk is low in urban areas and tourist routes. 2-dose vaccine series.
Rabies
Adventure travel · rural areas globally
Pre-exposure vaccination recommended for adventure travelers, hikers, cyclists, and anyone spending extended time in rural areas where medical care is hard to reach. 3-dose pre-exposure series. Seek medical care immediately after any animal bite regardless of vaccination status.
Travel clinic timing
Plan ahead
Visit a travel medicine clinic at least 4–6 weeks before departure — some vaccines require multiple doses spread over weeks. The CDC Travelers' Health site (wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel) lists destination-specific recommendations. Travel clinics are available at many pharmacies (CVS, Walgreens) without a referral.

💳 Cards & ATMs
Chase Sapphire Preferred / Reserve
No foreign transaction fee
No foreign transaction fee on all purchases. Reserve earns 3x on dining and travel abroad. Both use the Visa network — widely accepted globally. Reserve also includes trip delay and cancellation insurance.
Capital One Venture / Venture X
No foreign transaction fee
No foreign transaction fee. Visa network. Venture X earns 2x on all purchases everywhere — simple and reliable for international spending. One of the easiest cards for international use with no category complexity.
Charles Schwab Debit
Best ATM card abroad
Reimburses all ATM fees worldwide — including the foreign bank's own surcharge. No foreign transaction fee. No monthly balance minimum. The best debit card for international ATM withdrawals, full stop. Requires a Schwab brokerage account.
Amex Gold / Platinum
No foreign transaction fee
No foreign transaction fee. American Express acceptance is lower outside the US — always carry a Visa or Mastercard as backup in Europe, Asia, and Latin America. Amex is widely accepted in UAE, Australia, Japan, and UK.
Wise / Revolut
Multi-currency prepaid
Wise uses the mid-market exchange rate with a small transparent fee — usually the best rate available. Revolut offers fee-free exchange up to a monthly limit. Both are useful for currencies where ATM access is limited or rates are poor. Not a substitute for a primary bank card.
Cards to avoid abroad
High-fee cards
Most standard bank debit cards (Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo checking) charge 1–3% foreign transaction fees plus $2–$5 ATM fees per withdrawal. Check your card's terms before relying on it internationally.
ATM tips
General guidance
Use bank ATMs (not independent kiosks in airports/tourist areas — they charge high fees). Always decline "dynamic currency conversion" (DCC) when asked — choose to be charged in the local currency, not USD. Withdraw larger amounts less frequently to minimize per-transaction fees. Notify your bank before departure to avoid card freezes.
Cash by region
When to carry cash
Japan: cash is still widely used — many restaurants, temples, and smaller shops are cash-only. Morocco, Vietnam, Peru: cash needed for markets and small vendors. Western Europe, Australia, Singapore: card or contactless payment accepted almost everywhere. Always carry some local currency for taxis, tips, and emergencies.
