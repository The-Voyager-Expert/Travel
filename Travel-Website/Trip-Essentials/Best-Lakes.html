European Alps

Lake Bled
Bled, Slovenia
An emerald-green alpine lake with a fairy-tale island church at its centre, reached by traditional pletna boat. A medieval castle perches on a cliff 130 metres above the shore. The Julian Alps frame every angle. One of the most photographed lakes in the world.
☀️ May–Oct📏 2.1 km long🏊 Swimmable in summer
Bled Tourism
💎
Lake Bohinj
Triglav National Park, Slovenia
Slovenia's largest natural lake, surrounded by dense forest and 2,000-metre peaks inside Triglav National Park. Far quieter than nearby Bled. Crystal-clear glacial water reflects the mountains. The Savica Waterfall drops 78 metres at the lake's western end.
☀️ May–Oct📏 4.3 km long🏊 Swimmable in summer
Bohinj Tourism
🏞️
Lake Como
Lombardy, Italy
A deep Y-shaped lake carved by glaciers, lined with grand villas, cypresses, and pastel villages. The towns of Bellagio, Varenna, and Menaggio are connected by ferry. George Clooney's Villa Oleandra put it on the modern celebrity map, but the lake has drawn travellers since Roman times.
☀️ Apr–Oct📏 46 km long🌊 410 m max depth
Lake Como Info
🍋
Lake Garda
Trentino / Lombardy / Veneto, Italy
Italy's largest lake, stretching from alpine cliffs in the north to Mediterranean-climate lemon groves in the south. Sirmione's thermal springs sit on a peninsula, Riva del Garda draws windsurfers, and Limone clings to the western cliffs. Gardaland theme park is nearby.
☀️ Apr–Oct📏 51.6 km long🏊 Swimmable in summer
Visit Garda
🌿
Lake Brienz
Bernese Oberland, Switzerland
A turquoise alpine lake fed by glacial meltwater at the foot of the Brienzer Rothorn. The village of Iseltwald sits on a tiny peninsula — its wooden pier is one of the most photographed spots in Switzerland. Giessbach Falls drops 500 metres through forest into the lake.
☀️ May–Oct📏 14 km long🌊 260 m max depth
Interlaken Tourism
⛰️
Hallstätter See
Hallstatt, Austria
A mirror-still lake at the base of the Dachstein massif, reflecting the pastel-coloured houses of Hallstatt — a UNESCO World Heritage village. The oldest known salt mine in the world sits in the mountains above. One of the most visited villages in Austria.
☀️ May–Oct📏 5.9 km long🌊 125 m max depth
Hallstatt Tourism
🦌
Königssee
Berchtesgaden, Germany
Germany's cleanest lake, enclosed by sheer rock walls rising 1,800 metres. Electric boats glide to the baroque St. Bartholomä chapel, where a trumpeter plays to demonstrate the echo off the cliffs. Only electric-powered vessels allowed since 1909.
☀️ May–Oct📏 7.7 km long🌊 190 m max depth
Berchtesgaden Tourism
🏔️
Oeschinen Lake
Kandersteg, Switzerland
A UNESCO World Heritage alpine lake at 1,578 metres, ringed by 3,000-metre peaks and waterfalls fed by snowmelt. Reached by gondola and a short hike from Kandersteg. Rowboats available in summer; the water glows a deep blue-green. Ice fishing and skating in winter.
☀️ Jun–Oct📏 1.6 km long⛰️ 1,578 m elevation
Oeschinen Lake
Nordic & Balkans

Plitvice Lakes
Lika-Senj, Croatia
Sixteen terraced lakes connected by 90 waterfalls, cascading through dense beech and fir forest. Wooden boardwalks thread between the pools, which shift from azure to green to grey depending on minerals and light. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979.
☀️ Apr–Oct📏 16 interconnected lakes🌿 296 km² national park

Lake Ohrid
North Macedonia / Albania
One of Europe's oldest and deepest lakes, over 2 million years old, with 200+ endemic species. The medieval Church of St. John at Kaneo sits on a cliff directly above the water. A UNESCO World Heritage site for both nature and culture. Swimmable all summer.
☀️ May–Oct📏 30.4 km long🌊 288 m max depth
Ohrid Tourism
🐟
Lake Saimaa
Savonlinna, Finland
Finland's largest lake and the fourth largest in Europe, a labyrinth of 13,710 islands and endless forested shoreline. Home to the critically endangered Saimaa ringed seal (only ~430 remain). Midnight sun in summer, lakeside saunas year-round. Olavinlinna Castle sits on an island in Savonlinna.
☀️ Jun–Aug📏 4,400 km² surface🏝️ 13,710 islands
Visit Savonlinna
🌌
Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon
Vatnajökull, Iceland
A glacial lagoon at the edge of Europe's largest ice cap, filled with floating icebergs that calve from the Breiðamerkurjökull glacier. Icebergs wash up on the nearby Diamond Beach, where they glitter on black volcanic sand. The lagoon has quadrupled in size since the 1970s as the glacier retreats.
☀️ Year-round📏 18 km² surface🌊 248 m max depth
Visit Iceland
North America
💙
Crater Lake
Oregon, United States
The deepest lake in the United States, formed in the caldera of collapsed Mount Mazama 7,700 years ago. Fed entirely by rain and snowmelt — no inlets or outlets — giving it some of the clearest water on Earth. The deep blue colour is unmatched. Wizard Island rises from the western basin.
☀️ Jul–Sep📏 9.6 km wide🌊 594 m max depth
NPS Info
🏔️
Lake Tahoe
California / Nevada, United States
North America's largest alpine lake, straddling the California-Nevada border at 1,897 metres. Cobalt blue water with 21 metres of clarity surrounded by granite peaks and pine forest. Skiing in winter, beaches and boating in summer. Emerald Bay State Park is the crown jewel.
☀️ Year-round📏 35 km long🌊 501 m max depth
Visit Lake Tahoe
🌲
Moraine Lake
Banff, Alberta, Canada
A glacially-fed lake in the Valley of the Ten Peaks whose turquoise colour comes from light refracting off glacial rock flour. The view from the rockpile at the shore appeared on the Canadian $20 bill. Often ranked the most beautiful lake in the world.
☀️ Jun–Oct📏 0.5 km long⛰️ 1,884 m elevation

💎
Lake Louise
Banff, Alberta, Canada
An iconic turquoise lake below Victoria Glacier, backed by the historic Fairmont Chateau. The milky blue-green colour peaks in late June as glacial meltwater flows in. Canoe rentals on the lake; the Plain of Six Glaciers trail starts at the far shore.
☀️ Jun–Oct📏 2.4 km long⛰️ 1,750 m elevation
Banff & Lake Louise
🌊
Peyto Lake
Icefields Parkway, Alberta, Canada
A fox-head-shaped lake visible from Bow Summit, the highest point on the Icefields Parkway. The water is an almost unreal shade of turquoise from Peyto Glacier's rock flour. The viewpoint at 2,088 metres is one of the most dramatic in the Canadian Rockies.
☀️ Jun–Oct📏 2.8 km long⛰️ 1,860 m elevation
Parks Canada
🐻
Maligne Lake
Jasper, Alberta, Canada
The largest natural lake in the Canadian Rockies, famous for Spirit Island — a tiny tree-topped islet surrounded by glacial water and towering peaks. Accessible only by boat cruise or kayak. The 14 km paddle to Spirit Island is one of Canada's classic canoe trips.
☀️ Jun–Oct📏 22.5 km long🌊 97 m max depth
Parks Canada
Central & South America
🌋
Lake Atitlán
Sololá, Guatemala
A volcanic crater lake ringed by three volcanoes and a dozen indigenous Maya villages. Aldous Huxley called it the most beautiful lake in the world. Water taxis connect the villages. San Pedro La Laguna and Panajachel are the main bases. The lake sits at 1,562 metres.
☀️ Nov–Apr📏 18 km long🌊 340 m max depth
Visit Guatemala
🦙
Lake Titicaca
Peru / Bolivia
The world's highest navigable lake at 3,812 metres, straddling the Peru-Bolivia border on the Altiplano. The Uros people live on floating islands built from totora reeds. Isla del Sol on the Bolivian side is the mythical birthplace of the Inca sun god. Deep cobalt water stretches to the horizon.
☀️ May–Oct📏 190 km long🌊 281 m max depth
Visit Peru
🧊
Lago Argentino
Patagonia, Argentina
Argentina's largest lake, fed by multiple glaciers including the Perito Moreno Glacier — one of the few advancing glaciers on Earth. Boat tours pass towering ice walls that calve into the milky blue water. The gateway town of El Calafate sits on the southern shore.
☀️ Nov–Mar📏 1,466 km² surface🌊 500 m max depth
Argentina Tourism
🌲
Lago Todos los Santos
Los Lagos, Chile
Known as the "Emerald Lake" for its vivid green water, set in temperate rainforest beneath the snowcapped Osorno volcano. Part of the historic Andean Lake Crossing route between Chile and Argentina. Virtually no development along its shores — pure Patagonian wilderness.
☀️ Nov–Mar📏 33 km long🌊 337 m max depth
Chile Tourism
Africa
🦛
Lake Malawi
Malawi / Mozambique / Tanzania
The ninth-largest lake in the world and the most biodiverse — over 1,000 species of cichlid fish, more than any other lake on Earth. Clear, warm freshwater with sandy beaches that feel tropical. Cape Maclear is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Safe for swimming with no bilharzia at most lakeshore lodges.
☀️ May–Oct📏 569 km long🌊 706 m max depth
Malawi Tourism
🌸
Lake Nakuru
Nakuru, Kenya
A shallow alkaline lake in the Great Rift Valley, famous for vast flocks of lesser flamingos that turn the shoreline pink. Part of Lake Nakuru National Park, which also shelters white rhinos, leopards, and Rothschild's giraffes. The flamingo spectacle is one of the great wildlife sights in Africa.
☀️ Jun–Mar📏 62 km² surface🦩 Seasonal flamingo flocks
Magical Kenya
🌋
Lake Kivu
Rwanda / Democratic Republic of Congo
One of Africa's Great Lakes, sitting at 1,460 metres with the Virunga volcanoes rising behind. No hippos, no crocodiles, no bilharzia — making it one of the few swimmable great lakes in Africa. The Rwandan shore towns of Gisenyi and Kibuye have sandy beaches and warm water year-round.
☀️ Jun–Sep📏 89 km long🌊 480 m max depth
Visit Rwanda
Asia
🏯
Lake Kawaguchi
Yamanashi, Japan
The most accessible of the Fuji Five Lakes, offering the classic postcard reflection of Mount Fuji across still water. Cherry blossoms in spring and fiery momiji maples in autumn frame the view. The Chureito Pagoda viewpoint above the lake is one of Japan's most photographed scenes.
☀️ Year-round📏 5.7 km long🗻 Fuji reflection
Yamanashi Tourism
🛶
Phewa Lake
Pokhara, Nepal
A subtropical lake reflecting the Annapurna massif and Machhapuchhre (Fishtail Peak) at 6,993 metres. Colourful wooden rowboats dot the surface. The lakeside city of Pokhara is the gateway to the Annapurna Circuit. Tal Barahi Temple sits on a small island in the centre.
☀️ Oct–May📏 4 km long🏔️ Annapurna panorama
Nepal Tourism
🪷
Inle Lake
Shan State, Myanmar
A shallow freshwater lake where Intha fishermen row standing on one leg, wrapping the other around the oar. Floating gardens grow tomatoes on beds of water hyacinth. Stilted villages, monasteries, and weaving workshops line the shores. The lake sits at 880 metres in the Shan Hills.
☀️ Oct–Mar📏 22 km long🌊 3–4 m avg depth
Myanmar Tourism
🐉
Ha Long Bay (Freshwater: Ba Bể Lake)
Bắc Kạn, Vietnam
Vietnam's largest natural lake, surrounded by towering limestone karsts and primary forest inside Ba Bể National Park. Wooden stilt houses of the Tày ethnic minority line the shore. Boat trips pass through caves and under waterfalls. Far off the tourist trail — one of Southeast Asia's hidden lakes.
☀️ Apr–Nov📏 8 km long🌊 35 m max depth
Vietnam Tourism
Central Asia
🌊
Lake Issyk-Kul
Issyk-Kul Region, Kyrgyzstan
The world's second-largest alpine lake, set at 1,607 metres between the Tien Shan ranges. Despite the altitude, it never freezes — thermal activity keeps it warm enough to swim on the northern shore in summer. Sandy beaches ring an inland sea of deep blue water with 5,000-metre snow peaks behind.
☀️ Jun–Sep📏 182 km long🌊 668 m max depth
Discover Kyrgyzstan
💎
Iskanderkul
Fann Mountains, Tajikistan
A turquoise alpine lake at 2,195 metres in the Fann Mountains, named after Alexander the Great who allegedly passed through. Glacial water, sheer rock walls, and a 38-metre waterfall at the outlet. Virtually no tourists — one of Central Asia's most spectacular and least-visited lakes.
☀️ Jun–Sep📏 3.4 km long⛰️ 2,195 m elevation
Tajikistan Tourism

Lake Tekapo
Canterbury, New Zealand
A milky turquoise lake fed by the Godley River glacier, framed by the Southern Alps. The Church of the Good Shepherd on the shore is one of New Zealand's most photographed landmarks. Part of the Aoraki Mackenzie International Dark Sky Reserve — the stargazing is world-class.
☀️ Year-round📏 25 km long⛰️ 710 m elevation

Lake Wanaka
Otago, New Zealand
A glacial lake surrounded by mountains and golden tussock, famous for the lone willow tree (#ThatWanakaTree) growing from the water. Quieter and more laid-back than neighbouring Queenstown. Gateway to Mount Aspiring National Park and the Rob Roy Glacier track.
☀️ Year-round📏 45 km long🌊 300 m max depth
Lake Wanaka Tourism
🌋
Tongariro Emerald Lakes
Tongariro National Park, New Zealand
Three vivid emerald-coloured crater lakes along the Tongariro Alpine Crossing, one of the world's great day hikes. The colour comes from dissolved minerals leaching from surrounding volcanic vents. Steam rises from fumaroles. The volcanic plateau inspired the landscapes of Mordor in the Lord of the Rings films.
☀️ Nov–Apr📏 Crater lakes (small)🌋 Active volcanic zone

Siberia & Mongolia
🧊
Lake Baikal
Irkutsk Oblast, Russia
The deepest lake on Earth (1,642 metres), holding 20% of the world's unfrozen freshwater. Over 25 million years old, it shelters 1,800+ endemic species including the nerpa, the only freshwater seal. In winter the surface freezes into transparent ice with surreal cracks stretching to the horizon.
☀️ Jun–Sep (summer) · Jan–Mar (ice)📏 636 km long🌊 1,642 m max depth
Baikal Info
🐎
Khövsgöl Nuur
Khövsgöl, Mongolia
Mongolia's largest freshwater lake, known as the "Younger Sister of Baikal." Crystal-clear water surrounded by forested mountains and vast steppe. The Tsaatan reindeer herders live in the taiga to the northwest. Horseback riding along the shore is the classic way to explore. Freezes solid by November.
☀️ Jun–Sep📏 136 km long🌊 262 m max depth
Mongolia Tourism

South America's second-largest lake, shared between Chile and Argentina, famous for the Marble Caves — swirling blue-and-white marble formations carved by 6,000 years of wave action. The water is an intense milky turquoise from glacial sediment. Boat tours enter the cave tunnels.

A vast glacial lake in the Argentine Lake District surrounded by Valdivian temperate rainforest and snow-capped Andes peaks. The resort town of San Carlos de Bariloche sits on the southern shore. The Circuito Chico scenic drive loops through forest and past viewpoints above the lake's many arms.

Lake Powell
Utah / Arizona, United States
A reservoir filling Glen Canyon with 3,200 km of shoreline — more than the entire US Pacific coast. Red sandstone slot canyons, natural bridges, and arches surround the deep blue water. Houseboats are the classic way to explore. Rainbow Bridge, one of the world's largest natural bridges, sits in a side canyon.
☀️ Apr–Oct📏 299 km long🌊 170 m max depth

🦅
Flathead Lake
Montana, United States
The largest natural freshwater lake west of the Mississippi in the contiguous United States. Water so clear the rocky bottom is visible at 10+ metres. Surrounded by the Mission Mountains and cherry orchards. Wild Islands — a cluster of state-protected islands — are accessible by kayak.
☀️ Jun–Sep📏 45 km long🌊 113 m max depth
Flathead Lake Info
🌅
Jenny Lake
Grand Teton, Wyoming, United States
A glacial lake at the foot of the Teton Range, with the Grand Teton rising 2,100 metres directly above the water. The shuttle boat crosses to Cascade Canyon trailhead. The Inspiration Point viewpoint looks down on the lake and across Jackson Hole valley. One of the most dramatic mountain-lake settings in North America.
☀️ Jun–Sep📏 3.2 km long⛰️ 2,064 m elevation
