Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History
Washington, D.C.
The largest natural history collection in the world with over 148 million specimens and objects spanning geology, paleontology, zoology, and human origins. Located on the National Mall. Free admission.
📅 Founded 1910📦 148M+ specimens🎟️ Free

American Museum of Natural History
New York City
A complex of 21 interconnected buildings on Manhattan's Upper West Side housing 45 permanent exhibition halls, the Rose Center for Earth and Space planetarium, and over 34 million specimens and artifacts. One of the world's preeminent scientific and cultural institutions.
📅 Founded 1869📦 34M+ specimens👥 5M annual visitors

🦖
Field Museum of Natural History
Chicago, Illinois
Houses over 30 million specimens and objects across anthropology, botany, geology, and zoology. Home to SUE, the largest and most complete Tyrannosaurus rex skeleton ever discovered. The fifth-largest herbarium in the Western Hemisphere with nearly 3 million plant specimens.
📅 Founded 1893📦 30M+ specimens🦎 300K+ reptile/amphibian specimens

Smithsonian National Air and Space Museum
Washington, D.C.
Holds the largest collection of historic aircraft and spacecraft in the world. Displays include the Wright Flyer, the Spirit of St. Louis, the Apollo 11 command module, and a lunar sample visitors can touch. Free admission.
📅 Founded 1946✈️ 60K+ artifacts🎟️ Free

🏺
The Metropolitan Museum of Art
New York City
One of the world's largest art museums with over 1.5 million works spanning 5,000 years of human civilization. Major archaeological collections include the Temple of Dendur (Egyptian, 15 BC), Greek and Roman galleries, and arms and armor spanning medieval to Ottoman periods.
📅 Founded 1870📦 1.5M+ works👥 5.4M annual visitors
Official Site City Guide
🌋
Natural History Museum of Los Angeles County
Los Angeles, California
The largest natural and historical museum in the western United States with over 35 million specimens and artifacts. Houses extensive collections from the La Brea Tar Pits and a Dinosaur Hall featuring a growth series of T. rex specimens from baby to adult.
📅 Founded 1913📦 35M+ specimens🦕 Dinosaur Hall
Official Site City Guide
🧪
Exploratorium
San Francisco, California
A hands-on science museum on Pier 15 along the Embarcadero with over 650 interactive exhibits exploring perception, physics, biology, and the environment. Founded by physicist Frank Oppenheimer. Known for pioneering the interactive-exhibit museum model now adopted worldwide.
📅 Founded 1969🔬 650+ interactive exhibits📍 Pier 15, Embarcadero
Official Site City Guide
United Kingdom
📜
British Museum
London
A permanent collection of 8 million works — the largest in the world — spanning two million years of human history from every continent. Highlights include the Rosetta Stone, the Elgin Marbles from the Parthenon, Assyrian lion hunt reliefs, and the Lewis Chessmen. Free admission.
📅 Founded 1753📦 8M works🎟️ Free
Official Site City Guide
🦕
Natural History Museum
London
Over 80 million specimens spanning zoology, entomology, palaeontology, mineralogy, and botany in a Grade I-listed Romanesque building designed by Alfred Waterhouse. Set a UK museum record of 7.1 million visitors in 2025. Free admission.
📅 Opened 1881📦 80M+ specimens🎟️ Free
Official Site City Guide

Science Museum
London
Over 300,000 items documenting the development of science and technology, including Stephenson's Rocket, the Apollo 10 command module, and Charles Babbage's Difference Engine No. 2. Located on Exhibition Road in South Kensington, adjacent to the Natural History Museum. Free admission.
📅 Founded 1857📦 300K+ items🎟️ Free

Muséum national d'Histoire naturelle
Paris
Headquartered at the Jardin des Plantes on the Left Bank, this institution holds approximately 68 million specimens. The Grande Galerie de l'Évolution (reopened 1994 in an 1889 building) displays around 7,000 specimens. Origins trace to the royal Jardin du Roi, established 1635.
📅 Founded 1793📦 68M specimens🌿 Jardin des Plantes, 5th arr.

Musée du quai Branly – Jacques Chirac
Paris
Dedicated to the indigenous arts and civilizations of Africa, Asia, Oceania, and the Americas. Holds approximately 450,000 objects in a building designed by Jean Nouvel on the banks of the Seine near the Eiffel Tower. One of the world's foremost ethnographic collections.
📅 Opened 2006📦 450K objects📍 Near Eiffel Tower

Germany
🏛️
Pergamon Museum
Berlin
Houses full-scale reconstructions of monumental archaeological structures including the Pergamon Altar (2nd century BC), the Ishtar Gate of Babylon (575 BC), and the Market Gate of Miletus. Part of Berlin's Museum Island UNESCO World Heritage Site. Closed for renovation until 2027; partial reopening with Pergamon Altar and Islamic Art planned for June 2027.
📅 Opened 1930🏛️ UNESCO Museum Island🔧 Reopening June 2027
North Wing reopens June 2027. Full renovation expected through the 2030s.
Official Site City Guide
🦕
Museum für Naturkunde
Berlin
Germany's largest natural history collection with over 30 million objects documenting 4.5 billion years of Earth's history. Houses the world's tallest mounted dinosaur skeleton (Giraffatitan brancai, 13.27 m). The Wet Collection hall displays over 320,000 jars containing roughly one million preserved specimens.
📅 Founded 1810📦 30M+ objects🦕 World's tallest dino skeleton
Official Site City Guide
⚙️
Deutsches Museum
Munich
The world's largest science and technology museum by exhibition area (66,000 sq m) with over 28,000 exhibited objects across 20 permanent exhibitions covering astronautics, robotics, chemistry, classical optics, and more. Located on an island in the Isar River. Under partial renovation until 2028.
📅 Founded 1903📦 28K exhibited / 125K total📐 66,000 m² exhibition space
Official Site City Guide
Austria
💎
Naturhistorisches Museum Wien
Vienna
Around 30 million objects housed in an ornate 19th-century palace on the Ringstrasse. Collections date back over 250 years and include 5.5 million plant specimens. 39 exhibition rooms display over 100,000 objects. Home to the Venus of Willendorf (c. 25,000 BC), one of the oldest known works of art.
📅 Opened 1889📦 30M objects📐 8,460 m² exhibition space
Official Site City Guide
Italy
🏺
Museo Egizio
Turin
The oldest museum in the world dedicated exclusively to Egyptian archaeology and the largest Egyptian collection outside of Cairo. Houses over 40,000 artifacts including the Tomb of Kha and Merit (intact, 1400 BC), the Turin Royal Canon papyrus, and one of the world's largest collections of Egyptian papyri.
📅 Founded 1824📦 40K+ artifacts📜 Largest Egyptian collection outside Egypt
Official Site City Guide
Spain
🦴
Museo Nacional de Ciencias Naturales
Madrid
Spain's national natural history museum, founded during the Enlightenment era under King Charles III. Collections include over 11 million specimens across zoology, geology, paleontology, and ecology. Located on the Paseo de la Castellana. Houses significant fossil collections from Spanish archaeological sites.
📅 Founded 1771📦 11M+ specimens📍 Paseo de la Castellana
Official Site City Guide
Greece
🏛️
Acropolis Museum
Athens
Dedicated exclusively to the archaeological findings of the Athenian Acropolis and its slopes. Over 4,250 objects exhibited across 14,000 sq m, spanning the Greek Bronze Age (c. 3200 BC) to Byzantine Greece. Highlights include the Caryatids, the Parthenon frieze, and an excavated ancient Athenian neighborhood visible beneath the glass floor.
📅 Opened 2009📦 4,250+ objects📐 14,000 m²
Official Site City Guide
🏺
National Archaeological Museum
Athens
The largest archaeological museum in Greece and one of the most important in the world. Over 11,000 exhibits covering Greek antiquity from prehistory through late antiquity. Houses the Mask of Agamemnon, the Antikythera mechanism (the oldest known analog computer, c. 100 BC), and an extensive collection of Cycladic art.
📅 Founded 1829📦 11K+ exhibits⚙️ Antikythera mechanism
Official Site City Guide
Egypt
👑
Grand Egyptian Museum
Giza
The world's largest archaeological museum, opened November 2025 near the Giza pyramids. Over 100,000 artifacts across 12 permanent halls, including 20,000 displayed for the first time. Houses the complete Tutankhamun collection (5,398 pieces) in a single exhibition for the first time.
📅 Opened 2025📦 100K+ artifacts📐 167,000 m² built area
Official Site City Guide
Mexico
🗿
Museo Nacional de Antropología
Mexico City
The world's largest collection of pre-Columbian art, with around 600,000 artifacts across 23 exhibition rooms covering nearly 80,000 sq m. The largest and most visited museum in Mexico. Highlights include the Aztec Sun Stone, the massive Olmec colossal heads, and Maya jade death masks.
📅 Opened 1964📦 600K artifacts📐 79,700 m²
Official Site
China
🐉
National Museum of China
Beijing
The largest single-building museum in the world (200,000 sq m) with over 1.4 million items across 48 halls on the eastern side of Tiananmen Square. Collections span ancient bronzes, jade burial suits, Tang dynasty ceramics, and modern Chinese history. Houses the Houmuwu Ding, the heaviest ancient bronze vessel ever found (832 kg).
📅 Founded 2003📦 1.4M+ items📐 200,000 m²
Official Site City Guide
🏺
Terracotta Army Museum
Xi'an, Shaanxi
Built over the excavation site of Emperor Qin Shi Huang's mausoleum (210 BC). Three pits contain an estimated 8,000 life-size terracotta warriors, 130 chariots, and 670 horses — each figure with unique facial features. UNESCO World Heritage Site. One of the most significant archaeological discoveries of the 20th century.
📅 Discovered 1974🗿 8,000 warrior figures🏛️ UNESCO World Heritage
Official Site
Japan
⛩️
Tokyo National Museum
Tokyo
Japan's oldest and largest museum with over 120,000 objects spanning from prehistoric Jomon-period pottery to 19th-century samurai armor, including 89 designated National Treasures and 649 Important Cultural Properties. Six gallery buildings in Ueno Park. Approximately 3,000 items on rotating display at any time.
📅 Founded 1872📦 120K+ objects👑 89 National Treasures
Official Site City Guide
India
🕉️
Indian Museum
Kolkata
The oldest and largest museum in India with collections across archaeology, anthropology, geology, zoology, and art. Houses one of the finest collections of Gandhara sculpture, Egyptian mummies, Mughal-era paintings, and a meteorite collection. Founded during the British colonial period by the Asiatic Society.
📅 Founded 1814📦 100K+ objects📍 Oldest museum in Asia
Official Site
🏛️
National Museum
New Delhi
India's foremost museum of art, archaeology, and anthropology on Janpath in central Delhi. Over 200,000 objects spanning 5,000 years of Indian civilization, including Harappan (Indus Valley) artifacts, Buddhist relics, Chola bronzes, Mughal miniature paintings, and a collection of ancient coins.
📅 Founded 1949📦 200K+ objects📍 Janpath, New Delhi
Official Site

Significant collections of Australian Indigenous cultural heritage, Pacific cultures, and Australian natural history including extensive invertebrate and mineralogy holdings.

South America
🌿
Museo de La Plata
La Plata, Argentina
One of the most important natural history museums in South America, attached to the National University of La Plata. Houses extensive paleontological collections including South American megafauna, significant anthropological and archaeological holdings, and over 3.5 million specimens. Built in a neoclassical oval design.

Canada
🦕
Royal Tyrrell Museum of Palaeontology
Drumheller, Alberta
One of the world's largest paleontology museums, located in the badlands of Alberta's Dinosaur Provincial Park region. Houses over 160,000 fossils and more than 40 mounted dinosaur skeletons. Active excavation and research facility. Visitors can participate in dig programs in the surrounding badlands.
📅 Opened 1985🦴 160K+ fossils🦕 40+ mounted skeletons
Official Site
📜
Canadian Museum of History
Gatineau, Quebec
Canada's national museum of human history, located across the Ottawa River from Parliament Hill. Over 4 million artifacts documenting the cultural history of Canada and the world, with a focus on First Nations, Inuit, and Métis peoples. Houses the Grand Hall with the world's largest indoor collection of totem poles.
📅 Founded 1856📦 4M+ artifacts🪵 World's largest indoor totem pole collection
Official Site
East Africa
🦴
Nairobi National Museum
Nairobi, Kenya
Kenya's flagship museum, operated by the National Museums of Kenya, housing significant paleoanthropological collections including hominid fossils from the Turkana Basin and Olduvai Gorge region. Exhibits cover East Africa's natural history, ethnography, and contemporary art. The adjacent Snake Park houses live reptile exhibits.
📅 Founded 1910🦴 Hominid fossil collection📍 Museum Hill, Nairobi
Official Site
