Iceland
🌋
Fagradalsfjall
Reykjanes Peninsula, Iceland
One of the most accessible active eruption sites in the world. A series of fissure eruptions beginning in 2021 produced rivers of glowing lava visible from hiking trails less than an hour from Reykjavik. Landscape of fresh black lava fields and steaming vents.
🔥 Active📏 ~900 m elevation🥾 Moderate hike
Visit Iceland Reykjavik Guide
❄️
Eyjafjallajökull
South Iceland
The ice-capped stratovolcano whose 2010 eruption shut down European airspace for weeks. Its glacier-topped summit rises behind the famous Seljalandsfoss and Skógafoss waterfalls. The surrounding black-sand plains and moss-covered lava fields make it one of Iceland's most dramatic landscapes.
💤 Dormant📏 1,651 m🥾 Guided glacier hike
South Iceland Tourism Reykjavik Guide
Italy
🔥
Mount Etna
Sicily, Italy
Europe's tallest and most active volcano, rising above the Sicilian coast. Near-constant eruptions produce lava fountains and ash plumes visible from Catania and Taormina. Cable cars and 4x4s reach the upper craters; lower slopes are covered in vineyards and lava-tube caves.
🔥 Active📏 3,357 m🥾 Cable car + guided summit
Etna Park Sicily Guide
🏛️
Mount Vesuvius
Naples, Italy
The volcano that buried Pompeii and Herculaneum in 79 AD. A paved trail leads to the crater rim with views over the Bay of Naples. The most densely populated volcanic region in the world — three million people live in its shadow. The archaeological sites at its base are UNESCO World Heritage.
💤 Dormant📏 1,281 m🥾 Easy crater-rim walk
Vesuvius National Park Naples Guide
🌊
Stromboli
Aeolian Islands, Italy
Known as the "Lighthouse of the Mediterranean" for its near-continuous eruptions every 15–20 minutes for thousands of years. A steep evening hike rewards with front-row views of incandescent lava bursts against the night sky. The tiny volcanic island rises directly from the Tyrrhenian Sea.
🔥 Active📏 924 m🥾 Strenuous guided night hike
Visit Sicily Sicily Guide
Hawaii, United States
🔴
Kīlauea
Big Island, Hawaii
One of the world's most active volcanoes, erupting almost continuously from 1983 to 2018. The Halemaʻuma��u crater glows at night within Hawaiʻi Volcanoes National Park. Surrounding landscape of lava tubes, steam vents, petroglyphs, and miles of hardened lava flows meeting the ocean.
🔥 Active📏 1,247 m🥾 Drive-up viewpoints + trails
NPS Info Big Island Guide
🏔️
Mauna Kea
Big Island, Hawaii
Measured from its base on the ocean floor, this is the tallest mountain on Earth at over 10,000 meters. The summit hosts the world's premier astronomical observatories — the dry air above the clouds provides unmatched stargazing. Snow caps the peak in winter despite its tropical latitude.
💤 Dormant📏 4,207 m🥾 Drive to summit (4WD)
Visitor Info Station Big Island Guide
Central America
🌿
Arenal Volcano
La Fortuna, Costa Rica
A near-perfect conical stratovolcano rising above rainforest canopy and hot springs. Was one of the most active volcanoes in the world until entering a resting phase in 2010. The surrounding area is Costa Rica's adventure capital — zip lines, hanging bridges, and volcanic hot springs fed by geothermal heat.
💤 Resting phase📏 1,670 m🥾 Base trails + hot springs
Visit Costa Rica Arenal Guide
🟢
Poás Volcano
Central Valley, Costa Rica
Home to one of the largest acidic crater lakes in the world — a vivid turquoise pool of sulfuric water steaming within a massive crater. A paved path from the visitor center reaches the crater viewpoint in 20 minutes. Cloud forest surrounds the summit with hummingbirds and resplendent quetzals.
🔥 Active📏 2,708 m🥾 Easy paved trail
SINAC Parks San Jose Guide
🌑
Masaya Volcano
Masaya, Nicaragua
One of the few places on Earth where you can drive right up to the edge of an active lava lake. The Santiago crater glows orange at night, visible from a parking-lot viewpoint just meters from the rim. Spanish conquistadors called it "La Boca del Infierno" — the Mouth of Hell.
🔥 Active📏 635 m🥾 Drive-up crater rim
Visit Nicaragua
🏊
Acatenango & Volcán de Fuego
Antigua, Guatemala
An overnight hike up dormant Acatenango rewards with a front-row campsite overlooking Fuego — one of Central America's most active volcanoes, erupting with lava and ash every 20–30 minutes. Watching Fuego erupt against the stars from 4,000 meters is one of the world's great volcano experiences.
🔥 Fuego: Active📏 3,976 m (Acatenango)🥾 Strenuous overnight
Visit Guatemala
South America
🦙
Cotopaxi
Ecuador
One of the highest active volcanoes in the world and one of the most perfectly symmetrical. Its glacier-capped cone rises above the Andean páramo grasslands south of Quito. Climbers can summit with crampons and a guide; non-climbers hike to the José Rivas refuge at 4,800 meters for staggering views.
🔥 Active📏 5,897 m🥾 Mountaineering (summit) / Moderate (refuge)
Ecuador Travel
🏜️
Licancabur
Atacama Desert, Chile / Bolivia
A strikingly symmetrical cone volcano rising above the lunar landscape of the Atacama Desert. Its summit crater holds one of the highest lakes on Earth. The volcano dominates the skyline from San Pedro de Atacama and the surreal salt flats and flamingo lagoons at its base.
💤 Dormant📏 5,920 m🥾 Technical high-altitude climb
Chile Travel
Indonesia
🌅
Mount Bromo
East Java, Indonesia
A smoking crater set within the vast Tengger caldera — a sea of volcanic sand surrounded by sheer walls. The pre-dawn climb to the Penanjakan viewpoint reveals Bromo, Batok, and Semeru emerging from a lake of clouds at sunrise. One of the most iconic volcanic landscapes in Southeast Asia.
🔥 Active📏 2,329 m🥾 Moderate (jeep + short climb)
Indonesia Travel
💙
Kawah Ijen
East Java, Indonesia
Famous for its electric-blue sulfuric flames that burn in the dark and its turquoise acidic crater lake — the largest in the world. A pre-dawn hike reaches the crater rim where sulfur miners carry 80-kg loads up from the fumaroles. The blue fire is visible only before sunrise.
🔥 Active📏 2,769 m🥾 Moderate night hike
Indonesia Travel
🐉
Mount Rinjani
Lombok, Indonesia
Indonesia's second-highest volcano with a massive crater lake (Segara Anak) inside its caldera. A new volcanic cone rises from the lake itself. The multi-day trek to the crater rim is one of Southeast Asia's great hikes — stunning views across to Bali's Mount Agung and the Gili Islands below.
🔥 Active📏 3,726 m🥾 Strenuous multi-day trek
Indonesia Travel
Japan
🗻
Mount Fuji
Honshu, Japan
Japan's highest peak and most sacred mountain — a near-perfect symmetrical cone visible from Tokyo on clear days. Over 300,000 people climb it each summer season (July–September). The sunrise from the summit ("Goraiko") is a cultural rite of passage. Surrounded by five lakes and hot spring towns.
💤 Dormant📏 3,776 m🥾 Strenuous (summer season only)
Official Climbing Info Tokyo Guide
♨️
Mount Aso
Kumamoto, Kyushu, Japan
Home to one of the largest calderas in the world — 25 km across — with an active crater that periodically emits blue-green volcanic gas. The surrounding grasslands of Kusasenri are a rolling green expanse inside the caldera. Ropeway access (when alert levels permit) brings visitors to the crater rim.
🔥 Active📏 1,592 m🥾 Easy (ropeway or drive)
Kumamoto Tourism
Philippines
🏝️
Taal Volcano
Batangas, Philippines
A volcano within a lake within a volcano — one of the most unusual geological formations on Earth. The main crater sits on Volcano Island in the middle of Taal Lake, which itself fills a massive ancient caldera. Views from Tagaytay Ridge reveal the entire nested structure. One of the "Decade Volcanoes" identified for study.
🔥 Active📏 311 m🥾 Viewpoint from Tagaytay
Philippines Tourism
🍫
Mayon Volcano
Albay, Philippines
Considered the most perfectly shaped volcanic cone in the world. Its symmetrical slopes rise from rice paddies to a perpetually smoking summit. Frequent eruptions (over 50 in the last 400 years) keep the cone's geometry sharp. The ATV trails on its lower slopes offer dramatic close-up approaches.
🔥 Active📏 2,462 m🥾 ATV base tours / climbing restricted
Philippines Tourism
New Zealand
🟠
Tongariro Alpine Crossing
North Island, New Zealand
A 19.4 km day hike across an active volcanic landscape — steaming vents, the vivid Emerald Lakes, Red Crater, and Blue Lake. Tongariro doubled as Mordor in the Lord of the Rings films. The crossing traverses three volcanic peaks in a single day, passing between Mount Ngauruhoe's perfect cone and Tongariro's craters.
🔥 Active📏 1,967 m (Ngauruhoe)🥾 Strenuous full-day hike
DOC Info
⚪
White Island (Whakaari)
Bay of Plenty, New Zealand
New Zealand's most active volcano — a low-lying island 48 km offshore with a constantly steaming crater, fumaroles, and a vivid yellow-green sulfur lake. Before the 2019 eruption, boat and helicopter tours landed on the island. Currently viewable by scenic flight and boat tours around the island.
🔥 Active📏 321 m🥾 Scenic flights only (currently)
New Zealand Tourism
East Africa
🦍
Mount Nyiragongo
Virunga, Democratic Republic of Congo
Home to the world's largest persistent lava lake — a churning 200-meter-wide pool of molten rock inside the summit crater. The overnight hike sleeps in shelters on the crater rim with views of the glowing lake below. Located in Virunga National Park alongside mountain gorillas.
🔥 Active📏 3,470 m🥾 Strenuous overnight (guided)
Virunga National Park
🔺
Ol Doinyo Lengai
Tanzania
The only volcano on Earth that erupts natrocarbonatite lava — black, cold (by volcanic standards at 500°C), and fluid enough to flow like water. Known as the "Mountain of God" by the Maasai. Its summit crater contains bizarre lava formations unlike any other volcano. Views over Lake Natron and the Rift Valley.
🔥 Active📏 2,962 m🥾 Strenuous night climb
Tanzania Tourism
Pacific Northwest, United States
💨
Mount St. Helens
Washington, United States
The site of the catastrophic 1980 eruption that blew 400 meters off the summit and devastated 600 square kilometers of forest. The horseshoe-shaped crater and blast zone remain a dramatic testament to volcanic power. The Johnston Ridge Observatory offers direct crater views; the summit climb looks into the steaming dome.
🔥 Active📏 2,549 m🥾 Strenuous (permit required)
USFS Info Seattle Guide
🏔️
Mount Rainier
Washington, United States
The most heavily glaciated peak in the contiguous United States — 25 named glaciers cloak this massive stratovolcano visible from Seattle. Wildflower meadows at Paradise (1,647 m) contrast with the ice-covered summit. A major mountaineering objective; over 10,000 attempt the summit each year.
💤 Dormant📏 4,392 m🥾 Moderate (meadows) / Mountaineering (summit)
NPS Info Seattle Guide
Canary Islands, Spain
⭐
Mount Teide
Tenerife, Spain
Spain's highest peak and the third-tallest volcanic structure in the world (measured from the ocean floor). The caldera surrounding it is a moonscape of lava flows and rock formations. A cable car reaches near the summit; the peak itself requires a free permit. The night sky above is a Starlight Reserve.
💤 Dormant📏 3,718 m🥾 Cable car + easy summit (permit)
Volcano Teide
Ethiopia
🔶
Erta Ale
Danakil Depression, Ethiopia
A shield volcano in one of the hottest and lowest places on Earth hosting a persistent lava lake since at least 1906. The surrounding Danakil Depression — acid pools, salt flats, sulfur springs — is the closest thing to an alien landscape on this planet. Reached by camel caravan with Afar guides.
🔥 Active📏 613 m🥾 Expedition-level (multi-day)

Caribbean
♨️
La Soufrière
Guadeloupe, French West Indies
The highest peak in the Lesser Antilles, rising above tropical rainforest with steaming fumaroles, hot springs, and sulfur vents along the summit trail. The hike passes through cloud forest before emerging onto a lunar landscape of volcanic rock and mist. Accessible as a half-day hike from Basse-Terre.
🔥 Active📏 1,467 m🥾 Moderate jungle hike
Guadeloupe Tourism
