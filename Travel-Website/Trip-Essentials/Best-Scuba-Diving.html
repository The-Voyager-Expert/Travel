Best Scuba Diving

Caribbean & Central America
🇧🇿
Great Blue Hole
Belize
Giant marine sinkhole (300m across, 125m deep) with stalactites, reef sharks, and dramatic wall formations.
👁️ 30m visibility 🌡️ 26–29°C 📅 Apr–Jun 🦈 Reef sharks & stalactites
🇰🇾
Stingray CityGuide
Cayman Islands
Shallow sandbar where dozens of southern stingrays gather — one of the world's most accessible marine encounters.
👁️ 25m visibility 🌡️ 26–29°C 📅 Year-round 🐟 Southern stingrays
🇭🇳
Roatán
Honduras
Part of the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef with vertical wall dives starting at 6m depth and rich macro life.
👁️ 30m visibility 🌡️ 26–29°C 📅 Mar–Sep 🐢 Sea turtles & seahorses
🇲🇽
Cozumel
Mexico
World-famous drift diving along colorful walls with strong currents carrying divers past sea fans and sponges.
👁️ 30–40m visibility 🌡️ 25–29°C 📅 Mar–Jun 🐢 Eagle rays & turtles
🇧🇸
Tiger BeachGuide
Bahamas
Shallow sandy bottom at 6–12m depth where tiger sharks, lemon sharks, and hammerheads congregate reliably.
👁️ 15–25m visibility 🌡️ 24–28°C 📅 Oct–Jan 🦈 Tiger & hammerhead sharks
Southeast Asia & Pacific
🇮🇩
Raja Ampat
Indonesia
Highest recorded marine biodiversity on Earth — over 1,500 fish species and 75% of all known coral species.
👁️ 15–25m visibility 🌡️ 27–30°C 📅 Oct–Apr 🐠 Manta rays & wobbegongs
🇮🇩
Komodo National Park
Indonesia
Strong currents feed nutrient-rich waters with manta cleaning stations, pristine coral, and large pelagic fish.
👁️ 10–25m visibility 🌡️ 22–28°C 📅 Apr–Aug 🐙 Mantas & giant trevally
🇵🇭
Tubbataha ReefGuide
Philippines
UNESCO-listed atoll reef accessible only by liveaboard — pristine walls, sharks, mantas, and pelagics in open ocean.
👁️ 30–45m visibility 🌡️ 27–30°C 📅 Mar–Jun 🦈 Whale sharks & hammerheads
🇹🇭
Similan IslandsGuide
Thailand
Granite boulder formations create swim-throughs and archways with leopard sharks, mantas, and whale sharks seasonally.
👁️ 20–30m visibility 🌡️ 27–29°C 📅 Nov–Apr 🐋 Whale sharks (Feb–Apr)
🇲🇾
Sipadan Island
Malaysia
Oceanic island rising 600m from the seafloor with massive barracuda and jackfish tornadoes, turtles on every dive.
👁️ 20–40m visibility 🌡️ 26–30°C 📅 Apr–Dec 🐢 Barracuda tornadoes & turtles
Indian Ocean & Red Sea
🇪🇬
Ras Mohammed
Egypt
Dramatic wall dives at the tip of the Sinai Peninsula with hard coral gardens and schools of barracuda and jacks.
👁️ 20–40m visibility 🌡️ 22–28°C 📅 Mar–May, Sep–Nov 🐟 Barracuda & Napoleon wrasse
🇪🇬
SS Thistlegorm
Egypt
WWII cargo ship sunk in 1941, resting at 30m — motorcycles, trucks, and train carriages intact inside the holds.
👁️ 15–25m visibility 🌡️ 22–28°C 📅 Mar–May, Sep–Nov 🚢 WWII wreck & resident batfish
🇲🇻
South Ari AtollGuide
Maldives
Year-round whale shark encounters at cleaning stations along the atoll's outer reef, plus manta aggregation sites.
👁️ 20–30m visibility 🌡️ 27–30°C 📅 Year-round 🐋 Whale sharks year-round
🇸🇨
Aldabra Atoll
Seychelles
Remote UNESCO-listed atoll with pristine reefs, manta rays, reef sharks, and one of the world's largest giant tortoise populations on land.
👁️ 20–30m visibility 🌡️ 26–29°C 📅 Mar–May, Oct–Nov 🦈 Reef sharks & mantas
🇿🇦
Sodwana Bay
South Africa
Southernmost coral reefs in Africa with coelacanth sightings in deep caves and seasonal ragged-tooth shark aggregations.
👁️ 10–25m visibility 🌡️ 21–27°C 📅 Nov–May 🦈 Ragged-tooth sharks & coelacanths
Mediterranean & Atlantic
🇵🇹
AzoresGuide
Portugal
Mid-Atlantic volcanic islands with blue sharks, mobula rays, and sperm whale encounters on open-ocean dives.
👁️ 15–30m visibility 🌡️ 17–23°C 📅 Jun–Oct 🦈 Blue sharks & mobula rays
🇲🇹
Gozo
Malta
Limestone caves, arches, and inland sea formations with crystal-clear Mediterranean water and WWII wrecks nearby.
👁️ 30–40m visibility 🌡️ 15–25°C 📅 Apr–Nov 🐙 Caves & WWII wrecks
🇪🇸
Medes Islands
Spain
Protected marine reserve off Costa Brava with dense grouper populations, octopuses, and underwater tunnels.
👁️ 15–25m visibility 🌡️ 13–24°C 📅 May–Oct 🐟 Groupers & barracuda
🇮🇸
Silfra FissureGuide
Iceland
Tectonic fissure between the North American and Eurasian plates with glacial meltwater visibility exceeding 100m.
👁️ 80–100m visibility 🌡️ 2–4°C 📅 Year-round (drysuit) 💎 Glacial clarity & tectonic plates
Oceania
🇦🇺
Great Barrier Reef
Australia
World's largest coral reef system spanning 2,300 km with over 1,500 fish species and 400 types of coral.
👁️ 15–30m visibility 🌡️ 22–29°C 📅 Jun–Oct 🐢 Minke whales (Jun–Jul)
🇦🇺
Ningaloo Reef
Australia
Fringing reef accessible from shore with annual whale shark aggregation (Mar–Jul) and manta rays year-round.
👁️ 15–30m visibility 🌡️ 22–28°C 📅 Mar–Jul (whale sharks) 🐋 Whale sharks & humpbacks
🇵🇬
Kimbe Bay
Papua New Guinea
Volcanic seamounts rising from deep water with 860+ fish species, pristine soft corals, and dolphins on the surface.
👁️ 20–40m visibility 🌡️ 27–30°C 📅 Year-round 🐬 Dolphins & schooling barracuda
🇫🇯
Beqa Lagoon
Fiji
Known as the shark diving capital of the world — up to 8 shark species on a single dive including bulls and tigers.
👁️ 15–25m visibility 🌡️ 25–29°C 📅 Year-round 🦈 Bull & tiger sharks
🇵🇫
Fakarava
French Polynesia
UNESCO biosphere reserve with the famous south pass wall of 700+ grey reef sharks — one of the largest aggregations on Earth.
👁️ 25–40m visibility 🌡️ 26–29°C 📅 Jun–Oct 🦈 700+ grey reef sharks
🇪🇨
Galápagos Islands
Ecuador
Convergence of three ocean currents creates encounters with hammerhead schools, whale sharks, marine iguanas, and mola mola.
👁️ 10–20m visibility 🌡️ 16–24°C 📅 Jun–Nov 🦈 Hammerhead schools & marine iguanas
