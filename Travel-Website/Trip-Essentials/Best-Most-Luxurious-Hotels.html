France
Cheval Blanc Paris
Paris, France
From $1,800/nightThree Michelin Stars
LVMH spent years turning the old La Samaritaine department store into its Paris flagship — a butler assigned to every room, Seine views from nearly all of them. Arnaud Donckele runs the rooftop restaurant, Le Tout-Paris, which holds three Michelin stars, and the Dior Spa two floors down has a 30-metre pool lit entirely from below.

Hôtel du Cap-Eden-Roc
Cap d'Antibes, France
From $1,100/nightSince 1889
A private villa turned hotel in 1889 on 22 acres of pine-shaded gardens at the tip of Cap d'Antibes, with a seawater pool blasted into the rocks at Eden-Roc. A Riviera institution since the 1920s, when Gerald and Sara Murphy's summer-long stay here inspired F. Scott Fitzgerald's Tender Is the Night.

La Réserve Paris
Paris, France
From $1,200/nightTwo Michelin Stars
Behind an unmarked courtyard gate near the Champs-Élysées, this 40-room Haussmann mansion has no public lobby and no sign — you're either expected or you're not. Jérôme Banctel's restaurant Le Gabriel holds two Michelin stars, and the Nescens spa in the basement runs Swiss medical-grade longevity programs.

Italy
Aman Venice
Venice, Italy
From $1,400/nightGrand Canal Palazzo
Aman's Venice outpost occupies one of only eight monumental palazzos still standing on the Grand Canal, with 24 rooms wrapped around original 18th-century Tiepolo ceiling frescoes. Two private gardens back onto the water — genuinely rare in a city built wall-to-wall — and the kitchen will serve any dish, in any room, at any hour.
Official Site 🌐 Venice Guide Map
Belmond Hotel Cipriani
Giudecca, Venice, Italy
From $1,280/nightPrivate Lagoon Launch
79 rooms and suites on the quiet island of Giudecca, reached from St Mark's Square by the hotel's own launch across five minutes of lagoon — the crossing alone does most of the work of leaving Venice's crowds behind. The pool, filled with heated water, is the only one of its size in the city.

Passalacqua
Lake Como, Italy
From $1,500/nightLakefront Terraced Gardens
An 18th-century villa on Lake Como, restored from a private residence into just 24 rooms, with terraced gardens that step straight down to the water and George Clooney's villa in view across the lake. Breakfast, afternoon tea and evening aperitivo all come with the room rate.

Japan
Aman Tokyo
Otemachi, Tokyo, Japan
From $1,400/night21-Course Omakase
84 suites fill the top six floors of the Otemachi office tower in central Tokyo, arranged so every one is a suite. The spa spans two full floors with onsen-style hot baths and a 30-metre pool, and Musashi, the in-house sushi counter, serves a 21-course omakase.

Morocco
Royal Mansour Marrakech
Marrakech, Morocco
From $700/nightCommissioned by the King
King Mohammed VI commissioned this one personally — 53 private riads, each three stories with its own rooftop terrace and plunge pool, connected by underground tunnels so staff can appear and disappear without crossing a courtyard. La Grande Table Marocaine holds two Michelin stars.

Thailand
Capella Bangkok
Bangkok, Thailand
From $700/nightTwo Michelin Stars
101 rooms, suites and riverfront villas along the Chao Phraya, each with a dedicated Culturist — a concierge who curates the entire stay — and a private balcony over the water. Mauro Colagreco's Côte holds two Michelin stars.

United States
Aman New York
New York, United States
From $2,600/nightCrown Building
83 suites fill the Crown Building on Fifth Avenue, and there isn't a standard room in the building — the smallest suite still runs a soaking tub and Aman's signature minimalist design. The three-floor spa has two indoor pools and a jazz lounge, deliberately built to feel nothing like Manhattan.
