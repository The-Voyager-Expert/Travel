Australia
Park Hyatt Sydney
Sydney, Australia
From $900/nightOpera House Views
155 rooms and suites on the edge of Sydney Cove, built so low to the ground that every harbour-facing room sits at eye level with the Opera House shells — not above them, not below, directly across the water. Austria
Hotel Sacher Wien
Vienna, Austria
From $500/nightSince 1876
Directly opposite the Vienna State Opera and in the same family since Franz Sacher's son Eduard opened it in 1876, with 152 rooms furnished in deep reds and dark wood. The Sachertorte was invented here and is still made exclusively for the hotel under a legally protected recipe. The Rotes Bar is one of the most beautifully preserved red-lacquer rooms in any European hotel.

Across Bhutan

The Ritz Paris
Paris, France
From $2,300/nightSince 1898
César Ritz and Auguste Escoffier opened this converted 18th-century townhouse on Place Vendôme in 1898 — among the first hotels in Europe to offer en-suite bathrooms, electricity, and telephones in every room. Coco Chanel lived here for 34 years; Marcel Proust wrote much of In Search of Lost Time in his suite. A four-year renovation completed in 2016 reduced 159 rooms to 142 to allow for larger bathrooms.

From $500/nightFloating Marble Palace

Taj Mahal Palace
Mumbai, India
From $350/nightSince 1903
285 rooms in a building that has faced the Gateway of India and the Arabian Sea since 1903, when industrialist Jamsetji Tata reportedly built it after being denied entry to a whites-only hotel in the city. The original wing's Florentine Gothic exterior — a dome, turrets, and arched windows — is among the most photographed buildings in India. Survived the 2008 Mumbai attacks and reopened 21 months later after a full restoration.

Switzerland
Badrutt's Palace Hotel
St. Moritz, Switzerland
From $900/nightSince 1896
Operating since 1896 and still only open in winter (December to April) and a short summer season (July to September), Badrutt's Palace is the hotel most responsible for transforming St Moritz from a spa village into a global ski resort — the Badrutt family invented the practice of hosting wealthy British guests through the winter season in the 1860s to prove the weather was survivable. The King's Club basement nightclub, opened in 1976, is credited with inventing European alpine après-ski.

New York, United States
From $700/nightBirthplace of the Bloody Mary
238 rooms on East 55th and Fifth Avenue since 1904, built by John Jacob Astor IV — who died on the Titanic eight years later. Butler service is standard in every room — the St. Regis invented the concept of hotel butlers.
