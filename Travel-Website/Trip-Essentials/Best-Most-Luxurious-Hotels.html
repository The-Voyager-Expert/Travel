Australia
Park Hyatt Sydney
Sydney, Australia
From $900/nightOpera House Views
155 rooms and suites on the edge of Sydney Cove, built so low to the ground that every harbour-facing room sits at eye level with the Opera House shells — not above them, not below, directly across the water. The rooftop pool mirrors the same view. One of the few hotels in the world where the landmark outside the window genuinely improves with proximity rather than distance.

Austria
Hotel Sacher Wien
Vienna, Austria
From $500/nightSince 1876
Directly opposite the Vienna State Opera and in the same family since Franz Sacher's son Eduard opened it in 1876, with 152 rooms furnished in deep reds and dark wood. The Sachertorte was invented here and is still made exclusively for the hotel under a legally protected recipe. The Rotes Bar is one of the most beautifully preserved red-lacquer rooms in any European hotel.

Bhutan
Amankora
Across Bhutan
From $1,800/nightFive Lodges, One Kingdom
Five private lodges across the Paro, Thimphu, Punakha, Gangtey, and Bumthang valleys — each capped at 24 guests. The standard journey moves east across the country over seven to ten days, with guided access to Tiger's Nest, Punakha Dzong, and restricted monasteries that require government permits Aman handles entirely. Bhutan's high daily fee effectively makes this the most regulated luxury travel experience in the world.

France
Cheval Blanc Paris
Paris, France
From $1,800/nightThree Michelin Stars
LVMH spent years turning the old La Samaritaine department store into its Paris flagship — a butler assigned to every room, Seine views from nearly all of them. Arnaud Donckele runs the rooftop restaurant, Le Tout-Paris, which holds three Michelin stars, and the Dior Spa two floors down has a 30-metre pool lit entirely from below.

Hôtel du Cap-Eden-Roc
Cap d'Antibes, France
From $1,100/nightSince 1889
A private villa turned hotel in 1889 on 22 acres of pine-shaded gardens at the tip of Cap d'Antibes, with a seawater pool blasted into the rocks at Eden-Roc. A Riviera institution since the 1920s, when Gerald and Sara Murphy's summer-long stay here inspired F. Scott Fitzgerald's Tender Is the Night.

La Réserve Paris
Paris, France
From $1,200/nightTwo Michelin Stars
Behind an unmarked courtyard gate near the Champs-Élysées, this 40-room Haussmann mansion has no public lobby and no sign — you're either expected or you're not. Jérôme Banctel's restaurant Le Gabriel holds two Michelin stars, and the Nescens spa in the basement runs Swiss medical-grade longevity programs.

Germany
Hotel Adlon Kempinski
Berlin, Germany
From $600/nightBeside the Brandenburg Gate
Rebuilt in 1997 a hundred meters from the Brandenburg Gate, on the exact footprint of the 1907 original where Kaiser Wilhelm II, Charlie Chaplin, and Greta Garbo all stayed. 385 rooms, with the Lorenz Adlon Esszimmer earning two Michelin stars. The elephant fountain in the central atrium is a replica of the one Einstein photographed here in 1921.

Greece
Canaves Oia Epitome
Oia, Santorini, Greece
From $1,200/nightPrivate Caldera Infinity Pools
Thirteen suites carved into the caldera cliff at Oia, each with its own private infinity pool hanging over the volcanic drop. Named World's Best Hotel by Travel + Leisure readers in recent years. Unlike most Santorini hotels that cluster rooms around a shared pool, every suite here is entirely private — the volcanic face behind acts as a natural heat sink and keeps the stone walls cool through summer.

Hong Kong
The Peninsula Hong Kong
Tsim Sha Tsui, Hong Kong
From $700/nightSince 1928
The grande dame of Hong Kong since 1928, 300 rooms overlooking Victoria Harbour, and the longest-running Rolls-Royce fleet in the world — 14 custom Phantom Extended Wheelbase models in Peninsula green that meet every arriving guest. The rooftop Felix restaurant was designed by Philippe Starck with floor-to-ceiling glass walls on three sides, and the lobby afternoon tea is one of the most reliably-attended rituals in the city.

India
Taj Lake Palace
Udaipur, India
From $500/nightFloating Marble Palace
Built in 1746 as the summer pleasure palace of Maharana Jagat Singh II on a four-acre island in Lake Pichola, converted to a hotel in 1963. 66 rooms and suites of white marble, every one facing the water, with the City Palace visible on the ridge across the lake. The only way in or out is by hotel boat. Used as the villain's base in the James Bond film Octopussy in 1983.

Italy
Aman Venice
Venice, Italy
From $1,400/nightGrand Canal Palazzo
Aman's Venice outpost occupies one of only eight monumental palazzos still standing on the Grand Canal, with 24 rooms wrapped around original 18th-century Tiepolo ceiling frescoes. Two private gardens back onto the water — genuinely rare in a city built wall-to-wall — and the kitchen will serve any dish, in any room, at any hour.

Mexico
Las Ventanas al Paraíso, A Rosewood Resort
Los Cabos, Mexico
From $1,100/nightButler & Fireplace in Every Suite
71 suites along a private stretch of Sea of Cortés, each assigned a personal butler and fitted with a wood-burning fireplace — the desert cools sharply after dark and the fire is used. The hotel operates its own tequila blending program and a saltwater aquarium stocked with species endemic to the Cortés.

Peru
Belmond Sanctuary Lodge
Aguas Calientes, Peru
From $900/nightOnly Hotel at the Ruins Gate
31 rooms in the only hotel permitted to operate at the entrance to Machu Picchu itself — not in Aguas Calientes below, but at the gate of the citadel. There is no road: the only way in is by train from Cusco, then a bus up the switchbacks. Guests enter the site at dawn before day-visitor access opens and stay until after the crowds leave at dusk, giving each side of the visit to themselves.

Portugal
Six Senses Douro Valley
Lamego, Portugal
From $600/nightWine Country Manor
A 19th-century manor house ringed by vine terraces above the Douro, converted into 57 suites with a spa that folds local grape skins, wine lees, and port into its treatment menu. The cellar hosts the Douro Boys — five of the valley's most respected small producers — for twice-weekly tastings. The Douro train line runs directly past the property; Pinhão, the most photogenic river bend in Portugal, is 15 minutes away.

Seychelles
North Island
North Island, Seychelles
From $5,000/night11 Villas, One Island
Eleven villas on a private island in the Indian Ocean, each facing a different beach, with one of the most significant tortoise and seabird conservation programs of any private resort — giant tortoises wander the grounds freely. The villa interiors are built from reclaimed teak and ship timber; the only other structure on the island is a research station. Barack and Michelle Obama stayed here after his presidency ended.

Singapore
Raffles Singapore
Singapore
From $900/nightSince 1887
115 suites in the restored 1887 colonial building, each with a private parlour and bedroom — standard rooms were removed entirely in 2019. The Singapore Sling was invented at the Long Bar in 1915 by bartender Ngiam Tong Boon, and the original recipe is still used. The Lawn Butler greets every guest by name at the driveway gate. William Somerset Maugham described this as "standing for all the fables of the exotic East."

South Africa
Singita Ebony Lodge
Sabi Sand, South Africa
From $2,000/nightPrivate Game Reserve
Six suites and six cottages in the private Sabi Sand concession that borders Kruger National Park — the same unfenced ecosystem, with game drives that almost always produce lions and leopards because the reserve's density of both is among the highest in Africa. The wine cellar holds over 6,000 South African bottles and the sommelier runs tastings on request between drives. Two game drives daily are included.

Spain
Mandarin Oriental Ritz, Madrid
Madrid, Spain
From $700/nightTwo Michelin Stars
The 1910 Ritz designed by César Ritz himself, restored over three years and reopened under Mandarin Oriental in 2021 with 153 rooms. The Palm Court café sits under a 1907 Belle Époque stained-glass ceiling imported from Belgium. Quique Dacosta's Deessa restaurant holds two Michelin stars. The Prado Museum is a four-minute walk; the hotel has held an arrangement with it since opening that allows guests private access on closed days.

Switzerland
Badrutt's Palace Hotel
St. Moritz, Switzerland
From $900/nightSince 1896
Operating since 1896 and still only open in winter (December to April) and a short summer season (July to September), Badrutt's Palace is the hotel most responsible for transforming St Moritz from a spa village into a global ski resort — the Badrutt family invented the practice of hosting wealthy British guests through the winter season in the 1860s to prove the weather was survivable. The King's Club basement nightclub, opened in 1976, is credited with inventing European alpine après-ski.

Dubai, United Arab Emirates

United Kingdom
Claridge's
Mayfair, London, United Kingdom
From $900/nightSince 1856
210 rooms and suites in the Art Deco landmark on Brook Street, Mayfair, opened 1856 and in continuous operation as London's de facto royal guesthouse — 57 of its rooms were designated as Yugoslavian royal territory after World War II so the Yugoslav crown prince could be born on sovereign soil. Daniel Humm's Davies and Brook holds two Michelin stars. The Royal Suite has been used by Princess Grace of Monaco, Katharine Hepburn, and multiple heads of state for extended stays.
