Best Mountains & Rock Formations

Argentina
Fitz Roy (Cerro Chaltén)
El Chaltén, Argentina
A jagged granite spire in Los Glaciares National Park on the Argentine-Chilean Patagonian border. Notoriously difficult to climb, it towers over the trekking hub of El Chaltén.
Wikipedia 🌐 Buenos Aires Guide Map
Australia
Uluru (Ayers Rock)
Northern Territory, Australia
UNESCO Site
A massive sandstone monolith rising from the desert of Australia's Red Centre, sacred to the Anangu people. A UNESCO World Heritage site, it glows red at sunrise and sunset.
Wikipedia Parks Australia Map
Kata Tjuta (The Olgas)
Northern Territory, Australia
UNESCO Site
A group of large domed rock formations near Uluru in Australia's Northern Territory, sacred to the Anangu. Part of the same UNESCO park, they are explored via the Valley of the Winds walk.
Wikipedia Parks Australia Map
Bungle Bungles (Purnululu)
Western Australia, Australia
UNESCO SiteKnown Since 1983
A range of orange-and-black-striped sandstone beehive domes in the remote Kimberley region of Western Australia. A UNESCO World Heritage site accessible by 4WD or helicopter, the domes were largely unknown outside the region until 1983.
Wikipedia WA Parks Map
Canada
Mount Robson
British Columbia, Canada
Highest Peak in the Canadian Rockies
The highest peak in the Canadian Rockies, rising steeply in a British Columbia provincial park. The Berg Lake trail leads to glacier-fed lakes beneath its towering north face.
Wikipedia BC Parks Map
Mount Assiniboine
British Columbia / Alberta, Canada
Matterhorn of the Rockies
A pyramidal peak on the BC-Alberta border, often called the Matterhorn of the Rockies for its shape. Its remote provincial park is reached only by a long hike or helicopter.
Wikipedia BC Parks Map
Chile
Torres del Paine
Magallanes, Chile
W & O Treks
A range of granite towers and horns in Chilean Patagonia, centerpiece of Torres del Paine National Park. Its multi-day W and O treks are among the world's most celebrated hikes.
Wikipedia 🌐 Santiago Guide Map
Osorno Volcano
Los Lagos, Chile
Ski Resort
A near-perfect snow-capped volcanic cone in Chile's Lake District, often compared to Mount Fuji. It rises above Lake Llanquihue and offers skiing and views across the surrounding lakes.
Wikipedia Map
China
Zhangjiajie Pillars
Hunan, China
Thousands of towering quartz-sandstone pillars in Zhangjiajie National Forest Park in Hunan, China. Often shrouded in mist, they helped inspire the floating mountains in Avatar.
Wikipedia 🌐 Zhangjiajie Guide Map
Huangshan (Yellow Mountains)
Anhui, China
UNESCO Site
A UNESCO-listed granite range in Anhui, China, famed for jagged peaks, twisted pines, and seas of cloud. Long a subject of Chinese painting and poetry, it is reached by cable car and stone stairways.
Wikipedia Map
Egypt
Mount Sinai (Jebel Musa)
South Sinai, Egypt
A peak in Egypt's South Sinai, traditionally identified with the biblical mountain where Moses received the commandments. Many hike it overnight to watch sunrise from the summit.
Wikipedia 🌐 Cairo Guide Map
France
Mont Blanc
Chamonix, France / Courmayeur, Italy
The highest mountain in the Alps and in Western Europe, on the France-Italy border. A birthplace of mountaineering, it is circled by the classic Tour du Mont Blanc trek.
Wikipedia Chamonix Tourism Map
Piton de la Fournaise
Réunion, France
A shield volcano on Réunion Island in the Indian Ocean, one of the most active volcanoes on Earth. Frequent eruptions within its caldera draw hikers to viewpoints along the rim.
Wikipedia Réunion Tourism Map
Greece
Meteora
Thessaly, Greece
UNESCO Site
A complex of enormous sandstone pillars rising from the Thessaly plain in central Greece, their summits bearing six active Eastern Orthodox monasteries. A UNESCO dual World Heritage site combining natural and cultural significance.
Wikipedia Visit Meteora Map
Iceland
Kirkjufell
Snæfellsnes, Iceland
A steep, cone-shaped peak on the Snæfellsnes peninsula in western Iceland, rising beside a coastal waterfall. Often called the most photographed mountain in the country.
Wikipedia 🌐 Reykjavik Guide Map
Italy
Tre Cime di Lavaredo
Dolomites, Italy
UNESCO Site
Three distinctive rock towers in the Sexten Dolomites of northern Italy, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Their sheer walls are iconic in the Alps and ringed by a popular loop trail.
Wikipedia Dolomites Tourism Map
Seceda Ridgeline
Val Gardena, Dolomites, Italy
Cable Car Access
A sawtooth ridgeline above Val Gardena in the South Tyrol Dolomites, reached by cable car from Ortisei. Its jagged silhouette over green alpine meadows is a signature Dolomite view.
Wikipedia Val Gardena Tourism Map
Mount Etna
Catania, Sicily, Italy
UNESCO SiteTallest in Europe
The tallest active volcano in Europe, dominating eastern Sicily above Catania. Among the world's most active volcanoes, it is a UNESCO site with frequent eruptions and lava flows.
Wikipedia 🌐 Sicily Guide Map
Japan
Mount Fuji
Shizuoka / Yamanashi, Japan
UNESCO SiteHighest Peak in Japan
Japan's highest peak, an active volcano with a near-symmetrical cone that is a national symbol and UNESCO World Heritage site. It draws climbers during a short summer season.
Wikipedia Mt Fuji Climbing 🌐 Tokyo Guide Map
Jordan
Wadi Rum
Aqaba, Jordan
UNESCO Site
A desert valley of sandstone mountains and cliffs in southern Jordan, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Its dramatic landscape draws climbers, hikers, and visitors to Bedouin desert camps.
Wikipedia Visit Jordan 🌐 Petra Guide Map
Madagascar
Tsingy de Bemaraha
Melaky, Madagascar
UNESCO SiteSpires to 120m
A vast limestone plateau in western Madagascar eroded into a dense forest of razor-sharp karst needles, some reaching 120 meters. A UNESCO World Heritage site accessible only by guided traverse via bridges and ladders.
Wikipedia Map
Malaysia
Mount Kinabalu
Sabah, Malaysian Borneo
UNESCO SiteHighest in SE Asia
The highest peak in Southeast Asia's Malay Archipelago, in a UNESCO-listed national park in Sabah, Borneo. Its granite summit is reached by a popular two-day guided climb.
Wikipedia Sabah Tourism Map
Namibia
Spitzkoppe
Erongo, Namibia
1,728m PeakSan Rock Art
A group of bold granite inselbergs rising from the Namib Desert plains in northwest Namibia, the tallest reaching 1,728 meters. Known as the Matterhorn of Namibia, the boulders shelter ancient San rock paintings and make a dramatic desert campsite.
Wikipedia Namibia Tourism Map
Nepal
Mount Everest (Sagarmatha / Chomolungma)
Nepal / Tibet, China
Highest Peak on EarthFirst Summited 1953
The highest mountain on Earth above sea level, on the Nepal-Tibet border in the Himalayas. First summited in 1953, it anchors the famous Everest Base Camp trek.
Wikipedia Map
Machhapuchhre (Fishtail Peak)
Annapurna, Nepal
Sacred PeakUnclimbed Summit
A striking twin-summited peak in the Annapurna Himalayas near Pokhara, named for its fishtail shape. Considered sacred, it has never been officially climbed to the summit.
Wikipedia Map
Ama Dablam
Khumbu, Nepal
Everest Base Camp Trek
A dramatic peak in the Khumbu region of the Nepal Himalayas, prized for its sharp ridges and steep faces. Its distinctive profile is a highlight of the Everest Base Camp trek.
Wikipedia Map
New Zealand
Milford Sound (Mitre Peak)
Fiordland, New Zealand
Fiordland National ParkMilford Track
A fjord in Fiordland National Park on New Zealand's South Island, framed by the sheer Mitre Peak. Carved by glaciers and fed by waterfalls, it is a highlight of the Milford Track.

Aoraki / Mount Cook
Canterbury, New Zealand
New Zealand's Highest Peak
The highest mountain in New Zealand, a glaciated peak in the Southern Alps of the South Island. Its national park offers accessible valley walks such as the Hooker Valley track.
Wikipedia 🌐 Queenstown Guide Map
Norway
Trolltunga
Odda, Norway
Strenuous Day Hike
A flat rock slab jutting horizontally from a cliff high above Ringedalsvatnet lake in western Norway. Reached only by a long, strenuous day hike, it is a famous fjord-country viewpoint.
Wikipedia Visit Norway Map
Preikestolen (Pulpit Rock)
Forsand, Norway
Half-Day Hike
A flat-topped cliff that drops sheer to the Lysefjord in Ryfylke, Norway. One of the country's most visited natural landmarks, reached by a well-trodden half-day hike.
Wikipedia Visit Norway Map
Pakistan
K2 (Mount Godwin-Austen)
Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan / Xinjiang, China
World's 2nd-Highest PeakSavage Mountain
The second-highest mountain in the world, in the Karakoram Range on the China-Pakistan border. Steeper and far deadlier to climb than Everest, it is nicknamed the Savage Mountain.
Wikipedia Map
Peru
Vinicunca (Rainbow Mountain)
Cusco, Peru
Above 5,000m Elevation
A high Andean ridge near Cusco banded in vivid colors from layered mineral sediments. Sitting above 5,000 meters, it is reached by a demanding high-altitude day hike from Cusco.
Wikipedia 🌐 Cusco Guide Map
Rwanda
Virunga Volcanoes
Rwanda / Uganda / DR Congo
8 VolcanoesMountain Gorilla Refuge
A chain of eight volcanoes along the borders of Rwanda, Uganda, and DR Congo in the Albertine Rift. Their forested slopes are a last refuge of the endangered mountain gorilla.
Wikipedia Visit Rwanda Map
South Africa
Table Mountain
Cape Town, South Africa
Cable Car Access
A flat-topped sandstone massif overlooking Cape Town, forming the city's defining backdrop. A rotating cable car and numerous trails lead to its broad summit plateau.
Wikipedia Table Mountain Map

Matterhorn (Cervino)
Zermatt, Switzerland / Cervinia, Italy
Swiss-Italian Border
An almost symmetrical pyramidal peak on the Swiss-Italian border in the Pennine Alps. One of the most recognizable mountains in the world, it rises above the resort town of Zermatt.

Eiger North Face
Bernese Oberland, Switzerland
Jungfrau Railway Tunnel
A peak in the Bernese Alps whose immense north face is one of the most notorious climbs in mountaineering history. The Jungfrau Railway tunnels through the mountain to Jungfraujoch.

Tanzania
Mount Kilimanjaro
Kilimanjaro Region, Tanzania
Highest Peak in AfricaNo Technical Climbing Required
The highest mountain in Africa and the tallest free-standing mountain in the world, a dormant volcano in northern Tanzania. Its summit is reachable by trekkers without technical climbing.
Wikipedia Tanzania Tourism Map
Turkey
Cappadocia Fairy Chimneys
Göreme, Nevşehir, Turkey
UNESCO World Heritage Site
Thousands of volcanic tuff pillars — capped by harder rock — eroded across the valleys of Cappadocia in central Turkey, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Many were hollowed out as homes, churches, and underground cities over centuries.
Wikipedia Göreme Info Map
United States
Grand Teton
Wyoming, United States
Highest Peak in the Teton Range
The highest peak of the Teton Range in Wyoming, rising abruptly from the valley floor without foothills. Its sharp granite summit is a landmark of Grand Teton National Park.
Wikipedia NPS Info 🌐 Yellowstone Guide Map
Monument Valley
Arizona / Utah, United States
Navajo Nation
A cluster of towering red sandstone buttes on the Arizona-Utah border within the Navajo Nation. Its formations are icons of the American Southwest, seen in countless films.

Devils Tower
Wyoming, United States

A flat-topped igneous rock tower rising abruptly above the plains of northeastern Wyoming. The first United States national monument, its fluted columns are a magnet for rock climbers.

Half Dome
Yosemite, California, United States
Cable Route Hike
A granite dome in Yosemite National Park with a sheer near-vertical face rising above the valley. Its cable-assisted summit route is one of the park's most demanding day hikes.

Denali (Mount McKinley)
Alaska, United States
Highest Peak in North AmericaGreatest Vertical Rise
The highest peak in North America, at the heart of Denali National Park in Alaska. Measured from base to summit, its vertical rise is among the greatest of any mountain on land.

Washington, United States
Highest Peak in Washington150 km Wonderland Trail
A glacier-clad active volcano and the highest peak in Washington's Cascade Range. It looms over the Seattle region and is circled by the 150-kilometer Wonderland Trail.

Page, Arizona, United States
Navajo Nation LandGuided Tours Only
A flowing sandstone slot canyon on Navajo Nation land near Page, Arizona, carved by flash floods into wave-like passages of red and orange rock. Upper and Lower Antelope are accessible only on guided Navajo tours.

Bryce Canyon Hoodoos
Utah, United States
World's Largest Hoodoo Concentration
The world's largest concentration of hoodoos — tall limestone spires eroded into orange, red, and white columns — fills the amphitheaters of Bryce Canyon National Park in southern Utah.

Mount Roraima
Venezuela / Brazil / Guyana
Highest TepuiInspired The Lost World
The highest of the tepui table mountains, where Venezuela, Brazil, and Guyana meet in the Gran Sabana. Its sheer-walled plateau, home to unique species, inspired The Lost World.

Canaima, Venezuela
UNESCO World Heritage SiteTallest Waterfall on Earth
A vast tepui in Venezuela's Canaima National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site. From its edge plunges Angel Falls, the tallest uninterrupted waterfall on Earth.

Ha Long Bay Karst
Quảng Ninh, Vietnam
UNESCO Site
A seascape of roughly 1,600 limestone karst towers rising from the Gulf of Tonkin in northern Vietnam, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The formations — riddled with caves and grottoes — are explored by overnight junk cruises.
Wikipedia Vietnam Tourism Map
