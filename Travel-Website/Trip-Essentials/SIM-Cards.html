📡 Option Types
📶 eSIM Most flexible
A digital SIM activated via app before you leave. No physical swap, no lost card. Works on unlocked iPhone XS and later, and most Android phones from 2019+.
Cost ~$5–$30 for 7–14 days of data
Requires Unlocked phone with eSIM support
Best for Short trips, multi-country, last-minute setup
🛒 Local SIM Cheapest data
Physical SIM bought at the destination airport, phone shop, or convenience store. Usually the lowest per-GB cost.
Cost ~$5–$20 for unlimited or large data
Requires Unlocked phone, sometimes passport
Best for Longer stays, budget trips, high data use
📱 Carrier Plan Easiest
International add-on from your home carrier. No setup — your number works. Most expensive option per GB. Daily pass or included tier depending on carrier and plan.
Cost $0 (included) or $10–$15/day
Requires Nothing extra — uses your existing plan
Best for Short trips, work calls on your number
📶 eSIM Providers
Airalo
Marketplace — 200+ countries
Largest eSIM marketplace. Buy country or regional packages in the app. Regional plans cover EU, Asia, or global zones — useful for multi-country trips. Activate instantly; QR code or app install. Plans from ~$5.
Nomad
Marketplace — 170+ countries
Similar to Airalo. Often competitive on Europe and Asia pricing. Regional bundles available. App-based activation. Worth comparing prices against Airalo for your destination.
Holafly
Unlimited data — 160+ countries
Unlimited data plans priced per day. More expensive than data-capped options but removes the worry of running out. No hotspot on most plans. Best for heavy streamers or people who don't want to track usage.
T-Mobile
Carrier eSIM — Magenta and above
T-Mobile Magenta, Go5G, and higher plans include 5 GB of high-speed international data in 210+ countries, then unlimited 2G speeds. No daily charge. Works on eSIM or physical SIM — no separate activation needed.
📱 US Carrier International Plans
T-Mobile Magenta / Go5G
Included — 210+ countries
5 GB high-speed then unlimited 2G (256 kbps) in 210+ countries. Unlimited texting, $0.25/min calls. No daily charge. Go5G Plus and above include more high-speed data. Best carrier option if you have it — no action required.
AT&T International Day Pass
$10/day when used — 210+ countries
Charged only on days you use your phone internationally. Unlimited calls, texts, and data (slowed after 2 GB/day) for $10 per 24-hour period. Add the pass from the AT&T app before departing — it's not automatic.
Verizon TravelPass
$10/day when used — 210+ countries
Same model as AT&T — charged per day you use it. 2 GB high-speed then unlimited throttled data. Unlimited calls and texts. Enable in the My Verizon app before you leave.
💡 Tips
🔒 Check that your phone is unlocked before leaving. Most iPhones purchased outright or after 12 months of payments are unlocked. Call your carrier or check Settings → General → About → Carrier Lock (iPhone).
✈️ Enable Airplane Mode immediately after takeoff, then re-enable WiFi only. This prevents accidental roaming charges during the flight and while you're setting up your eSIM or local SIM on arrival.
🗺️ Download offline maps before you leave — Google Maps or Apple Maps let you save entire regions. This eliminates data use for navigation and works when you have no signal.
📞 Enable WiFi Calling on your phone (Settings → Phone → WiFi Calling on iPhone). When connected to hotel or airport WiFi, you can make and receive calls on your regular number at no charge.
💬 WhatsApp, iMessage, Signal, and FaceTime Audio all work over WiFi with no data plan. Use these for messaging and voice calls when you have a WiFi connection.
📶 Set up your eSIM before you leave — you need a data connection to activate it. If you wait until arrival, you'll need airport WiFi to complete the activation.
⚙️ On iPhone, go to Settings → Cellular → set your data plan explicitly so the eSIM or local SIM handles data and your home SIM handles calls only. This prevents accidental roaming on your primary number.
🌍 By Region
🇪🇺 Europe
eSIM works reliably across all countries. Local SIMs from any EU carrier roam free across the EU — buy one in the first country and use it everywhere. Airport kiosks in most major hubs. Airalo's Europe regional plan is a strong option.
🇯🇵 Japan
eSIM is the easiest option — IIJmio, Airalo, and Nomad all work well. Physical tourist SIMs are sold at Narita/Haneda vending machines and convenience stores (data-only; calls require a separate plan). Passport required for physical SIM registration.
🌏 Southeast Asia
Local SIMs are extremely cheap and widely available at airports. Thailand (AIS, DTAC), Singapore (Singtel, StarHub), and Vietnam all sell tourist SIMs at the arrivals hall. Data rates among the lowest in the world. eSIM also works in most major cities.
🇬🇧 UK
No longer in the EU — your EU SIM won't roam there free. Get a separate plan. eSIM works on all major UK networks (EE, O2, Vodafone). Three UK physical SIMs offer strong global roaming if you're also visiting other countries after the UK.
🌎 Middle East / Gulf
eSIM works in UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Israel (Airalo, Holafly). Physical SIM purchase requires passport registration and can take time at the counter. eSIM is the practical choice. Some VoIP apps are blocked in UAE — check ahead.
🌎 Latin America
eSIM coverage is expanding but variable — verify country support before departure. Local SIMs are easy in Mexico, Colombia, Argentina, and Brazil. T-Mobile's international data works in most countries but speeds vary. Airalo's Latin America regional plan covers multiple countries.
🇦🇺 Australia / NZ
eSIM works on Telstra, Optus, and Vodafone AU networks. Airalo and Nomad both offer Australia plans. Woolworths Mobile and Amaysim sell cheap prepaid SIMs at major supermarkets if you prefer physical. New Zealand options are thinner on eSIM — physical SIM from Spark or One NZ is reliable.
