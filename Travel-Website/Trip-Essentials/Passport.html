The 6-month validity rule. Many countries — including most of Europe, all of Southeast Asia, and large parts of the Middle East — require your passport to be valid for at least 6 months beyond your entry date. Airlines enforce this before you board. If your passport has less than 6 months remaining, renew before you travel internationally — regardless of how long your trip actually is.

Key Things to Know
📅
Apply 9–12 months before your next international trip. Routine processing runs 8–10 weeks door-to-door. Don't wait until 6 months out — if demand spikes, you may not make it in time without paying expedited fees.
✈️
The 6-month rule catches people off guard. Your passport may technically be valid, but if it has less than 6 months remaining after your return date, many countries will turn you away at the border — and airlines will deny boarding.
👧
Child passports expire in 5 years. Both parents (or legal guardians) must appear in person to apply. If one parent can't attend, they must provide a notarized Form DS-3053 Statement of Consent.
📄
Count your blank pages before every trip. Many countries require at least 2–4 blank pages. If you're running low and don't want a new passport, you can't add pages anymore — that program was discontinued. Renew instead.
🌍
Some visas require a full blank page. Countries like India, Brazil, and South Africa require a full blank page just for their visa stamp. Factor this in before trips that need visas stamped in-person.
🔒
Make a copy before every trip. Store a photo of your passport data page in your email or cloud storage. If your passport is stolen abroad, having the number and issue date speeds up emergency replacement at the nearest US embassy.
🏥
Register with STEP before every international trip. The Smart Traveler Enrollment Program at step.state.gov lets the US Embassy contact you in an emergency and notifies family if something happens abroad. Free and takes 2 minutes.
⚠️
Third-party appointment services are not the State Dept. Any site charging a fee to "book your passport appointment" is a third party. Appointments are free at travel.state.gov and by calling 877-487-2778 directly.
📷
No AI-edited or digitally altered photos. The State Department actively screens all submissions for AI-generated and edited images. Submit an original, unedited photo taken within the last 6 months — no filters, no software retouching, no AI tools. Altered photos are rejected.
