Pink Sands Beach
Harbour Island, Bahamas
Three miles of pale pink sand — the colour comes from microscopic coral organisms (Foraminifera) with bright pink shells. The sand stretches 50–100 feet wide along the Atlantic side. One of the most distinctive beaches on Earth.

A white-sand beach on an uninhabited island where friendly wild pigs swim out to greet arriving boats. Crystal-clear Exuma water. Accessible only by boat — one of the most unique beach experiences in the world.
