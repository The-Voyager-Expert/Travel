Argentina
Mendoza
Western Argentina
A high-altitude desert region in the Andean foothills, irrigated by mountain snowmelt and internationally recognized as the world's leading source of rich, deeply colored Malbec.

Australia
Barossa Valley
South Australia
A warm valley northeast of Adelaide, one of Australia's oldest wine regions with some of the world's oldest surviving vines, famous for full-bodied Shiraz and dry Eden Valley Riesling.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours Hotels Map
Austria
Wachau
Lower Austria
A UNESCO-listed stretch of terraced vineyards along the Danube, celebrated for peppery, mineral Grüner Veltliner and dry Riesling grown on ancient stone-walled terraces.
Wikipedia Wine Tours Hotels 🌐 Vienna Guide Map
Chile
Central Valley
Maipo · Rapel · Colchagua, Central Chile
Chile's viticultural heartland between the Andes and the coastal range, producing structured Cabernet Sauvignon and Carmenère, a Bordeaux grape that found a signature home here.
Wikipedia Wine Tours 🌐 Santiago Guide Map
France
Bordeaux
Gironde, Southwest France
France's largest fine-wine region, centered on the Gironde estuary and known for structured Cabernet-Merlot blends, sweet Sauternes, and its Left Bank and Right Bank classified châteaux.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Bordeaux Guide Map
Burgundy
Bourgogne, Eastern France
A narrow strip of limestone slopes producing single-vineyard Pinot Noir and Chardonnay, where the Burgundian climat system finely divides the land into hundreds of individually named plots.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours Map
Champagne
Marne & Aube, Northeast France
The cool northern region where the traditional method of secondary bottle fermentation was perfected, producing the world's benchmark sparkling wine from chalky soils around Reims and Épernay.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours Hotels Map
Rhône Valley
Southeastern France
A long corridor following the Rhône river, split between steep northern granite terraces of pure Syrah and warmer southern gravels where Grenache-led blends like Châteauneuf-du-Pape dominate.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Lyon Guide Map
Alsace
Grand Est, Northeast France
A sheltered strip along the eastern foothills of the Vosges mountains, marked by German-influenced aromatic whites and a picturesque Wine Route linking half-timbered villages like Riquewihr.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Strasbourg Guide 🌐 Colmar Guide Map
Provence
Southeastern France
A sun-drenched Mediterranean region and the world's benchmark for dry rosé, where Grenache and Cinsault yield pale pink wines across landscapes of vineyards, olive groves, and lavender.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Aix-en-Provence Guide 🌐 Nice Guide 🌐 Marseille Guide Map
Loire Valley
Central-Western France
France's longest wine region, tracing the Loire river past Renaissance châteaux and yielding versatile Chenin Blanc, crisp Sancerre Sauvignon Blanc, and light Cabernet Franc reds.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours Map
Germany
Mosel
Rhineland-Palatinate, Western Germany
A region of steep slate slopes on tight bends of the Mosel river, producing delicate, low-alcohol Riesling with vivid acidity, ranging from bone-dry to intensely sweet late-harvest wines.
Wikipedia Wine Tours Hotels Map
Rheingau
Hesse, Western Germany
A compact region on south-facing slopes where the Rhine turns west, historically influential in German Riesling and also known for Spätburgunder, the German name for Pinot Noir.
Wikipedia Hotels Map
Italy
Tuscany
Central Italy
A region of rolling hills and cypress-lined roads whose Sangiovese grape underpins Chianti, Brunello di Montalcino, and the Cabernet-based Super Tuscans of the coast.
Wikipedia Wine Tours Hotels 🌐 Florence Guide 🌐 Siena Guide Map
Piedmont
Northwestern Italy
A hilly region at the foot of the Alps, famed for the powerful, tannic Nebbiolo wines of Barolo and Barbaresco in the Langhe, alongside sweet sparkling Moscato d'Asti.
Wikipedia Wine Tours Hotels 🌐 Turin Guide Map
Veneto
Northeastern Italy
A high-volume region stretching from Lake Garda to the Adriatic, source of sparkling Prosecco from Glera, the rich Amarone made from dried Corvina grapes, and crisp Soave whites.
Wikipedia Wine Tours 🌐 Verona Guide 🌐 Venice Guide Map
Sicily
Southern Italy
A Mediterranean island where volcanic Etna vineyards grow elegant Nerello Mascalese reds, while the warmer lowlands ripen robust Nero d'Avola and the sweet Zibibbo of Pantelleria.
Wikipedia Etna Wine Tours 🌐 Sicily Guide Map
New Zealand
Marlborough
South Island, New Zealand
New Zealand's largest wine region, at the sunny northern tip of the South Island, whose intensely aromatic, zesty Sauvignon Blanc brought the country to global attention.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Wellington Guide 🌐 Queenstown Guide Map
Central Otago
South Island, New Zealand
The world's southernmost commercial wine region, set among dramatic mountains near Queenstown, with a continental alpine climate that yields concentrated, structured Pinot Noir.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Queenstown Guide Map
Portugal
Douro Valley
Northern Portugal
One of the world's oldest demarcated wine regions, a dramatic landscape of terraced schist slopes above the Douro river that produces fortified Port as well as dry Douro table wines.
Wikipedia Wine Tours Hotels 🌐 Porto Guide 🌐 Lisbon Guide Map
South Africa
Stellenbosch & Cape Winelands
Western Cape, South Africa
The historic core of South African wine near Cape Town, set among granite mountains, and home to Pinotage, a local crossing of Pinot Noir and Cinsault, alongside widely planted Chenin Blanc.
Wikipedia Wine Tours Hotels Map
Spain
Rioja
La Rioja & Basque Country, Northern Spain
Spain's most celebrated red-wine region, sheltered by the Cantabrian mountains along the Ebro valley, where Tempranillo is aged in oak through the Crianza, Reserva, and Gran Reserva tiers.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours Hotels 🌐 San Sebastián Guide Map
Priorat
Catalonia, Northeast Spain
A small, mountainous region defined by its distinctive licorella slate soils, producing concentrated, powerful Garnacha and Cariñena reds from old vines on steep terraces.
Wikipedia Wine Tours 🌐 Barcelona Guide Map
Jerez (Sherry Triangle)
Andalusia, Southern Spain
The triangle of towns on Andalusia's chalky albariza soils that produces sherry, a fortified wine aged through the solera system in styles from bone-dry Fino to sweet Pedro Ximénez.
Wikipedia Official Site Sherry Tours 🌐 Seville Guide Map
United States
Napa Valley
California
California's most prestigious wine region, a warm valley north of San Francisco Bay known for bold, ripe Cabernet Sauvignon and a dense concentration of wineries along its central corridor.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Napa Guide 🌐 San Francisco Guide Map
Willamette Valley
Oregon
A cool, rainy valley south of Portland whose marine climate and volcanic soils suit Pinot Noir, making it one of the New World's leading regions for the delicate Burgundian grape.
Wikipedia Official Site Wine Tours 🌐 Portland Guide Map
