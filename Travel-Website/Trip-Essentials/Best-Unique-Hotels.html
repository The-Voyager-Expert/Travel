Bolivia

Colchani, Salar de Uyuni, Bolivia
Built from 1 Million Salt BlocksWorld's Largest Salt Flat
A hotel constructed from approximately one million salt blocks at the edge of the world's largest salt flat. Floors, walls, ceilings, beds, tables, and chairs are all salt. The posted rule — no licking the walls — is not ironic.

Qualicum Beach, Vancouver Island, British Columbia, Canada
Hand-Built Wooden SpheresSuspended by Nautical Rigging
Four handbuilt wooden and fibreglass spheres suspended in a coastal rainforest by a rope system inspired by nautical rigging. Each sphere took roughly three years to build. Creator Tom Chudleigh began with just a concept sketch in 1994; no two spheres are identical.

North America's Only Ice HotelOpen Each January
North America's only 100% ice and snow hotel, reconstructed each winter at Village Vacances Valcartier from 2,300 blocks of ice and 15,000 tons of snow. The 30 rooms, ice restaurant, and 40-foot igloo performance hall are rebuilt entirely from scratch each season.

Boston, Massachusetts, United States
Converted from an 1851 JailOriginal Cell Block Rotunda Intact
Converted from the Charles Street Jail (built 1851), one of the finest examples of Boston Granite School architecture. The original rotunda and cell block structure are preserved and visible throughout the 300-room luxury hotel. Former inmates include Whitey Bulger and Frank Abagnale. Reopened as a hotel in 2007.
