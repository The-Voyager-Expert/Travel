Best Caves

Europe
Postojna Cave
Postojna, Slovenia
A 24-km karst cave system explored by electric train — the only cave railway in the world. Massive chambers filled with stalagmites, stalactites, and translucent curtain formations built over millions of years. Home to the olm (human fish), a blind cave salamander.
📏 24 km system🚂 Train ride inside🌡️ 10 °C year-round

Škocjan Caves
Divača, Slovenia
UNESCO World Heritage site with one of the largest underground river canyons on Earth. The Murmuring Cave chamber is 146 m high — large enough to fit St. Peter's Basilica. The Reka River disappears underground and carves through colossal passages.
📏 6 km system🏛️ UNESCO📐 146 m high chamber
Official Site Our Ljubljana Guide
Lascaux Cave
Montignac, France
Contains some of the most famous prehistoric cave paintings in the world — horses, stags, bison, and abstract symbols dating back 17,000 years. The original is sealed to preserve it; Lascaux IV is a full-scale replica built with laser-scanned precision.
🕯️ 17,000 years old🎟️ Timed entry🏛️ UNESCO
Official Site
Blue Grotto (Grotta Azzurra)
Capri, Italy
A sea cave where sunlight enters through an underwater opening and refracts to fill the entire chamber with an electric blue glow. Visitors enter lying flat in small rowboats through a metre-high opening. Known since Roman times as a place of wonder.
🚣 Rowboat entry☀️ Best Apr–Oct📐 60 m long

Eisriesenwelt
Werfen, Austria
The largest ice cave in the world, stretching 42 km into the Tennengebirge mountain. Towering ice formations — pillars, waterfalls, and curtains — formed by freezing meltwater. Explored by carbide lamp light. Cable car ascent to 1,641 m entrance.
📏 42 km system❄️ Ice formations☀️ May–Oct only
Official Site Our Salzburg Guide
Fingal's Cave
Staffa, Scotland
A sea cave formed entirely of hexagonal basalt columns — the same geology as the Giant's Causeway. Waves crash through the 75-m-deep chamber creating an eerie resonance that inspired Mendelssohn's Hebrides Overture. Accessible only by boat.
📐 75 m deep🚢 Boat access only☀️ Apr–Sep
National Trust Scotland
Drach Caves (Coves del Drac)
Mallorca, Spain
Four interconnected caves reaching 25 m below the surface, culminating in Lake Martel — one of the largest underground lakes in the world (177 m long). A live classical music concert is performed from illuminated boats on the lake at the end of each tour.
🎵 Live concert on lake📏 1.2 km tour☀️ Year-round
Official Site
Cueva de Altamira
Santillana del Mar, Spain
Called the "Sistine Chapel of Prehistoric Art." Polychrome paintings of bison, horses, and handprints created 14,000–36,000 years ago with astonishing artistic sophistication. The original is closed; the museum houses an exact replica of the painted ceiling.
🕯️ 36,000 years old🏛️ UNESCO🎟️ Museum replica
Museum Site
Asia & Oceania
Batu Caves
Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia
A limestone hill with a series of caves and cave temples. The main Cathedral Cave sits 100 m above ground, reached by a 272-step rainbow staircase flanked by a 42.7-m gold Murugan statue — the tallest in the world. A Hindu pilgrimage site for Thaipusam.
📐 100 m high🪜 272 steps☀️ Year-round
Malaysia Tourism
Sơn Đoòng Cave
Phong Nha, Vietnam
The largest cave in the world by volume — 9 km long, 200 m high, 150 m wide. So vast it has its own weather system, clouds forming inside the chamber. Collapsed sections (dolines) let in sunlight, growing entire jungles on the cave floor.
📐 200 m high📏 9 km long🥾 Multi-day trek

Waitomo Glowworm Caves
Waitomo, New Zealand
Thousands of Arachnocampa luminosa glowworms cling to the cave ceiling, emitting bioluminescent light that mimics a starry sky. Visitors drift through by boat in complete silence beneath the living constellations. The worms are unique to New Zealand.
✨ Bioluminescent🚣 Boat tour☀️ Year-round
Official Site
Marble Caves (Cuevas de Mármol)
General Carrera Lake, Chile
Water-sculpted marble formations rising from an intensely blue glacial lake on the Chile-Argentina border. Swirling blue, grey, and white patterns in the polished marble reflect off the turquoise water. Accessible only by boat or kayak.
🚣 Boat/kayak only💧 Glacial lake☀️ Best Dec–Mar
Chile Tourism
Deer Cave
Mulu, Borneo, Malaysia
One of the world's largest cave passages — 2 km long, 174 m high, and 150 m wide. At dusk, 2–3 million wrinkle-lipped bats spiral out of the cave in a ribbon visible for kilometres. The Garden of Eden, a hidden valley, lies behind the cave.
📐 174 m high🦇 3 million bats at dusk🏛️ UNESCO
Mulu National Park
Jeju Lava Tubes
Jeju Island, South Korea
A UNESCO-listed system of lava tubes formed by volcanic eruptions 100,000–300,000 years ago. Manjanggul is the highlight — 7.4 km long with a 7.6-m lava column (the tallest known). Multicoloured walls from mineral deposits and lava shelves line the tunnels.
📏 7.4 km🌋 Lava tube🏛️ UNESCO
Jeju Tourism
Puerto Princesa Underground River
Palawan, Philippines
An 8.2-km navigable underground river flowing directly into the sea — one of the longest in the world. Boat tours pass through cathedral-sized chambers with stalactites, bat colonies, and rock formations shaped like animals. A UNESCO World Heritage site.
📏 8.2 km🛶 Boat tour🏛️ UNESCO
Official Site Our Palawan Guide
Phong Nha Cave
Phong Nha, Vietnam
A river cave explored by motorboat through 7.7 km of passages with spectacular limestone formations illuminated in natural light where the ceiling opens. Part of the same Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng karst system as Sơn Đoòng but fully accessible by public tour.
📏 7.7 km🛶 Boat entry🏛️ UNESCO

Americas
Cave of the Crystals (Cueva de los Cristales)
Naica, Mexico
Contains the largest natural crystals ever found — selenite beams up to 12 m long and 4 m across, growing in a chamber 300 m underground. Temperatures reach 58 °C with 99% humidity, making unprotected visits lethal. Currently flooded and sealed; viewable through documentation only.
💎 12 m crystals🌡️ 58 °C⚠️ Sealed (not visitable)
Nat Geo Feature
Carlsbad Caverns
New Mexico, United States
Over 119 known caves in a Permian-age reef. The Big Room is the largest natural limestone chamber in North America — a single room you could fit 6 football fields inside. At dusk from May–October, 400,000+ Brazilian free-tailed bats spiral out of the natural entrance.
📐 Big Room: 33,210 m²🦇 400,000+ bats☀️ Year-round
NPS Info
Cenotes of Yucatán
Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico
Over 6,000 natural sinkholes formed when limestone bedrock collapsed into underground rivers. Some are open pools, others are enclosed caverns with shafts of light piercing the water. The ancient Maya considered them sacred portals to the underworld. Swimmable and diveable.
💧 Freshwater🤿 Diving/swimming☀️ Year-round

Antelope Canyon
Page, Arizona, United States
A slot canyon carved by flash floods into Navajo sandstone over millions of years. Narrow passageways (sometimes just 1 m wide) with smooth, flowing walls in bands of orange, red, and purple. Light beams pierce the Upper Canyon around midday, creating iconic shafts.
☀️ Best midday light📐 40 m deep🎟️ Guided only (Navajo)

Mammoth Cave
Kentucky, United States
The longest known cave system in the world — over 680 km explored and still growing. Five levels of passages formed over 10 million years. Contains rare cave fauna including eyeless crayfish and cave shrimp. Historic tours, wild caving, and river routes available.
📏 680+ km explored🏛️ UNESCO☀️ Year-round
NPS Info
Mendenhall Ice Caves
Juneau, Alaska, United States
Glacial ice caves formed within and beneath the Mendenhall Glacier. Translucent blue walls and ceilings with meltwater streams running through. Constantly changing and receding due to glacial melt — these caves are temporary formations that may not exist in a few decades.
❄️ Glacial ice⚠️ Receding/changing🥾 Hike + kayak access

Lechuguilla Cave
New Mexico, United States
One of the most decorated caves in the world — gypsum chandeliers, aragonite formations, and rare hydromagnesite balloons found nowhere else. At 489 m deep, it is the deepest limestone cave in the US. Closed to tourism to preserve pristine conditions; scientific access only.
📐 489 m deep📏 240+ km⚠️ Closed to public

Middle East & Africa
Al Hoota Cave
Al Hamra, Oman
A 2-million-year-old cave system beneath the Al Hajar Mountains — one of the few show caves on the Arabian Peninsula. Contains a subterranean lake home to blind cave fish (Garra barreimiae). Reached by an electric train from the visitor centre.
📏 4.5 km system🐟 Blind cave fish🚂 Electric train

Cango Caves
Oudtshoorn, South Africa
A 5-km dripstone cave system in the Swartberg foothills with chambers up to 107 m long and 16 m high. Van Zyl's Hall alone fits 2,000 people. The Adventure Tour squeezes through "The Devil's Chimney" and "The Letterbox" — passages as narrow as 27 cm.
📏 5 km system🧗 Adventure route☀️ Year-round

Iceland & Arctic
Vatnajökull Ice Caves
Vatnajökull, Iceland
Naturally formed ice caves beneath Europe's largest glacier. Crystal-blue walls created by compressed air-free ice, with light filtering through in shades of sapphire and cobalt. Each cave is unique and ephemeral — they form, shift, and collapse with the seasons.
❄️ Glacial ice☀️ Nov–Mar only🥾 Guide required

Þríhnúkagígur (Inside the Volcano)
Bláfjöll, Iceland
The only place on Earth where you can descend into a dormant volcano's magma chamber. An open-cage lift drops 120 m into a cavern the size of the Statue of Liberty, with walls banded in red, purple, yellow, and green mineral deposits from the last eruption 4,000 years ago.
📐 120 m descent🌋 Magma chamber☀️ May–Oct

Leiðarendi Lava Tube
Reykjavik area, Iceland
A 900-m lava tube just 25 minutes from Reykjavik, formed in an eruption roughly 2,000 years ago. Colourful mineral deposits (reds, greens, purples) coat the walls, with lava stalactites hanging from the ceiling. Small enough to feel immersive, accessible enough for a half-day trip.
📏 900 m🌋 Lava tube☀️ Year-round

Reed Flute Cave
Guilin, China
A 240-m limestone cave known for its extraordinary density of stalactites, stalagmites, and rock columns in dramatic formations. Named for the reeds growing at its entrance once used for flutes. Inscriptions on the walls date back to 792 AD. Illuminated with multicoloured lighting.
📏 240 m🕯️ 1,200+ years of visits☀️ Year-round

Hang Én
Phong Nha, Vietnam
The world's third-largest cave — a massive passage with a beach of fine sand inside, where light pours through a collapsed ceiling. Swiftlets nest in the thousands, and a pristine jungle grows in the doline. Requires a two-day trek through the national park to reach.
📐 120 m high🥾 2-day trek🏕️ Camp inside

Jenolan Caves
Blue Mountains, Australia
Among the oldest open caves in the world — formations dating back 340 million years. Eleven show caves connected by an underground river, each with distinct character: the Lucas Cave has a 54-m-high cathedral chamber; the Orient Cave features delicate helictites growing against gravity.
🕯️ 340 million years old📏 40+ km mapped☀️ Year-round

Tham Lod Cave
Pang Mapha, Thailand
A river cave you raft through on bamboo — the Nam Lang River flows straight through the mountain. Three main chambers contain coffin-shaped stalactites and prehistoric teak-wood coffins (1,200–2,200 years old) lodged on high ledges. At dusk, thousands of swifts pour in to roost.
🛶 Bamboo raft🕯️ Prehistoric coffins🐦 Swift roost at dusk

Krubera Cave (Veryovkina)
Abkhazia, Georgia
The deepest known cave on Earth — Veryovkina reaches 2,212 m below the entrance. Located in the Arabika Massif of the Western Caucasus. Requires weeks of expedition caving through vertical shafts, sumps, and tight passages. Accessible only to elite speleologists.
📐 2,212 m deep⚠️ Expedition only🧗 Elite caving

Sea Lion Caves
Florence, Oregon, United States
The largest sea cave in the United States — a natural cavern 93 m long and 7 storeys high, home to a colony of Steller sea lions year-round. An elevator descends 63 m through the cliff to a viewing area inside the cave where hundreds of sea lions haul out and bark.
📐 93 m long🦭 Sea lion colony☀️ Year-round
