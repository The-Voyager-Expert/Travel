Best Unique Museums

The world's best single-subject museums — places built entirely around one obsession. Beer breweries turned visitor experiences, car factories with showroom-museums, chocolate empires you can taste, aviation hangars full of legendary aircraft, railway halls preserving the golden age of rail, rockets that went to space, ships that crossed oceans, spy gadgets, miniature worlds, music halls of fame, and more.

Beer & Brewing
🍺
Guinness Storehouse
Dublin, Ireland
Seven floors inside a 1904 fermentation plant, built around a glass atrium shaped like a giant pint glass. Traces 260 years of Guinness history from Arthur Guinness's 9,000-year lease to the modern brewing process. Ends at the Gravity Bar — a panoramic rooftop with 360-degree views over Dublin. Ireland's most-visited paid attraction.
📅 Opened 2000👥 1.7M visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia Viator GetYourGuide Dublin Guide
🍻
Heineken Experience
Amsterdam, Netherlands
Self-guided tour through the original 1867 Heineken brewery on Stadhouderskade. Interactive exhibits cover brand history, brewing science, and marketing through multimedia rooms, a simulated brewing ride, and beer tastings. Ends with two pours in the rooftop bar overlooking the canal.
📅 Opened 2001👥 1M+ visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia Viator GetYourGuide Amsterdam Guide
🍺
Pilsner Urquell Experience
Plzeň, Czech Republic
Built around the brewery where the world's first golden lager was brewed in 1842. Tours descend into the original sandstone cellars where Pilsner Urquell still ages in oak barrels, with tastings of unfiltered, unpasteurized beer drawn straight from the barrel. The adjacent Brewery Museum occupies a preserved 15th-century malthouse — the only one of its kind in the world. Awarded World's Leading Beer Tour Visitor Experience 2025.
📅 Brewing since 1842🏆 World's first golden lager
Official Site Wikipedia Viator Prague Guide
🍺
Belgian Beer World
Brussels, Belgium
Opened in 2023 inside the former Brussels Stock Exchange (Bourse), a 19th-century neoclassical landmark. Three floors of immersive exhibits trace Belgium's UNESCO-recognized beer culture — from Trappist monasteries and lambic fermentation to modern craft brewing. Ends with a tasting bar on the rooftop terrace overlooking Grand Place.
📅 Opened 2023🏛️ Former Stock Exchange building
Official Site Wikipedia Brussels Guide
Cars & Automotive
🏎️
Mercedes-Benz Museum
Stuttgart, Germany
A double-helix building designed by UNStudio, where two spiraling ramps let visitors descend nine floors through 140 years of automotive history — from Karl Benz's 1886 Patent-Motorwagen (the world's first automobile) to modern Formula 1 cars. Over 160 vehicles and 1,500 exhibits across 16,500 square metres.
📅 Opened 2006🚗 160+ vehicles👥 800K+ visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Stuttgart Guide
🏎️
Porsche Museum
Stuttgart, Germany
A striking white cantilevered building appearing to float above its base, housing over 80 vehicles from Porsche's 700-car collection on rotation. Covers everything from the original 356 to Le Mans-winning 917s and modern 911 GT variants. A working workshop visible from the exhibit floor restores cars from the archive in real time.
📅 Opened 2009🚗 700+ in collection👥 6M total since opening
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Stuttgart Guide
🏎️
BMW Museum & BMW Welt
Munich, Germany
The silver bowl-shaped BMW Museum (1973) displays 125 cars, motorcycles, and engines across seven themed houses tracing 100+ years of BMW history. Next door, the futuristic BMW Welt (2007) is a free-entry showroom and delivery center where buyers collect new cars from a glass-tower display carousel.
📅 Museum opened 1973🚗 125 exhibits👥 847K visitors (2025)
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Munich Guide
🏎️
Museo Ferrari
Maranello, Italy
Steps from the Ferrari factory in Maranello, this museum showcases Ferrari's road and racing heritage — championship-winning Formula 1 cars from the Schumacher and Lauda eras, iconic road models from the 250 GTO to the LaFerrari, plus trophies, helmets, and immersive audiovisual timelines. A second location in Modena (Museo Enzo Ferrari) covers the founder's life story.
📅 Opened 1990🏁 F1 cars on display
Official Site Wikipedia Viator GetYourGuide Bologna Guide
🏎️
Cité de l'Automobile
Mulhouse, France
The world's largest car collection — 500 vehicles from 98 marques spanning 1878 to the present, housed in a vast former textile factory. Centerpiece is the Schlumpf Collection: the brothers secretly amassed cars for decades, including two of the six Bugatti Royales ever built.
📅 Public since 1982🚗 500 vehicles🏆 World's largest car collection
Official Site Wikipedia
🏎️
Petersen Automotive Museum
Los Angeles, United States
A striking stainless-steel ribbon facade on Wilshire Boulevard, housing 300+ vehicles celebrating car culture and Los Angeles's love affair with the automobile. Rotating exhibits feature Hollywood movie cars, custom hot rods, rare supercars, and a vault of over 250 vehicles in the basement accessible by guided tour only.
📅 Founded 1994🚗 300+ vehicles
Official Site Wikipedia Los Angeles Guide
Chocolate
🍫
Lindt Home of Chocolate
Kilchberg, Switzerland
The world's largest Lindt chocolate museum, built next to the original factory where Lindt has produced chocolate since 1899. A 9-metre chocolate fountain greets visitors in the atrium. Interactive exhibits trace the journey from cocoa bean to finished bar, with master chocolatier courses and the largest Lindt shop in the world at 500 square metres.
📅 Opened 2020👥 800K+ visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Zurich Guide
🍫
Chocolate Museum Cologne
Cologne, Germany
The world's largest chocolate museum, sitting on a peninsula in the Rhine near Cologne Cathedral. A working miniature production line shows every step from roasting to molding, and a 3-metre chocolate fountain lets visitors dip wafers into warm Lindt chocolate. Covers 5,000 years of chocolate history — from Mayan and Aztec cacao rituals to modern industrial production.
📅 Opened 1993👥 716K visitors (2025 record)
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide
🍫
Choco-Story
Bruges, Belgium
Set in a 15th-century wine tavern in the heart of medieval Bruges, this museum traces 4,000 years of chocolate history — from Olmec and Maya civilizations through Spanish colonial trade to Belgium's modern praline tradition. Live demonstrations by a master chocolatier, with tasting included in every visit.
📅 Opened 2004🍬 Live praline demonstrations
Official Site Wikipedia Bruges Guide
🍫
Maison Cailler
Broc, Switzerland
Visitor center of Switzerland's oldest surviving chocolate brand, operating in Broc since 1898. A one-hour multisensory tour walks through cocoa's origins, Swiss chocolate pioneers, and Cailler's production process — ending with an all-you-can-taste chocolate bar. Located in the Gruyères region, often combined with a visit to the medieval hilltop town and its famous cheese dairy.
📅 Museum opened 2006🧀 Near Gruyères
Official Site Wikipedia
🍫
Cadbury World
Birmingham, United Kingdom
Built on the grounds of the original Cadbury factory in the garden village of Bournville — a model workers' community built by the Cadbury family in 1893. Tells the story of the Quaker chocolate dynasty through interactive zones, a 4D cinema, and live chocolate-making demonstrations. Visitors watch Cadbury Creme Eggs being made on a working production line.
📅 Opened 1990🏭 Bournville factory grounds
Official Site Wikipedia
🍫
Museu de la Xocolata
Barcelona, Spain
Housed in a former convent in the Born quarter, tracing chocolate's journey from Mesoamerican origins through Barcelona's role as one of Europe's first ports to receive cacao from the New World. Displays elaborate chocolate sculptures of Barcelona landmarks, and visitors receive a chocolate bar as their entry ticket.
📅 Opened 2000🎟️ Entry ticket is a chocolate bar
Official Site Wikipedia Barcelona Guide
Aviation & Flight
✈️
Smithsonian Air & Space Museum
Washington, D.C., United States
The world's largest collection of historic aircraft and spacecraft. Displays the Wright Flyer (1903), the Spirit of St. Louis, the Apollo 11 command module, and a touchable lunar sample. The companion Udvar-Hazy Center near Dulles Airport houses the Space Shuttle Discovery, a Concorde, and the Enola Gay. Free admission at both locations.
📅 Founded 1946✈️ 60K+ artifacts🎟️ Free
Official Site Wikipedia Washington DC Guide
✈️
National Museum of the U.S. Air Force
Dayton, Ohio, United States
The world's largest military aviation museum, spread across 19 acres of indoor exhibit space at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base — the birthplace of powered flight. Over 350 aircraft and missiles, including the only publicly displayed B-2 stealth bomber, President Kennedy's Air Force One (SAM 26000), and experimental X-planes. Free admission and parking.
📅 Founded 1923✈️ 350+ aircraft🎟️ Free
Official Site Wikipedia
✈️
The Museum of Flight
Seattle, United States
The world's largest private air and space museum, at Boeing Field south of downtown Seattle. Over 150 aircraft including the first-ever Boeing 747 (City of Everett), a Concorde, a NASA Space Shuttle trainer with full cockpit access, and WWI-era fighters. The original Boeing "Red Barn" factory — where William Boeing built his first planes in 1916 — is preserved inside the museum.
📅 Founded 1965✈️ 150+ aircraft👥 465K visitors (2024)
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Seattle Guide
✈️
Musée de l'Air et de l'Espace
Le Bourget, France
One of the oldest aviation museums in the world, at Le Bourget Airport — where Lindbergh landed after crossing the Atlantic in 1927. The collection spans 150+ aircraft across 18,000 square metres, from an 1879 Massia-Biot glider to two Concordes (visitors can board both). Covers aviation, space exploration, and France's aerospace industry. Free permanent collection.
📅 Founded 1919✈️ 150+ aircraft🎟️ Free permanent collection
Official Site Wikipedia Paris Guide
✈️
RAF Museum London
London, United Kingdom
On the historic Hendon Aerodrome where many early flying milestones took place, this free-entry museum houses over 80 aircraft spanning the full history of the Royal Air Force. Highlights include a Spitfire, a Lancaster bomber, a Vulcan, and a Eurofighter Typhoon. A second site at RAF Cosford in Shropshire holds an additional 70+ aircraft.
📅 Opened 1972✈️ 80+ aircraft at Hendon🎟️ Free
Official Site Wikipedia London Guide
Trains & Railways
🚂
National Railway Museum
York, United Kingdom
The world's largest railway museum, with over 100 locomotives and 300 pieces of rolling stock spanning 200 years of British rail history. Home to the Mallard — holder of the world speed record for steam locomotives (126 mph, 1938) — and a collection of opulent royal carriages. The Great Hall is a former engine shed with a working turntable. Free admission.
📅 Opened 1975🚂 100+ locomotives🎟️ Free
Official Site Wikipedia
🚂
Cité du Train
Mulhouse, France
The largest railway museum in continental Europe, with over 100 locomotives and 200 pieces of rolling stock across 60,000 square metres of indoor and outdoor exhibition space. The collection includes steam, diesel, and electric trains, luxury wagons, railcars, and French presidential carriages. An outdoor railway panorama recreates period stations and landscapes.
📅 Public since 1971🚂 100+ locomotives
Official Site Wikipedia
🚂
Kyoto Railway Museum
Kyoto, Japan
Japan's largest railway museum, with 50+ retired trains displayed across 31,000 square metres — from early steam locomotives to Shinkansen bullet trains. A preserved roundhouse with 20 working steam engines, a driving simulator, and a massive HO-scale model railway diorama spanning 30 metres. Operated by JR West on the site of the former Umekoji locomotive depot.
📅 Opened 2016🚂 50+ trains🚄 Shinkansen collection
Official Site Wikipedia Kyoto Guide
🚂
Het Spoorwegmuseum
Utrecht, Netherlands
The Dutch national railway museum, housed in the 1874 Maliebaan station — one of the oldest surviving station buildings in the Netherlands. Over 80 historic trains spanning 200 years, including a replica of the "Arend" (the first locomotive to run in the Netherlands, 1839) and royal carriages. Visitors can arrive by train directly into the museum's own platform from Utrecht Centraal.
📅 Founded 1927🚂 80+ trains👥 350K visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia Amsterdam Guide
🚂
DB Museum
Nuremberg, Germany
Germany's oldest railway museum, tracing German rail history from the country's first railway line (Nürnberg-Fürth, 1835) to the modern ICE high-speed network. Displays include the royal train of Ludwig II of Bavaria, an original Adler locomotive replica, and a hands-on model railway hall. Run by Deutsche Bahn at the historic Nuremberg Transport Museum complex.
📅 Founded 1899🏛️ Germany's oldest railway museum
Official Site Wikipedia
Space & Rockets
🚀
Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex
Cape Canaveral, United States
NASA's launch site since 1968, where every Apollo mission, Space Shuttle, and SpaceX crew launch departed from. The visitor complex displays the Space Shuttle Atlantis suspended mid-flight, a Saturn V rocket laid horizontally in a 110-metre building, and the actual Apollo 14 capsule. Bus tours reach the Vehicle Assembly Building and active launch pads.
📅 Visitor complex since 1967🚀 Active launch site👥 1.7M visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia Viator GetYourGuide Orlando Guide
🚀
Space Center Houston
Houston, United States
The official visitor center of NASA's Johnson Space Center — Mission Control for every crewed spaceflight since Gemini 4 (1965). Displays the world's largest collection of moon rocks, a flown Apollo 17 command module, a full-size Space Shuttle replica mounted on the original NASA 905 carrier aircraft, and a Saturn V rocket. Tram tours reach the historic Apollo-era Mission Control room and astronaut training facilities.
📅 Opened 1992🌙 Largest moon rock collection
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide
🚀
Cité de l'Espace
Toulouse, France
Europe's premier space museum, in the city where Airbus and the European Space Agency's satellite operations are headquartered. A full-size Ariane 5 rocket stands upright in the park, visitors walk through a Mir space station module and a Soyuz capsule, and a planetarium and IMAX dome show space-themed films. Interactive astronaut training simulators let visitors experience a moonwalk and docking manoeuvres.
📅 Opened 1997🚀 Full Ariane 5 & Mir station👥 400K visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide
🚀
Cosmonautics Museum
Moscow, Russia
Built beneath the 107-metre titanium Monument to the Conquerors of Space near VDNKh park, this museum tells the Soviet side of the Space Race — from Sputnik and Laika to Gagarin's Vostok capsule and the Mir station. Displays include Valentina Tereshkova's suit (first woman in space), lunar soil samples, and full-size spacecraft modules visitors can enter.
📅 Opened 1981🛰️ Soviet space program collection
Official Site Wikipedia
Maritime & Ships
⚓
Vasa Museum
Stockholm, Sweden
Purpose-built around a single artifact: the Vasa, a 69-metre Swedish warship that sank on its maiden voyage in 1628 and was salvaged in 1961 — almost perfectly intact after 333 years on the seabed. The only fully preserved 17th-century ship in the world, displayed across six levels with over 700 original sculptures still attached. Scandinavia's most visited museum.
📅 Opened 1990⚓ 69m warship, sank 1628👥 1.5M visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Stockholm Guide
⚓
Fram Museum
Oslo, Norway
Houses the polar exploration ship Fram — the wooden vessel that has sailed farther north and farther south than any other surface ship in history. Used by Fridtjof Nansen (1893–96 Arctic drift) and Roald Amundsen (1910–12 race to the South Pole). Visitors walk on deck and below into the cramped crew quarters. Adjacent to the Kon-Tiki Museum with Thor Heyerdahl's legendary balsa raft.
📅 Opened 1936🧊 Farthest north & south ship
Official Site Wikipedia Oslo Guide
⚓
USS Midway Museum
San Diego, United States
A decommissioned aircraft carrier — the longest-serving carrier of the 20th century (1945–1992) — permanently moored at Navy Pier on San Diego's waterfront. Self-guided audio tours cover 60+ exhibits across the flight deck, hangar deck, and below-decks compartments. Over 30 restored aircraft on display, from WWII prop fighters to Vietnam-era jets. Flight simulators available on the flight deck.
📅 Museum since 2004✈️ 30+ aircraft on deck👥 1.3M visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide San Diego Guide
⚓
National Maritime Museum
London, United Kingdom
The world's largest maritime museum, in the UNESCO-listed Greenwich complex alongside the Royal Observatory and the Cutty Sark clipper ship. Covers Britain's 500-year seafaring history — from Tudor warships and Nelson's Trafalgar uniform (with the bullet hole) to modern ocean science. The adjacent Cutty Sark (separate ticket) is the world's sole surviving tea clipper, fully restored and elevated so visitors walk beneath her copper hull.
📅 Opened 1937🏛️ UNESCO World Heritage site🎟️ Free
Official Site Wikipedia London Guide
Science & Technology
🔬
Deutsches Museum
Munich, Germany
The world's largest science and technology museum, on an island in the River Isar since 1925. Over 28,000 objects across 73 exhibit halls spanning every branch of science — from a reconstructed coal mine 12 metres underground to a full-size sailing ship, early computers, Foucault pendulums, and musical instruments. A planetarium and live demonstrations run daily.
📅 Founded 1903🔬 28,000+ objects👥 1.5M visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Munich Guide
🔬
Exploratorium
San Francisco, United States
A hands-on science museum on Pier 15 overlooking San Francisco Bay — founded in 1969 by physicist Frank Oppenheimer as a laboratory for public learning. Over 650 interactive exhibits cover perception, physics, biology, and human behaviour. The Tactile Dome (a pitch-black maze navigated entirely by touch) and the outdoor Bay Observatory (wind, waves, fog) are signature experiences.
📅 Founded 1969🧪 650+ interactive exhibits
Official Site Wikipedia San Francisco Guide
🔬
Science Museum
London, United Kingdom
One of the world's great science collections, in South Kensington since 1857. Displays Stephenson's Rocket, the actual Apollo 10 command module, Babbage's Difference Engine No. 2, and Watson and Crick's original DNA model. An IMAX cinema and flight simulators complement the permanent galleries. Free admission to all main galleries.
📅 Founded 1857🏛️ 300,000+ items🎟️ Free
Official Site Wikipedia London Guide
🔬
NEMO Science Museum
Amsterdam, Netherlands
A striking copper-green ship-shaped building designed by Renzo Piano, rising from the harbour behind Amsterdam Centraal station. Five floors of hands-on exhibits let visitors build bridges, create chain reactions, generate electricity, and explore the science of the human body. The rooftop terrace (free access) offers panoramic city views. Particularly popular for families but designed for all ages.
📅 Opened 1997🏗️ Renzo Piano building👥 670K visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Amsterdam Guide
Music
🎵
ABBA The Museum
Stockholm, Sweden
An immersive experience inside Djurgården's Pop House, where visitors walk through ABBA's career from 1970s Eurovision to global superstardom. Stage costumes, gold records, the Polar Music Studio replica, and a hologram stage where visitors perform as the fifth ABBA member. Interactive stations let you mix tracks, record in a vocal booth, and try on digital versions of the iconic outfits.
📅 Opened 2013🎤 Interactive hologram stage

🎵
Rock & Roll Hall of Fame
Cleveland, United States
I.M. Pei's glass pyramid on the shore of Lake Erie, housing the definitive collection of rock music history. Inducted artists' memorabilia fills seven floors — from Elvis's leather jumpsuit and Jimi Hendrix's Stratocaster to Nirvana's handwritten lyrics and Beyoncé's stage costumes. Annual induction ceremonies, rotating exhibits, and a concert hall hosting live performances year-round.
📅 Opened 1995🎸 I.M. Pei building👥 600K visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia
🎵
Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP)
Seattle, United States
Frank Gehry's swooping metallic building at the base of the Space Needle, founded by Microsoft co-founder Paul Allen. Dedicated to contemporary popular culture — rock, hip-hop, sci-fi, fantasy, and video games. Permanent exhibits include Jimi Hendrix's guitars (he grew up in Seattle), Nirvana memorabilia, a Sound Lab with instruments anyone can play, and rotating exhibits on artists from Taylor Swift to horror cinema.
📅 Opened 2000🏗️ Frank Gehry building
Official Site Wikipedia Seattle Guide
🎵
Beethoven-Haus
Bonn, Germany
The birthplace of Ludwig van Beethoven, preserved as a museum since 1889 — the oldest composer museum in Germany. The modest Baroque townhouse displays original manuscripts, his last piano (an 1817 Broadwood), ear trumpets made for his deafness, and personal letters. A digital archive and concert hall next door host performances of his chamber music in an intimate setting.
📅 Museum since 1889🎹 Beethoven's birthplace

Spy & Espionage
🕵️
International Spy Museum
Washington, D.C., United States
The world's largest public collection of espionage artifacts, relocated in 2019 to a purpose-built facility on L'Enfant Plaza. Over 7,000 objects spanning centuries of intelligence work — from a WWII Enigma machine and a CIA-issued lipstick pistol to Bond-villain-style gadgets and Cold War dead drops. Interactive missions let visitors adopt a cover identity and complete spy challenges across the building.
📅 Opened 2002🕵️ 7,000+ artifacts👥 700K visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Washington DC Guide
🕵️
German Spy Museum
Berlin, Germany
On Leipziger Platz, metres from where the Berlin Wall once split the city into rival intelligence zones. Covers espionage from antiquity through the Cold War — when Berlin was the spy capital of the world with over 80 intelligence agencies operating simultaneously. Displays include original Stasi surveillance equipment, CIA and KGB gadgets, cipher machines, and a laser maze visitors must navigate without tripping the beams.
📅 Opened 2015🏛️ At former Berlin Wall site
Official Site Wikipedia Berlin Guide
🕵️
KGB Museum
Prague, Czech Republic
A private collection of Soviet-era intelligence equipment in central Prague, run by a Russian collector who spent decades acquiring declassified KGB hardware. Displays include spy cameras disguised as buttons and cigarette cases, a radio hidden in a tree stump, an assassination umbrella (Bulgarian Secret Service), and personal effects from double agents. Small but dense — every object has an operational backstory.
📅 Private collection📷 Spy cameras & gadgets
Official Site Prague Guide
Miniatures & Models
🏗️
Miniatur Wunderland
Hamburg, Germany
The world's largest model railway and miniature world, in a converted warehouse in Hamburg's Speicherstadt. Over 16,000 metres of track, 1,100 trains, 130,000 trees, 300,000 lights, and 280,000 hand-crafted figures create nine detailed world regions — from Alpine villages to a functioning miniature airport with planes that taxi and take off. Took 1 million working hours to build and is still expanding.
📅 Opened 2001🚂 16 km of track👥 1.4M visitors/year
Official Site Wikipedia Hamburg Guide
🏗️
LEGO House
Billund, Denmark
The "Home of the Brick" — a 12,000 square-metre experience centre in LEGO's hometown, designed by BIG architects as 21 interlocking white blocks resembling oversized LEGO bricks. Four colour-coded experience zones (red/blue/green/yellow) encourage building, creativity, and play. A Masterpiece Gallery displays fan-built creations, and a 15-metre LEGO waterfall cascades through the central atrium. The Tree of Creativity — a 15-metre LEGO tree made from 6.3 million bricks — is the centrepiece.
📅 Opened 2017🧱 25M+ LEGO bricks inside
Official Site Wikipedia
🏗️
Madurodam
The Hague, Netherlands
The Netherlands in miniature — a 1:25 scale outdoor park displaying meticulous replicas of famous Dutch landmarks, infrastructure, and landscapes. Over 5,500 miniature trees, working windmills, a landing Schiphol plane, container ships navigating Rotterdam harbour, and tulip fields that bloom in season. Founded as a war memorial in 1952, named after George Maduro who died at Dachau. Interactive elements let visitors flood polders, load ships, and control planes.
📅 Opened 1952🇳🇱 1:25 scale Netherlands
Official Site Wikipedia GetYourGuide Amsterdam Guide
Film & Cinema
🎬
Academy Museum of Motion Pictures
Los Angeles, United States
The largest museum in the United States dedicated to filmmaking, opened by the same organization that awards the Oscars. Renzo Piano's design connects a restored 1939 Streamline Moderne building to a futuristic glass-and-concrete sphere housing a 1,000-seat theatre. Permanent exhibits cover the entire filmmaking process — from storyboarding and costume design to VFX and sound. Displays include Dorothy's ruby slippers, the Rosebud sled from Citizen Kane, and Hitchcock's camera equipment.
📅 Opened 2021🏗️ Renzo Piano building
Official Site Wikipedia Los Angeles Guide
🎬
Studio Ghibli Museum
Mitaka, Tokyo, Japan
Designed by Hayao Miyazaki himself as "a museum that is interesting and which relaxes the soul" — nestled in a wooded park in western Tokyo. The building feels like stepping into a Ghibli film: stained glass, spiral staircases, and a rooftop garden with a life-size Robot Soldier from Laputa. Exhibits show the animation process from sketch to screen, and a tiny theatre screens exclusive short films not available anywhere else. Tickets sell out months in advance.
📅 Opened 2001🎨 Designed by Miyazaki
Official Site Wikipedia Tokyo Guide

Leavesden, United Kingdom
The Making of Harry Potter — a behind-the-scenes walking tour of the actual film sets at Leavesden Studios where all eight movies were shot over a decade. Walk through the Great Hall, Dumbledore's office, Diagon Alley, and the Forbidden Forest. See original props, costumes, and animatronic creatures. The outdoor backlot includes Privet Drive and the Hogwarts Bridge. A Butterbeer bar serves the signature drink. One of the UK's top-rated visitor attractions.

FIFA Museum
Zurich, Switzerland
Three floors dedicated to the global game — from the origins of football to every World Cup final. Displays include the original Jules Rimet Trophy, match-worn shirts from Pelé and Maradona, and the ball from the 2014 final. Interactive galleries let visitors test their goalkeeping reflexes and commentary skills. A rooftop pitch and a FIFA-themed pinball hall add to the experience.

Lausanne, Switzerland
The official museum of the International Olympic Committee, on the shores of Lake Geneva in the Olympic capital. Three floors trace the Games from ancient Olympia to the present — torches, medals, opening-ceremony costumes, and athlete memorabilia from every modern Olympiad. The outdoor Olympic Park descends terraced gardens to the lakefront with sculptures and athletic installations. A temporary exhibit hall hosts major rotating shows.

Barcelona, Spain
Inside the Camp Nou complex — the largest stadium in Europe — this is Catalonia's most-visited museum. Displays six European Cups, multiple Ballon d'Or trophies, Messi's golden boots, and a century of club history. The stadium tour walks visitors through the tunnel, pitch-side, commentary boxes, and the away team dressing room. Being redesigned as part of the Espai Barça renovation (phased reopening 2025–26).
📅 Opened 1984🏆 6 Champions League trophies👥 1.9M visitors/year

A full-size court lets visitors shoot at regulation hoops, and a virtual-reality experience puts you in the shoes of an NBA player. Over 300 inductees honoured with interactive exhibits, game-worn jerseys (Jordan, Magic, Bird), and milestone artifacts from every era.

Whisky & Spirits

At the top of the Royal Mile, next to Edinburgh Castle. A barrel ride through the whisky-making process, a scent challenge across Scotland's whisky regions, and guided tastings from a collection of 3,384 bottles — the world's largest Scotch whisky collection, amassed by a Brazilian collector over 35 years and displayed in a vault-like room. Tours range from introductory to masterclasses with rare single malts.
📅 Opened 1988🥃 3,384-bottle collection

Jameson Distillery Bow St.
Dublin, Ireland
The original Jameson distillery in Smithfield, operating from 1780 to 1971 and now a visitor experience in the restored buildings. Tours explain triple distillation and the difference between Irish and Scotch whiskey, ending with a comparative tasting of Jameson against a Scotch and a bourbon. Premium experiences include blending your own bottle and cocktail-making masterclasses at the rooftop bar.
📅 Distillery since 1780🥃 Visitor experience since 1997

Johnnie Walker Princes Street
Edinburgh, United Kingdom
Diageo's flagship whisky experience, opened in 2021 in a 1930s Art Deco department store on Edinburgh's main shopping street. Eight floors of immersive, personalised whisky journeys — visitors complete a flavour profile quiz and receive a tailored tasting flight. Kentucky Bourbon Trail
Kentucky, United States
Not a single museum but a passport-style tour of 18+ working bourbon distilleries across central Kentucky — where 95% of the world's bourbon is made. Highlights include Maker's Mark (hand-dipped red wax bottles in a picturesque National Historic Landmark distillery), Woodford Reserve (limestone spring water, copper pot stills, a Civil War-era stone building), and Buffalo Trace (the oldest continuously operating distillery in America, free tours). Each offers warehouse tastings straight from the barrel.
📅 Trail established 1999🥃 18+ distilleries🏆 95% of world's bourbon
