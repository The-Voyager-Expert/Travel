Best Castles

Europe
Neuschwanstein Castle
Schwangau, Germany
The fairy-tale castle that inspired Disney's Sleeping Beauty Castle. Built by King Ludwig II of Bavaria in 1869 on a rugged cliff above Hohenschwangau village. Romanesque Revival interiors with Byzantine-inspired throne hall, swan motifs throughout, and panoramic Alpine views from the balcony.
📅 Built 1869–1886🏛️ UNESCO tentative👥 1.4 M visitors/year
Official Site
Edinburgh Castle
Edinburgh, Scotland
A fortress built atop an extinct volcanic plug that has dominated Edinburgh's skyline since the 12th century. Houses the Scottish Crown Jewels, the Stone of Destiny, and the medieval St Margaret's Chapel — the oldest surviving building in Scotland. The One O'Clock Gun fires daily.
📅 12th century⛰️ Volcanic rock👥 2.2 M visitors/year
Official Site
Prague Castle
Prague, Czech Republic
The largest ancient castle complex in the world according to Guinness World Records — 70,000 m² spanning Romanesque, Gothic, and Baroque architecture. Contains St Vitus Cathedral, the Old Royal Palace, Golden Lane, and the Story of Prague Castle exhibition. Seat of Czech heads of state since 870 AD.
📅 Founded 870 AD📐 70,000 m²🏛️ UNESCO
Official Site
Château de Chambord
Chambord, France
The largest château in the Loire Valley and a masterpiece of French Renaissance architecture. Built as a hunting lodge for King Francis I, it features 440 rooms, 365 fireplaces, and a double-helix staircase attributed to Leonardo da Vinci. Set within a 5,440-hectare walled park.
📅 Built 1519–1547🏛️ UNESCO📐 440 rooms
Official Site
Alhambra
Granada, Spain
A 13th-century Nasrid palace-fortress complex perched above Granada with the Sierra Nevada as backdrop. Intricate Islamic geometric carvings, muqarnas ceilings, the Court of the Lions with its 124-column arcade, and the Generalife gardens with their water channels make it Spain's most-visited monument.
📅 13th–14th century🏛️ UNESCO👥 2.7 M visitors/year
Official Site
Windsor Castle
Windsor, England
The oldest and largest inhabited castle in the world — a working royal residence for over 900 years. William the Conqueror chose the site in 1070. Features St George's Chapel (burial place of 10 monarchs), the State Apartments, and the Semi-State Rooms opened only in winter.
📅 Founded 1070📐 55,000 m²👑 Active royal residence
Official Site
Château de Versailles
Versailles, France
The palace that defined royal excess — 2,300 rooms including the Hall of Mirrors (73 m long, 357 mirrors), the King's Grand Apartments, and Marie Antoinette's estate. The formal gardens span 800 hectares with 1,400 fountains. Seat of French power from Louis XIV until the Revolution.
📅 Built 1661–1715🏛️ UNESCO👥 8.1 M visitors/year
Official Site
Tower of London
London, England
A 1,000-year-old fortress on the Thames that served as royal palace, prison, armoury, treasury, and execution site. Houses the Crown Jewels of England, guarded by Yeoman Warders (Beefeaters). The White Tower built by William the Conqueror in 1066 anchors the complex. Ravens kept by royal decree.
📅 Founded 1066🏛️ UNESCO👥 2.9 M visitors/year
Official Site
Bran Castle
Brașov, Romania
A 14th-century fortress perched on a cliff in a Carpathian mountain pass — marketed as "Dracula's Castle" for its resemblance to Bram Stoker's description (though Vlad the Impaler likely only passed through). Narrow stairways, secret passages, a tower courtyard, and a weapons collection inside.
📅 Built 1377–1388⛰️ Carpathian pass👥 800 K visitors/year
Official Site
Hohenzollern Castle
Bisingen, Germany
A hilltop castle rising 855 m above the Swabian Alps — ancestral seat of the Prussian royal house and German emperors. The current neo-Gothic structure (rebuilt 1846–1867) sits atop ramparts with 360-degree views. Houses the Prussian Crown and Frederick the Great's personal effects.
📅 Rebuilt 1846–1867⛰️ 855 m elevation👑 Hohenzollern dynasty
Official Site
Alcázar of Segovia
Segovia, Spain
A ship-shaped fortress on a rocky crag at the confluence of two rivers — one of the inspirations for Disney's Cinderella Castle. Originally a Roman fort, expanded into a royal palace where Isabella I was crowned Queen of Castile in 1474. Its pointed turrets and slate roofs define the Segovia skyline.
📅 12th century🏛️ UNESCO (Old Town)👑 Coronation site
Official Site
Conwy Castle
Conwy, Wales
One of Edward I's iron ring of fortresses built to subdue Wales — completed in just four years (1283–1287) at enormous cost. Eight massive round towers, two barbicans, and a great hall surviving to full height. The town walls encircling Conwy survive intact with 21 towers.
📅 Built 1283–1287🏛️ UNESCO🛡️ 8 towers
Official Site
Castelo de São Jorge
Lisbon, Portugal
A Moorish castle crowning Lisbon's highest hill with sweeping views over the Tagus River and the city's terracotta rooftops. Fortified since the Iron Age, captured by crusaders in 1147. Eleven towers connected by battlements, an archaeological site with Moorish-era ruins, and peacocks roaming the gardens.
📅 11th century (Moorish)🛡️ 11 towers🌇 Panoramic views
Official Site
Castel del Monte
Andria, Italy
A mysterious octagonal castle built in 1240 by Holy Roman Emperor Frederick II. Every element follows the number eight — octagonal plan, eight octagonal towers, eight rooms on each floor. No moat, no drawbridge, no stable — its true purpose (hunting lodge, temple, astronomical instrument) remains debated.
📅 Built 1240🏛️ UNESCO📐 Octagonal geometry
Official Site
Pena Palace
Sintra, Portugal
A Romanticist palace on a hilltop above Sintra painted in vivid red and yellow — a deliberate mix of Gothic, Manueline, Moorish, and Renaissance styles. Built in 1854 atop a ruined monastery. The surrounding park features exotic trees from every Portuguese colony. Visible from Lisbon on clear days.
📅 Built 1842–1854🏛️ UNESCO🌈 Colourful facade
Official Site
Predjama Castle
Postojna, Slovenia
A castle built into the mouth of a 123-m-high cliff face — the largest cave castle in the world. A network of secret tunnels through the karst rock behind it made it impregnable for centuries. Legend holds the robber baron Erazem used the cave passages to resupply during a year-long siege.
📅 13th century📐 123 m cliff🦇 Cave tunnels behind
Official Site
Kronborg Castle
Helsingør, Denmark
The Renaissance castle immortalized as Elsinore in Shakespeare's Hamlet. Strategically positioned on the narrowest point of the Øresund strait to collect tolls from every passing ship for 400 years. Massive casements, a 62-m-long ballroom, and Holger Danske's statue sleeping in the cellar.
📅 Built 1574–1585🏛️ UNESCO🎭 Shakespeare's Elsinore
Official Site
Heidelberg Castle
Heidelberg, Germany
A partly ruined Renaissance castle on a forested hillside 80 m above the Old Town — one of the most romantic ruins in Germany. Destroyed twice by Louis XIV's troops and struck by lightning, it was left unrestored as a deliberate symbol of transience. The world's largest wine barrel (228,000 litres) sits in its cellar.
📅 13th century🍷 World's largest barrel📐 80 m above town
Official Site
Burg Eltz
Wierschem, Germany
A medieval castle rising from a rocky spur surrounded on three sides by the Elzbach River — never destroyed, continuously owned by the same family for 33 generations since 1157. Eight towers cluster above the forest canopy. The treasury holds 500+ works of gold, silver, porcelain, and weapons spanning 800 years.
📅 Since 1157👑 Same family 33 generations🌲 Forest setting
Official Site
Malbork Castle
Malbork, Poland
The largest castle in the world by surface area — a colossal red-brick fortress built by the Teutonic Knights as their headquarters in 1274. Three concentric castles-within-castles covering 21 hectares on the banks of the Nogat River. Heavily restored after WWII destruction, now a museum of medieval armour and amber.
📅 Built 1274🏛️ UNESCO📐 21 hectares (largest by area)
Official Site
Eilean Donan Castle
Dornie, Scotland
Scotland's most photographed castle — a 13th-century fortress on a tidal island where three lochs meet. Destroyed by Royal Navy bombardment in 1719 during a Jacobite uprising, then painstakingly rebuilt (1919–1932) as a romantic Highland icon. The stone bridge approach against a backdrop of misty mountains is instantly recognizable.
📅 13th century / rebuilt 1919🏝️ Tidal island🎬 Highlander filming
Official Site
Warwick Castle
Warwick, England
A medieval fortress on a bend of the River Avon — one of England's finest and most complete castles. Built by William the Conqueror in 1068, expanded with massive towers by the Beauchamps. Caesar's Tower and Guy's Tower dominate the skyline. Now an immersive attraction with trebuchets, jousting, and the world's largest working catapult.
📅 Founded 1068🛡️ Complete medieval fortress🎯 Trebuchet demonstrations
Official Site
Lovrijenac Fortress
Dubrovnik, Croatia
A dramatic cliff-top fortress perched 37 m above the Adriatic outside Dubrovnik's western walls — known as "Dubrovnik's Gibraltar." Triangular in plan with walls up to 12 m thick on the seaward side. Famously used as the Red Keep in Game of Thrones. Above its entrance: "Non bene pro toto libertas venditur auro" (Freedom is not sold for all the gold).
📅 11th century🎬 Game of Thrones📐 12 m thick walls

Château de Chillon
Veytaux, Switzerland
A medieval island castle on the shore of Lake Geneva beneath snow-capped Alps — Switzerland's most visited historic building. 400 years of Savoy rule left behind Gothic halls, painted chapels, underground vaults, and the dungeon that inspired Lord Byron's poem "The Prisoner of Chillon" (1816). Visible from Montreux promenade.
📅 12th century🏔️ Lake Geneva setting📖 Byron's poem
Official Site
Hohensalzburg Fortress
Salzburg, Austria
One of the largest and best-preserved medieval fortresses in Europe — perched 506 m above Salzburg's baroque Old Town. Built in 1077 and expanded over 600 years, it was never conquered. Reached by funicular or a steep 20-minute walk. The princely chambers, a 200-pipe organ, and 360-degree Alpine panoramas await at the top.
📅 Built 1077📐 250 m long🏛️ UNESCO (Old Town)
Official Site
Buda Castle
Budapest, Hungary
The historical palace complex of the Hungarian kings atop Castle Hill on the Buda side of the Danube. First completed in 1265, destroyed and rebuilt multiple times. The current neo-Baroque palace (1896–1912) houses the Hungarian National Gallery and Budapest History Museum. The Fisherman's Bastion and Matthias Church sit beside it.
📅 Founded 1265🏛️ UNESCO🌇 Danube panorama

Corvin Castle
Hunedoara, Romania
A Gothic-Renaissance castle considered one of the largest in Europe — built on a rock above the Zlaști River with a dramatic 30-m bridge approach. Commissioned in 1446 by John Hunyadi, father of King Matthias Corvinus. Tall buttressed towers, a Knights' Hall with rose-coloured marble columns, and a bear pit in the courtyard.
📅 Built 1446📐 7,000 m² floor🌉 30 m bridge entry
Official Site
Castel Sant'Angelo
Rome, Italy
A cylindrical fortress on the banks of the Tiber — originally Emperor Hadrian's mausoleum (139 AD), later a papal fortress, prison, and refuge connected to the Vatican by an elevated passetto. The rooftop terrace where Puccini's Tosca leaps to her death offers one of Rome's finest panoramas. A bronze archangel crowns the summit.
📅 Built 139 AD🏛️ Mausoleum → fortress🎭 Puccini's Tosca
Official Site
Mont Saint-Michel
Normandy, France
A fortified abbey-island rising 80 m from vast tidal flats — one of the most recognizable silhouettes in the world. A Benedictine monastery since 966, surrounded by medieval ramparts and a village clinging to the granite rock. At high tide the island is completely surrounded by the sea; at low tide you can walk across the sand.
📅 Founded 966🏛️ UNESCO🌊 Tidal island
Official Site
Château d'If
Marseille, France
A fortress on a tiny limestone island in the Bay of Marseille — made world-famous by Alexandre Dumas as the prison of Edmond Dantès in The Count of Monte Cristo. Built in 1524 by Francis I, it served as a state prison for nearly 400 years. The 20-minute boat ride from the Old Port reveals the castle rising sheer from the Mediterranean.
📅 Built 1524🏝️ Island fortress📖 Count of Monte Cristo
Official Site
Drottningholm Palace
Stockholm, Sweden
The private residence of the Swedish royal family — a 17th-century Baroque palace on Lovön island modelled after Versailles. The 18th-century court theatre still stages operas using original 230-year-old stage machinery. Formal gardens, an exotic Chinese Pavilion, and Lake Mälaren surround it. Open to visitors year-round despite being the active royal home.
📅 Built 1662🏛️ UNESCO👑 Active royal residence
Official Site
Dublin Castle
Dublin, Ireland
The seat of British rule in Ireland for over 700 years — built on the site of a Viking settlement in 1204. The medieval Record Tower is the only surviving original structure; the rest evolved into Georgian and Gothic Revival state apartments. Now used for presidential inaugurations and state receptions. The Chester Beatty Library, one of Europe's finest collections of manuscripts, sits in its grounds.
📅 Founded 1204🏛️ State apartments📚 Chester Beatty Library
Official Site
Cité de Carcassonne
Carcassonne, France
Europe's largest medieval walled city — a double ring of ramparts with 52 towers enclosing a complete fortified town on a hilltop above the Aude River. Inhabited since the Neolithic, fortified by Romans, Visigoths, and finally the French crown. Viollet-le-Duc's 19th-century restoration (controversial but spectacular) gave it the fairy-tale profile visible for miles.
📅 Pre-Roman – 13th century🏛️ UNESCO📐 52 towers, 3 km walls
Official Site
Schönbrunn Palace
Vienna, Austria
The 1,441-room summer residence of the Habsburgs — Austria's answer to Versailles. Maria Theresa transformed it into a Rococo masterpiece in the 1740s; the six-year-old Mozart performed in its Mirror Room. The formal gardens stretch to the Gloriette hilltop arcade. A zoo (the world's oldest, 1752), maze, and orangery occupy the grounds.
📅 Built 1696–1712🏛️ UNESCO📐 1,441 rooms
Official Site
Castello Sforzesco
Milan, Italy
A massive 15th-century fortress in the heart of Milan built by Francesco Sforza on the ruins of a Visconti castle. The 70-m Torre del Filarete guards the entrance; inside are courtyards hosting museums with Michelangelo's final sculpture (Rondanini Pietà), Leonardo's ceiling frescoes, and an Egyptian collection. Parco Sempione stretches behind it.
📅 Built 1450🎨 Michelangelo + Leonardo📐 70 m tower
Official Site
Middle East & North Africa
Krak des Chevaliers
Homs Governorate, Syria
The best-preserved Crusader castle in the world — T. E. Lawrence called it "the finest castle in the world." A concentric fortress with two massive walls, a glacis, and a 60-m-wide moat. Held by the Knights Hospitaller for 129 years (1142–1271) and never taken by direct assault.
📅 12th century🏛️ UNESCO🛡️ Concentric design
UNESCO
Citadel of Saladin
Cairo, Egypt
A medieval Islamic fortification on the Mokattam hills overlooking Cairo — the seat of Egyptian government for nearly 700 years. Saladin began construction in 1176 to defend against Crusaders. The Muhammad Ali Mosque (Alabaster Mosque) dominates the skyline with its Ottoman domes and 82-m minarets.
📅 Built 1176–1183🏛️ UNESCO (Historic Cairo)🕌 Alabaster Mosque

Aït Benhaddou
Ouarzazate, Morocco
A fortified village (ksar) of earthen clay buildings clustered along a hillside above the Ounila River. A former caravan stop on the route between the Sahara and Marrakech. Its dramatic silhouette has appeared in Gladiator, Game of Thrones, and Lawrence of Arabia. Families still live within the walls.
📅 11th century🏛️ UNESCO🎬 Major film location
UNESCO
Topkapi Palace
Istanbul, Turkey
The primary residence of Ottoman sultans for nearly 400 years (1465–1856) — a sprawling complex of courtyards, pavilions, and gardens on a promontory overlooking the Bosphorus and Golden Horn. Houses the Imperial Treasury (86-carat Spoonmaker's Diamond), the Prophet Muhammad's mantle and sword, and the Imperial Harem of 400 rooms.
📅 Built 1459–1465🏛️ UNESCO📐 700,000 m² grounds
Official Site
Citadel of Aleppo
Aleppo, Syria
One of the oldest and largest castles in the world — a massive fortified palace atop a tell (artificial mound) rising 50 m above Aleppo's Old City. Continuously occupied since the 3rd millennium BC by Hittites, Greeks, Byzantines, Ayyubids, and Mamluks. The glacis (sloped stone wall) and arched entrance bridge are iconic. Damaged in the 2012–2016 conflict, now under restoration.
📅 3rd millennium BC🏛️ UNESCO📐 50 m mound
UNESCO
Asia
Himeji Castle
Himeji, Japan
Japan's most spectacular original castle — a brilliant white five-story keep (the "White Heron") that survived WWII bombing, earthquakes, and 400 years of history without major damage. Completed in 1609, its complex of 83 buildings features ingenious defensive mazes, hidden gun ports, and stone-dropping windows.
📅 Completed 1609🏛️ UNESCO📐 83 buildings
Official Site
Matsumoto Castle
Matsumoto, Japan
The "Crow Castle" — one of Japan's twelve original castles with a striking black exterior that contrasts against the Japanese Alps behind it. A flatland castle (hirajiro) built in 1594 with an attached moon-viewing turret added specifically for gazing at the night sky. Six stories, steep wooden stairs, original keep.
📅 Built 1594🗾 National Treasure🏔️ Alps backdrop
Official Site
Mehrangarh Fort
Jodhpur, India
A colossal fortress rising 125 m above Jodhpur's "Blue City" on a sheer cliff face. Founded in 1459, its walls are up to 36 m high and 21 m wide. Inside: intricately carved sandstone palaces, mirrored halls, and a museum. The city's blue-painted houses stretch to the horizon below the ramparts.
📅 Founded 1459📐 125 m above city🔵 Blue City views
Official Site
Amber Fort
Jaipur, India
A Rajput fortress-palace of red sandstone and marble overlooking Maota Lake. Built by Maharaja Man Singh I in 1592, its Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace) is covered in tiny mirrors that turn candlelight into a starfield. The 11-km outer wall snakes over the Aravalli hills like a mini Great Wall.
📅 Built 1592🏛️ UNESCO (Jaipur)✨ Mirror Palace

Forbidden City
Beijing, China
The world's largest palace complex — 980 buildings across 72 hectares, enclosed by a moat and a 7.9-m wall. Imperial seat of power for 24 emperors across two dynasties (Ming and Qing) over 500 years. The Hall of Supreme Harmony is the largest surviving wooden structure of its type in China.
📅 Built 1406–1420🏛️ UNESCO📐 980 buildings
Official Site
Osaka Castle
Osaka, Japan
A five-story castle tower rising from massive stone walls above a double moat — the symbol of Osaka. Built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in 1583 to unify Japan, destroyed and rebuilt multiple times. The current ferro-concrete reconstruction (1931) houses a museum of the siege of Osaka. Cherry blossom season transforms the surrounding park into a pink canopy.
📅 Founded 1583🌸 Cherry blossom park📐 Largest stone walls in Japan
Official Site
Gyeongbokgung Palace
Seoul, South Korea
The largest of the Five Grand Palaces of the Joseon Dynasty — built in 1395 as the main royal palace of Korea for 500 years. 330 buildings across a 40-hectare site backed by Bukhansan Mountain. The changing of the guard ceremony at Gwanghwamun Gate and the Gyeonghoeru Pavilion floating on a lotus pond are highlights.
📅 Built 1395📐 330 buildings👑 Joseon Dynasty
Official Site
Red Fort
Delhi, India
A massive red sandstone fortress complex built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1639 when he moved his capital from Agra to Delhi. Walls rise 33 m from a moat fed by the Yamuna River. Inside: the Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience) once held the Peacock Throne, marble pavilions, and irrigated gardens. India's Prime Minister raises the flag here every Independence Day.
📅 Built 1639–1648🏛️ UNESCO📐 254 acres
Official Site
Potala Palace
Lhasa, Tibet, China
A 13-story fortress-palace rising 117 m from the Red Hill at 3,700 m altitude — the winter residence of the Dalai Lamas from 1649 until 1959. The White Palace (living quarters) and Red Palace (religious halls) contain over 1,000 rooms, 10,000 shrines, and 200,000 statues. The highest ancient palace in the world.
📅 Built 1645–1694🏛️ UNESCO⛰️ 3,700 m altitude
UNESCO
Americas
Castillo San Felipe del Morro
San Juan, Puerto Rico
A 16th-century Spanish citadel guarding the entrance to San Juan Bay — six levels of fortification rising from sea level to 43 m. Built to defend against Caribbean pirates and foreign navies, it withstood attacks by Drake, the Dutch, and the US Navy. Iconic lighthouse at the tip of the headland.
📅 Built 1539–1790🏛️ UNESCO📐 6 levels
NPS
Chapultepec Castle
Mexico City, Mexico
The only royal castle in the Americas — built atop Chapultepec Hill (2,325 m) and used as a residence by Emperor Maximilian I and later Mexican presidents. Now houses the National Museum of History. Murals by Siqueiros and O'Gorman, panoramic terraces over Mexico City, and the Bosque de Chapultepec below.
📅 Built 1785⛰️ 2,325 m hilltop🏛️ National Museum
Official Site
Castillo de San Marcos
St. Augustine, Florida, United States
The oldest masonry fort in the continental United States — built of coquina (compressed seashells) that absorbed cannonballs instead of shattering. Constructed by Spain in 1672 to protect the oldest European settlement in the US. Never taken by force in over 300 years of active military use.
📅 Built 1672–1695🏛️ National Monument🐚 Coquina stone
NPS
Africa
Fasil Ghebbi
Gondar, Ethiopia
A royal enclosure of castles and palaces from the 17th century — often called "Africa's Camelot." Emperor Fasilides built the first castle in 1636, and successive emperors added their own. The compound mixes Ethiopian, Portuguese, Moorish, and Indian architectural influences behind a 900-m stone wall.
📅 Built 1636🏛️ UNESCO📐 7 hectares
UNESCO
Cape Coast Castle
Cape Coast, Ghana
A whitewashed slave-trading fortress on Ghana's coast — originally built by Sweden in 1653 and later expanded by Britain. Tens of thousands of enslaved Africans passed through the "Door of No Return" to ships bound for the Americas. Now a museum and memorial; the dungeons below the governor's quarters remain intact.
📅 Built 1653🏛️ UNESCO🚪 Door of No Return
Ghana Museums
