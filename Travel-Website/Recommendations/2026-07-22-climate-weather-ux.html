💡 Improvement Ideas

Proposals only — never implemented by this routine. Ranked roughly by impact-to-effort.

#1
Climate-Finder cards: rainfall indicator

Add a small "☔ Wet" / "☀️ Dry" badge or a rain-days bar to each result card for the selected month. The rain and rain_days fields are already in window.TravelClimate — no new data needed. A traveler choosing between Lima and Lisbon in March needs wet/dry context as much as temperature.

#2
Climate-Finder: add a rainfall filter

A third control alongside the temperature sliders: a "Rainy days per month" range (0–5 / 5–12 / 12+). Pairs naturally with temperature to surface genuinely pleasant destinations — e.g. "22–28°C, under 6 rainy days" surfaces the Mediterranean in May/June rather than Bangkok in March.

#3
Climate presets / one-tap filters

Pre-fill the sliders with named comfort profiles: "☀️ Hot & Dry" (28–36°C, ≤4 rain days), "🌤 Perfect Summer" (22–28°C), "🧥 Cool City Break" (10–18°C), "⛷ Ski Season" (−10–4°C). Reduces the configuration burden for first-time visitors and makes the tool feel opinionated rather than mechanical.

#4
Weather.html → Climate-Finder cross-link: "Best months for [City]"

Below the city panel, show a row: "Best months (18–27°C, ≤8 rain days): Apr · May · Jun · Oct" — computed live from that city's CLIMATE entry. Clicking a month jumps to Climate-Finder pre-filtered to that month and temp band. Ties the two tools together into a research loop rather than leaving them as independent pages.

#5
Climate-Finder: city-name search to filter results

Currently there is no way to find a specific city in the results grid — you have to scan visually through 226 cards. A small search input that filters the rendered cards by city name (client-side, instant) would help users who know they want Paris but want to see its specific month/temperature data in context of the other results.

#6
Compare two cities in Weather.html

A "Compare" toggle that shows two city panels side-by-side (or stacked on mobile) — same month selector, both updating together. The infrastructure is already there; it's a layout and state change, not new data. Particularly useful when choosing between two finalist destinations.

#7
Shareable search-state links from Climate-Finder

Climate-Finder already writes URL params (?mon=, minc=, maxc=, u=) — but there is no share button or visible URL update as the user adjusts filters. A small "Copy link" pill beneath the results summary would make the tool shareable: e.g. "Mediterranean summer: warm & dry in August." Zero new data; just surfacing the existing state mechanism.
