Improvement ideas — look, function & feature
JSAuto-compute section counts from the DOM
All four hardcoded count badges (Schengen, Rest-of-world, Free arrival card, Visa-on-arrival) drifted this cycle. A small DOMContentLoaded script that counts .etype .row children per section and writes the result into each .etype-count span would keep these permanently accurate with zero manual maintenance — and prevent a repeat of this audit's most tedious fix.
Priority: High · Effort: Low (≈15 lines of JS)
FeatureExpand "Apply" button coverage
The JS APPLY object only injects a button for 12 of roughly 20 countries that have stable official portals. Missing: Jordan (evisa.jordan.gov.jo), Maldives (imuga.immigration.gov.mv), Nepal (online.nepalimmigration.gov.np — already cited in the rnote but no button), Bhutan (doi.gov.bt — same). Travelers at those rows have to copy the URL manually when an "Apply →" tap would do.
Priority: Medium · Effort: Low (add 4 object entries)
EditorialEgypt → reclassify to eVisa section
Egypt now has visa2egypt.gov.eg — US$30, approved in 24–72 hours, apply online before you land. That's operationally identical to Turkey's or Vietnam's eVisa. Keeping Egypt in "Visa required" (which reads as "embassy visit, complex") materially undersells how simple the process now is. Moving it to the eVisa section would set traveler expectations correctly.
Priority: Medium · Effort: Low (move one row + update section count)
EditorialBhutan and Nepal — wrong section
Both are in "Free arrival card — file online before you land." The section description says "No visa." Neither qualifies: Bhutan requires a paid visa + US$100/night SDF; Nepal is a paid visa-on-arrival (USD 30/50/125 tiers). Bhutan fits "Visa required"; Nepal fits "Visa on arrival." The mismatch sets incorrect expectations — travelers in the "no visa" section don't expect to pay for one.
Priority: High · Effort: Low (move two rows)
UXThailand pending-change — amber callout, not italic text
The 60→30-day cut caveat is currently inline italic inside a long rnote. A traveler booking a 45-day trip to Thailand could easily miss it. For a policy change this consequential — the first 60-day visit after it takes effect would become illegal — an .enote amber callout directly below the row would make it visually impossible to overlook. The .enote class already exists on the page with exactly this visual treatment.
Priority: High · Effort: Low (wrap existing text in .enote)
JSETIAS countdown — no official date is set
The countdown targets new Date('2026-10-01'), but the EU has announced no specific launch date — only "sometime in 2026." When travelers see "X days until ETIAS," they assume it's a confirmed deadline. If the date slips (as ETIAS has done repeatedly), the counter will say "0 days" and then go negative. Consider replacing the day-count with "Q4 2026 est." until an official date is published.
Priority: Medium · Effort: Low (change countdown display string)
MobileSticky legend consumes too much mobile viewport
On screens ≤ 600px the legend renders the 6 entry-type pills in a single column, occupying ~280px before any visa content appears. The search row is also hidden on mobile, so the sticky block's only function there is the pill filter — which most visitors won't use. A "Filter by entry type ▼" disclosure button that expands/collapses the pills on mobile would reclaim those ~280px for content on the initial view.
Priority: Low · Effort: Medium (toggle + CSS)
ContentVietnam — 83 international entry points worth noting
Vietnam expanded to 83 international entry points in 2026, approving 41 new ones (seaports, border crossings, regional airports) in the first half of the year. The current rnote says "international airports." Travelers arriving on cruises or overland from Cambodia/Laos need to know their crossing is covered by the eVisa — and the broader list is a genuine selling point for flexibility.
Priority: Low · Effort: Low (one sentence addition to rnote)
