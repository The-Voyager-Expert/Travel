Day 5 — New Stops
Stop 1 Bargello Museum
Florence's 13th-century palace-turned-prison — Italy's first national museum (1865). Home to Donatello's bronze and marble David, Verrocchio's David, and Michelangelo's Bacchus and Brutus. World's finest concentration of Renaissance sculpture.
🏛 Closed Tuesday ⏰ ~1 hr 30 min 📍 Via del Proconsolo 4, Centro Storico 4.6★ WhichMuseum Photo: David by Donatello — CC BY-SA 3.0
Stop 2 Museo di San Marco
Fra Angelico painted a fresco in each of 44 monks' cells between 1438–1445 — the world's largest collection of his work. The Annunciation at the dormitory stairhead is a defining 15th-century painting. Savonarola's cell and crucifix preserved on the first floor.
🏛 Closed Monday ⏰ ~1 hr 30 min 📍 Piazza San Marco 1, San Marco 4.5★ WhichMuseum Photo: Fra Angelico Annunciation — CC BY 2.0
Stop 3 Brancacci Chapel
Masaccio began the fresco cycle in 1425, died at 27; Filippino Lippi completed it 60 years later. The Tribute Money and Expulsion from Eden pioneered atmospheric perspective in Western painting. Michelangelo studied here — Torrigiani broke his nose in these sessions.
🏛 Closed Tuesday ⏰ ~45 min 📍 Piazza del Carmine, Oltrarno 4.5★ WhichMuseum Photo: Tribute Money Masaccio — Wikimedia Commons
