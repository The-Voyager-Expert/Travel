Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Lava Butte
1. Lava Butte
↳ A 500-foot red cinder cone that erupted about 7000 years ago, ringed by the black basalt of the Lava Lands flow. A road spirals to the rim, where a lookout tower and crater trail open onto a sweep of Cascade peaks and the high-desert lava plain below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1.5 hr
⚠️ The visitor center and summit road open May 15 through September 15 only.
⚠️ Mid-June to early September the summit road is reached by shuttle from Lava Lands Visitor Center only — no personal vehicles allowed during shuttle hours.
📍 Lava Lands Visitor Center · 58201 S Hwy 97
🚕 5 min → Lava River Cave
2. Lava River Cave
↳ Oregon's longest lava tube at nearly one mile, carved by basalt flows over 80000 years ago. Walk through towering chambers and constricted passages with a rented lantern — a self-guided underground stretch that stays a steady 42 degrees no matter the season above.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Timed-Entry Ticket 4.6⭐ · recreation.gov
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1.5 hr
⚠️ Open for the season May through September only.
⚠️ Timed-entry tickets sell out 2 - 3 weeks ahead in summer — book before your trip. No walk-up when sold out.
📍 Lava River Cave · S Hwy 97
🚕 45 min → Paulina Falls
3. Paulina Falls
↳ A twin 80-foot waterfall where Paulina Creek pours over the rim of the Newberry Caldera. A short trail leads to an overlook at the lip and a second viewpoint at the base, where the two ribbons drop side by side into a rust-and-grey rock amphitheatre.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
⚠️ Paulina Lake Road closes mid-November through late May under snow — the caldera is reachable only in the warm months.
📍 Paulina Falls · Paulina Lake Rd
🚕 20 min → Big Obsidian Flow
4. Big Obsidian Flow
↳ Oregon's youngest lava flow — about 1300 years old — a glittering mile-wide field of black volcanic glass and chalk-white pumice inside the Newberry Caldera. A one-mile interpretive loop climbs onto the flow itself, with Paulina and East Lakes spreading out below.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ The trail climbs onto loose obsidian and pumice — stay on the path, and skip it in rain or snow when the glass turns slick.
📍 Big Obsidian Flow Trailhead · Paulina Lake Rd
🚕 55 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 40 min → Smith Rock State Park
1. Smith Rock State Park
↳ World-class basalt formations rising 650 ft above the Crooked River gorge. The 3.4-mile River Trail loops through cathedral spires, past Monkey Face column and Morning Glory Wall, with sweeping canyon views the entire way.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~3 hr
⚠️ Parking fills by 9:00am on summer weekends — arrive early or use the overflow lot north of the park.
📍 9241 NE Crooked River Dr · Terrebonne
🚕 8 min → Peter Skene Ogden Viewpoint
2. Peter Skene Ogden Viewpoint
↳ At Peter Skene Ogden State Scenic Viewpoint the Crooked River cuts a sheer 300-foot slot through the basalt, spanned by three historic bridges side by side. A short cliffside path runs to railed overlooks straight down into the gorge — dramatic and reached in minutes from the car.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
⚠️ The rim is unfenced beyond the developed overlooks — the drop is sheer 300 feet, so keep back from the edge.
📍 Peter Skene Ogden Viewpoint · US-97 · Terrebonne
🚕 25 min → Pilot Butte State Scenic Viewpoint
3. Pilot Butte State Scenic Viewpoint
↳ An extinct cinder cone rising 500 feet above Bend, offering an uninterrupted 360° panorama of nine Cascade peaks — Mt. Bachelor, South Sister, Broken Top, Mt. Jefferson, and more. Drive or hike the paved 1-mile summit trail.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Summit road closed November through April.
📍 1310 NE Hwy 20
🚕 10 min → High Desert Museum
4. High Desert Museum
↳ A 135-acre natural history campus in the ponderosa pines south of Bend, bringing the high desert to life with live river otters, raptors, and porcupines alongside 19th-century settler cabins and rotating gallery exhibitions. The outdoor wildlife loop and historical trail run about two hours end-to-end.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Closed in January and February — verify dates before visiting in winter.
📍 59800 S Hwy 97
🚕 12 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 38 min → Tumalo Falls
1. Tumalo Falls
↳ A 97-foot ribbon of water plunging into a narrow basalt canyon, reached by a 3.4-mile round-trip trail through ponderosa pines along Tumalo Creek. One of the most dramatic single-drop waterfalls in Central Oregon.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~2.5 hr
⚠️ Road closed November through May.
⚠️ Parking lot fills by 9:00am on summer weekends — arrive before 8:30am or after 3:00pm.
📍 Tumalo Falls Day Use Area · Skyliners Rd
🚕 50 min → Sparks Lake
2. Sparks Lake
↳ A shallow lava-dammed lake on the Cascade Lakes Scenic Byway, ringed by marsh and black lava and backed by Mt. Bachelor and South Sister. The mirror-still water at first light is the scene photographer Ray Atkeson made famous — the 2.5-mile shoreline loop hugs the view.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5 hr
⚠️ The Cascade Lakes Highway closes under snow late November through late May, and the gravel access road is rough for low-clearance cars in early summer.
📍 Sparks Lake Day Use · Cascade Lakes Hwy
🚕 8 min → Devils Lake
3. Devils Lake
↳ A tiny glacier-fed lake so clear and shallow it glows an unreal Caribbean turquoise over its white pumice bed. A few steps from the byway pullout deliver the whole scene — float, wade, or just stand at the shore and take in one of the most startling colors in the Cascades.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
⚠️ On the Cascade Lakes Highway — closed under snow late November through late May along with the rest of the byway.
📍 Devils Lake · Cascade Lakes Hwy
🚕 50 min → Benham Falls
4. Benham Falls
↳ The Deschutes River squeezes through a narrow basalt channel and drops in churning cascades just north of Sunriver. A flat 2-mile round-trip trail through old-growth ponderosa pines leads to overlooks above the white water — an easy, dramatic finish to a day of Cascade alpine lakes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ The trailhead is reached via a short gravel road off Hwy 97 — closed under snow November through April.
📍 Benham Falls Trailhead · Deschutes National Forest
🚕 12 min → hotel
