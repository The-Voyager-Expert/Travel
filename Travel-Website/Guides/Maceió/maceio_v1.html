Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Pajuçara Natural Pools
1. Pajuçara Natural Pools
↳ Jangada rafts carry visitors 1 km offshore to a vast tidal reef where shallow saltwater pools form between coral heads. Best at low tide, when the pools reach waist depth and the water turns glass-clear turquoise.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2h
⚠️ Accessible at low tide only — check tide tables before heading out

🚕 12 min → Praça dos Martírios
2. Praça dos Martírios
↳ Maceió's grandest civic square, anchored by the neoclassical Palácio dos Martírios — former governor's residence, now a museum, draped in blue-and-white Portuguese azulejos. Imperial palms and period streetlamps line the esplanade, with the Teatro Deodoro facing across.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Praça dos Martírios · Centro
🚶 20 min · 🚕 8 min → Bairro do Jaraguá
3. Bairro do Jaraguá
↳ Maceió's original port district, where 19th-century merchant warehouses line cobblestone lanes to the working harbor. Painted colonial facades in ochre and terracotta frame the Capelinha de Nossa Senhora dos Navegantes — a seafarers' chapel barely larger than a living room, overlooking fishing boats and freighters.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Rua Sá e Albuquerque · Jaraguá
🚕 12 min → Mirante São Gonçalo do Amarante
4. Mirante São Gonçalo do Amarante
↳ A hilltop viewpoint where a single panorama holds the Atlantic coastline, the silver-blue expanse of Lagoa Mundaú, and the city skyline. The adjoining baroque church of São Gonçalo do Amarante is one of the oldest in Alagoas. Sunset turns the lagoon into a copper mirror.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rua Barão de Penedo · Petrópolis

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 40 min → Praia do Francês
1. Praia do Francês
↳ A wide horseshoe beach behind a natural reef, where a glass-bottom boat called Barco Cristal ferries visitors out to see staghorn coral, parrotfish, and reef rays through the hull. The outer reef wall drops into deep Atlantic blue — visible through the hull even without overhead sun.
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2h

🚕 12 min → Barra de São Miguel
2. Barra de São Miguel
↳ A thin peninsula at the mouth of the Lagoa Azul where the lagoon and the Atlantic run parallel, separated by a strip of white sand barely 50 meters wide. From the tip you can see both bodies of water — calm turquoise lagoon on one side, open ocean swells on the other — without moving your feet.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Av. Beira Mar · Barra de São Miguel
🚕 12 min → Marechal Deodoro Historic Center
3. Marechal Deodoro Historic Center
↳ Brazil's original Alagoas capital and birthplace of the first republic president. Cobblestone lanes of whitewashed baroque churches and 17th-century convents have barely changed since the colonial era. The Igreja e Convento de Santa Maria Madalena sits at the lagoon's edge, its bell tower doubling in the still water.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Praça da Imaculada Conceição · Centro Histórico
🚕 22 min → Praia do Gunga
4. Praia do Gunga
↳ A coconut-flanked peninsula at the Roteiro River mouth where rusted-red sandstone cliffs rise 20 m from turquoise water and a crescent of white sand separates the river lagoon from open Atlantic. Access is by boat from Barra do Roteiro — the absence of a road is precisely why it looks like this.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 5:30pm
⏰ ~2h

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Museu Théo Brandão
1. Museu Théo Brandão
↳ Housed in a 19th-century palacete on the Avenida da Paz, the museum documents Alagoas folk culture through textiles, religious objects, and carnival artifacts. The permanent collection includes Guerreiro costumes, Bumba Meu Boi figures, and pieces tied to the religious and festive traditions of the state.
📖 Wikipedia

🚕 18 min → Lagoa Mundaú Nine Islands
2. Lagoa Mundaú Nine Islands
↳ A schooner voyage through nine mangrove islands of Lagoa Mundaú, where stilt-house fishing communities appear between channels so narrow the hull brushes the roots. Guides point out roseate spoonbills, herons, and net-fishing techniques unchanged in a century. Returns to Pontal da Barra.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 10:00am
⏰ ~3h
⚠️ Schooner departures at 8:00am and 10:00am only
📍 Pontal da Barra · Bebedouro
🚕 55 min → Praia do Carro Quebrado
3. Praia do Carro Quebrado
↳ Layered red, white, and yellow sandstone cliffs rising 35 m above a strip of untouched beach with no road access. The only way down is by dune buggy from the plateau, dropping through a steep sand ramp to the shore. No kiosks, no parasols — just the cliffs and the South Atlantic.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Praia do Carro Quebrado · Barra de Santo Antônio
🚕 58 min → Farol de Ponta Verde
4. Farol de Ponta Verde
↳ A 19th-century lighthouse on the headland of Maceió's urban coast, still operational and accessible on foot. At low tide the receding ocean exposes a broad reef flat and a natural sandbar connecting the lighthouse base to outer rock outcroppings — the same reef system that feeds the Pajuçara pools further north.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Av. Álvaro Otacílio · Ponta Verde

1. Praia de Jatiúca
↳ Maceió's main surf beach runs for 2 km through the Jatiúca neighborhood, backed by coconut palms and beachfront kiosks. Northeast-facing and unobstructed, it draws local surfers from dawn and swimmers through the afternoon. The beach widens north toward the reef flats.

2. Praia de Jacarecica
↳ Seven kilometers north of the urban strip, Jacarecica is where the coconut grove comes down to the tide. The beach narrows between the palms and the breakers, with fishing skiffs hauled above the high-tide mark and no development beyond small kiosks.

3. Praia de Ipioca
↳ 24 km north of the hotel, Ipioca marks the boundary where the city's coastal reach runs out and the Atlantic takes over. Dense coconut palms close in to the dune line, and the beach runs for 5 km without development. No kiosks or parasols beyond a small strip near the access road.

2. Instituto Histórico e Geográfico de Alagoas
↳ Founded in 1869, IHGAL holds the most comprehensive archive of Alagoas history — 18th-century geographic manuscripts, Lampião's personal effects, and records documenting the Palmares quilombo. The museum display rooms on the ground floor are open to walk-in visitors; library access is by appointment.

3. Catedral Metropolitana de Maceió
↳ Built on the site of a 1611 chapel at Praça Dom Pedro II, the Metropolitan Cathedral was elevated to its current status when Pope Leo XIII created the Diocese of Alagoas in 1900. The neoclassical facade features twin bell towers visible across the plaza.

↳ The foundation holds nearly 3,000 works — sacred sculpture, colonial religious art, and Brazilian academic paintings assembled by Senator Pierre Chalita over five decades. The Baroque sacristy section and colonial gold-leaf altarpiece are the collection's core.

🌊 Water, in Layers

Maceió is the only city I researched where you see fresh water, salt water, reef water, and lagoon water all within the same day — and each is a genuinely different shade. The lagoon sits at grey-green, stained by mangrove tannins. The ocean at Pajuçara is shallow turquoise above the reef. At Gunga, where the Roteiro River meets the sea, there is a visible boundary line where two colors run parallel without mixing.

Sururu — the small blue mussel from Lagoa Mundaú — only exists there because of the brackish mix. The lagoon is the freshwater overflow from interior rivers meeting the tidal salt intrusion from the Atlantic. The mussel colonizes the exact salinity band where neither freshwater nor saltwater species can thrive. Maceió's most famous dish grows at a boundary.
