1. Lime Kiln Point State Park
↳ The only Washington state park designed specifically for watching whales from shore. Lime Kiln Lighthouse stands on the island's westernmost bluff above Haro Strait — one of the most reliable land-based orca-watching sites in the world. A seasonal naturalist station operates on site.

2. American Camp
↳ The 1859 American military encampment at the island's south end, now the southern unit of San Juan Island National Historical Park. Open coastal prairie rolls to bluffs above the English Channel. Officers' quarters and laundress quarters survive on site; bald eagles and red foxes are common in the open meadows.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Cattle Point Light
↳ A 1935 Art Deco lighthouse at the southeastern tip of San Juan Island, marking the southern entrance to San Juan Channel. The headland is open year-round with sweeping views toward the Olympic Mountains. Named in 1841 by the Wilkes Expedition — the first geographic feature named in the Pacific Northwest.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Cattle Point Rd · South End
🚕 15 min → The Whale Museum
4. The Whale Museum
↳ A research and interpretive museum in Friday Harbor dedicated to the cetaceans of the Salish Sea. Skeletal mounts, research displays, and a real-time sighting station track the Southern Resident killer whale pods. The museum operates the Orca Network hotline and stranding response program.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → English Camp
1. English Camp
↳ The British Royal Marines garrison at Garrison Bay, forming the northern unit of San Juan Island National Historical Park. A formal garden, barracks, blockhouse, and commissary survive from the 1860s joint-occupation period. The Young Hill trail begins at the site and climbs to the island's highest open summit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Mitchell Bay Rd · Garrison Bay
🚕 8 min → Roche Harbor
2. Roche Harbor
↳ A historic resort village at the island's north end built around a 19th-century lime works. Hotel de Haro is one of Washington's oldest continuously operating hotels. Grounds include the Art Deco McMillin Mausoleum in a forest clearing and the International Sculpture Park above the marina.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~2.5 hr

🕊️ A War Over a Pig

In June 1859, an American settler on San Juan Island shot a Hudson's Bay Company pig that had been rooting through his potato garden. What followed was one of the stranger episodes in US–British relations: each side sent troops, escalating to over 400 American soldiers and 5 British warships in the harbor, while the two commanding officers on the ground independently decided they had no intention of starting a war over the incident.

For 12 years, US and British soldiers occupied opposite ends of the same 55-square-mile island under formal joint military occupation, sharing whiskey and celebrating each other's national holidays. Kaiser Wilhelm I of Germany, asked to arbitrate, ruled in favor of the United States. The British garrison left peacefully. The pig, which started the whole thing, was the only casualty of the entire affair — and the reason Day 1 and Day 2 of this itinerary visit opposite ends of the same island.
