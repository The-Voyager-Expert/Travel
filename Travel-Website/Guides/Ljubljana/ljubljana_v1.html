1. Triple Bridge
↳ Plečnik's signature trio of bridges fans out from Prešeren Square across the Ljubljanica, the pink Franciscan Church rising behind. The two outer footbridges he added turned a single road crossing into the city's most photographed gathering place.

2. Ljubljana Town Hall
↳ The arcaded Renaissance-Baroque seat of the city since the Middle Ages, fronting Mestni trg. A copy of Robba's Fountain of the Three Carniolan Rivers stands outside, and the painted lanes of Mestni, Stari and Gornji trg unspool the Old Town from its door.

3. Ljubljana Castle
↳ The hilltop fortress crowning the city, its oldest walls medieval. Ride the funicular up for the glass viewing tower, the Museum of Puppetry, the ramparts and a 360° sweep over the red roofs toward the Kamnik Alps.

4. Dragon Bridge
↳ The Vienna Secession bridge guarded by four copper dragons — the winged beast that is Ljubljana's emblem. One of Europe's earliest reinforced-concrete arch bridges, and the city's most beloved photo stop.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 14 min · 🚕 5 min → Plečnik House
1. Plečnik House
↳ The preserved home and studio of Jože Plečnik, the architect who reshaped Ljubljana. A timed guided loop runs through his cylindrical glass-house addition, library and garden — kept exactly as he left it, now UNESCO-listed. Book the tour ahead at mgml.si.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Karunova ulica 4 · Trnovo
🚶 7 min · 🚕 4 min → National & University Library
2. National & University Library
↳ Plečnik's modernist masterpiece. A dim entrance stair of black marble climbs toward a light-filled reading room, while the facade weaves brick and stone like textile. Step into the hall to feel his idea of knowledge as ascent toward the light.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 10:00am - 7:00pm
🚫 Closed Saturday & Sunday
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Turjaška ulica 1 · Center
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Congress Square
3. Congress Square
↳ Ljubljana's grandest square, laid out in the early nineteenth century and landscaped by Plečnik. The leafy Zvezda park is ringed by the University, the Philharmonic and the Baroque Ursuline Church — UNESCO-listed as part of Plečnik's legacy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Kongresni trg · Center

4. Tivoli Park
↳ The city's great green lung — five square kilometres rolling up to wooded Rožnik hill. Plečnik's Jakopič Promenade runs its spine, lined with monumental lampposts and year-round open-air photography.

Postojna · 1 hr
↳ One of the world's great show caves: an electric train rolls deep into twenty-four kilometres of karst galleries and dripstone halls. The station sits a short walk-plus-shuttle to the cave entrance.

↳ Slovenia's third city beneath a dramatic hilltop castle — the ruins of the Counts of Celje's medieval stronghold with panoramic views over the Savinja valley, and a well-preserved old town of Baroque houses below. Direct IC trains run several times a day on the main Ljubljana–Maribor line.

↳ Slovenia's second city on the Drava, home to the world's oldest grapevine and a compact riverside Old Town of squares and wine cellars. Direct InterCity trains run several times a day.

🐉 City of the Dragon

Ljubljana wears two signatures at once. One is the dragon — coiled on the city's coat of arms and cast four times in copper over the Dragon Bridge — said to descend from the Argonauts and Jason, who legend has slaying a marsh beast where the city now stands. The other is a single architect: Jože Plečnik, who in the early twentieth century quietly rebuilt his hometown into a stage set of bridges, colonnades, squares and a library that reads like a temple. Almost every stop on this two-day route is either his work or framed by it, and UNESCO has since listed the ensemble as a rare case of a city shaped by one human imagination.

For a capital, it is improbably green and small. Cars are kept out of the centre, the Ljubljanica curls through café terraces, and Tivoli's forest begins a ten-minute walk from the cathedral — which is why Ljubljana was named European Green Capital. Two unhurried days is truly enough to know it, and the river, the castle hill and the espresso are never more than a few minutes apart.
