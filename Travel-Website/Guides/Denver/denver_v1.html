Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Union Station
1. Denver Union Station
↳ The 1914 Beaux-Arts terminal is the social heart of downtown — a restored Great Hall under soaring arched windows and brass chandeliers, lined with bars, cafés, and the boutique Crawford Hotel. Trains still run beneath it; the hall itself is the draw.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 5:30am - 12:00am
⏰ ~45 min
📍 1701 Wynkoop St · LoDo
🚶 28 min · 🚕 8 min → Colorado State Capitol
2. Colorado State Capitol
↳ The gold-domed seat of Colorado's government, its dome leafed in real Colorado gold. Free dome tours climb 99 steps to an open-air gallery with a sweeping Front Range panorama, and the west steps carry the brass marker reading exactly one mile above sea level.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 7:30am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Saturday & Sunday
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 200 E Colfax Ave · Capitol Hill
🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min → Denver Art Museum
3. Denver Art Museum
↳ The building alone justifies a stop: Daniel Libeskind's titanium-clad Frederic C. Hamilton Building is a jagged burst of angular shards, one of the boldest pieces of deconstructivist architecture in the country. Inside is a deep American Indian collection and strong modern and Western galleries.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Denver Art Museum · 4.2⭐ · denverartmuseum.org
🏛️ Thursday - Tuesday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Wednesday
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 100 W 14th Ave Pkwy · Golden Triangle
🚕 28 min → Red Rocks Park & Amphitheatre
4. Red Rocks Park & Amphitheatre
↳ A natural amphitheatre cradled between two 300-foot sandstone monoliths in the foothills west of the city, with near-perfect acoustics and a view straight back to the Denver skyline. Free to wander on non-concert days — climb the seating rows or walk the short trails through the red rock formations.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 14 min → Denver Botanic Gardens
1. Denver Botanic Gardens
↳ A 24-acre oasis a mile from downtown, laid out as a run of themed gardens — an alpine rock garden, water gardens, one of the largest Japanese gardens in the country, and the glass Boettcher Memorial Tropical Conservatory packed with rainforest plants. One of the finest botanical gardens in the country.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Denver Botanic Gardens · 4.8⭐ · botanicgardens.org
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 1007 York St · Cheesman Park
🚶 24 min · 🚕 8 min → Molly Brown House Museum
2. Molly Brown House Museum
↳ The 1889 Queen Anne and Richardsonian Romanesque mansion of Margaret "Molly" Brown — the Titanic survivor and reformer immortalized as the "Unsinkable Molly Brown." Guided tours walk its restored Victorian rooms, filled with period furnishings and her own belongings, behind an ornate rusticated-stone facade.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Molly Brown House Museum · 4.4⭐ · mollybrown.org
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr

3. Denver Museum of Nature & Science
↳ One of the largest natural-history museums in the western United States — towering mounted dinosaur skeletons, walk-through wildlife dioramas, a world-class gems-and-minerals hall, and the Gates Planetarium. Its north-facing windows frame downtown Denver against the full sweep of the Front Range.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Golden
1. Golden
↳ Colorado's former territorial capital, strung along Clear Creek at the foot of the foothills. The arched "Welcome to Golden" sign spans Washington Avenue above Victorian brick storefronts, kayakers and tubers ride the creek through town in summer, and North and South Table Mountains rise on either side.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Washington Ave · Downtown Golden
🚕 18 min → Buffalo Bill Museum & Grave
2. Buffalo Bill Museum & Grave
↳ On the crest of Lookout Mountain, the grave of William "Buffalo Bill" Cody lies behind an iron rail beside an observation deck with a sweeping view over Denver and the Great Plains. The grave and deck are free; the adjoining museum holds Wild West Show posters, costumes, saddles, and antique firearms.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 987½ Lookout Mountain Rd · Golden
🚕 20 min → Dinosaur Ridge
3. Dinosaur Ridge
↳ A tilted sandstone hogback where more than 250 Cretaceous dinosaur tracks and Jurassic fossil bones lie exposed along the roadside. This is where the first Stegosaurus and Apatosaurus were unearthed in 1877 — now a National Natural Landmark you can walk right up to on a self-guided road.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 16831 W Alameda Pkwy · Morrison
🚕 24 min → hotel

⛰️ Mile-High Notes

Downtown Denver sits crooked on purpose. The oldest streets — Larimer, Wynkoop, the whole LoDo core around your hotel — were laid out parallel to the South Platte River and Cherry Creek, so the historic center runs at a 45-degree diagonal to the strict north-south grid that governs the rest of the city. Once you notice the twist, it explains why Union Station and the 16th Street corridor feel like their own pocket of downtown.

And the "Mile High" line isn't one line. The Capitol's west steps have carried the exactly-5,280-feet mark in three different spots over the years, each re-surveyed as measurement got more precise. The current one is the brass word-marker reading "One Mile Above Sea Level" — stand on it and you're a mile up, give or take a re-survey.
