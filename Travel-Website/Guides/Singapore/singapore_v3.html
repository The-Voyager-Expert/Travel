Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → ArtScience Museum
1. ArtScience Museum
↳ Moshe Safdie’s lotus-flower–shaped museum at Marina Bay Sands — eleven petal galleries exploring art, science, and technology. The permanent teamLab Future World installation fills four rooms with walk-in reactive environments. The petals funnel rainwater into an indoor waterfall in the central atrium.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ ArtScience Museum · Marina Bay 4.5⭐ · artsciencentre.com.sg
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 6 Bayfront Avenue · Marina Bay Sands
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Marina Bay Sands SkyPark
2. Marina Bay Sands SkyPark
↳ The 57th-floor Observation Deck atop Marina Bay Sands — 200 metres above ground with a 360-degree view of the Singapore skyline, Marina Bay, and the Straits of Singapore. On a clear day the view extends south to the Riau islands. The public deck is accessible from Tower 3; the rooftop pool is hotel guests only.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Marina Bay Sands SkyPark · Bayfront 4.5⭐ · marinabaysands.com
🏛️ Daily 11:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 10 Bayfront Avenue · Tower 3
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Singapore Flyer
3. Singapore Flyer
↳ The 165-metre observation wheel on Marina Bay — largest in Asia on opening in 2008. Gondolas seat 28; one full rotation is 30 minutes with views from the CBD to the Straits of Singapore. The wheel rotates clockwise — a feng shui change: the original direction was deemed to drain fortune from the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Singapore Flyer · Marina Bay · 4.5⭐ · 52+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 30 Raffles Avenue · Marina Bay
🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Supertree Grove
4. Supertree Grove & Garden Rhapsody
↳ Eighteen vertical gardens, 25–50 metres tall — steel trunks clad in 162,000 plants that collect rainwater and photovoltaic energy. The free nightly Garden Rhapsody light-and-sound show synced to the trees runs at 7:45pm and 8:45pm. The OCBC Skyway bridge between two trees is ticketed, covered on Day 2.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 18 Marina Gardens Drive · Gardens by the Bay
🚕 15 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Cloud Forest
1. Cloud Forest
↳ Indoor cloud forest inside a glass dome — a 35-metre mountain draped in 60,000 tropical plants, with a walkway spiralling from summit to ground. The waterfall at the summit drops 35 metres through the plant-covered mountain face — the world's tallest indoor waterfall.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Cloud Forest + Flower Dome · Bayfront · 4.8⭐ · 23+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 18 Marina Gardens Drive · Gardens by the Bay
🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Flower Dome
2. Flower Dome
↳ The world's largest glass greenhouse — 1.28 hectares under one span. Nine zones recreate cool-dry subtropical landscapes from baobab groves to a Mediterranean garden. The central display beds change five to six times per year around themed floral shows. Covered by the same combo ticket as Cloud Forest.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Cloud Forest + Flower Dome combo · Bayfront · 4.8⭐ · 23+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 18 Marina Gardens Drive · Gardens by the Bay
🚶 2 min · 🚕 3 min → OCBC Skyway
3. OCBC Skyway
↳ A 128-metre walkway linking two Supertrees at the 22-metre level — open steel grid with a slight sway. Best close-up view of the Supertree structure and the Marina Bay skyline. Evening access puts you inside the Garden Rhapsody light show, playing at canopy level around you.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ OCBC Skyway · Gardens by the Bay · 4.6⭐ · 57+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 18 Marina Gardens Drive · Gardens by the Bay
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Marina Barrage
4. Marina Barrage
↳ A dam across the Marina Channel that created Singapore's first urban reservoir. The rooftop lawn is the kite-flying spot closest to the city centre — on weekends families fly box kites against the CBD skyline. The Sustainable Singapore Gallery inside explains the city's water strategy and is free.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 9:00am - 9:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 9:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 8 Marina Gardens Drive

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 15 min · 🚕 6 min → Asian Civilisations Museum
1. Asian Civilisations Museum
↳ Housed in the 1867 Empress Place Building on the Singapore River — the world’s leading pan-Asian art museum. Twelve galleries cover 20,000 years of Asian history. The Tang Shipwreck gallery holds the largest Tang dynasty cargo ever recovered from a single vessel — an Arab dhow sunk off Sumatra in the 9th century.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Asian Civilisations Museum · Civic District 4.5⭐ · nhb.gov.sg
🏛️ Saturday - Thursday 10:00am - 7:00pm · Friday 10:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 1 Empress Place
🚶 9 min · 🚕 4 min → Buddha Tooth Relic Temple
2. Buddha Tooth Relic Temple
↳ A five-storey Tang-dynasty-style Buddhist temple completed in 2007, anchoring Chinatown's South Bridge Road. The fourth floor enshrines a venerated tooth relic of the Buddha in a 3,500 kg gold stupa; the rooftop holds an orchid garden and a giant prayer wheel.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1h
🆓 Free
⚠️ Tooth relic viewing gallery open 9:00am - 12:00pm · 3:00pm - 6:00pm only
📍 288 South Bridge Road · Chinatown
🚕 9 min → Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple
3. Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple
↳ One of Singapore's oldest Hindu temples, founded in 1881 by Tamil immigrants on Serangoon Road and dedicated to the goddess Kali. It survived WWII bombing intact — locals took it as divine protection. The gopuram and shrines teem with brightly painted deity sculptures.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 5:30am - 12:00pm · 5:00pm - 9:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 141 Serangoon Road · Little India
🚶 18 min · 🚕 6 min → Sultan Mosque
4. Sultan Mosque
↳ Singapore's largest mosque and a national monument, first built in 1824 for Sultan Hussein Shah and rebuilt in its present form in 1932. The base of the great golden dome is ringed with the ends of glass bottles donated by poorer Muslims, so all could share in its construction.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Saturday - Thursday 10:00am - 12:00pm · 2:00pm - 4:00pm · Friday 2:30pm - 4:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Modest dress required — robes provided free at the entrance
📍 3 Muscat Street · Kampong Glam
🚕 12 min → hotel
Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Singapore Botanic Gardens
1. Singapore Botanic Gardens
↳ 74 hectares established in 1859 — the only tropical botanic garden on the UNESCO Heritage list. Heritage Trees along the Tanglin Core date to the 1860s; Swan Lake is the most photographed precinct. Free NParks concerts run Sunday mornings at Shaw Symphony Stage. Orchid Garden is ticketed on Stop 2.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 5:00am - 12:00am
⏰ ~1h 30 min
🆓 Free
📍 1 Cluny Road
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → National Orchid Garden
2. National Orchid Garden
↳ 1,000 species and 2,000 hybrids — the world's largest tropical orchid display. The VIP section has orchids named after heads of state, including the Princess Diana orchid and Mandela's Gold. The Cool House maintains cloud forest conditions for highland orchid species from Borneo and New Guinea.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ National Orchid Garden · Tanglin · 4.7⭐ · 71+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 1 Cluny Road · Botanic Gardens
🚕 12 min → MacRitchie Reservoir
3. MacRitchie Reservoir
↳ Singapore's oldest reservoir · 1868, inside the Central Catchment Nature Reserve — the last primary rainforest on the island. The boardwalk traces the water's edge; macaques, monitor lizards, and flying lemurs are common. The trail extends 11 km return to TreeTop Walk · Stop 4 through the forest canopy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Venus Drive Car Park · Central Catchment Nature Reserve
🚶 40 min · 🚕 15 min → TreeTop Walk
4. TreeTop Walk
↳ A 250-metre suspension bridge 25 metres above the forest floor in the Central Catchment Nature Reserve — a rare view into the rainforest canopy. Hornbills and flying squirrels use the same airspace. Access is one-way north to south by trail from MacRitchie; the bridge sways gently.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Friday 9:00am - 5:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 8:30am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~30 min
⚠️ Advance reservations required — quota limited, book before arrival

Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Fort Canning Park
1. Fort Canning Park
↳ The hill where Raffles built his residence in 1822, and site of the Battlebox — the bunker where Britain decided to surrender to Japan on 15 February 1942. The grounds include a replica 14th-century Malay fort gate and nine heritage gardens. The Battlebox museum inside the original tunnels is separately ticketed.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
⚠️ Battlebox Museum: Tuesday–Sunday 10:00am – 6:00pm — separately ticketed
📍 51 Canning Rise
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → National Gallery Singapore
2. National Gallery Singapore
↳ Southeast Asia's largest public art collection — over 8,000 works — inside the restored former Supreme Court and City Hall on the Padang, where the Japanese surrender was signed in 1945. A rooftop bridge joins the two monuments and opens a view across the Padang to Marina Bay.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ National Gallery Singapore · Civic District 4.5⭐ · nationalgallery.sg
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 1 St Andrew’s Road · Civic District
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → St Andrew’s Cathedral
3. St Andrew’s Cathedral
↳ Singapore's largest cathedral and a national monument — the island's only Gothic Revival cathedral, completed in 1862. Its brilliant white walls are Madras chunam, a polished plaster of shell lime, egg white, and sugar. Indian convict labour built it; the spire echoes Salisbury Cathedral.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 11 St Andrew’s Road · Civic District
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Clarke Quay
4. Clarke Quay
↳ The historic trading stretch of the Singapore River, where bumboats unloaded cargo into riverside godowns until the 1980s. The restored shophouse warehouses now hold restaurants and bars, and the promenade runs past Cavenagh Bridge and Boat Quay. Best after sunset, when the quay lights up.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 3 River Valley Road · Clarke Quay
🚶 12 min · 🚕 4 min → hotel
Day 6
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Katong
1. Katong & East Coast Peranakan Walk
↳ Katong — Singapore's Peranakan heartland — a grid of pastel shophouses little changed since the 1950s. The tour covers Joo Chiat Road's pre-war shophouses, East Coast hawker stalls, and Guan Hoe Soon · oldest Peranakan restaurant, 1953. At 328 Katong Laksa the laksa is cut short enough to eat with a spoon.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Dakota MRT Exit B
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Peranakan Mansion
2. Peranakan Mansion
↳ Baba-Nyonya material culture from the mid-19th to early 20th century — porcelain, furniture, silverware, and textiles in a restored Katong villa. The curator-led tour covers Peranakan wedding traditions, nyonya kebaya embroidery, and the Chinese-Malay-European synthesis defining the Peranakan aesthetic.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 11:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 214 Joo Chiat Road · Peranakan Mansion
🚕 20 min → Jewel Changi Airport
3. Jewel Changi Canopy Park
↳ Jewel Changi Airport's indoor garden, centred on the Rain Vortex — a 40-metre waterfall through a glass dome, the world's largest indoor waterfall. The Shiseido Forest Valley wraps five levels around the cascade. The rooftop Canopy Park has walking nets, glass-floor Sky Nets, glass bridges, and a hedge maze.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Jewel Changi Canopy Park · Changi · 4.5⭐ · 6+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Monday - Thursday 10:00am - 10:00pm · Friday - Sunday 10:00am - 11:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 78 Airport Boulevard · Changi
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Changi Chapel & Museum
4. Changi Chapel & Museum
↳ Memorial to 87,000 Allied POWs held at Changi from 1942 to 1945. The museum holds carved panels, letters, and personal effects made by prisoners. The replica timber chapel is the site’s spiritual focus. The Stanley Warren Murals, painted in secret by a British POW in 1942, are the defining artistic record.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1h
🆓 Free
📍 1000 Upper Changi Road North
🚕 25 min → hotel
Day 7
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Henderson Waves
1. Henderson Waves
↳ Singapore's highest pedestrian bridge at 36 metres — undulating above Henderson Road between Mount Faber Park and Telok Blangah Hill Park. Seven wave-shaped arches form hollow ribs with sheltered benches above the treeline. Part of the Southern Ridges trail crossing four parks from Labrador to Mount Faber.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Henderson Road · above
🚶 5 min · 🚕 5 min → Southern Ridges Walk
2. Southern Ridges Walk
↳ Nine kilometres through four parks from Labrador to Mount Faber — no road crossings. The Canopy Walk, a 280-metre walkway above Alexandra Road, gives the best view of Keppel Port and the Sentosa cable car. The ridge was cleared for military use before 1968; regrowth has since restored much of the original canopy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Southern Ridges
🚶 30 min · 🚕 10 min → Faber Peak Cable Car
3. Faber Peak Cable Car
↳ The Mount Faber Line runs from Faber Peak to HarbourFront and across to Sentosa, crossing the strait at 67 metres above the water. The gondola view looks down on Keppel Container Port and across the tanker anchorage. The roundtrip is the best elevated view of the Singapore Strait from any public transport.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Sentosa Cable Car · Mount Faber · 4.9⭐ · 16+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Daily 8:45am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 109 Mount Faber Road · Mount Faber Park
🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → AltitudeX
4. AltitudeX
↳ Outdoor adventure park at Faber Peak summit — Singapore's only urban ziplines · nine lines from 105 metres, a 38-metre abseil tower, and a SkyBridge walkway above the canopy. The zipline platforms look over Keppel Harbour and Sentosa. Advance booking required; slots sell out on weekends.

🍮 Local Tastes
Hainanese Chicken Rice
↳ Poached chicken and rice cooked in chicken stock, served with three dipping sauces: ginger-sesame, chilli-lime, and dark soy. Singapore’s national dish — slower-cooked and more precisely seasoned than its Hainanese ancestor.
📍 Tian Tian
Chilli Crab
↳ Sri Lanka crabs stir-fried in a sweet-spicy tomato-egg sauce — sweet from palm sugar, sour from vinegar, fiery from sambal. Mantou · fried buns served alongside to mop the sauce. Singapore’s most celebrated seafood dish.
📍 Jumbo Seafood · Long Beach Seafood
Laksa
↳ Rice vermicelli in a spiced coconut-prawn broth, garnished with cockles, fish cake, and laksa leaf. Katong-style is cut short enough to eat with a spoon. A Peranakan synthesis: Malay rempah spice paste, Chinese noodle technique.
📍 328 Katong Laksa · Sungei Road Laksa
Kaya Toast
↳ Charcoal-toasted bread spread with kaya · pandan-coconut-egg jam and cold salted butter, with soft-boiled eggs in dark soy and white pepper. The definitive kopitiam breakfast — pair with kopi · sock-brewed robusta with condensed milk.
