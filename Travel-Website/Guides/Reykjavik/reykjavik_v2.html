1. Hallgrímskirkja
↳ Iceland's tallest building and most recognisable landmark — a concrete expressionist church whose 74-metre tower offers a 360° panorama over the city and Faxaflói Bay. The Leif Eriksson statue stands at the entrance.

2. Settlement Exhibition
↳ Built around a Viking-age longhouse discovered in 2001 — the best-preserved 10th-century settlement remains in Reykjavík, displayed in situ beneath a glass floor with immersive multimedia archaeology.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 47 min → Þingvellir National Park
1. Þingvellir National Park
↳ A rift-valley national park on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge where the North American and Eurasian plates pull apart. Site of Iceland's Alþing parliament founded in 930, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2004.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Þingvellir National Park
🚕 50 min → Geysir & Strokkur
2. Geysir & Strokkur
↳ An active geothermal field in the Haukadalur valley with hot springs, mud pools and fumaroles. The fountain geyser Strokkur erupts every 6 to 10 minutes to 15 to 20 metres, beside the dormant Great Geysir that named all geysers.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Geysir Geothermal Area
🚕 13 min → Gullfoss
3. Gullfoss
↳ The "Golden Falls" — a two-tiered waterfall on the glacial Hvítá river dropping 11 metres then 21 metres into a canyon up to 70 metres deep. Saved from hydroelectric development and protected as a conservation site since 1979.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Gullfoss
🚕 52 min → Kerið
4. Kerið
↳ A volcanic crater lake in the Grímsnes area, formed roughly 6,500 years ago. The caldera is about 55 metres deep, with walls of red volcanic rock encircling vivid blue-green water.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Kerið Crater
🚕 1h 5min → hotel
Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 50min → Seljalandsfoss
1. Seljalandsfoss
↳ A 60-metre waterfall on the Seljalands river, fed by meltwater from the Eyjafjallajökull glacier-volcano. A footpath leads behind the falling water — one of few waterfalls in the world offering this view.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Seljalandsfoss
🚕 25 min → Skógafoss
2. Skógafoss
↳ A 60-metre-high, 25-metre-wide waterfall on the Skógá river in the village of Skógar. A staircase climbs to a viewing platform at the top, and the spray frequently throws rainbows across the falls on sunny days.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Skógafoss
🚕 25 min → Dyrhólaey
3. Dyrhólaey
↳ A 120-metre promontory and nature reserve forming Iceland's southernmost point, named for the large sea-eroded arch in its headland. Puffins nest on the cliffs from May to August, and a lighthouse crowns the summit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Dyrhólaey
🚕 20 min → Reynisfjara
4. Reynisfjara
↳ A black-sand beach near Vík backed by basalt columns and the Hálsanefshellir cave, with the Reynisdrangar sea stacks offshore. Powerful sneaker waves make the surf zone dangerous — a warning-light system marks restricted areas.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Reynisfjara Beach
🚕 2h 25min → hotel
Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 2h 20min → Búðakirkja
1. Búðakirkja
↳ An iconic black wooden church in the hamlet of Búðir, set in the Búðahraun lava field on the south coast of Snæfellsnes. The current church dates to 1848; its pitch-painted timber against the lava is among the most photographed in Iceland.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Búðakirkja
🚕 20 min → Arnarstapi
2. Arnarstapi
↳ A small fishing village on the southern side of Snæfellsnes, within Snæfellsjökull National Park. Known for dramatic basalt sea cliffs, the Gatklettur stone arch, summer seabird colonies, and a coastal trail to neighbouring Hellnar.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Arnarstapi
🚕 15 min → Djúpalónssandur
3. Djúpalónssandur
↳ A black-pebble beach within Snæfellsjökull National Park at the foot of the glacier. It holds four historic fishermen's lifting stones once used to test crew strength, and the rusted iron remains of the British trawler Epine, wrecked here in 1948.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Djúpalónssandur
🚕 1h 15min → Kirkjufell
4. Kirkjufell & Kirkjufellsfoss
↳ A 463-metre peak near Grundarfjörður, often called the most photographed mountain in Iceland, with the small Kirkjufellsfoss waterfall providing the classic foreground. It appeared as the "arrowhead mountain" in Game of Thrones.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Kirkjufell & Kirkjufellsfoss
🚕 2h 35min → hotel

Day 7
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 57 min → Blue Lagoon
1. Blue Lagoon
↳ Iceland's most famous geothermal spa — milky blue silica-rich waters set in a lava field, with in-water bar, silica face masks, and a multi-level retreat complex. Advance booking is mandatory; slots fill weeks ahead.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ bluelagoon.com
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~3h
📍 Norðurljósavegur 9 · Grindavík
🚕 26 min → Bridge Between Continents
2. Bridge Between Continents
↳ A short pedestrian bridge over the Reykjanes rift — the visible gap between the Eurasian and North American tectonic plates, one of the few places on earth where you can walk between continents above sea level.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Bridge Between Continents
🚕 10 min → Gunnuhver
3. Gunnuhver Hot Springs
↳ Iceland's largest hot-spring field — bubbling mud pools and roaring steam vents at the tip of the Reykjanes UNESCO Global Geopark. Named for a legendary ghost whose energy is said to rise here in perpetuity.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Gunnuhver
🚕 22 min → Valahnúkamöl
4. Valahnúkamöl Sea Cliffs
↳ Dramatic black-lava sea cliffs at the tip of the Reykjanes Peninsula, pounded by Atlantic surf and home to nesting seabirds. The ruins of Iceland's oldest lighthouse · 1878 stand at the cliff edge as a final landmark.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Valahnúkamöl

Day 8
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 2h 15min → Háifoss
1. Háifoss
↳ A 122-metre waterfall on the river Fossá at the inner edge of Þjórsárdalur near the volcano Hekla. One of the highest waterfalls in Iceland, it plunges into a deep, narrow canyon beside its smaller neighbour Granni.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Háifoss
🚕 1h → Sigöldugljúfur
2. Sigöldugljúfur
↳ A highland canyon lined with dozens of slender waterfalls cascading over moss-covered cliffs into a blue-green river, earning it the nickname Valley of Tears. The flow was reduced after the Sigalda hydropower plant opened in 1978.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Sigöldugljúfur
🚕 45 min → Landmannalaugar
3. Landmannalaugar
↳ A geothermal area in the Fjallabak Nature Reserve, famed for multicoloured rhyolite mountains, the Laugahraun lava field, and a natural hot spring beside the campground. It is the start of the Laugavegur trail.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~3h
📍 Landmannalaugar

Skyr
↳ Iceland's ancient dairy product — thick, mild, high-protein, and closer to strained yoghurt than cheese. Eaten as breakfast with berries and cream, or as a dessert. Icelanders have eaten it for over a thousand years.
📍 Café Loki
Kleinur
↳ Iceland's traditional twisted doughnut — a fried pastry dusted in sugar, lighter than a German Berliner, with a distinct cardamom-tinged flavour. Found in every bakery and petrol station across the country.

↳ Slow-smoked lamb, traditionally smoked over sheep dung and birch. The smoke is deep and complex; the meat is tender and slightly gamey. Served cold in thin slices on flatbread · flatkaka, or hot with potatoes and white sauce.

Kjötsúpa — Lamb Soup
↳ Iceland's national soup — bone-in lamb simmered for hours with root vegetables · turnip, rutabaga, carrot, potato and pearl barley. Thick, warming, and deeply savoury. The standard winter meal for a thousand years.
