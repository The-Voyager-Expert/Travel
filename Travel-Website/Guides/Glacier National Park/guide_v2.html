1.Lake McDonald
↳ The largest lake in Glacier National Park, stretching 10 miles through the western valleys with dense cedar forests lining the shore. The turquoise waters reflect the surrounding peaks, and pull-offs along the road offer excellent photography angles of the mountain backdrop.

2.Trail of the Cedars · Avalanche Gorge
↳ A 1-mile wheelchair-accessible boardwalk loop through old-growth western red cedar forest, descending to Avalanche Gorge where Cedar Creek has carved a narrow canyon with 100-foot walls. Mostly flat, 30 minutes round-trip. Cathedral-like canopy and cascading water throughout.

🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Near Avalanche Creek Campground
🚶 10 min · 🚕 8 min → Avalanche Lake Trailhead
3.Avalanche Lake
↳ A popular hiking destination with two options: a 1-mile flat boardwalk through wildflower meadows, or the full 5.9-mile round-trip to an alpine lake ringed by 2,000-foot cliff walls and waterfalls. The basin reflects granite peaks, creating a dramatic cirque landscape.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open daily dawn to dusk
⏰ 1 hr 30 min to 2 hr 30 min
📍 Avalanche Campground
📊 Elevation gain: 757 ft
🚙 40 min → Logan Pass
4.Logan Pass
↳ The highest point on Going-to-the-Sun Road at 6,646 feet, straddling the Continental Divide. The visitor center and panoramic viewpoints reveal dozens of glaciated peaks across the park's spine. Views shift from western cedar valleys to eastern alpine tundra.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Visitor center open 9am–5pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Mile 31.5 Going-to-the-Sun Road
🚶 2.7 mi RT · 🚕 5 min → Hidden Lake Overlook
5.Hidden Lake Overlook
↳ A 2.7-mile round-trip hike ascending a raised overlook with views into Hidden Lake basin. Mountain goats frequent exposed alpine ridges. Wildflower meadows bloom July–August, displaying pink primrose and glacier lilies against expanding Continental Divide vistas.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open daily dawn to dusk
⏰ 1 hr 30 min
📍 Logan Pass Visitor Center
📊 Elevation gain: 551 ft

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚙 15 min → Swiftcurrent Lake
1.Swiftcurrent Lake
↳ A pristine alpine lake in Many Glacier valley surrounded by 3,000-foot peaks and hanging glaciers. Turquoise water reflects Mount Cleveland and Mount Jackson. The historic boathouse offers boat shuttles reducing hiking distance to upper lakes by 2–4 miles.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open daily dawn to dusk
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Many Glacier parking area
Choose one: Grinnell Glacier · Iceberg Lake · or Cracker Lake
2.Grinnell Glacier Trail
↳ The flagship Many Glacier hike, climbing 2,047 feet over 10 miles to a receding glacier at a pristine cirque lake. Route traverses multiple turquoise lakes—Swiftcurrent, Lake Josephine, Grinnell—with boat shuttles available to reduce the hike to 7.6 miles.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open daily dawn to dusk
⏰ 3–5 hr
📍 Many Glacier Trailhead
📊 Elevation gain: 2,047 ft
3.Iceberg Lake Trail
↳ An accessible high-alpine option climbing 1,200 feet over 9.6 miles to a pristine lake where icebergs float year-round in summer, calved from hanging glaciers. Trail traverses subalpine forest and wildflower meadows with views of Mount Wilbur and Mount Jackson.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open daily dawn to dusk
⏰ 4–5 hr
📍 Many Glacier Trailhead
📊 Elevation gain: 1,200 ft
4.Cracker Lake Trail
↳ A less-crowded alternative with equal scenic reward, climbing 1,400 feet over 12.4 miles to a brilliant turquoise lake beneath Mount Siyeh. Trail passes through forest and subalpine terrain, emerging into an open basin with cascading streams and hanging glacier visible on the mountainside.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open daily dawn to dusk
⏰ 5–6 hr
📍 Many Glacier Trailhead area
📊 Elevation gain: 1,400 ft

🚗 Getting Around
🚗 Car Rental
Enterprise, Hertz, and Budget have desks at Kalispell Regional Airport (45 minutes from East Glacier). A personal vehicle is essential for accessing park roads and trailheads on Going-to-the-Sun Road.
🚎 Shuttle Services
Seasonal shuttle services run along Going-to-the-Sun Road and throughout Many Glacier area (July–early September). Historic Xanterra shuttle buses connect key trailheads and reduce driving stress on narrow mountain sections.
🚢 Boat Shuttles
Glacier Park Boat Company operates daily boat shuttles on Swiftcurrent Lake (Many Glacier valley) from 7am–5pm (seasonal). Shuttles reduce hiking distances by 2–4 miles on backcountry trail routes.
🚕 Ride Apps
Uber and Lyft have limited availability in East Glacier (rural Montana). Reliable car rental from Kalispell airport is the preferred option.
🍽️ Dining
Glacier Park Lodge Dining Room
Full-service restaurant within the historic lodge. Breakfast, lunch, and dinner featuring Montana beef and seasonal dishes. Reservations recommended for dinner during peak season.
Serrano's Pinon Cafe
Casual cafe in East Glacier village with sandwiches, salads, and local pastries. Popular with hikers and families. Located in East Glacier village center.
Johnson's of East Glacier
Traditional Montana restaurant serving burgers, steaks, and fly-fishing lodge fare. Mountain dining experience in a rustic setting.
Local Tastes
Montana fly-fishing cuisine dominates East Glacier—fresh trout prepared simply (pan-fried or grilled). Local lodges feature bison steaks and elk as signature proteins. The Blackfeet Nation reservation borders the park; Native cuisine influences regional dining.
🛣️ Scenic Drives
Going-to-the-Sun Road
The park's most iconic drive: 50 miles from west to east, crossing the Continental Divide at Logan Pass (6,646 ft). Switchbacks, cliff-side sections, and panoramic pullouts. Open seasonally (July–October typically; winter closure October–May due to snow). Allow 2–3 hours for the full drive without stops.
Many Glacier Road
A 12-mile scenic approach through subalpine forest to Many Glacier Valley. Leads to the park's densest hike trailheads and historic hotel. Turquoise alpine lakes visible en route. Open seasonally (late May–September).
Two Medicine Road
A quieter scenic route on the east side, featuring turquoise lakes, mountain views, and less foot traffic than Going-to-the-Sun Road. Historic Two Medicine Hotel at road's end. Open seasonally (June–October).
🦌 Wildlife Viewing
Mountain Goats
Frequently seen on alpine ridges, especially Hidden Lake Overlook and Gunsight Pass. White coats blend into snowfields; observe from a distance (25+ feet minimum).
Grizzly Bears
Present throughout the park; most active dawn/dusk. Make noise on trails, hike in groups, carry bear spray. Stay 100+ feet away if spotted. Bears are most active June–September.
Mule Deer & Bighorn Sheep
Mule deer common in lower valleys; bighorn sheep on higher ridges. Both are most visible at dawn and dusk. Do not approach or feed.
Eagles & Waterfowl
Bald eagles, osprey, and loons inhabit the park's lakes. Binoculars enhance viewing at Swiftcurrent Lake, Iceberg Lake, and Cracker Lake overlooks.
👨‍🎓 Ranger Programs
📅 Guided Ranger Walks
Free ranger-led walks depart daily from major trailheads and visitor centers. Topics include geology, ecology, wildlife, and park history. Half-day and full-day guided hikes available through concessionaires. Duration: 1–2 hours. No reservations needed; meet at posted times or ask at the lodge.
📅 Evening Campfire Programs
Ranger presentations held nightly at Many Glacier, Lake McDonald, St. Mary, and Apgar campgrounds during peak season. Programs cover bears, glaciers, trail safety, and indigenous history. Free and family-friendly. Typically 8pm–9pm; schedule varies by location.
👨‍🎓 Junior Ranger Program
Children ages 6–12 can participate in the Glacier Explorer badge program, earning badges through activities, hikes, and ranger interactions. Badge workbooks available at any visitor center for a small fee.
