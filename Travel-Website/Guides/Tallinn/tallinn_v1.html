Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 11 min · 🚕 6 min → Alexander Nevsky Cathedral
1. Alexander Nevsky Cathedral
↳ Tallinn's largest and most ornate Orthodox cathedral, crowned with black onion domes and a wall of bells. Built 1894–1900 atop Toompea hill, its mosaic-and-icon interior is a deliberate counterpoint to the Lutheran Old Town below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Lossi plats 10 · Toompea
🚶 4 min · 🚕 5 min → Kohtuotsa Viewing Platform
2. Kohtuotsa Viewing Platform
↳ The postcard view of Tallinn: a railed terrace on Toompea's edge looking over the red-tiled roofs, spires and medieval wall of the Lower Town to the harbour and bay beyond. Best in soft morning or late-afternoon light.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Kohtu 12 · Toompea
🚶 9 min · 🚕 6 min → Kiek in de Kök
3. Kiek in de Kök
↳ A 38-metre artillery tower from 1475 whose walls still hold 16th-century cannonballs. Six floors of fortification history connect underground to the Bastion Passages — cool stone tunnels dug beneath the ramparts.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Kiek in de Kök & Bastion Passages · 4.5⭐ · linnamuuseum.ee
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Komandandi tee 2 · Old Town
🚶 6 min · 🚕 6 min → Tallinn Town Hall Square
4. Tallinn Town Hall Square
↳ The heart of the Lower Town for eight centuries — a cobbled square ringed by pastel merchant houses and anchored by the Gothic Town Hall, the only surviving Gothic town hall in Northern Europe. Café terraces by day, festivals and markets by season.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Raekoja plats · Old Town
🚶 6 min · 🚕 5 min → St. Olaf's Church
5. St. Olaf's Church
↳ Once reputedly the tallest building in the world, this medieval church spire still defines Tallinn's skyline. A tight 258-step climb up the tower opens onto a narrow gallery with a sweeping 360° view over the Old Town and the sea.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ St. Olaf's Church Tower · 4.6⭐ · visittallinn.ee
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Lai 50 · Old Town

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 33 min · 🚕 9 min → Kadriorg Palace
1. Kadriorg Palace
↳ A rose-and-white Baroque palace begun in 1718 by Peter the Great for Catherine I, set in formal flower gardens and a wooded park. Inside, the Kadriorg Art Museum hangs Western European and Russian paintings beneath a lavish ceremonial hall.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Kadriorg Art Museum · 4.5⭐ · kadriorumuuseum.ekm.ee
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Weizenbergi 37 · Kadriorg
🚶 4 min · 🚕 4 min → KUMU Art Museum
2. KUMU Art Museum
↳ The main building of the Art Museum of Estonia and the country's flagship gallery — a sweeping limestone-and-glass structure carved into the Lasnamäe escarpment, named European Museum of the Year in 2008. Estonian art from the 18th century to today.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ KUMU Art Museum · 4.6⭐ · kumu.ekm.ee
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Weizenbergi 34 · Kadriorg
🚕 13 min → Seaplane Harbour
3. Seaplane Harbour
↳ The Estonian Maritime Museum set inside vast 1917 seaplane hangars — pioneering thin-shell concrete domes. Walk the deck of the British-built 1937 submarine Lembit, board ships and a replica Short 184 seaplane, and explore on three levels: sky, sea and underwater.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Seaplane Harbour · 4.6⭐ · meremuuseum.ee
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Vesilennuki 6 · Kalamaja

↳ A dark, rum-based Estonian liqueur spiced with cinnamon, vanilla and citrus oil — invented in Tallinn in 1960 and now the city's signature digestif, taken neat or in coffee.
