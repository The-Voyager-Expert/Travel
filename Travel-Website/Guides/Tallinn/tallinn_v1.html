Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 11 min · 🚕 6 min → Alexander Nevsky Cathedral
1. Alexander Nevsky Cathedral
↳ Tallinn's largest and most ornate Orthodox cathedral, crowned with black onion domes and a wall of bells. Built 1894–1900 atop Toompea hill, its mosaic-and-icon interior is a deliberate counterpoint to the Lutheran Old Town below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free

2. Kohtuotsa Viewing Platform
↳ The postcard view of Tallinn: a railed terrace on Toompea's edge looking over the red-tiled roofs, spires and medieval wall of the Lower Town to the harbour and bay beyond. Best in soft morning or late-afternoon light.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Kohtu 12 · Toompea
🚶 9 min · 🚕 6 min → Kiek in de Kök
3. Kiek in de Kök
↳ A 38-metre artillery tower from 1475 whose walls still hold 16th-century cannonballs. Six floors of fortification history connect underground to the Bastion Passages — cool stone tunnels dug beneath the ramparts.
📖 Wikipedia

4. Tallinn Town Hall Square
↳ The heart of the Lower Town for eight centuries — a cobbled square ringed by pastel merchant houses and anchored by the Gothic Town Hall, the only surviving Gothic town hall in Northern Europe. Café terraces by day, festivals and markets by season.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min

5. St. Olaf's Church
↳ Once reputedly the tallest building in the world, this medieval church spire still defines Tallinn's skyline. A tight 258-step climb up the tower opens onto a narrow gallery with a sweeping 360° view over the Old Town and the sea.
📖 Wikipedia

1. Kadriorg Palace
↳ A rose-and-white Baroque palace begun in 1718 by Peter the Great for Catherine I, set in formal flower gardens and a wooded park. Inside, the Kadriorg Art Museum hangs Western European and Russian paintings beneath a lavish ceremonial hall.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Kadriorg Art Museum · 4.5⭐ · kadriorumuuseum.ekm.ee
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr

2. KUMU Art Museum
↳ The main building of the Art Museum of Estonia and the country's flagship gallery — a sweeping limestone-and-glass structure carved into the Lasnamäe escarpment, named European Museum of the Year in 2008. Estonian art from the 18th century to today.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Seaplane Harbour
↳ The Estonian Maritime Museum set inside vast 1917 seaplane hangars — pioneering thin-shell concrete domes. Walk the deck of the British-built 1937 submarine Lembit, board ships and a replica Short 184 seaplane, and explore on three levels: sky, sea and underwater.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 6 min → St. Nicholas Church
1. St. Nicholas Church · Niguliste Museum
↳ A 13th-century Gothic merchants' church operating as the Niguliste Museum, home to Estonia's most important collection of medieval ecclesiastical art. The centrepiece is Bernt Notke's Dance of Death — a 7.5-metre fragment of a late-15th-century painting, one of the rarest surviving examples in Europe.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Niguliste Museum · 4.5⭐ · nigulistemuuseum.ekm.ee

2. Dominican Monastery
↳ A Dominican friary documented from 1246, partially surviving the 1524 Reformation destruction. The cloister, refectory, and chapter house ruins stand open to the sky; a lapidarium in surviving vaulted spaces holds Estonia's finest collection of medieval carved stonework.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Dominican Monastery · 4.5⭐ · kloostri.ee

3. Tallinn City Wall
↳ The most intact medieval city wall in the Baltic — 1.9 km of the original 2.35 km circuit still stands, with 26 of the original 46 towers surviving. The Hellemann Tower and the wall-walk section on Müürivahe Street offer a rampart stroll and connected tower interior.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Hellemann Tower & Wall Walk · 4.5⭐ · visittallinn.ee
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:30am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Müürivahe · Old Town
🚶 18 min · 🚕 6 min → Patarei Prison
4. Patarei Prison
↳ A Tsarist-era sea fortress built in the 1820s that served as a prison from the interwar period through 2002 — first under Soviet occupation, then independent Estonia. The brick complex stands largely unchanged, with preserved cells, a hospital block, and execution rooms open for self-guided visits.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Patarei Sea Fortress–Prison · 4.5⭐ · patareiprison.org
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min

🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Vabamu Museum
1. Vabamu Museum of Occupations and Freedom
↳ Estonia's foremost museum of the Soviet and Nazi occupation periods — five themed galleries tracing 1940–1991 through personal stories and artefacts. The permanent exhibition covers deportations, everyday life under occupation and the Singing Revolution that ended Soviet rule.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Vabamu Museum of Occupations and Freedom · 4.1⭐ · vabamu.ee
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Toompea 8b · Toompea
🚶 12 min · 🚕 6 min → KGB Prison Cells
2. KGB Prison Cells
↳ The basement cells of the former KGB headquarters at Pagari 1 — nerve centre of Soviet secret police operations in Estonia from the 1940s to 1991. Original interrogation rooms, solitary cells and holding areas are preserved as 'Locked Up Stories', tracing the fates of Estonians detained here.
📖 Wikipedia

1. Estonian Open Air Museum
↳ A 72-hectare open-air museum on the shores of Kopli Bay with 74 original rural buildings relocated from across Estonia — thatched farmsteads, a working windmill, a fishermen's village and an 18th-century manor. Buildings are staffed in summer with craftspeople demonstrating traditional trades.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 8 min → Tallinn TV Tower
1. Tallinn TV Tower
↳ A 314-metre tower built for the 1980 Moscow Olympics sailing regatta at Pirita, with a glass-floor observation deck at 175 metres offering panoramic views over the Old Town and Baltic coast. The tower played a symbolic role in 1991 when Estonian workers defied Soviet orders to hand it over.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Tallinn TV Tower · 4.4⭐ · teletorn.ee
🏛️ Monday - Thursday 10:00am - 9:00pm · Friday - Sunday 10:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Kloostrimetsa tee 58a · Pirita
🚶 20 min · 🚕 5 min → Pirita Convent
2. Pirita Convent
↳ The roofless Gothic ruin of a 15th-century Bridgettine double monastery — at its consecration in 1436 the largest church in medieval Livonia. Ivan the Terrible's forces burned it in 1577 during the Livonian War; the massive limestone walls and arched window frames have stood open to the sky since.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ piritaklooster.ee
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~30 min

1. Raekoja plats
↳ The broad neoclassical main square at the heart of Tartu's Old Town, flanked by the pale-yellow 18th-century Town Hall. The Kissing Students fountain at its centre is Estonia's most-photographed sculpture — the gathering point of the university city, with café terraces and Toome Hill as backdrop.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

2. University of Tartu Museum
↳ The university history museum housed inside the roofless nave of Tartu Cathedral — a 13th-century Gothic ruin on Toome Hill enclosed in the 1790s as the university library. The collection traces 200 years of academic life, Estonian scientific thought and the role of the university in the national awakening movement.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Estonian National Museum
↳ One of Northern Europe's largest ethnographic museums, built on a former Soviet air base whose old runway corridor forms the central gallery axis. Permanent exhibitions cover Estonian culture, folk traditions and daily life from prehistory to the present, with rotating international shows in the wings.
📖 Wikipedia

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Paldiski · 45 min
↳ A Soviet-era port town on a limestone headland — abandoned nuclear submarine training facilities, a rugged coastal cliff walk, and almost no tourists; a strange and striking half-day.
🎫 book at: Elron or Omio
Rakvere · 1 hr 15 min
↳ A compact medieval castle town on the edge of the Lahemaa plateau — the 14th-century Rakvere Castle and its reconstructed battlement walk are the centrepiece, with a manageable old town and an excellent local food hall nearby.
🎫 book at: Elron or Omio
Tartu · 2 hr
↳ Estonia's university city and intellectual capital — the Tartu University Main Building, the botanical garden, and the Town Hall square make it a complete half-day on its own; the Estonian National Museum is a further hour on the edge of town.
🎫 book at: Elron or Omio
Narva · 2 hr 30 min
↳ Estonia's easternmost city on the Russian border — the 14th-century Narva Castle faces the Ivangorod Fortress directly across the Narva River, the most dramatic face-off of two medieval fortresses in the Baltic. The restored town hall square is compact and walkable.
🎫 book at: Elron or Omio
Pärnu · 2 hr 30 min
↳ Estonia's summer capital — a broad sandy beach, a compact Art Deco old town, and the Pärnu Mud Baths spa resort all within easy walking distance of the central station.
🎫 book at: Elron or Omio
