🍮 Local Tastes
San Francisco Sourdough
↳ Tangy, chewy loaves leavened by a wild Bay Area yeast — Lactobacillus sanfranciscensis. Bakers here have kept the same mother dough alive since the 1849 Gold Rush.
📍 Boudin Bakery · Tartine Manufactory · Acme Bread
Mission Burrito
↳ The oversized foil-wrapped burrito invented in the 1960s on 24th Street — rice, beans, choice of meat, salsa, cheese rolled into a flour tortilla. Long enough to be a meal, dense enough to need two hands.
📍 La Taqueria · El Farolito · Taqueria Cancún
Cioppino
↳ The Italian-American fisherman's stew born on the Wharf in the late 1800s — Dungeness crab, mussels, clams, shrimp, and white fish simmered in a tomato-wine broth. Crusty sourdough on the side to mop the bowl.
📍 Sotto Mare · Tadich Grill
Irish Coffee
↳ The American debut of Irish coffee — perfected at Buena Vista Café in 1952, where a barman cracked the trick of floating the cream on hot whiskey-coffee. Still made the same way at the same long bar, four glasses at a time.
📍 The Buena Vista Café
