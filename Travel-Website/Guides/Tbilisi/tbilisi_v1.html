🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Anchiskhati Basilica
1.Anchiskhati Basilica
↳ The oldest surviving church in the city — a 6th-century basilica built under King Dachi with warm brick walls, frescoed interiors, and a bell tower added in the 17th century.

2.Abanotubani
↳ The sulphur bath district of Old Town — domed brick bathhouses channelling natural hot springs, grey-brown domes protruding from the hillside below Narikala. The city takes its name from the Georgian for warm waters.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

3.Tbilisi Cable Car
↳ An aerial tramway crossing the Kura River gorge from Rike Park to the heights above Narikala — sweeping panorama of the Old Town rooftops, river, and forested cliffs. Requires the Metromoney transit card.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rike Park · Old Town
🚶 15 min · 🚕 8 min → National Botanical Garden
4.National Botanical Garden
↳ A 128-hectare hillside garden cascading through the ravine below Narikala — waterfall, rose gardens, exotic tree collections, stone pathways through dense woodland. Founded in the 17th century as the royal garden of the Kartli kingdom.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 1 Botanikuri St · Abanotubani

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 18 min · 🚕 8 min → Simon Janashia Museum
1.Simon Janashia Museum
↳ The national history museum housing the most significant archaeological collection in the Caucasus — Bronze Age to medieval, with highlights including a Colchian gold torque collection and ancient Greek coins from Black Sea trade routes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 3 Rustaveli Ave · Mtatsminda
🚶 20 min · 🚕 7 min → Bridge of Peace
2.Bridge of Peace
↳ A glass-and-steel pedestrian bridge over the Mtkvari River connecting Rike Park to the Old Town embankment. Its LED canopy ripples after dark — the view from centre takes in the full panorama of Old Town cliffs and Metekhi Church across the water.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rike Park · Old Town
🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Metekhi Church
3.Metekhi Church
↳ A 13th-century Georgian Orthodox church on a sheer cliff above the Mtkvari River, on a site with a church since the 5th century. Flanked by the equestrian statue of King Vakhtang Gorgasali — best seen from the opposite bank at Rike Park.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 Metekhi Rise · Avlabari
🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Narikala Fortress
4.Narikala Fortress
↳ A 4th-century fortress above Old Town, expanded by Persians and Arabs and now a picturesque ruin threaded with walking paths. The walls enclose the restored St. Nicholas Church and offer commanding views over the sulphur bath district and the river.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Open Air Museum of Ethnography
1.Open Air Museum of Ethnography
↳ A 52-hectare open-air museum preserving over 70 relocated traditional dwellings — from the fortified stone tower-houses of Khevsureti to the carved wooden balconied homes of Adjara, spread across a forested hillside above the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 2 Turtle Lake Rd · Vake
🚕 20 min → Holy Trinity Cathedral
2.Holy Trinity Cathedral
↳ The largest cathedral in the Caucasus — completed in 2004 on a hilltop above Avlabari, its 87-metre gilded dome rising above the city skyline. Lavish mosaics, carved stone, and icons on a scale matching the exterior. The terrace looks out over the whole of Old Town.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Sameba Square · Avlabari
🚶 25 min · 🚕 15 min → Tbilisi Funicular
3.Tbilisi Funicular
↳ A century-old funicular railway climbing the forested slope of Mtatsminda to a hilltop park with commanding city panoramas. Requires the Metromoney transit card. At the top, the Mtatsminda Pantheon sits just below the summit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 12:00am
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Besiki St · Vera
🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Mtatsminda Pantheon
4.Mtatsminda Pantheon
↳ A hillside cemetery on the forested slopes of Mtatsminda where Georgia's most revered writers and public figures are buried. The graves of Ilia Chavchavadze, Akaki Tsereteli, and Niko Pirosani emerge from woodland paths in a setting as quiet as it is charged with meaning.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 15 min · 🚕 7 min → Freedom Square
1.Freedom Square
↳ The historic heart of the capital — a formal square anchored by a tall column bearing a gilded statue of Saint George, erected in 2006 to mark independence. It connects Old Town with Rustaveli Avenue and has been the site of every major public event in Georgia's modern history.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Freedom Square · Old Town
🚶 25 min · 🚕 12 min → Kartlis Deda
2.Kartlis Deda
↳ A 20-metre aluminium statue of a woman in Georgian national dress on the crest of Sololaki Hill, erected in 1958 for the city's 1500th anniversary. She holds a wine bowl for friends and a sword for enemies. The terrace delivers one of the widest panoramas over Old Town.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Sololaki Hill · Sololaki
🚕 40 min → Chronicle of Georgia
3.Chronicle of Georgia
↳ A monumental outdoor installation on the shore of a reservoir — 16 stone columns around 35 metres tall, carved top to bottom with bas-reliefs tracing Georgian history from biblical origins through to Soviet times. Staggering up close, extraordinary set against the water.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Reservoir shore · Samgori
🚕 25 min → Kus Tba
4.Kus Tba
↳ A natural mountain lake in the Vake hills, encircled by a 3-km forested walking path with open views across the valley. Accessible by cable car from Vake Park below — a complete contrast to the dense stone texture of Old Town, and a natural close to the last day.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Vake hills · Vake
🚕 20 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Khinkali
Georgia's iconic soup dumpling — thick dough filled with spiced meat broth, twisted into a knot at the top. Eaten by hand, holding the knot while drinking the broth before biting through. The knot is left on the plate.
📍 Old Town · city-wide
Khachapuri
A boat-shaped cheese pastry in its Adjarian form — filled with sulguni cheese and finished with a raw egg cracked into the centre. Eaten by tearing the crust ends and stirring the egg into the molten cheese.
📍 Old Town · city-wide
Churchkhela
Strings of walnuts dipped into concentrated grape juice thickened with flour, forming candle-shaped sweets in vibrant reds and purples. Available at Old Town market stalls throughout the year.
📍 Old Town markets · Abanotubani

✨ Claude Inspiration

The city doesn't announce itself. There are no marquee boulevards demanding attention, no single monument that organises the rest. Instead, it reveals itself gradually — in the texture of the sulphur-stained bathhouse domes, in the way the Old Town cliffs drop straight into the river, in the particular amber of late afternoon light on the carved wooden balconies.

What makes it unusual is how many different eras coexist without apparent tension. Persian hammams next to Soviet housing blocks next to a glass pedestrian bridge lit by LEDs — and the city absorbs all of it. The Chronicle of Georgia columns stand at a reservoir outside the centre, enormous and largely unvisited, carved with a thousand years of history and left quietly to the elements.

The Caucasus is one of the world's most complicated crossroads, and this is where you begin to understand what that means on the ground.
