2. Devil's Bridge Trail
↳ A 4.4-mile out-and-back hike to Arizona's largest natural sandstone arch. The trail winds through red rocks and passes directly under the 65-foot arch bridge for views and photography.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
🆓 Free

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Oak Creek Canyon ·
1. Oak Creek Canyon Scenic Drive
↳ A 14-mile drive along Route 89A north of Sedona with switchbacks, overlooks, and canyon wall views. Multiple pullouts offer photo stops and short walks to viewpoints above the canyon floor.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Route 89A North · Oak Creek Canyon
🚕 8 min → Airport Mesa ·
2. Airport Mesa
↳ A mesa above the city with a 3.5-mile loop trail offering 360-degree views of the red rock formations and valley. One of four recognized vortex sites with a paved overlook accessible from the parking area.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Airport Road · West
🚕 25 min → Montezuma Castle ·
3. Montezuma Castle National Monument
↳ Well-preserved 12th-century cliff dwellings built by the Sinagua people. A 0.75-mile easy trail loops past the main 5-story cliff dwelling with interpretive plaques explaining the ancient inhabitants.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 2800 Montezuma Castle Road · Camp Verde
🚕 8 min → Walnut Canyon ·
4. Walnut Canyon National Monument
↳ A scenic canyon with a 1-mile trail descending past cliff dwellings through a walnut grove. Shade from trees and views of ancient structures line the path.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
🆓 Free
📍 10 East Walnut Canyon Road · Flagstaff
🚶 44 min · 🚕 30 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Mexican Red Chile
↳ Red chile sauces and stews appear across menus here. Chile rellenos and enchiladas rojas are standard items at most Sedona Mexican restaurants.
📍 Elote Cafe · Mesa Grill
Mesquite-Grilled Foods
↳ Mesquite wood native to the Arizona desert gives a smoky flavor to grilled meats. Many local restaurants feature mesquite-grilled specialties on their menus.
📍 Mariposa Latin Inspired Grill · Canyon Breeze
Prickly Pear
↳ Prickly pear fruit and pads appear in salads, sauces, and margaritas across the city. The flavor is mildly sweet with slight tartness and a vivid magenta color.
📍 Tlaquepaque Arts Village · Uptown restaurants
Arizona Wine
↳ Sedona sits near the Verde Valley AVA. Local tasting rooms serve Grenache, Tempranillo, and other varietals grown in the high desert mountains.
📍 Javelina Leap Winery · Page Springs Cellars
