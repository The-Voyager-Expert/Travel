🍮 Local Tastes
Mexican red chile: Red chile is used in soups, stews, and sauces across Sedona restaurants. Chile rellenos and enchiladas rojas with red chile sauce are standard menu items.
Mesquite-grilled foods: Mesquite wood native to the Arizona desert imparts a smoky flavor to grilled meats. Many local restaurants feature mesquite-grilled specialties.
Prickly pear cactus: Local cuisine features prickly pear fruit and pads in salads, sauces, and margaritas. The flavor is mildly sweet with slight tartness.
Arizona wine: Sedona is near Arizona wine country in the Verde Valley AVA. Local tasting rooms serve Grenache, Tempranillo, and other varietals grown in the mountains.
🚗 Food Delivery
DoorDash: Available in Sedona with delivery from restaurants in Uptown, Midtown, and Old Town areas.
Uber Eats: Active in Sedona for restaurant delivery and local takeout options.
Grubhub: Limited coverage in Sedona but available for select restaurants in central areas.
🎭 Shows
Sedona International Film Festival: Annual festival held in February featuring international documentaries, short films, and feature films at venues across Sedona.
Sedona Performing Arts Center: Host to theater productions, musicals, comedy shows, and local performances throughout the year.
Tlaquepaque Gallery Nights: Monthly art walks with extended hours featuring local artists and live music in the courtyard.
🚌 Getting Around
🚕 Ride-hailing: Uber and Lyft operate in Sedona. Wait times are longer than urban areas due to the rural location.
No public transit: Sedona does not have a public bus or tram system. Most travel is by personal vehicle or rental car.
🚆 Train Stations Near Hotel
No major train stations within easy reach of Sedona. The nearest Amtrak station is in Flagstaff · 30 miles north · served by the Southwest Chief route. Travelers arrive by car or fly into Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport.
🗺️ Day Trips
Grand Canyon South Rim: 120 miles north · 3-hour drive · UNESCO World Heritage Site with canyon views, hiking trails, and visitor centers.
Flagstaff: 30 miles north · 1-hour drive · Historic mountain town with Lowell Observatory, Northern Arizona University, and forests.
Phoenix: 120 miles south · 2-hour drive · Arizona's capital with museums, shopping, dining, and nearby Papago Park hiking.
⭐ Michelin
Sedona has no Michelin-rated restaurants as of the latest Michelin Guide.
❗️ Heads Up
Altitude: Sedona sits at 4,350 feet elevation. Drink water throughout the day and pace hikes accordingly.
Peak season timing: March through May and October through November bring increased crowds. Arrive at trailheads before 7:00am for parking.
Trail conditions: Check conditions with rangers before departing. Bring sun protection and adequate water for red rock trails.
