🍮 Local Tastes
Mexican red chile: Authentic Mexican cuisine is a staple due to Sedona's proximity to Mexico. Red chile is used in soups, stews, and sauces. Try chile rellenos and enchiladas rojas with red chile sauce.
Mesquite-grilled foods: Mesquite wood native to the Arizona desert imparts a distinctive smoky flavor to grilled meats. Many local restaurants feature mesquite-grilled specialties.
Prickly pear cactus: Local cuisine features prickly pear fruit and pads in salads, sauces, and margaritas. The taste is mildly sweet with slight tartness.
Arizona wine: Sedona is near Arizona wine country. Local tasting rooms serve Arizona wines featuring Grenache, Tempranillo, and other varietals grown in the mountains.
🚗 Food Delivery
DoorDash: Available in Sedona with delivery from most restaurants in Uptown, Midtown, and Old Town areas.
Uber Eats: Active in Sedona for restaurant delivery and local takeout options.
Grubhub: Limited coverage in Sedona but available for select restaurants in central areas.
🎭 Shows
Sedona International Film Festival: Annual festival held March through April featuring international documentaries, short films, and feature films at various venues.
Sedona Performing Arts Center: Host to theater productions, musicals, comedy shows, and local performances throughout the year.
Tlaquepaque Gallery Nights: Monthly art walks with extended hours and special events featuring local artists and live music.
🚌 Getting Around
🚕 Ride-hailing: Uber and Lyft operate in Sedona. Due to rural location, wait times may be longer than urban areas. Surge pricing applies during peak times.
No public transit: Sedona does not have a public bus or tram system. Most travel is by personal vehicle or rental car.
⭐ Michelin
Sedona has no Michelin-rated restaurants as of the latest Michelin Guide.
❗️ Heads Up
Altitude: Sedona sits at 4,350 feet elevation. Visitors from lower elevations may experience mild altitude effects. Drink water throughout the day and take hikes at a comfortable pace.
High season crowds: March through May and October through November are peak seasons. Hikes can be crowded in early morning. Arrive before 7:00am for parking.
Summer heat: June through August temperatures exceed 100°F. Early morning and evening activities are recommended. Most shops have strong air conditioning.
Flash flood risk: During monsoon season from July through September, slot canyons and creek-crossing hikes can flood rapidly after rain. Verify creek conditions with rangers before crossing.
