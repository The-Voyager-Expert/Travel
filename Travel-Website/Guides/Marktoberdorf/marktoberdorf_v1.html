Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 9 min · 🚕 3 min → St. Martin's Church
1. St. Martin's Parish Church
↳ Marktoberdorf's landmark church, rebuilt in 1732–1738 by the town's own master builder Johann Georg Fischer and counted among the most beautiful Rococo churches in the Allgäu — a light-filled nave with an ornate high altar, minutes from the hotel.
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~20 min
🆓 Free
📍 Kirchplatz · Stadtmitte
🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Schloss Marktoberdorf
2. Schloss Marktoberdorf
↳ The former summer residence of the Augsburg prince-bishops, given its Baroque shape in 1722 by Johann Georg Fischer and now home to the Bavarian Music Academy. The grounds and the 18th-century Kurfürstenallee lime avenue are freely open to wander.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
⚠️ Interior visits only via the Bavarian Music Academy's guided tours; the grounds are freely accessible.
📍 Kurfürstenstraße 19 · Stadtmitte
🚶 10 min · 🚕 3 min → Künstlerhaus
3. Künstlerhaus Marktoberdorf
↳ Marktoberdorf's contemporary-art house, pairing a barrier-free new gallery with the historic Dr.-Geiger-Haus for changing exhibitions and art-education programmes — a compact, well-curated stop that rounds out the town's cultural core.
🏛️ Tuesday - Friday 3:00pm - 6:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 2:00pm - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~45 min
⚠️ Admission tickets are sold at the entrance.
📍 Kemptener Straße 5 · Stadtmitte

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 28 min → Hohenschwangau Castle
1. Hohenschwangau Castle
↳ The neo-Gothic palace King Maximilian II rebuilt in 1833–1837 on a medieval fortress above Lake Alpsee, and the childhood home of King Ludwig II. Its lived-in royal rooms and lake-and-mountain setting make it the perfect first half of a castle day.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 4:30pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Guided tour only — collect timed tickets at the Ticket Center in Hohenschwangau village.
📍 Alpseestraße 30 · Hohenschwangau · Schwangau
🚶 25 min · 🚕 5 min → Neuschwanstein Castle
2. Neuschwanstein Castle
↳ King Ludwig II's fairy-tale Romanesque-Revival palace, perched on a crag above the Pöllat gorge and the model for Disney's Sleeping Beauty castle. The interiors are seen by guided tour; the classic view is from the Marienbrücke footbridge above the falls.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
⚠️ Timed-entry guided tour only — book online well ahead; allow a 30–40 min uphill walk or shuttle from the village.
📍 Neuschwansteinstraße 20 · Schwangau
🚕 18 min → Forggensee
3. Forggensee
↳ Bavaria's fifth-largest lake and Germany's largest reservoir by area, ringed by shoreline viewpoints that frame the turquoise water against the Allgäu and Ammergau Alps — a calm, scenic close to a castle-filled day, just north of Füssen.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
⚠️ The reservoir is drawn down in winter — fullest and most scenic from May to mid-October.
📍 Uferstraße · Füssen
🚕 32 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 30 min → Wieskirche
1. Wieskirche
↳ An oval Rococo pilgrimage church completed in 1754, the masterwork of the Zimmermann brothers and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983. Standing alone in alpine meadows, its dazzling white-and-gold interior under a vast painted ceiling is one of the great rooms of Bavaria.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Closed to sightseeing during Mass — avoid Sunday and holiday mornings.
📍 Wies 12 · Steingaden
🚕 12 min → Rottenbuch Abbey
2. Rottenbuch Abbey
↳ A former Augustinian collegiate church founded in 1073, its Romanesque bones wrapped in exuberant 18th-century High Baroque — frescoes by Matthäus Günther and stucco by Josef Schmuzer. A serene counterpoint to the Wies, in a quiet Pfaffenwinkel village.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Klosterhof · Rottenbuch
🚕 33 min → hotel
Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 24 min → Residenz Kempten
1. Residenz Kempten
↳ The prince-abbots' palace, the first grand monastery complex built in Germany after the Thirty Years' War, with Rococo state rooms — the Prunkräume — decorated in the 1730s and 1740s. The lavish ceremonial apartments are the draw, seen on a guided tour.
🏛️ Tuesday - Thursday 10:00am - 4:00pm
🚫 Closed Friday - Monday
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ State rooms by guided tour only; tickets sold on site.
📍 Residenzplatz 4 · Stiftsbezirk · Kempten
🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → St. Lorenz Basilica
2. St. Lorenz Basilica
↳ The Baroque former abbey church, one of the first great churches raised in Germany after the Thirty Years' War and consecrated in 1748, a minor basilica since 1969. Its bright twin-towered nave anchors Kempten's abbey quarter, steps from the Residence.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Not open to visits during services.
📍 Stiftplatz · Stiftsbezirk · Kempten
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Kempten Old Town
3. Kempten Old Town
↳ The historic burgher core of the former free imperial city, centred on the Rathausplatz with its Renaissance town hall and fountain and the neighbouring St.-Mang-Platz — a free stroll through Kempten's civic heart, distinct from the abbey quarter.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rathausplatz · Altstadt · Kempten
🚶 22 min · 🚕 7 min → Cambodunum
4. Archäologischer Park Cambodunum
↳ An open-air Roman museum on the site of Cambodunum — often cited as the oldest written-recorded town in Germany and the first capital of the province of Raetia. A reconstructed temple precinct, baths and forum let you walk the Roman town across the Iller from the old centre.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Open March to November; tickets at the park entrance.
📍 Cambodunumweg 3 · Kempten
🚕 26 min → hotel
Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 58 min → Breitachklamm
1. Breitachklamm
↳ One of the deepest rock gorges in Central Europe, opened up in 1905 and threaded by a boardwalk between sheer walls and rushing waterfalls. A thrilling, cool walk near Oberstdorf — dramatic with summer torrents or winter ice.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Tickets at the gorge entrance; closed for maintenance early November to mid-December and mid-March to late April.
📍 Klammstraße 47 · Tiefenbach · Oberstdorf
🚕 12 min → Nebelhorn
2. Nebelhorn
↳ A 2,224 m summit above Oberstdorf reached by the rebuilt Nebelhornbahn, the highest cable car in the Allgäu. The top station opens a panorama of some 400 Alpine peaks — the big-mountain finale to the trip.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 4:45pm
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
⚠️ Cable-car tickets at the valley station; bring a warm layer — alpine weather shifts fast.
📍 Nebelhornstraße 67 · Oberstdorf
🚕 55 min → hotel
Day 6
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 30 min → Eisenberg Castle Ruins
1. Hohenfreyberg & Eisenberg Castle Ruins
↳ Twin medieval ruins crowning a double-topped ridge before the Tannheim mountains — Eisenberg, begun in 1313, and the larger Hohenfreyberg, raised 1418–1432, both burned in 1646. A scenic loop hike links the two with sweeping Alpine views over the Ostallgäu.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Hilltop ruins reached on foot — a 4.5 km loop with 170 m of climbing; wear proper shoes.
📍 Zell · Eisenberg
🚕 22 min → Hohes Schloss Füssen
2. Hohes Schloss Füssen
↳ One of Swabia's finest late-Gothic castle complexes, the Augsburg bishops' summer residence, famous for the trompe-l'œil illusion paintings of 1499 covering its courtyard and outer walls. It crowns Füssen's old town and houses a branch of the Bavarian State Painting Collections.
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 11:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ Tickets at the castle or the Füssen city museum.
📍 Magnusplatz 10 · Füssen
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Lechfall
3. Lechfall
↳ A 12 m stepped weir where the turquoise Lech leaves the Alps and rushes into a narrow limestone gorge at Füssen's southern edge. The 1895 König-Max-Steg footbridge gives a head-on view of the falls — a quick, photogenic stop to end the day.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~25 min
📍 Tiroler Straße · Füssen
🚕 28 min → hotel

1. Marienplatz & New Town Hall
↳ Munich's central square, dominated by the neo-Gothic New Town Hall with its 85 m tower. The Rathaus-Glockenspiel re-enacts two 16th-century Munich stories in a 15-minute carillon show at 11:00am and 12:00pm daily.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Marienplatz 8 · Altstadt · Munich
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Frauenkirche
2. Frauenkirche
↳ Munich's brick-Gothic cathedral, built in barely 20 years from 1468, whose twin domed towers are the city's defining silhouette. The vast, luminous hall church is free to enter and just a couple of minutes off Marienplatz.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Saturday 8:00am - 8:00pm · Sunday 9:30am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Not open to visits during services.
📍 Frauenplatz 12 · Altstadt · Munich
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Munich Residenz
3. Munich Residenz
↳ The Wittelsbachs' former royal palace and the largest city palace in Germany — ten courtyards and some 130 rooms, including the spectacular Renaissance Antiquarium. A blockbuster of decorated halls and royal collections in the heart of the Altstadt.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Timed tickets — book online ahead.
📍 Residenzstraße 1 · Altstadt · Munich
🚶 15 min · 🚕 6 min → Englischer Garten
4. Englischer Garten
↳ Munich's English Garden is one of the world's largest urban parks, laid out from 1789, with beer gardens, meadows and the Chinese Tower. At its southern edge the Eisbach forms a permanent standing wave where river surfers ride year-round — a fine place to wind down before the train home.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Prinzregentenstraße · Lehel · Munich
