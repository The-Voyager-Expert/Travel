рҹҢІ Portland's Secret Geometry

Portland sits at the confluence of two major rivers вҖ” the Willamette and the Columbia вҖ” and three mountain views: Hood, St. Helens, and Adams. Almost every high point in the city, from Pittock Mansion to Mount Tabor's summit, frames at least one of these volcanoes on a clear day. The city's grid was deliberately designed with narrow 200-foot blocks вҖ” nearly half the standard US block size вҖ” which gives downtown Portland more street corners per mile than almost any American city. That's why walking feels so natural here.

The International Rose Test Garden was established in 1917 partly as a safe haven вҖ” rose cultivars were shipped from European gardens threatened by World War I, making Portland's hillside a kind of living ark for endangered varieties. Over a century later, the garden still receives trial varieties that may never be sold commercially.
