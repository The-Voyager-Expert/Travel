↳ A walled classical Ming Dynasty garden built by 65 artisans from Suzhou, Portland's sister city, enclosing 40,000 sq ft of covered walkways, bridges, and Lake Zither at its center.

📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:30am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1h

2. Portland Japanese Garden
↳ Widely described as the most authentic Japanese garden outside Japan, this 12.5-acre sanctuary in Washington Park holds five distinct garden styles, a Cultural Village with galleries, a tea house, and seasonal exhibitions.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Portland Japanese Garden · Washington Park 4.8⭐ · japanesegarden.org

↳ A 153-acre living museum with 6,000 individual trees of 2,300 species from every continent, connected by 12 miles of trails in the West Hills, free to explore year-round.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 45 min · 🚕 14 min → Mount Tabor Park
1. Mount Tabor Park
↳ An extinct volcanic cinder cone rising 636 feet in southeast Portland, designated the first Urban Quiet Park in the US, with forested trails, historic 1890s reservoirs, and a clear-day view of Mount Hood.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 5:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30min
🆓 Free
📍 SE 60th Avenue · Mount Tabor
🚶 35 min · 🚕 10 min → Oregon Museum of Science and Industry
2. Oregon Museum of Science and Industry (OMSI)
↳ A riverside science and technology museum featuring five hands-on exhibit halls, a planetarium, a large-screen theater, and the USS Blueback submarine moored on the Willamette — the last diesel submarine built for the US Navy.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ OMSI · Inner Southeast 4.5⭐ · omsi.edu
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:30am - 5:30pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2h
📍 1945 SE Water Avenue · Inner Southeast
🚕 30 min → Multnomah Falls
3. Multnomah Falls
↳ Oregon's tallest waterfall at 620 feet, plunging in two tiers in the Columbia River Gorge and drawing over two million visitors a year. A paved trail leads to the Benson Footbridge spanning the lower cascade.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2h
🆓 Free
📍 Historic Columbia River Highway · Columbia River Gorge
🚕 30 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Marionberry Pie
↳ Oregon's signature blackberry cultivar, bred at Oregon State University in 1956 and grown almost exclusively in the Willamette Valley. Shows up in pies, jam, ice cream, and pastries across the city.
📍 Ken's Artisan Bakery · 338 NW 21st Avenue
Voodoo Doughnut
↳ Portland's cult doughnut institution, open since 2003 on SW Third Avenue. Known for outlandish toppings, the bacon maple bar, and the Voodoo Doll — a raspberry-filled doughnut served with a pretzel stake.
📍 Voodoo Doughnut Old Town · 22 SW 3rd Avenue
Dungeness Crab
↳ The Pacific Northwest's premier shellfish, fished from waters off the Oregon coast. Seasonal (November–June), served cracked whole at Portland restaurants sourcing from Pacific Coast boats.
📍 Jake's Famous Crawfish · 401 SW 12th Avenue
Stumptown Coffee
↳ Portland's original third-wave coffee roaster, founded in 1999 on SE Division. The Hair Bender espresso blend — named for the salon that preceded the café — is a city staple at the original location and across Portland.
📍 Stumptown Coffee Division · 4525 SE Division Street
