↳ Portland's cult doughnut institution on SW Third Avenue. Known for outlandish toppings, the bacon maple bar, and the Voodoo Doll — a raspberry-filled doughnut served with a pretzel stake.

🌲 Portland's Secret Geometry

Portland sits at the confluence of two major rivers — the Willamette and the Columbia — and three mountain views: Hood, St. Helens, and Adams. Almost every high point in the city, from Pittock Mansion to Mount Tabor's summit, frames at least one of these volcanoes on a clear day. The city's grid was deliberately designed with narrow 200-foot blocks — nearly half the standard US block size — which gives downtown Portland more street corners per mile than almost any American city. That's why walking feels so natural here.

The International Rose Test Garden was established in 1917 partly as a safe haven — rose cultivars were shipped from European gardens threatened by World War I, making Portland's hillside a kind of living ark for endangered varieties. Over a century later, the garden still receives trial varieties that may never be sold commercially.
