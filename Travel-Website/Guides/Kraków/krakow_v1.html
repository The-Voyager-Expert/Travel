1. Wawel Castle
↳ Royal seat of Polish kings for five centuries, commanding a limestone hill above the Vistula with Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque architecture. The Royal Apartments display Flemish tapestries; the Crown Treasury holds the Szczerbiec coronation sword and historic regalia.

2. Wawel Cathedral
↳ Poland's national sanctuary and coronation church, holding the tombs of kings, queens, and national heroes in 19 chapels. The Sigismund Chapel with its gilded dome is considered the greatest Polish Renaissance monument; the bell tower offers panoramic views of the Vistula valley.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Rynek Główny
↳ The largest medieval market square in Europe at 40 000 sq m, ringed by townhouses, palaces, and church towers. The Renaissance Cloth Hall at its center still houses craft stalls and a gallery above. Street musicians, flower stalls, and café terraces fill it day and night.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5 hr

4. St. Mary's Basilica
↳ Gothic twin-towered basilica at the Market Square, famous for the 15th-century Veit Stoss altarpiece — the greatest Gothic sculpture in Europe at 13 meters, blazing with painted saints and gilt. Every hour, a bugler plays the Hejnał Mariacki trumpet call from the taller tower, a tradition since the 13th century.
📖 Wikipedia

🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 16 min · 🚕 7 min → Kraków Barbican
1. Kraków Barbican
↳ One of Europe's best-preserved medieval barbicans, this circular Gothic fortress was built in the 1490s as an advance outpost for the Florian Gate. Three-meter-thick walls, seven turrets, and 130 arrow slits guarded the city's northern entrance. Summer brings medieval reenactments to the courtyard.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Czartoryski Museum
↳ Poland's oldest public museum anchored by Leonardo da Vinci's Lady with an Ermine — one of only four Leonardo paintings in the world. The 19th-century Czartoryski collection also includes Rembrandt's Landscape Before a Storm, Greek and Etruscan antiquities, and an extraordinary treasury of decorative arts.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Collegium Maius
↳ Oldest university building in Poland — the 1364 Jagiellonian University's core. The late-Gothic arcaded courtyard is among the finest in Central Europe. The museum displays instruments that Copernicus may have used here, medieval astronomical tools, and ceremonial regalia. Guided visit required.
📖 Wikipedia

1. Old Synagogue
↳ The oldest surviving Jewish religious building in Poland, dating to the 15th century and now serving as a museum of Kazimierz Jewish history. The Renaissance hall houses Torah scrolls, religious objects, documents, and photographs chronicling centuries of Jewish life and the tragedy of the Holocaust.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Galicia Jewish Museum
↳ The permanent exhibition Traces of Memory presents large-format photographs of surviving Jewish traces across former Galicia — crumbling cemeteries, hidden synagogues, memorial sites — with contemporary testimony. The museum focuses on living Jewish heritage, not only destruction.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Schindler's Factory
↳ Schindler's factory in Podgórze now houses Kraków Under Nazi Occupation 1939–1945, documenting life from invasion through the ghetto's liquidation with original objects, multimedia, and reconstructed spaces. Considered among the finest Holocaust memorial museums in the world — pre-book well in advance.

🍮 Local Tastes
Pierogi
↳ Filled dumplings — boiled or pan-fried, stuffed with potato and cheese, meat, sauerkraut and mushroom, or sweet cheese and blueberries. Milk bars and pierogi specialists offer the broadest variety.
📍 Milk bars · Plac Nowy · Kazimierz
Żurek
↳ Sour rye soup — fermented rye starter base with hard-boiled egg, white sausage, and often served in a hollowed bread bowl. Tangy and filling; a staple of Polish winter cooking.
📍 Any traditional restaurant · Old Town
Bigos
↳ Poland's national hunter's stew of sauerkraut, fresh cabbage, various meats, mushrooms, and spices, slow-cooked over days until deeply savory. Every cook has their own version; traditionally improves with each reheating.
📍 Starka · ul. Józefa 14 · Kazimierz
Kiełbasa
↳ Polish smoked sausage made from pork, veal, or a blend, seasoned with garlic and marjoram. Grilled kiełbasa is a street food staple — look for it at outdoor stalls around the Market Square and Kazimierz.
📍 Street stalls · Rynek Główny · Old Town
Oscypek
↳ Smoked sheep's milk cheese from the Tatra region with PDO status. Carved into a decorative spindle shape and cold-smoked over spruce wood; sold by highland vendors on the Market Square — try it grilled with cranberry jam.
📍 Highland vendors · Rynek Główny · Old Town
Obwarzanek
↳ Ring-shaped braided bread roll unique to Kraków, sold from street carts since the 14th century. Sprinkled with sesame, poppy seeds, or salt; best eaten fresh and warm. A protected regional product.
📍 Street carts throughout Old Town

↳ The UNESCO-listed Wieliczka Salt Mine has been excavated since the 13th century and descends to 135 meters through a labyrinth of chambers, lakes, and chapels carved in salt — including the stunning St. Kinga's Chapel. One of the most visited sites in Poland.

🏰 Why Kraków

Kraków is one of Europe's great fortunate cities — spared from wartime destruction in a region that lost almost everything. Walking its streets feels less like visiting a reconstructed past and more like slipping inside a living medieval city: the cobblestones, the market square trumpet call, the layered architecture of five centuries under Polish kings. The Royal Route from Wawel down to the Market Square is one of the finest urban sequences on the continent, and the city earns its place among them without the tourist inflation that dims older fame.

What sets Kraków apart is the weight of what Kazimierz carries alongside the beauty of the Old Town. The Jewish quarter's survival — battered, repurposed, but still recognizable — and the unflinching documentation at Schindler's Factory and the Galicia Jewish Museum make a visit here something more than sightseeing. The distance between the light of the Market Square and the gravity of Podgórze is about twenty minutes by foot. Both belong to the same city, and the same trip.
