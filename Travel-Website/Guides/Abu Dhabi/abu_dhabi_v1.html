Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 4 min → Qasr Al Watan
1. Qasr Al Watan
↳ The working Presidential Palace, opened to the public as a cultural landmark — a vast white-and-gold complex of domed halls, a great hall floored in 350,000 tiles, and a library beneath a crystal dome. Less a museum than a statement of how the UAE sees itself.

2. Observation Deck at 300
↳ The highest public vantage point in the city, on level 74 of Etihad Towers Tower 2. Floor-to-ceiling glass looks out over the Corniche, the Presidential Palace grounds, and the Gulf — best in the late afternoon as the light softens over the water.

3. Qasr Al Hosn
↳ Abu Dhabi's oldest stone building — a watchtower over the island's only freshwater well that grew into the ruling family's fort. Reopened after a decade of restoration, it now tells the story of the city from pearling settlement to capital.

4. Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque
↳ One of the world's largest mosques and the UAE's defining landmark — 82 white-marble domes, reflective pools, the largest hand-knotted carpet on earth, and chandeliers of Swarovski crystal. Arrive late for the moment the floodlights come up against the dusk sky.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 14 min → Louvre Abu Dhabi
1. Louvre Abu Dhabi
↳ Jean Nouvel's "rain of light" museum on Saadiyat Island — a 180-metre silvery dome floating over white galleries, where a single ticket carries you across human history from ancient civilisations to the present, told as one shared story rather than by region.
📖 Wikipedia

🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
📍 Saadiyat Cultural District · Saadiyat Island
🚕 21 min → Ferrari World Abu Dhabi
2. Ferrari World Abu Dhabi
↳ The world's first Ferrari-branded park, under a vast red roof on Yas Island — home to Formula Rossa, the fastest roller coaster on earth, which hits 240 km/h in under five seconds. A full afternoon of rides built around the marque's racing history.

3. SeaWorld Abu Dhabi
↳ The world's largest indoor marine-life park, on Yas Island — eight realms wrapped around a 25-million-litre aquarium, with a research and rescue centre at its core. A modern, climate-controlled counterpoint to a day in the heat.

🍮 Local Tastes
Karak Chai
↳ The UAE's everyday street drink — strong black tea boiled with cardamom and sometimes saffron, sweetened with evaporated milk. Sold from roadside cafeterias for small change and drunk all day long.
📍 Arabian Tea House · Al Fanar
Luqaimat
↳ Golden deep-fried dough dumplings, crisp outside and soft within, drizzled with date syrup and sesame — the classic Emirati sweet, especially in Ramadan.
📍 Al Fanar · Mezlai
Machboos
↳ The Emirati national dish — spiced basmati rice slow-cooked with meat or fish, dried lime, saffron and a blend of warm spices. The centrepiece of a traditional table.
📍 Mezlai · Al Fanar

🕌 What I Noticed

Abu Dhabi compresses about three centuries into a fifteen-minute drive. Qasr Al Hosn — the watchtower built to guard the island's only well — now sits almost in the shadow of the towers it eventually paid for, and Erth, the first Emirati restaurant to earn a Michelin star, is tucked into the same fort's grounds. The oldest and newest versions of the city share one address.

It's also a place that does very little by halves. The Grand Mosque holds the world's largest hand-knotted carpet; Ferrari World runs the fastest roller coaster on earth; SeaWorld is the largest indoor marine park; the Louvre is the first of its name to open outside France. Two days here is less a tour than a sequence of superlatives — which is, in the end, exactly the impression the place is built to leave.
