Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 6 min · 🚕 3 min → Aksla
1. Aksla
↳ The 418-step Fjellstua staircase climbs from Kongens gate through forested hillside to a viewing platform 189 metres above Ålesund's rooftops. From the top, the city's Art Nouveau centre unfolds across a cluster of islands in the Borgundfjord — one of the most photographed views in Norway. Free access day and night.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Kongens gate stairs · Sentrum
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Jugendstilsenteret
2. Jugendstilsenteret
↳ Norway's only museum dedicated to the Art Nouveau movement, housed in the 1907 Apoteker building. The permanent collection traces how Ålesund's 1904 fire reconstruction created the densest concentration of Jugendstil architecture in Norway. KUBE art museum is included in the same ticket.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Kongens gate
3. Kongens gate
↳ Rebuilt 1904–1907 after fire destroyed the city overnight. Kaiser Wilhelm II funded reconstruction; German architects fused Jugendstil ornament with local granite. Three blocks of symmetrical facades with towers, turrets, and dragon motifs — the most coherent Art Nouveau streetscape in Scandinavia.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Kongens gate · Sentrum

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 9 min → Atlanterhavsparken
1. Atlanterhavsparken
↳ Norway's largest aquarium on Tueneset peninsula, facing the open Atlantic. The centrepiece is a 4-million-litre tank where halibut, wolffish, and cod swim alongside divers during twice-weekly feeding shows. Science Centre and Aquaculture Display Centre included in the same ticket.
🎟️ Atlanterhavsparken · Tueneset · 4.3⭐ · atlanterhavsparken.no
🏛️ Monday - Friday 10:00am - 4:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 9:30am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 Tueneset · Borgundnes
🚕 30 min → Alnes Lighthouse
2. Alnes Lighthouse
↳ Built in 1852 on the western tip of Godøy island, Alnes fyr guards the approach to Ålesund's archipelago. The red tower stands 11 metres above a rock shelf at the Atlantic's edge, flanked by a restored keeper's cottage with a seasonal café. On a clear day the horizon stretches unbroken to the open sea.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Alnesveien · Godøy

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Sunnmøre Museum
1. Sunnmøre Museum
↳ Norway's largest open-air maritime museum on a fjordside meadow 8km east of Ålesund. Fifty-five relocated buildings trace coastal life from the Iron Age to the 20th century. The centrepiece is the replica Viking ship Borgundknarren — built from a 12th-century wreck found in the Borgundfjord.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Storneskaia pier
1. Runde Island
↳ Norway's southernmost major seabird colony: 100,000 Atlantic puffins, gannets, kittiwakes, and razorbills nest on the 200-metre basalt cliffs of Runde's western face each May–August. The Atlantic trail to the cliff edge is one of the most dramatic short walks in western Norway.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~4h
⚠️ Puffins best May–August · boat connections reduced outside season
📍 Runde island · Herøy municipality

Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Storneskaia pier
1. Geirangerfjord
↳ UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2005. Geirangerfjord runs 15 kilometres between sheer cliff walls past the Seven Sisters, the Suitor, and the Bridal Veil waterfalls — the fjord floor drops below 260 metres. Day-tour boats depart from Ålesund's Storneskaia pier each morning.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~8h
⚠️ Best reached by guided day tour — see Tours section
📍 Geirangerfjord · Stranda municipality
🚕 1h → Trollstigen
2. Trollstigen
↳ Eleven hairpins at 11% grade above a 180-metre waterfall, with a glass-railed platform cantilevered over the gorge: Trollstigen is one of the world's most photographed mountain roads. Most day tours from Ålesund combine a Geirangerfjord cruise with an afternoon drive through Trollstigen.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1h
🆓 Free
⚠️ Road closed in winter — open mid-May to October
📍 Trollstigen · Rauma municipality

🍮 Local Tastes
Klippfisk Bacalao
↳ Ålesund's signature dish — dried and salted cod prepared Portuguese-style with tomatoes, olives, and paprika. The fish traveled from Ålesund's trawler fleet to Lisbon, became bacalhau, and returned to Norway as bacalao.
📍 Apotekergata No. 5 · Sentrum
Møre Lamb
↳ Free-range lamb from the coastal heathlands of Møre og Romsdal — salt air and wild herbs give a distinctly mineral character. Leaner and more flavourful than lowland lamb. Available as roast and chops at Norwegian-kitchen restaurants.
📍 Apotekergata No. 5 · Sentrum
Skillingsbolle
↳ Norwegian cardamom cinnamon bun — softer and more aromatic than a Swedish kanelbullar, enriched with cardamom in both the dough and the filling. A staple at Ålesund cafés and bakeries.
📍 Dråpe Kaffehus · Storgata
Brunost
↳ Brown cheese — made by boiling whey until the sugars caramelize. Sweet, slightly fudgy, unmistakably Norwegian. Eaten on crispbread or waffles. Available at the Alnes Lighthouse café and any Ålesund breakfast table.
📍 Alnes Lighthouse café · Godøy
Vafler
↳ Norwegian heart-shaped waffles — thinner and softer than Belgian waffles, eaten warm with sour cream and strawberry jam or brunost. A fixture at coastal cafés; the Alnes Lighthouse keeper's cottage café serves them with the fjord view.
📍 Alnes Lighthouse café · Godøy
