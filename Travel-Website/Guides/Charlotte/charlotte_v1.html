Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Bechtler Museum of Modern Art
1. Bechtler Museum of Modern Art
↳ A private collection assembled by Swiss art dealer Andreas Bechtler: Giacometti, Picasso, Miró, Calder, Tinguely, Warhol, and Le Corbusier across roughly 1,400 works spanning 1900–1970. Building by Mario Botta. Punches far above its weight for a city of Charlotte's size.
📖 Wikipedia

🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min

🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Mint Museum Uptown
2. Mint Museum Uptown
↳ The Uptown branch of the Mint Museum focuses on craft and design — ceramics, glass, fashion, textiles, and American decorative arts. One of the Southeast's strongest craft collections, occupying a striking contemporary tower in the Levine Center for the Arts plaza.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟 Mint Museum Uptown · mintmuseum.org

🚶 2 min · 🚕 3 min → Harvey B. Gantt Center for African-American Arts + Culture
3. Harvey B. Gantt Center for African-American Arts + Culture
↳ Rotating exhibitions of African-American visual arts, history, and culture. Named after Harvey Gantt, Charlotte's first Black mayor and the first Black student admitted to Clemson University. Located in the same Levine Center plaza as the Bechtler and Mint.

4. NASCAR Hall of Fame
↳ High-production museum tracing NASCAR's history from its moonshine-running roots to the modern circuit. Actual race cars, interactive driving simulators, and an induction gallery for the sport's legends. Charlotte is the sport's headquarters, making this its natural home.

2. Freedom Park
↳ A 98-acre urban park in the Myers Park neighborhood with a central lake, looping paths, public art, and a pavilion. Connects to Charlotte's greenway network. A natural midday break between Uptown and the afternoon's further-out stop.

3. Billy Graham Library
↳ A biographical museum and memorial to the Charlotte-born evangelist built on his childhood farm grounds. Includes a recreated 1937 dairy barn with multimedia exhibits, a prayer garden, and the graves of Billy and Ruth Graham. Free admission. Content is devotional in tone.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → U.S. National Whitewater Center
1. U.S. National Whitewater Center
↳ A 1,300-acre outdoor campus built around a world-class artificial whitewater course. The land activity complex offers ziplines, aerial ropes courses, climbing walls, and a bouldering facility on separate-ticket packages. Hosted the 2021 Canoe Slalom World Cup and U.S. Olympic team trials. Restaurants and a brewery on site.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Cheerwine
↳ A cherry soft drink created in Salisbury, NC in 1917. Burgundy in color, with a sweet-tart cherry taste lighter than national cherry sodas. A genuine Piedmont institution found everywhere in Charlotte — grocery stores, restaurants, and vending machines throughout the city.
📍 Grocery stores · restaurants throughout Charlotte
Lexington-Style BBQ
↳ Piedmont barbecue tradition: pork shoulder only, smoked over hardwood coals, served with a thin vinegar-and-ketchup "dip." Originated 45 miles northeast in Lexington, NC, the acknowledged center of Piedmont BBQ culture.
📍 Midwood Smokehouse · 1401 Central Avenue
Livermush
↳ A western NC breakfast staple: pork liver and trimmings blended with cornmeal and spices, formed into a loaf, sliced, and pan-fried until the edges crisp. Served at diner breakfasts across Charlotte. Polarizing but genuinely local — closer to scrapple than sausage.

Raleigh
↳ North Carolina's capital: the NC Museum of Natural Sciences (one of the Southeast's largest natural history museums, free), the NC Museum of Art, the State Capitol grounds, and the Fayetteville Street corridor. ❗️ Heads Up
Uptown museums — Monday closure
↳ The Bechtler, Mint Museum Uptown, and Harvey B. Gantt Center all close on Mondays. Plan Day 1 for Tuesday through Saturday.
U.S. National Whitewater Center — car required
↳ The USNWC is 25 minutes west of Uptown and not reachable by transit. Uber or a rental car is required for Day 3.
Billy Graham Library — devotional context
↳ The museum presents Graham's life from a faith perspective. Visitors of all backgrounds are welcome but should expect an overtly devotional tone throughout the exhibits.
✨ Claude Inspiration

Charlotte's cultural district packs three genuinely strong museums into a single city block — the Bechtler, the Mint Museum Uptown, and the Harvey B. Gantt Center all share the Levine Center for the Arts plaza on South Tryon Street. The Bechtler is the quiet revelation: a personal collection assembled by a Swiss patron that includes Giacometti, Calder, and Tinguely works that would anchor any major European institution. It is the kind of museum that rewards visitors who arrive without expectations and leave reconsidering what a mid-sized American city can hold.
