Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Elbphilharmonie
1. Elbphilharmonie
↳ Herzog & de Meuron's concert hall, a glass-and-steel wave set on a 1960s brick warehouse on the Elbe. The free public Plaza wraps the building at 37 m for a 360° view of the harbour and city; timed Plaza tickets are issued at the visitor centre.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 11:30pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Platz der Deutschen Einheit 1 · HafenCity
🚶 11 min · 🚕 4 min → Speicherstadt
2. Speicherstadt
↳ The world's largest contiguous warehouse district, built on oak piles from 1883 and UNESCO-listed since 2015. Red-brick neo-Gothic storehouses line the tidal canals; the Wasserschloss on the Poggenmühlen bridge is its most-photographed view.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Poggenmühlenbrücke · Speicherstadt
🚶 6 min · 🚕 3 min → Miniatur Wunderland
3. Miniatur Wunderland
↳ The world's largest model railway, spread across themed miniature worlds — Hamburg, the Alps, Scandinavia, the USA and a working model airport — with thousands of trains, vehicles, ships and aircraft in motion. Germany's most-visited attraction.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Miniatur Wunderland · 4.7⭐ · miniatur-wunderland.com
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Kehrwieder 2 · Speicherstadt
🚶 16 min · 🚕 5 min → St. Nikolai Memorial
4. St. Nikolai Memorial
↳ The bombed-out shell of the neo-Gothic St. Nikolai church, kept as a memorial to the victims of war. Its 147 m spire was briefly the tallest building in the world; a glass lift climbs to a 76 m viewing platform, with an exhibition on the 1943 air raids in the crypt.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 6 min · 🚕 3 min → Hamburger Rathaus
1. Hamburger Rathaus
↳ Neo-Renaissance city hall built 1886–1897 with 647 rooms and a 112 m tower; one of the few completely preserved historicist civic buildings in Germany. The open courtyard with its Hygieia fountain and the vaulted Rathausdiele foyer are freely accessible.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Sunday 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Rathausmarkt 1 · Altstadt
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Jungfernstieg & Binnenalster
2. Jungfernstieg & Binnenalster
↳ Hamburg's grand 600 m boulevard along the southern shore of the Binnenalster lake — Germany's first asphalt-paved street, laid in 1838. The promenade gives a panoramic view of the Alster fountain and the city skyline reflected in the water.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Jungfernstieg · Neustadt
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Planten un Blomen
3. Planten un Blomen
↳ A 47-hectare inner-city park whose name is Low German for "plants and flowers." It holds Europe's largest Japanese garden with a teahouse, tropical greenhouses, and a lake with water-light concerts in summer evenings.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 11:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Holstenwall 8 · Neustadt
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → St. Michaelis Church
4. St. Michaelis Church Tower
↳ Hamburg's most iconic landmark — the largest Protestant baroque church in northern Germany. Its 132 m copper-roofed tower offers a 360° viewing platform at 106 m reached by lift or 452 steps, overlooking the harbour, Elbe, and city centre.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Landungsbrücken
1. Landungsbrücken
↳ Hamburg's 700 m historic floating dock and waterfront terminal, built 1907–1909 and flanked by two green-domed towers. The elevated walkway above the S-Bahn station gives a sweeping panorama over the Elbe, harbour cranes, and passing container ships.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Bei den St. Pauli-Landungsbrücken · St. Pauli
🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Alter Elbtunnel
2. Alter Elbtunnel
↳ A 426 m pedestrian tunnel running 24 m beneath the Elbe, opened in 1911 — an engineering marvel of its era with original Art Nouveau tilework and terracotta reliefs of fish and marine life. The south exit offers a panoramic view back toward the Hamburg skyline.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 St. Pauli-Landungsbrücken 7 · St. Pauli
🚕 23 min → Blankenese Treppenviertel
3. Blankenese Treppenviertel
↳ A 700-year-old former fishing village with 58 staircases comprising nearly 5,000 steps winding around the 72 m Süllberg hill. Mediterranean-feeling white houses, hidden gardens, and panoramic Elbe views at every turn — unlike anything else in a northern European city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Treppenviertel · Blankenese
🚕 25 min → Chilehaus
4. Chilehaus
↳ A ten-story Brick Expressionist office building designed by Fritz Höger in 1924, whose two facades meet at an impossibly sharp angle like the prow of a ship. Part of the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Kontorhaus District — the world's first purpose-built office quarter.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Fischertwiete 2 · Altstadt
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → hotel

⚓ Notes from the Harbour City

Hamburg has more bridges than Venice, Amsterdam and London combined — over 2,500 of them — a consequence of building a port across the marshy islands where the Alster meets the Elbe. Almost every walk in the old centre crosses water.

Three days trace the city's whole arc. Day 1 follows the waterfront from the 2017 Elbphilharmonie through the 1880s Speicherstadt to the 1943 ruin of St. Nikolai — port wealth, war, and reinvention stacked along one mile of the Elbe. Day 2 pulls back to the civic core: the Rathaus with its 647 rooms, the Alster lake that makes Hamburg feel more like a Scandinavian capital than a German port, and the Michel tower that has marked the city's skyline since 1786. Day 3 descends to the working harbour and the Elbe tunnel that runs beneath it, then reaches the fishing-village staircases of Blankenese — proof that Hamburg's edges are as surprising as its centre.
