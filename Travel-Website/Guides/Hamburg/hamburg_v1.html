1. Elbphilharmonie
↳ Herzog & de Meuron's concert hall, a glass-and-steel wave set on a 1960s brick warehouse on the Elbe. The free public Plaza wraps the building at 37 m for a 360° view of the harbour and city; timed Plaza tickets are issued at the visitor centre.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 11:30pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free

🚶 11 min · 🚕 4 min → Speicherstadt
2. Speicherstadt
↳ The world's largest contiguous warehouse district, built on oak piles from 1883 and UNESCO-listed since 2015. Red-brick neo-Gothic storehouses line the tidal canals; the Wasserschloss on the Poggenmühlen bridge is its most-photographed view.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Poggenmühlenbrücke · Speicherstadt
🚶 6 min · 🚕 3 min → Miniatur Wunderland
3. Miniatur Wunderland
↳ The world's largest model railway, spread across themed miniature worlds — Hamburg, the Alps, Scandinavia, the USA and a working model airport — with thousands of trains, vehicles, ships and aircraft in motion. Germany's most-visited attraction.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Miniatur Wunderland · 4.7⭐ · miniatur-wunderland.com
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Kehrwieder 2 · Speicherstadt
🚶 16 min · 🚕 5 min → St. Nikolai Memorial
4. St. Nikolai Memorial
↳ The bombed-out shell of the neo-Gothic St. Nikolai church, kept as a memorial to the victims of war. Its 147 m spire was briefly the tallest building in the world; a glass lift climbs to a 76 m viewing platform, with an exhibition on the 1943 air raids in the crypt.
📖 Wikipedia

⚓ Notes from the Harbour City

Hamburg has more bridges than Venice, Amsterdam and London combined — over 2,500 of them — a consequence of building a port across the marshy islands where the Alster meets the Elbe. Almost every walk in the old centre crosses water.

The four stops on this day trace the city's whole arc within a single mile of waterfront: the medieval merchant canals along the Nikolaifleet, the 19th-century warehouse empire of the Speicherstadt, the 1943 ruin of St. Nikolai, and the 2017 Elbphilharmonie. Port wealth, war and reinvention, stacked along one stretch of the Elbe.
