Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Igreja de Santo António
1. Igreja de Santo António
↳ A small 18th-century church with an interior of breathtaking talha dourada — gilded carved woodwork over every surface, a painted barrel vault and blue-and-white azulejos. Entered through the municipal museum, it ranks among the finest baroque interiors in Portugal and is a national monument.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Forte da Ponta da Bandeira
↳ A compact 17th-century sea fort guarding the mouth of the harbour, reached by a short bridge over the water. Its ramparts open clean views back over the bay and the old town walls, and inside is a small exhibition on the Portuguese age of discovery.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Praia do Camilo
↳ A pocket beach framed by golden cliffs and reached by a much-photographed wooden staircase of around 200 steps. The descent opens onto turquoise water and a rock tunnel through to a second cove — the most striking small beach within walking reach of town.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

4. Ponta da Piedade
↳ Lagos's signature headland: ochre cliffs, sea stacks and arches dropping into clear water, with a 1913 lighthouse and a flat boardwalk along the rim. Railed platforms hang over the coves; a boat through the grottoes below is the classic way to see the formations up close.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 35 min → Fortaleza de Sagres
1. Fortaleza de Sagres
↳ A windswept clifftop fortress on Henry the Navigator's promontory, where Portugal planned its 15th-century voyages of discovery. Inside the walls lie the giant pebble compass rose and a lone chapel, with the Atlantic crashing on three sides.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Promontório de Sagres · Sagres
🚕 8 min → Cabo de São Vicente
2. Cabo de São Vicente
↳ The southwesternmost point of mainland Europe — sheer 75-metre cliffs the Romans called the Sacred Promontory, capped by a powerful lighthouse. Sunset here, where the land simply ends at the Atlantic, is among the most dramatic on the continent.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm

🧭 The Edge of the Known World

Both days of this trip trace the same thread without meaning to. The caravels that opened the Age of Discovery were rigged and crewed out of Lagos; the school of navigation that plotted their routes sat on the Sagres promontory you stand on in Day 2; and Cape St. Vincent was, for centuries, the literal edge of the known world — the last cliff before the blank part of the chart. Day 1 is the harbour the ships left from, Day 2 is the headland they aimed past.

It also rewards a little timing. The cliffs of Ponta da Piedade glow warmest in late-afternoon light, and Cape St. Vincent is the classic sunset cliff — so if Day 2's drive runs long, that is the cape working in your favour, not against it. Arrive at the lighthouse for the last hour of light and the wind, the cliffs and the falling sun do the rest.
