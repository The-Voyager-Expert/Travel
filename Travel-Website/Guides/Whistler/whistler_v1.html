Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Village Gondola Base
1.PEAK 2 PEAK Gondola
↳ A gondola connecting the two mountains at 436 m above the valley floor — formerly the world's longest unsupported span at 3.03 km. The 11-minute crossing passes between Roundhouse Lodge and Rendezvous Lodge with open views across the valley and terrain falling away on both sides.
📖 Wikipedia

⚠️ Mid-June to early September only. Buy online in advance to guarantee boarding time.
📍 4220 Gateway Drive · Village Centre
🚶 15 min · 🚕 7 min → Cloudraker Skybridge & Raven's Eye
2.Cloudraker Skybridge & Raven's Eye
↳ A 130 m suspension bridge at 2,182 m, crossing the Bowl between West Ridge and the summit — one of the highest pedestrian suspension bridges in North America. The Raven's Eye lookout adds a 360° view across the Coast Mountain range and the Tantalus Range.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Summer Alpine Experience Ticket · 4.7⭐ · Stop 1 purchase
🏛️ Daily 11:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Requires the Peak Express Chairlift from Roundhouse Lodge. Closes in poor visibility without advance notice.
📍 Peak Summit · Upper Mountain

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 20 min · 🚕 8 min → Lost Lake Park
1.Lost Lake Park
↳ A 56-hectare park surrounding a calm lake accessible on foot from the village — sandy beach, swimming water, a floating raft offshore, and a 4 km loop trail. One of the few free, car-free recreation areas in the corridor; a free shuttle from Village Square serves it in summer.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
📍 4700 Lost Lake Road · Lost Lake
🚕 20 min → Cheakamus Lake
2.Cheakamus Lake
↳ A glacier-fed alpine lake at 915 m inside Garibaldi Provincial Park — 5.7 km² of turquoise water enclosed by forested slopes rising to peaks 1,600 m above the shoreline. The 3.8 km trail from the parking area ends at a lakeshore campground and a viewpoint toward Rainbow Glacier.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~3 hr
⚠️ Access road closed November through May.
📍 Cheakamus Lake Trailhead · Function Junction
🚕 20 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Village Gondola Base
1.High Note Trail
↳ A 9.4 km alpine loop from the Peak along an exposed ridgeline above 2,100 m. The trail traverses the Bowl past the Cloudraker Skybridge, drops through alpine meadows to the Cheakamus Lake viewpoint, and returns via Harmony Lake. Elevation change 450 m; terrain stays above treeline throughout.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Summer Alpine Experience Ticket — whistlerblackcomb.com
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~3 hr 30 min
⚠️ Mid-June to mid-September only. Trail begins after the Peak Express Chairlift. Dress in layers.
📍 Roundhouse Lodge · Upper Mountain
🚕 30 min → Brandywine Falls Provincial Park
2.Brandywine Falls Provincial Park
↳ A 70 m waterfall where Brandywine Creek drops over a lava flow lip formed during the Late Pleistocene, part of the Garibaldi Volcanic Belt. A 10-minute walk from the parking area reaches a fenced viewpoint; the full loop extends 2.7 km through old-growth forest and past Lava Lake.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Highway 99 · Brandywine
🚕 30 min → hotel

🏔️ Claude Inspiration

Whistler is built on a contradiction. The mountains here are genuinely extreme — glaciated, crevassed, with snowfields that have never melted within recorded memory — and yet the village at the base is as comfortable and well-provisioned as any in the world. Most alpine resorts resolve this tension by keeping the wilderness at a safe distance, legible through glass from a well-heated lodge. Whistler doesn't. The gondola deposits you at 2,182 m with a 130-metre suspension bridge crossing a bowl whose walls drop away sharply on both sides. The Cheakamus Lake trail ends at the water's edge with nothing between the visitor and the glacier across the valley. The High Note Trail traverses a ridgeline where the terrain falls steeply enough in both directions that the route itself demands attention. The mountains aren't scenery here — they're the condition everything else is organized around. The village exists at their pleasure.
