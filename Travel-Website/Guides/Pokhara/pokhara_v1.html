Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 8 min → Phewa Lake & Tal Barahi Temple
1. Phewa Lake & Tal Barahi Temple
↳ Nepal's most visited lake stretches 5.7 km² at 742 m altitude; rowboats from the Lakeside ghat reach the two-storey pagoda temple on its island, sacred to the goddess Barahi and the most important religious monument in Pokhara.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Phewa Lake Ghat · Lakeside
🚕 15 min → Devi's Falls
2. Devi's Falls
↳ The overflow from Phewa Lake plunges into a narrow gorge and disappears into a 150 m underground tunnel — one of the few waterfalls in the world that vanishes entirely below ground.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
💵 Cash Only
📍 Siddhartha Highway · Chhorepatan

3. Gupteshwor Mahadev Cave
↳ At 2.9 km the longest cave in Nepal; the underground passage carries the water from Devi's Falls through limestone chambers decorated with stalactites and twin Shiva shrines.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
💵 Cash Only
📍 Chhorepatan · opposite Devi's Falls
🚕 10 min → International Mountain Museum
4. International Mountain Museum
↳ The world's foremost Himalayan mountaineering museum; thirteen galleries trace the geology and ecology of the Himalayas, document every 8,000 m peak, and display summit gear from Edmund Hillary, Tenzing Norgay, Reinhold Messner, and dozens of other expeditions.
📖 Wikipedia

1. Seti River Gorge
↳ The milky-white Seti Gandaki has cut a gorge through central Pokhara barely 2 m wide in places but 20 m deep; the K.I. Singh Bridge offers the clearest view of the river vanishing into its slot canyon below the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min

2. Australian Camp
↳ A 45-minute hike from the Kande trailhead climbs to a ridgeline at 2,060 m with a clear line of sight across the full Annapurna massif — no mountain permit or trekking agency required, just the ACAP conservation permit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~3h
⚠️ ACAP permit required — purchase at the ACAP office in Lakeside before departing

🍮 Local Tastes
Dal Bhat
↳ The national meal: steamed rice with lentil soup, seasonal vegetable curry, and pickled condiments — refills are always free, and every Lakeside set-meal restaurant serves a reliable version.
📍 Rosemary Kitchen · Lakeside
Momo
↳ Nepali dumplings with spiced meat or vegetable filling; pan-fried or steamed, always served with a sharp tomato-sesame dipping sauce — Pokhara has some of the best in the country.
📍 Lakeside
Thakali Khana Set
↳ The mountain feast of the Thakali people from the Mustang corridor — ghee-rich dal, smoked mutton, buckwheat bread, and fermented greens; Pokhara is the entry point to Mustang and has the best Thakali restaurants outside the region itself.
📍 Lakeside
Sel Roti
↳ Sweet, ring-shaped deep-fried rice bread — eaten at breakfast or as a festival snack; the crisp exterior and chewy interior are unlike any other Himalayan bread.

🏔️ The Hidden Geography

Pokhara sits at 822 m, but the peaks visible from Sarangkot — Annapurna I at 8,091 m, Machhapuchhare at 6,993 m, Dhaulagiri at 8,167 m — are among the highest on Earth and closer than almost any major viewpoint in Nepal. The reason you can see them at all from a city is geological accident: a flat river valley carved by the Seti Gandaki ended up exactly at the right distance from the Himalayan wall, roughly 30 km, which is close enough to fill the horizon but far enough that the full massif fits in a single field of view at ground level. Most mountain panoramas in Nepal require days of trekking to reach that viewing distance.

The Seti Gandaki running invisibly through the middle of the city is the same water system as Phewa Lake: the lake's overflow drains south as the Phusre Khola, vanishes at Devi's Falls into underground caves, and re-emerges as part of the Seti's channel. The milky-white colour comes from glacial flour — particles of rock ground to powder by the Annapurna glaciers upstream — which gives the river its alternative name, the Milk River.
