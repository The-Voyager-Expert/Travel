Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Castle
1. Osaka Castle
↳ The city's defining landmark, a reconstructed castle tower rising above vast moats and stone walls. Eight floors of museum exhibits chart Toyotomi Hideyoshi's unification campaigns. The observation deck commands a full-circle panorama over the central skyline.

3. Namba Yasaka Shrine
↳ A Shinto shrine dominated by a 12-metre lion-head stage built in 1975. The lion's eyes double as lights and its nose as a speaker. Locals believe standing before the open mouth devours misfortune.
📖 Wikipedia

4. Dotonbori
↳ A canal-side entertainment strip lined with giant mechanical signs, neon facades, and open-front food stalls. The Glico Running Man sign has marked the Ebisu Bridge crossing since 1935. Best visited after dark when every surface glows.

🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Sumiyoshi Taisha
1. Sumiyoshi Taisha
↳ One of Japan's oldest shrines, founded in the third century before Buddhism arrived. Its four main halls use the pure Sumiyoshi-zukuri style with straight roofs free of continental influence. The steeply arched Sorihashi Bridge is the visual centrepiece.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 2-9-89 Sumiyoshi · Sumiyoshi-ku

2. Tsutenkaku Tower
↳ A 103-metre observation tower rebuilt in 1956 at the heart of Shinsekai, the city's retro entertainment quarter. The district around its base preserves a postwar atmosphere of kushikatsu shops and game arcades unchanged for decades.

3. Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan
↳ One of the world's largest aquariums, built around a central Pacific Ocean tank nine metres deep housing whale sharks. A descending spiral ramp passes through 15 habitat zones from Arctic ice to tropical reef. Over 30,000 creatures across 620 species.

1. Cup Noodles Museum Osaka Ikeda
↳ Built where Momofuku Ando invented instant ramen in 1958. A tunnel of 800 product packages lines the entrance hall. The My CupNoodles Factory lets visitors design a personal cup and choose from four soups and 12 toppings.

2. Umeda Sky Building
↳ Two 40-storey towers joined at the top by a circular Floating Garden Observatory at 173 metres. An open-air rooftop deck offers unobstructed 360-degree views. The escalator crossing between towers through open air is the architectural set piece.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Osaka Tenmangu
↳ A Shinto shrine founded in 949 dedicated to Sugawara no Michizane, patron of scholarship. The annual Tenjin Matsuri held here each July ranks among Japan's three greatest festivals. The main hall's ornate roof carvings repay close inspection.

🍮 Local Tastes
Takoyaki
↳ Crisp-shelled wheat batter balls filled with diced octopus, pickled ginger, and green onion. Invented in 1935. Finished with thick sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and seaweed.
📍 Wanaka Sennichimae · Takoyaki Juhachiban · Aizuya
Okonomiyaki
↳ A savoury cabbage pancake griddled with pork, egg, and a yam-enriched batter. The local style mixes all ingredients before cooking, unlike Hiroshima's layered method. Each table has its own teppan griddle.
📍 Fukutaro · Mizuno · Ajinoya
Kushikatsu
↳ Deep-fried skewers of meat, seafood, and vegetables coated in panko and dipped once in a communal sauce. Double-dipping is famously forbidden. Shinsekai is the original heartland.
📍 Daruma Dotonbori · Yaekatsu · Kushikatsu Tanaka
Kitsune Udon
↳ Thick wheat noodles in light dashi broth topped with a large slab of sweetened fried tofu. Created in the 1890s at Usami Tei Matsubaya, the city's oldest surviving udon shop.
📍 Usami Tei Matsubaya · Tsurutontan Dotonbori

🌿 Claude Inspiration

The city's identity is built on street food in a way no other Japanese city matches. Dotonbori and Shinsekai offer the purest version of kuidaore — eating yourself bankrupt — the local philosophy of prioritizing food over appearances. The combination of accessible shrines, castle history, modern observation points, and a food culture dense enough to fill three days makes this one of the most rewarding short trips in East Asia.
