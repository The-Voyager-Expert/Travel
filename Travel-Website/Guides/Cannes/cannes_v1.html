Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Palais des Festivals & Chemin des Étoiles
1. Palais des Festivals & Chemin des Étoiles
↳ Home of the Cannes Film Festival and the most recognized cinema steps in the world. Walk the actual red carpet staircase where directors and stars ascend every May. The Chemin des Étoiles embeds nearly 400 signed celebrity handprints — Deneuve · Stone · Stallone — into the pavement outside.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 1 Boulevard de la Croisette · La Croisette
🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Vieux-Port de Cannes
2. Vieux-Port de Cannes
↳ A working port where fishing boats sit alongside nine-figure superyachts at the foot of the medieval hilltop. Quai Saint-Pierre lines the waterfront with terrace restaurants opening to the water. The port hosts the Cannes Yachting Festival each September and on ordinary days is the most alive stretch of the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Quai Saint-Pierre · Le Suquet
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Le Suquet
3. Le Suquet
↳ The original Cannes before the glamour — a medieval hilltop quarter of cobblestone lanes · fishermen's houses · and umbrella pines climbing toward the old château. From the summit the view opens over the entire Bay of Cannes: La Croisette curving east and the Lérins Islands floating in turquoise water on the horizon.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rue du Mont Chevalier · Le Suquet
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Musée de la Castre
4. Musée de la Castre
↳ Inside Le Suquet's 12th-century château: Mediterranean and Middle Eastern antiquities — Phoenician · Greek · Egyptian and Oceanian collections in castle halls. The tower is the highlight: 109 steps to a 360° panorama of the bay and Lérins Islands that no rooftop bar can match.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Vieux-Port · 🚢 15 min → Île Sainte-Marguerite
1. Île Sainte-Marguerite
↳ The larger Lérins island holds Fort Royal — a 17th-century state prison whose most famous tenant was the Man in the Iron Mask · held here from 1687 to 1698. The cell survives intact with its legend unresolved. Beyond the fort: eucalyptus and pine forests · no cars · and crystal water on every shore.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Fort Royal · 4.5⭐ · cannes.com
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:30pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 Fort Royal · Île Sainte-Marguerite
🚢 10 min → Île Saint-Honorat
2. Île Saint-Honorat
↳ One of the oldest continuously inhabited monastic sites in Europe — founded around 410 AD · Cistercian monks still live and pray here today. The fortified 11th-century tower that served as refuge during pirate raids stands at the water's edge. The walk passes ruined chapels and monastic vineyards.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 7:30pm
⏰ ~2 hrs
🆓 Free

🚢 20 min → Vieux-Port · 🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → La Malmaison
3. La Malmaison
↳ A Belle Époque seaside villa that opened as an art centre in 1952 and returned after a full expansion that tripled its exhibition space. Cannes' most serious contemporary art venue — shows rotate several times a year and consistently bring major international and French names to the Riviera coast.

🍮 Local Tastes
Socca
↳ Thin chickpea-flour pancake cooked on a copper plate in a wood-fired oven — golden and crispy at the edges. A Riviera street staple served hot with pepper and olive oil.
📍 Rue Meynadier · Le Suquet
Pissaladière
↳ Provençal flatbread tart with slow-cooked caramelized onions · black olives · anchovies. Savory and deeply flavored. Sold by the slice at Le Suquet bakeries.
📍 Le Suquet bakeries
Bouillabaisse
↳ The original Provençal fish stew from Marseille — broth first with croutons and saffron rouille · then the fish. Rockfish and rascasse from the morning boats. Order for two minimum.
📍 L'Assiette Provençale
Tarte Tropézienne
↳ Brioche filled with light cream and orange blossom water. Invented in Saint-Tropez but a Riviera staple throughout the coast. Best at room temperature from a local pâtisserie.
📍 City Centre pâtisseries

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Antibes · 12 minutes
↳ Rampart-walled old town on the Riviera coast — the Picasso Museum in the Château Grimaldi and the largest yacht harbor in the Mediterranean. SNCF TER runs every 15 minutes direct.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
Nice · 30 minutes
↳ The French Riviera capital — the Promenade des Anglais · Vieux-Nice Baroque old town · Cours Saleya market. SNCF TER runs every 30 minutes direct.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
Monaco · 50 minutes
↳ The Prince's Palace above Monte-Carlo · the casino square · the Oceanographic Museum · the Formula 1 circuit. SNCF TER via Nice. Book early during race weekends.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
Menton · 55 min
↳ The most Italian town in France on the Italian border — the baroque Basilica Saint-Michel above a pastel waterfront, the Cocteau Museum, and lemon groves on the hillside. SNCF TER direct via Nice.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
Ventimiglia · Italy · 1h 5 min
↳ The first Italian town across the border — a large Friday market, a medieval borgo above the modern town, and the coastal Roman ruins at Nervia. Cross-border SNCF TER runs hourly.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
