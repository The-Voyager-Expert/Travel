🌴 Colonial Compression

Three days in Colombo are three walks through the same 400 meters of coastline that the Dutch, British, and Sri Lankans each claimed in turn. The hospital they built to trade, the parliament they built to govern, and the hotel their guests built to face the Indian Ocean are all still standing within five minutes of each other — none of them museums, all of them still in use.

That compression — Dutch colonial brick against Victorian stucco against a 19th-century grand hotel — is what makes the Fort district unlike any other colonial-era city center in South Asia.
