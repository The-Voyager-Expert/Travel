1. Wellington Cable Car
↳ The bright-red funicular has climbed from Lambton Quay to the Kelburn hilltop since 1902, rumbling through three lit tunnels to a lookout over the harbour, the rooftops and the green belt — Wellington's signature five-minute ride.

2. Wellington Botanic Garden
↳ Twenty-five hectares of hillside gardens roll down from the cable-car summit — native bush, a fern gully, the Lady Norwood rose garden and the historic Dominion Observatory, all threaded with paths back toward the city.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Zealandia Te Māra a Tāne
↳ The world's first fully fenced urban ecosanctuary — 225 hectares of regenerating valley behind a predator-proof fence, where 32 km of tracks pass free-living tuatara, kākā, takahē and other rare native wildlife minutes from the CBD.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Zealandia

4. Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa
↳ New Zealand's national museum on the waterfront — six floors of Māori treasures, a colossal squid, the Gallipoli giants modelled by Wētā, an earthquake house and a living carved marae, all wrapped in interactive, storytelling exhibits.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Flat White
↳ Espresso under a thin layer of steamed micro-foam — a drink Wellington proudly claims to have invented in the late 1980s. The city brands itself the coffee capital of New Zealand, and the flat white is its house pour.

Wellington Craft Beer
↳ Wellington is New Zealand's craft-beer capital, dense with independent breweries. Garage Project, born in a Te Aro petrol station, is the flagship — wildly inventive small-batch beers poured at its taprooms.

✨ The Coolest Little Capital

Wellington packs an outsized amount of life into a tight bowl of harbour and hills, and two days is enough to feel it. The shape of this trip is deliberate: spend the first day climbing — cable car up, gardens down, then out to Zealandia where the bush is loud with birds that exist almost nowhere else — and end on the flat at Te Papa by the water. The second day belongs to the eastern hills and the film-making peninsula, two short rides that bookend the harbour.

Build slack into the plan for the things Wellington does better than almost anywhere: a flat white that started here, a Garage Project beer brewed down the road, a wander up Cuba Street between dinners. The wind is real — they don't call it Windy Welly for nothing — so keep a layer handy and treat a clear, still afternoon at Mount Victoria as the gift it is.
