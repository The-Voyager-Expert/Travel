Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 4 min · 🚕 2 min → Naples Pier
1. Naples Pier
↳ Historic wooden pier extending into the Gulf of Mexico, offering free dolphin viewing and nightly sunsets. Built in 1888, this working pier is home to fishing crews and local wildlife spotters. The pier is where locals and visitors converge at golden hour — dolphins breach offshore while fishermen cast lines, and the sun sinks into the gulf with astonishing color. Free access.
📖 Wikipedia
⏰ ~45 min
📍 12th Avenue South · Downtown Waterfront

2. Naples Beach
↳ Voted one of America's best beaches by Travel Channel. Ten miles of pristine white sand backed by natural dunes and sea oats, with few crowds and calm gulf waters ideal for swimming. Public beach access, free parking.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary
1. Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary
↳ National Audubon Society preserve protecting 13,000 acres featuring North America's largest remaining virgin bald cypress forest. A 2.25-mile elevated boardwalk winds through diverse ecosystems — cypress swamp, pine flatwoods, wet prairie — with regular sightings of alligators, wading birds (roseate spoonbills, wood storks), and native wildlife. Sunrise or early morning visits offer the best light and coolest temperatures.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Naples Botanical Garden
↳ World-class 170-acre botanical garden showcasing plants and design principles from tropical and subtropical regions — Brazil, Caribbean, Peru, Thailand, and more. Walking tours guide visitors through orchid collections, native plants, and seasonal blooms. Perfect for mid-day exploration and shade from mature trees.

3. Baker Museum
↳ Contemporary art museum featuring innovative installations across 15 galleries on three floors. Notable for Dale Chihuly's monumental glass sculptures, including a fiery cascading chandelier that dominates the central atrium. The museum emphasizes modern and contemporary work with rotating exhibitions.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Rookery Bay (Rising Tide Kayaking)
1. Rising Tide Explorers · Kayaking Tour
↳ Guided kayaking through mangrove tunnels and oyster reefs of Rookery Bay with expert naturalist guides who highlight dolphins, osprey, wading birds, and manatees. Small group tours (4-6 paddlers) with included complimentary photography and wildlife spotting expertise. Morning paddle through calm waters with high probability of marine life encounters.

📍 Rookery Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve · South Naples

2. Tin City
↳ Historic waterfront marketplace in converted fish house and cannery with locally owned shops, galleries, and seafood eateries. Working fishing docks at the marina with fresh seafood available at casual counters. Open-air seating with water views, local vibe, and casual lunch spots serving grouper sandwiches and shrimp.
📖 Wikipedia
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Tin City · Downtown Waterfront

🍮 Local Tastes
Stone Crab
Naples' signature seasonal delicacy available October 15 through May 1. Served hot with drawn butter or chilled with tangy mustard sauce. Try at Truluck's, Captain & Krewe, or Kelly's Fish House.
Grouper Sandwich
Local coastal classic of fried grouper from local fishermen, served with fries and coleslaw in a bun. Grouper & Chips is the must-visit destination for this iconic sandwich.
Sanibel Pink Shrimp
Regional delicacy found on many Naples restaurant menus. Fresh Gulf shrimp with distinctive pink color and sweet flavor profile.
Gulf Seafood
Fresh-catch preparations highlight mahi-mahi, snapper, and local fish. Most restaurants feature daily changing seafood specials from that morning's catch.

🚌 Getting Around
Rideshare
Uber and Lyft widely available throughout Naples. Recommended for travel between scattered attractions and returning from evening dining. Typical ride cost: $8-$18 within city limits.
Public Transit
Collier Area Transit (CAT) bus system with electronic farebox accepting cash, mobile tickets, and reloadable smart cards. Limited coverage due to spread-out suburban layout. Ride passes available via rideCAT app.
Walking
Downtown Naples and Old Naples (5th Avenue South and 3rd Street South) are pedestrian-friendly for shopping, galleries, and dining. Most other attractions require car or rideshare.
Biking
30+ miles of bike lanes and shared-use paths. Flat terrain ideal for cycling. Many destinations accessible by bike from downtown.
Car Rental
Recommended for exploring beyond downtown. Distances between attractions and beaches make car rental beneficial for 3-day stay.

❗️ Heads Up
Stone Crab Season
Stone crab season runs October 15 through May 1 only. Summer and early fall travel will not include this signature delicacy on menus.
Naples Botanical Garden Closure
The Botanical Garden is closed October through May for maintenance. Plan this attraction only if traveling June-September.
Museum Hours
Baker Museum closes Monday and has afternoon-only hours on Sunday. Verify hours before visiting.
Hurricane Season
Naples is in the Gulf Coast hurricane belt (June-November). Summer travel carries higher storm risk. Tours and outdoor activities may be cancelled on short notice.
🌊 Claude Inspiration
Naples has the rhythm of a place where water orchestrates time. The pier at sunset becomes a stage where locals and dolphins negotiate who gets the best light. The mangrove kayaks glide so quietly that you hear the marsh itself — the click of fiddler crabs, the splash of feeding fish, osprey announcing territory. This is beach town life calibrated to wildlife, not crowds. The grouper sandwich at Grouper & Chips is a three-minute conversation with what Gulf fishermen caught that morning. Stay in downtown's mix of galleries and restaurants; walk to breakfast, rideshare to birds.
