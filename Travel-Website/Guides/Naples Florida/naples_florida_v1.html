1. Naples Pier
↳ Historic wooden pier extending into the Gulf of Mexico since 1888. Free access to watch dolphins breach offshore, fishermen cast lines, and sunset light paint the water. Golden hour is peak time — locals and tourists converge as the sun dips into the gulf with astonishing color.

📍 12th Avenue South · Downtown Waterfront

2. Naples Beach
↳ Ten miles of pristine white sand voted one of America's best beaches. Calm gulf waters ideal for swimming, natural dunes backed by sea oats, few crowds. Public beach access with free parking.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary
1. Corkscrew Swamp Sanctuary
↳ National Audubon Society preserve spanning 13,000 acres with North America's largest remaining virgin bald cypress forest. Elevated 2.25-mile boardwalk winds through cypress swamp, pine flatwoods, and wet prairie. Regular sightings: alligators, roseate spoonbills, wood storks, native wildlife. Sunrise or early morning visits offer best light and coolest temperatures.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 5:30pm
⏰ ~2h 30 min

2. Naples Botanical Garden
↳ World-class 170-acre botanical garden showcasing plants from tropical and subtropical regions — Brazil, Caribbean, Peru, Thailand. Walking tours through orchid collections, native plantings, seasonal blooms. Mature trees provide ample shade.

↳ Contemporary art museum with 15 galleries across three floors. Notable for Dale Chihuly's monumental glass sculptures, including a fiery cascading chandelier dominating the central atrium. Focus on modern and contemporary work with rotating exhibitions.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Rookery Bay (Rising Tide Kayaking)
1. Rising Tide Explorers · Kayaking Tour
↳ Guided kayaking through mangrove tunnels and oyster reefs with expert naturalist guides. Small group tours (4-6 paddlers) highlight dolphins, osprey, wading birds, manatees. Morning paddle through calm waters with high probability of marine life encounters. Complimentary photography included.
⏰ ~2h 30 min
📍 Rookery Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve · South Naples
🚕 10 min → Tin City for lunch
2. Tin City
↳ Historic waterfront marketplace in converted fish house and cannery with locally owned shops, galleries, and seafood eateries. Working fishing docks at the marina, fresh seafood at casual counters, open-air seating with water views. Casual lunch spot for grouper sandwiches and shrimp.
📖 Wikipedia
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Tin City · Downtown Waterfront
🚕 12 min → Naples Princess (afternoon)
3. Naples Princess · Sightseeing Cruise
↳ Luxury sightseeing cruise featuring dolphin spotting, eagle sightings, and waterfront estate viewing. Climate-controlled interior with full-service bar or open-air sundeck seating. Afternoon departure ideal for post-lunch activity with evening light.
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Downtown Marine Terminals · Naples Waterfront
🚕 8 min → Hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Stone Crab — Signature seasonal delicacy (October 15 - May 1). Served hot with drawn butter or chilled with tangy mustard sauce.
Grouper Sandwich — Local coastal classic of fried grouper from local fishermen, fries, coleslaw in a bun. Grouper & Chips is the destination.
Sanibel Pink Shrimp — Regional delicacy found on many menus. Fresh Gulf shrimp with distinctive pink color and sweet flavor.
Gulf Seafood — Fresh-catch preparations of mahi-mahi, snapper, local fish. Daily specials from that morning's catch.

🚌 Getting Around
Rideshare · Uber and Lyft widely available. Typical ride: $8-$18 within city limits.
Public Transit · Collier Area Transit (CAT) bus system. Limited coverage. Ride passes via rideCAT app.
Walking · Downtown Naples (5th Avenue South, 3rd Street South) is pedestrian-friendly for shopping, galleries, dining.
Biking · 30+ miles of bike lanes and shared-use paths. Flat terrain ideal for cycling.
Car Rental · Recommended for exploring beyond downtown. Distances between attractions make car rental beneficial.
❗️ Heads Up
Stone Crab Season · Available October 15 - May 1 only. Summer travel will not include this signature delicacy.
Naples Botanical Garden Closure · Closed October - May. Plan this attraction only for June-September travel.
Museum Hours · Baker Museum closes Monday. Verify hours before visiting.
Hurricane Season · Naples is in the Gulf Coast hurricane belt (June-November). Summer travel carries higher storm risk.
