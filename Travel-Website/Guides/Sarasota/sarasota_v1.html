1. The John and Mable Ringling Museum of Art
↳ A world-class art museum housed in a 66-room Italian Renaissance-style mansion. The museum holds one of the greatest collections of baroque paintings and sculptures outside Europe — works by Rubens, Van Dyck, and Poussin dominate the main gallery. The mansion itself, Ca' d'Zan, is a masterpiece of 1920s architecture. The grounds cover 66 acres with sprawling gardens, baroque statuary, and the historic Asolo Theatre. Essential for art lovers and architecture enthusiasts.

2. Ca' d'Zan Mansion
↳ John Ringling's 66-room mansion, Italian Renaissance revival masterpiece completed in 1925. Palatial rooms with imported marble, gilded ceilings, and mahogany panelling overlook Sarasota Bay. The bedroom, library, and loggia are highlights. A guided tour reveals the opulence and the art collection woven throughout the home. The rooftop loggia offers panoramic water views.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Guided tours only – included with museum ticket
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 5401 Bay Shore Road · Downtown

3. Ringling Museum Gardens
↳ Formal gardens sprawling across the 66-acre estate, featuring baroque statuary, Italian Renaissance fountains, and manicured landscapes. Pathways wind through rose gardens, tropical plantings, and water features. Ideal for a leisurely afternoon stroll after touring the mansion and galleries. The fountain courts and sculpture collections rival European palace grounds.

🏛️ Open with museum admission

1. Marie Selby Botanical Gardens
↳ Forty acres of botanical paradise with more than 20,000 orchids, bromeliads, and tropical plants. The gardens feature lagoons, bamboo groves, rain forest sections, and a banyan grove. Waterfalls and walking paths weave through themed gardens. The orchid conservatory is world-renowned. The banyan grove is a highlight — massive, ancient trees creating a cathedral-like canopy. One of the finest botanical gardens in North America.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Marie Selby Gardens · 4.7⭐ · selby.org

2. Siesta Key Beach
↳ Consistently ranked among America's best beaches. Two miles of fine white quartz sand, shallow warm gulf waters, and excellent sunset views. The beach is wide, family-friendly, and backed by parks with picnic facilities and lifeguards. The sand is so pure and white it squeaks when you walk on it. Beach bars and casual restaurants line the shore. Perfect for swimming, shelling, and relaxing.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hrs

3. St. Armands Circle
↳ Charming roundabout shopping and dining district between Lido and Siesta Keys. Independent boutiques, art galleries, and world-class restaurants line the circular promenade. The area is sophisticated but walkable — no big-box chains. Palm-lined streets, outdoor dining, and elegant storefronts. A perfect spot for an evening stroll and dinner after the beach.

1. Spanish Point Historic Preserve
↳ Fifteen acres of pristine peninsula preserving Sarasota's human and natural history. The historic site includes the Guptill House (Victorian homestead), native plant gardens, and a network of trails. Kayak rental available for exploring the shoreline. The property showcases native Florida ecosystems and pre-Columbian archaeology. Excellent for understanding the region's cultural heritage and paddling through mangrove channels.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Tuesday – Sunday 11:00am - 4:00pm

2. Mote Marine Laboratory & Aquariums
↳ Leading nonprofit research institute with outstanding public aquariums. The Shark Reef Aquarium is the centerpiece — a 135,000-gallon tank featuring nurse, sawfish, and bonnethead sharks. Other exhibits showcase sea turtles, seahorses, and Gulf of Mexico marine life. Interactive touch pools and educational presentations run throughout the day. Research partnerships with universities worldwide make this a serious marine science institution.

3. Downtown Sarasota Arts District
↳ Vibrant cultural hub centered on Main Street with galleries, theaters, and public art installations. The Ringling School of Art and Design anchors the district. Street-level galleries feature contemporary local artists. The Van Wezel Hall (arts venue) and Asolo Repertory Theatre are world-class cultural institutions. Galleries, boutiques, and cafes line walkable streets. An excellent evening destination for culture and dining.

🍮 Local Tastes
Stone crab claws — October through May season. Sweet, tender meat with a distinctive flavor. Served chilled with mustard sauce at every upscale restaurant.
Fresh grouper — Gulf catch, often prepared simply grilled or blackened. Mild, flaky white fish reflecting the local fishery.
Key lime pie — Tart, creamy, and iconic to South Florida. Every bakery and dessert menu features a version.
Cuban sandwich (Cubano) — Roast pork, ham, Swiss cheese, pickles, and mustard pressed on Cuban bread. A Sarasota staple with strong Miami influence.
🚌 Getting Around
🚕 Ride-hailing — Uber and Lyft operate throughout Sarasota. Short rides downtown cost $8–15. No bike-share system city-wide, but many hotels offer bike rentals.
🚌 Public transit — Sarasota County Area Transit (SCAT) runs local bus routes. Routes are infrequent (30–60 min). Useful only for planned daytime travel; not recommended for restaurants and evening activities.
🚗 Rental cars — Recommended for exploring beaches and sites outside downtown. Major agencies at Sarasota–Bradenton International Airport (SRQ), 10 miles north. Parking is free or cheap at most beaches and attractions.
🚴 Biking — Flat terrain and scenic paths. Bayfront Trail runs 40+ miles along the coast. Hotel bike rentals available.
🎭 Shows
🎪 Van Wezel Hall — State-of-the-art performing arts venue hosting Broadway tours, symphony, ballet, and concerts. World-class programming.
🎪 Asolo Repertory Theatre — Professional resident theatre with a full season of plays and musicals. Housed partly in a historic 1798 Italian court theatre transplanted to Sarasota.
🎪 Sarasota Orchestra — Symphony orchestra with a full concert season October through April. Performs at Van Wezel Hall.
