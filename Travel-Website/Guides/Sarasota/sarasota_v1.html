1. Ringling Museum
↳ World-class art museum with baroque paintings by Rubens and Van Dyck, housed in a 66-room Italian Renaissance mansion. The grounds span 66 acres with baroque statuary, fountains, and formal gardens. One ticket covers museum, mansion, and gardens—a unified full-day complex.

2. Ca' d'Zan
↳ John Ringling's 66-room mansion, Marble halls, mahogany panelling, and gilded ceilings frame palatial rooms overlooking Sarasota Bay. Guided tours included with museum admission reveal opulent interiors. The rooftop loggia offers panoramic water views. Architectural companion to the museum galleries.

3. Ringling Gardens
↳ Formal 66-acre gardens with baroque statuary, Italian fountains, rose gardens, and tropical plantings. Pathways connect fountain courts and sculpture collections rivaling European palace grounds. Ideal for unhurried exploration and photography completing the full museum and mansion experience in one day.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 15 min · 🚕 8 min → Marie Selby Botanical Gardens
1. Marie Selby Botanical Gardens
↳ Forty acres with 20,000 orchids, bromeliads, and tropical plants. Lagoons, bamboo groves, rain forest sections, and a banyan grove with cathedral-like canopy. Waterfalls and walking paths throughout. The orchid conservatory is world-renowned. One of the finest botanical gardens in North America.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hrs 30 min
📍 900 South Palm Avenue
🚶 10 min · 🚕 10 min → Siesta Key Beach
2. Siesta Key Beach
↳ Consistently ranked among America's best beaches. Two miles of fine white quartz sand, shallow warm gulf waters, and excellent sunset views. The beach is wide, family-friendly, and backed by parks with lifeguards. The sand squeaks when you walk on it. Beach bars and casual restaurants line the shore.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 948 Beach Road
🚶 3 min · 🚕 5 min → St. Armands Circle
3. St. Armands Circle
↳ Charming roundabout shopping and dining district between Lido and Siesta Keys. Independent boutiques, art galleries, and world-class restaurants line the circular promenade. Palm-lined streets and elegant storefronts. Perfect for an evening stroll and dinner after the beach.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 20 min · 🚕 12 min → Spanish Point Historic Preserve
1. Spanish Point Historic Preserve
↳ Fifteen acres preserving Sarasota's human and natural history. The historic site includes the Guptill House, native plant gardens, and walking trails. Kayak rental available for exploring the shoreline and mangrove channels. The property showcases native Florida ecosystems and archaeology.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 11:00am - 4:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 337 North Tamiami Trail
🚶 15 min · 🚕 15 min → Mote Marine Laboratory
2. Mote Marine Laboratory
↳ Nonprofit research institute with public aquariums. The Shark Reef Aquarium features a 135,000-gallon tank with nurse and sawfish sharks. Exhibits showcase sea turtles, seahorses, and Gulf of Mexico marine life. Interactive touch pools and educational presentations run throughout the day.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 1600 Ken Thompson Parkway
🚶 15 min · 🚕 8 min → Downtown Sarasota Arts District
3. Downtown Sarasota Arts District
↳ Cultural hub centered on Main Street with galleries, theaters, and public art. Street-level galleries feature contemporary local artists. Van Wezel Hall and Asolo Repertory Theatre are world-class institutions. Boutiques and cafes line walkable streets. Excellent for evening culture and dining.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
