Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 25 min · 🚕 8 min → Römerberg
1. Römerberg
↳ Frankfurt's medieval market square, centered on the Römer city hall with its stepped gables. The square was reconstructed after WWII and anchors the Dom-Römer quarter. The Gerechtigkeitsbrunnen stands at its center.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Römerberg · Altstadt · 60311 Frankfurt am Main
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min
2. Historisches Museum Frankfurt
↳ Frankfurt's principal history museum, housed in the medieval Saalhof complex. Thirteen halls trace the city from Roman origins to the present, with reconstructed medieval cityscapes, artifacts from the Holy Roman Empire, and a complete model of pre-WWII Frankfurt.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~1 hr 30 min

🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min
3. Kaiserdom
↳ Frankfurt Cathedral served as the election and coronation site for Holy Roman Emperors for more than four centuries. The late Gothic tower rises 95 meters above the Altstadt. The Wahlkapelle, where the seven prince-electors gathered before each coronation, is accessible inside the nave.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 Domplatz 1 · Altstadt · 60311 Frankfurt am Main
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min
4. Paulskirche
↳ Germany's first democratically elected parliament convened in this circular church in the mid-19th century, drafting a constitution and debating national unification. Now a national monument and event hall, it hosts the Peace Prize of the German Book Trade each October.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Paulsplatz 11 · Altstadt · 60311 Frankfurt am Main
🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min
5. Goethe-Haus
↳ The four-story townhouse where Johann Wolfgang von Goethe was born and lived until leaving for Weimar at 26. Reconstructed after WWII, it shows the private library, puppet theater, and writing rooms of his early life. The adjacent Goethe Museum holds related paintings and manuscripts.
📖 Wikipedia

1. Liebieghaus Sculpture Collection
↳ Frankfurt's sculpture museum spans 5,000 years from Ancient Egypt through the Renaissance in a late 19th-century villa on the Museumsufer. Key holdings include Hellenistic bronzes, medieval carved altarpieces, and a renowned Rococo small sculpture collection spanning Bouchardon to Houdon.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Städel Museum
↳ One of Germany's foremost art museums, with 700 years of European painting from the 14th century to the present. Key holdings include Botticelli's Venus, Vermeer's The Geographer, and major German Expressionism. The underground extension houses the contemporary collection.

3. Deutsches Filmmuseum
↳ Germany's leading film museum traces cinema from early optical toys to digital production. A restored early 20th-century cinema, projection equipment from five eras, and rotating international exhibitions anchor the permanent collection. The cinematheque programs daily repertory screenings.

4. Museum Angewandte Kunst
↳ Frankfurt's applied arts and design museum spans 5,000 years in a historic villa and a late 20th-century extension by Richard Meier. Permanent collections cover Islamic art, medieval manuscripts, European furniture across five centuries, and industrial design including the Braun product archive.

1. Senckenberg Museum
↳ One of Europe's largest natural history museums, housing Germany's most extensive dinosaur collection. The permanent exhibition spans paleontology, mineralogy, zoology, and anthropology across three floors, anchored by a 27-meter Diplodocus skeleton and multiple complete tyrannosaur specimens.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Bockenheimer Warte
↳ Frankfurt's last surviving medieval watchtower, marking the former western city gate. The Gothic cylindrical tower with its battlemented crown stands at the busy Bockenheimer Warte square adjacent to the university quarter, a short walk north of the Senckenberg Museum.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~15 min
📍 Bockenheimer Warte · Bockenheim · 60325 Frankfurt am Main
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min
3. Palmengarten
↳ Frankfurt's historic botanic garden covers 22 hectares with specialist plant collections, a large rose garden, and a network of themed glasshouses. The Tropicarium spans desert, savanna, and cloud-forest climate zones. Seasonal outdoor plantings change across spring and summer.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Palmengarten Frankfurt · 4.6⭐ · palmengarten.de
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min

4. Alte Oper
↳ Frankfurt's neo-Renaissance opera house was destroyed in WWII and rebuilt as a concert and events hall, preserving the original facade and ceremonial foyer. The main auditorium seats 2,500. The forecourt, lined with a reflecting pool, is open 24/7 and a central Westend gathering point.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min

2. Jüdisches Museum Frankfurt
↳ Germany's oldest Jewish museum, housed in the Rothschild Palais and expanded with a purpose-built wing. Permanent exhibitions trace 900 years of Jewish life in Frankfurt, with dedicated coverage of the Rothschild banking family, the medieval Judengasse ghetto, and the Enlightenment community.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Eiserner Steg
↳ Frankfurt's iron pedestrian suspension bridge across the Main offers unobstructed views of the Cathedral, the Dom-Römer roofline to the east, and the financial district skyline to the north. The bridge connects the Altstadt embankment to the Museumsufer on the south bank.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Eiserner Steg · Altstadt · 60311 Frankfurt am Main
🚶 15 min · 🚕 6 min
4. Main Tower Observatory
↳ Frankfurt's only publicly accessible skyscraper observation deck, on the roof at the 56th floor of the 200-meter Main Tower. Panoramic 360-degree views cover the financial district, the Taunus hills to the north, and the Odenwald on clear days.

🍮 Local Tastes
Grüne Soße
↳ Frankfurt's signature dish: a cold herb sauce made from exactly seven fresh herbs — sorrel, chervil, borage, cress, parsley, chives, salad burnet — blended with egg and sour cream. Served over boiled potatoes or hard-boiled eggs.
📍 Schwarzer Stern · Römerberg 6
Handkäse mit Musik
↳ Sour milk cheese rounds marinated in oil, vinegar, caraway seeds, and onions. The "music" refers to the digestive consequence of the raw onion. A fixture of every Apfelwein tavern, served with bread and butter.
📍 Zum Gemalten Haus · Schweizer Straße 67
Apfelwein — Ebbelwoi
↳ Frankfurt's fermented apple cider, dry and tart, served in a ceramic jug called a Bembel and drunk from ribbed glasses called Gerippte. The Sachsenhausen neighborhood is the historic center of Apfelwein culture.
📍 Apfelwein taverns · Sachsenhausen
Frankfurter Würstchen
↳ Thin, lightly smoked pork sausages with Protected Designation of Origin status — legally named Frankfurter only when made in Frankfurt. Served in pairs, heated in hot water rather than grilled, with mustard and bread.
📍 Kleinmarkthalle · Hasengasse 5–7 · Innenstadt

✨ The Mainhattan Paradox

Frankfurt is the only major European city that was almost entirely destroyed in WWII and then built itself back twice — first into a glass-tower financial capital unlike anything on the continent, then into an obsessive reconstruction of the medieval Altstadt it had lost. The Dom-Römer project rebuilt 35 historic houses from historical records over a decade. Frankfurt now contains, within walking distance, the densest concentration of postwar skyscrapers in continental Europe and the most literally reconstructed medieval quarter of any German city.

The Museumsufer amplifies this. Fifteen world-class museums occupy a two-kilometer stretch of the south bank — sculpture, film, applied arts, architecture, ethnography, communications — built with municipal ambition starting in the late 20th century and now rivaling any museum mile in the world. On a single day you can walk from a Hellenistic bronze collection through 700 years of European painting into a restored early 20th-century cinema. Frankfurt rewards the traveler who ignores the airport and the trade fair and stays for the city underneath.
