Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Ferry Building Marketplace
1. Ferry Building Marketplace
↳ Beaux-Arts ferry terminal opened 1898, restored 2003 as a living food hall — nearly 50 artisan vendors, oyster bars, bread counters, and farmers-market stalls under the 245-foot clock tower. The city's iconic gateway from the Bay.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 1 Ferry Building
🚶 16 min · 🚕 6 min → Coit Tower
2. Coit Tower
↳ A 210-foot concrete column on the summit of Telegraph Hill, completed 1933 from a public bequest. Inside, sweeping Depression-era frescoes by 25 muralists circle the lobby; outside, the parapet gives a 360° panorama from Alcatraz to the Bay Bridge.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 1 Telegraph Hill Blvd
🚶 16 min · 🚕 7 min → Alcatraz Island
3. Alcatraz Island
↳ The rock in the middle of the Bay — military fortress, lighthouse, and federal penitentiary that held Al Capone. The cellhouse audio tour, narrated by former guards and inmates, walks D-Block, the dining hall, and the recreation yard.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Palace of Fine Arts
1. Palace of Fine Arts
↳ The lone survivor of the 1915 Panama–Pacific International Exposition — Bernard Maybeck's domed Roman rotunda behind a swan-filled lagoon. Recast in concrete in the 1960s, it remains the most photographed structure outside the Bay's bridges.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 3601 Lyon St
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Crissy Field
2. Crissy Field
↳ A former military airfield turned waterfront promenade — tidal marsh, dune grasses, and a flat shoreline path with the Golden Gate Bridge looming straight ahead. Locals run, picnic, and watch the kite-surfers.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 1199 East Beach
🚶 27 min · 🚕 8 min → Golden Gate Bridge Welcome Center
3. Golden Gate Bridge Welcome Center
↳ Stand under the south tower of the Art Deco icon — 4,200-foot main span, International Orange paint, opened 1937 and once the world's longest suspension bridge. The pavilion holds a 12-foot test tower and the walkway entrance.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Golden Gate Bridge Plaza
🚕 14 min → Lands End & Sutro Baths
4. Lands End & Sutro Baths
↳ The rocky westernmost edge of the city, where the Coastal Trail traces the cliffs. At its foot lie the concrete bones of the Sutro Baths — once the world's largest indoor saltwater swimming complex, opened 1896, burned 1966.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 680 Point Lobos Ave
🚕 22 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → San Francisco Chinatown
1. San Francisco Chinatown
↳ The oldest and largest Chinese enclave in North America, settled in the 1850s and rebuilt after 1906 with the swooping rooflines and lantern-lit alleys you see today. Wander the Dragon Gate, Portsmouth Square, fortune-cookie alleys, and herbal pharmacies on foot.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 Dragon Gate · Grant Ave at Bush St
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Cable Car Museum
2. Cable Car Museum
↳ A working powerhouse — the four giant sheaves that pull the cables under every cable-car line in the city spin in the middle of the floor. Inside the same Mason Street barn: vintage 1870s grip cars, ringing bells, and the easy explanation of why those wood-and-steel cars still climb the hills.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Thursday 10:00am - 4:00pm · Friday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 1201 Mason St
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Grace Cathedral
3. Grace Cathedral
↳ A neo-Gothic cathedral rising from the crown of Nob Hill — bronze Ghiberti door replicas, twin labyrinths in tile and stone, and a Keith Haring altarpiece make it one of SF's most quietly extraordinary interiors. The nave seats 1,200 under soaring ribbed vaulting.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 1100 California St · Nob Hill
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Lombard Street
4. Lombard Street
↳ The crookedest street in the world — eight switchbacks built in 1922 to tame a 27% grade down Russian Hill. Walk it from the top for the postcard view of bay, hydrangea beds, and red-brick paving; cars crawl down at five miles per hour beside you.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 1000 Lombard St
🚶 27 min · 🚕 8 min → hotel

1. California State Capitol
↳ California's working seat of government, fronted by a Corinthian portico and crowned by a copper-domed rotunda completed in 1874. Free guided tours run hourly — period offices restored to 1900, the working Assembly and Senate chambers, and the surrounding 40-acre Capitol Park arboretum.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 8:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Saturday & Sunday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 1315 10th St · Sacramento
🚶 19 min · 🚕 6 min → California State Railroad Museum
2. California State Railroad Museum
↳ 21 fully restored locomotives and rolling stock, from a chugging 1862 wood-burner to a 1937 Pullman sleeper you can walk through. The exhibits trace the Chinese rail workers who hammered the Transcontinental Railroad together — the most thorough rail museum on the West Coast.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ California State Railroad Museum
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 125 I St · Sacramento
🚶 4 min · 🚕 2 min → Old Sacramento Waterfront
3. Old Sacramento Waterfront
↳ 28 acres of preserved Gold Rush–era blocks along the Sacramento River — wooden boardwalks, gas lamps, and the brick storefronts of the original Pony Express terminus. The Eagle Theatre and B.F. Hastings Building sit on the same streets that supplied the 1849 miners.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 1002 2nd St · Sacramento

🍮 Local Tastes
San Francisco Sourdough
↳ Tangy, chewy loaves leavened by a wild Bay Area yeast — Lactobacillus sanfranciscensis. Bakers here have kept the same mother dough alive since the 1849 Gold Rush.
📍 Boudin Bakery · Tartine Manufactory · Acme Bread
Mission Burrito
↳ The oversized foil-wrapped burrito invented in the 1960s on 24th Street — rice, beans, choice of meat, salsa, cheese rolled into a flour tortilla. Long enough to be a meal, dense enough to need two hands.
📍 La Taqueria · El Farolito · Taqueria Cancún
Cioppino
↳ The Italian-American fisherman's stew born on the Wharf in the late 1800s — Dungeness crab, mussels, clams, shrimp, and white fish simmered in a tomato-wine broth. Crusty sourdough on the side to mop the bowl.
📍 Sotto Mare · Tadich Grill
Irish Coffee
↳ The American debut of Irish coffee — perfected at Buena Vista Café in 1952, where a barman cracked the trick of floating the cream on hot whiskey-coffee. Still made the same way at the same long bar, four glasses at a time.
📍 The Buena Vista Café
