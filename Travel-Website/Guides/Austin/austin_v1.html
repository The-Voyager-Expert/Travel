Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 27 min · 🚕 6 min → Texas State Capitol
1. Texas State Capitol
↳ Texas's 1888 capitol is built of distinctive pink granite and stands taller than the U.S. Capitol in Washington. Free self-guided and docent-led tours lead through the rotunda, the legislative chambers, and the soaring dome.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Saturday - Sunday 9:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
🆓 Free
📍 1100 Congress Ave · Downtown
🚶 14 min · 🚕 3 min → Bullock Texas State History Museum
2. Bullock Texas State History Museum
↳ The state's flagship history museum traces Texas from its Native and Spanish-colonial roots through statehood and the oil boom across three floors. The polished granite lobby floor and the 35-foot bronze star out front are landmarks in their own right, and an IMAX theater rounds out a visit.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Bullock Texas State History Museum · 4.6⭐ · thestoryoftexas.com
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 1800 N Congress Ave · Capitol Complex
🚶 23 min · 🚕 6 min → LBJ Presidential Library
3. LBJ Presidential Library
↳ On the University of Texas campus, the presidential library holds 45 million pages of Lyndon Johnson's papers and a faithful seven-eighths-scale replica of his Oval Office. Exhibits cover the Great Society, the civil-rights era, and the Vietnam War.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ LBJ Presidential Library · 4.7⭐ · lbjlibrary.org
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 2313 Red River St · University of Texas
🚕 10 min → Congress Avenue Bridge
4. Congress Avenue Bridge
↳ From spring through fall, up to 1.5 million Mexican free-tailed bats — the largest urban bat colony in North America — stream out from under this downtown bridge at dusk. Crowds gather on the banks and the deck to watch the nightly emergence over Lady Bird Lake.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
⚠️ Bats emerge near sunset, mid-March to early November
📍 100 S Congress Ave · Downtown

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 11 min → Barton Springs Pool
1. Barton Springs Pool
↳ A spring-fed, three-acre swimming hole inside Zilker Park, fed straight from the Edwards Aquifer. A beloved local ritual, it is lined by a grassy slope and shaded by pecan trees.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 5:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Closed Thursdays 9:00am - 7:00pm for cleaning
📍 2201 Barton Springs Rd · Zilker Park
🚶 13 min · 🚕 4 min → Zilker Botanical Garden
2. Zilker Botanical Garden
↳ Twenty-six acres of themed gardens — rose, Japanese, prehistoric, and cactus among them — terraced into a hillside above Barton Creek. The Hartman Prehistoric Garden recreates a dinosaur-era landscape around real local fossil finds.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Zilker Botanical Garden · 4.2⭐ · zilkergarden.org
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 2220 Barton Springs Rd · Zilker Park
🚶 7 min · 🚕 7 min → Lady Bird Lake
3. Lady Bird Lake
↳ A reservoir on the Colorado River ringed by the Ann and Roy Butler Hike-and-Bike Trail, whose lakeside boardwalk carries walkers and cyclists right out over the water with downtown skyline views. The calm, no-motor water is Austin's hub for kayaking and paddleboarding.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Lady Bird Lake · Downtown
🚕 12 min → Mount Bonnell
4. Mount Bonnell
↳ At about 775 feet, one of Austin's highest points and a lookout since the 1850s. A short stair-climb reaches a stone pavilion with sweeping views over Lake Austin, the Hill Country, and the 360 Bridge — best at sunset.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 5:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 3800 Mount Bonnell Rd · Northwest Hills
🚕 15 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Rainey Street Historic District
1. Rainey Street Historic District
↳ A two-block strip of early-twentieth-century Craftsman bungalows that were converted, one by one, into bars, restaurants, and coffee shops, creating Austin's most atmospheric outdoor drinking district. The compact street is flat, open-air, and best experienced in the late morning before crowds arrive.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rainey St · Downtown
🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Stevie Ray Vaughan Memorial
2. Stevie Ray Vaughan Memorial
↳ A life-size bronze statue of blues guitarist Stevie Ray Vaughan stands on Auditorium Shores at the south bank of Lady Bird Lake, facing downtown. Sculptor Ralph Helmick cast it in 1993 and it has become one of the most visited spots on the hike-and-bike trail.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 800 W Riverside Dr · Auditorium Shores
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → South Congress Avenue
3. South Congress Avenue
↳ The most celebrated mile in Austin, South Congress runs south from the Congress Avenue Bridge through a dense corridor of boutiques, vintage stores, food trucks, and neon-sign motels. The view north from mid-SoCo frames the State Capitol dome at the end of the straight avenue.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 S Congress Ave · SoCo
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Umlauf Sculpture Garden & Museum
4. Umlauf Sculpture Garden & Museum
↳ Over 130 bronze, marble, and cast-stone sculptures by UT professor Charles Umlauf fill a shaded outdoor garden and indoor gallery set into a wooded ravine at the edge of Zilker Park — one of the city's most peaceful stops, with works from small portrait busts to large figurative bronzes beneath live oaks.
📖 Wikipedia

❗️ Heads Up
❗️ Barton Springs Pool — Thursday Cleaning
↳ The spring-fed pool runs every day except Thursday, when it closes 9:00am - 7:00pm for cleaning — the only day with no daytime swim.
Workaround: Swim any day but Thursday; if Thursday is the only option, come after 7:00pm, when guarded swim runs to 10:00pm.
❗️ Congress Avenue Bridge — Bats Are Seasonal
↳ The 1.5-million-bat colony is only here mid-March to early November, and emerges near sunset — nothing to see in winter or by day.
Workaround: Arrive 20 - 30 minutes before sunset in season; warm, dry evenings bring the earliest, biggest flights.
❗️ Mount Bonnell — Car Break-ins at the Lot
↳ Austin police flag the Covert Park parking area as a hot spot for vehicle burglaries, especially at the busy sunset hour.
Workaround: Leave nothing visible in the car; take valuables with you on the short climb to the summit.
🦇 Why Austin Feels Like Austin

Two of this guide's stops are really the same story told in stone and water: the Capitol's pink granite was quarried at Granite Mountain near Marble Falls, the same Hill Country uplift whose limestone springs feed Barton Springs Pool. The city's grandest building and its favorite swimming hole come from one slab of geology.

The bats are an accident too. When the Congress Avenue Bridge was rebuilt in 1980, its new expansion joints happened to make ideal bat roosts — and a piece of infrastructure nobody designed for wildlife became the largest urban bat colony in North America, then one of the city's signature sights.
