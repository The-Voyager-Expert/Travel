Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 20 min · 🚕 10 min → Forbidden City
1. Forbidden City
↳ Imperial palace of 24 Ming and Qing emperors built in 1420 — 72 hectares, 980 buildings, nearly a million artefacts. The world's largest palace museum. The ceremonial axis from Tiananmen to the Imperial Garden is one of architecture's supreme processions. UNESCO World Heritage.

2. Jingshan Park
↳ The imperial garden directly north of the Forbidden City on Beijing's historic axis — the artificial hill was piled from earth excavated when digging the palace moat. Wanchun Pavilion offers the finest aerial view of the Forbidden City's sea of yellow-roofed courtyards.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Temple of Heaven
1. Temple of Heaven
↳ The 15th-century imperial complex where emperors prayed for good harvests. The triple-tiered Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests — built in 1420 without nails — is one of China's most iconic silhouettes. The surrounding park hosts morning tai chi, calligraphy, and opera sessions daily. UNESCO World Heritage.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~2.5 hrs
⚠️ Buildings open 8:00am - 5:30pm. Park + buildings need separate tickets at gate. Arrive early for morning park life.
📍 1 Tiantan East Rd · Dongcheng
🚕 20 min → Nanluoguxiang
2. Nanluoguxiang Hutong
↳ The most celebrated hutong in the city — an 800-metre Yuan-dynasty lane flanked by eight courtyard cross-lanes. Craft shops, breweries, cafes, and baijiu bars fill the alley. Wander Mao'er or Banchang Hutong for original courtyard gatehouses away from the main crowd.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~2 hrs
🆓 Free
📍 Nanluogu Xiang · Dongcheng

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 35 min → Summer Palace
1. Summer Palace
↳ The Qing imperial retreat — 2.9 sq km centred on Kunming Lake. The 728-metre Long Corridor whose ceilings depict 14,000 painted scenes is the longest painted gallery in the world. The Marble Boat, 17-Arch Bridge, and Garden of Harmonious Interest are among its landmarks. UNESCO World Heritage.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~4 hrs
⚠️ Buildings open 8:30am - 5:00pm. Park + buildings need separate tickets at gate. Nov–Mar: park opens 7:00am; buildings close 4:30pm.
📍 19 Xinjian Gongmen Rd · Haidian
🚕 35 min → hotel
Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h → Mutianyu Great Wall
1. Mutianyu Great Wall
↳ The finest restored section of the Great Wall accessible from Beijing — 2.25km of Ming-dynasty fortifications across forested ridgelines with 22 watchtowers. Built 1368, reinforced 1570s. Far less crowded than Badaling. Cable car ascent, toboggan slide descent.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~3.5 hrs
⚠️ Nov–Mar closes 5:00pm. Wall, cable car, and toboggan need separate tickets at gate. Book a guided group tour for transport — see Tours section.

🌆 Claude Inspiration

Beijing does not hide its weight. Every axis is deliberate — the 8km line from Yongding Gate through Tiananmen, through the Forbidden City's southern and northern gates, through Jingshan's pavilion, all the way to the Bell and Drum Towers. Stand at Wanchun Pavilion and you are standing on that axis, looking south at nine centuries of deliberate urban geometry. No city on earth has maintained an idea of itself with quite this degree of spatial insistence.

What is easy to miss, in all the grandeur, is how intimate the hutongs become. The city's entire imperial story plays out on a colossal scale — yet fifty metres off any of those grand axes, it becomes a village. Old men in doorways. Children on bicycles. A dumpling shop with two tables and a hand-painted sign. The hutong is what the palace was built to protect: ordinary life in courtyard houses arranged around the axis of a family rather than an emperor. Both Beijings are real, and you need both to understand the place.
