Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 9 min → Stoney-Baynard Plantation Ruins
1. Stoney-Baynard Plantation Ruins
↳ Tabby ruins of a late-18th-century plantation house built by Captain Stoney around 1793, standing amid live oaks and Spanish moss deep inside Sea Pines. The two surviving chimneys are among the oldest structures on Hilton Head. A non-resident gate fee applies to enter Sea Pines.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Sea Pines Forest Preserve
↳ A 605-acre maritime forest sheltering live oaks, freshwater lagoons, and the 4,000-year-old Indian Shell Ring — among the oldest monuments in the Southeast, built by Archaic peoples over centuries. The 3-mile trail passes lagoons where alligators and wood storks share the shallows. Sea Pines gate fee applies.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free

🚶 30 min · 🚕 8 min → Harbour Town Lighthouse
3. Harbour Town Lighthouse
↳ The octagonal red-and-white lighthouse rising 90 feet above Calibogue Sound at the southwestern tip of Sea Pines. The 114-step climb yields panoramic views to Daufuskie Island; the base holds maritime heritage exhibits. Admission charged at the door; Sea Pines gate fee also applies.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 7:30pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 149 Lighthouse Rd · Sea Pines
🚕 12 min → Coligny Beach Park
4. Coligny Beach Park
↳ The island's most popular public beach, a wide Atlantic strand where packed sand stretches in both directions. Lifeguarded in season, with restrooms, outdoor showers, and bike racks. Coligny Plaza boardwalk sits just behind the dunes with surf shops, rentals, and casual eateries.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 1 N Forest Beach Dr · Coligny Circle

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 22 min → Pinckney Island National Wildlife Refuge
1. Pinckney Island National Wildlife Refuge
↳ A 4,053-acre mosaic of salt marsh, tidal creek, and maritime forest just west of the causeway, managed by U.S. Fish and Wildlife. Trails from a 1-mile loop to a 4.5-mile circuit to Shell Point run through territory of egrets, roseate spoonbills, and alligators. No vehicles beyond the parking area.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~3 hr
🆓 Free
📍 US-278 · Pinckney Island
🚕 14 min → Coastal Discovery Museum at Honey Horn
2. Coastal Discovery Museum at Honey Horn
↳ A Smithsonian Affiliate on 70 acres of historic Honey Horn Plantation, where live oaks shade walking trails, a salt marsh boardwalk, and the island's oldest surviving plantation buildings. Exhibits cover Lowcountry ecology, indigenous history, and Gullah Geechee culture. Free; farmers market every Tuesday.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 28 min · 🚕 8 min → Fort Howell
3. Fort Howell
↳ Earthwork fortification built in 1864 by the 32nd United States Colored Troops to defend Mitchelville from Confederate raids. The surviving ditches and ramparts are the only remaining Civil War fortification on Hilton Head — built by the same formerly enslaved soldiers who garrisoned them.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 Beach City Rd at Dillon Rd · Mitchelville
🚶 10 min · 🚕 3 min → Mitchelville Historic Freedom Park
4. Mitchelville Historic Freedom Park
↳ The site of Mitchelville, established in 1862 as the first self-governed community of freed Black people in America — predating the Emancipation Proclamation. Freedpeople here created the first compulsory public school for Black children in the country. Interpretive panels and a memorial pavilion mark the ground.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 229 Beach City Rd · North Island
🚕 18 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 42 min → Hunting Island Lighthouse
1. Hunting Island Lighthouse
↳ South Carolina's only publicly accessible lighthouse, rising 136 feet in Hunting Island State Park 42 miles from Hilton Head. The 1875 cast-iron tower was moved inland as the shoreline shifted; 167 steps yield one of the coast's most dramatic panoramas. State park entry required.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~3 hr
📍 Hunting Island State Park · Hunting Island
🚕 48 min → Old Town Bluffton
2. Old Town Bluffton
↳ The historic core of Bluffton on a bluff above the May River, where the 1857 Church of the Cross anchors antebellum cottages converted into art studios, galleries, and farm-to-table restaurants. The waterfront Promenade overlooks the tidal river and the oyster beds that define the Lowcountry table.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Calhoun St · Old Town Bluffton
🚕 24 min → Fish Haul Creek Park
3. Fish Haul Creek Park
↳ A county park on the northern salt marsh fringe of Hilton Head, where a boardwalk trail leads through spartina grass to an overlook on Fish Haul Creek. One of the island's best birding spots for shorebirds, clapper rails, and wintering waterfowl — consistently uncrowded.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 325 Beach City Rd · North Island
🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Driessen Beach Park
4. Driessen Beach Park
↳ A broad public beach on Hilton Head's Atlantic coast, noticeably less crowded than Coligny, with a fishing pier, shaded picnic shelters, restrooms, and open ocean views. Late afternoon draws dolphins working the surf line.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 64 Bradley Beach Rd · North Island
🚕 16 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Lowcountry Boil
↳ Fresh shrimp, smoked sausage, corn, and new potatoes boiled in seasoned water — the Lowcountry communal dish, found at casual waterfront spots across the island.
📍 Skull Creek Boathouse · Hudson's Seafood House
She-Crab Soup
↳ Atlantic blue crab meat and roe simmered with cream and dry sherry — the definitive Lowcountry bisque. The roe gives it a briny depth no substitution can replicate.
📍 Alexander's Restaurant · Skull Creek Boathouse
Bluffton Oysters
↳ May River oysters from Bluffton's tidal flats — harvested fresh and roasted in the shell over an open fire. At their peak October through April when the water cools.
📍 Bluffton Oyster Company · Old Town Bluffton
Boiled Peanuts
↳ Raw peanuts boiled for hours in salted water — South Carolina's state snack, sold from roadside stands. Soft, briny, and nothing like the dry-roasted version.
📍 Coastal Discovery Museum Farmers Market — Tuesday · Coligny Farmers Market — Saturday

✨ Mitchelville

The most remarkable thing about Mitchelville isn't that it existed — it's when. In March 1862, nearly ten months before the Emancipation Proclamation, a community of formerly enslaved people on Hilton Head Island had already established a functioning town with elected governance and mandatory public education. The first compulsory public school for Black children in America opened here in 1862, in a place that had been a plantation the year before. Fort Howell stands 0.5 miles away on the same road — built by the soldiers who defended that society. Walking between them on Beach City Road connects two extraordinary chapters of the same story in about ten minutes.
