Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 15 min → Fort Jefferson
1. Fort Jefferson National Monument
↳ A star-shaped fortress built on Garden Key in the Dry Tortugas, 70 miles west of Key West. Built 1846–1876 to protect commerce in the Gulf of Mexico. The 16-inch guns were never fired in anger. Civil War prison later held Dr. Samuel Mudd, convicted of conspiracy in Lincoln's assassination. Accessible only by boat or seaplane; ferry service runs daily.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Dry Tortugas National Park — Ferry Day Trip · nps.gov
🏛️ Daily 8:00am departure
⏰ ~2 hr 15 min each way
⚠️ Reservations recommended · Bring sunscreen · Sea conditions vary
📍 Ferry departs from Key West waterfront
🚕 25 min → Ernest Hemingway Home
2. Ernest Hemingway Home & Museum
↳ Spanish Colonial mansion built in 1851, purchased by author Ernest Hemingway in 1931. He lived here with his second wife and wrote some of his major works. The property is famous for its population of six-toed cats, descendants of Hemingway's own cats. Tours include the house interior, study, and grounds with tropical gardens.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Ernest Hemingway Home & Museum · hemingwayhome.com
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Tickets at gate or online
📍 907 Whitehead St · Old Town
🚶 10 min → Duval Street
3. Duval Street
↳ The main commercial and entertainment street of Key West, running north-south through the Old Town. One mile long, lined with shops, galleries, bars, and restaurants. The street is both a shopping destination and cultural walkway — street performers, galleries, and historic storefronts dating to the 1800s. The Southernmost Point marker is at the south end of Duval Street.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Always open
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
⚠️ Parking available nearby · Pedestrian-friendly
📍 Duval St · Old Town
🚶 15 min → Mallory Square
4. Mallory Square Sunset
↳ An open waterfront plaza on the northwest side of Old Town Key West, hosting a nightly street festival celebrating the sunset. Street performers, artists, musicians, and vendors fill the square. The sun sinks into the Gulf of Mexico with clear visibility. The tradition began in the 1960s and has become Key West's signature evening gathering. Free to attend; donations welcome for performers.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily, 30 min before sunset
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
⚠️ Bring cash for buskers · Arrive early for good spot
📍 1 Mallory Sq · Waterfront
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 10 min → Key West Aquarium
1. Key West Aquarium
↳ The oldest aquarium in Florida, built in 1932. Houses over 200 species of marine life native to Florida Keys waters — sea turtles, stingrays, groupers, snappers, and Caribbean reef sharks. Open-air tanks and indoor exhibits. Touch pools allow visitors to interact with rays and starfish. Feeding demonstrations daily at 11:00am and 4:00pm.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Key West Aquarium · keywestaquarium.com
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Feeding times at 11am and 4pm
📍 1 Whitehead St · Waterfront
🚶 15 min → Fort Zachary Taylor State Park
2. Fort Zachary Taylor State Park
↳ The oldest masonry fort in Florida, built 1845–1866. During the Civil War, it was the only fort in the Confederate South never captured by Union forces. Built in a diamond shape to withstand cannon fire. The fort museum exhibits artifacts from the Civil War, shipwrecks, and the Keys' pirate history. Excellent snorkeling nearby with tropical fish.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Fort Zachary Taylor State Park · floridastateparks.org
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - sunset
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Entrance fee · Snorkeling gear available
📍 3560 South Roosevelt Blvd · South Beach
🚕 10 min → Smathers Beach
3. Smathers Beach
↳ Key West's best beach, a half-mile stretch of sandy shoreline on the south side. The widest and cleanest beach in Key West, popular with swimmers, families, and sunbathers. Calm, shallow waters protected by a reef. Amenities include restrooms, showers, and nearby shops. Free parking. The sand is white and fine — brought in from other locations and regularly maintained.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily sunrise to sunset
⏰ ~2 hr
🆓 Free
⚠️ Bring sunscreen · Parking available · Lifeguards on duty
📍 1801 South Roosevelt Blvd · South Beach
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 8 min → Key West Butterfly Conservatory
1. Key West Butterfly Conservatory
↳ An indoor botanical garden filled with thousands of live butterflies from around the world. The climate-controlled glass structure mimics tropical rainforest conditions. Over 60 species of butterflies from five continents. Visitors walk through the conservatory as butterflies land freely on guests. Gift shop with butterfly-themed items.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Key West Butterfly Conservatory · keywestbutterfly.com
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
⚠️ Tickets at entrance · Photography allowed
📍 1224 Duval St · Old Town
🚶 5 min → Key West Shipwreck Museum
2. Key West Shipwreck Museum
↳ Located in a replica 65-foot watchtower (the original tower stood in the 1800s). Exhibits salvaged artifacts from shipwrecks, coins, cannons, and personal items from the wrecks that lined the Keys. The museum tells the stories of wrecking as an industry — salvagers who profited from shipwrecks. Interactive exhibits and a working light at the top of the tower.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Key West Shipwreck Museum · shipwreckmuseum.com
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ Stairs to tower observation deck
📍 1 Whitehead St · Waterfront
🚕 15 min → Glass Bottom Boat Tour
3. Glass Bottom Boat Tour
↳ A boat tour with glass panels in the hull allowing views of the underwater reef ecosystem without getting wet. Tour departs Key West harbor and heads to coral reefs where fish, sea turtles, and coral formations are visible. Tours run 2–3 times daily. The boat stays in calm, shallow waters suitable for all ages. Narrated by crew members knowledgeable about marine life.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Key West Glass Bottom Boat Tours · keywestglassboattours.com
🏛️ Daily tours at 11:00am, 1:00pm, 3:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ Book online recommended · Seasickness possible in rough seas

🍮 LOCAL TASTES
Key Lime Pie — A dessert made with key lime juice, sweetened condensed milk, egg yolks, and a crust. Tart and creamy. Served everywhere in Key West — no trip is complete without trying it.
Conch Salad — Raw conch meat mixed with citrus juice, peppers, onions, and cilantro. A Bahamian-influenced dish popular throughout the Keys. Served as an appetizer at many seafood restaurants.
Stone Crab Claws — Seasonal (October–May). Steamed or boiled crab claws served with mustard sauce. Meat is sweet and tender. A luxury item with high price to match.
