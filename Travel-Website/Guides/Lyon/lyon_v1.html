Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 30 min · 🚕 10 min → Lugdunum
1. Lugdunum
↳ The only museum dedicated to Roman Lyon, built into the hillside below the ancient theatre. The capital of Gaul under Augustus, Lyon was birthplace of emperors Claudius and Caracalla. Covers 500 years through sculpture, mosaics, daily objects, and the world's largest collection of Gallo-Roman inscriptions.
📖 Wikipedia

🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 17 Rue Cléberg · Fourvière
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Fourvière Basilica
2. Fourvière Basilica
↳ The basilica defining Lyon's skyline — neo-Byzantine, built 1872–1896 as a votive offering after the Franco-Prussian War. The interior glitters with 5,000 sq metres of mosaics. The esplanade commands the finest panorama in the city: all of Lyon stretching south along the Saône and Rhône.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 8 Place de Fourvière · Fourvière
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Vieux Lyon Traboules
3. Vieux Lyon Traboules
↳ Secret passageways threading through Renaissance courtyards connecting parallel streets. Built by silk merchants to carry silk away from rain, later used by the French Resistance to move unseen. The largest Renaissance urban district north of the Alps — no other city has this scale of traboule network.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
⚠️ Most traboules are in private residential buildings — look for blue plaques marking public-access entries; ring the buzzer and push the door.
📍 Rue Saint-Jean · Old Town
🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Cathédrale Saint-Jean
4. Cathédrale Saint-Jean
↳ Lyon's Gothic cathedral, built 1180–1480. The facade holds 280 carved medallions; the interior features a 14th-century astronomical clock that still chimes with moving figures. Consecrated by Pope John XXII in 1316, it anchors the old quarter and marks the start of the main traboule routes.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 35 min · 🚕 10 min → Musée des Confluences
1. Musée des Confluences
↳ Lyon's most visited museum at the tip of the Presqu'île, where the Saône meets the Rhône. A spectacular deconstructivist building covering natural history, anthropology, cosmology, and civilisation across all continents — fossils, Egyptian mummies, contemporary ethnography. Worth visiting for the building alone.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Musée des Confluences · 4.5⭐ · museedesconfluences.fr
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:30am - 6:30pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 86 Quai Perrache · Confluence
🚕 15 min → Musée des Beaux-Arts
2. Musée des Beaux-Arts
↳ One of France's greatest art museums outside Paris, housed in a former Benedictine abbey on Place des Terreaux. Spans 5,000 years from ancient Egypt through Impressionism — Monet, Renoir, Gauguin, Matisse. The permanent collection fills 70 rooms; the serene cloister courtyard is one of Lyon's finest spaces.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Musée des Beaux-Arts · 4.6⭐ · mba-lyon.fr

🚶 25 min · 🚕 8 min → Maison des Canuts
3. Maison des Canuts
↳ The last working silk house in Lyon — guided tours demonstrate 19th-century Jacquard looms showing how Lyon's famous silk was produced. The 1831 canut uprising was one of the first workers' revolts in European history. The only place in the city where the full silk-weaving process can be watched live.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Place des Terreaux
1. Place des Terreaux
↳ Lyon's great civic square, dominated by the 17th-century Hôtel de Ville and the monumental Bartholdi fountain — four rearing horses pulling a chariot of France, cast in 1892. Beneath the square, 69 water jets are set flush in the paving, illuminated after dark as part of Daniel Buren's 1994 redesign.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Place des Terreaux · Presqu'île
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Place Bellecour
2. Place Bellecour
↳ The great open square at the heart of Lyon's Presqu'île — 312 m by 200 m, one of Europe's largest pedestrian plazas. At its centre stands an equestrian statue of Louis XIV, commissioned by the king himself in 1686.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Place Bellecour · Presqu'île

3. Musée Miniature et Cinéma
↳ Hyperrealistic miniature room sets — each built to extraordinary detail by a single artist — alongside original film props from Alien, Indiana Jones, and Jurassic Park, housed in a 16th-century Renaissance mansion in Vieux Lyon.

4. Institut Lumière
↳ The Lumière family villa where cinema was born on 28 December 1895. The Belle Époque house and gardens are preserved as a museum covering the Cinématographe, early colour photography, and the Lumières' scientific legacy. The original hangar still stands.

↳ Lyon's great covered food market on Cours Lafayette, named after Paul Bocuse. Over 50 specialist vendors — charcutiers, fromageries, boucheries, pâtisseries, traiteurs — fill the 19th-century iron-and-glass hall at the centre of Lyon's gastronomic identity.

🚶 25 min · 🚕 8 min → Amphithéâtre des Trois Gaules
2. Amphithéâtre des Trois Gaules
↳ Built in 19 CE, this is the oldest Roman amphitheater in Gaul outside Italy — constructed to host the annual assembly of the three Gallic provinces at the confluence of the Saône and Rhône. The partially excavated ruin sits in the Croix-Rousse hillside, its curved seating banks still visible against the slope.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rue Lucien Sportisse · Croix-Rousse
🚶 30 min · 🚕 10 min → Parc de la Tête d'Or
3. Parc de la Tête d'Or
↳ The largest urban park in France at 117 hectares, laid out in 1856 by the Bühler brothers along the Rhône in the 6th arrondissement. The park contains a free zoological garden, a large lake with rowing boats, rose gardens with over 16,000 plants, and botanical greenhouses — Lyon's defining open-air space.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 11:00pm
⏰ ~2 hrs
⚠️ Oct–Mar closes 9:00pm
📍 Place du Général Leclerc · 6th arr
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Musée d'Art Contemporain de Lyon
4. Musée d'Art Contemporain de Lyon
↳ Renzo Piano's Cité Internationale complex on the Rhône quay, directly at Parc de la Tête d'Or. The MAC holds no permanent collection — the entire building reorganises around each temporary exhibition, making every visit a different architectural experience.

🍮 Local Tastes
Quenelle de brochet
↳ Lyon's signature dish — a light, airy pike-fish dumpling with Nantua sauce. Between soufflé and mousse; no other city eats these. Every bouchon serves them; Daniel et Denise makes the most celebrated version.
📍 Daniel et Denise · Café des Fédérations · La Mère Jean
Rosette de Lyon
↳ Lyon's icon of charcuterie — a large dry-cured pork sausage in a natural pork bladder casing, aged for months until dense and deeply flavoured. Served sliced thin on charcuterie boards in every bouchon.
📍 Les Halles Paul Bocuse · any bouchon
Cervelle de canut
↳ The silkworkers' fresh cheese — fromage blanc beaten with cream, shallots, garlic, chives, and herbs. The name was a mock-insult aimed at the canuts; the dish outlasted it and appears on every bouchon menu as starter or dessert.
📍 Bouchon Les Lyonnais · Café des Fédérations
Tarte aux pralines roses
↳ A vivid pink tart filled with molten caramel of crushed sugar-coated almonds — the colour is entirely natural from the almond coating. Sweet, crunchy, and unique to Lyon. Found in every boulangerie.
📍 any boulangerie · Les Halles Paul Bocuse
Bugne lyonnaise
↳ Deep-fried strips of sweet enriched dough dusted with powdered sugar — Lyon's version of a fried dough pastry, traditionally eaten at Mardi Gras but available year-round. Crisp at the edges, chewy at the centre.
📍 any boulangerie

Grenoble · 1 hr 30 min
↳ The capital of the French Alps — surrounded on three sides by mountain ranges, with a cable car ascending to the 18th-century Bastille fortress above the city. The Musée de Grenoble is one of the finest modern art museums in France.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
Beaune · 1 hr 40 min
↳ The wine capital of Burgundy — a compact medieval walled city built around the Hôtel-Dieu, surrounded by the greatest Pinot Noir vineyards in the world. The Marché aux Vins lets you taste across multiple appellations in one building.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
Geneva · Switzerland · 1 hr 55 min
↳ Lakeside Swiss city at the foot of the Alps — the jet d'eau fountain on Lake Geneva, the Palais des Nations, old town with Saint-Pierre Cathedral, and the Carouge quarter. Direct TGV with frequent departures via Lyon Part-Dieu station.

Annecy · 2 hrs
↳ A medieval lakeside city ringed by the Alps — the lake water is the clearest in Europe, the old town is built along canals, and the Château d'Annecy dates to the 11th century. Best of the Alpine day trips.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio
Dijon · 2 hrs
↳ The capital of Burgundy and of mustard — a preserved medieval city with the Palais des Ducs de Bourgogne, a covered market full of regional cheeses and wines, and the starting point for the Route des Grands Crus wine road.
🎫 book at: SNCF Connect or Omio

✨ A Note on Lyon

Lyon rewards those who resist the obvious. The city's greatest pleasures are hidden inside it — through the blue plaque, behind the buzzer, down the corridor that connects rue Saint-Jean to nowhere in particular. The traboules were built to keep silk dry; they ended up keeping an entire city's soul intact.

Plan for two good meals a day, not one spectacular one. The bouchon at lunch — carafe of Beaujolais, quenelle in Nantua sauce, praline tart — is an institution that no amount of fine dining can replace. Save the starred restaurants for dinner and let the afternoon belong to the museum you weren't expecting to love.
