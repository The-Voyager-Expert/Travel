Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Grossmünster
1.Grossmünster
↳ Zurich's defining landmark — a Romanesque twin-towered cathedral that anchors the east bank of the Limmat and gave birth to the Swiss Reformation under Ulrich Zwingli. Climb the Karlsturm for one of the best 360° views over the old city, the river, and on clear days the Alps.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Grossmünsterplatz · Altstadt
🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Fraumünster
2.Fraumünster
↳ A 9th-century convent church transformed into one of Europe's most extraordinary stained-glass interiors. Marc Chagall designed five towering chancel windows — each 10 metres high, each a different dominant color, depicting a Biblical narrative in luminous jewel tones.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Fraumünster entry · 4.7⭐ · fraumuenster.ch
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 4:00pm

📍 Münsterhof 2 · Altstadt
🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Lindenhof
3.Lindenhof
↳ A hilltop square with nearly 2,000 years of history — a Roman fort stood here in 15 BC, a Carolingian imperial palace followed in the 9th century. Today a tranquil park where locals play chess under linden trees, with a wide view over the Limmat and the Grossmünster.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Lindenhof · Altstadt
🚶 6 min · 🚕 4 min → Niederdorf
4.Niederdorf
↳ The medieval quarter north of the Limmat — pedestrianized alleys, guild houses, and open-front cafés that have been the social heart of Zurich's Old Town for centuries. Walk Niederdorfstrasse in the late afternoon when the terraces fill up with wine bars, bakeries, and neighbourhood bars.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Niederdorfstrasse · Altstadt
🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 7 min · 🚕 3 min → Swiss National Museum
1.Swiss National Museum
↳ The largest historical museum in Switzerland occupies a neo-Gothic castle directly beside the main train station. Six permanent exhibitions trace Swiss history from the Stone Age to the present — arms collections, medieval artifacts, decorative arts, and the full sweep of Swiss cultural identity.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Swiss National Museum admission · 4.6⭐ · landesmuseum.ch
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm · Thursday 10:00am - 7:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2 hrs
📍 Museumstrasse 2 · Altstadt
🚶 18 min · 🚕 7 min → ETH Poly Terrasse
2.ETH Poly Terrasse
↳ The stone terrace of ETH Zürich's main building sits on a wooded hill above the Kunsthaus and delivers Zurich's finest free panorama — the full span of the Old Town below, Lake Zurich stretching south, and the Alps as a white backdrop on clear days.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Rämistrasse 101 · Hochschulen
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Kunsthaus Zürich
3.Kunsthaus Zürich
↳ Switzerland's largest art museum and one of the finest in Central Europe — Monet, Van Gogh, Picasso, Giacometti — the largest collection anywhere, and a major modern wing that doubled the gallery space. Budget at least two hours; plan around the Giacometti Foundation rooms, which are without equal.
📖 Wikipedia

1.ZSG Lake Zurich Cruise
↳ The Zürichsee-Schifffahrtsgesellschaft has operated steamships and motor vessels on Lake Zurich. The long round-trip to Rapperswil passes vineyard slopes, orchard villages, and opens wide Alpine views on clear days.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~1 hr 30 min

🚶 18 min · 🚕 7 min → Chinese Garden of Zurich
2.Chinese Garden of Zurich
↳ A gift from Zürich's partner city Kunming, this classical Chinese garden on the Zürichhorn lakefront is among the finest of its kind in Europe. Dragon-backed walls, lacquered pavilions, a koi pond, and a tea house sit within two enclosed acres — a complete tonal contrast to the Swiss city outside its gates.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Lindt Home of Chocolate
1.Lindt Home of Chocolate
↳ A genuinely immersive chocolate museum anchored by the world's largest Lindt chocolate fountain — a nine-metre flowing tower of couverture that greets you at the door. The museum traces 175 years of Swiss chocolate-making with interactive exhibits, tasting stations, and the largest Lindt shop on the planet.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 25 min · 🚕 12 min → Museum Rietberg
2.Museum Rietberg
↳ One of Europe's finest non-European art museums, housed in the 19th-century Villa Wesendonck — where Wagner composed Tristan und Isolde — in Rieterpark. Collections span Asia, Africa, the Americas, and Oceania: Indian Chola bronzes, Chinese jade, Japanese Nō masks, and Yoruba bronzes.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min → Zürich HB
1.Uetliberg
↳ Zurich's home mountain at 870 metres — reachable in 25 minutes on the S10 from Hauptbahnhof. A viewing tower at the summit delivers a panorama spanning the full length of Lake Zurich, the city grid below, and on clear mornings the entire Alpine chain from Säntis to the Bernese Oberland.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Uetliberg Summit · Uetliberg
🚕 35 min → Felsenegg
2.Felsenegg
↳ The endpoint of the Planet Trail — a 7.5 km ridge walk from Uetliberg that passes scale models of the solar system in the forest path. Felsenegg at 805 metres has its own panoramic restaurant terrace and is the top station of the Luftseilbahn Adliswil–Felsenegg cable car, which drops to Adliswil in five minutes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Felsenegg · Adliswil

↳ The most famous address in Swiss confectionery — founded at Paradeplatz, still in the same family, still making Luxemburgerli fresh each morning. The upstairs café is a Zurich institution: marble tables, pastry trolleys, and a ringside view over the city's financial heart.

↳ The University of Zurich's botanical garden on Zollikerstrasse holds more than 9,000 plant species across outdoor beds and three large climate-controlled glasshouses — tropical rainforest, succulent desert, and Victorian fern house.

↳ The most powerful waterfall in Europe — 150 metres wide, 23 metres high, with up to 600 cubic metres of water per second in summer. Walk the full network of viewing platforms on both banks; take the short boat ride to the central rock for a view straight up into the cascade.

2.Stein am Rhein
↳ One of Switzerland's best-preserved medieval towns — the Rathausplatz is ringed by half-timbered houses whose entire façades are covered in painted murals depicting guild histories, biblical scenes, and heraldic ornaments. The cobblestoned square is unchanged since the 16th century.
📖 Wikipedia

1.Chapel Bridge
↳ The Kapellbrücke is the most photographed bridge in Switzerland and the oldest surviving wooden covered bridge in Europe — still spanning the Reuss, decorated with 17th-century painted panels depicting Lucerne's history. Walk its full 204-metre length and look down into the clear green river.
📖 Wikipedia

2.Lion Monument
↳ Carved directly from the sandstone cliff to a design by Bertel Thorvaldsen, the Löwendenkmal commemorates the 786 Swiss Guards who died defending the Tuileries. Mark Twain called it "the most mournful and moving piece of stone in the world."
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min

1.Old City of Berne
↳ The UNESCO-listed medieval capital is defined by six kilometres of arcaded sandstone colonnades — the Lauben — that keep the city walkable in any weather. The Zytglogge clock tower, the Münster cathedral, the Bundeshaus, and the painted fountains of the Gerechtigkeitsgasse all fall within a short walk.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

📍 Zytglogge · Altstadt Bern

2.Bear Park
↳ Berne takes its name from the bear and has kept live bears since the Middle Ages. The current Bärenpark — open below the Nydeggbrücke — gives four brown bears a forested hillside with a natural stream and an Aare bathing pool.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Grosser Muristalden 6 · Marzili

3.Rose Garden
↳ The Rosengarten sits on the ridge above the Old Town's Aare loop, and the panorama from its terrace is the classic Berne postcard — medieval rooftops and the Münster spire curving inside a broad river bend, the Bernese Alps beyond.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

1.Interlaken
↳ The town between two lakes — Thun and Brienz — with an unobstructed view of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau that stops you cold when you first see it. Walk the Höheweg promenade and find the framed three-peak view from the Kursaal gardens.

2.Grindelwald First
↳ The gondola from Grindelwald village to First is a 25-minute ascent through increasingly vertiginous terrain, finishing with a direct facing view of the Eiger North Face. At the top: the First Cliff Walk, the First Flyer zipline, and easy trails to Bachalpsee lake with its mirror-image reflection of the Schreckhorn.

🏔️ On Zürich

Zürich confounds first expectations. You arrive primed for a stiff, expensive banking city and instead find a place that has spent decades turning itself into one of the most liveable, most beautiful small cities in Europe. The lake is cleaner than a swimming pool. The trams run to the second. The old town is genuinely medieval. And then you turn a corner into a Chagall interior, or ride a century-old funicular up to a university terrace with the Alps spread out in front of you, and the city reveals itself properly.

The best version of Zürich is slow Zürich — the version you get when you stop trying to tick it off. Sit at a Niederdorf terrace at 6pm when the after-work crowd fills the square. Take the S10 on a weekday morning when the mist is still on the mountain and you have the Uetliberg tower almost to yourself. Find the table by the window at Zeughauskeller on a Tuesday night. The city rewards those who stay long enough to let it breathe.
