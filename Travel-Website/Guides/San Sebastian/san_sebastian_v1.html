🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Basílica de Santa María del Coro
1.Basílica de Santa María del Coro
↳ San Sebastián's principal Baroque church, built between 1743 and 1774. The Churrigueresque façade on Calle Mayor rises above the Old Quarter rooftops; inside, gilded altarpieces converge on the venerated Virgin of the Chorus — the city's patron — at the heart of a richly decorated nave.

🆓 Free

🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Monte Urgull
2.Monte Urgull
↳ A forested hill rising from the Old Town harbour, crowned by the medieval Castillo de la Mota and a monumental Sacred Heart statue above the city. Walking its rampart paths reveals a sweeping panorama across La Concha Bay, Isla de Santa Clara, and the Basque coastline toward France.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ May–September hours extended; October–April closes at 7:30pm.
📍 Paseo Nuevo · Parte Vieja
🚶 40 min · 🚕 10 min → Peine del Viento
3.Peine del Viento
↳ Eduardo Chillida's 1977 masterwork — "Comb of the Wind." Three curved iron claws are anchored into granite cliffs at the base of Monte Igueldo, reaching into Atlantic spray. Stone vents beneath the terrace channel the sea air, producing a theatrical whistle when waves crash through in heavy weather.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Eduardo Chillida Pasealekua · Ondarreta
🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Monte Igueldo
4.Monte Igueldo
↳ A 1912 funicular climbs to the summit of Monte Igueldo, where a circular panorama takes in the full arc of La Concha Bay, Isla de Santa Clara, Monte Urgull, and the Atlantic horizon. The vintage amusement park at the top dates from the same era as the funicular itself.

1.San Telmo Museoa
↳ The oldest museum in the Basque Country, housed in a 16th-century Dominican convent converted in the 1930s. Elías Salaverría's panoramic frescoes wrap the old church nave; the adjacent contemporary wing holds Basque history collections spanning prehistoric artefacts to the present.

2.Beach of La Concha
↳ A sheltered semicircular bay curving between Monte Urgull and Monte Igueldo — considered one of the finest urban beaches in Europe. The 19th-century Paseo de la Concha promenade lines the shore; Isla de Santa Clara floats at the centre of the bay.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Paseo de la Concha · La Concha
🚶 30 min · 🚕 8 min → Miramar Palace
3.Miramar Palace
↳ The summer royal residence built for Queen María Cristina in 1893, set on a promontory between La Concha and Ondarreta beaches. The English neo-Tudor manor now belongs to the city; its surrounding park commands a panorama across both bays from the highest point on the promenade.
📖 Wikipedia

4.Kursaal Auditorium
↳ Rafael Moneo's 1999 landmark at the Zurriola rivermouth — two luminous glass cubes rising from the Urumea estuary. The building houses a concert hall, auditorium, and convention centre; at night the translucent façade glows above Zurriola beach.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 15 min · 🚕 6 min → Zurriola Beach
1. Zurriola Beach
↳ The Gros neighbourhood's Atlantic-facing beach, separated from La Concha by the Kursaal headland and exposed to open ocean swells. Where La Concha is sheltered and promenade-lined, Zurriola faces north-west — the waves break harder and the crowd skews younger. The Kursaal glass cubes anchor the rivermouth end.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Zurriola Hiribidea · Gros
🚶 18 min · 🚕 7 min → Aquarium of San Sebastián
2. Aquarium of San Sebastián
↳ Housed in the 1928 Palacio del Mar at the old harbour, the aquarium traces the natural history of the Cantabrian Sea and the Basque fishing tradition. A 360-degree tunnel passes through a tank holding sharks, rays and large Atlantic species; the oceanographic collection covers ship models and marine charts.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Jardines de Alderdi Eder
3. Jardines de Alderdi Eder
↳ The formal garden terrace between the old town and the La Concha promenade, planted with magnolias and centred on the city's landmark street clock. The gardens front the neoclassical city hall — built in 1887 as a casino — marking the hinge between the Parte Vieja alleyways and the open bay.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Alderdi Eder · La Concha
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Santa Catalina Bridge
4. Santa Catalina Bridge
↳ The most elaborate of the Urumea's three bridges, built in 1905 in Spanish Renaissance style with iron lamp standards and ceramic balustrades. It links the Parte Vieja to Amara; from mid-span, the river framing of the old town roofline and the Kursaal is one of the city's definitive views.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Paseo de Francia · Gros

🍮 Local Tastes
Pintxos
↳ The Basque answer to tapas — small bites anchored to bread with a toothpick or served hot from the plancha. Stand at the bar, order two or three per stop, and move between bars with a glass of txakoli.
📍 Borda Berri · La Cuchara de San Telmo · Bar Nestor
Gilda
↳ San Sebastián's emblematic pintxo — a skewer of anchovy, green olive, and guindilla pepper in olive oil. Named after Rita Hayworth's character in the 1946 film, it was invented at Bar Casa Vallés.
📍 Bar Casa Vallés · Atari Gastroteka
Basque Cheesecake
↳ The burnished, crustless cheesecake invented at La Viña in 1990 — now copied worldwide but still best at the source. Creamy inside with a deliberately dark, almost caramelised top.
📍 La Viña
Txakoli
↳ The region's slightly sparkling, bone-dry white wine — poured from height to aerate it, fizzing into the glass. Brisk acidity makes it the natural pintxos partner.
📍 Parte Vieja · Old Town

✨ A Note on San Sebastián

San Sebastián is one of the highest concentrations of Michelin stars per capita anywhere on Earth — a small city that outsized its geography by deciding that food was taken as seriously as art. The Old Town operates as a kind of living laboratory where every bar is in permanent competition with its neighbour.

Walk the pintxos circuit at 8pm on any weekday and you'll find locals who eat this way not because it's traditional, but because it's genuinely the best way to eat. The Peine del Viento is the right place to understand why Chillida kept returning to the Basque coast his whole life — those iron claws are not a monument to the landscape, they're a conversation with it.
