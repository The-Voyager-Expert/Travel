Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 28 min · 🚕 10 min → Notre-Dame Basilica
1. Notre-Dame Basilica
↳ Gothic Revival masterpiece built by architect James O'Donnell. The interior — hand-carved wood, gilded altarpiece, and deep blue polychrome vaulting — is among the most celebrated in North America. The AURA evening light show illuminates the main altar.
📖 Wikipedia

⚠️ Timed-entry tickets required — buy online before arrival.
📍 110 rue Notre-Dame Ouest · Old Montreal
🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Pointe-à-Callière Museum
2. Pointe-à-Callière Museum
↳ Montreal's archaeology and history museum, built directly over the city's founding point. Glass floors reveal 18th-century sewers, cemetery foundations, and the original confluence of the St. Lawrence and the Little St. Pierre rivers — the only place in North America where layers of settlement are visible this way.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Friday 10:00am - 5:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 11:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 350 Place Royale · Old Montreal
🚶 6 min · 🚕 3 min → Notre-Dame-de-Bon-Secours Chapel
3. Notre-Dame-de-Bon-Secours Chapel
↳ One of Montreal's oldest churches, the stone "Sailors' Chapel" just north of the old market, hung with model ships left by mariners giving thanks for safe passage. The Marguerite Bourgeoys Museum adjoins it, and the spire climb opens a sweeping view over the Old Port and the river.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~45 min
📍 400 rue Saint-Paul Est · Old Montreal
🚶 7 min · 🚕 3 min → Grande Roue de Montréal
4. Grande Roue de Montréal
↳ Canada's largest observation wheel at 60 metres, located on the Old Port waterfront. 42 climate-controlled glass cabins, each rotation lasting 18 to 20 minutes. Views sweep across Old Montreal, the St. Lawrence River, Mount Royal, and the downtown skyline — particularly striking at dusk.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 11:00am - 11:00pm
⏰ ~30 min

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → McGill University
1. McGill University
↳ Founded on the McGill farm, the campus occupies a wide greensward at the foot of Mount Royal. The Roddick Gates on rue Sherbrooke frame a view up the central walk to the neoclassical Arts Building, with the mountain rising directly behind. Set within the Golden Square Mile historic district.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 845 rue Sherbrooke Ouest · Downtown

2. Montreal Museum of Fine Arts
↳ Canada's most-visited art museum — 44,000 works across five interconnected pavilions spanning both sides of rue Sherbrooke Ouest. Collections cover Quebec contemporary art, European Impressionism, African art, and Inuit carvings. Permanent collections free; temporary exhibitions ticketed.

3. Place des Arts
↳ Montreal's largest performing arts complex — 6 concert halls anchoring the Quartier des Spectacles. The outdoor esplanade becomes the main stage of the Festival International de Jazz de Montréal in late June through early July, with dozens of free outdoor performances drawing millions of attendees.

4. Chinatown
↳ One of the oldest Chinatowns in North America, established by railroad workers in the 19th century. Ceremonial gates on rue de la Gauchetière mark the pedestrian zone of dim sum restaurants, herbal shops, bubble tea cafés, and Asian grocery stalls. The main street is pedestrianized.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Biodôme de Montréal
1. Biodôme de Montréal
↳ Converted from the Olympic cycling velodrome, the Biodôme replicates five complete ecosystems under a single roof: Tropical Rainforest, Laurentian Maple Forest, Gulf of St. Lawrence, Labrador Coast, and Sub-Antarctic Islands — 4,500 animals and 750 plant species across 6,500 square metres.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 4777 avenue Pierre-De Coubertin · Hochelaga-Maisonneuve
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Montreal Tower Observatory
2. Montreal Tower Observatory
↳ The world's tallest inclined tower at 175 metres, angled at 45 degrees. An exterior funicular climbs the tower's inclined spine to an open observatory platform. On clear days views extend 80 km, covering the entire island, the St. Lawrence, and the Laurentian Mountains to the north.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 4141 avenue Pierre-De Coubertin · Hochelaga-Maisonneuve
🚶 12 min · 🚕 4 min → Montreal Botanical Garden
3. Montreal Botanical Garden
↳ The third-largest botanical garden in the world — 22,000 plant species across 75 hectares and 10 exhibition greenhouses. The Chinese Garden is the largest authentic Ming-dynasty garden outside China; the Japanese Garden with its traditional pavilion and tea garden is equally exceptional.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 4101 rue Sherbrooke Est · Hochelaga-Maisonneuve
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Insectarium
4. Insectarium
↳ The largest insect museum in North America — 160,000 specimens and 250 live species. A recent renovation introduced immersive experiences: an underground burrowing simulation, a tropical butterfly greenhouse, and a room where visitors handle live insects.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 6 min → Parc du Mont-Royal
1. Parc du Mont-Royal
↳ Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted — the landscape architect behind Central Park. The 190-metre summit lookout delivers a full panorama of Montreal, the St. Lawrence, and on clear days the Adirondacks. The Tam-Tams drumming circle gathers at the George-Étienne Cartier monument every Sunday afternoon in summer.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Chemin Olmsted · Mont-Royal
🚕 12 min → Saint Joseph's Oratory
2. Saint Joseph's Oratory
↳ The largest church in Canada by volume, capping the north slope of Mount Royal. Built around Brother André's original chapel, the copper-domed basilica was enlarged six times. Brother André, now canonized, is buried here; his reliquary draws two million pilgrims annually. The Crypt Museum traces the full history.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1h
🆓 Free
📍 3800 chemin Queen Mary · Côte-des-Neiges
🚕 18 min → Parc La Fontaine
3. Parc La Fontaine
↳ Montreal's best-loved neighborhood park — 36 hectares in the Plateau Mont-Royal with two lakes, summer pedal boats, and the Théâtre de Verdure open-air theater hosting free concerts from June through August. Sunday afternoons gather impromptu crowds with food vendors and picnics.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Avenue Du Parc-La Fontaine · Plateau Mont-Royal
🚶 25 min · 🚕 10 min → Church of the Madonna della Difesa
4. Church of the Madonna della Difesa
↳ The spiritual heart of Little Italy, raised by immigrants from Molise and decorated by Guido Nincheri, the "Michelangelo of Montreal." A Romanesque cupola crowns the Greek-cross nave, and a famous fresco of an equestrian Mussolini — painted before the war — looks down from behind the altar.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~30 min

🍮 Local Tastes
Poutine
↳ French fries topped with fresh cheese curds and hot brown gravy — the curds must squeak when bitten. Born in rural Quebec, now omnipresent from diners to fine-dining riffs. The original: just fries, curds, and sauce.
📍 La Banquise · Plateau Mont-Royal
Montreal Smoked Meat
↳ Brisket dry-cured in a black pepper and coriander rub, then smoked and hand-sliced thin to medium on rye bread with yellow mustard. Distinct from New York pastrami in brine, cure time, and cut. Schwartz's on Saint-Laurent is the original.
📍 Schwartz's Hebrew Delicatessen · The Main
Montreal-Style Bagel
↳ Hand-rolled, boiled in honey water, and baked in a wood-fired oven — smaller, denser, and sweeter than a New York bagel, with a larger hole. Sesame and poppy are the two canonical types. St-Viateur and Fairmount bake them around the clock.
📍 St-Viateur Bagel · Mile End
Tourtière
↳ Quebec meat pie — ground pork, veal, and beef spiced with cloves, cinnamon, and allspice in a double-crust pastry. A Christmas Eve staple across Quebec for generations. In Montreal it appears year-round on bistro menus.
📍 Jean-Talon Market · Little Italy
Maple Products
↳ Quebec produces 72% of the world's maple syrup — from pure syrup in graduated amber grades to maple butter and tire d'érable pulled over ice. Jean-Talon Market has the best selection of small-producer maple goods.
📍 Jean-Talon Market · Little Italy
