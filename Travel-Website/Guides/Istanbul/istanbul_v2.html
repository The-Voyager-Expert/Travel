Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Hagia Sophia
1. Hagia Sophia
↳ Byzantine architecture's supreme achievement — a domed basilica that served as the world's largest cathedral for nearly a thousand years. The dome's 40 windows make it appear to float. 6th-century gold mosaics and Ottoman calligraphy coexist on the same walls.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 7:30pm
⏰ ~2h
⚠️ Closed to non-Muslim visitors Friday 12:00pm – 2:00pm for prayers
📍 Sultanahmet Square · Sultanahmet
🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Topkapi Palace
2. Topkapi Palace
↳ The nerve centre of the Ottoman Empire — four courtyards, the Harem, the Treasury's Topkapi dagger and 86-carat Spoonmaker's Diamond, and the Sacred Relics chamber with the Prophet Muhammad's mantle. The Fourth Courtyard terrace overlooks the Bosphorus, the Sea of Marmara, and the Golden Horn simultaneously.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Topkapi Palace · 4.7⭐ · muze.gov.tr
🏛️ Wednesday - Monday 9:00am - 6:30pm
🚫 Closed Tuesday
⏰ ~3h
📍 Babıhümayun Cad. · Cankurtaran
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Basilica Cistern
3. Basilica Cistern
↳ An underground Byzantine reservoir — a vast flooded forest of 336 marble columns, their reflections stretching across still water in amber torchlight. Two upside-down Medusa heads serve as column bases in the northwest corner; nobody knows why, and nobody has agreed in a millennium and a half.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Basilica Cistern · 4.7⭐ · yerebatan.com
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 Yerebatan Cd. 1/3 · Sultanahmet
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Blue Mosque
4. Blue Mosque
↳ Sultan Ahmed I's 17th-century imperial mosque — the only one in Istanbul with six minarets and faced entirely in hand-painted Iznik tiles, over 20,000 in cobalt blue. Enter through the courtyard, remove shoes, and walk the central nave under cascading half-domes and 260 windows.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 6:45pm
⏰ ~45min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Closed briefly during prayer times; check daily schedule

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Istanbul Archaeological Museums
1. Istanbul Archaeological Museums
↳ Three interconnected museums on the Topkapi grounds — centred on the Alexander Sarcophagus, whose battle reliefs are the finest Greek marble carving in the world. The Tiled Kiosk is the oldest non-religious Ottoman building; the Ancient Orient wing holds Babylonian gates and the Treaty of Kadesh.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Istanbul Archaeological Museums · 4.6⭐ · muze.gov.tr
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 Osman Hamdi Bey Yokuşu Sk · Gülhane
🚶 22 min · 🚕 10 min → Süleymaniye Mosque
2. Süleymaniye Mosque
↳ Mimar Sinan's masterpiece — the mosque crowns the Third Hill with a dome nearly as wide as Hagia Sophia's. Sinan calibrated 138 windows so the space reads as pure geometry rather than mass. The külliye gardens behind hold Sinan's own tomb, deliberately small.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~45min
🆓 Free
📍 Prof. Sıddık Sami Onar Cd. No:1 · Fatih
🚶 28 min · 🚕 14 min → Galata Tower
3. Galata Tower
↳ A 14th-century Genoese stone tower rising 67 metres above Beyoğlu — the gallery at the top gives a 360° view over the Golden Horn, the Historic Peninsula, the Bosphorus, and the Asian shore. Legend holds that Hezarfen Ahmed Çelebi glided from here across the Bosphorus on artificial wings. Arrive at golden hour.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Dolmabahçe Palace
1. Dolmabahçe Palace
↳ The last great Ottoman palace — replacing Topkapi as the imperial seat for the final decades of the empire. The 285-room interior is European Baroque with Ottoman scale: the Crystal Staircase has Baccarat railings; the ceremonial hall chandelier weighs four and a half tonnes.

↳ The 32-km strait dividing Europe from Asia is best seen from the water — a public ferry past Ottoman palaces, Bosphorus fortresses, yalı mansions, and the two suspension bridges. The round-trip cruise from Eminönü pier departs once in the late morning; European palaces to starboard, Asia to port.

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Chora Church
1. Chora Church
↳ The finest surviving Byzantine mosaics in the world — 14th-century scenes from the life of Christ and the Virgin Mary cover every surface of the narthex in gold, ochre, and lapis. The Anastasis fresco in the inner chapel shows Christ pulling Adam and Eve from their tombs.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Chora Church · 4.8⭐ · muze.gov.tr
🏛️ Saturday - Thursday 9:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Friday
⏰ ~1h 30min
📍 Kariye Camii Sk. · Edirnekapı
🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Fener-Balat
2. Fener-Balat
↳ Two adjacent Golden Horn neighborhoods — Fener is the historic seat of the Greek Orthodox Patriarchate; Balat preserves the old Jewish quarter in painted wooden houses in coral, cobalt, and saffron. Walk the steep alleys uphill past the Church of St. George for the best surviving Ottoman street scene in Istanbul.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Balat · Fatih
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Pierre Loti Hill
3. Pierre Loti Hill
↳ A hilltop above Eyüp cemetery reached by cable car — the view takes in the full Golden Horn, the rooftops of Balat and Fener, and on clear days the Sea of Marmara. The café, named after French novelist Pierre Loti who wrote of his love for Istanbul here, serves çay at outdoor tables.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~1h 30min

Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 30 min → Beylerbeyi Palace
1. Beylerbeyi Palace
↳ The Ottoman summer palace on the Asian Bosphorus shore — 26 rooms and 6 halls in European Baroque and Ottoman style, ordered by Sultan Abdülaziz in 1865. Empress Eugénie of France stayed here in 1869. The Bosphorus terraces look directly across to Dolmabahçe on the European shore.
📖 Wikipedia

🚕 15 min → Çamlıca Hill
2. Çamlıca Hill
↳ Istanbul's highest natural point — 288 metres on the Asian shore with a 360° panorama: both Bosphorus bridges, the Old City skyline, the Princes' Islands in the Sea of Marmara, and the Asian hills. The Büyük Çamlıca Mosque on the summit is the largest mosque in Turkey.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Büyük Çamlıca · Üsküdar
🚕 15 min → Maiden's Tower
3. Maiden's Tower
↳ A Byzantine tower on a rock in the Bosphorus — the image that defines Istanbul's Asian-side waterfront. Built as a lighthouse in the 18th century on a 5th-century Byzantine predecessor, reached by a 5-minute ferry from Salacak shore. Both banks of the Bosphorus are in arm's reach from the top.
📖 Wikipedia

🌙 The Layered Ezan

Istanbul has more than 3,000 mosques. At each of the five daily prayers, every one of them calls the adhan — the Islamic call to prayer — simultaneously, but not quite. Each muezzin is offset by a few seconds from his neighbors, so what you hear from any elevated point in the city is a cascading wave: the call begins on the Asian shore, rolls across the water, and reaches the European hills in overlapping ripples that last two to three minutes before fading.

The effect is best experienced at dusk from an elevated vantage — the hotel terrace, the Galata Tower gallery, Pierre Loti Hill above the Golden Horn, or Çamlıca Hill on the Asian side with the whole European skyline in view. You do not need to be Muslim for this to be deeply moving. It is the acoustic signature of Istanbul, unrepeatable anywhere else on earth, and it happens five times a day.

On Day 1, step outside Hagia Sophia at dusk and stand still. The call begins from the minarets directly above you — then, a few seconds later, from the Blue Mosque across the square — then, more distantly, from mosques across the water on the Asian shore. The entire city answers at once.
