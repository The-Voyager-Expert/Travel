Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Hagia Sophia
1. Hagia Sophia
↳ Byzantine architecture's supreme achievement — a domed basilica that served as the world's largest cathedral for nearly a thousand years. The vast dome, 5th-century gold mosaics, and the sheer scale of the nave create an atmosphere impossible to find anywhere else on earth.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 Sultanahmet Square · Sultanahmet
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Blue Mosque
2. Blue Mosque
↳ Sultan Ahmed I's 17th-century imperial mosque — the only one in Istanbul with six minarets and the only Ottoman mosque faced entirely in hand-painted Iznik tiles, over 20,000 of them in cobalt blue. Enter through the courtyard, remove shoes, and walk the central nave under the cascading half-domes and 260 windows.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~45min
🆓 Free
📍 Sultanahmet Square · Sultanahmet
🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Basilica Cistern
3. Basilica Cistern
↳ An underground Byzantine reservoir — a vast flooded forest of 336 marble columns, their reflections stretching across the still water in amber torchlight. Two upside-down Medusa heads serve as column bases in the northwest corner; nobody knows why, and nobody has agreed in a millennium and a half.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Basilica Cistern · 4.7⭐ · yerebatan.com
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 Yerebatan Cd. 1/3 · Sultanahmet
🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Topkapi Palace
4. Topkapi Palace
↳ The nerve centre of the Ottoman Empire — four courtyards, the Harem, the Treasury's Topkapi dagger and 86-carat Spoonmaker's Diamond, and the Sacred Relics chamber with the Prophet Muhammad's mantle. The Fourth Courtyard terrace overlooks the Bosphorus, the Sea of Marmara, and the Golden Horn simultaneously.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Istanbul Archaeological Museums
1. Istanbul Archaeological Museums
↳ Three interconnected museums on the Topkapi grounds — centred on the Alexander Sarcophagus, whose battle reliefs are the finest Greek marble carving in the world. The Tiled Kiosk is the oldest non-religious Ottoman building; the Ancient Orient wing holds Babylonian gates and the Treaty of Kadesh.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Istanbul Archaeological Museums · 4.6⭐ · muze.gov.tr
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 Osman Hamdi Bey Yokuşu Sk · Gülhane
🚶 22 min · 🚕 10 min → Süleymaniye Mosque
2. Süleymaniye Mosque
↳ Mimar Sinan's masterpiece — the mosque crowns the Third Hill with a dome nearly as wide as Hagia Sophia's. Sinan calibrated 138 windows so the space reads as pure geometry rather than mass. The külliye gardens behind hold Sinan's own tomb, deliberately small.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~45min
🆓 Free
📍 Prof. Sıddık Sami Onar Cd. No:1 · Fatih
🚶 28 min · 🚕 14 min → Galata Tower
3. Galata Tower
↳ A 14th-century Genoese stone tower rising 67 metres above Beyoğlu — the gallery at the top gives a 360° view over the Golden Horn, the Historic Peninsula, the Bosphorus, and the Asian shore. Legend holds that Hezarfen Ahmed Çelebi glided from here across the Bosphorus on artificial wings. Arrive at golden hour.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Dolmabahçe Palace
1. Dolmabahçe Palace
↳ The last great Ottoman palace — replacing Topkapi as the imperial seat for the final decades of the empire. The 285-room interior is European Baroque with Ottoman scale: the Crystal Staircase has Baccarat railings; the ceremonial hall chandelier weighs four and a half tonnes.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Dolmabahçe Palace · 4.7⭐ · millisaraylar.gov.tr
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:00am - 5:30pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2h
📍 Dolmabahçe Cd. · Beşiktaş
🚕 35 min → Rumeli Hisarı Fortress
2. Rumeli Hisarı Fortress
↳ Sultan Mehmed II built this fortress in four months at the narrowest point of the Bosphorus — directly across from the older Asian shore fortress — to throttle Byzantium's sea supply before the conquest. Three massive towers still stand intact. The views from the ramparts are among the finest in Istanbul.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Baklava
↳ Layers of hand-stretched filo filled with pistachios or clotted cream, drenched in sugar syrup and clarified butter. The pistachio version from Gaziantep is the standard; Hafız Mustafa serves it fresh daily.
📍 Hafız Mustafa 1864 · Sirkeci
Sultanahmet Köfte
↳ The dish the district is named for — hand-formed grilled meatballs seasoned with cumin and onion, served with white bean stew and spicy tomato sauce. Order and eat standing at Tarihi Sultanahmet Köftecisi.
📍 Tarihi Sultanahmet Köftecisi · Divanyolu
Balık Ekmek
↳ A grilled mackerel fillet in a half-baguette with onions, lettuce, lemon, and sumac — the iconic Istanbul street sandwich sold from bobbing boats at Eminönü. The fish is caught in the Bosphorus.
📍 Eminönü waterfront · Eminönü
Simit
↳ Istanbul's street bread — a circular sesame-coated ring baked until the crust crunches, sold from red-lacquered carts at all hours; the traditional breakfast with a glass of çay.
📍 Street carts · Sultanahmet Square
Meze
↳ The opening tableau of a Turkish dinner — cold starters in small plates: haydari, patlıcan salatası, tarama, dolma, and beyaz peynir. Order a selection at a meyhane with a glass of raki poured over ice.
📍 Any meyhane · Asmalımescit · Beyoğlu

🌙 The Layered Ezan

Istanbul has more than 3,000 mosques. At each of the five daily prayers, every one of them calls the adhan — the Islamic call to prayer — simultaneously, but not quite. Each muezzin is offset by a few seconds from his neighbors, so what you hear from any elevated point in the city is a cascading wave: the call begins on the Asian shore, rolls across the water, and reaches the European hills in overlapping ripples that last two to three minutes before fading.

The effect is best experienced at dusk from an elevated vantage — the hotel terrace, the Galata Tower gallery, the ramparts of Rumeli Hisarı, or a rooftop café in Sultanahmet with a glass of çay. You do not need to be Muslim for this to be deeply moving. It is the acoustic signature of Istanbul, unrepeatable anywhere else on earth, and it happens five times a day.

On Day 1, step outside Hagia Sophia at dusk and stand still. The call begins from the minarets directly above you — then, a few seconds later, from the Blue Mosque across the square — then, more distantly, from mosques across the water on the Asian shore. The entire city answers at once.
