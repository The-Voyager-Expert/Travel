Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Cascais Old Town
1. Cascais Old Town & Harbour
↳ Fishing village turned royal summer retreat in the 1870s. Both pasts visible: old cottages beside belle-époque villas, working harbour, 14th-century Citadela walls to the beach. Daily fish auction at the lota — open to visitors.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Largo 5 de Outubro
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Casa das Histórias Paula Rego
2. Casa das Histórias Paula Rego
↳ Museum by Pritzker architect Souto de Moura, twin terracotta towers echoing Sintra's chimneys. Large paintings by Paula Rego — folk tales, Dickens, and the Salazar era: strong women in dark, charged scenes. No booking required.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Av. da República
🚶 25 min · 🚕 7 min → Boca do Inferno
3. Boca do Inferno
↳ A sea cave 2.5 km west of Cascais where the Atlantic erupts through a limestone arch with a deep concussive boom — best within 90 min of high tide. The flat coastal path from town is entirely paved with sea views the whole way.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Estrada da Boca do Inferno
🚕 12 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 17 min → Praia do Guincho
1. Praia do Guincho
↳ Wildest beach on the Estoril Coast — a 1.3 km arc backed by Serra de Sintra, 9 km northwest of Cascais. World-class windsurfing; always windier and cooler. Dress warmly even in summer; tall dunes shelter non-surfers. Parking free.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Estrada do Guincho
🚕 14 min → Museu Condes de Castro Guimarães
2. Museu Condes de Castro Guimarães
↳ Late Gothic Revival manor on the seafront, given to town in 1930. Inside: 25,000-volume library, 16th-century illuminated text, Moorish tile panels, silver from two centuries. The park — lake, peacocks, shaded path — is free to enter.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1h
📍 Av. Rei Humberto II de Itália
🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min → Cascais-Estoril Coastal Promenade
3. Cascais-Estoril Coastal Promenade
↳ Flat 3 km promenade links Estoril to Cascais past pocket beaches, belle-époque villas, and café terraces — 40 min either way. Best late afternoon: locals fill the bars and the lota fish auction is in full swing. Bike rentals in Cascais.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30 min

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Marina de Cascais
1. Marina de Cascais
↳ Modern marina opened in 1999 with around 650 berths, ringed by a horseshoe of restaurants, gelato counters, and bars beneath a replica-fort façade. The flat quayside promenade is the easiest stroll in town — sailboats on one side, the open bay on the other.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Marina · Baía
🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Cidadela de Cascais
2. Cidadela de Cascais
↳ A fortress begun in the 15th century to guard the bay, later a royal summer residence. Its walls now enclose the Bairro dos Museus art district — galleries, artists' studios, a design hotel, and quiet cobbled lanes that open onto the sea wall and the marina below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Largo da Cidadela
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Farol de Santa Marta
3. Farol de Santa Marta
↳ A squat lighthouse from the 1860s wrapped in blue-and-white azulejo bands, still guiding ships into the bay. The headland around it is an open clifftop viewpoint over the Atlantic; the keepers' houses hold a small lighthouse museum, with the tiled Casa de Santa Maria next door.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Farol de Santa Marta
🚶 10 min · 🚕 3 min → Parque Marechal Carmona
4. Parque Marechal Carmona
↳ The town's green lungs beside the Castro Guimarães museum — broad lawns, shaded gravel paths, a duck pond, roaming peacocks, and a casual garden café. A favourite local spot for families and the easiest place to slow down before heading back to the hotel.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 Parque Marechal Carmona
🚕 12 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Peixe Grelhado
↳ Grilled fish, dressed with olive oil, garlic, and sea salt, cooked whole over charcoal. The fishing boats still go out daily from Cascais harbour. Best options: dourada — sea bream, robalo — sea bass, cherne — wreckfish.
📍 Baluarte
Pastel De Nata
↳ The custard tart. Flaky concave pastry shell, warm egg-custard centre with a caramelised top, dusted with cinnamon. Best eaten within minutes of leaving the oven.

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Lisbon · 35 minutes
↳ Portugal's capital — Alfama's Moorish lanes, the Manueline Belém Tower and Jerónimos Monastery, and Pombaline Baixa rebuilt after the 1755 earthquake. CP Linha de Cascais runs four trains per hour direct to Cais do Sodré.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Sintra · 1 hour 10 minutes
↳ The royal hill town wrapped in UNESCO palaces — Pena Palace's painted volumes, the Moorish Castle's 8th-century walls, the Initiation Well at Quinta da Regaleira. Two-train route via Cais do Sodré and Lisbon Rossio Station.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Setúbal · 1h 15 min
↳ A working port city on the Sado estuary — the gateway to Serra da Arrábida nature reserve and the Sado dolphin colony. Take CP Cascais line to Cais do Sodré, then Fertagus across the bridge to Setúbal.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Santarém · 1h 20 min
↳ A clifftop Ribatejo city overlooking the Tagus plain — the Gothic Igreja de Santa Cruz, the panoramic Portas do Sol gardens, and Portugal's self-styled Gothic capital. CP Intercidades via Cais do Sodré.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Évora · 1h 45 min
↳ Alentejo's walled Roman city — the Temple of Diana, a UNESCO cathedral quarter, and the bone chapel at Igreja de São Francisco. CP Intercidades service via Cais do Sodré and Entrecampos.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
