1. Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque
↳ The UAE's largest mosque and one of the world's most beautiful — 82 white-marble domes, mirror-still reflecting pools, and the planet's largest hand-knotted carpet beneath a chandelier dripping with Swarovski crystal. Modest dress is required and entry is free.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Qasr Al Watan
↳ Not a museum but a working presidential palace opened to the public — a showcase of Arabian craftsmanship, from the 37 m Great Hall dome to a library of 50,000 books and a hall displaying gifts of state.

✨ Two Cities, One Country

Two days in the UAE is really two days in two different moods. Dubai is vertical and restless — a city that builds upward and outward and asks you to look down on it from a deck. Abu Dhabi, an hour and a quarter south, is horizontal and ceremonial — white marble, presidential halls, and a museum that turns sunlight into rain. Pairing them in one trip is the whole point: the future-tense skyline on Day 1, the monumental and the sacred on Day 2.

A few things that make the two days run smoothly. Every headline stop here is timed-entry — book Burj Khalifa, Museum of the Future and Louvre Abu Dhabi slots before you arrive, and aim Burj Khalifa for the late-afternoon golden hour. The Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is free but closes to visitors on Friday mornings for prayers, so a Friday Day 2 means an afternoon arrival. And in June, everything indoors is a relief — the heat is real, so the air-conditioned decks, palaces and galleries are not just sights, they're shelter.
