Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 4 min → Strasbourg Cathedral
1. Strasbourg Cathedral
↳ One of the great Gothic cathedrals of Europe. Its single 142-metre spire made it the tallest building in the world for over two centuries, and the carved west front, rose window, and Renaissance astronomical clock are unmissable. A 332-step climb reaches the rooftop platform over the Grande Île.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 7 min · 🚕 9 min → Place Kléber
2. Place Kléber
↳ Strasbourg's largest square and civic heart, ringed by elegant facades and anchored by the neoclassical Aubette. A statue of the Strasbourg-born General Kléber stands at its centre, and in summer the open plaza fills with cafés, markets, and events.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Place Kléber · Grande Île
🚶 6 min · 🚕 1 min → La Petite France
3. La Petite France
↳ The most picturesque quarter of the Grande Île — a maze of cobbled lanes and canals lined with half-timbered tanners' and millers' houses, their steep roofs once used for drying hides. Flower-decked bridges and waterside terraces make it Strasbourg's postcard.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Place Benjamin Zix · Petite France
🚶 4 min · 🚕 4 min → Ponts Couverts
4. Ponts Couverts
↳ A line of three medieval bridges guarded by four square watchtowers, survivors of Strasbourg's old ramparts at the western tip of the Grande Île. The towers and weirs frame the canals of Petite France — the classic view is from the terrace of the Barrage Vauban just behind them.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Place du Quartier Blanc · Petite France

1. Batorama Boat Tour
↳ A 70-minute cruise on the river Ill — the classic way to see Strasbourg. Glass-roofed boats leave the Palais Rohan landing, drop through the lock of Petite France, and glide past the German-era Neustadt and the European institutions, with multilingual audio commentary throughout.

⏰ ~1 hr 15 min

🚶 13 min · 🚕 6 min → Place de la République
2. Place de la République
↳ The monumental centrepiece of the Neustadt, the German imperial quarter now UNESCO-listed alongside the Grande Île. A circular garden of ginkgo and magnolia is ringed by grand Wilhelmine palaces — the Palais du Rhin, the National Library, and the Théâtre National.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Place de la République · Neustadt
🚶 9 min · 🚕 1 min → Église Saint-Paul
3. Église Saint-Paul
↳ A soaring neo-Gothic church of pink Vosges sandstone, built as the garrison church for the German imperial army. Its twin 76-metre spires rise straight from the river Ill on the tip of an island, mirrored in the water — one of the Neustadt's most photographed silhouettes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Place du Général Eisenhower · Neustadt
🚶 21 min · 🚕 4 min → Parc de l'Orangerie
4. Parc de l'Orangerie
↳ Strasbourg's oldest and largest park, landscaped around the Pavillon Joséphine. Shaded alleys lead to a lake with rowing boats, a waterfall, and a free-roaming stork enclosure — the bird that has been Alsace's emblem for centuries. A green finish beside the European quarter.
🏛️ Daily 7:30am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr

❗️ Heads Up
❗️ Barrage Vauban — Terrace Closed
↳ The Vauban Dam's panoramic rooftop terrace is closed for a major renovation.
Workaround: View the Ponts Couverts from the dam's bridge level or the Petite France quais instead.
❗️ Astronomical Clock — No Sunday Show
↳ The cathedral's astronomical-clock procession runs once a day at 12:30pm but not on Sundays when mass is celebrated.
Workaround: Come on a weekday and join the queue from about 11:45am for the 12:30pm show.
❗️ Strasbourg Museums — Closed Tuesday
↳ The city's municipal museums including the Palais Rohan museums and the Museum of Modern Art all close on Tuesdays.
Workaround: Plan any museum visit for a day other than Tuesday.
🕰️ A City Between Two Worlds

Strasbourg has spent its whole history on a seam. Its cathedral spire was the tallest structure on Earth for well over two centuries, and beneath it Gutenberg spent his Strasbourg years tinkering with the movable type that would remake the world — a fitting birthplace for a city that has always been a crossing point of ideas as much as of rivers.

What makes the two days fit together is the layering. Day 1 is the French Grande Île — half-timbered, intimate, medieval. Day 2 is the Neustadt, the monumental quarter Germany built to stamp its own grandeur onto the city. UNESCO now lists them together, two empires' visions of the same place side by side, which is why the European Parliament chose Strasbourg as its seat: a city fought over so often it became the natural home for reconciliation.
