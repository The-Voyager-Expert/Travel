🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Kahal Zur Israel Synagogue
1. Kahal Zur Israel Synagogue
↳ The oldest synagogue in the Americas, founded by Sephardic Jewish settlers during Dutch colonial rule in 1636. The current building is a museum documenting Jewish life in Pernambuco, with a ritual bath preserved beneath the floor.

2. Torre Malakoff
↳ A Neo-Gothic astronomical observatory and gateway built in 1852 for the Naval Arsenal. Named after the Battle of Malakhov during the Crimean War, the tower now functions as a cultural centre and exhibition space with views over the port and old city.

3. Museu Cais do Sertão
↳ An immersive museum celebrating northeastern Brazilian sertão culture and the music of Luiz Gonzaga. Opened in 2014 inside a refurbished waterfront warehouse, with sound, light, and tactile displays recreating the interior landscape.

4. Capella Dourada
↳ Considered the finest example of Brazilian Baroque art, the Golden Chapel was completed in 1697 by the Franciscan Order. The interior is encrusted with 120 kg of gold leaf applied over intricately carved cedar wood, making it one of the richest colonial-era interiors in South America.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Holy Saviour of the World Cathedral
1. Holy Saviour of the World Cathedral
↳ The oldest religious site in the Olinda UNESCO World Heritage area, begun in 1537 and elevated to a cathedral in 1676. Sits at the Alto da Sé summit with panoramic views of Recife's coastline and the historic rooftops below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 12:00pm · 2:00pm - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1h
🆓 Free

🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Basilica and Monastery of St. Benedict
2. Basilica and Monastery of St. Benedict
↳ A working Benedictine monastery founded in the 16th century, with a basilica holding one of Brazil's most ornate gilded altars — 22-karat gold leaf over carved timber. The complex also houses a museum of sacred art and a school operating since 1799.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Oficina Cerâmica Francisco Brennand
1. Oficina Cerâmica Francisco Brennand
↳ The studio and garden of sculptor Francisco Brennand, set on a former tile factory on the edge of the Atlantic Forest. Over 2,000 ceramic sculptures fill the outdoor grounds, factory floors, and a private chapel — large-scale figurative and mythological pieces created over a career spanning six decades.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2h
📍 Propriedade Santo Cosme e Damião s/n · Várzea
🚶 20 min · 🚕 8 min → Instituto Ricardo Brennand
2. Instituto Ricardo Brennand
↳ A private museum in a Tudor Revival castle built in 2002, holding the world's largest Frans Post collection, a 3,000-piece medieval armory, and rotating exhibitions across 77,000 m² of Atlantic Forest gardens.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Forte de São Tiago das Cinco Pontas
1. Forte de São Tiago das Cinco Pontas
↳ A five-pointed star fort built by the Dutch in 1630, rebuilt in stone by the Portuguese in 1654 after they retook Recife. Today it houses the Museu da Cidade, tracing the city's colonial and modern history through photographs and artifacts.

🍮 Local Tastes
Tapioca
↳ A thin lacy crêpe made from hydrated cassava starch cooked on a dry griddle. Filled with cheese, coconut, chocolate, or meat, tapioca is the signature street food of Pernambuco and northeastern Brazil.
📍 Feira da Boa Viagem · Mercado de São José
Bolo de Rolo
↳ A rolled guava paste cake — thin layers of sponge wrapped tightly around a streak of guava preserves, creating a spiral cross-section when sliced. A registered geographical indication of Pernambuco state.
📍 Mercado de São José
Caldo de Cana
↳ Fresh sugarcane juice pressed to order through a mechanical roller, often served over ice with a squeeze of lime. A staple of Brazilian street markets, particularly refreshing in Recife's tropical heat.
📍 Antigo · Alto da Sé · Olinda
Macaxeira Frita
↳ Fried cassava served crisp on the outside and soft within, a side dish at almost every northeastern Brazilian table. A starchier, richer alternative to fries, accompanying grilled meats and regional dishes.
📍 Parraxaxá · Entre Amigos o Bode

❗️ Heads Up
Capella Dourada — Closed All Weekend
↳ The Golden Chapel closes Saturday and Sunday. Only open Monday through Friday. Plan Day 1 on a weekday or this stop is unavailable.
Forte das Cinco Pontas — Closed Monday & Tuesday
↳ The fort closes both Monday and Tuesday. Schedule Day 4 from Wednesday through Sunday to access the museum.
Brennand Art Complex — Both Venues Closed Monday
↳ Both Oficina Cerâmica and Instituto Ricardo Brennand close Monday. The entire Day 3 must fall on Tuesday through Sunday.
Instituto Ricardo Brennand — Afternoon Opening Only
↳ The Instituto opens at 1:00pm. Visit Oficina Cerâmica first in the morning, then arrive at Instituto no earlier than 1:00pm.
