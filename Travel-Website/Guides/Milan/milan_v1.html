Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 6 min → Milan Cathedral
1. Milan Cathedral
↳ Milan's cathedral is one of the largest Gothic churches on earth, five centuries in the making and crowned with a forest of 135 marble spires and thousands of statues. The real reward is the rooftop walk, where you stroll among the pinnacles with the gilded Madonnina overhead and the Alps on a clear day.

2. Teatro alla Scala
↳ La Scala is the most storied opera house in the world — the stage that launched Verdi, Puccini, and Maria Callas. Without a performance ticket you can still tour the theatre museum, peer into a velvet-and-gilt box over the auditorium, and see costumes, busts, and instruments tracing two centuries of opera.

🚶 7 min · 🚕 5 min → Pinacoteca di Brera
3. Pinacoteca di Brera
↳ One of Italy's foremost collections of painting, set in a grand palazzo around a colonnaded courtyard. The galleries hold masterworks that anchor any survey of Italian art — Raphael's Marriage of the Virgin, Mantegna's foreshortened Dead Christ, Caravaggio's Supper at Emmaus, and Hayez's The Kiss.

4. Brera District
↳ Milan's bohemian quarter, a grid of cobbled lanes around the art academy lined with galleries, antique dealers, candlelit trattorie, and tiny cafés. Via Fiori Chiari and Via Madonnina are the heart of it — a slow late-afternoon wander before aperitivo is the way to see it.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 17 min · 🚕 7 min → Sforza Castle
1. Sforza Castle
↳ The vast brick fortress of the Sforza dukes, ringed by towers and battlements and entered beneath the soaring Filarete Tower. The courtyards are free to wander; inside, the civic museums hold Michelangelo's unfinished final work, the Rondanini Pietà. Step through the back gate and Parco Sempione opens up beyond.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 7:30pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
🆓 Free

🚶 5 min · 🚕 5 min → Parco Sempione
2. Parco Sempione
↳ Milan's great central park, laid out behind the castle in English-garden style with winding paths, a lake, and long lawns. At the far end stands the Arco della Pace, a neoclassical triumphal arch, and a climbable iron lookout tower gives a quick view over the greenery — a restful breather between heavy sights.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:30am - 11:30pm

🚶 11 min · 🚕 6 min → Santa Maria delle Grazie
3. Santa Maria delle Grazie
↳ A serene Renaissance church whose adjoining refectory holds Leonardo da Vinci's Last Supper — one of the most famous paintings in the world. Only small groups enter for a strict 15-minute slot, so booking well ahead is essential. The Bramante apse and cloister of the church itself are worth lingering over too.
📖 Wikipedia

4. Basilica di Sant'Ambrogio
↳ Founded by Milan's patron Saint Ambrose in the 4th century and rebuilt in the 11th, this is the model of Lombard Romanesque — a brick atrium, two mismatched bell towers, and a golden altar inside. It is one of the oldest and most atmospheric churches in the city, and far quieter than the Duomo.

1. San Maurizio al Monastero Maggiore
↳ A plain street facade hides the most dazzling interior in Milan — every wall and vault frescoed top to bottom by Bernardino Luini and his circle, which earns it the nickname "the Sistine Chapel of Milan." It was the church of an enclosed Benedictine convent, and entry is free.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Basilica di San Lorenzo Maggiore
↳ One of the oldest churches in Milan, an early-Christian basilica with a great domed plan and Roman spolia built into its walls. Out front stand the Colonne di San Lorenzo — sixteen Corinthian columns from the 3rd century, the city's finest Roman relic and a favourite evening gathering spot.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 12 min · 🚕 6 min → Navigli
3. Navigli
↳ Milan's canal district, the last visible stretch of a medieval waterway network partly engineered by Leonardo. The Naviglio Grande and Naviglio Pavese are lined with vintage shops, artisan studios, and bars; come at dusk, when the towpaths fill for the city's famous aperitivo and the water catches the light.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Risotto alla Milanese
↳ Creamy short-grain rice stained gold and perfumed with saffron, finished with butter and Parmesan — Milan's defining first course, often served alongside ossobuco.
📍 Ratanà · Antica Trattoria della Pesa
Cotoletta alla Milanese
↳ A bone-in veal cutlet pounded, breaded, and fried in butter until golden — the original Milanese cutlet, served tall and crisp.
📍 Da Oscar · Trattoria del Carmine
Ossobuco con Risotto
↳ Veal shank braised slowly until the marrow melts, traditionally paired with saffron risotto and finished with gremolata — Lombard comfort food at its richest.
📍 Antica Trattoria della Pesa · Ratanà
Panettone
↳ The tall, domed sweet bread studded with candied fruit and raisins, invented in Milan and now eaten across Italy at Christmas — bakeries sell mignon versions year-round.
📍 Pasticceria Marchesi · Pasticceria Cova
Negroni Sbagliato
↳ The "mistaken" Negroni — Campari and sweet vermouth topped with sparkling wine instead of gin — invented at Bar Basso in Milan in the 1970s and now an aperitivo icon.
📍 Bar Basso

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Pavia · 35 min
↳ The Certosa di Pavia — one of the most elaborate Renaissance monasteries in Italy, with an intricate marble facade and frescoed cloisters — sits just outside a university city of Roman bridges and Lombard churches. Direct regional train.
🎫 book at: trenitalia.com or Omio
Lake Como · 40 min
↳ Italy's most glamorous lake, ringed by villa gardens and steep wooded shores. A half-hour train ride drops you on the Como waterfront, where ferries reach Bellagio, Varenna, and Menaggio.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
Bergamo · 50 min
↳ A funicular climbs to the Città Alta, a walled hilltop old town of Venetian ramparts, a frescoed basilica, and quiet piazzas — one of northern Italy's most beautiful and least-rushed medieval cores.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
Turin · 1 hr
↳ The elegant former royal capital, all arcaded boulevards and baroque squares, home to the world-class Museo Egizio — the greatest hoard of Egyptian antiquities after Cairo — and the soaring Mole Antonelliana.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
Verona · 1 hr 20 min
↳ Shakespeare's city of Romeo and Juliet, built around a remarkably preserved Roman arena that still hosts summer opera. Pastel palazzi, the Adige river, and Piazza delle Erbe make it a romantic day out.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
