Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Skyline Gondola
1. Skyline Gondola
↳ The Southern Hemisphere's steepest cable car climbs 450 metres to Bob's Peak, where the whole of Queenstown, Lake Wakatipu and the jagged Remarkables open up below. The summit terrace, luge track and stargazing all share the view.

2. TSS Earnslaw
↳ A 1912 coal-fired steamship — the "Lady of the Lake" — still cruises Lake Wakatipu under her original engines. Watch the stokers shovel coal, then take in the alpine shoreline from the open deck on a 90-minute sailing.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Queenstown Gardens
↳ A leafy peninsula jutting into Lake Wakatipu, planted from 1867 — giant sequoias, a rose garden, a frisbee-golf course and a lakeside loop with the Remarkables framed at every turn.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Kawarau Bridge Bungy
1. Kawarau Bridge Bungy
↳ The birthplace of commercial bungy: in 1988 A.J. Hackett opened the world's first jump site on this 1880 gold-rush suspension bridge. It's a 43-metre plunge to the Kawarau River, with an optional water touch.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ bungy.co.nz
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 State Highway 6 · Gibbston
🚕 20 min → Arrowtown
2. Arrowtown
↳ A perfectly preserved 1860s gold-mining town — a tree-lined avenue of stone and timber cottages on Buckingham Street, a restored Chinese miners' settlement on Bush Creek, and autumn colour that draws crowds every April.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Buckingham Street · Arrowtown

3. Shotover Jet
↳ Big-red jet boats blast through the sheer rock canyons of the Shotover River at 85 km/h — the only operator licensed to run the canyons. Expect inches-from-the-rock passes and full 360-degree spins.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 45 min → Glenorchy & Paradise
1. Glenorchy & Paradise
↳ A 46-km drive along Lake Wakatipu leads to Glenorchy's glacier-carved valley, then beyond to the settlement of Paradise — filming ground for Isengard, Lothlórien and Amon Hen in the Lord of the Rings trilogy. Mount Earnslaw towers over the Dart River flats and the beech forests that doubled as Middle-earth.
📖 Wikipedia

🚕 20 min → Lake Hayes
2. Lake Hayes
↳ A glacier-formed lake set between rolling hills and the Crown Range, famous for near-perfect mountain reflections in its still water. The 8-km loop track circles the shore through farmland and native plantings, with Coronet Peak rising above the far end.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 State Highway 6 · Lake Hayes
🚕 20 min → Queenstown Hill Time Walk
3. Queenstown Hill Time Walk
↳ A 5.6 km return track climbing 500 metres through pine forest to the summit of Te Tapu-nui at 907 m, where Lake Wakatipu and the Remarkables unfold in every direction. Six geological time-plates mark the route, and the wrought-iron Basket of Dreams sculpture crowns the summit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
📍 60 Belfast Terrace · Belfast Terrace Trailhead

4. Kiwi Birdlife Park
↳ A family-run 2-hectare wildlife sanctuary just below the Skyline Gondola, home to over 20 species of native New Zealand wildlife. Nocturnal kiwi houses allow close encounters with the national bird in dim light, alongside kea parrots, the endemic tuatara reptile and the endangered whio blue duck.

2. Ledge Bungy
↳ A 47-metre freestyle bungy jump at the Skyline summit, 400 metres above Queenstown — the city, lake and the Remarkables spread below as you free-fall. Unlike the gorge jump at Kawarau, the Ledge allows run-ups, handstands and any launch style the jumper chooses.

Day 6
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 5min → Roy's Peak
1. Roy's Peak
↳ A 1,578m summit above Lake Wānaka with a 16km return trail climbing 1,200m through golden tussock — the panoramic view from the top ridge is one of the most photographed in New Zealand. Start early; the track can be busy by mid-morning.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~5 hr 30 min
📍 Mount Aspiring Road · Wānaka
🚕 5 min → That Wānaka Tree
2. That Wānaka Tree
↳ A lone willow growing from the shore of Lake Wānaka near the town waterfront — its reflection in the still water has made it one of New Zealand's most photographed subjects. The tree is accessible year-round from the public lakefront path.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Lakefront Drive · Wānaka
🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Mount Aspiring National Park
3. Mount Aspiring National Park
↳ New Zealand's third-largest national park at 355,000 hectares, spanning glaciers, rivers and tussock valleys from the Haast Pass to Fiordland. The visitor centre in Wānaka serves as the gateway to the 3,033m peak of Mount Aspiring.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 1 Ballantyne Road · Wānaka
🚕 15 min → Lake Hāwea
4. Lake Hāwea
↳ A deep glacial lake separated from Lake Wānaka by a narrow neck of land, with turquoise water surrounded by snow-capped peaks. A small lakeside settlement sits at the southern end, and the lake offers a quieter alternative to the busier Wānaka waterfront.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Lake Hāwea Township · Otago

💎 Notes from the Lake

Both of your days trace the same gold rush. The Shotover River that the jet boats tear through was once called the richest gold-bearing river in the world, and the Kawarau Gorge Suspension Bridge you bungy off was built in 1880 as a goldfields access route. Arrowtown is that rush frozen in place. So the adventure capital of New Zealand is really a mining town that found a second fortune in adrenaline — A.J. Hackett's 1988 leap off a 108-year-old bridge is the moment the switch flipped.

Lake Wakatipu appears to breathe. Its surface rises and falls about 10 centimetres every 25 minutes — a slow standing wave, or seiche, caused by the lake's lightning-bolt shape. Māori tradition tells it as the heartbeat of the giant Matau, sleeping at the bottom with his knees drawn up to form the lake's distinctive bend. The TSS Earnslaw has been steaming across that slow tide since 1912.
