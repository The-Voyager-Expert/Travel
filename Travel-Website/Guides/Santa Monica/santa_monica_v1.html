🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Santa Monica Pier
1. Santa Monica Pier
↳ Built in 1909 and extended in 1916, this municipal pier marks the western terminus of historic U.S. Route 66. It holds Pacific Park amusement park with a solar-powered Ferris wheel, a 1922 hippodrome carousel, and the Santa Monica Pier Aquarium below the deck.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Ocean Front Walk · Santa Monica Pier
🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Santa Monica State Beach
2. Santa Monica State Beach
↳ A 3.5-mile white-sand beach managed by Los Angeles County, stretching from the pier north toward Malibu. Facilities include a paved multi-use path, volleyball courts, lifeguard towers, and access to the 22-mile Marvin Braude Bike Trail running the length of Santa Monica Bay.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Pacific Coast Highway · Santa Monica
🚶 15 min · 🚕 4 min → Third Street Promenade
3. Third Street Promenade
↳ Three car-free blocks of outdoor retail, dining, and street performance in the heart of downtown Santa Monica, running between Broadway and Wilshire Boulevard. Anchored at the south end by Santa Monica Place; licensed street performers occupy designated zones throughout the corridor.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 1351 3rd Street Promenade · Downtown
🚶 20 min · 🚕 7 min → Bergamot Station Arts Center
4. Bergamot Station Arts Center
↳ A former railroad depot — first used in 1875 as a stop on the Los Angeles and Independence Railroad — converted into a 5.5-acre campus of more than 20 fine art galleries, studios, and the City Garage theater. The Metro E Line's 26th St/Bergamot Station stop is immediately adjacent.

💡 Claude Inspiration

The Marvin Braude Bike Trail runs 22 miles from Will Rogers State Beach north of the pier to Torrance Beach in the south — completely flat along the water. Starting at Santa Monica Pier and riding south to Venice takes about 30 minutes on a rental bike, passing Muscle Beach, the Venice Boardwalk, and the Venice Canals.

Bike rentals are available at the pier and along Ocean Front Walk.
