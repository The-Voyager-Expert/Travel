Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 2 min · 🚕 1 min → Porto Cervo
1. Porto Cervo
↳ The heart of the Costa Smeralda — a purpose-built resort village of curved pastel facades the stone-terraced Piazzetta and a 700-berth marina where mega-yachts moor. Wander the arcaded lanes and the pedestrian steps down to the old port.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Piazzetta Costa Smeralda · Porto Cervo
🚶 6 min · 🚕 3 min → Stella Maris Church
2. Stella Maris Church
↳ A small white masterpiece of Mediterranean-modern sacred architecture by Michele Busiri Vici built on land donated by the Aga Khan. Inside hangs a 16th-century Mater Dolorosa attributed to El Greco.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Via della Chiesa · Porto Cervo
🚕 23 min → Nuraghe La Prisgiona
3. Nuraghe La Prisgiona
↳ A Bronze Age nuragic complex in the Capichera valley — a central tower of stacked granite ringed by a village of some 90 huts occupied from the 14th to the 9th century BC. Deep stone-walled well and hut floors are still legible on the ground.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Località Capichera · Arzachena
🚕 4 min → Giants' Grave of Coddu Vecchiu
4. Giants' Grave of Coddu Vecchiu
↳ A Bronze Age gallery-grave of the Gallura type. A central granite stele nearly four metres tall carved with a horizontal moulding and a small false door fronts a curved exedra where rites were held.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Località Capichera · Arzachena

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 35 min → Palau ferry · 🚢 15 min → La Maddalena
1. La Maddalena
↳ The archipelago's main island and town — a granite harbour of pastel houses reached by boat across the Strait of Bonifacio. Napoleon fought his first action here. The island anchors a full-day boat tour of the surrounding islands booked in the Tours section.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Piazza Umberto I · La Maddalena
🚢 20 min → Caprera
2. Caprera
↳ A pine-and-granite island joined to La Maddalena by a causeway entirely inside the national park. Giuseppe Garibaldi bought half of it lived his last years here and is buried on it; the twin coves of Cala Coticcio are nicknamed "Tahiti."
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Cala Coticcio · Caprera
🚢 20 min → Budelli
3. Budelli
↳ The uninhabited island famous for the Spiaggia Rosa whose sand glows pink from crushed shells of the microscopic Miniacina miniacea. The beach is fenced off to protect it so boats hold offshore for a front-row view of the colour.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Spiaggia Rosa · Isola di Budelli
🚢 15 min → Spargi
4. Spargi
↳ An uninhabited granite island in the heart of the archipelago known for the sheltered turquoise waters of Cala Corsara on its south coast. The sandy bay is a standard stop on all day-boat loops out of Palau.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Cala Corsara · Isola di Spargi

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 35 min → Palau
1. Palau
↳ The mainland harbour town facing the archipelago with a compact centre of granite buildings and a waterfront lined with ferries to La Maddalena. The old Aragonese watchtower gives a sense of the Gallura's working life before the yacht era.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Piazza Fresi · Palau

2. Bear Rock
↳ The Roccia dell'Orso — a 120-metre granite formation above Palau weathered by wind into the unmistakable shape of a crouching bear. A short panoramic path leads up from the car park; long used by sailors as a landmark across the strait.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Via Capo d'Orso · Palau

3. La Maddalena Archipelago National Park
↳ Italy's geomarine park protecting all 28 islands and islets of the archipelago — 180 km of coastline of white granite wind-carved rock and translucent water. From Punta Sardegna the whole scatter of islands spreads out across the strait.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Punta Sardegna · Palau

4. Spiaggia del Pevero
↳ The Costa Smeralda's largest beach — a sheltered crescent of fine white sand backed by holm oak and granite boulders with calm shallow turquoise water ideal for swimming and paddling.

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 32 min → Basilica di San Simplicio
1. Basilica di San Simplicio
↳ Sardinia's foremost Romanesque church raised in pale granite in the Pisan style on a low hill once used as a burial ground. A Roman necropolis surrounds it and it remains the best-preserved medieval church on the island.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~40 min
🆓 Free
📍 Via San Simplicio · Olbia
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Olbia Archaeological Museum
2. Olbia Archaeological Museum
↳ A ship-shaped museum on Peddone island displaying Roman shipwrecks raised from the ancient harbour — amphorae anchors and hull timbers recovered from the sea floor around Olbia. The building itself is a landmark of contemporary Sardinian architecture.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Isola Peddone · Olbia

3. Corso Umberto I
↳ Olbia's pedestrian main street — a wide boulevard of granite pavement lined with bars bakeries and local shops running from Piazza Margherita to the historic quarter. The corso is the everyday pulse of a working Sardinian city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Corso Umberto I · Olbia
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Piazza Margherita
4. Piazza Margherita
↳ The civic heart of Olbia — a broad granite square of palm trees and outdoor terraces anchoring the north end of the corso. Ringed by cafes. In the evening locals fill the outdoor tables for aperitivo.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Seadas
↳ A large fried pastry parcel filled with fresh pecorino and lemon zest served warm under a pour of bitter corbezzolo honey — Sardinia's defining dessert.
📍 Officina del Gusto · Ristorante Su Cumbidu
Culurgiones
↳ Hand-pleated ravioli sealed in a wheat-ear pattern filled with potato pecorino and mint usually dressed in a simple tomato-and-onion sauce.
📍 Trattoria del Corso · Ristorante Su Cumbidu
Porceddu
↳ Suckling pig slow-roasted on a spit until the skin crackles then scented with myrtle — the centrepiece of a Sardinian feast.
📍 Ristorante Su Cumbidu · Officina del Gusto
Pane Carasau
↳ Wafer-thin twice-baked flatbread nicknamed carta da musica for its parchment crispness — served across the island as an accompaniment to nearly every meal.
📍 Trattoria del Corso · Officina del Gusto
Mirto
↳ The island's signature digestivo — a herbal bittersweet liqueur steeped from the dark-blue berries of the myrtle shrub. Poured cold at the end of a meal.
📍 Ristorante Su Cumbidu · Trattoria del Corso

🌊 Threads of the Emerald Coast

One name keeps surfacing across this whole itinerary: Prince Karim Aga Khan. He and a consortium invented the Costa Smeralda out of empty scrubland in the early 1960s and hired the architect Michele Busiri Vici to shape Porto Cervo's curves — and that same architect designed the little Stella Maris church on Day 1 on land the prince himself donated. A single modern vision sits underneath the whole coast.

The other thread is far older and pulls the opposite way. Fifteen minutes inland from the yacht marina the nuraghe and the giants' grave at Capichera were already ancient when Rome arrived — a granite civilisation that left more than 7,000 towers across the island and not one line of writing. Standing at Coddu Vecchiu the billionaires' harbour feels very young indeed.

And the human note: on Caprera Garibaldi — the man who unified Italy — spent his last years growing vegetables on a rocky island and is buried there. The archipelago that now fills with day-boats was to him the quietest place he could find.
