The Sixth Floor Museum
↳ The Sixth Floor Museum occupies the former Texas School Book Depository floor used in the Kennedy assassination of November 1963. Exhibits document the assassination, investigation, and Kennedy's presidency, with the sniper's perch preserved behind glass.

Nasher Sculpture Center
↳ The Nasher Sculpture Center holds one of the world's premier private sculpture collections in a Renzo Piano–designed building and garden. Works by Rodin, Picasso, Matisse, Giacometti, and Serra fill the naturally lit galleries and garden pavilions.

Perot Museum of Nature and Science
↳ The Perot Museum is a Morphosis-designed natural history and science museum with 11 permanent halls covering paleontology, geology, space, and engineering. The building's dramatic tilted-cube form is a Dallas architectural landmark.

🍮 Local Tastes
Texas Barbecue
↳ Dallas-style barbecue features oak-smoked brisket sliced from the flat and the point, pork ribs, and jalapeño-cheddar sausage served on butcher paper with white bread and pickles. Pecan Lodge in Deep Ellum is the current standard.
📍 Pecan Lodge
Tex-Mex
↳ Dallas Tex-Mex traces a direct line from border cantinas — enchiladas smothered in chili gravy, puffy tacos, cheese-topped chile con carne, and frozen margaritas that the city invented. The cuisine is Texas-born and distinct from interior Mexican cooking.
📍 El Fenix
Fletcher's Corny Dog
↳ The State Fair of Texas invented the corny dog — a hot dog on a stick dipped in cornmeal batter and deep-fried — and Fletcher's has made them at Fair Park since the fair's earliest decades. At Big Tex they go fast; arrive early in the season.
📍 Fair Park
Frozen Margarita
↳ Dallas lays claim to the world's first frozen margarita machine, installed at Mariano Martinez's restaurant in 1971 — the original machine is now in the Smithsonian. Every Tex-Mex table in the city pours the frozen version as standard.
📍 Mariano's
