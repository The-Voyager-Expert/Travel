1. The Sixth Floor Museum
↳ The Sixth Floor Museum occupies the former Texas School Book Depository floor used in the Kennedy assassination of November 1963. Exhibits document the assassination, investigation, and Kennedy's presidency, with the sniper's perch preserved behind glass.

3. Nasher Sculpture Center
↳ The Nasher Sculpture Center holds one of the world's premier private sculpture collections in a Renzo Piano–designed building and garden. Works by Rodin, Picasso, Matisse, Giacometti, and Serra fill the naturally lit galleries and garden pavilions.

4. Perot Museum of Nature and Science
↳ The Perot Museum is a Morphosis-designed natural history and science museum with 11 permanent halls covering paleontology, geology, space, and engineering. The building's dramatic tilted-cube form is a Dallas architectural landmark.

1. Reunion Tower Geo-Deck
↳ The Reunion Tower Geo-Deck is a 470-foot outdoor observation platform on Dallas's most recognizable landmark — a geodesic sphere atop a 561-foot tower — with 360-degree panoramic views of the skyline, high-definition telescopes, and interactive touchscreens identifying landmarks across the horizon.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Dallas World Aquarium
↳ The Dallas World Aquarium occupies a converted 1913 warehouse with a five-story tropical rainforest atrium housing over 85,000 specimens. Manatees, orinoco crocodiles, jaguars, arapaima, and hundreds of free-flying tropical birds inhabit interconnected environments across multiple levels.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Pioneer Plaza
↳ Pioneer Plaza holds the world's largest bronze sculpture — 49 life-size longhorn cattle and 3 trail-riding cowboys across 4.2 landscaped acres with a waterfall. Sculptor Robert Summers made every steer at six feet high, commemorating the historic Shawnee Trail cattle drives.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 1428 Young St · Convention Center District
🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Klyde Warren Park
4. Klyde Warren Park
↳ Klyde Warren Park is a 5.2-acre public park built on a concrete deck over the Woodall Rodgers Freeway, connecting downtown Dallas to Uptown. The great lawn, children's garden, dog park, and food trucks anchor civic life at the foot of the Arts District.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 11:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 2012 Woodall Rodgers Fwy · Arts District

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Hall of State
1. Hall of State
↳ The Hall of State is the signature building of the 1936 Texas Centennial Exposition at Fair Park — an Art Deco masterpiece with a gilded Great Hall, bronze entry doors, and WPA murals documenting Texas history. It is a National Historic Landmark and the finest Art Deco building in the state.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Saturday 10:00am - 5:00pm · Sunday 1:00pm - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 3939 Grand Ave · Fair Park
🚶 4 min · 🚕 2 min → African American Museum
2. African American Museum
↳ The African American Museum, Dallas holds one of the largest African American fine art collections in the southern United States, spread across four wings of folk art, religious art, and contemporary works documenting African American experience and cultural heritage from enslavement through the civil rights era.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Friday 11:00am - 5:00pm · Saturday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Sunday & Monday
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 3536 Grand Ave · Fair Park
🚕 12 min → Dallas Arboretum and Botanical Garden
3. Dallas Arboretum and Botanical Garden
↳ The Dallas Arboretum covers 66 acres along the eastern shore of White Rock Lake, with 19 themed gardens including the eight-acre Lay Family Garden. The grounds are known for seasonal floral displays — tulips in spring, dahlias in fall — and sweeping views across the lake from the garden terraces.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Texas Barbecue
↳ Dallas-style barbecue features oak-smoked brisket sliced from the flat and the point, pork ribs, and jalapeño-cheddar sausage served on butcher paper with white bread and pickles.
📍 Pecan Lodge · Deep Ellum
Tex-Mex
↳ Dallas Tex-Mex traces a direct line from border cantinas — enchiladas smothered in chili gravy, puffy tacos, and frozen margaritas that the city claims to have invented.
📍 El Fenix · Uptown
Fletcher's Corny Dog
↳ The State Fair of Texas invented the corny dog — a hot dog on a stick dipped in cornmeal batter and deep-fried — and Fletcher's has made them at Fair Park since the fair's earliest decades.
📍 Fair Park
Frozen Margarita
↳ Dallas lays claim to the world's first frozen margarita machine, installed at Mariano Martinez's restaurant in 1971 — the original machine is now in the Smithsonian.
📍 Mariano's
