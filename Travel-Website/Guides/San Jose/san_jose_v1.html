1. Winchester Mystery House
↳ The Winchester Mystery House is a 160-room mansion built continuously from 1884 to 1922 by Sarah Winchester, heir to the Winchester rifle fortune, with no master architectural plan. The house is a California Historical Landmark and listed on the National Register of Historic Places; its anomalies include stairs that rise only 10 feet across 44 steps, doors that open onto sheer drops, and spider-web tracery windows produced by Tiffany's. The construction was motivated by Winchester's belief that building must never stop, resulting in a structure that has no logical internal organization.

2. San Jose Municipal Rose Garden
↳ The San Jose Municipal Rose Garden covers 5.5 acres in the city's historic Rose Garden neighborhood, with over 3,500 rose shrubs across 189 varieties. Designated America's Best Rose Garden by All-America Rose Selections in 2010, the garden also serves as an official AARS test site — receiving award-winning cultivars before public release anywhere else in the country. It was established in 1927 and is free and publicly accessible year-round.

3. Rosicrucian Egyptian Museum
↳ The Rosicrucian Egyptian Museum holds the largest collection of ancient Egyptian antiquities in the Western United States — more than 2,000 objects including mummies, canopic jars, scarabs, and an intact collection of funerary papyri. The campus buildings are the only museum structures in the world built in the style of Ancient Egyptian architecture, constructed during the height of 1920s Egyptomania. A full-scale replica rock-cut tomb modeled on the Beni Hasan tombs allows visitors to enter a replication of an actual excavation context.

4. The Tech Interactive
↳ The Tech Interactive is Silicon Valley's public science center, housed in a 1998 Ricardo Legorreta building in downtown San Jose. The museum directly engages the science and engineering systems shaping contemporary life — biotech, robotics, clean energy, genetics, and computing — and includes an IMAX Dome theater, one of the largest domed screens in the western United States. Exhibits include a BioTinkering Lab, robotics challenge floors, and interactive climate and computing installations.

🍮 Local Tastes
Pho
↳ San Jose has the largest Vietnamese diaspora community outside Vietnam; the pho served along Story Road and King Road in East San Jose is regionally diverse and among the most authentic in the country. Order broth-based soups with thin rice noodles, fresh herbs, and bean sprouts.
📍 Little Saigon · Story Rd · East Side
Com Tam (Broken Rice)
↳ A Vietnamese working-class staple — broken rice served with grilled pork chop, fried egg, and pickled daikon. San Jose's version, particularly from the Grand Century Mall food court, is considered definitive outside Vietnam.
📍 Grand Century Mall Food Court · 111 Story Rd · East Side
Street Tacos
↳ San Jose's large Mexican-American community runs some of the best taquerias in California; Alum Rock Ave in East San Jose is the center of the city's al pastor, carnitas, and birria tradition.
📍 Alum Rock Ave · East Side
