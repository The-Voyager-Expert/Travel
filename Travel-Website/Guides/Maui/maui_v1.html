Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 15min → Haleakalā National Park
1. Haleakalā National Park
↳ A 10,023-foot shield volcano where the summit reveals a vast caldera of red and black cinder cones — the same moonscape that trained Apollo astronauts. Sliding Sands Trail descends into the crater while Koʻolau Gap frames clouds pouring through sheer cliff walls in one of Hawaiʻi's most otherworldly panoramas.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~3 hr
⚠️ Summit Visitor Center closes 3:00pm daily.
⚠️ Sunrise visits require advance reservation — book 60 days out.
📍 Haleakalā Crater Rd · Kula
🎫 book at: recreation.gov
🚕 45 min → ʻIao Valley State Monument
2. ʻIao Valley State Monument
↳ A sacred 2,250-foot basalt spire rising from a lush stream valley — the ʻIao Needle is Maui's most iconic landmark, revered by Native Hawaiians and the site of the decisive 1790 Battle of Kepaniwai that united the Hawaiian Islands under Kamehameha I.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Temporarily closed through June 26 — bridge safety upgrades. Reopens June 27.
📍 ʻIao Valley Rd · Wailuku
🚕 30 min → Hoʻokipa Beach
3. Hoʻokipa Beach
↳ Maui's legendary windsurfing arena — the north shore's crescent bay frames year-round board sports action in world-class trade winds, and each evening Hawaiian green sea turtles haul out on the sand below the clifftop lookout.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Hana Hwy · Pāʻia
🚕 45 min → hotel

↳ A jet-black sand beach carved from ancient lava on the Road to Hana — volcanic sea arches, a freshwater lava tube cave, and a 12th-century Hawaiian heiau frame one of the world's most dramatically beautiful shorelines.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Mākena State Park
1. Mākena State Park
↳ Big Beach — Oneloa — is a half-mile sweep of coarse white sand backed by kiawe scrub and the lava slopes of Puʻu ʻŌlaʻi cinder cone. A short scramble leads to Little Beach, a sheltered cove for bodyboarding. The finest undeveloped coastline on Maui's south shore.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Mākena Alanui Rd · Mākena
🚕 10 min → La Pérouse Bay
2. La Pérouse Bay
↳ The southern end of Mākena Alanui Road terminates at a vast 18th-century lava field — the most recent volcanic flow on Maui, dating to roughly 1790. The bay beyond it holds some of the clearest water on the island, with spinner dolphins and dense reef fish in the submerged lava formations along the shore.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Mākena Alanui Rd · La Pérouse
🚕 20 min → Keawakapu Beach
3. Keawakapu Beach
↳ A wide crescent of golden sand between Wailea and Kīhei, less trafficked than the resort beaches to the south and fringed by kiawe trees. Snorkeling reefs anchor both ends of the bay; at the north end a small rocky point holds turtles. Sunsets here are unobstructed over the Kahoʻolawe silhouette.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 S Kīhei Rd · Kīhei
🚕 10 min → hotel

🌊 A Valley Named for the Dead, a Bay Named for the Vanished

ʻĪao Valley's 1790 battle left behind a name that still describes exactly what happened there. Kamehameha I's forces trapped Maui's defenders in the narrow valley and fought so ferociously that fallen warriors are said to have literally blocked the ʻĪao Stream — giving the battle its name, Kepaniwai, "the damming of the waters." Hawaiian accounts describe the river running red for days afterward. The victory here was decisive in Kamehameha's campaign to unite the islands under one ruler.

La Pérouse Bay carries the name of a man who came, left, and was never seen again. French navigator Jean-François de Galaup, comte de La Pérouse, anchored here in 1786 — the first European to ever set foot on Maui — then continued his Pacific expedition. Two years later his ships sailed out of Botany Bay, Australia, and vanished completely. Nothing was known of their fate for nearly forty years, until wreckage was finally traced to a reef in the Solomon Islands, where the ships had gone down in a storm and survivors had been killed onshore. The bay is the last confirmed place his expedition made friendly contact with anyone before it disappeared.
