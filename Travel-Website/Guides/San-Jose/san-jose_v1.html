🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Winchester Mystery House
1. Winchester Mystery House
↳ A 160-room Victorian mansion built from 1884 to 1922 with no master plan. California Historical Landmark and National Register property. Known for stairs that rise only 10 feet across 44 steps, doors opening onto sheer drops, and Tiffany spider-web windows.

2. San Jose Municipal Rose Garden
↳ Covers 5.5 acres with over 3,500 rose shrubs across 189 varieties. Designated America's Best Rose Garden by All-America Rose Selections in 2010 and an official AARS test site receiving award-winning cultivars before public release. Established 1927; free admission year-round.

3. Rosicrucian Egyptian Museum
↳ Holds the largest collection of ancient Egyptian antiquities in the Western United States — more than 2,000 objects including mummies, canopic jars, and funerary papyri. Buildings are the only museum structures in the world in Ancient Egyptian style. Full-scale replica rock-cut tomb based on the Beni Hasan tombs.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Alum Rock Park
1. Alum Rock Park
↳ California's oldest municipal park, established 1872. Covers 720 acres in the Diablo Range foothills with 13 miles of trails through Penitencia Creek canyon. From 1890 to 1932 it was a nationally known health spa with 27 mineral springs — the stone grottos that housed them line the canyon floor.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 15350 Penitencia Creek Rd · East Foothills
🚕 12 min → Japanese Friendship Garden
2. Japanese Friendship Garden
↳ A six-acre walled garden in Kelley Park, dedicated 1965 and modeled on Okayama's Korakuen Garden — one of Japan's Three Great Gardens. Three ponds at different elevations connected by streams and waterfalls, stocked with koi gifted from the sister city in 1966. Includes a tea house and moon bridge.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 1300 Senter Rd · Kelley Park
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → History Park
3. History Park
↳ A 14-acre open-air museum with 32 original and replica buildings recreating an early 1900s town. A half-scale replica of San Jose's 1881 Electric Light Tower rises 115 feet, and volunteers run vintage trolleys through the grounds.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 635 Phelan Ave · Kelley Park

4. Peralta Adobe-Fallon House Historic Site
↳ San Jose's oldest building, constructed 1797 by one of the city's founders. California Historical Landmark #866 and National Register property. The adjacent 1855 Thomas Fallon House is a fully furnished Victorian mansion with 15 period rooms.

🔍 The Myth and the Dig

The Winchester Mystery House's popular legend — that a grieving heiress was told by a Boston medium to keep building forever to appease the ghosts of everyone killed by Winchester rifles — didn't come from Sarah Winchester's own lifetime at all. Historians have traced the séance story to a book published decades after her death and found no record that the medium it names ever existed. The room tour guides now call the "séance room" was more likely just the gardener's quarters. Much of what visitors hear inside the house was shaped by the 1920s carnival operators who leased the property after Sarah died and needed a better story than "a wealthy widow renovated her house for decades" to sell tickets.

The museum next door exists because its founder believed he had personally been initiated into an ancient mystery tradition during a trip to France. Harvey Spencer Lewis went on to found the fraternal order that still owns the site, and began collecting Egyptian antiquities to support that belief — starting with a single small statue of the goddess Sekhmet. He didn't stop at buying pieces: in 1921 he helped fund real archaeological excavations at the ancient capital of Akhenaten, and after his own trip to Egypt later in the decade, the collection grew large enough to become the biggest gathering of Egyptian antiquities anywhere in the western United States.
