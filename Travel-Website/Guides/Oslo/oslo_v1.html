Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 20 min · 🚕 7 min → Oslo Opera House
1. Oslo Opera House
↳ Snøhetta's opera house rises from the Oslofjord with a marble-clad roof that slopes to the waterline — designed so anyone can walk up and across it. The angled white surfaces shift tone with the light. Home of the Norwegian National Opera and Ballet; free rooftop access any time.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Kirsten Flagstads plass 1 · Bjørvika
🚶 18 min · 🚕 7 min → Aker Brygge
2. Aker Brygge
↳ Oslo's converted 19th-century shipyard, now a waterfront promenade of glass-fronted restaurants, boutiques, and galleries along the inner Oslofjord. The view across to Akershus Fortress is one of the city's most iconic. Connects directly to Tjuvholmen and the Astrup Fearnley Museum.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Stranden 1 · Aker Brygge
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Astrup Fearnley Museum of Modern Art
3. Astrup Fearnley Museum of Modern Art
↳ Renzo Piano's waterfront museum on the Tjuvholmen peninsula holds one of Scandinavia's most significant private collections of post-war and contemporary art — Damien Hirst, Jeff Koons, Matthew Barney. The sail-like glass roof and the adjoining sculpture park visible from the fjord are as compelling as what's inside.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 30 min · 🚕 10 min → Vigeland Sculpture Park
1. Vigeland Sculpture Park
↳ Gustav Vigeland's life work — 212 bronze and granite sculptures spread across 80 acres of Frogner Park, all depicting the cycle of human life from birth to death. The Monolith, a 14-metre column of 121 intertwined figures, anchors the central plateau. The park is always open and always free.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Nobels gate 32 · Frogner
🚶 15 min · 🚕 8 min → Norwegian National Museum
2. Norwegian National Museum
↳ Europe's largest collection of Norwegian and Scandinavian art in a monumental building on the Aker Brygge waterfront. The permanent collection includes Munch's The Scream tempera version, Tidemand and Gude's Brudeferd i Hardanger, and the full span of Norwegian art from the 18th century to today.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Norwegian National Museum · Aker Brygge · 4.7⭐ · nasjonalmuseet.no
🏛️ Tuesday - Friday 10:00am - 8:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2h
📍 Brynjulf Bulls plass 3 · Aker Brygge
🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Akershus Fortress
3. Akershus Fortress
↳ The medieval castle and fortress complex that has guarded the Oslofjord since the 13th century. The grounds include Akershus Castle, the Norwegian Armed Forces Museum, and sweeping views across the fjord from the ramparts. The grounds are free to walk; the castle interior requires a separate ticket.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 25 min · 🚕 9 min → Grünerløkka
1. Grünerløkka
↳ Oslo's most characterful neighborhood — independent coffee shops, vinyl stores, vintage markets, galleries, and small restaurants fill the late-19th-century streets. The Akerselva runs its eastern edge. Birkelunden park is the social anchor in summer.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Thorvald Meyers gate · Grünerløkka
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Akerselva River Walk
2. Akerselva River Walk
↳ The Akerselva runs 8 km through Oslo on a continuous greenway path. The stretch through Grünerløkka past the old textile mills at Beyer bridge is the most scenic — waterfalls, brick factory buildings, and urban wildlife corridors all within walking distance of the centre.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Ankerbrua · Grünerløkka
🚶 20 min · 🚕 8 min → Oslo Botanical Garden
3. Oslo Botanical Garden
↳ The University of Oslo's botanical garden at Tøyen — Norway's oldest scientific garden. Summer brings the rock garden and alpine collection into full flower; the large glasshouses hold tropical and subtropical plants year-round. The Munch neighbourhood and Natural History Museum are next door.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1.5h
🆓 Free
📍 Sars gate 1 · Tøyen

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Holmenkollen Ski Jump
1. Holmenkollen Ski Jump
↳ The iconic ski jump tower above Oslo, rebuilt for the Ski World Championships. The observation deck at the top — 60 metres up, 450 metres above sea level — gives a panoramic view across Oslo to the Oslofjord. The ski museum beneath the jump is the world's oldest, tracing 4,000 years of skiing history.
📖 Wikipedia

2. MUNCH Museum
↳ The world's largest collection of Edvard Munch's work — over 26,000 paintings, drawings, prints, and watercolors in a cantilevered tower at Bjørvika. Spans Munch's full career. The oil and pastel versions of The Scream are here; the tempera hangs in the National Museum.

1. Fredrikstad Gamlebyen
↳ Scandinavia's best-preserved fortified old town. Bastioned walls, moat, and cobblestone streets sit on the west bank of the Glomma — connected to Fredrikstad station by a car ferry every ten minutes. Inside the walls: galleries, craft studios, a medieval church, and a café in the old prison.

2. Kongsten Fort
↳ A 17th-century outwork fort on Galgeberget hill above Gamlebyen, built as an advance warning position. The earthwork bastions and stone tower are well-preserved; the panorama at the top takes in the Glomma estuary, the outer Oslofjord, and Gamlebyen's full fortifications.

🍮 Local Tastes
Brunost
↳ Brown cheese — made by boiling whey until the sugars caramelize. Sweet, slightly fudgy, unmistakably Norwegian. Eaten on crispbread or waffles. Gudbrandsdalsost is the standard variety; Ekte Geitost is sharper.
📍 Mathallen · Vulkan
Vafler
↳ Norwegian heart-shaped waffles — thinner and softer than Belgian waffles, eaten warm with sour cream and strawberry jam or brunost. A staple at Norwegian mountain lodges and fjord cafés.
📍 Kaffistova · Sentrum
Smørebrød
↳ Open-faced rye bread sandwiches — the Norwegian version of the Scandinavian smørrebrød tradition. Common toppings: roast beef with remoulade, cured meats, eggs with anchovies. Lunch staple in Oslo's traditional cafés.
📍 Grand Café · Karl Johans gate
Gravlaks
↳ Cured salmon — salt, sugar, dill, and sometimes aquavit cured for 48 hours. Thinly sliced and served with mustard-dill sauce on dark rye. One of Norway's oldest preparations, served everywhere from grand hotel cafés to street kiosks.
📍 Mathallen · Vulkan
Aquavit
↳ Norway's national spirit — distilled from grain or potato, flavored with caraway or dill, then barrel-aged. Linie Aquavit barrels cross the equator twice by ship. Served cold; accompanies smørebrød and festive meals.
📍 Theatercaféen · City Centre
