Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 19 min · 🚕 4 min → Haga
1. Haga
↳ Gothenburg’s oldest suburb, laid out in 1648 and now a cobblestoned quarter of timber-and-stone landshövdingehus lined with cafés, antikvariat and craft shops. Pedestrian Haga Nygata is the spine of the stroll.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Haga Nygata · Haga
🚶 5 min · 🚕 1 min → Skansen Kronan
2. Skansen Kronan
↳ A squat octagonal crown-fortress of 1698 on the Risåsberget hill above Haga, built to repel a Danish attack that never came. The grassy ramparts give a free 360° panorama over the rooftops, harbour and cranes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Skansberget · Haga
🚶 21 min · 🚕 4 min → Garden Society of Gothenburg
3. Garden Society of Gothenburg
↳ One of northern Europe’s best-preserved 19th-century parks, laid out in 1842 beside the moat. The 1878 cast-iron Palm House shelters Mediterranean and tropical plants, and the Rose Garden runs to some 4,000 bushes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Slussgatan 1 · Centrum
🚶 10 min · 🚕 3 min → Götaplatsen
4. Götaplatsen
↳ The monumental cultural square crowning Kungsportsavenyn, framed by the Concert Hall, City Theatre and Museum of Art. At its centre stands Carl Milles’ bronze Poseidon of 1931, the unofficial emblem of the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min

1. Gothenburg Botanical Garden
↳ One of Europe’s largest botanical gardens, with some 16,000 species across 175 hectares of arboretum, valleys and a Rock Garden the Michelin Green Guide rates two stars. Admission to the grounds is voluntary.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Carl Skottsbergs gata 22A · Änggården
🚶 15 min · 🚕 3 min → Slottsskogen
2. Slottsskogen
↳ Gothenburg’s great green lung, an English-style landscape park opened in 1874 with open meadows, ponds, free-roaming Nordic farm animals and the blazing Azalea Valley in late spring. Entry is free.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Slottsskogen · Linné

3. Liseberg
↳ Scandinavia’s most-visited amusement park, open since 1923. Wooden coaster Balder and the Helix launch coaster headline, but the flower-lined avenues, fountains and old-world charm are half the draw.

⚓ Notes from the Harbour City

Gothenburg was laid out by Dutch engineers in the 1620s, and the bones still show: the moat you glide along on the Paddan boat, the grid of canals, the street names ending in -hamn, the old word for harbour. Poseidon up on Götaplatsen does not face the city — he faces west, out toward the sea that built the place. Once you notice it, the whole town tilts toward the water.

The other thread is wood. Haga’s landshövdingehus — stone ground floor, two timber storeys above — were a clever dodge around a 19th-century fire law, and that same humane, low-rise scale carries straight into Slottsskogen and the Garden Society. For a shipping and industrial city, Gothenburg keeps choosing the garden over the tower.
