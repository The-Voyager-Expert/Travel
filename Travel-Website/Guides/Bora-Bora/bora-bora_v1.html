1. Mount Otemanu Viewpoint
↳ The Fitiiu Point lookout frames Mount Otemanu, the extinct basalt plug rising 727 meters at the center of Bora Bora. The summit requires technical gear, but this accessible viewpoint captures the full volcanic silhouette against the encircling reef and open Pacific.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr

🍮 Local Tastes
Poisson cru
French Polynesia's national dish: raw tuna cubed and marinated in fresh lime juice, then finished in coconut milk with cucumber, tomato, and spring onion. Found at every restaurant on the island.
📍 Local restaurants · Vaitape
Poê
A baked pudding made from ripe banana or papaya mixed with arrowroot starch, sweetened and cooked until firm. A traditional Polynesian dessert served warm or at room temperature.
📍 Local restaurants · Vaitape
Ma'a Tinito
A slow-cooked one-pot stew of pork, kidney beans, and vegetables influenced by the Chinese immigrant community in the Society Islands. A staple at local family gatherings and casual restaurants.
📍 Local restaurants · Vaitape
Fafaru
Raw fish soaked in seawater with fermented sea urchin and left to cure for several days. A deeply traditional delicacy with a strong fermented flavor, found at local gatherings.
📍 Local gatherings · Vaitape area

✨ Claude Inspiration

Bora Bora's reputation as the world's most beautiful island rests on a specific geography: an extinct basalt volcano ringed by a shallow lagoon of extraordinary clarity, enclosed within a coral reef. That geometry — mountain, lagoon, reef, open Pacific — is visible from every point on the island and from the air above it. The Conrad on Motu To'opua sits inside the lagoon itself, making the reef and the mountain the view from every direction. Three days is enough to see the lagoon from its surface, from a 4×4 on the ridgeline above it, and from the air above it.
