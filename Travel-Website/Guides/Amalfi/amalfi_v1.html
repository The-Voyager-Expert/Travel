Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Ancient Arsenal of the Maritime Republic
1. Ancient Arsenal of the Maritime Republic
↳ The medieval shipyard where the Maritime Republic of Amalfi built and repaired its war galleys. Two surviving stone aisles of pointed Arab-influenced arches and ten pillars now hold the Museo della Bussola e del Ducato Marinaro, tracing the city's seafaring heyday when it rivalled Venice, Pisa and Genoa.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Amalfi Cathedral & Cloister of Paradise
↳ The Duomo di Sant'Andrea crowns a dramatic 62-step staircase above the piazza, its striped Arab-Norman facade ablaze with golden mosaic. Inside are bronze doors cast in Constantinople before 1066, the baroque crypt of St Andrew, and the serene 13th-century Cloister of Paradise with its interlaced Moorish arches.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Museo della Carta
↳ A 13th-century paper mill in the Valle dei Mulini, still making Amalfi's famous cotton-rag bambagina paper on original water-powered vats and hammers fed by the Canneto stream. The Milano family's working museum shows how the town built a second fortune on handmade paper.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Via delle Cartiere 23 · Valle dei Mulini
🚶 6 min · 🚕 4 min → Valle delle Ferriere
4. Valle delle Ferriere
↳ A lush state nature reserve climbing the gorge above the paper mills, where the Canneto stream tumbles in waterfalls past ruined ironworks and ancient mills. The sheltered valley shelters the giant fern Woodwardia radicans — a pre-glacial relict found almost nowhere else in Italy.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~3 hr
📍 Trailhead at Piazza Flavio Gioia · Centro Storico

Day 2

1. Grotta dello Smeraldo
↳ An emerald sea cave 7 km west of town, reachable by boat from the pier or by descending 450 steps from the coastal road. Inside, sunlight filtering through a submerged opening turns the sea floor a luminous jade green; a rowboat circuits the cave past a ceramic Nativity scene lowered here in 1956.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Atrani
↳ Italy's smallest municipality by area, pressed into a limestone cleft a 15-minute walk east of town. Medieval lanes climb past the domed Santa Maria Maddalena, the tiny Piazza Umberto I with its fountain, and a sandy beach where local families swim — entirely off the coach-tour circuit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr

3. Villa Rufolo
↳ A 13th-century Arab-Norman estate built by the Rufolo merchant family — tower, cloister, and terraced gardens spilling 350 metres down to the Tyrrhenian. Wagner set Parsifal's enchanted garden here in 1880; the same stage hosts the Ravello Festival's open-air concerts each July and August.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Piazza Duomo · Ravello
🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min → Villa Cimbrone
4. Villa Cimbrone
↳ A medieval estate assembled in the early 1900s by English aristocrat Lord Grimthorpe, its 11-acre garden of hedges, temples, and pergolas ending at the Belvedere of Infinity — a terrace of marble busts at the cliff's edge with a panorama Gore Vidal called the finest view in the world.
📖 Wikipedia

🍋 The Town That Ran on Water

Three of today's stops tell one quiet story: water. The same Canneto stream that still turns the hammers at the Museo della Carta once powered dozens of paper mills up the Valle dei Mulini — and it begins higher still in the Valle delle Ferriere, where it feeds the waterfalls and keeps the giant ferns alive. Amalfi traded on the sea, but it ran on the mountain water pouring down behind it.

And the Arsenal is the coast's quiet giant. A maritime republic that once rivalled Venice, Pisa and Genoa is now reduced to two stone aisles and ten pillars at the water's edge. Step inside, and the pretty cluster of lemon-stepped lanes you walked through briefly becomes the capital of a Mediterranean power.
