Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 45min → Subterranean River
1. Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park
↳ UNESCO World Heritage Site and New Seven Wonders of Nature finalist. Park guides paddle visitors by boat through 8.2 km of underground river inside a limestone karst mountain above a navigable underground lagoon.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 3:30pm
⏰ ~4 hr
📍 Sabang Wharf · Sabang
🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Sabang Mangrove Paddle Boat
2. Sabang Mangrove Paddle Boat Tour
↳ Local boatmen paddle small outriggers through dense mangrove channels lining the Sabang shoreline. Monitor lizards, kingfishers, and macaque monkeys are visible at close range from the water.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Sabang Wharf · Sabang
🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Sabang Beach
3. Sabang Beach
↳ A jungle-fringed black-sand beach at the foot of forested cliffs above St. Paul Bay. Swimmers enter calm shallow water sheltered by headlands. Basic food stalls and hammocks line the shore.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Sabang Beach · Sabang
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Sabang X Zipline
4. Sabang X Zipline
↳ An 800-meter cable crossing from a hilltop platform above Sabang Beach to a tower near the wharf, with the South China Sea and jungle canopy below. Riders launch seated; return is by forest trail.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Santa Lourdes Pier
1. Honda Bay — Pambato Reef
↳ A protected coral reef in Honda Bay where sea turtles, reef fish, and coral formations are visible in 2 to 5 meters of clear water. The first snorkeling stop on the Honda Bay island-hopping circuit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Santa Lourdes Pier · Honda Bay
🚢 25 min → Luli Island
2. Honda Bay — Luli Island
↳ A narrow white sandbar in Honda Bay that disappears at high tide. At low tide the bar stretches roughly 100 meters surrounded by turquoise shallows. The name translates as "sinking and emerging."
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Luli Island · Honda Bay
🚕 40 min → Baker's Hill
3. Baker's Hill — Puerto Princesa
↳ A hilltop park and bakery complex southwest of the city center, covered with life-sized fairy-tale sculptures and themed gardens. The grounds include a popular pie shop selling pineapple and ube varieties and a viewpoint overlooking Puerto Princesa Bay.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Bgy. San Pedro · Puerto Princesa
🚕 30 min → Iwahig River
4. Iwahig River Firefly Watching
↳ An evening paddle along the Iwahig River through mangroves colonized by thousands of synchronously flashing fireflies. Guides row small outrigger boats in silence as both banks pulse with light between 7:00pm and 9:00pm.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00pm - 10:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Advance booking recommended; best on moonless nights
📍 Iwahig River · Barangay Iwahig
🚕 35 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h → Ugong Rock
1. Ugong Rock Adventures — Puerto Princesa
↳ A limestone cave system where visitors spelunk through narrow passages fitted with rope guides. The cave exits onto a platform from which a face-down zipline drops riders down the rock face to a landing pad below.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Ugong Rock Adventures
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 4:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Barangay Tagabinet · Puerto Princesa
🚕 35 min → Wildlife Rescue Center
2. Palawan Wildlife Rescue and Conservation Center
↳ The only Philippine crocodile captive breeding facility open to visitors, housing over 900 crocodiles in open enclosures visible from elevated walkways. The center also holds bearcat, monitor lizards, and native birds rescued from poaching.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Palawan Wildlife Rescue and Conservation Center
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 4:45pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Barangay Irawan · Puerto Princesa
🚕 20 min → Plaza Cuartel
3. Palawan Massacre Memorial — Plaza Cuartel
↳ A WWII memorial marking the site of the 1944 Palawan Massacre, where 139 American prisoners of war were killed by Japanese forces. The original garrison wall ruins remain standing with plaques and statues documenting the event around an open plaza.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Taft Street · City Proper
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Immaculate Conception Cathedral
4. Immaculate Conception Cathedral
↳ The seat of the Roman Catholic Diocese of Puerto Princesa, a twin-towered Spanish colonial church on Rizal Avenue rebuilt after WWII. The white neoclassical facade overlooks the main city plaza and Jose Rizal monument on the central boulevard.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Rizal Avenue · City Proper
🚕 15 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Chao Long
↳ A Vietnamese-Filipino beef noodle soup unique to Puerto Princesa. Vietnamese immigrants brought the pho-style technique; locals adapted it into a sweeter heartier broth with thick rice noodles and large meat cuts.
📍 Bona's Chaolong · Pham Chaolong
Tamilok
↳ A saltwater woodworm harvested from rotting mangrove wood, eaten raw with vinegar, calamansi, and chili. Texture resembles a large oyster. Found only in Palawan.
📍 Kinabuch's Grill and Bar
Crocodile Sisig
↳ A Palawan twist on Filipino sizzling pork sisig made with crocodile meat from the city's licensed farm. Mild and firm, served chopped and sizzling on a cast-iron plate with calamansi and chili.
📍 Kinabuch's Grill and Bar
