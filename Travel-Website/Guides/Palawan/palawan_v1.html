Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 105 min → Underground River
1. Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park
↳ UNESCO World Heritage site and New Seven Wonders of Nature. Park guides paddle visitors by boat through 8.2 km of underground river inside a limestone mountain, past cathedral-sized chambers and massive stalactite formations. The cave entrance is reached by a 15-minute boat ride from Sabang Wharf.

2. Sabang Mangrove Paddle Boat Tour
↳ Local boatmen paddle small outrigger boats through dense mangrove channels lining the Sabang shoreline. The route covers roughly 1 km of shaded waterways where monitor lizards, kingfishers, and macaque monkeys are visible along the banks at close range.

3. Sabang Beach
↳ A jungle-fringed black-sand beach at the foot of the forested cliffs surrounding St. Paul Bay. The beach is the staging point for all Sabang activities and has basic food stalls and hammocks. Swimmers enter calm, shallow water sheltered by the surrounding headlands.

4. Sabang X Zipline
↳ An 800-meter cable crossing from a hilltop platform to a tower above Sabang Beach, with the South China Sea and jungle canopy spread below. Riders are harnessed in pairs and launched in a seated position. The return to the beach is by trail.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Santa Lourdes Pier
1. Pambato Reef Marine Sanctuary
↳ A protected coral reef in Honda Bay with shallow gardens accessible to snorkelers. Sea turtles, reef fish, and coral formations are visible in 2–5 meters of clear water. The sanctuary is the first snorkeling stop on the Honda Bay island-hopping circuit and requires a marine sanctuary entrance fee.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟 Walk-in · boat from Santa Lourdes Pier
🏛️ Daily 7:00am – 4:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Santa Lourdes Pier · Honda Bay
🚢 15 min → Starfish Island
2. Starfish Island
↳ A white-sand beach island in Honda Bay famous for the dense population of blue starfish visible in the shallow seagrass beds just offshore. Visitors wade in knee-deep water among hundreds of starfish. The island has a beachfront picnic area and is the lunch stop on most island-hopping packages.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟 Walk-in · boat from Pambato Reef
🏛️ Daily 7:00am – 4:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Starfish Island · Honda Bay
🚢 10 min → Luli Island
3. Luli Island
↳ A narrow white sandbar in Honda Bay that disappears entirely at high tide, leaving only a row of palm trees standing in open water. At low tide, the bar stretches roughly 100 meters and is surrounded by turquoise shallows. The name is shorthand for "lulubog, lilitaw" — sinking and emerging.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟 Walk-in · boat from Starfish Island
🏛️ Daily 7:00am – 4:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Luli Island · Honda Bay
🚕 45 min → Iwahig River
4. Iwahig River Firefly Watching Cruise
↳ An evening paddle along the Iwahig River after dark, through mangroves colonized by thousands of synchronously flashing fireflies. Guides row small outrigger boats in silence as the trees pulse with light on both banks. The effect is strongest between 7:00pm and 9:00pm on moonless nights.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 60 min → Ugong Rock
1. Ugong Rock Adventures
↳ A limestone cave system in Barangay Tagabinet where visitors spelunk through narrow passages fitted with rope guides. The cave exits onto a platform from which a Superman-style zipline drops riders face-down and horizontal down the rock face to a landing pad below. No special equipment needed — helmets provided at the site.
📖 Wikipedia
📅 Ugong Rock Adventures · Walk-in at the site
🕐 8:00am · ⏳ 2 hr · 👥 Small group
🏛️ Daily 8:00am – 4:00pm
📍 Barangay Tagabinet · Puerto Princesa
🚕 35 min → Wildlife Center
2. Palawan Wildlife Rescue and Conservation Center
↳ The only Philippine crocodile captive breeding facility in the country, housing over 900 crocodiles in open enclosures visible from elevated walkways. The center also holds bearcat (binturong), monitor lizards, and native birds rescued from poaching. A farm area sells crocodile products from legally farmed animals.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟 Walk-in · Palawan Wildlife Rescue and Conservation Center
🏛️ Daily 8:00am – 4:45pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Barangay Irawan · Puerto Princesa
🚕 25 min → Baker's Hill
3. Baker's Hill
↳ A hilltop park and bakery complex southwest of the city center, covered with life-sized fairy-tale sculptures and themed gardens across rolling terrain. The grounds include a popular pie shop (pineapple and ube varieties) and a viewpoint overlooking Puerto Princesa Bay and the Palawan countryside.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟 Free entry · Baker's Hill
🏛️ Daily 8:00am – 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Bgy. San Pedro · Puerto Princesa
🚕 10 min → Palawan Butterfly Eco-Garden
4. Palawan Butterfly Eco-Garden and Tribal Village
↳ A screened garden housing over 150 butterfly species in free flight, including the Palawan birdwing — one of the largest butterflies in the Philippines. An adjacent tribal village section displays artifacts, tools, and a reconstruction of a Batak indigenous dwelling. The garden also maintains a small orchid collection.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Chao Long
↳ A Vietnamese-Filipino beef noodle soup unique to Puerto Princesa. Vietnamese immigrants brought the pho-style technique; locals adapted it into a sweeter, heartier broth with thick rice noodles and large meat cuts. Eaten all day across the city.
📍 Bona's Chaolong · Pham Chaolong · Lou Chao Long Hauz
Tamilok
↳ A saltwater woodworm harvested from rotting mangrove wood, eaten raw with vinegar, calamansi, and chili. Texture resembles a large oyster. Found only in Palawan; the city's most distinctive exotic delicacy.
📍 Kinabuch's Grill and Bar
Crocodile Sisig
↳ A Palawan twist on the classic Filipino sizzling pork sisig — made with crocodile meat from the city's licensed farm. Mild and firm, served chopped and sizzling on a cast-iron plate with calamansi and chili.
📍 Kinabuch's Grill and Bar
