Day 1 · 🌊 Sabang Underground River
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 105 min → Underground River
1. Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park
↳ UNESCO World Heritage site and New Seven Wonders of Nature. Park guides paddle visitors through 8.2 km of underground river inside a limestone mountain, past cathedral-sized chambers and massive stalactite formations. The boat departs from Sabang Wharf, a short walk from the visitor center.

2. Sabang Mangrove Paddle Boat Tour
↳ A guided paddle through dense mangrove channels that line the shoreline near Sabang. Local boatmen navigate narrow waterways teeming with wildlife — monitor lizards, kingfishers, and macaque monkeys visible along the banks. The trip covers roughly 1 km of the mangrove interior.

Day 2 · 🐠 Honda Bay & Iwahig Fireflies
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Honda Bay
4. Honda Bay Island Hopping
↳ A full-morning boat trip across Honda Bay's turquoise waters, stopping at three islands. Pambato Reef Marine Sanctuary is the snorkeling highlight — shallow coral gardens with sea turtles and reef fish. Starfish Island has dense starfish beds in clear shallows. Luli Island's sandbar disappears at high tide.

🚕 45 min → Iwahig River Firefly Watching
5. Iwahig River Firefly Watching Cruise
↳ An evening paddle along the Iwahig River after dark, where mangrove trees lining the banks are colonized by thousands of synchronously flashing fireflies. Guides row small outrigger boats in silence through the darkness. The effect is strongest on moonless nights.

Day 3 · 🧗 Ugong Rock & Wildlife Center
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 60 min → Ugong Rock
6. Ugong Rock Adventures
↳ A limestone cave system in Barangay Tagabinet where visitors spelunk through narrow passages fitted with rope guides — no special equipment needed. The cave exits onto a platform from which a Superman-style zipline (face-down, horizontal position) drops riders down the rock face to the base below.
📅 Walk-in at Ugong Rock
🏛 Daily 8:00am – 4:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Barangay Tagabinet · Puerto Princesa
🚕 35 min → Palawan Wildlife Rescue Center
7. Palawan Wildlife Rescue and Conservation Center
↳ The only Philippine crocodile captive breeding facility in the country, housing over 900 crocodiles in open enclosures visible from elevated walkways. The center also holds bearcat (binturong), monitor lizards, and native birds rescued from poaching. A farm area sells crocodile products made from legally farmed animals.

🍮 Local Tastes
Chao Long
↳ A Vietnamese-Filipino beef noodle soup unique to Puerto Princesa. Vietnamese immigrants brought the pho-style technique; locals adapted it into a sweeter, heartier broth with thick rice noodles and large meat cuts. Eaten all day across the city.
📍 Bona's Chaolong · Pham Chaolong · Lou Chao Long Hauz
Tamilok
↳ A saltwater woodworm harvested from rotting mangrove wood, eaten raw with vinegar, calamansi, and chili. The texture resembles a large oyster. Found only in Palawan, it is the city's most distinctive exotic delicacy.
📍 Kinabuch's Grill and Bar
Crocodile Sisig
↳ A Palawan twist on the classic Filipino sizzling pork sisig — made here with crocodile meat from the city's licensed farm. The meat is mild and firm, served chopped and sizzling on a cast-iron plate with calamansi and chili on the side.
📍 Kinabuch's Grill and Bar
