Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Nauset Light Beach
1. Nauset Light Beach
↳ A dramatic cliff-backed beach anchored by the historic red-and-white striped Nauset Light, one of the most photographed lighthouses in America. The 1923 lighthouse towers 120 feet above the sand, and the wide beach stretches for two miles along the Atlantic side of Cape Cod National Seashore.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open year-round, sunrise to sunset
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 120 Nauset Light Road · Eastham
🚕 8 min → Cape Cod National Seashore
2. Cape Cod National Seashore
↳ A sprawling 43,000-acre coastal preserve protecting 40 miles of pristine Atlantic beaches and dunes. The seashore encompasses six major beaches, pitch-pine forests, freshwater ponds, salt marshes, and a network of walking trails. The landscape shifts from windswept dunes and beach plum thickets to sheltered maritime woodlands.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Cape Cod National Seashore · 4.8⭐ · nps.gov
🏛️ Beaches open year-round, dawn to dusk
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
💰 $25 vehicle pass
📍 Multiple beaches · Outer Cape
🚕 6 min → Marconi Beach
3. Marconi Beach
↳ A stunning oceanfront bluff location with dramatic views, this beach marks the historic site of the Marconi wireless telegraph station that operated 1903 to 1917. The wide sandy beach and one of Cape Cod's most accessible National Seashore parking lots make it an easy stop along the outer cape.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open year-round, sunrise to sunset
⏰ ~1 hr 15 min
📍 Marconi Road · Wellfleet
🚕 20 min → Herring Cove Beach
4. Herring Cove Beach
↳ A wide, crescent-shaped beach on the western edge of Provincetown, famous for its spectacular sunsets over Cape Cod Bay. The warm, sheltered waters are ideal for swimming, and the open beach offers excellent views of sailing vessels and fishing boats coming into Provincetown Harbor.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Sunrise to sunset, year-round
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Optimal at sunset · arrive 45 min before for viewing
📍 Herring Cove Beach · Provincetown
🚕 5 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Cape Cod Maritime Museum
1. Cape Cod Maritime Museum
↳ A museum dedicated to Cape Cod's maritime heritage, centered on three centuries of sailing ships, fishing fleets, and whaling expeditions. The collection includes scrimshaw art, ship models, navigational instruments, and artifacts from the packet ships and clippers that made Cape Cod a center of global commerce.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Cape Cod Maritime Museum · 4.6⭐ · capecodmaritimemuseum.org
🏛️ Tuesday - Saturday 10:00am - 4:00pm
🏛️ Sunday 12:00pm - 4:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 135 South Street · Hyannis
🚕 10 min → Chatham Lighthouse
2. Chatham Lighthouse
↳ A striking pair of red-brick lighthouse towers stands on a dramatic ocean bluff in Chatham. Built in 1808, the twin lights have guided ships around the treacherous Chatham Bars for over two centuries. The site offers sweeping views of the Atlantic and excellent opportunities to spot gray seals basking on the rocks below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Viewing year-round, 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 70 Main Street · Chatham
🚶 3 min · 🚕 1 min → Chatham Main Street
3. Chatham Main Street
↳ The heart of Chatham village is a charming tree-lined street lined with galleries, antique shops, bookstores, and restaurants occupying historic buildings. The atmosphere captures the essence of a classic Cape Cod town, with water views and a relaxed pace that invites lingering exploration and conversation.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Shops and galleries open daily, 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Main Street · Chatham
🚕 5 min → Skaket Beach
4. Skaket Beach
↳ Facing westward across Cape Cod Bay, Skaket Beach is renowned as the best sunset beach on Cape Cod. At low tide, the bay recedes to reveal a quarter-mile of exposed sand, creating the perfect expanse for exploration. The warm, calm bay waters and sandy bottom make it ideal for swimming and families.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Sunrise to sunset, year-round
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Optimal at sunset · arrive 45 min before for viewing
📍 Skaket Road · Orleans
🚕 15 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 30 min → Sandwich Glass Museum
1. Sandwich Glass Museum
↳ A museum celebrating the exquisite art and history of Sandwich glass, the distinctive pressed glass manufactured in this Cape Cod town from 1825 until 1888. The collection spans 6,000 objects, from delicate dinner services to ornate vases and candlesticks, and live glassblowing demonstrations show artisans creating pieces in the traditional method.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Sandwich Glass Museum · 4.7⭐ · sandwichglassmuseum.com
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 129 Main Street · Sandwich
🚕 3 min → Heritage Museums & Gardens
2. Heritage Museums & Gardens
↳ Spread across 100 acres of beautifully landscaped grounds, this museum complex combines exceptional gardens with diverse art and cultural exhibits. Highlights include a restored 1908 carousel, an American automobile collection, military artifacts, Wampanoag heritage exhibits, and rotating contemporary art shows.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Heritage Museums & Gardens · 4.6⭐ · heritagemuseumsandgardens.org
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~3 hr
📍 67 Grove Street · Sandwich
🚕 15 min → Wellfleet Bay Wildlife Sanctuary
3. Wellfleet Bay Wildlife Sanctuary
↳ A Mass Audubon sanctuary spanning 1,183 acres, Wellfleet Bay protects diverse ecosystems including salt marshes, Goose Pond freshwater areas, sandy barrier beach, and pitch-pine maritime forests. Five miles of hiking trails provide access for wildlife observation and bird-watching, with over 309 bird species documented at the sanctuary.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Saturday 9:00am - 4:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Wellfleet · Outer Cape
🚕 10 min → Eastham Windmill
4. Eastham Windmill
↳ The only remaining post-mill of its kind on Cape Cod, this historic windmill was built in Plymouth in 1680 and moved to Eastham in 1793. The authentic structure rotates on a central post to catch the wind, and interior tours during summer months explain the traditional grain-grinding process that sustained the town's early settlers.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ July - August 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Town Green · Eastham
