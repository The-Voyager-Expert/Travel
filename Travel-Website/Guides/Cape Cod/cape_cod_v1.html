2. Cape Cod National Seashore
↳ 43,000-acre coastal preserve protecting 40 miles of unspoiled Atlantic beaches. Six major beaches, pitch-pine forests, ponds, and marshes form a rare intact coastal ecosystem.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ National Seashore · 4.8⭐ · nps.gov
🏛️ Daily, dawn to dusk
⏰ ~2 hr 30 min
📍 Outer Cape
🚕 6 min → Marconi Beach
3. Marconi Beach
↳ High oceanfront bluffs frame this beach marking the historic Marconi wireless telegraph station site from 1903–1917. Wide sandy beach and large parking lot provide easy access.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily, sunrise to sunset
⏰ ~1 hr 15 min

5. Cape Cod Maritime Museum
↳ Waterfront museum in Hyannis displays maritime history, ship models, whaling artifacts, and trade-vessel exhibits spanning three centuries of Cape Cod's seafaring past.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Maritime Museum · capecodmaritimemuseum.org
🏛️ Daily 10am–5pm
⏰ ~1 hr 45 min
📍 Hyannis
🚕 8 min → Chatham Light
6. Chatham Light
↳ Iconic 52-foot red-and-white twin lighthouses mark the southeastern tip of Cape Cod. Built 1841–1878, they stand on dramatic ocean bluffs with sweeping Atlantic views and accessible beach access nearby.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily, sunrise to sunset
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Chatham

↳ Historic downtown lined with galleries, shops, and restaurants overlooking the scenic Chatham Harbor. Waterfront park benches offer views of fishing boats and sailboats.

10. Wellfleet Bay Wildlife Sanctuary
↳ Massachusetts Audubon sanctuary protecting 1,000 acres of marshes, pine forests, and tidal flats. Walking trails and an observation tower provide opportunities for bird watching and nature study.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily, dawn to dusk

12. Eastham Windmill
↳ 1680 Dutch-style windmill is the oldest industrial structure on Cape Cod. Restored and open for tours, it represents the region's colonial grain-milling heritage and maritime economy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily, varies by season
⏰ ~45 min
