Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Piazza Maggiore
1. Piazza Maggiore
↳ Bologna's monumental open-air living room, ringed by the Palazzo d'Accursio, the medieval Palazzo del Podestà and the Palazzo dei Banchi. At its northern edge stands Giambologna's bronze Neptune, trident raised over the city for more than four centuries.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Piazza Maggiore · Historic Center
🚶 3 min · 🚕 5 min → Basilica di San Petronio
2. Basilica di San Petronio
↳ The sixth-largest church in Europe, begun in the 14th century and never finished — its façade is famously half pink-and-white marble, half bare brick. Inside runs Cassini's brass meridian line, a 67-metre floor sundial that was once the most accurate in the world.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 Piazza Maggiore · Historic Center
🚶 3 min · 🚕 1 min → Archiginnasio
3. Archiginnasio
↳ The 16th-century seat of Europe's oldest university, its courtyard and staircases blanketed with thousands of painted student coats of arms. Upstairs hides the spruce-wood Anatomical Theatre — tiered benches, a marble dissection slab, and statues of flayed figures keeping watch.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~45 min

🚶 5 min · 🚕 11 min → Santa Maria della Vita
4. Santa Maria della Vita
↳ A small Baroque church hiding one of Italy's most arresting sculptures: Niccolò dell'Arca's Compianto sul Cristo Morto, a group of seven life-sized terracotta mourners frozen mid-scream encircling the dead Christ — D'Annunzio called it "a scream in stone."
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Compianto · 4.5⭐ · genusbononiae.it

5. Basilica di Santo Stefano
↳ The "Seven Churches" — a labyrinth of interlocking chapels, courtyards and cloisters layered up from the 5th century around a copy of Jerusalem's Holy Sepulchre. The most atmospheric corner of Bologna, opening onto one of its loveliest squares.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 6 min → Basilica di San Domenico
1. Basilica di San Domenico
↳ The mother church of the Dominican order, holding the magnificent Arca di San Domenico — a marble tomb worked over two centuries by Nicola Pisano, Niccolò dell'Arca and a young Michelangelo, who carved the angel and two saints here at the close of the 15th century.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 12:00pm · 3:30pm - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 Piazza San Domenico 13 · Historic Center
🚶 14 min · 🚕 7 min → Basilica di San Francesco
2. Basilica di San Francesco
↳ Bologna's great French-Gothic basilica — soaring flying buttresses rare for an Italian church, a carved marble high altar, and twin brick bell towers rising over a quiet square. One of the finest Gothic churches in northern Italy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:30am - 12:00pm · 3:00pm - 7:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Piazza Malpighi 9 · Historic Center
🚶 15 min · 🚕 3 min → Portico di San Luca
3. Portico di San Luca
↳ The longest covered walkway on earth — 3.8 km and 666 arches climbing from the city gate up Colle della Guardia. Built to shelter the annual procession of the Madonna's icon, it is now part of Bologna's UNESCO World Heritage porticoes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Porta Saragozza · Saragozza
🚕 9 min → Santuario della Madonna di San Luca
4. Santuario della Madonna di San Luca
↳ The domed hilltop basilica at the portico's end, 300 m above the plain, cradling a venerated Byzantine icon of the Madonna. Its terrace delivers Bologna's finest panorama — red rooftops spilling out toward the Apennines.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 Via di San Luca 36 · Colle della Guardia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min → Le Due Torri
1. Le Due Torri
↳ Two medieval towers by rival noble families at Piazza di Porta Ravegnana. Torre degli Asinelli stands 97.2 m tall with 498 steps to the top and requires timed-entry tickets. Torre Garisenda, the shorter leaning companion at 48 m, is fenced off and closed to the public.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ Torre degli Asinelli: 498 steps · timed tickets required · book in advance
📍 Piazza di Porta Ravegnana · Historic Center
🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Quadrilatero
2. Quadrilatero
↳ The medieval food market district just east of Piazza Maggiore — a grid of narrow lanes lined with fishmongers, cheese shops, butchers and salumi stalls operating under the same porticoes for centuries. The street vendors remain the commercial core of the district.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Saturday 7:00am - 1:00pm
🚫 Closed Sunday
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Via Pescherie Vecchie · Historic Center
🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Pinacoteca Nazionale di Bologna
3. Pinacoteca Nazionale di Bologna
↳ Bologna's principal art museum, housed in a former Jesuit novitiate on Via delle Belle Arti near the university quarter. The collection spans 14th- to 17th-century Italian painting with particular depth in Bolognese works — Vitale da Bologna, Francia, Perugino, Raphael, and the Carracci among the holdings.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:00am - 7:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Via delle Belle Arti 56 · University Quarter
🚶 15 min · 🚕 6 min → MAMbo
4. MAMbo – Museo d'Arte Moderna di Bologna
↳ Bologna's museum of modern and contemporary art, opened in 2007 in a former industrial bakery in the Porto district. The permanent collection covers 20th- and 21st-century Italian art, and the museum incorporates the Museo Morandi — more than 250 works by the Bologna-born painter Giorgio Morandi.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Via Don Minzoni 14 · Porto District

🍮 Local Tastes
Tagliatelle al Ragù
↳ The true "Bolognese" — fresh egg ribbons folded through a slow-cooked meat ragù of beef and pancetta. Never spaghetti, never drowned in tomato.
📍 Trattoria Anna Maria · Osteria dell'Orsa · Ristorante Diana
Tortellini in Brodo
↳ Tiny navel-shaped pasta hand-folded around pork, mortadella and Parmigiano, served floating in clear capon broth — Bologna's festive dish, eaten year-round.
📍 Ristorante Diana · Trattoria Anna Maria · Trattoria Gianni
Mortadella di Bologna
↳ The original of what the world calls "baloney" — silky cured pork studded with fat and pistachios, sliced paper-thin or cubed with an aperitivo.
📍 Salumeria Simoni · Tamburini · Mercato delle Erbe
Crescentine & Tigelle
↳ Warm griddled flatbreads split and stuffed with squacquerone cheese and cured meats — the everyday bread of the Emilian hills.
📍 Osteria dell'Orsa · Trattoria Anna Maria · Sfoglia Rina
