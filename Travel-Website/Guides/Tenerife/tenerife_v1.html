Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 18 min · 🚕 7 min → Lago Martiánez
1. Lago Martiánez
↳ A complex of seawater lagoon pools and landscaped gardens designed by Canarian artist César Manrique. Five pools, a volcanic island restaurant and lava-rock terraces sit on the Atlantic edge of Puerto de la Cruz — Manrique's most-visited public work and the template for a generation of Canarian architecture.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Loro Parque
↳ Started as the world's largest parrot collection, Loro Parque has grown into one of Europe's most awarded wildlife parks — gorillas, tigers, chimpanzees, penguins and the largest free-flying parrot show on the planet, across 135,000 m² of tropical garden on the northern tip of Puerto de la Cruz.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 30 min → Drago Milenario
1. Drago Milenario
↳ A single dragon tree estimated at around 800 years old, standing 17 metres tall with a trunk circumference of 20 metres in the hilltop garden of Icod de los Vinos. The species was sacred to the ancient Guanche people of the Canary Islands, and this specimen is the largest known member of the family still alive.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 15 min
📍 Plaza de la Constitución s/n · Icod de los Vinos
🚕 35 min → Masca
2. Masca
↳ A cluster of whitewashed houses clinging to a ridge in the Teno massif at 650 metres, with steep-sided barrancos dropping to the sea on both sides. One of Tenerife's most photographed villages — the narrow streets, terraced gardens and views towards La Gomera are the draw. The ravine hike descends 7 km to the coast.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Masca · Buenavista del Norte
🚕 30 min → Los Gigantes
3. Los Gigantes
↳ Sheer basalt cliffs rising 400 to 600 metres vertically from the Atlantic on Tenerife's southwest coast — the most dramatic sea-cliff scenery on the island. The small marina below is the departure point for whale- and dolphin-watching boats and sunset cruises into the shadow of the rock wall.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 45 min → Teide
1. Teide
↳ Mount Teide is a 3,718-metre stratovolcano — the highest peak in Spain and a UNESCO World Heritage Site of extraordinary geological interest: lava fields, pumice plains, basalt spires and endemic flora across 190 km² of volcanic landscape. The cable car ascends to 3,555 metres in eight minutes.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Teleférico del Teide
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 4:00pm

🚕 50 min → Siam Park
2. Siam Park
↳ A 185,000 m² Thai-themed water park on Tenerife's southwest coast, consistently ranked among the world's best. The Tower of Power is a near-vertical 28-metre slide through a shark tank; the Dragon is a 750-metre family raft ride; the Wave Palace generates 3-metre artificial surf.
📖 Wikipedia

1. La Laguna
↳ San Cristóbal de La Laguna is a UNESCO World Heritage City — Tenerife's original colonial capital and the template for every Spanish settlement that followed in the Americas. The Renaissance Cathedral, the Iglesia de la Concepción and the ochre-and-white merchant palaces lay out five centuries of Atlantic history.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Saturday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Sunday

2. Anaga Rural Park
↳ A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve at Tenerife's northeastern tip — one of the last remnants of the subtropical laurisilva forest that once covered southern Europe. Deep ravines, cloud-level ridges above 1,000 metres, and trails through a moss-covered forest that feels unlike anything else in Spain.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Papas Arrugadas con Mojo
↳ Tenerife's most universal dish — small Canarian potatoes boiled in heavily salted water until the skin wrinkles and a white salt crust forms, served with mojo rojo and mojo verde. Found on every local menu on the island.
📍 Mesón Los Gemelos · La Cofradía de Pescadores
Gofio
↳ The defining staple of Canarian cuisine — grain flour roasted before grinding, eaten for millennia by the Guanche and still on every table in Tenerife. Try escaldón: gofio stirred into fish stock until it sets into a thick paste.
📍 Mesón Los Gemelos · Tasca Ihüey
Barraquito
↳ Tenerife's layered coffee drink — condensed milk on the bottom, a shot of Licor 43, a double espresso, foamed milk and a sliver of lemon and cinnamon on top. Served unstirred; unique to the Canary Islands.

🌋 A Mountain That Has Never Finished Cooling

Tenerife sits at the centre of a geometric paradox: a Spanish island with an African latitude, a subtropical coastline pinned beneath a summit that catches snow. Mount Teide, at 3,718 metres the highest peak in Spain, rises from banana plantations and Canarian pine forest to bare volcanic rock in under an hour's drive. Almost nowhere else can you stand in a cloud forest in the morning and be under desert sun by noon — or watch the shadow of the volcano fall across the clouds below you and realise you are looking at the world from inside a weather system.

The island carries the weight of the Atlantic on its back. The Guanche — the Berber-speaking people who lived here before the Spanish conquest — are absorbed now, but the landscape still speaks their logic: the dragon tree at Icod, older than any European settlement on the island, was sacred to them for its bleeding red resin, and the ridge village of Masca still looks more like North Africa than Castile. La Laguna, the colonial capital, was built as the template for every Spanish city that followed in the Americas — its grid, its central plaza, its civic scale — which means that every time you walk its streets you are walking through the blueprint of a continent.
