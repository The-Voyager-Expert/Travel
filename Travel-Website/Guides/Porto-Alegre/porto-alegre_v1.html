Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 7 min · 🚕 3 min → Farroupilha Park
1. Farroupilha Park
↳ Porto Alegre's main city park — 43 hectares designed by French urbanist Alfred Agache, opened in 1935. The Arco do Triunfo gates mark the original entrance; inside are the Orchidarium, Araújo Viana amphitheater, lakes, and a Japanese garden. Sundays bring the Brique da Redenção fair along the perimeter.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30min
📍 Av. José Bonifácio · Farroupilha
🚶 20 min · 🚕 5 min → Theatro São Pedro
2. Theatro São Pedro
↳ Porto Alegre's oldest theatre, opened in 1858 on Praça da Matriz. The neoclassical facade with its columned portico and the restored interior — gilded balconies, painted ceiling, Venetian chandelier — are intact from the 1984 renovation led by Eva Sopher. Home to the Porto Alegre Symphony Orchestra and Opera season.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Friday 10:00am - 6:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1h
📍 Praça da Matriz · s/n · Centro Histórico
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Farol Santander Porto Alegre
3. Farol Santander Porto Alegre
↳ A 1927-1931 beaux-arts tower on Alfândega Square — Porto Alegre's most recognized skyline building, now a cultural center. Eighteen floors of galleries, a rooftop with panoramic views over Lake Guaíba, and a preserved banking hall with original marble counters and vault door.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 7:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1h 30min

Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Moinhos de Vento Park
1. Moinhos de Vento Park
↳ Porto Alegre's most popular neighborhood park — 11 hectares in the Moinhos de Vento district, popularly called Parcão. A replica Azorean windmill anchors the southern end; a duck pond, tennis courts, outdoor gym, and children's library fill the grounds. Connects to the Moinhos restaurant strip.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Rua Radecki · s/n · Moinhos de Vento
🚕 12 min → Parque Marinha do Brasil
2. Parque Marinha do Brasil
↳ A 70-hectare lakefront park on the northern shore of Lake Guaíba — Porto Alegre's largest urban sports complex. Running tracks, cycling paths, football pitches, and volleyball and tennis courts spread across the grounds. Unobstructed water views toward Guaíba island; hosts the annual Acampamento Farroupilha.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30min
📍 Av. Borges de Medeiros · s/n · Praia de Belas
🚶 22 min · 🚕 8 min → Praça da Alfândega
3. Praça da Alfândega
↳ Porto Alegre's central historic square — flanked by the Memorial do Rio Grande do Sul, MARGS, and the Farol Santander tower. The original customs house gave the square its name; fountains, mature plane trees, and café chairs fill the wide stone plaza on weekday lunches and weekend afternoons.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

Day 6
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Jardim Botânico de Porto Alegre
1. Jardim Botânico de Porto Alegre
↳ A 43-hectare botanical garden with 725 plant species, maintained by the state of Rio Grande do Sul since 1942. The portico entrance leads to collections organized by biome — Atlantic Forest fragments, Pampa grassland, cacti, and orchids. A lake with native aquatic flora and a planetarium occupy the eastern section.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2h
📍 Av. Salvador França · 1427 · Jardim Botânico
🚕 20 min → Solar dos Câmara
2. Solar dos Câmara
↳ Porto Alegre's oldest surviving residential building, constructed between 1818 and 1824 for the Viscount of São Leopoldo. The two-story colonial structure with arched ground-floor loggias was restored in 1874 and now serves as a cultural venue and archive. The interior preserves original stonework and timber details.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 9:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Saturday & Sunday
⏰ ~1h
📍 Rua Duque de Caxias · 1231 · Centro Histórico
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Viaduto Otávio Rocha
3. Viaduto Otávio Rocha
↳ A 1932 art deco viaduct spanning the valley between the Centro Histórico and Cidade Baixa. The arched concrete structure carries Rua Borges de Medeiros overhead; the underpass is lined with murals and hosts weekly food and design markets. Declared Cultural Heritage of Porto Alegre.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45min
📍 Rua Borges de Medeiros · s/n · Centro Histórico

🍮 Local Tastes
Churrasco Gaúcho
↳ Rio Grande do Sul's defining barbecue tradition — thick cuts of beef slow-cooked over wood embers on long skewers, with a communal ritual that distinguishes it from all other regional styles in Brazil.
📍 Churrascaria Galpão Crioulo · Churrascaria Barranco
Chimarrão
↳ Bitter hot yerba mate drunk communally from a gourd with a metal straw — a daily ritual throughout Rio Grande do Sul, offered as a greeting in homes and shared on every occasion.
📍 Mercado Público
Arroz de Carreteiro
↳ A one-pot rice dish made with charque — sun-dried salted beef — with onion and garlic; a gaucho staple from the wagon-driver traditions of the Brazilian pampa, still served in traditional restaurants across the state.
