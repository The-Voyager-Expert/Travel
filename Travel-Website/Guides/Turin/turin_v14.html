Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Piazza San Carlo
1. Piazza San Carlo
↳ Turin's finest baroque square — 4 arcaded sides, 2 church façades at one end, & the bronze rider at the hub. All 4 sides line with cafés open all day; they fill up at dusk for the aperitivo hour & stay busy late into the night.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45min
📍 Piazza San Carlo
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Piazza Castello
2. Piazza Castello
↳ The royal heart of Turin, ringed by Palazzo Madama, Palazzo Reale, & the San Lorenzo church. The square sits on the 2 km by 1 km Roman grid — the best preserved in all of Italy — & it is free to walk & to see, with the arcades of Via Po & Via Roma running off it.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Piazza Castello
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Caffè Al Bicerin
3. Caffè Al Bicerin
↳ Turin's great café, open at the Consolata since 1763 AD. The bicerin: 3 layers — coffee, hot choc, & cream; take each in turn from base to top & do not stir it. Tolstoy & Nietzsche & Dumas all sat & drank here; the room is from 1867.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Museo Egizio
1. Museo Egizio
↳ Best Egyptian museum outside Cairo — 40 000 items in the 1824 Academy of Sciences. Hall of Kings: 16 royal statues all in a row. The Turin Papyrus is the oldest geographical document on earth · 1 160 BC; the intact tomb of Kha is the only one of its kind ever found.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Palazzo Reale
↳ The Savoy royal home from 1660 to 1865 — seat of Italian unity, now the Musei Reali complex. State rooms: a throne hall & a 4 000-piece arms room. The Royal Library holds a Leonardo da Vinci drawing. The Holy Shroud Chapel reopened after a long restoration.

3. Palazzo Carignano & Risorgimento Museum
↳ Guarini built this palace in 1679; Vittorio Emanuele II was born in it & Italy's first parliament met in it in 1848. The brick front is all convex & concave — a key Guarini trick. The hall is where Italy voted to be one & free.

1. Superga Basilica & Royal Tombs
↳ Juvara's 1731 church at 672 m on a hill — the best view in Piedmont, from the Alps to Gran Paradiso. The crypt holds 54 royal Savoy tombs. Take the rack railway up from Stazione Sassi in 18 min; a marker on the apse recalls the 1949 air crash.

3. Mole Antonelliana
↳ Turin's 167 metre tower — Antonelli began it as a synagogue in 1863; it now holds the Museo Nazionale del Cinema. Climb 5 floors on a ramp from old magic lanterns to film. The glass lift to the top gives the best Alps view in the city.

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 22 min · 🚕 8 min → Parco del Valentino
1. Parco del Valentino
↳ Turin's great green park by the Po — 550 000 sq m, laid out in English romantic style by Kurten in the 1850s & free to enter. Chestnut allées, a long stone bank, rowing clubs, & the old Castello del Valentino on its north end.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Corso Massimo d'Azeglio
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Borgo Medievale del Valentino
2. Borgo Medievale del Valentino
↳ A rebuild of a Piedmontese medieval village of 1884, made for the Italian Expo by D'Andrade & based on 32 real old buildings, every arch a true copy of a real one. Entry to the borgo is free; the Rocca castle inside has a small fee.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Lingotto & Pinacoteca Agnelli
↳ The old 1923 Fiat plant by Mattè Trucco — the car line ran up to a rooftop test track, now a hotel. Piano's art hall holds 25 works incl the finest Canalettos in Italy, plus Tiepolo, Picasso & Matisse. Walk up to see the banked oval on the roof.

🍮 Local Tastes
Bicerin
↳ Turin's layered signature drink: equal parts espresso, thick drinking chocolate, and whole fresh cream in a small glass tumbler. Do not stir — drink in layers. Invented at Caffè Al Bicerin in 1763 and unchanged since.
📍 Caffè Al Bicerin
Agnolotti del Plin
↳ Small hand-pinched pasta pockets filled with braised meat · veal, pork, and rabbit. "Plin" means pinch in Piedmontese dialect. The dough should be almost translucent; the filling dense and savoury.
📍 Pastificio Defilippis
Vitello Tonnato
↳ Cold sliced veal draped in a creamy sauce of tuna, capers, and mayonnaise. Served at room temperature as a first course. The tuna is the sauce; the dish is about the veal — order it at any serious Piedmontese restaurant.
📍 Porto di Savona
Gianduiotto
↳ Turin's signature chocolate: a small curved ingot of dark chocolate blended with local Tonda Gentile hazelnut paste. Invented in 1865 and still individually wrapped in gold foil at artisan chocolatiers.
📍 Caffè San Carlo arcade
Vermouth di Torino
↳ Turin is the birthplace of vermouth: invented by Antonio Benedetto Carpano here in 1786. Order it straight over ice with an orange peel as an aperitivo. Carpano Antica Formula and Punt e Mes are the local benchmarks.
📍 Any café bar on Piazza San Carlo
Bagna Cauda
↳ A warm communal dip of olive oil, butter, anchovies, and garlic — a peasant dish from the autumn harvest, served October through March. Dip raw cardoons, peppers, and bread in the shared pot at the table.
📍 Consorzio

Cuneo · 1h
