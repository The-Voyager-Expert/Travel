Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Fort Jefferson Marina
1. Fort Jefferson
↳ A 19th-century brick fortress 70 miles west of Key West, reachable by ferry or seaplane. Surrounded by pristine coral reefs and the deepest blue-green water in the Keys, the fort sits isolated in the Dry Tortugas National Park — a place that feels like the edge of the world. The hexagonal fortification, never fully completed, was used as a military outpost and later a prison (holding Dr. Samuel Mudd, the doctor accused of Lincoln assassination). Today it is a snorkeling and diving destination with trails around the ramparts and unobstructed views across 70 miles of open Gulf.

2. Glass Bottom Boat Tour
↳ A boat with a glass floor that lets you see the coral reef and marine life without getting wet — shallow reefs with brain coral, sea fans, grouper, and parrotfish visible from the deck. Tours depart from Key West and are typically 2-3 hours long, ideal for a late-afternoon experience after returning from Fort Jefferson.

🚕 10 min → Mallory Square
3. Mallory Square Sunset
↳ The iconic Key West sunset gathering at Mallory Square Plaza every evening at dusk — street musicians, local artists, and crowds of tourists and residents converging to watch the sun drop into the Gulf of Mexico. The square is filled with carnival atmosphere, food vendors, and the famous ritual of applause as the sun touches the horizon. It is a uniquely Key West experience and the single most photographed moment in the island.

🎟️ Free

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 50 min → Bahia Honda State Park
1. Bahia Honda State Park
↳ The largest undeveloped beach in the Keys, with white sand and calm turquoise water. The park spans the island and includes the old Bahia Honda Rail Bridge, a historic structure from the Flagler Railroad that once connected the islands. The beach is excellent for swimming, snorkeling in shallow reefs just offshore, and viewing the bridge at sunset. Unlike many Key West beaches which are narrow and crowded, Bahia Honda is spacious and pristine.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Daily 8:00am - sunset

2. Swimming with Dolphins
↳ An interactive encounter with Atlantic bottlenose dolphins in their natural habitat at Islamorada. The dolphins are wild and free-swimming, and visitors enter the water to observe them up close — an experience that is both thrilling and humbling, seeing these intelligent marine mammals in their element. Tour operators follow strict animal welfare guidelines and the dolphins are never forced to interact.

🚕 50 min → Key West
3. Duval Street Historic District
↳ The heart of Key West — a mile-long street lined with historic buildings dating to the 19th and early 20th centuries when Key West was one of the wealthiest cities per capita in the United States, due to wrecking (salvaging shipwrecks) and cigar manufacturing. Today Duval Street is a living museum of Conch architecture, colonial homes, galleries, and bars, best experienced on foot at dusk when the street fills with locals and the light turns golden.

🎟️ Free
🏛️ 24 hours
⏰ ~1.5 hr (walking and exploring)

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 45 min → John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park
1. John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park
↳ The first underwater coral reef state park in the United States, protecting 178 square nautical miles of coral reef just offshore from Key Largo. Visitors can snorkel or dive among pristine coral formations — brain coral, elkhorn, and sea fan — while seeing tropical fish, sea turtles, and rays. The iconic Christ of the Abyss statue sits on the reef floor at 25 feet depth, a bronze sculpture installed in 1965 that has become the most photographed dive site in the Keys.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park · floridastateparks.org
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - sunset
⏰ ~3 hr (boat snorkel tour included)
📍 Overseas Highway · Key Largo
🚕 30 min → Key West
2. Key West Butterfly Conservatory
↳ An enclosed tropical garden filled with over 2,000 live butterflies from around the world — morpho butterflies from South America, swallowtails, monarchs, and species with wingspans up to 6 inches. Visitors walk through humid pathways as butterflies land on flowers, clothing, and skin, creating an intimate encounter with one of nature's most beautiful transformations. The conservatory also covers the life cycle of butterflies and their crucial role as pollinators.

3. Key West Shipwreck Museum
↳ Housed in a replica of a 19th-century wrecker's warehouse, the museum tells the story of the thousands of ships that wrecked on the reef during Key West's heyday as a wrecking capital. Artifacts include cannons, anchors, gold coins, and the lens from a shipwreck's lighthouse. The centerpiece is a recreated merchant ship complete with period furnishings, and a climbing tower offers views over the Key West harbor and the reef where so many ships met their fate.

🚌 Getting Around
Ride-Hailing Apps

Uber and Lyft operate in Key West. Most rides within Key West proper are 8–15 minutes and cost between $12–$20. To reach the upper keys (John Pennekamp, Bahia Honda), expect 40–60 minute drives and higher fares. Booking in advance is recommended for early-morning Dry Tortugas ferry departures.

Key West Tram

The Island City Trolley is a hop-on hop-off open-air trolley that covers Duval Street and major downtown attractions. Useful for a first-day orientation or a quick bypass of walking Duval Street in heat. A day pass is approximately $30 per person.

Bicycles

Key West is flat and bike-friendly. Many hotels rent bicycles for around $15 per day. Riding to Mallory Square or along the waterfront is popular, though Duval Street can be crowded with pedestrians during peak hours.

🍮 Local Tastes

Conch Salad — Raw conch marinated in lime juice, onion, and hot pepper. A Key West staple found at beachside stands and upscale restaurants alike, served in a pineapple half or coconut bowl for an island presentation.

Mahi-Mahi (Dolphinfish) — The most common local catch, grilled or blackened, served with rice and lime. It is mild, flaky, and sweet, and appears on nearly every restaurant menu.

Key Lime Pie — The unofficial dessert of the Keys — condensed milk, egg yolks, and key lime juice baked in a pie crust. The filling is tangy and creamy, and the pie is traditionally served without a meringue in Key West, topped only with sweetened whipped cream or a thin lime zest.

Grouper Sandwich — Fried or blackened grouper on a roll with tartar sauce or remoulade. A lunchtime favorite at casual beachside joints and better restaurants, especially at the historic Seaport area.

Yellowtail Snapper — A delicate local fish, often grilled whole and served with butter and lemon. Slightly sweeter and more delicate than mahi-mahi.

🚗 Food Delivery

DoorDash, Grubhub, and Uber Eats all operate in Key West with broad restaurant coverage. Delivery typically takes 30–50 minutes depending on location and time of day. Expect a 15–20% service fee plus delivery charges. For the best Key West dining, local pickup or eating in-restaurant is recommended — many dishes do not travel well.

✨ Claude Inspiration

The Reef as Museum — The Florida Keys are the only living coral barrier reef in U.S. waters, a delicate ecosystem that has survived hurricanes, fishing pressure, and climate change. Snorkeling here feels like entering a natural museum where every coral head, sea fan, and tropical fish tells a story of adaptation and survival. The reefs are a humbling reminder that the natural world operates at a scale and complexity beyond human engineering.

Isolation and Beauty — Driving down the Overseas Highway, you cross 42 bridges connecting islands that feel progressively more remote and beautiful. By Key West, you are at the southern terminus of continental U.S. — the end of the road. This isolation creates a unique culture, a mix of old-money shipwreckers, artists, retired navy, fishing families, and Cuban immigrants, all existing in a place that is as much Caribbean as American.
