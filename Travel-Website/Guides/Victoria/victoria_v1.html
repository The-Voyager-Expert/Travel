Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Fan Tan Alley
1. Fan Tan Alley · Victoria Chinatown
↳ Canada's oldest Chinatown, established in the 1850s, with the ornate Gate of Harmonious Interest on Fisgard Street. Fan Tan Alley runs behind it — the narrowest alley in Canada at roughly one metre wide threading through a network of historic courtyards lined with small shops.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Fisgard Street · Old Town
🚶 12 min · 🚕 4 min → BC Parliament Buildings
2. BC Parliament Buildings
↳ The 1897 Romanesque Revival complex designed by Francis Rattenbury rises along the Inner Harbour waterfront. Free guided tours of the legislative chambers and ornate rotunda run during recesses; the illuminated facade at night is one of Victoria's defining views.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 8:30am - 4:30pm
🚫 Closed Saturday & Sunday

3. Royal BC Museum
↳ One of Canada's finest regional museums — a life-sized woolly mammoth, reconstructed Old Town storefronts from the 1900s, and an extensive First Nations longhouse collection with original totem poles and ceremonial objects spanning 13,000 years of natural and human history.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Royal BC Museum · 4.5⭐ · royalbcmuseum.bc.ca
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2.5 hr
📍 675 Belleville Street · Inner Harbour
🚶 25 min · 🚕 8 min → Ogden Point Breakwater
4. Ogden Point Breakwater
↳ A 900-metre granite breakwater extending into the Strait of Juan de Fuca, built in 1919 and still in active use as Victoria's cruise ship terminal. The walkable top of the seawall gives unobstructed views of the Olympic Mountains and the entrance to Esquimalt Harbour.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Dallas Road · James Bay

1. Prince of Whales
↳ The waters around Victoria are among the most reliable in the world for Southern Resident killer whales, plus humpbacks and minkes. Prince of Whales runs Zodiac and covered-vessel excursions from the Inner Harbour causeway into the Strait of Juan de Fuca.

⚠️ May - October season only · departures from 9:00am
📍 812 Wharf Street · Inner Harbour

2. Beacon Hill Park
↳ A 75-hectare urban park stretching from downtown to the ocean bluffs above Dallas Road, home to Canada's tallest totem pole at 38.9 metres, a flock of resident peacocks, a Victorian cricket pitch, and a broad lawn overlooking the Strait of Juan de Fuca and Mount Baker on the Washington State horizon.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5 hr
📍 Douglas Street · Fairfield
🚶 8 min · 🚕 4 min → Government House Gardens
3. Government House Gardens
↳ The official residence of the Lieutenant Governor of British Columbia, surrounded by 14 hectares of heritage gardens open to the public year-round. The formal rose garden, perennial borders, and orchard date to the early 20th century. The grounds were designated a National Historic Site in 2013.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
📍 1401 Rockland Avenue · Rockland
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Craigdarroch Castle
4. Craigdarroch Castle
↳ A 39-room Romanesque Revival mansion completed in 1890 for coal baron Robert Dunsmuir — he died before moving in. The original hardwood woodwork, leaded stained glass, and Victorian furnishings are intact throughout four floors; the tower observation deck looks out across the city to the Salish Sea.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 30 min → Hatley Castle
1. Hatley Castle
↳ A 650-acre lakeside estate built in 1908 for coal magnate James Dunsmuir. The Edwardian castle houses Royal Roads University; guided tours cover the state rooms, library, and terraced grounds including a Japanese garden, Italian garden, and rose garden. The castle exterior appeared in several X-Men films.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Hatley Park National Historic Site · 4.5⭐ · royalroads.ca
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Castle interior tours April - September only
📍 2005 Sooke Road · Colwood
🚕 8 min → Fort Rodd Hill & Fisgard Lighthouse
2. Fort Rodd Hill & Fisgard Lighthouse
↳ A late-19th-century coastal artillery battery guarding the approach to Esquimalt Naval Base, with the 1860 Fisgard Lighthouse — the first permanent lighthouse on Canada's Pacific coast — connected by a rocky causeway. The gun emplacements, barracks, and searchlight rooms are open to explore.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Fort Rodd Hill · Parks Canada · 4.6⭐ · pc.gc.ca
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1.5 hr
⚠️ Open May 1 - October 15
📍 603 Fort Rodd Hill Road · Colwood
🚕 15 min → Goldstream Provincial Park
3. Goldstream Provincial Park
↳ Old-growth Douglas fir and western red cedar forest along the Goldstream River, 28 kilometres north of Victoria. The 30-minute trail to the 47-metre Niagara Falls waterfall passes trees over 600 years old. In November Pacific salmon fill the lower river in one of the largest chum runs on Vancouver Island.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 2930 Trans-Canada Highway · Langford

4. Butchart Gardens
↳ A 55-acre floral estate established in 1904 by Jennie Butchart in an exhausted limestone quarry on the Saanich Peninsula. The centrepiece Sunken Garden drops 14 metres below the original quarry rim; the Italian, Japanese, and Rose gardens fill the surrounding grounds. Saturday evenings in summer close with fireworks.
📖 Wikipedia

🌊 Two Dynasties, One Island

The Dunsmuir family built two of the three castles on this itinerary — and they never spoke to each other at the end. Robert Dunsmuir, the coal baron who commissioned Craigdarroch, died in 1889 before the castle was finished. His son James went on to build Hatley Castle on a far grander scale. The two estates sit roughly 15 kilometres apart; the family's coal fortune funded both, and a bitter inheritance dispute split the heirs for years. Visiting them across Day 2 and Day 3 turns the itinerary into a quiet family saga across the same generation of money and ambition.

Victoria's reputation as "more British than Britain" is partly accurate and partly theatre — the city actively cultivated the image in the early 20th century to attract tourists and settlers. The Fairmont Empress, the formal afternoon teas, the hanging flower baskets on Government Street: most of these traditions were deliberate marketing choices rather than organic inheritance. Canada's oldest Chinatown is a 10-minute walk from the Parliament buildings that enforced the Chinese Head Tax. Both histories are visible in the same city blocks, usually without comment.
