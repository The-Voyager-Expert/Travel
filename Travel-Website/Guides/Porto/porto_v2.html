Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 4 min → São Bento Railway Station
1. São Bento Railway Station
↳ Porto's main station, one of the world's great interiors: 20,000 hand-painted azulejo tiles by Jorge Colaço · 1905–1916. Entry free. Go early to stand undisturbed in the hall — a working station with trains to Braga and the Douro Valley.

🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Palácio da Bolsa
2. Palácio da Bolsa
↳ Neoclassical merchant palace · 1842–1910. The Arab Room — gilded stucco modelled on the Alhambra — is seen on the house's own 30-min tour. Next door, Igreja de São Francisco has 400 kg of gold leaf on every surface. Easy to pair in one stop.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:00am - 6:30pm

🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Clérigos Tower
3. Clérigos Tower
↳ Porto's defining skyline: a 76-metre Baroque bell tower by Nasoni · 1754. Climb 225 steps for a 360° panorama over the roofscape, the Douro, and the Atlantic on clear days. The ticket includes the gilded oval church and museum below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm

📍 Rua de São Filipe de Nery
🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Ribeira Waterfront
4. Ribeira Waterfront
↳ Porto's UNESCO-listed medieval riverside — tilting townhouses in yellow, terracotta, and green above Cais da Ribeira quay. Best at golden hour. Cross Dom Luís I Bridge lower deck for the full view back; the narrow lanes behind are quietest.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Dom Luís I Bridge
1. Dom Luís I Bridge
↳ Double-deck iron arch bridge · 1886 by Théophile Seyrig — not an Eiffel bridge. Lower deck: wine cellars south, Porto's rooftops north. Upper deck, 45 m above, carries the Metro — cross it for the aerial view back over the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Ponte Dom Luís I
🚶 5 min · 🚕 5 min → Vila Nova de Gaia
2. Vila Nova de Gaia — Port Wine Cellars
↳ Port wine lodges terracing the Gaia hillside — rock-cut cellars called caves, each aging a different house's blend in oak barrels. Sandeman, Graham's, Cálem, and Ferreira terrace the slope; Graham's has the best Douro views. Free to walk the quayside between them.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Cais de Gaia · Vila Nova de Gaia
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Douro River — Six Bridges Cruise
3. Douro River — Six Bridges Cruise
↳ A two-hour cruise under all six Douro bridges: Ribeira townhouses north, wine cellars terracing Gaia south, out toward the river mouth at Foz. Best in late-afternoon light. Departs the Cais de Gaia pier; a small-group boat keeps the deck uncrowded.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Coliseu do Porto
1. Coliseu do Porto
↳ Porto's grand Art Deco concert hall on Rua de Passos Manuel — a curving Streamline Moderne landmark of the early 20th century, its rounded glass-and-stone facade and neon marquee lighting up the Baixa after dark. A protected building and still the city's main stage.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~15 min
📍 R. de Passos Manuel
🚶 3 min · 🚕 1 min → Capela das Almas
2. Capela das Almas
↳ A small chapel on pedestrian Rua de Santa Catarina wrapped outside in nearly 16,000 blue-and-white azulejo tiles — laid in 1929 in an 18th-century style, depicting the lives of St Francis and St Catherine. The most photographed facade on the shopping street.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 7:30am - 5:30pm · Saturday 7:30am - 12:30pm · 4:30pm - 5:30pm · Sunday 7:30am - 12:30pm
⏰ ~15 min
📍 R. de Santa Catarina
🚶 6 min · 🚕 6 min → Igreja de Santo Ildefonso
3. Igreja de Santo Ildefonso
↳ An 18th-century Baroque church above Praça da Batalha, its twin-tower granite facade faced with some 11,000 azulejo tiles by Jorge Colaço — the same artist as São Bento — laid in 1932. Free to step inside between services.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Saturday 9:00am - 12:15pm · 3:00pm - 5:15pm · Sunday 9:00am - 11:00am · Monday 3:00pm - 5:15pm
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Praça da Batalha
🚶 8 min · 🚕 7 min → Sé do Porto
4. Sé do Porto
↳ Porto's fortress-like Romanesque cathedral, begun in the 12th century on the city's highest hill. The Gothic cloister is lined with azulejos and the terrace out front gives one of the widest views over the old town and down to the river. The cathedral itself is free to enter.

🍮 Local Tastes
Francesinha
↳ Porto's defining dish — layers of bread, wet-cured ham, linguiça sausage, and steak, sealed under melted cheese and a poured-over sauce of beer, tomato, and hot pepper. Rich, heavy, and magnificent.
📍 Café Santiago
Pastel De Nata
↳ The custard tart. Flaky concave pastry shell, warm egg-custard centre with a caramelised top, dusted with cinnamon. Best eaten within minutes of leaving the oven.
📍 Confeitaria do Bolhão
Bacalhau
↳ Salt-dried cod — Portugal's defining ingredient. Most common: à Brás — shredded with egg and matchstick potatoes; com natas — cream-baked; and à Lagareiro — olive-oil-roasted with potatoes.
📍 Taberna dos Mercadores
Port Wine
↳ Fortified wine from the Douro Valley, aged in the Gaia lodges. Categories that matter: Ruby — young, fruit-forward; Tawny — barrel-aged and nutty, 10- and 20-year the sweet spots; Vintage — the collector's port.
📍 Cálem · Vila Nova de Gaia

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Braga · 1 hour
↳ Portugal's ecclesiastical capital holds some of the oldest churches in the country and the dramatic Bom Jesus staircase — a Baroque pilgrimage site above the city. The compact old town centres on the medieval Sé cathedral and treasury. A half-day covers the main sights.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Guimaraes · 1 hour
↳ The birthplace of Portugal — the 10th-century castle where the first king of Portugal was born, the Ducal Palace of the Bragancans, and a UNESCO medieval centre of granite arcades and yellow buildings.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Viana do Castelo · 1 hour
↳ A handsome coastal town at the mouth of the Lima river — the Praca da Republica, the hilltop Basilica of Santa Luzia reached by funicular, and long Atlantic beaches. CP trains run on the Minho line throughout the day.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Aveiro · 1 hour 10 minutes
↳ A small canal city with colourful moliceiro boats, Art Nouveau facades, and ovos moles — egg-yolk pastries in wafer shells. Take the canal boat first, then walk the old quarter past tiled facades. The Aveiro Museum and São Gonçalinho chapel are the highlights.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Douro Valley · 2 hours
↳ Portugal's wine country — terraced vineyards on steep schist slopes producing port. The Linha do Douro train offers a window-seat journey through the valley. Régua and Pinhão are the best stops; Pinhão station has celebrated azulejo panels on its platforms.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
