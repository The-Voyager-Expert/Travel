1. Chautauqua Park & Flatirons
↳ A National Historic Landmark and the only Chautauqua west of the Mississippi still in operation since its founding. The 40-acre grounds sit at the base of the Flatirons, steeply tilted sandstone slabs that define the city's skyline. The Auditorium and Dining Hall remain in active use.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Pearl Street Mall
↳ A four-block pedestrian historic district closed to traffic in 1977 and named a Great Public Space in America by the American Planning Association. The brick promenade is lined with 19th-century commercial buildings — over 70% locally owned — anchored at the 1300 block by the County Courthouse.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Pearl St between 11th & 15th · Downtown
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min
3. Boulder Creek Path
↳ A 5.5-mile paved multi-use path following Boulder Creek from the canyon mouth to Stazio Ballfields, eliminating all street crossings via bridges and underpasses. It passes the Main Library, Civic Area Park, and the Dushanbe Teahouse — a gift from the sister city in Tajikistan.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Eben G. Fine Park · Canyon Mouth
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min
4. the Boulderado
↳ The city's oldest luxury hotel, opened January 1, 1909. The lobby features a cantilevered cherry-wood staircase and a stained-glass atrium ceiling — both original. The 1908 Otis elevator is still in daily operation. On the National Register of Historic Places since 1994.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Green Chile
↳ Colorado's defining condiment — roasted Hatch or Pueblo green chiles slow-cooked into a thick sauce poured over breakfast burritos, smothered fries, and eggs. Ask for "Christmas" to get red and green together.
📍 Illegal Pete's · Pearl Street · Sancho's Broken Arrow
Rocky Mountain Bison
↳ Colorado-raised bison is leaner than beef with a richer and sweeter flavor. Farm-to-table menus feature bison alongside elk and Colorado-raised trout.
📍 Fate Brewing Company · The Kitchen · Pearl Street
Colorado Craft Beer
↳ The city has one of the highest concentrations of craft breweries per capita in the US. The local style runs toward hoppy IPAs and small-batch experimental ales.
