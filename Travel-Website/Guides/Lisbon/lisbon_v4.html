Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Sé Cathedral
1. Sé de Lisboa
↳ Portugal's oldest cathedral, built in 1147 on the site of the Moors' main mosque. Romanesque façade and twin towers; the 1755 earthquake damaged the interior. Entry includes the Gothic cloisters and a treasury with medieval reliquaries.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Sé de Lisboa · Alfama 4.3⭐ · patriarcado-lisboa.pt
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Largo da Sé
🚶 15 min · 🚕 6 min → Carmo Convent
2. Convento do Carmo
↳ Gothic monastery · 1389 devastated by the 1755 earthquake — the nave roof collapsed, leaving a roofless skeleton open to the sky. Skeletal arches shelter the Museu Arqueológico do Carmo: mummies, pre-Columbian artefacts, and Roman stones.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Convento do Carmo · Chiado 4.5⭐ · museuarqueologicodocarmo.pt
🏛️ Monday - Saturday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Sunday
⏰ ~1h
📍 Largo do Carmo
🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Praça do Comércio
3. Praça do Comércio
↳ Lisbon's grand riverside square, rebuilt after 1755 on the site of the Royal Palace. Three sides are Pombaline arcades in ochre and white; the fourth opens onto the Tagus via the Cais das Colunas steps.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Praça do Comércio
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Alfama
4. Alfama
↳ Lisbon's oldest neighbourhood — a Moorish maze of alleys from the castle hill to the river. Survived 1755 largely intact, preserving a medieval street pattern unique in Western Europe. Fado was born here.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Largo do Chafariz de Dentro
🚕 15 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 22 min → Jerónimos Monastery
1. Mosteiro dos Jerónimos
↳ Finest Manueline monument — built early 1500s near Vasco da Gama's India launch point. Ornate cloisters and vaulted church intact; church holds tombs of Vasco da Gama and Camões. Pastéis de Belém bakery · since 1837 is next door.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Mosteiro dos Jerónimos · Belém 4.8⭐ · 800+ reviews · Skip-the-Line — GetYourGuide
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:30pm

⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Praça do Império
🚶 6 min · 🚕 2 min → Padrão dos Descobrimentos
2. Padrão dos Descobrimentos
↳ A 52-metre monument built in 1960 for the 500th anniversary of Henry the Navigator's death. Carries stone effigies of 33 explorers and poets, Henry at the bow. Terrace at top has views across Belém.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Padrão dos Descobrimentos · Belém 4.5⭐ · padraodosdescobrimentos.pt
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 7:00pm

📍 Av. Brasília
🚶 8 min · 🚕 2 min → Belém Tower
3. Torre de Belém
↳ Manueline fortification at the Tagus mouth, built 1515–1521 as the ceremonial gateway for the Voyages of Discovery. Limestone façade with rope motifs and armillary spheres — Portugal's most intricate Manueline stonework. UNESCO Site.
📖 Wikipedia

4. MAAT
↳ Riverside museum in 15,000 crackle-glazed ceramic tiles by Amanda Levete, opened 2016 alongside a restored power station. The Museum of Art, Architecture and Technology spans both buildings. Rooftop entry is free.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Castelo de São Jorge
1. Castelo de São Jorge
↳ 11th-century Moorish fortress captured in 1147, royal residence for four centuries. Walkable battlements offer the best 360° panorama in Lisbon — Alfama, the Tagus, and the Ponte 25 de Abril. Arrive at opening time to beat the queues.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Castelo de São Jorge · Alfama 4.5⭐ · 600+ reviews · Skip the Line — Viator
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 R. de Santa Cruz do Castelo
🚶 14 min · 🚕 7 min → Mouraria
2. Mouraria
↳ Lisbon's old Moorish quarter — a warren of alleys between the castle and Graça, home to fado's roots. Tiled tabacarias, community gardens, the Intendente market square, and street art layered over Moorish walls. Quieter and more local than Alfama.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Largo do Intendente Pina Manique
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Miradouro da Graça
3. Miradouro da Graça
↳ Open-air platform atop Lisbon's highest hill with views of São Jorge Castle, Alfama rooftops, and the Tagus. Graça draws locals rather than tourists. Best vantage for morning light over the castle and the best sunset spot on the hill.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Calçada da Graça
🚶 6 min · 🚕 2 min → Miradouro da Senhora do Monte
4. Miradouro da Senhora do Monte
↳ Lisbon's highest miradouro — a chapel-side terrace in Graça with a 270° view from the castle and Alfama to the Ponte 25 de Abril. Almost entirely locals: few tourists make the climb. Benches fill at dusk as the Tagus turns gold.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Largo Monte
🚕 13 min → hotel
Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 13 min → Bairro Alto
1. Bairro Alto
↳ Lisbon's bohemian hillside neighbourhood — a 16th-century grid of narrow streets lined with vintage shops, tascas, and fado houses. Unhurried by day with boutique browsing and neighbourhood lunch spots. One of Lisbon's oldest residential quarters and the birthplace of fado culture.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Bairro Alto
🚶 4 min · 🚕 7 min → Igreja de São Roque
2. Igreja de São Roque
↳ Plain Mannerist façade hiding eight ornate side chapels. The standout is the Chapel of São João Baptista: commissioned by King João V, built in Rome from lapis lazuli, agate, porphyry, and ivory, consecrated by the Pope, then dismantled and shipped to Lisbon in 1747.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 Largo Trindade Coelho
🚶 5 min · 🚕 7 min → Chiado
3. Chiado
↳ Lisbon's literary and cafe district. Rua Garrett climbs from Baixa to Largo de Camões, lined with the Bertrand bookshop · 1732, the world's oldest operating bookshop, Café A Brasileira where Fernando Pessoa drank his bicas, and elegant 18th-century facades rebuilt after the 1988 fire.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Rua Garrett
🚶 17 min · 🚕 5 min → Príncipe Real
4. Príncipe Real
↳ Lisbon's most sophisticated neighbourhood — concept-store boutiques and shaded terraces around the Jardim do Príncipe Real. The leafy garden square hosts a weekend fair; the Natural History Museum is a short walk away.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Praça do Príncipe Real

Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Oceanário de Lisboa
1. Oceanário de Lisboa
↳ One of Europe's largest aquariums, built for Expo '98 with 1 million litres and a central ocean tank visible from four habitats. The structure appears to float on the Tagus. Sea otters and jellyfish gallery are highlights; book online.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Oceanário de Lisboa · Parque das Nações 4.7⭐ · 300+ reviews · Skip-the-Line — Viator
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 Esplanada Dom Carlos I s/nº
🚶 20 min · 🚕 7 min → Parque das Nações promenade
2. Parque das Nações
↳ Modern riverside district built for Expo '98 on reclaimed industrial land. The promenade along the Tagus opens up clear views of the Vasco da Gama Bridge — Europe's longest bridge at 17.3 km. Passes the Portugal Pavilion by Álvaro Siza Vieira, Gare do Oriente, and a waterfront cable car.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Parque das Nações
🚶 12 min · 🚕 4 min → Gare do Oriente
3. Gare do Oriente
↳ Designed by Santiago Calatrava for Expo '98, the soaring steel-and-glass canopy casts forest-like shadows across the platforms. Lisbon's main intercity rail hub — trains to Porto, Faro, and Madrid — plus metro and bus terminal.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Av. Dom João II
🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Telecabine Lisboa
4. Telecabine Lisboa
↳ Gondola cable car running 1.25 km over the Expo '98 waterfront above the Tagus riverside. Cabins rise above the park rooftops giving unobstructed views of the Vasco da Gama Bridge and the wide Tagus estuary — the best aerial perspective of the whole Parque das Nações.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Passeio de Neptuno · Parque das Nações

↳ Historic waterfront district anchored by the Mercado da Ribeira food hall and the neon-lit Pink Street. The ferry terminal connects to Cacilhas. Evenings: bars behind the market and wine bars on Rua Nova do Carvalho run until 2:00am.

↳ Bohemian hilltop viewpoint anchored by the Adamastor statue — the mythological sea giant from Camões' Os Lusíadas. Popular with locals at all hours; sunset brings guitars, wine, and commanding river views west toward the sea.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 7
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Museu Nacional do Azulejo
1. Museu Nacional do Azulejo
↳ Portugal's foremost azulejo museum in the former Madre de Deus Convent · founded 1509. Panels span the 15th century to the present. Highlight: a 23-metre baroque panel · c.1700 depicting Lisbon before the 1755 earthquake.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Museu Nacional do Azulejo · Xabregas 4.7⭐ · museudoazulejo.gov.pt

🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~2h
📍 R. Madre de Deus 4
🚶 24 min · 🚕 10 min → Marvila
2. Marvila
↳ Eastern Lisbon's former industrial riverfront — 19th-century warehouses converted into artist studios, design spaces, and architecture firms running from the Beato convent along the Tagus docks. Raw brick, river light, almost no tourists: the most unhurried and photogenic corner of the city.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Pastéis De Nata
↳ The Portuguese custard tart — flaky, blistered pastry shell, scorched cinnamon-dusted egg-yolk custard inside. Invented at the Jerónimos Monastery before the 18th century; the original recipe is still made by a handful of master bakers.
📍 Pastéis de Belém · Manteigaria
Ginjinha
↳ Sour-cherry liqueur, served in tiny shot glasses · with or without a soaked cherry at the bottom. Small Lisbon ginjinha bars are a stand-up-and-go institution — you order, drink at the counter, leave.
📍 A Ginjinha

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Queluz · 25 min
↳ 18th-century rococo palace — Portugal's "Versailles" — with ornate state rooms, formal gardens, and the original palace kitchen. The quickest palace day trip on the CP network.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Sintra · 40 min
↳ UNESCO World Heritage mountain village with palaces embedded in forest — Pena Palace · fairytale Gothic-Romantic hilltop, the Moorish Castle, Quinta da Regaleira · esoteric gardens and initiation well. The most worth doing of all the day trips; go early to beat the crowds.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Cascais · 40 min
↳ Elegant coastal town at the mouth of the Tagus — Atlantic seafront promenade, the Boca do Inferno sea-cave viewpoint, the old fishing quarter, and the best esplanade lunch spots on the Lisbon coast. Relaxed and easy — a complete contrast to Lisbon's hills.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Santarém · 48 min
↳ The Gothic capital of Portugal — a walled hilltop city above the Tagus floodplain with a remarkable concentration of Manueline and Gothic churches; views at the Portas do Sol terrace across the river plains are some of the finest in the country.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Évora · 1 hr 20 min
↳ UNESCO-listed Alentejo walled city — Roman temple · 1st–2nd c. AD, Gothic Évora Cathedral, and the Chapel of Bones · Igreja de São Francisco built with 5,000 skulls. Walkable in an afternoon; Alentejo wine and pork make the lunch stop essential.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
