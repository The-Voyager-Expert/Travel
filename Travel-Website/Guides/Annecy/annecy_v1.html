1. Château d'Annecy
↳ The dukes' castle that crowns the old town from a rocky spur. Built in stages from the 12th to 16th centuries as the seat of the Counts of Geneva, its towers and great halls now hold the town's museum of regional art and the alpine-lakes observatory.

2. Vieille Ville
↳ The "Venice of the Alps" — a maze of pastel arcaded houses, cobbled lanes and the turquoise Thiou canal threading beneath flower-hung bridges. Rue Sainte-Claire and its arcades are the heart of it, busiest on market mornings.

3. Palais de l'Isle
↳ The stone-prowed islet moored in the middle of the Thiou — Annecy's most photographed monument. The 12th-century building has been a lordly residence, a courthouse and a prison; today it houses the town's history and architecture interpretation centre.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Cathédrale Saint-Pierre
1. Cathédrale Saint-Pierre
↳ The bishop's cathedral on Rue Royale, built in stages from 1611 to 1933 in a blend of Baroque and Neoclassical styles. Saint Francis of Sales was ordained as Bishop of Geneva here in 1602. The interior is calm and vaulted, with a notable organ and 19th-century stained glass.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:30am - 12:00pm · 2:00pm - 6:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free

2. Basilique de la Visitation
↳ An Art Deco basilica on the hill above Annecy, completed in 1930 and housing the relics of Saint Francis of Sales and Saint Jane of Chantal. The elevated terrace is the finest vantage point over the city, the rooftops, and the full sweep of Lake Annecy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~30 min

3. Gorges du Fier
↳ A narrow limestone gorge carved by the Fier river, 15 km northwest of Annecy. A wooden walkway bolted to the cliff face runs 250 metres above the torrent through gaps only a few metres wide, past sculpted rock formations and the Lac des Ilettes beyond. One of the most dramatic gorge walks in the French Alps.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
⚠️ Seasonal — open April to October; closed November to March
📍 Route des Gorges · Lovagny

4. Semnoz
↳ The mountain plateau rising to 1704 m directly south of Annecy, a 20-minute taxi from the old town. The summit ridge opens to panoramic views of the lake, the Pre-Alps and, in clear weather, Mont Blanc and the full alpine chain. In summer the road reaches the top; in winter it becomes a cross-country ski area.
📖 Wikipedia

1. Talloires
↳ The most celebrated village on the lake — stone houses and terraced restaurants where the water colour deepens and La Tournette rises above the far shore. The setting attracted painters since the 19th century and earned Talloires its place as one of France's most scenic lakeside villages.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Route du Port · Talloires
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Abbaye de Talloires
2. Abbaye de Talloires
↳ A 10th-century Benedictine abbey on the lakeshore, converted to a private house in the Revolution and now an auberge. The medieval stonework and arcaded cloister facade still face the water, and the narrow lanes of the village around it are largely unchanged since the 11th century.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Place de l'Église · Talloires
🚕 15 min → Col de la Forclaz
3. Col de la Forclaz
↳ A mountain pass at 1157 m above the eastern shore — the continent's premier paragliding launch site and one of its most photographed lake viewpoints. From the belvedere the entire lake lies below, La Tournette rises behind Talloires, and dozens of coloured canopies fill the air on clear days.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
⚠️ Road to the Col closes in winter — accessible by foot year-round from Doussard or Talloires
📍 Col de la Forclaz · Montmin

4. Château de Menthon-Saint-Bernard
↳ A fairy-tale castle on a spur above the eastern shore, inhabited by the same family since the 11th century. Its towers, ramparts and great Gothic hall inspired the design of Cinderella's Castle at Disneyland. The family still lives in the upper floors; the ground rooms and terraces are open for guided tours.

💧 Why the Water Runs Clear

Lake Annecy's almost unreal turquoise isn't an accident. By the 1960s the lake was dying — sewage from the lakeside towns had turned it murky and swimming was banned. A regional syndicate ran a then-radical fix: a ring main collected every town's wastewater and carried it out of the basin entirely. The lake recovered so completely that it is now often called the cleanest large lake in Europe, fed by underwater springs and an alpine river you can see straight to the bottom of.

That same water leaves the lake through the Thiou — the canal you cross all day in the old town. At roughly 3.5 km it is one of the shortest rivers in France, and for centuries its current did all the work: it powered the mills, tanneries and forges that built medieval Annecy, which is why the Palais de l'Isle sits mid-stream like a stone ship. The postcard you photograph is really a piece of working industrial plumbing that never stopped running.
