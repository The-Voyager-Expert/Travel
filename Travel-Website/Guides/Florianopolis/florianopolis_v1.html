2. Fortaleza de São José da Ponta Grossa
↳ An 18th-century Portuguese military fortress begun in 1740 on a rocky headland at the north strait of Santa Catarina Island. Built as part of a triangular defensive system, it was restored in the 1990s and is managed by the Federal University of Santa Catarina.
📖 Wikipedia

🚕 33 min → Trilha do Morro das Aranhas
3. Trilha do Morro das Aranhas
↳ A 2.5 km coastal trail climbing to 246 m over a rocky headland that separates Praia do Santinho and Praia de Moçambique. The route passes through a Private Natural Heritage Reserve with views of the open Atlantic on both sides.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min

↳ A large coastal dune system on the east side of Santa Catarina Island separating Praia da Joaquina from Lagoa da Conceição. Sandboard rentals are available from vendors at the base of the dunes.

3. Ilha do Campeche
↳ An uninhabited island 1.6 km long containing more than 100 pre-Columbian petroglyphs across 10 archaeological sites, listed by IPHAN as National Archaeological and Landscape Heritage since 2000. Interior trails require an accredited guide available on the island.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~3 hr

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 22 min → Mirante do Morro das Pedras
1. Mirante do Morro das Pedras
↳ A hilltop viewpoint above the southern coast with views of Armação Beach, Caldeirão Beach, Lagoa do Peri, and Ilha do Campeche offshore. Between June and November this is the only accessible viewpoint in Florianópolis where southern right whales are spotted from shore.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Rodovia Francisco Thomaz dos Santos 2210 · Morro das Pedras
🚕 6 min → Lagoa do Peri
2. Lagoa do Peri
↳ The largest freshwater lagoon on the Santa Catarina coast at 5.2 km², set within a 4,274-hectare municipal park. Activities include swimming, kayaking, fishing, and hiking. The lagoon also supplies drinking water to several neighborhoods of the island.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Estrada Geral do Peri · Peri
🚶 24 min · 🚕 10 min → Lagoinha do Leste
3. Lagoinha do Leste
↳ A remote pristine beach accessible only by a 2.3 km trail from Pântano do Sul — a 75-min hike each way. A small freshwater lagoon sits behind the beach. There is no road access, which keeps the beach uncrowded even in peak season.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~3 hr
⚠️ Trail-only access · 75-min hike each way from Pântano do Sul trailhead · cash-only food kiosks open December - March and weekends only
📍 Pântano do Sul · Pântano do Sul

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Ponte Hercílio Luz
1. Ponte Hercílio Luz
↳ A 821 m suspension bridge inaugurated in 1926, for decades the only fixed link between Santa Catarina Island and the mainland. Closed to vehicles in 1991 and restored as a pedestrian promenade in 2019. Its twin towers are the most recognizable silhouette of Florianópolis.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Ponte Hercílio Luz · Centro
🚶 10 min · 🚕 3 min → Palácio Cruz e Souza
2. Palácio Cruz e Souza
↳ The former Santa Catarina state government palace, rebuilt 1894–1898 and converted into the Museu Histórico de Santa Catarina in 1986. Named after the Symbolist poet Cruz e Sousa, the interior features a Carrara marble staircase, bronze knight statues, and Art Nouveau stained glass.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Friday 10:00am - 6:00pm · Saturday - Sunday 10:00am - 4:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Free admission on Sundays
📍 Praça XV de Novembro 227 · Centro
🚶 2 min · 🚕 3 min → Catedral Metropolitana
3. Catedral Metropolitana
↳ The principal Catholic church of Florianópolis, originally founded in 1675 under the dedication to Our Lady of the Exile. Rebuilt in Neoclassical style and elevated to cathedral status in 1908, it anchors Praça XV de Novembro alongside the Palácio Cruz e Souza as the seat of the Archdiocese of Florianópolis.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Praça XV de Novembro · Centro
🚶 10 min · 🚕 3 min → hotel
Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Santo Antônio de Lisboa
1. Santo Antônio de Lisboa
↳ The oldest settled neighborhood on the island, founded by Azorean colonists in 1751. Cobblestone lanes, a whitewashed colonial church, and Azorean houses were spared from the redevelopment that reshaped the rest of Florianópolis. The waterfront has seafood restaurants and bobbin-lace artisans.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 R. Abelardo Ottoni · Santo Antônio de Lisboa
🚕 22 min → Jurerê Internacional
2. Jurerê Internacional
↳ A planned resort beach on the north coast of Santa Catarina Island with calm, clear water sheltered from Atlantic swell. The beach is known for its fine white sand, upscale beach clubs, and an open-air shopping strip — the most developed and polished stretch of coastline on the island.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Av. dos Búzios · Jurerê Internacional
🚕 15 min → Praia dos Ingleses
3. Praia dos Ingleses
↳ A 4.83 km open-ocean beach in the northeast of Santa Catarina Island named after an English ship that ran aground here in the colonial era. The right end of the beach has calm, shallow water ideal for families; the left end generates consistent surf for bodyboarding and stand-up paddleboarding.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 R. Dom João Becker · Ingleses
🚕 45 min → hotel
Day 6
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Barra da Lagoa
1. Barra da Lagoa
↳ A fishing village on the tidal channel connecting Lagoa da Conceição to the Atlantic, forming a 14 km combined beach with Praia de Moçambique. Home to a TAMAR sea turtle centre. Wooden fishing boats share the channel with swimmers; the ocean-side beach has consistent surf.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Av. Prefeito Acácio Garibaldi São Thiago · Barra da Lagoa
🚕 10 min → Praia Mole
2. Praia Mole
↳ A 960 m surf beach on the eastern coast, flanked by granite headlands and backed by the Galheta hill. Praia Mole receives consistent Atlantic swells that make it the most sought-after surf spot on the island. Paragliders land on the sand from launches off the hillside above.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Acesso Praia Mole · Praia Mole
🚕 25 min → Ribeirão da Ilha
3. Ribeirão da Ilha
↳ An 18th-century Azorean settlement in the southwestern bay, now Brazil's largest oyster-producing district. The main road is lined with waterfront restaurants serving oysters and shellfish farmed in the surrounding bays. Bobbin-lace making is still practised by local artisans.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Rod. Baldicero Filomeno · Ribeirão da Ilha
🚕 22 min → hotel

🧭 Ilha da Magia

Florianópolis earns its nickname Ilha da Magia — Magic Island — for the way it compresses an entire geography into a single destination: a 285 m hilltop looking down on two bays simultaneously, an 18th-century stone fortress at the water's edge, a coastal trail where the Atlantic wraps around both sides, a lagoon framed by dunes, and a beach that asks you to earn it with 75 minutes on foot. The three days here do not repeat themselves. Each reads differently: the north is historic and wild, the east is luminous and open, and the south is quietly remote. The city itself recedes once you leave downtown. What remains is the island.
