Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 30 min · 🚕 12 min → Stanford University Main Quad
1. Stanford University Main Quad
↳ Stanford's 1890s sandstone Romanesque quad, designed by Olmsted's firm, frames Memorial Church at its center. The church's mosaic façade is the campus's most photographed image; the Sermon on the Mount mosaic in the apse is 58 feet wide.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Serra Mall · Stanford
🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Hoover Tower
2. Hoover Tower
↳ Stanford's 285-foot Spanish Colonial tower · 1941, home to the Hoover Institution. The observation deck delivers a 360° panorama: SF Bay, Mount Tamalpais north, Santa Cruz Mountains south, and on clear days the Sierra Nevada east.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 4:30pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 590 Escondido Mall · Stanford
🚶 8 min · 🚕 7 min → Cantor Arts Center
3. Cantor Arts Center
↳ Stanford's art museum · 38,000 works, 5,000 years is anchored by the outdoor Rodin Sculpture Garden — largest Rodin bronze collection outside Paris, with a full cast of The Gates of Hell, The Thinker, and The Burghers of Calais.

↳ The main spine of Palo Alto — Google, Facebook, and PayPal all started on this corridor. Best at lunch: Coupa Café for Venezuelan arepas and single-origin coffee, Baumé for French tasting menus, Sushi Tomi for Tsukiji-trained sashimi.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 50 min → Pier 33 · San Francisco
1. Alcatraz Island
↳ Federal penitentiary 1934–1963, housing Al Capone, the Birdman, and Machine Gun Kelly. The cellhouse audio tour narrated by former guards and inmates is among the finest in the US. Ferry from Pier 33, a 15 minute crossing. Book two weeks ahead from May through October.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:40am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~2h 30 min
⚠️ Book two weeks ahead May through October — the official ferry sells out
📍 Pier 33 · San Francisco
🚶 12 min · 🚕 10 min → Fisherman's Wharf
2. Fisherman's Wharf
↳ Pier 39 hosts 300–1,000 sea lions on floating docks year-round. Live Dungeness crab from Jefferson Street tanks and chowder in sourdough bowls are the authentic visit. The Powell–Hyde cable car turnaround is worth the queue.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Jefferson Street · San Francisco
🚶 20 min · 🚕 8 min → Coit Tower
3. Coit Tower
↳ A 210-foot column on Telegraph Hill with a view to Alcatraz. Inside: 19 WPA murals · 1934 by 26 artists depicting California farm workers. The Filbert Steps trail from the base passes a garden with a wild flock of cherry-headed conures.
📖 Wikipedia

🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1h

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 15min → Muir Woods National Monument
1. Muir Woods National Monument
↳ Old-growth coast redwoods reaching 258 feet in a quiet canyon at the foot of Mount Tamalpais. The boardwalk loops past Cathedral Grove and Redwood Creek. The tallest trees germinated around 1,000 years ago — already mature when the Crusades began.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2h
⚠️ Parking and the shuttle must be reserved in advance — no first-come parking at the monument
📍 1 Muir Woods Rd · Mill Valley
🚕 15 min → Sausalito
2. Sausalito
↳ A Mediterranean-feeling waterfront town across the Golden Gate from SF, with the city skyline framed across the bay. Bridgeway's galleries and cafés line the marina, and the Gate 5 houseboat community — a 1960s artists' colony — floats at the north end.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Bridgeway · Sausalito
🚕 1h 10min → hotel
Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 15min → Cannery Row · Monterey
1. Cannery Row
↳ The sardine-packing waterfront John Steinbeck made famous, on Monterey Bay. The old Hovden Cannery now houses the Monterey Bay Aquarium, and the boardwalk runs past tide pools, sea otters, and the recreated cannery façades along the bay.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Cannery Row · Monterey
🚕 15 min → 17-Mile Drive
2. 17-Mile Drive
↳ A scenic toll road looping through Pebble Beach between Pacific Grove and Carmel-by-the-Sea. The Lone Cypress on its granite headland is the defining image; the drive passes Bird Rock, Spanish Bay, and Pebble Beach Golf Links, ending near storybook Carmel.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2h
⚠️ Toll road — a per-vehicle gate fee is charged at each entrance
📍 17-Mile Drive · Pebble Beach
🚕 1h 20min → hotel
Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 25min → Napa Valley
1. Napa Valley
↳ California's most celebrated wine appellation, 30 miles of rolling vineyard hills along the Silverado Trail and Highway 29. It made its name at the 1976 Paris Tasting, outscoring top Bordeaux and Burgundy blind. The scenic drive between Napa, Yountville, and Oakville is the day.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~3h
📍 Napa Valley · Yountville
🚕 35 min → Castello di Amorosa
2. Castello di Amorosa
↳ A faithful 13th-century Tuscan castle built over 14 years on a Calistoga hillside — 107 rooms, a moat, drawbridge, frescoed great hall, and a genuine dungeon. The architecture and the valley views from the ramparts are the draw, beyond the working winery inside.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 4045 St Helena Hwy · Calistoga
🚕 1h 40min → hotel

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Allied Arts Guild
1. Allied Arts Guild
↳ A 1929 Spanish Colonial complex in Menlo Park — two Stanford graduates built whitewashed studios and courtyards modeled on the Alcázar of Seville. A dozen artisan studios · potters, weavers, jewelers are still active.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Saturday 10:00am - 5:00pm

2. Computer History Museum
↳ 100,000 objects, 2,000 years of computing — from a Sumerian clay tablet to the original Google servers. Highlights: a working Babbage Difference Engine, the PDP-1 · first video game, a Cray-1 supercomputer, and Alan Turing materials.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Wednesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday - Tuesday

3. Shoreline at Mountain View
↳ A 750-acre wildlife refuge on SF Bay — reclaimed landfill now wetland habitat for 300+ bird species. The lake loop trail runs along Shoreline Lake with unbroken Santa Cruz Mountains views. Google's campus borders the park to the south.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 50 min → Ferry Building · San Francisco
1. Ferry Building & Embarcadero
↳ The 1898 Embarcadero landmark — a sandstone clock tower modeled on the Giralda in Seville. Inside is SF's anchor public market hall · artisan cheeses, oysters, Acme bread, Blue Bottle Coffee; a producer-only farmers market fills the plaza Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
🆓 Free
📍 1 Ferry Building · San Francisco
🚕 12 min → Painted Ladies & Alamo Square
2. Painted Ladies & Alamo Square
↳ Seven Queen Anne–style Victorians on the east side of Alamo Square · 1892–1896 — the postcard view frames the row against the modern SF skyline rising behind them. Famous from the opening credits of Full House. Alamo Square Park is a hilltop with 360° city views.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Steiner Street · San Francisco
🚕 10 min → Lombard Street & Powell-Hyde Cable Car
3. Lombard Street & Powell-Hyde Cable Car
↳ The "crookedest street in the world" — eight switchback turns dropping down Russian Hill between Hyde and Leavenworth, lined with hydrangeas. Reach it via the Powell-Hyde Cable Car · itself a National Historic Landmark, which climbs Nob Hill and crests right at the top of the Lombard zigzag.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~1h 30 min

Dungeness Crab
↳ Sweet firm Pacific cold-water crab with a distinctive brininess. Season runs November through June; May is prime. SF's crab tradition goes back to Italian immigrant fishermen at the waterfront. Served cracked with butter and sourdough.
📍 Fisherman's Wharf Crab Stand
San Francisco Sourdough
↳ Chemically distinct from other sourdough: wild yeast and Lactobacillus sanfranciscensis produce the sharp sourness and thick crust. Boudin Bakery has maintained a continuous starter since 1849 — the same culture from the gold rush.
📍 Boudin Bakery
Mission Burrito
↳ Invented in SF's Mission District in the 1960s — a large flour tortilla steamed and wrapped around rice, beans, meat, salsa, guacamole, and sour cream in a foil sleeve. Bigger than a standard burrito, with rice, always foil-wrapped.
📍 La Taqueria
Clam Chowder In A Sourdough Bowl
↳ Thick New England-style chowder in a hollowed-out sourdough boule — the bread soaks up the chowder as you eat. Popularized at Fisherman's Wharf walk-up stands in the 1970s. Good chowder: fresh clams and enough cream to coat a spoon.
📍 Boudin Bakery
