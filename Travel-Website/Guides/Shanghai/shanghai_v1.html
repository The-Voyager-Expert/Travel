🍮 Local Tastes
Xiao Long Bao
↳ Shanghai's signature soup dumpling — a pleated parcel of pork and hot broth steamed in bamboo baskets. Bite a small hole and sip the broth first. Nanxiang Mantou Dian in Yuyuan is the original house.
📍 Nanxiang Mantou Dian · Din Tai Fung
Sheng Jian Bao
↳ Pan-fried dumplings with a crispy sesame-studded base and a soft steamed top, filled with pork and hot broth. Cooked in a flat iron pan and eaten with vinegar and ginger. Da Hu Chun is the old master.
📍 Da Hu Chun
Hong Shao Rou
↳ Red-braised pork belly slow-cooked in soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, rock sugar and star anise until meltingly tender and lacquered in a dark glaze — the defining dish of Shanghainese home cooking.
📍 Lü Bo Lang
Cong You Ban Mian
↳ Scallion oil noodles tossed with rendered scallion oil, soy sauce and crispy fried scallions — supremely simple and unmistakably Shanghainese. A ten-second dish that takes an hour of slow frying to perfect.
📍 Noodle Bull · Yang's Noodle House
Tang Bao
↳ A giant soup dumpling the size of a small balloon, served with a straw to drink the pork or crab broth through before eating the wrapper. A theatrical Shanghai specialty unique to this city.
📍 Jia Jia Tang Bao
Da Zha Xie
↳ Hairy crab from Yangcheng Lake — a short-season delicacy eaten steamed with ginger vinegar. Females peak in October and males in November. If visiting in autumn, this is the dish.
📍 Wang Bao He

🎩 Shanghai in Two Directions

Shanghai is the rare city that holds two worlds in a single glance: stand on the Bund at dusk and the 52 colonial facades of old trading empires glow in the last light while, across 400 metres of brown river, the futurist towers of Pudong flicker to life. The city has been reinventing itself at speed for 200 years — swallowing fishing village, treaty port, jazz age, revolution and megacity in rapid succession — and somehow it never loses its appetite for what comes next.

Eat a sheng jian bao for breakfast, a hairy crab in autumn and xiao long bao whenever the queue is short enough. Follow Wukang Road until you find a doorway worth stopping at. There will always be one.
