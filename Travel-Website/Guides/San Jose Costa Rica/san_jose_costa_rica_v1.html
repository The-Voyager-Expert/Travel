1. Museo Nacional de Costa Rica
↳ The national museum occupies a restored 1910 fortress set in peaceful grounds. Exhibits span Costa Rican history, archaeology, art, and natural history across galleries linked by covered colonnades. The whitewashed stone exterior and turrets are as notable as the pre-Columbian artifacts and colonial art inside.

2. Mercado Central
↳ The Central Market is a sprawling covered arcade of vendor stalls where locals shop for produce, flowers, spices, and traditional Costa Rican goods. The energy and chaos are deliberate · vendors hawk their wares, the air fills with the scent of fresh produce and flowers, and the pace is distinctly San José.

3. Teatro Nacional
↳ Costa Rica's national theater sits at the heart of downtown San José. The ornate exterior clad in imported Italian tile is instantly recognizable. Inside, red velvet, gold accents, and a painted ceiling make for an opulent setting. Evening performances are ideal, but daytime tours show the architecture and interior detail.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1 hr
1. Poás Volcano
↳ Poás is one of the world's most active volcanoes. A 20-minute walk from the parking area reaches the crater overlook where the dramatic 1.5 km basin is often clouded in mist, but on clear days you can see steaming vents and turquoise crater lakes. The surrounding park has short cloud-forest trails and observation decks.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~2 hrs

2. La Paz Waterfall Gardens
↳ A private reserve with multiple waterfalls, wildlife viewing, and landscaped gardens. The main attraction is the Five Falls trail system where you descend through cloud forest to view five separate cascades. The gardens also feature a butterfly conservatory, hummingbird feeders, and a serpent exhibit. The setting and scale rival national park experiences.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛 10am - 5pm Daily
⏰ ~2.5 hrs

3. Sarapiquí River
↳ A wide, scenic river that winds through lowland rainforest. Rafting here ranges from mild Class II-III rapids suitable for families to guided wildlife spotting from boats or kayaks. The river is rich with wildlife — macaws, toucans, crocodiles, and monkeys — and a float or mild rapids trip is a quintessential rainforest experience without the intensity of more remote rivers.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 2.5 hrs
1. Carara National Park
↳ Carara is one of Costa Rica's premier protected rainforests, known for its transition zone between dry and humid forests and exceptional wildlife density. The main attraction is the Scarlet Macaw trail, a 2.5 km loop through primary forest where large macaw flocks are virtually guaranteed. The park also protects crocodile habitats, howler monkeys, and 140+ bird species.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛 8am - 4pm Daily
⏰ ~3 hrs
📍 Puntarenas Province · Costa Rica
🚕 20 min
2. Tarcoles Bridge
↳ A simple bridge over the Tarcoles River offers one of Costa Rica's most concentrated crocodile-viewing opportunities. The river below is home to large American crocodiles — sometimes 10-15 can be spotted sunbathing on the riverbanks at once. Views are best in early morning or late afternoon when crocodiles are most active.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~45 min

3. Manuel Antonio National Park
↳ Manuel Antonio combines pristine beaches with rainforest trails in one compact, walkable park. The main beach is backed by jungle, providing opportunities to spot sloths, monkeys, and tropical birds. Trails range from easy sand walks to more demanding climbs to ridge viewpoints. The park is also a nesting site for sea turtles.
