1. Getty Villa
↳ A museum dedicated to the arts and cultures of ancient Greece, Rome, and Etruria, housed in a recreated 1st-century Roman villa. The collection spans 44,000 objects — sculptures, mosaics, decorative arts, and manuscripts — acquired by J. Paul Getty beginning in 1945.

2. Malibu Lagoon State Beach
↳ A California State Park where Malibu Creek meets the Pacific, encompassing a protected coastal lagoon and a beach recognized as one of the premier longboard surf breaks in the world. Three surf points generate long right-hand waves that established the international surfing reputation of this coastline.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Adamson House
3. Adamson House and Malibu Lagoon Museum
↳ A 1929 Moorish Spanish Colonial Revival residence designed by Stiles O. Clements for the Rindge family. Notable for its Malibu Potteries tile on floors, walls, fountains, and facades — the last major production run of the manufacturer founded by May K. Rindge. Designated a National Historic Landmark in 1977.
📖 Wikipedia

4. Point Dume State Beach and Preserve
↳ A 34-acre California state preserve on a volcanic headland rising 200 feet above the Pacific. A trail leads to the clifftop, a documented observation point for gray whale migration in winter and California sea lion haul-outs on the rocks below. The beach includes tide pools accessible via stairs.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 18 min → Solstice Canyon
1. Solstice Canyon
↳ A hiking area preserving Roberts Ranch, owned by supermarket chain founders Fred and Florence Roberts from 1932. Architect Paul Williams designed the family's ranch house around the canyon's creek and a waterfall in 1952; a 1982 wildfire left only its foundation, reached today by an easy trail.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
🆓 Free

🚕 5 min → Zuma Beach
2. Zuma Beach
↳ The largest county-run beach in Los Angeles County, with 1.8 miles of wide sand acquired by the county in 1941 after tax foreclosure on the Marblehead Land Company. The 1959 film Gidget drew crowds of surfers here through the 1960s, and the beach remains one of the region's cleanest stretches of coastline.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 30000 Pacific Coast Hwy · Malibu
🚕 6 min → El Matador State Beach
3. El Matador State Beach
↳ The most photographed of the three pocket beaches making up Robert H. Meyer Memorial State Beach, reached by a steep bluff-top staircase. Sea stacks and collapsed sea caves rise directly from the sand, casting shifting shadows through the day and drawing photographers at sunset.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 32350 Pacific Coast Hwy · Malibu
🚕 5 min → Leo Carrillo State Park
4. Leo Carrillo State Park
↳ A 2,513-acre park named for actor and conservationist Leo Carrillo, who served on the California State Parks Commission. Sea caves, tide pools, and reef formations line 1.5 miles of beach at the Los Angeles-Ventura county line; the Woolsey Fire burned through nearly the entire park in November 2018.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min

1. King Gillette Ranch
↳ A 588-acre former estate built in the 1920s for razor magnate King C. Gillette, its buildings designed by architect Wallace Neff. Later a Catholic seminary for a quarter-century, the property opened to the public in 2007 and now serves as the main visitor center for the Santa Monica Mountains.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Malibu Creek State Park
↳ An 8,215-acre park preserving the Malibu Creek canyon, home to the Chumash for millennia before Twentieth Century Fox used its rock formations and meadows as filming locations for M*A*S*H, Planet of the Apes, and more than 100 other productions. Trails lead to the Rock Pool and Century Lake.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 1925 Las Virgenes Rd · Calabasas
🚕 22 min → Topanga State Park
3. Topanga State Park
↳ At 11,000 acres, the largest state park within the boundaries of a major American city, reaching from Topanga Canyon to within a few miles of the Pacific. Trails from the Trippet Ranch entrance climb to viewpoints including Eagle Rock and Parker Mesa Overlook, with sightlines to the ocean and the San Fernando Valley.
📖 Wikipedia

🌊 On Malibu

Malibu is not a city in the conventional sense — it is 27 miles of Pacific coastline incorporated in 1991 primarily to give residents control over development. That tension between private land and public beach runs through everything here. The Rindge family spent decades trying to keep the public off what they believed was their private coast; their legal battles produced the estate system, the celebrity enclave, and eventually the Adamson House and its tile collection, which survived only because Rhoda Rindge built on the lagoon rather than the bluff.

The Getty Villa is a separate provenance entirely — an oil fortune translated into a replica Roman villa stuffed with antiquities — but it sits on the same coast for the same reason: the light, the views, the feeling that wealth and the Pacific belong together. Point Dume is what was here before any of that.
