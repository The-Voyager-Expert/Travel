Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 2 min · 🚕 1 min → Manuel Antonio National Park
1. Manuel Antonio National Park
↳ The park protects 1,625 hectares of primary rainforest and four white-sand beaches on the Central Pacific coast. White-faced capuchins, three-toed sloths, squirrel monkeys, and scarlet macaws are spotted at trail level. Playa Manuel Antonio is the main beach — calm water and a tombolo connecting to Punta Catedral.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Quepos
1. Quepos
↳ A working fishing town and sportfishing hub on the Pacific coast. Marina Pez Vela occupies the waterfront — 300 slips, open walkways over the water, and unobstructed views of the bay and forested hills. The compact town centre holds a covered market, central park, and the flat grid of Barrio El Carmen.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Marina Pez Vela · Quepos
🚕 10 min → Isla Damas
2. Isla Damas
↳ A mangrove island in the Damas Estuary, 6 km north of Quepos. Boat tours navigate narrow tidal channels through 800+ hectares of red mangrove forest where crocodiles bask, white-faced monkeys move through the canopy, anteaters cling to roots, and kingfishers and herons work the waterline.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h → Nauyaca Waterfalls
1. Nauyaca Waterfalls
↳ Twin-tiered waterfall on a private reserve inland from Dominical. The upper fall drops 45 metres into a wide pool; the lower fall drops a further 15 metres. A 3.2 km trail from the trailhead crosses secondary forest and cattle pasture. Swimming is permitted in both pools.

🍮 Local Tastes
Gallo Pinto
↳ Black beans and rice cooked together with Salsa Lizano, served with eggs, natilla, and fried plantains at breakfast. The Costa Rican national dish — every soda in Quepos serves it.
📍 Soda Sanchez · Quepos
Casado
↳ The standard Costa Rican lunch plate: white rice and black beans side by side, a protein, patacones, and salad. Best value at any local soda.
📍 Soda Sanchez · Quepos
Ceviche
↳ Raw corvina or mahi-mahi marinated in lime juice with onion, cilantro, and jalapeño, served cold with soda crackers. The Central Pacific coast standard.
📍 El Patio de Café Milagro · Playa Espadilla
Patacones
↳ Twice-fried green plantain rounds, flattened and salted, served as a side or topped with beans, cheese, or guacamole. Ubiquitous across every meal type.
📍 Soda Sanchez · Quepos
Café de Tarrazú
↳ Highland Arabica from the Tarrazú region south of San José — one of the world's most-exported specialty coffees. Clean, bright, with low bitterness. Café Milagro has been roasting Tarrazú since 1994.

🌿 Where the Forest Meets the Water

The park is one of the few places where primary rainforest and white-sand beach share the same footpath. You walk through dense canopy — sloths overhead, capuchins two metres away on a parallel branch — and step out onto Playa Manuel Antonio without the scenery changing register. The transition is so abrupt it feels designed.

Day 2 inverts this: the Damas mangroves are the park's wilder, quieter inverse. No beach, no casual swimmers — just 800 hectares of tidal channels that the forest has grown down into. The boat navigates at a crocodile's pace because that is the only pace the place allows.
