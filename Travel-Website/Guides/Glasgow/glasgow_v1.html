✨ A City That Makes Things

Glasgow rewards walking with your head tilted up. The wealth of the shipbuilding and tobacco eras went straight into stone — red and blonde sandstone tenements, the muscular City Chambers, the spires of the old town — so the city centre reads like an open-air gallery of Victorian ambition. Then it keeps surprising you: Zaha Hadid's silver wave on the Clyde, the Kibble Palace's iron bubble of ferns, a medieval cathedral that quietly outlasted the Reformation.

It is also a city that makes things and feeds you well while doing it — a Cantonese institution older than most of its rivals, a Michelin kitchen above a tenement, a teacake empire in the suburbs. Give the West End an unhurried afternoon, take the little orange Subway at least once for the fun of it, and save an evening for a whisky bar where the list runs to four figures.
