## DAY 1 — City Centre / Cathedral (all FREE)

### 1. George Square  — `George_Square` — FREE — 🏛 Open 24/7 — ⏰ ~20 min — 📍 George Square · City Centre
↳ Glasgow's central civic square (laid out 1781), ringed by grand Victorian architecture incl. the City Chambers; the city's principal gathering and ceremonial space.
⚠️ FLAG: Under an 18-month redevelopment — the 11 statues were removed (in storage Jul 2025–Jul 2027); square may still be hoarded/under works during a June 2026 visit. Describe the square, not the monuments. **Selection question: a fenced works-site is a weak stop — consider trimming to a brief pass-through or swapping.**
📸 https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/A_beautiful_day_in_George_Square_%2841897130182%29.jpg/800px-A_beautiful_day_in_George_Square_%2841897130182%29.jpg — CC BY 2.0 — alt "George Square, Glasgow"

### 2. Glasgow Cathedral (St Mungo's) — `Glasgow_Cathedral` — FREE (donations) — ⏰ ~45 min — 📍 Castle Street · Townhead
↳ Magnificent medieval cathedral, the only Scottish mainland medieval cathedral to survive the Reformation largely intact, built over the tomb of St Mungo.
🏛 1 Apr–30 Sep: Daily 9:30am–5:00pm (Sun opens 1:00pm); 1 Oct–31 Mar: Daily 10:00am–4:00pm (Sun opens 1:00pm). Lunch close Mon–Sat 12:30–1:30pm. (HES; working church.)
🚫 Restricted Sunday mornings (worship only).
📸 https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Glasgow_Cathedral_-_exterior.jpg/800px-Glasgow_Cathedral_-_exterior.jpg — CC BY-SA 4.0 — alt "Glasgow Cathedral"

### 3. Glasgow Necropolis — `Glasgow_Necropolis` — FREE — ⏰ ~45 min — 📍 Castle Street · Townhead
↳ Dramatic Victorian garden cemetery on a hill beside the cathedral, ~50,000 burials and elaborate monuments, reached via the Bridge of Sighs.
🏛 Daily 7:00am–4:30pm (Friends of Glasgow Necropolis).
📸 https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Glasgow_Necropolis_%28HDR%29_%288038799566%29.jpg/800px-Glasgow_Necropolis_%28HDR%29_%288038799566%29.jpg — CC BY 2.0 — alt "Glasgow Necropolis"

### 4. Provand's Lordship — `Provand's_Lordship` — FREE — ⏰ ~30 min — 📍 Castle Street · Townhead
↳ The oldest house in Glasgow (built 1471), one of only four surviving medieval buildings in the city; 17th-c. furniture, royal portraits, St Nicholas Garden behind.
🏛 Tue–Thu & Sat 10:00am–5:00pm; Fri & Sun 11:00am–5:00pm. (Monday status ambiguous across sources — omit Monday claim / pin to conservative open days.)
📸 https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Provand%27s_Lordship%2C_Glasgow.jpg/800px-Provand%27s_Lordship%2C_Glasgow.jpg — Attribution license — alt "Provand's Lordship"

## DAY 2 — West End (all FREE)

### 1. Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum — `Kelvingrove_Art_Gallery_and_Museum` — FREE — ⏰ ~2 hr — 📍 Argyle Street · West End (Kelvingrove)
↳ Glasgow's most-visited free attraction (opened 1901), 22 galleries: art, natural history, Ancient Egypt, arms & armour, Mackintosh; Dalí's Christ of St John of the Cross; free daily organ recitals.
🏛 Mon–Thu 10:00am–5:00pm; Fri 11:00am–5:00pm; Sat 10:00am–5:00pm; Sun 11:00am–5:00pm. Open daily. (Restoration works ongoing, museum open.)
📸 (interior) https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/General_internal_view._Kelvingrove_Art_Gallery_and_Museum%2C_Glasgow%2C_Scotland..JPG/800px-General_internal_view._Kelvingrove_Art_Gallery_and_Museum%2C_Glasgow%2C_Scotland..JPG — CC BY-SA 4.0 — alt "Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum"

### 2. University of Glasgow (Gilbert Scott Building / Cloisters) — `University_of_Glasgow` — FREE — ⏰ ~1 hr — 📍 University Avenue · West End (Gilmorehill)
↳ Founded 1451; Gothic Revival main building (Gilbert Scott, 1870s) crowns Gilmorehill. The vaulted Cloisters link the East & West Quadrangles to Bute Hall and the Hunterian Museum; featured in Outlander & Cloud Atlas.
🏛 Cloisters/campus publicly accessible daily; welcome point Mon 9:30am–5:00pm, Tue–Thu 9:00am–5:00pm, Fri 9:00am–4:00pm. (Official site no longer publishes an exact daily cloisters table — flag.) Optional ticketed SRC guided tours (glasgowunisrc.org), not required for entry.
📸 (interior cloisters) https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Cloisters%2C_University_Of_Glasgow.jpg/800px-Cloisters%2C_University_Of_Glasgow.jpg — CC BY-SA 4.0 — alt "University of Glasgow Cloisters"

### 3. Glasgow Botanic Gardens (Kibble Palace) — `Glasgow_Botanic_Gardens` — FREE — ⏰ ~1 hr 30 min — 📍 730 Great Western Road · West End (Kelvinside)
↳ 20-ha West End gardens (1842), 9,000+ plants; centrepiece the A-listed Kibble Palace, a curved Victorian wrought-iron glasshouse (re-erected 1873) with tree ferns, carnivorous plants, koi pond and marble statues.
🏛 Grounds daily 7:00am–dusk; glasshouses (Kibble Palace) Apr–Sep 10:00am–6:00pm, winter 10:00am–4:15pm. Open daily.
📸 (interior Kibble Palace) https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Inside_Kibble_Palace_Glasshouse.JPG/800px-Inside_Kibble_Palace_Glasshouse.JPG — CC BY-SA 3.0 — alt "Kibble Palace"

## DAY 3 — Clyde Waterfront

### 1. Riverside Museum — `Riverside_Museum` — FREE — ⏰ ~1 hr 30 min — 📍 100 Pointhouse Place · Partick (Clyde waterfront)
↳ Award-winning transport & technology museum in Zaha Hadid's wave-roofed building at the Kelvin/Clyde confluence — locomotives, trams, cars, ships, and a recreated cobbled Glasgow street.
🏛 Mon–Thu 10:00am–5:00pm; Fri 11:00am–5:00pm; Sat 10:00am–5:00pm; Sun 11:00am–5:00pm. Open daily.
📸 (Zaha Hadid facade — building is the attraction) https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Riverside_Museum_Glasgow_EC2018.jpg/800px-Riverside_Museum_Glasgow_EC2018.jpg — CC BY-SA 4.0 — alt "Riverside Museum"

### 2. The Tall Ship Glenlee — `Glenlee_(barque)` — TICKETED (changed 2026) — ⏰ ~1 hr — 📍 150 Pointhouse Place · Partick (beside Riverside Museum)
↳ 1896 Clyde-built three-masted steel barque, one of only five surviving Clyde sailing ships afloat; roam the decks, cabins and hold; on-board café.
🎟 Booking venue-direct: thetallship.com (now ticketed; Adult £4.50 — money never ships in guide). (GYG listing exists but per-product rating/review not verifiable → use venue-direct, shape 2b/2c.)
🏛 Daily 10:00am–5:00pm (Tue opens 11:00am). Last admission 30 min before close.
📸 https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/1._The_Tall_Ship_Glenlee_beside_the_Riverside_Museum%2C_Glasgow%2C_Scotland.jpg/800px-1._The_Tall_Ship_Glenlee_beside_the_Riverside_Museum%2C_Glasgow%2C_Scotland.jpg — CC BY-SA 4.0 — alt "The Tall Ship Glenlee"
