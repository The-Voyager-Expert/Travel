2. Arena di Verona
↳ One of the best-preserved Roman amphitheatres in the world, built by Rome to seat 30,000. Its pink-limestone arcades still ring Piazza Bra and stage the city's summer opera festival.

1. Casa di Giulietta
↳ The medieval courtyard a Victorian legend attached to Juliet Capulet — whose balcony is the most photographed spot in Verona. The house is a museum of Romeo and Juliet, the city and the letters that have been left here since the 1930s.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Piazza dei Signori
↳ Verona's governmental square, ringed by the medieval Palazzo del Podestà, the Renaissance Loggia del Consiglio and the Gothic Palazzo della Ragione. Dante's statue stands at the centre — he took refuge here as a guest of the Scaligeri in exile.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Piazza dei Signori · Centro Storico
🚶 1 min · 🚕 2 min → Arche Scaligere
3. Arche Scaligere
↳ The Gothic funerary monuments of the Scaligeri lords, erected over the family church of Santa Maria Antica in the 13th and 14th centuries. The wrought-iron fence and canopied tombs — some topped with equestrian figures — are the finest Gothic funerary sculpture in Italy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Via Santa Maria Antica · Centro Storico
🚶 18 min · 🚕 8 min → Basilica di San Zeno Maggiore
4. Basilica di San Zeno Maggiore
↳ Verona's greatest Romanesque church, built in the 12th century on the grave of the city's patron saint. The bronze doors and rose window on the façade, and Mantegna's altarpiece inside, make it one of the finest Romanesque ensembles in northern Italy.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Sant'Anastasia
1. Sant'Anastasia
↳ Verona's largest church, a Gothic Dominican basilica begun in 1290 and completed two centuries later. Its interior holds Pisanello's fresco of St George and the Princess — one of the masterpieces of International Gothic painting in Italy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Saturday 9:00am - 6:00pm · Sunday 1:00pm - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Piazza di Sant'Anastasia · Centro Storico
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Cattedrale di Verona
2. Cattedrale di Verona
↳ Verona's Romanesque cathedral, flanked by the baptistery of San Giovanni in Fonte and the church of Sant'Elena. Titian's Assumption of the Virgin hangs in the first chapel on the left — one of the most important paintings in the Veneto outside a museum.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Saturday 10:00am - 5:30pm · Sunday 1:00pm - 5:30pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Piazza Duomo 21 · Centro Storico
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Teatro Romano
3. Teatro Romano
↳ A Roman theatre carved into the hillside above Ponte Pietra in the 1st century BC — the oldest performing arts venue still in use in Italy. The terrace above holds the finest panorama of Verona's rooftops, river bends and the surrounding hills.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Teatro Romano e Museo Archeologico

🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Giardino Giusti
4. Giardino Giusti
↳ A Renaissance garden laid out in 1570 on the hillside above the Adige, with clipped cypress avenues, grottos and a belvedere at the top. Goethe walked here in 1786 and called it the finest garden he had seen — the view over Verona bears him out.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr

💌 City of Star-Crossed Lovers

Verona trades on a love story that never happened — Shakespeare set Romeo and Juliet here without ever seeing the place — yet the city wears the myth so gracefully that it feels true. The real romance is the stone: a Roman arena that still fills with opera under the stars, a Roman bridge rebuilt arch by arch from the river after the war, a market square laid over a forum that has never stopped being the heart of town.

Walk it in a single day and Verona reveals its trick — everything is close, everything overlaps. From the tower you see the whole loop you just walked: the castle on the bend, the amphitheatre's pale ellipse, the red rooftops sliding down to the Adige. Climb down, cross Ponte Pietra at dusk, and the hills above light up gold. No balcony required.
