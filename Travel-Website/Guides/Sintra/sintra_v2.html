Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 12 min → Pena Palace
1. Pena Palace
↳ The 1842 Romanticist palace crowning the Sintra hills — a riot of ochre and crimson towers, Moorish arches, and a Triton gateway, raised by Ferdinand II on the ruins of a monastery. The terraces give the widest view in Sintra. Arrive at opening to beat the queue for the palace interior.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~2h
📍 Estrada da Pena
🚶 10 min · 🚕 3 min → Moorish Castle
2. Moorish Castle
↳ 8th-century battlements built by the Moors, snaking along the ridge across the valley from Pena. The walk along the restored ramparts gives a falcon's view over the village, the Atlantic, and the palaces. Steep, uneven stone steps — proper shoes matter.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Castelo dos Mouros
🚶 30 min · 🚕 8 min → Quinta da Regaleira
3. Quinta da Regaleira
↳ An 1910 estate by Luigi Manini for a Freemasonry-obsessed millionaire. Centrepiece: the Initiation Well — a 27-metre spiral tower descending into grottos and tunnels. The underground network is easy to get turned around in — pace yourself.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 7:30pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Rua Barbosa du Bocage 5
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Sintra National Palace
4. Sintra National Palace
↳ Iberia's oldest palace in continuous royal use — twin conical chimneys above the village. Inside: Manueline ceilings, Moorish azulejo tiles from the 1400s, gilded Swan Chamber. Arrive at opening or late afternoon to avoid the midday crush.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~1h
📍 Largo Rainha Dona Amélia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Monserrate Palace
1. Monserrate Palace
↳ A Moorish-Gothic-Indian fantasy palace built in the 1860s for English millionaire Francis Cook, set in botanical gardens of palms, ferns, and waterfalls gathered from across the Empire. The roofs are under restoration with scaffolding on the monument; the interiors and gardens stay open.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Park and Palace of Monserrate · 4.1⭐ · 14+ reviews — Viator
🏛️ Daily 9:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1h 30 min
📍 Estrada de Monserrate
🚕 20 min → Cabo da Roca
2. Cabo da Roca
↳ The westernmost point of mainland Europe — a granite headland where the cliffs drop 140 m to the Atlantic, crowned by a working lighthouse and a stone marker quoting Camões: where the land ends and the sea begins. A café and souvenir kiosk are the only other buildings.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:30pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Estrada do Cabo da Roca · Colares
🚕 20 min → Boca do Inferno
3. Boca do Inferno
↳ The "Mouth of Hell" — a collapsed sea cave on the Cascais coast where the Atlantic surges through a stone arch and explodes against the cliffs. A short railed loop circles the chasm; loudest and most dramatic at high tide. Free to visit and open around the clock.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Av. Rei Humberto II de Itália · Cascais
🚶 18 min · 🚕 5 min → Cascais
4. Cascais
↳ The fishing village turned royal resort where Portugal's kings summered in the 1870s. A walkable old town of cobbled lanes and tiled mansions opens onto a curved bay of bobbing boats, with the Cidadela fortress and a palm-lined promenade along the seafront. End the day with sunset over the water.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Largo Luís de Camões · Cascais

🍮 Local Tastes
Travesseiro & Queijada
↳ Sintra's two signature pastries. The travesseiro is a pillow of puff pastry filled with egg-and-almond cream; the queijada is a dense tart of fresh cheese, egg, and cinnamon — Sintra's original sweet, made since the Middle Ages.
📍 Piriquita
Pastel de Nata
↳ The custard tart. Flaky concave pastry shell, warm egg-custard centre with a caramelised top, dusted with cinnamon. Best eaten within minutes of leaving the oven.

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Lisbon · 40 minutes
↳ Portugal's capital — Alfama's Moorish lanes, the Manueline Belém Tower and Jerónimos Monastery, and Pombaline Baixa rebuilt after the 1755 earthquake. CP Linha de Sintra runs four trains an hour direct to Rossio Station in the centre.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Cascais · 1 hour 10 minutes
↳ The fishing village turned royal retreat on the Estoril coast — old town, harbour, the Boca do Inferno blowhole, and the wild Atlantic of Praia do Guincho. Two-train route via Cais do Sodré in Lisbon.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Setubal · 1 hr 25 min
↳ A working port at the mouth of the Sado estuary and the gateway to the Serra da Arrabida — limestone cliffs above turquoise coves, a Moorish hilltop fort, and the Igreja de Jesus with the earliest Manueline stonework in Portugal. Train via Lisboa Oriente and Fertagus.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Evora · 1 hr 40 min
↳ A UNESCO-listed Alentejan city — a Roman temple of Diana standing in the middle of town, a Gothic cathedral, the Chapel of Bones, and a medieval aqueduct. AP and IC trains depart Lisboa Oriente direct to Évora station.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
Coimbra · 2 hr 30 min
↳ Portugal's medieval university city on the Mondego river — the Baroque Biblioteca Joanina, the Romanesque Se Velha cathedral, and the student quarter where fado originated. Alfa Pendular trains depart Lisboa Oriente direct to Coimbra-B station.
🎫 book at: cp.pt or Omio
