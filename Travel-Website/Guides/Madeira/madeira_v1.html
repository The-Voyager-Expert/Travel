Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 15 min · 🚕 5 min → Teleférico do Funchal
1. Teleférico do Funchal
↳ Gondolas glide 1,800 m over Funchal's rooftops to Monte village, 560 m up. The 15-minute ascent reveals the harbour and Atlantic below. At the top: Monte Palace garden at your feet, the wicker toboggan run to the right, and a square with coffee. Ride up and walk back, or take the toboggan for the descent.

🚶 8 min · 🚕 5 min → Monte Palace Tropical Garden
2. Monte Palace Tropical Garden
↳ Palace across 70,000 m² of terraced hillside: tile-work fountains, Zen lake crossings, 100,000 cacti in a greenhouse, and free-roaming peacocks. Inside: Portuguese azulejo tiles and South African art across six centuries. Start at the top terrace and work down through the Zen garden to the ornamental lake.

3. Sé Cathedral
↳ Consecrated in 1514 under King Manuel I, the oldest building in Funchal. The plain exterior hides a ceiling of 1,400 ivory-inlaid Mudéjar panels, and a 16th-century side chapel. The choir stalls are the secret — reach them through the side entrance.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~45 min

🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Rua de Santa Maria
4. Rua de Santa Maria
↳ Funchal's oldest street, rebuilt as an open-air gallery. Over 200 wooden doors painted by local and international artists — abstracts, portraits, tropical murals, fado scenes. The street still functions: small tascas, wine shops, and pastry counters open onto the lane.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Rua de Santa Maria · Zona Velha
🚶 3 min · 🚕 3 min → Forte de São Tiago
5. Forte de São Tiago
↳ The ochre-yellow fortress at the east end of Zona Velha, built in 1614 to guard Funchal's harbour. English, Dutch, and French forces tried these waters; the walls held. Walk the ramparts for an Atlantic view and a sightline back along Rua de Santa Maria. The ground floor holds rotating exhibitions.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Jardim Botânico da Madeira
1. Jardim Botânico da Madeira
↳ Eight terraced hectares above Funchal on the Reid's Hotel estate. Plants by continent of origin: cacti from the Americas beside anthuriums from Borneo beside dragon trees native to the island. The view down over Funchal's harbour to the Atlantic is built into the layout — the garden's best feature is what it frames.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Jardim Botânico da Madeira · 4.5⭐ · jardimbotanico.madeira.gov.pt

2. Cabo Girão
↳ Europe's highest sea cliff at 580 m — a glass-floored skywalk cantilevered over the drop, looking straight down to cultivated terraces worked on the cliff face below, still farmed by villagers lowered by rope. The Atlantic presses against the base. Nothing else on Madeira matches this scale.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
⚠️ Skywalk platform has paid entry

🚕 10 min → Câmara de Lobos
3. Câmara de Lobos
↳ A fishing village 9 km west of Funchal where Churchill painted the harbour repeatedly. The coloured boats still come in daily; fishermen land espada at the stone pier while poncha is poured at the same tables. Looking back up at Cabo Girão's cliffs from here, the cliff's true scale becomes legible.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 35 min → Pico do Arieiro
1. Pico do Arieiro
↳ Third-highest peak in Madeira at 1,818 m — a road-accessible summit above the clouds, looking out over volcanic ridges and peaks. On clear days the island's entire central spine is visible. The PR1 trail starts here for the 11 km ridge walk to Pico Ruivo; the summit platform views alone justify the drive.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 ER 202 · Santo da Serra
🚕 30 min → Curral das Freiras
2. Curral das Freiras
↳ A village hidden inside a volcanic caldera so deep the nuns of Santa Clara fled here from pirates in the 16th century. The Eira do Serrado viewpoint sits 800 m above the valley, looking straight down at terraced gardens ringed by vertical rock walls. The road down cuts through seven tunnels carved into the caldera.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Miradouro da Eira do Serrado · Curral das Freiras
🚕 35 min → Ribeiro Frio & Balcões Levada
3. Ribeiro Frio & Balcões Levada
↳ A mountain village at 860 m with a trout farm, from which the PR11 trail follows a levada 1.5 km through laurisilva forest to the Balcões viewpoint. The path runs beside a water channel through ancient laurel and til trees draped in moss; the viewpoint opens to Madeira's north coast valleys and the sea below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30 min
⚠️ Trail registration required before visiting

🍮 Local Tastes
Poncha
↳ Madeira's signature cocktail: aguardente de cana muddled with local honey and citrus juice. Served at every Zona Velha bar — the passion fruit version is the modern island riff.
📍 Gavião Bar · Venda da Donna Maria
Espetada
↳ Whole chunks of beef skewered on a long bay-laurel stick, salted only, then grilled over a wood fire. Served hanging on iron hooks at the table — the original Madeiran barbecue, born in the mountain villages above Funchal.
📍 Cidade Velha · O Tapassol
Bolo do Caco
↳ Flat round bread cooked on a basalt slab, served warm with garlic butter. Found at every cafe and market stall; the go-to base for sandwiches at Mercado dos Lavradores.
📍 Mercado dos Lavradores · Padaria Pastelaria Mariazinha
Madeira Wine
↳ The island's fortified wine, aged for decades in oak by fractional blending — four dryness levels from Sercial to Malmsey. Sip Verdelho as aperitivo; Malmsey with dessert. Most wine tours include a cellar visit.
📍 Blandy's Wine Lodge · IVBAM
Bolo de Mel
↳ Dense dark molasses cake spiced with cinnamon, anise, and cloves. Made once a year around Christmas but sold year-round in Funchal; the flavor deepens over weeks, and a whole cake keeps for months.
📍 Boutique Cafe Lido · Padaria Pastelaria Mariazinha

🛷 The Toboggan Run

From Monte Palace, turn left out of the garden exit and walk two minutes to the top of Caminho do Monte, where two men in white linen suits and straw hats will ease you into a wicker basket-sled and push you downhill. The carreiros do Monte have been doing this since 1850 — steering with their feet and guiding with hemp ropes — and nothing about the method has changed. You cover 2 km of steep cobblestone at up to 48 km/h, arriving at Livramento, not the cable car terminal. From Livramento it's a ten-minute walk or a short Bolt back to the hotel. Skip the waiting taxis at the bottom.
