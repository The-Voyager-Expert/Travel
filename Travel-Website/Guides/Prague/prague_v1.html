Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 2 min · 🚕 3 min → Old Town Square
1. Old Town Square
↳ Prague's medieval heart for ten centuries, ringed by Gothic and Baroque facades, the twin spires of the Týn Church, and the pastel Rococo Kinský Palace. The Jan Hus memorial anchors the cobbles, alive with buskers day and night.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Staroměstské náměstí · Staré Město
🚶 1 min · 🚕 2 min → Old Town Hall Tower & Astronomical Clock
2. Old Town Hall Tower & Astronomical Clock
↳ The 600-year-old astronomical clock is the world's oldest still operating — every hour the Twelve Apostles parade past two windows as a skeleton tolls the bell. Climb the Gothic tower, or ride the lift, for the finest rooftop view over the square's spires.
📖 Wikipedia

3. Municipal House
↳ Prague's Art Nouveau masterpiece, opened in 1912 and dripping with mosaics, stained glass, and murals by Alfons Mucha. The lavish Smetana Hall is home to the Czech Philharmonic, and the guided tour opens grand salons otherwise closed to visitors.

4. Josefov
↳ The best-preserved Jewish quarter in Europe — six synagogues, the haunting Old Jewish Cemetery with its 12,000 leaning headstones, and the dazzling Moorish-style Spanish Synagogue. One Jewish Museum ticket covers them all.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 6 min · 🚕 5 min → Charles Bridge
1. Charles Bridge
↳ Commissioned by Charles IV in 1357, this 520-metre Gothic stone bridge over the Vltava is lined with 30 Baroque saints and guarded by a tower at each end. Crowded by day, magical at dawn — come early for empty cobbles and mist beneath the castle.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Karlův most · Staré Město
🚶 5 min · 🚕 4 min → St. Nicholas Church
2. St. Nicholas Church
↳ The grandest Baroque church north of the Alps, its green dome and bell tower defining the Lesser Town skyline. The frescoed nave holds one of Europe's largest ceiling paintings, and the gilded interior is pure high-Baroque theatre. Mozart played the organ here in 1787.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ St. Nicholas Church
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Malostranské náměstí · Malá Strana

3. Prague Castle & St. Vitus Cathedral
↳ The largest ancient castle complex in the world, seat of Bohemian kings and Czech presidents for a thousand years. Its centrepiece, the soaring Gothic St. Vitus Cathedral, holds royal tombs and a luminous Art Nouveau window by Mucha. The circuit adds the Old Royal Palace and Golden Lane.
📖 Wikipedia

4. Petřín Lookout Tower
↳ A 64-metre steel echo of the Eiffel Tower, raised for the 1891 Jubilee Exhibition atop Petřín hill's orchards and rose gardens. Climb the 299 steps, or ride the lift, for the finest 360° view in Prague — castle, river, and red rooftops spread below.

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 8 min · 🚕 6 min → Wenceslas Square
1. Wenceslas Square
↳ Less a square than a grand 750-metre boulevard — the stage for modern Czech history, from the 1918 republic to the 1968 Prague Spring and the 1989 Velvet Revolution. The neo-Renaissance National Museum crowns the top, and the St. Wenceslas statue marks its heart.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Václavské náměstí · Nové Město
🚶 12 min · 🚕 7 min → Dancing House
2. Dancing House
↳ Frank Gehry and Vlado Milunić's 1996 deconstructivist landmark — a glass-and-concrete tower that seems to sway like a dancing couple, nicknamed "Fred and Ginger." Ride up to the rooftop terrace for a sweeping view of the Vltava and the castle beyond.

3. Vyšehrad
↳ The "high castle" on a cliff above the Vltava — Prague's legendary birthplace, ringed by Baroque ramparts with the city's most peaceful panorama. Inside lie the neo-Gothic Basilica of Sts Peter and Paul and the cemetery where Dvořák and Smetana rest.

❗️ Heads Up
❗️ Astronomical Clock — The hourly show is brief
↳ The Apostles parade lasts under a minute and underwhelms the dense crowd that gathers — a prime pickpocket spot.
Workaround: Watch from the edge of the square, keep bags zipped and in front, and climb the tower for the real payoff.
❗️ Street Taxis — Tourist overcharging
↳ Hailed taxis around Old Town and the Castle routinely rig meters or quote flat tourist rates many times the fair fare.
Workaround: Order a Bolt or Uber in-app so the price is fixed and tracked; skip the taxi ranks at tourist hubs.
❗️ Currency Exchange — Street-booth scams
↳ Booths advertising "0% commission" bury terrible rates in the fine print, and street offers to change money are always a scam.
Workaround: Pay by card or withdraw Czech koruna from a bank ATM. Never change money on the street.
❗️ Prague Castle — Security queues
↳ Every visitor passes airport-style screening at the gates, and St. Vitus develops long lines by late morning.
Workaround: Arrive at opening, enter via the quieter eastern Opyš gate, and see the cathedral first.
❗️ Old Town Square — Tourist-trap pricing
↳ Cafés right on the square add steep covers, per-item bread and service charges, and inflated drink prices.
Workaround: Walk two or three streets off the square, and read the bread and water prices on the menu first.
🎻 Notes from the Road

Prague rewards looking up. The "city of a hundred spires" actually carries closer to a thousand, and the skyline you trace from Petřín or the Vyšehrad ramparts is essentially unchanged since the Habsburgs — the medieval core survived two world wars almost untouched, which is exactly why it feels like stepping inside a painting.

A lovely thread runs through this itinerary: Mozart. He premiered Don Giovanni at the Estates Theatre in 1787, declaring "my Praguers understand me," and that same week he is said to have played the organ at St. Nicholas Church in the Lesser Town — both places appear in this guide, a few hundred metres apart.

One detail to test on Charles Bridge: Charles IV, an enthusiast of numerology, reportedly laid the foundation stone at 5:31am on the 9th day of the 7th month, 1357 — giving the ascending-then-descending sequence 1-3-5-7-9-7-5-3-1. Whether or not it is true, it is the kind of story that makes a 14th-century bridge feel designed by someone who would have loved a good spreadsheet.
