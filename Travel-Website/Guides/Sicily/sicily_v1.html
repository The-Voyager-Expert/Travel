Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Teatro Antico di Taormina
1. Teatro Antico di Taormina
↳ A 3rd-century BC Greco-Roman theatre carved into the hillside, seating 10,000. The tiered stone seats frame Mount Etna and the Ionian Sea through the remains of the ancient stage wall — one of the most dramatic backdrops in the ancient world.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Villa Comunale
↳ A terraced public garden perched above the coast, built in the 1890s by Florence Trevelyan. Subtropical plants, eccentric Victorian follies called beehives, and unobstructed views across the bay to Calabria. The most tranquil spot in Taormina.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~45 min

🚕 8 min → Isola Bella
3. Isola Bella
↳ A tiny tidal island connected to the shore by a narrow sand spit, set in a turquoise cove below vertical cliffs. Known as the Pearl of the Ionian Sea, it became a nature reserve in 1990. The interior houses a small museum of Mediterranean plants.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:00am - 6:00pm

🚕 20 min → Castelmola
4. Castelmola
↳ A medieval hilltop village 550 metres above Taormina, perched on a rocky peak with Norman castle ruins. The 78-step climb to the top delivers a 360-degree panorama of Mount Etna, the Ionian coast, and the Strait of Messina. Listed among the Most Beautiful Villages in Italy.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h 30 min

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 15 min → Mount Etna
1. Mount Etna
↳ Europe's highest and most active volcano at 3,357 metres. A cable car ascends to 2,500 metres; optional 4x4 vehicles and guides reach the 2,900-metre crater zone. The summit landscape is a lunar terrain of black lava fields, steaming vents, and shifting craters — active eruptions are visible year-round.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Alcantara Gorge
↳ A dramatic volcanic canyon carved by the Alcantara River through ancient Etna lava flows. Walls rise 25 metres in hexagonal basalt columns — the same crystalline geometry as the Giant's Causeway — with cold, clear river water running through the narrows.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 20 min → Neapolis Archaeological Park
1. Neapolis Archaeological Park
↳ A vast open-air museum containing the 5th-century BC Greek Theatre, the 23-metre-high Ear of Dionysius cave with extraordinary acoustics, and the Latomie quarries. The theatre once held 15,000 spectators and is still used for performances in summer. UNESCO World Heritage.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:30am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~2h 30 min
📍 Viale Paradiso 14 · Syracuse
🚶 25 min · 🚕 10 min → Temple of Apollo
2. Temple of Apollo
↳ The oldest Doric temple in Sicily, dating to 565 BC. Its massive stone columns mark the entrance to Ortigia island and have survived transformations into a Byzantine church, Islamic mosque, and Norman chapel — 2,600 years of layered history visible in a single structure.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Largo XXV Luglio · Ortigia
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Cathedral of Syracuse
3. Cathedral of Syracuse
↳ A Baroque cathedral built directly into a 5th-century BC Greek temple — the original Doric columns of the Temple of Athena are still embedded in the nave walls. The ornate 18th-century facade conceals 2,500 years of continuous sacred use. One of the most extraordinary architectural palimpsests in the Mediterranean.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Piazza Duomo · Ortigia
🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Fountain of Arethusa
4. Fountain of Arethusa
↳ A freshwater spring that flows directly into the sea on Ortigia's western shore, surrounded by papyrus plants — the only place in Europe where papyrus grows naturally outside Egypt. Named for the Greek myth of the nymph Arethusa, who was transformed into this spring by Artemis.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Largo Aretusa · Ortigia

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 50 min → Piazza del Duomo
1. Piazza del Duomo
↳ The theatrical heart of Catania's UNESCO Baroque quarter, rebuilt in black volcanic lava stone after the 1693 earthquake. The Elephant Fountain — a lava-stone elephant carrying an Egyptian obelisk — anchors the square, backed by the Cathedral of Sant'Agata's white limestone facade.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → Roman Theatre of Catania
2. Roman Theatre of Catania
↳ A 2nd-century AD Roman theatre seating 7,000, built in lava stone and partially buried under medieval buildings. Roman arches emerge through apartment basements — a surreal layering where ancient infrastructure and modern life occupy the same walls.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Via Vittorio Emanuele II 266 · Centro Storico
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Monastery of San Nicolò l'Arena
3. Monastery of San Nicolò l'Arena
↳ The second-largest Benedictine monastery in Europe. Founded in 1558 and rebuilt after the 1693 earthquake, it now houses the University of Catania. The massive cloister and Roman-era remains beneath the floors are the highlights. UNESCO World Heritage.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Piazza Dante 32 · Centro Storico
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Castello Ursino
4. Castello Ursino
↳ A 13th-century fortress built by Frederick II as a coastal stronghold — the 1669 Etna eruption pushed the shoreline 500 metres out, leaving the castle stranded inland. Its four cylindrical towers and thick walls now house Catania's civic museum, with Greek, Roman, and medieval collections.
📖 Wikipedia

🍮 Local Tastes
Granita Siciliana
↳ Semi-frozen dessert made with sugar, water, and fresh fruit or almonds — served for breakfast with a warm brioche bun. Sicily's signature morning ritual, distinct in texture and tradition.
📍 Bam Bar · Bar Pasticceria Etna
Cannoli
↳ Crisp fried pastry tubes filled with sweet ricotta cream. The shell must be filled to order — a pre-filled cannolo signals a tourist trap. The best use sheep's milk ricotta.
📍 La Pignolata Guinness Cannoli · Bar Pasticceria Etna
Arancini
↳ Deep-fried rice balls stuffed with ragù, mozzarella, and peas — a Sicilian street food staple since 10th-century Arab rule. In eastern Sicily, they are conical; in Palermo, round.
📍 La Pignolata · Bam Bar
Pasta alla Norma
↳ Catania's signature pasta: rigatoni with fried aubergine, tomato sauce, and salted ricotta. Named after Bellini's opera Norma — the Catanese word for perfection.
📍 Osteria Rosso Divino · Trattoria da Nino
Almond Wine of Castelmola
↳ A sweet fortified wine infused with bitter almonds, produced exclusively in the hilltop village of Castelmola above Taormina. Served as an aperitivo or dessert wine.
📍 Bar Turrisi · Caffè San Giorgio

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Catania · 45 min
↳ UNESCO Baroque city rebuilt in black volcanic lava stone after the 1693 earthquake. Piazza del Duomo with its elephant fountain, vibrant fish market, Roman theatre, and the Benedictine Monastery — all in dark volcanic contrast with white limestone facades.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
Messina · 45 min
↳ Gateway to Sicily at the narrowest point of the Strait. The Norman Cathedral houses Europe's largest astronomical clock with a daily mechanical show at noon. Views across to mainland Calabria.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
Syracuse · 2 hr 10 min
↳ Once the most powerful city in the Greek world. The Neapolis Archaeological Park holds a massive Greek Theatre and the Ear of Dionysius cave; Ortigia island has a cathedral built into a Greek temple and the mythological Fountain of Arethusa. UNESCO World Heritage.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
Cefalù · 2 hr 30 min
↳ Medieval town pressed between a 270-metre rock promontory and the sea. The Norman cathedral contains Sicily's oldest Byzantine mosaics — a monumental Christ Pantocrator predating Monreale. UNESCO World Heritage.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
Noto · 2 hr 30 min
↳ The Baroque capital of Sicily, rebuilt entirely after 1693 in golden limestone. Via Nicolaci's ornate palazzo balconies and the Cathedral represent the apex of Sicilian Baroque. UNESCO World Heritage.
🎫 book at: Trenitalia or Omio
