2. Norton Simon Museum
↳ One of the densest private collections in the US — Raphael, Rembrandt, Degas, Van Gogh, Picasso, plus a South Asian sculpture wing. Garden features Rodin's Burghers of Calais · Closed Tuesdays and Wednesdays; open until 7:00pm on Friday and Saturday.

1. Descanso Gardens
↳ 150-acre oak woodland in La Cañada Flintridge — one of North America's largest camellia collections · 100,000+ plants, peak Jan–Mar, plus a Japanese stroll garden and Rosarium. Weekend tram covers the full grounds in 40 min.
📖 Wikipedia

2. The Huntington
↳ Henry Huntington's 207-acre San Marino estate — Gainsborough's Blue Boy, a rare-book library with the Gutenberg Bible, and 16 themed gardens including the 12-acre Chinese Garden. Book timed entry a week ahead; weekends sell out.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Griffith Observatory
1. Griffith Observatory
↳ The 1935 Art Deco observatory on Mount Hollywood — canonical LA skyline view, Oschin Planetarium, and a Zeiss refractor free most clear evenings. Rebel Without a Cause and La La Land filmed here. Planetarium shows need a timed ticket.

2. The Broad
↳ Eli Broad's 2015 contemporary museum — Koons, Basquiat, Kara Walker, and Kusama's Infinity Mirrored Room · timed slot, book on arrival. General entry free; the 'veil and vault' facade is Downtown LA's most photographed.

Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 35 min → Santa Monica Pier
1. Santa Monica Pier
↳ The 1909 wooden pier — Pacific Park rides, solar-powered Ferris wheel, and the 1922 hippodrome carousel. A bronze plaque marks the western end of Route 66. Best sunset views from the far rail; Heal the Bay Aquarium sits below.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 200 Santa Monica Pier
🚶 5 min · 🚕 6 min → Palisades Park
2. Palisades Park
↳ A 26-acre bluff-top park along Ocean Avenue — palm-lined paths, unobstructed views to Malibu and Palos Verdes, and the city's only Moreton Bay Fig large enough to shade an entire block. Benches face west — prime seats for Pacific sunsets.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Ocean Ave
🚕 10 min → Venice Beach
3. Venice Beach
↳ Abbot Kinney's 1905 Venice of America — the Ocean Front Walk runs past street performers, Muscle Beach, and the Venice Skate Park. The original canals a few blocks inland offer a quieter side; Abbot Kinney Blvd has the best lunch spots.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2h
📍 Venice Beach Boardwalk

Day 7
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 1h 15min → Mission San Juan Capistrano
1. Mission San Juan Capistrano
↳ Founded 1776 — the "Jewel of the Missions," famed for its returning swallows and the dramatic ruins of the 1806 Great Stone Church, toppled by an 1812 earthquake. The Serra Chapel is California's oldest building still in use; an audio tour winds through gardens and adobe.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Mission San Juan Capistrano · 4.7⭐ · missionsjc.com
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 9:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 26801 Old Mission Rd · San Juan Capistrano
🚕 25 min → Heisler Park
2. Heisler Park
↳ A bluff-top park along Laguna's shoreline — paved paths above tide pools, palm-framed Pacific overlooks, public sculpture, and stairs down to Divers Cove. The marine-protected coves below hold some of Southern California's clearest water; sunset here is the town's ritual.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 375 Cliff Dr · Laguna Beach
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Laguna Village
3. Laguna Village
↳ Laguna's walkable downtown — Forest Avenue's galleries and pocket courtyards lead down to Main Beach, where a boardwalk, lawn, and lifeguard tower meet the sand. An artists' colony since the 1910s; the village stroll itself is the draw.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Forest Ave · Laguna Beach
🚕 1h 15min → hotel

❗️ Heads Up
❗️ The Getty Villa — Reach it via PCH only
↳ After the Palisades fire the Sunset Blvd approach stays closed; only Pacific Coast Highway reaches the Villa, through a 25 mph zone.
Workaround: Drive in on PCH and reserve the free timed entry online in advance.
❗️ Disneyland — Reservation and ticket must match
↳ A dated ticket alone is not enough on busy days; the park reservation and the dated ticket must be for the same park, booked separately.
Workaround: Link the ticket in the Disneyland app and lock the park reservation the night before.
