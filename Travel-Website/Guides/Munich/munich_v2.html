Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Marienplatz
1. Marienplatz
↳ Munich's central square since 1158, framed by the Neo-Gothic Neues Rathaus and the historic Altes Rathaus. The Glockenspiel performs daily at 11:00am · noon · summer 5:00pm — 43 bells and 32 life-size figures re-enacting a 1568 royal wedding and the Schäfflertanz.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Marienplatz 1
🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Frauenkirche
2. Frauenkirche
↳ Munich's late-Gothic cathedral, built 1468–1488 in red brick with two onion-domed towers that are the unmistakable skyline silhouette. Inside, the so-called Devil's Footprint in the entry stone is part of every Munich legend told by every local.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 8:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Frauenplatz 12
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → St. Peter's Church
3. St. Peter's Church
↳ Munich's oldest parish church and the city's nickname namesake — Alter Peter. The 91-meter tower climb of 306 steps, no elevator, delivers the single best Altstadt panorama: Marienplatz directly below, Frauenkirche, the Alps on a clear day.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Monday - Friday 9:00am - 6:30pm · Saturday - Sunday 10:00am - 6:30pm
⏰ ~45 min
💵 Cash Only
📍 Rindermarkt 1
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Munich Residenz
4. Munich Residenz
↳ The Wittelsbach dynasty's seat of power for 500 years and one of Europe's grandest urban palaces — Antiquarium hall is the single-room highlight, a 70-meter Renaissance gallery of antique busts beneath ceiling frescoes. Treasury and Court Church round out the visit.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ shop.residenz-muenchen.de
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~3h
📍 Residenzstr. 1
🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 35 min · 🚕 12 min → Nymphenburg Palace
1. Nymphenburg Palace
↳ The Wittelsbachs' summer residence — a baroque palace stretching 700 meters across formal gardens. The Gallery of Beauties commissioned by Ludwig I — 36 portraits of women he admired, including Lola Montez — is the room everyone remembers; the park itself is enormous and free.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ shop.schloss-nymphenburg.de
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~3h
📍 Schloss Nymphenburg 1
🚶 35 min · 🚕 18 min → Olympiapark
2. Olympiapark
↳ The 1972 Summer Olympics grounds — Günter Behnisch and Frei Otto's acrylic tent-roof structures still read as the future of architecture half a century on. The park is a public Munich playground now: lake, hills, the stadium where Mark Spitz won seven golds.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Spiridon-Louis-Ring 21
🚶 8 min · 🚕 3 min → Olympiaturm
3. Olympiaturm
↳ The 291-meter Olympic Tower — Munich's tallest structure and the city's most committed viewpoint. Elevator to the observation deck at 190 meters runs every few minutes; on a clear Föhn day the Alps stretch the entire southern horizon.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 11:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Spiridon-Louis-Ring 7
🚶 7 min · 🚕 3 min → BMW Welt
4. BMW Welt
↳ BMW's flagship delivery hall and brand experience — Coop Himmelblau's twin-cone steel-and-glass building is itself the attraction. Free walk-in showroom with current models, motorcycles, and the M division floor; the BMW Museum next door is the paid deep-dive.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:30am - 12:00am
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Am Olympiapark 1
🚶 35 min · 🚕 15 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 25 min · 🚕 8 min → Haus der Kunst
1. Haus der Kunst
↳ A monumental colonnaded gallery on the southern edge of the Englischer Garten, built in 1937 and now one of Europe's most ambitious contemporary-art kunsthalles — large-scale international shows, no permanent collection, the building's own fraught history part of the visit.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Wednesday - Monday 10:00am - 8:00pm
🚫 Closed Tuesday
⏰ ~1.5 hr
📍 Prinzregentenstr. 1
🚶 10 min · 🚕 4 min → Monopteros
2. Monopteros
↳ Leo von Klenze's circular Greek-temple folly sits on an artificial hill that hands you Munich's best skyline view: Frauenkirche's two onions, the Theatinerkirche's yellow flank, the Alps in the distance.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Monopteros
🚶 12 min · 🚕 4 min → Bayerisches Nationalmuseum
3. Bayerisches Nationalmuseum
↳ One of the largest decorative-arts museums in Europe — a vast Prinzregentenstraße palace of Bavarian craftsmanship: medieval ivories, Riemenschneider woodcarvings, baroque period rooms, armour, and a celebrated basement of historical Nativity crèches.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Tuesday - Sunday 10:00am - 5:00pm
🚫 Closed Monday
⏰ ~1.5 hr
📍 Prinzregentenstr. 3
🚶 14 min · 🚕 8 min → Hofgarten
4. Hofgarten
↳ The Renaissance Court Garden laid out for Elector Maximilian I in 1613 — formal box hedges, a central temple to Diana, gravel paths radiating from a circular fountain. The quiet counterpoint to the Englischer Garten's wild lawns, tucked behind the Residenz.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Hofgartenstr. 1
🚶 18 min · 🚕 7 min → hotel

1. Hohensalzburg Fortress
↳ The largest fully-preserved medieval castle in central Europe, perched on the Festungsberg over Salzburg's old town. Built 1077, expanded through the 16th century, never taken by siege — the Princes' Chambers and Salzburg Bull organ are the room-by-room highlights.
📖 Wikipedia

⏰ ~2.5h
📍 Mönchsberg 34
🚶 16 min · 🚕 5 min → Mirabell Gardens
2. Mirabell Gardens
↳ Baroque parterre gardens commissioned by Prince-Archbishop Wolf Dietrich in 1606 for his mistress, redesigned by Fischer von Erlach. The Pegasus Fountain steps where Maria sings "Do-Re-Mi" — every Sound of Music tour group on the planet ends up here.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 9:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Mirabellplatz 4
🚶 9 min · 🚕 3 min → Salzburg Altstadt
3. Salzburg Altstadt
↳ UNESCO-listed historic core — narrow Getreidegasse with its iron guild signs, Mozart's birthplace at no. 9, the cathedral square where The Sound of Music's Maria leaves the abbey. The whole district is pedestrian; one slow loop is the way.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Getreidegasse 9
🚶 4 min · 🚕 2 min → Salzburg Cathedral
4. Salzburg Cathedral
↳ The Baroque cathedral where Mozart was baptized in 1756 — rebuilt 1614–1628 after fire, the twin towers close the Residenzplatz. The frescoed nave by Mascagni is one of the grandest Baroque interiors in the German-speaking world; the baptismal font where Mozart was christened sits in the left aisle.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Domplatz 1

1. Hohenschwangau Castle
↳ The yellow Neo-Gothic castle where Ludwig II grew up — built by his father Maximilian II on medieval ruins. Visit Hohenschwangau first to understand the boy-king's romantic obsessions before the bigger castle across the valley puts them into Disney scale.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Neuschwanstein Castle
↳ The most photographed castle in the world — Ludwig II's love letter to Wagner, built 1869–1886 in dramatic Romanesque revival. The interiors are unfinished and theatrical: a Throne Room with no throne, a Singers' Hall inspired by Parsifal, the king's bedroom in carved Gothic.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ shop.ticket-center-hohenschwangau.de
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 6:00pm

3. Marienbrücke
↳ The iron bridge over the Pöllat Gorge that delivers the postcard view of Neuschwanstein — the one in every search result, every poster, every screensaver. Built for Maximilian II's wife Marie in 1866 and refurbished in 2015; the wait can be brutal in season but the angle is unbeatable.
📖 Wikipedia

4. Füssen Altstadt
↳ The compact medieval town at the end of the Romantic Road — Hohes Schloss rises above the Reichenstraße, the former Benedictine monastery of St. Mang anchors the far end. The painted Renaissance facades and the Lech waterfall beneath the old town bridge make this the natural last stop before the train home.
📖 Wikipedia

🚉 ARRIVE Nürnberg Hbf: 🚶 22 min · 🚕 7 min → Kaiserburg Nuremberg
1. Kaiserburg Nuremberg
↳ The Imperial Castle that every Holy Roman Emperor between 1050 and 1571 stayed at — the sandstone keep, the deep well, and the Sinwell Tower form Nuremberg's defining skyline. The Imperial Apartments and Romanesque Double Chapel are the interior payoff.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Albrecht Dürer's House
↳ The half-timbered four-story house where the Northern Renaissance's biggest name lived 1509–1528. Period kitchen, the original printing press, and the recreated workshop where Melencolia I was cut into copper — small museum, outsized weight.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ tickets.museen.nuernberg.de

3. Hauptmarkt
↳ Nuremberg's central square — the Gothic Schöner Brunnen fountain from 1396, gilded, 19 meters of stone tracery; the Frauenkirche church with its mechanical Männleinlaufen clock that performs at noon; and the wide market square that hosts the world's most famous Christkindlmarkt in December.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1h
📍 Hauptmarkt
🚶 7 min · 🚕 3 min → St. Lorenz Church
4. St. Lorenz Church
↳ The Gothic twin-tower church begun in 1250 — the Angelic Salutation carved by Veit Stoss in 1518 hangs suspended in the nave in polychrome limewood, one of the finest late-Gothic sculptures in Germany. The Krafft Tabernacle rises 18 meters beside it. Five minutes south of the Hauptmarkt.
📖 Wikipedia

🚉 ARRIVE Garmisch-Partenkirchen: 🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Zugspitze
1. Zugspitze
↳ Germany's highest peak — 2,962 meters, the border with Austria runs across the summit. Two ways up: the Bayerische Zugspitzbahn cogwheel railway from Garmisch via Sonnalpin, or the direct Eibsee-Seilbahn cable car from Eibsee. Glacier, panorama platform, four-country view on clear days.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 4:30pm
⏰ ~4h
⚠️ Last cable car down departs at 4:30pm — miss it and there is no road off the summit.
📍 Olympiastraße 27
🚶 5 min · 🚕 2 min → Eibsee
2. Eibsee
↳ The turquoise alpine lake at the foot of the Zugspitze — 7.2 km shoreline trail, water clear enough to count stones at depth, the cable-car station on the south bank. The lake walk delivers the Zugspitze-from-below view that the summit never quite gives you back.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1.5h
📍 Eibsee
🚕 25 min → Partnachklamm
3. Partnachklamm
↳ A 700-meter gorge carved by the Partnach river — narrow rock walls, walkways drilled into the cliff, mist from the cascades soaking the path. Originally cut to float timber down to Partenkirchen; the same passage today is one of the most dramatic short hikes in the Bavarian Alps.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1.5h
⚠️ Closes after heavy rain — check the day's status at the entrance ticket office.
📍 Wildenauer Straße 18
🚶 20 min · 🚕 5 min → Garmisch-Partenkirchen Altstadt
4. Garmisch-Partenkirchen Altstadt
↳ The Lüftlmalerei old town — Frühlingsstraße lined with 18th-century facades painted in vivid Bavarian alpine fresco: saints, hunting scenes, trompe-l'œil columns. The medieval St. Martin's Church anchors the far end and the snow-capped Zugspitze closes the sky above the market square.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min

🍮 Local Tastes
Weisswurst
↳ The Munich-invented white veal-and-pork sausage from 1857 — eaten before noon by tradition. Order with sweet mustard, a soft Brezn, and a Weissbier; cut the casing, never bite through it.
📍 Zum Franziskaner · Schneider Bräuhaus · Wirtshaus in der Au
Brezn
↳ Bavarian-style pretzel — thicker arms than the Swabian original, salted, dark mahogany crust. Hand-twisted dough dipped in a lye bath before baking; breakfast, weisswurst pairing, or split with butter.
📍 Rischart · Hofpfisterei
Schweinebraten
↳ Bavarian roast pork with crisp-bubbled crackling and dunkles beer gravy, served over a knödel — potato or bread dumpling — the size of a fist. The Sunday-lunch dish at every Brauhaus.
📍 Augustiner-Keller · Spatenhaus an der Oper
Obatzda
↳ Bavarian beer-garden spread — ripened Camembert mashed with butter, sweet paprika, onion, caraway, a splash of weissbier. Eaten with radish slices and pretzel, never on bread; born in 1920s Tegernsee.

⛲️ Day Trips by Train
Dachau · 20 min
↳ The first Nazi concentration camp, opened in 1933 and now a memorial and documentation centre — one of the most important historical sites in Germany. The old town of Dachau also has a palace, parks, and a long tradition as an artist colony predating the Third Reich.
🎫 book at: bahn.de or Omio
Augsburg · 35 min
↳ A 2,000-year-old Roman city — the UNESCO-listed Fuggerei is the world's oldest social housing settlement, still inhabited and founded in 1516. The Maximilianstraße, Renaissance fountains, and the Goldener Saal are all walkable via the main station.
🎫 book at: bahn.de or Omio
Landsberg am Lech · 55 min
↳ A beautifully preserved medieval walled town on the Lech river — the Bayertor gate, frescoed Baroque Rathaus, and river promenade make it one of Bavaria's most attractive small towns. Direct regional train on the Augsburg line.
🎫 book at: bahn.de or Omio
Regensburg · 1h 30 min
↳ UNESCO-listed medieval Altstadt on the Danube — the Stone Bridge built in 1146, Gothic cathedral, and the oldest continuously running sausage kitchen in the world at the bridge's foot. The cleanest preserved old town in Bavaria.
🎫 book at: bahn.de or Omio
Passau · 2h
↳ A Baroque city at the confluence of three rivers — the Danube, Inn, and Ilz — with the Veste Oberhaus fortress above the old town and St. Stephen's Cathedral, home to the largest church organ in the world.
🎫 book at: bahn.de or Omio
