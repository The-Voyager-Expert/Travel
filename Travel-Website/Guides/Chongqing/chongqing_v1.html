Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Liziba Station
1. Liziba Station
↳ The monorail station built straight through the sixth to eighth floors of a 19-storey residential tower — Chongqing's most famous piece of mountain-city engineering. Trains glide into the building, pause inside, and slip out the other side. A purpose-built viewing platform across the street frames the spectacle.

2. Eling Park
↳ The highest point of the Yuzhong peninsula — a classical hilltop garden on the ridge between the Yangtze and Jialing rivers. Climb the seven-storey Kansheng Tower for a 360° sweep over both rivers and the skyline stacked on the hills between them: the best single place to read the city's geography.

🚕 13 min → People's Auditorium
3. Chongqing People's Auditorium
↳ A colossal 1950s assembly hall crowned with a triple-eaved green-and-red dome modelled on Beijing's Temple of Heaven — one of the boldest pieces of traditional-revival architecture in China. It anchors the vast People's Square, where locals gather to dance in the evenings.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
📍 173 Renmin Road · Yuzhong
🚕 8 min → Hongya Cave
4. Hongya Cave
↳ An eleven-storey cascade of traditional stilted houses built into the cliff above the Jialing River. By day it is a warren of teahouses and snack stalls; after dark the whole cliff lights up gold and the view from Qiansimen Bridge is pure Spirited Away. Arrive before sunset and eat your way down the levels.

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 24 min → Ciqikou Ancient Town
1. Ciqikou Ancient Town
↳ A Ming-and-Qing-era porcelain port on the Jialing River — flagstone lanes climbing the hillside between timber shophouses, teahouses and chilli-strung snack stalls. Watch candy-makers hammer maltose, sample mahua dough twists, and duck into the quieter alleys above the main street where old Chongqing still breathes.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hrs 30 min

2. Raffles City Exploration Deck
↳ The Crystal — a 300-metre glass skybridge laid across four skyscrapers, 250 metres above the meeting of the Yangtze and Jialing. A 270° panorama over Chaotianmen, an open-air glass platform, and a vertigo-grade skywalk along the spine. Time it for golden hour and watch the city light up.

3. Yangtze River Cableway
↳ Chongqing's flying gondola — a 1,166-metre aerial tramway strung across the Yangtze since 1987, when it carried commuters. Skyscrapers slide past the windows, the river rolls below, and at night the lit peninsula spreads out behind you. Ride round-trip and save the skyline for the return leg.

↳ The definitive museum of the Three Gorges — a vast four-floor complex facing the People's Auditorium across the civic square. Collections cover Ba culture, ancient Sichuan, the Yangtze's epic history, and artefacts rescued from submerged cities before the reservoir filled.

2. Jiefangbei Monument
↳ A 27-metre clock tower built in 1945 to commemorate victory over Japan. It stands at the intersection of Minzu and Minquan roads, surrounded by the busiest pedestrian plaza in southwest China. National Key Cultural Relic since 2013.

3. Pipa Mountain Park
↳ A forested hilltop park on the highest ridge of the Yuzhong peninsula. Shaded paths climb through bamboo and subtropical trees to pavilions with panoramic views over the Jialing River, the Yangtze and the ridge-stacked skyline. The quietest vantage point in central Chongqing.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr

🚕 10 min → Chaotianmen Square
4. Chaotianmen Square
↳ The confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing at the tip of the Yuzhong peninsula — for centuries the dock where imperial edicts arrived and trade entered the city. A wide esplanade overlooks both rivers; the Raffles City towers rise directly behind. Evenings bring the lit skyline reflected on both rivers.

🍮 Local Tastes
Chongqing Hotpot
↳ The city's invention and obsession: a bubbling nine-grid pot of beef-tallow broth packed with chilli and numbing huajiao pepper, born on the docks where boatmen stewed offal. Locals eat it year-round, even in the summer heat.
📍 Peijie Old Hot Pot · Liuyishou Hot Pot
Chongqing Xiaomian
↳ The breakfast of the mountain city — springy wheat noodles in a slick of chilli oil, sesame paste, garlic and huajiao, eaten standing at hole-in-the-wall counters. Order wanza mian to add silky stewed peas.
📍 Pangmei Noodle Restaurant
Suanlafen
↳ Hot-and-sour glass noodles hand-pulled from sweet potato starch, drowned in black vinegar and chilli, topped with peanuts and sprouts. Chongqing's street-stall classic — the Bayi Road version is the benchmark.

🌶️ The City That Grows Sideways and Up

Chongqing refuses every flat-city assumption. The monorail at Liziba doesn't go around the apartment tower — it goes through floors six to eight, and the residents say they barely hear it. The Crystal at Raffles City is a skyscraper that lies down: 300 metres of glass laid horizontally across four towers, so the observation deck is also a bridge. Even the cableway began life in 1987 as ordinary public transport — a bus line in the sky for dock workers — and only later did the city realise its commute was a world-class attraction.

The two days trace a quiet historical arc. Ciqikou was the porcelain port where the Jialing's cargo came ashore a thousand years ago; Chaotianmen, where the hotel stands, was the gate where officials greeted imperial edicts arriving by river. The same two rivers that made Chongqing a trading post now make it a spectacle — everything worth seeing in this city is, one way or another, a way of looking at the water.
