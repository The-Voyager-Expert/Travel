Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Grand Egyptian Museum
1. Grand Egyptian Museum
↳ The world's largest single-civilization museum, housing over 100,000 artifacts including the complete Tutankhamun collection and the two solar boats of Khufu. Connected to the Giza plateau by an elevated walkway.
📖 Wikipedia

2. Giza Pyramid Complex
↳ UNESCO World Heritage Site containing the pyramids of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure — built during Egypt's Fourth Dynasty. The site includes the Great Sphinx, valley temples, and a workers' village; the only intact ancient Wonder of the World.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2.5 hr
📍 Al Haram Street · Giza
🚕 35 min → Saqqara
3. Saqqara
↳ The Step Pyramid of Djoser is the world's oldest large-scale stone structure. The necropolis also contains the Pyramid of Unas — home to the oldest corpus of religious texts — and the Serapeum underground chambers of the sacred Apis bulls.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Saqqara Necropolis · Mit Rahina

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Cairo Citadel
1. Cairo Citadel
↳ A fortified Ayyubid complex built by Saladin on a spur of the Mokattam Hills, serving as Egypt's seat of government for nearly 700 years. Within the walls: the Mosque of Muhammad Ali with its Ottoman alabaster interior and panoramic views across the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1.5 hr
📍 Salah Salem Street · Mokattam Hills
🚶 7 min · 🚕 4 min → Mosque-Madrassa of Sultan Hassan
2. Mosque-Madrassa of Sultan Hassan
↳ A Mamluk monumental complex at Salah al-Din Square combining a mosque with four madrassas for the Sunni schools of jurisprudence. The twin minarets reach 84 meters — the entrance portal is one of the tallest in the Islamic world.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Salah al-Din Square · al-Khalifa
🚕 5 min → Al-Azhar Park
3. Al-Azhar Park
↳ A 30-hectare public park built by the Aga Khan Trust for Culture on a site used for centuries as a rubbish dump. The park incorporates a restored Ayyubid wall and elevated sightlines across the minarets and domes of the historic quarter.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 10:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Salah Salem Street · al-Darb al-Ahmar
🚕 20 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Coptic Cairo
1. Coptic Cairo
↳ A religious district built over the Roman Babylon Fortress, containing the Hanging Church, the Church of Saints Sergius and Bacchus, the Ben Ezra Synagogue, and the Coptic Museum — the largest collection of Coptic art in the world.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Mar Girgis · Fustat
🚕 20 min → Khan el-Khalili
2. Khan el-Khalili
↳ A Mamluk-era bazaar in the historic center organized around specialist trades — gold, spices, copperwork, and textiles. The district includes El-Fishawi Café, which has operated continuously since the late 18th century.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Saturday - Thursday 9:00am - 11:59pm · Friday 2:00pm - 11:59pm
⏰ ~1.5 hr
📍 El-Hussein Square · al-Gamaliyya
🚕 20 min → Egyptian Museum
3. Egyptian Museum
↳ A neoclassical building on Tahrir Square housing over 170,000 Egyptian antiquities — the first national museum in the region. The Royal Mummies Hall contains preserved remains of rulers including Ramses II and Hatshepsut.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 7:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Tahrir Square · Qasr al-Nil
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Koshary
↳ Egypt's national street dish: layers of macaroni, rice, vermicelli, lentils, and chickpeas topped with garlicky tomato sauce, spiced vinegar, and fried onions.
📍 Koshary Abou Tarek · Bab al-Luq
Ta'amiya
↳ A version of falafel made from ground fava beans rather than chickpeas — fried crisp and served in flatbread with salad and tahini. Distinct from the chickpea falafel found elsewhere in the region.
📍 Felfela · Bab al-Luq
Feteer Meshaltet
↳ A flaky layered pastry prepared on a large griddle, served sweet with honey or jam or savory with cheese or minced meat. Vendors called feteer-makers stretch and fold the dough to order.
📍 Downtown street bakeries
Karkadai
↳ A deep-red hibiscus infusion served cold or hot at coffeehouses and street stalls throughout the city — tart and lightly sweetened, central to the traditional ahwa coffeehouse culture.
📍 El-Fishawi Café · al-Gamaliyya
