Day 1

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Grand Egyptian Museum

Grand Egyptian Museum

↳ The world's largest single-civilization museum, opened in full in 2025. Houses over 100,000 artifacts including all 5,398 pieces from Tutankhamun's tomb and the two solar boats of Khufu. Connected to the Giza pyramid plateau by an elevated walkway.

Giza Pyramid Complex

↳ UNESCO World Heritage Site containing the pyramids of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure, built during Egypt's Fourth Dynasty (c. 2600–2500 BCE). The site also includes the Great Sphinx, funerary temples, and a workers' village — the oldest surviving Wonder of the Ancient World.

🚕 35 min → Saqqara

Saqqara

↳ The Step Pyramid of Djoser (c. 2650 BCE) is the world's oldest major stone structure and the first pyramid built in Egypt. The wider necropolis contains elaborately carved mastaba tombs, the Serapeum underground burial chambers, and the Pyramid of Unas — home to the oldest corpus of religious texts (Pyramid Texts).

Day 2

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Cairo Citadel

Cairo Citadel

↳ A medieval Ayyubid fortification begun by Saladin in 1176 CE on a promontory of the Mokattam Hills; it served as Egypt's seat of government for nearly 700 years. Within the complex, the Mosque of Muhammad Ali (completed 1857) dominates the skyline with its Ottoman-style alabaster interior and twin minarets visible from across the city.

📖 Wikipedia

🏛 Daily 8am–5pm

⏰ ~1.5 hr

📍 Salah Salem Street · Mokattam Hills, Islamic Cairo

🚶 7 min · 🚕 3 min → Sultan Hassan Mosque

Mosque-Madrassa of Sultan Hassan

↳ A Mamluk monumental complex constructed 1356–1363 CE at Salah al-Din Square, combining a mosque and four madrassas for the four Sunni schools of jurisprudence. Its two minarets reach 84 meters — recognized as one of the finest surviving examples of Mamluk architecture in the world.

Al-Azhar Park

↳ A 30-hectare public park opened in 2005 in the al-Darb al-Ahmar district, developed by the Aga Khan Trust for Culture on a site that was a centuries-old garbage dump. The park incorporates a restored 12th-century Ayyubid wall and provides elevated sightlines over the minarets of Historic Cairo.

Day 3

🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Coptic Cairo

Coptic Cairo

↳ A historic religious district built over the Roman-era Babylon Fortress, containing the 5th-century Hanging Church (al-Muallaqah), the 4th-century Church of Saints Sergius and Bacchus, the 9th-century Ben Ezra Synagogue, and the Coptic Museum — one of the largest collections of Coptic art in the world.

📖 Wikipedia

🏛 Churches daily 9am–5pm; Coptic Museum closed Mon

⏰ ~2 hr

📍 Mar Girgis · Old Cairo (Misr al-Qadima)

🚕 20 min → Khan el-Khalili

Khan el-Khalili

↳ A major bazaar in the historic center of Cairo established during the Mamluk era in the 14th century. The district is organized around specialty trades — gold, spices, copperwork, textiles — and includes El-Fishawi Café, which has operated continuously since 1773.

Egyptian Museum

↳ Established in 1902 on Tahrir Square, this neoclassical building houses over 170,000 Egyptian antiquities and was the first national museum in the Middle East. The Mummy Room contains preserved remains of rulers including Ramses II and Hatshepsut.

🍮 Local Tastes

Koshary
↳ Egypt's national street dish: a layered bowl of macaroni, rice, vermicelli, lentils, and chickpeas with garlicky tomato sauce, chili vinegar, and fried onions. Every corner of Cairo has a koshary stand.
📍 Koshary Abou Tarek
Ta'amiya
↳ Cairo's version of falafel, made from ground fava beans rather than chickpeas, fried and served in aish baladi flatbread with salad. Distinct from the chickpea version found elsewhere in the region.
📍 Felfela
Feteer Meshaltet
↳ A flaky, layered pastry traced to Pharaonic-era cooking, served sweet (honey, jam, powdered sugar) or savory (cheese, minced meat). Prepared by specialists called feteermakers who stretch and fold the dough across a large griddle.
📍 Downtown Cairo street bakeries
Karkadai
↳ A deep-red hibiscus infusion served cold or hot throughout Cairo's coffeehouses and street stalls. Tart, lightly sweetened, and consumed year-round — ubiquitous alongside the traditional ahwa coffeehouse culture.
📍 El-Fishawi Café, Khan el-Khalili

🚌 Getting Around

🚕 Uber / Careem
Uber operates reliably in Cairo across Downtown, Zamalek, and Giza. Careem (Uber-owned) is an alternative that accepts cash payments. Both apps are the standard way to get between sites.
🚝 Cairo Metro
↳ Three operational lines covering 61 stations. Line 1 serves Downtown (Sadat/Tahrir Square) and Old Cairo (Mari Girgis station for Coptic Cairo). Line 2 reaches Giza station — then a short ride to the pyramids.
Cairo Metro — operated by National Authority for Tunnels. Lines: 3. Women-only car on every train (the central carriage).
