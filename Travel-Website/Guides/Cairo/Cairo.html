Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 25 min → Grand Egyptian Museum
1. Grand Egyptian Museum
↳ The world's largest single-civilization museum, housing over 100,000 artifacts including all pieces from Tutankhamun's tomb and the two solar boats of Khufu. Connected to the Giza pyramid plateau by an elevated walkway.

2. Giza Pyramid Complex
↳ UNESCO World Heritage Site containing the pyramids of Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure, built during Egypt's Fourth Dynasty. The site includes the Great Sphinx, funerary temples, and a workers' village — the oldest surviving Wonder of the Ancient World.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8am–5pm
⏰ ~2.5 hr
📍 Al Haram Street · Giza
🚕 35 min → Saqqara
3. Saqqara
↳ The Step Pyramid of Djoser is the world's oldest major stone structure and the first pyramid built in Egypt. The necropolis contains mastaba tombs with carved reliefs, the Serapeum underground burial chambers, and the Pyramid of Unas — home to the oldest corpus of religious texts.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8am–5pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Saqqara Necropolis · Mit Rahina

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Cairo Citadel
1. Cairo Citadel
↳ A medieval Ayyubid fortification begun by Saladin on a promontory of the Mokattam Hills, serving as Egypt's seat of government for nearly 700 years. Within the complex, the Mosque of Muhammad Ali dominates the skyline with its Ottoman-style alabaster interior and twin minarets visible across the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8am–5pm
⏰ ~1.5 hr
📍 Salah Salem Street · Mokattam Hills
🚶 7 min · 🚕 3 min → Mosque-Madrassa of Sultan Hassan
2. Mosque-Madrassa of Sultan Hassan
↳ A Mamluk monumental complex at Salah al-Din Square combining a mosque and four madrassas for the four Sunni schools of jurisprudence. Its two minarets reach 84 meters — among the finest surviving examples of Mamluk architecture in the world.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8am–5pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Salah al-Din Square · Islamic Cairo South
🚕 5 min → Al-Azhar Park
3. Al-Azhar Park
↳ A 30-hectare public park in the al-Darb al-Ahmar district, developed by the Aga Khan Trust for Culture on a site that served as a centuries-old garbage dump. The park incorporates a restored Ayyubid wall and offers elevated sightlines over the minarets of Historic Cairo.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily until 10pm
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Salah Salem Street · al-Darb al-Ahmar
🚕 20 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Coptic Cairo
1. Coptic Cairo
↳ A historic religious district built over the Roman-era Babylon Fortress, containing the Hanging Church, the Church of Saints Sergius and Bacchus, the Ben Ezra Synagogue, and the Coptic Museum — one of the largest collections of Coptic art in the world.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Churches daily 9am–5pm · Coptic Museum closed Mon
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Mar Girgis · Old Cairo
🚕 20 min → Khan el-Khalili
2. Khan el-Khalili
↳ A major Mamluk-era bazaar in the historic center of Cairo organized around specialty trades — gold, spices, copperwork, and textiles. The district includes El-Fishawi Café, which has operated continuously since the late 18th century.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Sat–Thu 9am–midnight · Fri reduced hours
⏰ ~1.5 hr
📍 El-Hussein Square · Islamic Cairo North
🚕 20 min → Egyptian Museum
3. Egyptian Museum
↳ A neoclassical building on Tahrir Square housing over 170,000 Egyptian antiquities — the first national museum in the Middle East. The Mummy Room contains preserved remains of rulers including Ramses II and Hatshepsut.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 9am–7pm · Fri 9–11am and 1:30–7pm
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Tahrir Square · Downtown Cairo
🚶 5 min · 🚕 3 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Koshary
↳ Egypt's national street dish: a layered bowl of macaroni, rice, vermicelli, lentils, and chickpeas with garlicky tomato sauce, chili vinegar, and fried onions.
📍 Koshary Abou Tarek · Downtown Cairo
Ta'amiya
↳ Cairo's version of falafel made from ground fava beans — distinct from the chickpea version found elsewhere in the region. Fried and served in flatbread with salad.
📍 Felfela · Downtown Cairo
Feteer Meshaltet
↳ A flaky layered pastry prepared on a large griddle, served sweet with honey or jam, or savory with cheese or minced meat. Specialist vendors called feteermakers stretch and fold the dough to order.
📍 Downtown Cairo street bakeries
Karkadai
↳ A deep-red hibiscus infusion served cold or hot throughout Cairo's coffeehouses and street stalls. Tart and lightly sweetened — ubiquitous alongside the traditional ahwa coffeehouse culture.
📍 El-Fishawi Café · Khan el-Khalili
