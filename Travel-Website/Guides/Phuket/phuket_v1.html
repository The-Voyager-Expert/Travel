Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 8 min → Phuket Old Town
1. Phuket Old Town
↳ A UNESCO-recognized Sino-Portuguese heritage district of ornate shophouses, vibrant street murals, and Peranakan culture built during the 19th-century tin and rubber boom — the historic heart of the island.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Thalang Road · Old Town
🚕 12 min → Big Buddha
2. Big Buddha
↳ A 45-meter white marble-clad Maravija Buddha enthroned on Nakkerd Hill, offering panoramic views across the Andaman Sea, Chalong Bay, and three island coasts — Phuket's most iconic landmark.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 7:30pm
⏰ ~1 hr
🆓 Free
⚠️ Dress code enforced — shoulders and knees must be covered. Sarongs available at the entrance. The inner sanctum is temporarily closed following a landslide. Exterior terraces and panoramic views fully accessible.
📍 Nakkerd Hill · Chalong
🚕 10 min → Karon Viewpoint
3. Karon Viewpoint
↳ A terraced clifftop panorama 200 meters above the Andaman Sea, framing three southern bays — Kata Noi, Kata, and Karon — in a single uninterrupted sweep. One of Phuket's finest vantage points.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Patak Road · Karon
🚕 10 min → Promthep Cape
4. Promthep Cape
↳ Phuket's southernmost headland jutting into the Andaman Sea, legendary for panoramic sunsets viewed from a lighthouse-topped cliff. Arrive 45 minutes before sunset for the best vantage — the spectacle of the sun dropping over the horizon draws crowds from across the island.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Mu 8 · Rawai
🚕 25 min → hotel
Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 20 min → Chalong Pier
1. Phang Nga Bay & James Bond Island
↳ A UNESCO-class limestone karst seascape encompassing 42 islets and hidden lagoons — crowned by Ko Tapu · James Bond Island · the stilted Muslim fishing village of Koh Panyee · and sea caves accessible only by kayak. Best explored on a full-day guided boat tour.
📖 Wikipedia

🎟️ James Bond Island and Phang Nga Bay by Premium Yacht — TripAdvisor
🏛️ Daily 7:30am - 9:00am
⏰ ~8 hr
⚠️ James Bond Island requires a guide or chartered boat — no public ferry from Phuket. National park entrance fee included in all qualifying tours.
📍 Chalong Pier · Chalong
🚕 45 min → hotel
Day 3
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 10 min → Khao Rang Viewpoint
1. Khao Rang Viewpoint
↳ A forested hilltop 100 metres above the island's capital giving a sweeping 360° panorama — urban Phuket to the north, Chalong Bay and the Andaman Sea to the south, and the Big Buddha on Nakkerd Hill on clear days. A public park with a fitness trail and shaded benches.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~45 min
📍 Khao Rang Hill · City Center
🚕 18 min → Rawai Beach
2. Rawai Beach
↳ A wide curving bay on Phuket's southern shore — the island's main longtail-boat staging beach, not a swimming beach. Brightly painted longtail boats anchor offshore offering crossings to Coral Island; the waterfront road is lined with fresh-seafood stalls and a Chao Le sea-gypsy village at the eastern end.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Viset Road · Rawai
🚕 15 min → Coral Island
3. Coral Island · Ko Hae
↳ A small island 3 km southeast of Rawai with two clear-water beaches — Banana Beach to the north and Long Beach to the west. The surrounding reef gives the island its name; the water is shallow and calm. Longtail boats from Rawai cross in 15 minutes and land directly on the beach.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~2 hr
📍 Ko Hae · off Rawai
🚕 32 min → Sirinat National Park
4. Sirinat National Park
↳ Phuket's only national park, covering the northwestern coast with four beaches — Nai Yang, Mai Khao, Nai Thon, and Sai Kaeo — plus mangrove forests. Mai Khao Beach is 11 km long and one of Thailand's last sea-turtle nesting sites; the visitor centre at Nai Yang sits beside a coral reef visible in calm season.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Nai Yang Beach · Thalang
🚕 35 min → hotel
Day 4
🏨 From Hotel: 🚕 15 min → Wat Chalong
1. Wat Chalong
↳ Phuket's most revered Buddhist temple, a compound of five ornate buildings including the Grand Pagoda said to enshrine a bone fragment of the Lord Buddha. The main hall features vivid murals of the Buddha's life and an Emerald Reclining Buddha.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 7:00am - 5:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Dress code: shoulders and knees covered. Scarves available at the entrance.
📍 Chaofa West Road · Chalong
🚕 15 min → Kata Beach
2. Kata Beach
↳ A horseshoe bay on the southwest coast with consistent surf, clear water, and a tree-lined shore. The main Kata Yai beach stretches 1.5 km; the smaller Kata Noi cove to the south offers calmer conditions. One of Phuket's most scenic swimming beaches.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Patak Road · Karon
🚕 22 min → Kamala Beach
3. Kamala Beach
↳ A quieter crescent bay on the mid-west coast backed by a traditional Muslim fishing village, with shallow calm water ideal for swimming and a laid-back atmosphere distinct from the busier resort beaches further south.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr
📍 Kamala · Kathu
🚕 10 min → Patong
4. Patong Beach
↳ Phuket's main resort beach — a 3 km arc of white sand fronting consistent surf and the island's liveliest beachfront. Watersports fill the beach by day; Bangla Road's open-air bars and markets begin at dusk.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
📍 Patong · Kathu
🚕 28 min → hotel
Day 5
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Rassada Pier
1. Ko Phi Phi Don
↳ The largest of the Phi Phi archipelago, shaped by twin limestone peaks connected by a narrow palm-fringed isthmus with beaches on both sides. A short climb to the Ko Phi Phi Don Viewpoint gives the iconic twin-beach panorama across the Andaman Sea.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Accessible by speedboat from Rassada Pier only — no car access. Day trips depart 8:00–9:00am.
📍 Ko Phi Phi Don · Krabi Province
🚕 5 min → Ko Phi Phi Leh · Maya Bay
2. Ko Phi Phi Leh & Maya Bay
↳ An uninhabited island of sheer limestone cliffs enclosing world-famous Maya Bay — a crescent beach made iconic by the film The Beach. Maya Bay reopened after a four-year ecological closure; visitor caps and boat restrictions are now enforced.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 8:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~1 hr 30 min
⚠️ Boats must anchor offshore — no landing at peak hours. Visitor numbers capped daily by the national park authority.
📍 Ko Phi Phi Leh · Krabi Province
🚕 5 min · 🚶 12 min → hotel
Day 6
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Rassada Pier
1. Ko Similan
↳ The signature island of the Similan archipelago — a beach of white sand and granite boulders framing a coral-rich sea. The distinctive Sailboat Rock — also called Donald Duck Rock — granite formation crowns the island's peak and gives Ko Similan its classic silhouette.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~2 hr
⚠️ Open November to May only. Accessible by guided boat tour from Rassada Pier; crossing takes 2.5 hr by speedboat.
📍 Ko Similan · Phang Nga Province
🚕 10 min → Mu Ko Similan National Park
2. Mu Ko Similan National Park
↳ A marine national park of 9 islands in the Andaman Sea, rated among Asia's premier snorkeling and diving destinations. Reef sharks, manta rays, and whale sharks move through waters with visibility of 25–30 m. The park closes June–October; all visits are by licensed guided boat tour.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 6:30am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~5 hr
⚠️ Park closed June–October. National park entrance fee included in all qualifying tours.
📍 Mu Ko Similan · Phang Nga Province
🚕 5 min · 🚶 12 min → hotel

🍮 Local Tastes
Mee Hokkien
↳ Thick springy egg noodles tossed with soy-braised pork, dried shrimp, and crispy garlic — a Hokkien Chinese import that became a Phuket staple served at morning kopitiam stalls for over a century.
📍 Kopitiam by Wilai
O-Tao · Oyster & Taro Pancake
↳ A Phuket street-food classic of Hokkien origin — fresh oysters and taro seared into a crispy egg pancake, served with a tangy chilli-vinegar dipping sauce. Best eaten hot from a street wok in Old Town.
📍 Old Town street stalls · Thalang Road
Kaeng Tai Pla
↳ Phuket's most intensely flavored curry — a pungent deep-red broth built on fermented fish kidneys, bamboo shoots, and a paste of dried chillis. Uniquely Southern Thai and unforgettable once you've had the real version.
📍 Raya Thai Cuisine · One Chun

🌅 Claude Inspiration

The Last Hour Before Sunset

Promthep Cape is one of the most celebrated sunset points in Southeast Asia — and the last 45 minutes before the sun drops into the Andaman are extraordinary. The limestone headland glows amber, longtail boats drift below, and the crowd of hundreds falls almost silent as the colors shift. No filter needed.

Build Day 1 so you arrive here at the right moment: Karon Viewpoint late afternoon, then Promthep Cape for the finale. Everything else on the itinerary is secondary to getting this timing right.
