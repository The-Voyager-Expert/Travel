1. National Naval Aviation Museum
↳ The world's oldest naval aviation museum housing 150+ restored aircraft from Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard. Blue Angels practice flights are visible from the flight line. Interactive exhibits, flight simulators, and restoration shops showcase four centuries of naval aviation history on the sprawling NAS Pensacola base.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 10 min · 🚕 5 min → Pensacola Lighthouse & Maritime Museum
1. Pensacola Lighthouse & Maritime Museum
↳ Built in 1858, this 160-foot Pensacola Lighthouse is the second-oldest continuously operating lighthouse in the United States. The 177-step spiral climb offers panoramic views across Pensacola Bay to downtown and the surrounding coast. The Maritime Museum exhibits ship models, navigation instruments, and the history of Pensacola's role as a major port and naval center.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Admission · $9.50 adults · pensacolalighthouse.org
🏛️ Daily 9:00am - 4:00pm

2. Fort Pickens
↳ A massive five-bastion coastal defense fort built between 1829 and 1834, Fort Pickens defended Pensacola Bay for nearly two centuries. The brick fortress spans 50 acres within Gulf Islands National Seashore, offering self-guided tours through casemates, gun emplacements, and barracks. The surrounding landscape includes pristine beaches, nature trails, and commanding views of the Gulf of Mexico.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Free Entry · National Seashore pass $30 · nps.gov

🚶 12 min · 🚕 5 min → Historic Pensacola Village
3. Historic Pensacola Village
↳ A living museum preserving 450+ years of Pensacola history across a six-block district. Authentically furnished homes from the Spanish and American colonial periods, including the 1805 Lavalle House and Victorian-era Lear-Rocheblave House, sit alongside a historic French schoolhouse, church, and shops. Guided tours at 11 AM, 1 PM, and 2 PM bring the stories of residents to life through period interiors and interpretive exhibits.
📖 Wikipedia
🎟️ Admission · $12.50 per site · historicpensacola.org
🏛️ Tue-Sat 10:00am - 4:00pm, Sun 12:00pm - 4:00pm

1. Pensacola Beach
↳ Pensacola Beach stretches 16 miles of pristine white sand famous for its distinctive squeaking quality—caused by the quartz content. Crystal-clear turquoise water and a welcoming, family-friendly atmosphere make it one of Florida's premier coastal destinations. The beach offers parasailing, kayaking, paddleboarding, fishing, and jet ski rentals, while nearby shops and restaurants line the beachfront.

3. Opal Beach
↳ Part of Gulf Islands National Seashore, Opal Beach offers a quieter alternative to the main Pensacola Beach. Pristine white sand, clear water, and minimal crowds characterize this natural barrier-island beach. Full facilities including restrooms, showers, and picnic shelters support a day of swimming, shelling, and relaxation. Lifeguards are on duty during summer months.
