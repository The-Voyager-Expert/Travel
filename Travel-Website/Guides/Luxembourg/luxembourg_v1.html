Day 1
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Place Guillaume II
1. Place Guillaume II
↳ The city's grand civic square, known to locals as the Knuedler, framed by the neoclassical Town Hall and centred on a bronze equestrian statue of Grand Duke William II. A produce market fills it on Wednesday and Saturday mornings — a relaxed first taste of the upper town.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Place Guillaume II · Ville Haute
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Grand Ducal Palace
2. Grand Ducal Palace
↳ The official town residence of the Grand Duke, a richly ornamented Flemish-Renaissance facade from the 1570s and one of the city's most photographed buildings. A ceremonial sentry stands guard out front; the interior opens to guided tours only in high summer.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 17 Rue du Marché-aux-Herbes · Ville Haute
🚶 4 min · 🚕 3 min → Notre-Dame Cathedral
3. Notre-Dame Cathedral
↳ Luxembourg's national cathedral, built by the Jesuits between 1613 and 1621 and crowned by three slender black spires. Inside are luminous stained glass, the venerated statue of the Comforter of the Afflicted, and a crypt holding the tombs of the Grand Ducal family.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 12:00pm · 2:00pm - 5:30pm
⏰ ~30 min
🆓 Free
📍 Rue Notre-Dame · Ville Haute
🚶 3 min · 🚕 2 min → Gëlle Fra
4. Gëlle Fra
↳ The Golden Lady — a gilded figure of victory atop a 21-metre granite obelisk on Place de la Constitution, raised in 1923 for Luxembourg's war volunteers and now the nation's symbol of freedom. Behind it opens the classic panorama over the Pétrusse valley and the Adolphe Bridge.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~20 min
📍 Place de la Constitution · Ville Haute
🚶 6 min · 🚕 4 min → Chemin de la Corniche
5. Chemin de la Corniche
↳ A pedestrian promenade laid along the 17th-century ramparts, famously called "the most beautiful balcony in Europe." It hangs above the Alzette with sweeping views down into the Grund, across to Neumünster Abbey and the old Wenzel fortifications — the single best walk in the city.
🏛️ Open 24/7
⏰ ~30 min
📍 Chemin de la Corniche · Ville Haute
🚶 5 min · 🚕 4 min → Bock Casemates
6. Bock Casemates
↳ The honeycomb of underground galleries carved into the Bock promontory where Count Sigfried founded the city in 963. Austrian engineers turned it into kilometres of gun chambers and tunnels — the "Gibraltar of the North" — with loophole views over the valley. The fortress and old town are UNESCO World Heritage.
📖 Wikipedia

Day 2
🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 15 min · 🚕 8 min → Neumünster Abbey
1. Neumünster Abbey
↳ A former Benedictine abbey of 1606 set on the Alzette in the lower town of Grund, now the Neimënster cultural centre. Wander the cloisters and riverside courtyards, look in on the free exhibitions, and take in the abbey against the cliffs and ramparts above — the prettiest corner of the city.
📖 Wikipedia
🏛️ Daily 10:00am - 6:00pm
⏰ ~45 min
🆓 Free
📍 28 Rue Münster · Grund
🚶 18 min · 🚕 9 min → Pfaffenthal Panoramic Elevator
2. Pfaffenthal Panoramic Elevator
↳ A free glass lift that drops 71 metres through the cliff face, linking the upper town near Pescatore park to the Pfaffenthal valley below. The ride and the open viewing platform deliver a thrilling head-on view of the viaduct, the ramparts and the Alzette gorge.
🏛️ Daily 5:45am - 1:00am
⏰ ~15 min
🆓 Free
⚠️ Out of service for maintenance on the first Monday of each month.
📍 Montée de Pfaffenthal · Pfaffenthal
🚶 20 min · 🚕 8 min → Fort Thüngen
3. Fort Thüngen
↳ The "Three Acorns" fort of 1733, its trio of round towers restored and standing guard at the edge of the Kirchberg plateau. The ramparts and tunnels are free to roam, and the Musée Dräi Eechelen inside traces the story of the great fortress from 1443 to its dismantling.
📖 Wikipedia

🚶 2 min · 🚕 2 min → Mudam
4. Mudam
↳ Luxembourg's contemporary art museum, a luminous glass-and-stone pavilion that I. M. Pei built atop the old fort bastions. The rotating exhibitions are strong, but the building itself — all light wells and clean geometry rising from the ramparts — is the real draw.

❗️ Heads Up
❗️ Grand Ducal Palace — Interior tours summer only
↳ The interior opens to guided tours only from mid-July to the end of August; the rest of the year only the facade and the guard are visible.
Workaround: Time your visit for summer to tour inside; otherwise enjoy the exterior, which is striking year-round.
❗️ Mudam — Closes Tuesday Not Monday
↳ Unlike most city museums, which close Monday, Mudam closes on Tuesdays and stays open late on Wednesday until 9:00pm.
Workaround: Plan Kirchberg for any day except Tuesday; Wednesday evening is the quietest slot.
❗️ Bock Casemates — Timed 15-minute entry
↳ Admission runs in 15-minute slots between 9:45am and 5:00pm, and the site closes on 25 December and 1 January.
Workaround: Arrive early in summer to beat the queue and allow 45 minutes underground.
✨ A City on Two Levels

Luxembourg's secret is its verticality. Almost everything worth seeing sits on the same compact upper plateau, but the magic is in the gorges that fall away on every side — the Pétrusse and the Alzette carving green ravines straight through the middle of a national capital. Spend Day 1 looking down from the ramparts, then Day 2 walking the valleys you were looking at, and the city reassembles itself into three dimensions.

Lean on the fact that every bus, tram and train in the country is free to ride. There is no ticket to fumble and no fare to calculate — take the tram up to Kirchberg, ride the funicular and the glass lift between the upper town and the valley floor, and let the elevators do the climbing your legs would otherwise dread. It quietly turns a hilly city into an easy one.
