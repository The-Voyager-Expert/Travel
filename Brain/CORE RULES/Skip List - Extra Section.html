🚷 Skip List

Phase: 5 — Per-section build

Summary: The final element in the guide — a small footnote naming the venues skipped because they were already visited.

§ 1. What ships

When the destination has entries in the internal Cities Skip List, the guide ships a Skip List footnote naming the venues that were cut from research because they were already visited. When the destination has no skip list, nothing ships — no footnote, no heading, no line.

§ 2. Placement

The Skip List is the last element in the guide. It sits after every other section, at the very bottom of the final page. Nothing follows it.

§ 3. Look

The Skip List is a footnote, not a section card. It carries no title banner and no icon heading. It is a single line of small italic grey text — the lead-in label "Skipping:" followed by the skipped venues separated by the middle dot.

Skipping: [Venue] · [Venue] · [Venue]

§ 4. Source

The venue list comes from the destination's entry in the Cities Skip List — Part 4 of Brain/Reference/Brain.md. A destination with no entry there ships no skip-list footnote. That list governs which venues are cut before research; this footnote reports what was cut, so the reader knows what was intentionally left out. The order follows the skip list.

§ 5. Recheck on rebuild

Editing the Cities Skip List changes no guide on its own and never triggers a guide rebuild. The footnote is brought current only when the guide is itself rebuilt with new content. Every time a guide is rebuilt with new content, the build rechecks the destination's entry in the Cities Skip List and brings the footnote into parity with it — adding venues newly listed, removing venues no longer listed, and keeping the order of the list. A rebuilt guide whose footnote does not match the current skip list has not been brought current.
