✏️ Day Structure

Phase: 3 — Day shape

Summary: How a day is shaped — trip length, the From-Hotel opener, stop numbering, route shape, stop count, and the Train Day quota and pattern.

§ 1. Trip length

Build for the number of days the trip covers, ignoring arrival and departure days.

§ 2. Day shape

Every day starts from the hotel. The day always opens with the 🏨 From Hotel: banner — no exceptions.

🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min] → [First Stop]

§ 3. Stop numbering

Every day starts fresh from Stop 1. Stop numbers belong to the day, not the trip — they reset at the beginning of each new day, regardless of how many stops came before.

A stop can be a regular day or train stop.

A train stop is a trip to another city. The train blocks wrap around the stops in [Destination]. Train stops are always the last stop(s) in the trip.

§ 4. Route shape

Be efficient — plan to save time. Stops must flow geographically so the route never doubles back on itself. Each stop should be close to the next, moving in a direction that ends naturally near the hotel — not looping back through places already passed.

Only span multiple zones in the same day when there isn't enough to fill a day in one area alone — a small city with three worthwhile stops has no choice. In larger cities, keep each day to one zone and save the rest for another day.

§ 5. Stop count rules

  • Full self-guided day — ≥4 stops, or fewer when a single distant or all-day stop sets the day's pace — down to one.

§ 6. Train Day quota

A guide with 5 or more days must include at least one Train Day. Ship without when no train-day destination is reachable. A guide with 4 or fewer days must not include a Train Day.

Each Train Day carries a 🎫 book at: [Train Ticket Operator].

§ 7. 🚊 Train Day pattern

A round-trip train trip to another city. This is a day-level pattern, not a stop type — the train wraps around stops in [City].
