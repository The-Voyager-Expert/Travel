🚦 Motion Rule

Phase: 4 — Per-stop build (and Phase 5 when extras include motion)

Summary: Walk and ride rules for every transit banner, plus all banner formats.

§ 1. Stops pattern

Actual computed minutes only — never from ride-share connectors, APIs, or pricing estimators.

The 🚕 ride time is a driving-mode route duration, taken from a major mapping app (Google or Apple) in driving mode. Where neither provides driving coverage for a destination's region, the ride time is taken from a mapping app local to that region, in its driving mode. A local fallback is used only where the major apps have no driving coverage.

🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min]
🚕 [M min]

🚕 only — when walk time exceeds 40 min, omit the 🚶 row and show 🚕 only.

🚤 Car-free cities — in cities with no road network (e.g. Venice), the ride leg is by water. Replace 🚕 with 🚤 in every motion banner and from-hotel row; the time is the fastest private water-taxi door-to-door minutes. 🚶 walking is unchanged, and 🚕 (driving-mode time, per § 1) remains the ride glyph everywhere else. 🚤 follows the identical time format and ride-only rules as 🚕.

🚶 [N min] · 🚤 [M min]

🚎 Tram — when a real tram route exists at that location, add a sub-line directly below any transit banner. The tram row follows the time — never precedes it:

🚶 X min → [Board] · 🚎 [N] tram to [End] → [Alight] · 🚶 T min → [Destination]

🚝 Metro — only when metro is planned by request. Same inline pattern as tram:

🚶 X min → [Board] · 🚝 [N] metro to [End] → [Alight] · 🚶 T min → [Destination]

🚢 Ferry — when a ferry route connects two stops or replaces a walk/ride leg. Appears as a standalone segment with time and destination, or chained inline with other motion icons:

🚢 [N min] → [Destination]
🚶 X min → [Pier] · 🚢 [N min] → [Destination Pier] · 🚶 T min → [Destination]

§ 2. Extra section pattern

Extra sections are reference sections, not itinerary stops. Walk and ride times are measured from the hotel and follow the stops pattern .

With motion rows: 📅 Tours · ☕ Cappuccino · 🫕 Restaurants Near Hotel · 🚆 Stations Near Hotel · 🏓 Pickleball · 🎭 Shows · ⭐ Michelin

No motion rows: 🍽️ Downtown · 🍮 Local Tastes · ❗️ Heads Up

§ 3. Day wrapper

Every itinerary day has a required opener and a required closer. Between-stops banners connect every consecutive stop pair.

a. Without train

Day opener — REQUIRED, first line of every day

🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min] → [First stop]

Between stops — one banner between every consecutive pair

🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min] → [Next stop]

Day closer — REQUIRED, last element of every day

🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min] → hotel

b. With train

Train day opener

🏨 From Hotel: 🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min] → [Departure station]

Train block header — outbound

🚊 [Departure station] → [Arrival station] · [Operator] · [Duration]

Train-day arrival (outbound) — arriving in [City]

🚉 ARRIVE [Arrival station]: 🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min] → [First stop]

Train block header — return

🚊 [Arrival station] → [Home station] · [Operator] · [Duration]

Train-day arrival (return) — coming back to the home city

🚉 ARRIVE [Home station]: 🚶 [N min] · 🚕 [M min] → hotel
