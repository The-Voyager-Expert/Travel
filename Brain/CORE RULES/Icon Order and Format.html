🚤 Car-free ride. In cities with no road network (e.g. Venice), 🚤 replaces 🚕 as the ride glyph in every motion banner and from-hotel row — the time is the fastest private water-taxi door-to-door minutes. 🚤 follows the identical time format and ride-only-when-walk-exceeds-40-min rules as 🚕 (Motion Rule.html). 🚕 stays the ride glyph (driving-mode time, per Motion Rule.html) in all road cities; the two never mix in one guide.

Picture — photo block · always last in the stop, after motion.

Tilde rule. The tilde ~ belongs to one icon only — ⏰ Avg Time Spent (position 4b), where the visit length is an estimate (⏰ ~1 hr). Every other value is exact and carries no tilde: ⏳ Duration is a published figure (⏳ 45 min, never ⏳ ~45 min), and walk, ride, start-time, and travel-time values are concrete numbers. ~ after anything other than ⏰ is wrong.

§ 4. — Train Icons

Train icons appear in their own structured elements on train days — not as motion rows inside regular stop boxes.

# Icon Meaning Format
1 🚊 Regional train header 🚊 {departure station} → {arrival station} · {operator} · {duration}
2 🚄 High-speed train header 🚄 {departure station} → {arrival station} · {operator} · {duration}
3 none Timetable rows {dep time} {station} → {arr time} {station}   one row per viable departure
4 🚉 Arrive banner 🚉 ARRIVE {station}: 🚶 N · 🚕 M → {first stop}   used for both destination arrival and return

🚆 — Train-Day day-card marker (Trip Overview). The icons above appear inside the train block during the stops. Separately, 🚆 marks a train day on its Trip Overview day card — Day {N} · 🚆 Train Day — {Destination}. It is the same glyph used for the Stations Near Hotel section title/pill; both Trip-Overview uses of 🚆 are sanctioned. The in-block train headers stay 🚊 / 🚄 — never 🚆.
