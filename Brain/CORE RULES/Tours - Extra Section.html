📅 Tours

Phase: 5 — Per-section build

Summary: How to find, qualify, and format guided tours.

§ 1. What ships

Objective: Variety of tours.

Search for the best tours in [City] or leaving from [City] across the following sources:

  • First tier: Viator · GetYourGuide · TripAdvisor — a flat pool with no ordering between them.
  • Fallback: any popular local operator (in Asia: Klook).

Selection. Small-group takes priority over large-group. Walking tours: minimum 2 and maximum 4 per guide across all platforms combined. The maximum 4 is a hard cap. The minimum 2 is a target, not a floor — ship fewer when not enough qualifying walking tours can be found, and document the reason in the low-count comment (format and placement: the Guide Entry Counts file in Brain/Reference/).

City rule. A tour qualifies only when it is held in [City] or departs from [City]. Tours that depart from a different city and merely pass through or stop in [City] do not qualify, regardless of rating or review count.

§ 2. Excluded tour types

The following never ship, regardless of rating or review count: private tours · museum-heavy tours · hop-on hop-off buses · segway tours · bike tours · e-bike tours · horse carriage tours · pub crawls · boat parties.

§ 3. Grouping and order

Tours are grouped by platform and ship in this order — all Viator first, then all GetYourGuide, then all TripAdvisor. Within each platform group the tours are numbered from 1, and the number restarts at 1 for each platform.

§ 4. Rating bar

4.5+⭐ · minimum 6 reviews — everywhere. The rating must be visible at page level; a rating that is not visible is treated as 0.

When the rating is not visible, in order:

  1. Try a different web search query (for example, site:viator.com [product-code] rating reviews).
  2. Escalate to a live page render in the browser.
  3. As a last resort, confirm on the live page manually.

§ 5. Minimum count per platform

Every guide includes at least 5 tours from Viator, 5 from GetYourGuide, and 5 from TripAdvisor. This is a hard minimum. A guide ships fewer than 5 on a platform only when a <!-- low-count: [reason] --> comment documents the shortfall: it names each short platform with its count (e.g. Viator 3 of 5) and gives a specific, verifiable reason — the qualifying bar (4.5★ · 6+ reviews), the search method, or a concrete city fact. A generic reason such as "small market" or "limited availability" is not sufficient documentation and the guide fails.

When one platform has no qualifying tours but others do, that platform's group ships the platform heading followed by one fixed negative-finding line, identical in wording for every platform and varying only by platform name and [City]: No qualifying tours on [Platform] in [City]. The wording never changes with the platform or the reason; the reason is recorded only in the low-count build comment.

§ 6. Entry Format(s)

The address should provide the street name and neighborhood. The city is included only when the address is in a city different from the destination [City]. No postal code. The country is included only when the address is in a different country from the destination [City].

§ 8. When none qualify

When no platform has a qualifying tour, ship the negative-finding line:

No qualifying tours in [City].

When only one platform lacks qualifying tours, that platform's group instead carries the line with the platform name (§ 5): No qualifying tours on [Platform] in [City].
