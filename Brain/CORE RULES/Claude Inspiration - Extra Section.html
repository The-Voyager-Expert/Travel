Claude Inspiration

Phase: 5 — Per-section build (optional, always last)

Summary: A free-form space where Claude shares anything that surfaced during research and felt worth keeping — a historical detail, an unexpected connection between two stops, a quiet pattern noticed across the trip. Not a duplicate of anything already in the guide.

§ 1. What ships

  • Optional. Ships only when something genuinely worth sharing came up during research. If nothing surfaced, skip the section entirely — no negative-finding line, no placeholder.
  • Free-form prose. No template, no required structure, no bullet checklist.
  • Multiple entries are fine — each as its own <p> block. No cap on count. The bar stays high: only things Claude genuinely found interesting, not padding.
  • No duplication with content already in another section of the guide.

§ 2. Icon, title, and color theme

Claude picks the icon, section title, and color theme per guide — whatever fits the city's personality. All three can be different every time. No fixed palette — choose freely.

This freedom applies to the section heading only — not the nav pill. The Extras-row pill pointing here is always exactly ✨ Claude Inspiration (fixed label, fixed icon; validator hard-fails on any deviation — see Trip Overview.html). The pill is wayfinding and stays constant; the section heading below it is the free creative choice.

Emoji rule: Claude picks any emoji that is not already in use by another guide section. Reserved emojis — those with a fixed role in the icon system (motion icons, section headers, stop markers, etc.) — are off-limits, as they carry a defined meaning elsewhere. Any emoji from the available pool in the Emoji Library is a valid choice. Using an emoji here does not reserve it and does not affect its status in the Emoji Library.

Apply the chosen color as a theme-{color} class on the section div. The class colors the section title, underline, and card background automatically.

§ 3. Placement

Always the last section before the Skip List footnote — after the last extra section. The Skip List footnote (when present) appears after it. No other section follows. Locked placement.

§ 4. Entry Format

<div class="claude-inspiration theme-{color}" id="claude-inspiration">
  <div class="extras-title">{Icon} {Title}</div>
  <p>{First observation — free-form prose}</p>
  <p>{Second observation — optional, only if genuinely earned}</p>
  <p>{...}</p>
</div>

Title heading, container, and divider styling are copied from any other extra section — the rules above govern the choices, the styling stays consistent.
