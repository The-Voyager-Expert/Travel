🏨 Hotel Banner

Phase: 2 — Guide structure

Summary: The blue banner at the top of the title page.

§ 1. Entry

[City] [Country]
[Hotel Name] or [Rental Name]
[Hotel Address · Neighborhood] ← per Links.html

The address provides the street name and neighborhood, separated by a middle dot. When the hotel has a published street number, it precedes the street name. No postal code or country.

The country is written as its full name. An abbreviation, a country code, or any subdivision below the national level — state, province, territory, or region — does not appear in the country label.

§ 2. Three-line format

The banner carries exactly three lines in this order: city (uppercase), hotel or rental name, address. No additional lines appear in the banner.

§ 2a. Typography — locked sizes (approved 2026-06-26)

The city is 18px (set by .title-city). The three remaining text elements — hotel name, address, and country label — each use 14px. No element may carry an inline font-size that deviates from these sizes. Sizes are set in guide-style.css and enforced by brain_check at session start.

  • City: uppercase, weight 700, 18px (set by .title-city — separate from the three locked elements below)
  • Hotel name (.title-hotel): 14px, weight 500
  • Address (.title-address): 14px, weight 500 — inline after hotel name on the same row, preceded by a middle dot (·) separator
  • Country (.title-country): 14px, weight 600, uppercase — right-aligned on the city row

The "Updated Month Year" stamp (.title-updated) appears on the same row as hotel name and address, right-aligned (margin-left: auto), at 11px in muted colour. It is injected by toolbar.js and must not appear in guide HTML.

§ 2b. Mobile presentation (≤ 600 px, approved 2026-07-11)

On phones guide-style.css re-lays the same three text elements. This is presentation only — the guide HTML is unchanged, and § 2 / § 2a still govern content and desktop typography.

  • City and country share one baseline just above the gradient line (matches desktop).
  • The hotel name and address form ONE tappable white card glued directly under the line — hotel name on top (14px, weight 600 on mobile for the card-title look, gold #8a6c1a), the address beneath as the Google-Maps link with a ↗ cue. Both stay single-line (ellipsis if long) so the banner is a fixed height and TRIP OVERVIEW lands consistently.
  • The "Updated Month Year" stamp is moved out of the banner to the bottom-right of the guide's "Also on this site" card (toolbar.js → repositionUpdatedStamp()). It is still injected by toolbar.js and still absent from guide HTML.

The address <a> carries min-height:0 to cancel mobile.css's 40px tap-target a{} rule, which was inflating the card on the ~22 legacy guides that still link mobile.css.

§ 3. Scope

The banner appears on the title page only. It does not repeat, summarise, or link to the accommodation elsewhere in the guide.

§ 4. Hotel naming rule

The hotel or rental name appears exactly once in the guide — in the title banner. Every other reference to the accommodation uses the generic word "hotel" (lowercase). The proper name does not appear in prose, motion banners, day openers, or the day closer (correct: "→ hotel"; never "→ {Hotel Name}").
