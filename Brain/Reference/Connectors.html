🏨 Hotel Research

✅ TripAdvisor

search_hotels → hotel_details → compare_hotels. Search by city, get full review details, ratings breakdown, and side-by-side comparison of up to 3 hotels. Best for deep review analysis and bathroom/room quality intel from guest reviews.

Tested: Searched Sintra hotels May 2026 — extracted bathroom quality signals from review text.

✅ Booking.com

accommodations_search with free_cancellation filter. Often surfaces different hotels than TripAdvisor. Rich filters: meal plan, property type, star rating, free cancel. Discovered live as a connector mid-session.

Tested: Searched Sintra May 17–19 with free cancel filter — found Passeio Sintra Collection (9.5 score) not visible on TripAdvisor.

✅ Trivago

Aggregates prices from Booking.com, Expedia, Hotels.com, Priceline. Three tools: trivago-search-suggestions (get location ID — always call first), trivago-accommodation-search (search by location ID), trivago-accommodation-radius-search (search by coordinates + radius). Best for price comparison across platforms.

Tested: Sintra search — found Villa Bela Vista (9.8 / 1305 reviews, €399) as top contender.

⚠ Always call trivago-search-suggestions first to get location ns+id — cannot search by city name directly.

✅ Expedia

search_hotels + search_flights. Good for bundled flight+hotel discovery and route-finding.

Tested: Searched LIS→AMS flights May 2026 — found TAP TP671 direct $253.

hotel-search, hotel-details. Compares hotel prices across booking platforms and surfaces direct-booking rates. Use alongside TripAdvisor + Booking.com + Trivago for price comparison — adds a fourth data point focused on direct-booking discounts.

📋 Hotel Research Playbook

The multi-source approach developed during Sintra research (May 2026). Use all three platforms — they surface different hotels and different signals.

Step 1 — TripAdvisor

search_hotels by city + dates. Get top 5–10 results by rating. For each candidate, call hotel_details to extract bathroom/room quality signals from guest reviews. Look for keywords: "modern bathroom," "great shower," "renovated," "small room," "dated," "drainage issues."

Step 2 — Booking.com

accommodations_search with free_cancellation=true. Often surfaces brand-new properties (high score, low review count) not yet on TripAdvisor. Check score + review count together — 9.5 with 50 reviews = brand new, not necessarily better than 9.2 with 800 reviews.

Step 3 — Trivago

trivago-search-suggestions → trivago-accommodation-search. Cross-reference prices from all platforms. Use for final price check and to catch any strong contenders missed by TripAdvisor + Booking.com.

Hard rules

  • Free cancellation is MANDATORY. Non-refundable only as absolute last resort.
  • Location over price — best neighbourhood beats cheapest room.
  • Modern bathrooms = high priority. Read review text, not just scores.
  • If a hotel scores very high (9.5+) but has very few reviews (<100), flag it as brand-new — great or unknown, not proven.

🗺️ Tours & Experiences

✅ Viator

search_experiences (natural language + dates + currency) → get_experience_details. Returns live ratings, review counts, prices, duration, free-cancellation flag, and direct booking URL. Use this instead of browsing Viator — one call gives structured data faster and safer.

Tested: Searched Sintra May 17–18 — found Private Local Sintra Tour by classic Renault 4L/Jeep (⭐4.98, 358 reviews, €79/person, 4–6hrs, free cancel).

⚠ sessionId must be reused across both tool calls in the same session. Use format: travel-{city}-{year}.

💡 Playbook: For every destination, search Viator proactively before building a day plan. Filter by dates, use EUR currency. Private tours are often surprisingly affordable. Always check freeCancellation flag.

🌐 Browser — Claude in Chrome MCP

✅ Claude in Chrome

The bot-block override. When direct web_fetch returns 403 or client-rendered content (blank page, "enable JavaScript"), switch immediately to Chrome MCP — no asking. Tools: mcp__Claude_in_Chrome__navigate → mcp__Claude_in_Chrome__get_page_text.

Standard fallback cascade (any site, any 403): direct fetch → site:{domain} WebSearch → Chrome MCP. Execute each step immediately without asking permission to try the next one. Exception: platforms marked ⚡ or ❌ in Brain/Reference/Platforms.md skip direct fetch — start at site: WebSearch.

Use for: GetYourGuide (no MCP connector, bot-blocks direct fetch) · Viator when site: search isn't enough · any live site that 403s · booking page verification · any page that needs JavaScript to render.

⚠ Never ask "should I use the browser?" or "want me to try Chrome?" — that question IS the punt. Just use it.

Tools available: navigate, get_page_text, read_page, find, javascript_tool, form_input.

🔍 Control Chrome

Second, simpler browser connector — tab-level control of the user's Chrome: open_url, get_current_tab, get_page_content, list_tabs, switch_to_tab, close_tab, go_back/go_forward, reload_tab. Claude in Chrome remains the primary browser tool (richer: javascript_tool, form_input, find); use Control Chrome only when simple open/read of a tab is enough.
