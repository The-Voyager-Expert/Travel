✅ Pre-Ship Checklist

Phase: 6 — Ship gate

Summary: The final self-audit Claude runs before marking any guide build done. Answer each question honestly. Any "no" means the guide is not ready to ship — fix the gap and re-run the checklist.

§ 1. When to run

Before marking a guide build "done" — every time. Not optional. § 10 includes running the validator as part of this checklist — answer every section before reaching it.

This includes patch sessions. Any session that modifies guide HTML runs this checklist and the validator before saying done. The scope of the change is not an exemption.

Resuming an incomplete build: read _build/build_state.md before doing anything. An unchecked Phase 6 means the guide is not done. Complete Phase 6 before any done claim.

§ 2. Build-state tracker

The tracker at Travel-Website/Guides/{City}/_build/build_state.md exists and every [ ] for Phases 0–5 is flipped to [x]? Any unchecked phase means a file was skipped — go read it before continuing.

§ 3. Research & selection

  • Was the skip list checked first (Brain/Reference/Brain.md (Part 4 — Cities Skip List) at the ## [City] heading)?
  • Every candidate came from a trusted source — Fodor's, Culture Trip, Rough Guides, Atlas Obscura, Rick Steves (European destinations only), National Geographic, or the official tourism board?
  • No blogs, no AI SEO content, no affiliate listicles, no user-generated lists?
  • No top-10 thinking — fame alone wasn't a reason to include?
  • Meaningful over full — no padded days with filler picks?

§ 4. Day shape

  • Each day opens with the 🏨 From Hotel: banner?
  • Each day closes with the hotel return?
  • Stop count meets the rule: ≥4 for full self-guided days, with down to 1 permitted when a single distant or all-day stop sets the day's pace (per Day Structure.html § 5)?
  • Train Day quota satisfied: ≥5-day guide includes at least one Train Day (or explicitly omits when no reachable destination exists); ≤4-day guide includes none (per Day Structure.html §§ 6–7)?
  • Geographic clustering — one loop or one path, no backtracking?

§ 5. Format

  • Title page: three lines (CITY uppercase / Hotel or Rental name / Address — format is {Street number + name} · {Neighborhood}, no postal code, no country, per Hotel Banner.html § 1)? Neighborhood inclusion is a human check — the validator only verifies a house number digit is present.
  • Every stop block follows the icon order in Icon Order and Format.html?
  • Every motion banner follows Motion Rule.html (opener, between-stop banners, and day closer all present and correct)?
  • Every 📍 address row follows Links.html § 6 format: {Street} · {Neighborhood} — no postal code; city included only when the address is in a city different from the destination; country included only when in a different country; the address text itself is the clickable destination anchor (no separate token)?
  • Every 🎟 ticket-box follows the format in Tickets.html?
  • Toolbar present: the toolbar-mount div + toolbar.js script are the first items inside the container, with data-depth="2" and data-maxwidth="940" (legacy — inert, but still required markup) (per Brain/Reference/Toolbar.html; enforced by validator TB-1 through TB-8)?
  • Guide has no inline footer div of its own and does not load footnote.js directly — footer sharing is ⛔ retired (per Brain/Reference/Toolbar.html § 6; enforced by validator TB-9)?

§ 6. Photos

  • Every stop has a photo harvested in the same pass as the stop research (not deferred)?
  • Every photo genuinely depicts the stop's subject — not a generic city shot (per Photos Rules.html § 3; human check — validator cannot verify subject match)?
  • Every photo is from Wikimedia Commons with an acceptable license (Public Domain · CC0 · CC-BY · CC-BY-SA)?
  • Every photo file is stored locally in Travel-Website/Guides/{City}/_build/assets/ and referenced by relative path — downloaded via commons_photo.py --download. No upload.wikimedia.org hotlinks: the validator hard-fails every Commons hotlink and the retired <!-- hotlink: … --> comment no longer authorises one (see Photos Rules.html § 5 step 7 + § 9)?
  • Every photo has plain, short alt text — just the subject name, no "photo of" prefix (per Photos Rules.html § 8)?
  • Any photo whose filename contains a weak-filename pattern (Building, Main_Building, Exterior, Facade, Outside, Front_view) was checked for a better alternative — or carries the <!-- photo-ok: no better-licensed alternative found for {stop name} --> sign-off comment (per Photos Rules.html § 10)?
  • Stops without a usable Commons image ship the "No pictures found" line, not silent omission?

§ 7. URLs

  • Every URL returns HTTP 200?
  • Every URL page carries ≥100 words of editorial content?
  • Every Google Maps 📍 URL actually points to the correct place — content correctness, not just format (human check — validator cannot verify)?
  • Bot-blocked platforms (Viator, GetYourGuide, TheFork, Michelin, TripAdvisor, Klook) verified via site: search-result inspection?
  • Verification log present at Travel-Website/Guides/{City}/_build/verification_log.json?
  • Every external anchor carries target="_blank"?
  • No link in the guide carries an underline — the stylesheet removes them globally; no element reintroduces one (per Links.html § 8)?

§ 8. Extra sections

Every extra section is present and either populated or carries its negative-finding line — except the conditional sections (❗️ Heads Up, Claude Inspiration, Skip List), which ship nothing when empty (per-item notes below). Order must match this canonical sequence:

  • 🗓️ Weekly Closures
  • 📅 Tours — every tour is held in or departs from the guide city; tours that depart from a different city and merely pass through do not qualify (per Tours - Extra Section.html § 1 City rule; human check — validator cannot verify because address format omits the guide city from local addresses)
  • ☕ Cappuccino
  • 🫕 Restaurants Near Hotel
  • 🍽️ Downtown Restaurants
  • 🍮 Local Tastes
  • 🚗 Food Delivery
  • 🎭 Shows, Performances & Concerts — every entry is a destination-permanent performance, not a touring production (per Shows, Performances & Concerts - Extra Section.html § 2); all entries verified to be scheduled and running at time of ship (human check — validator cannot verify)
  • 🚌 Getting Around
  • 🚆 Train Stations Near Hotel
  • ⛲️ Day Trips by Train
  • 🏓 Pickleball (California, Arizona, and Oregon only — silently omitted elsewhere)
  • ⭐ Michelin Restaurants
  • ❗️ Heads Up (conditional — ships only when entries exist)
  • ✨ Claude Inspiration (optional — silently omitted when nothing surfaced)
  • Skip List footnote (conditional — very last; ships only when the city has a skip list)

§ 9. Data integrity

  • Every factual row (🏛 hours, 🚶 walk, 🚕 ride, ⏰ duration, 📍 address, 🎟 booking) comes from a confirmed source — venue's own site, Google Places, or a verifiable third-party listing?
  • No hedging language ("typically open," "usually," "approximately," "around," etc.) in any factual row?
  • No fabricated data — every fact live-verified this build, not pulled from memory or past guides?
  • No money symbols ($, €, £, ¥, ISO codes) anywhere in the shipped guide?
  • No tilde (~) anywhere in the guide except as the ⏰ Avg Time Spent prefix (⏰ ~1 hr) — the single carve-out; never on ⏳ Duration, walk/ride times, or prose (Icon Order and Format.html § 2)?
  • No placeholders ({TBD}, {TODO}, "fill in later")?

§ 11. Post-ship

All steps below happen in a single pass. Partial updates break the chain and block the validator.

  • New card added in the correct country section of guides_index.html, in alphabetical order by city name within that country, with the right data-guide-num and data-status="want" (blue dot — default for every new guide; remove only after you've been)? — when the country has no section yet, create one in alphabetical position using the existing section pattern (flag + country name + count) (per Navigation.html § 5 step 1)
  • Card subtitle (dest-sub) — opt-in, gated, rare. A card may carry one italic sub-line naming a non-obvious natural marquee the guide covers — a major geological phenomenon, famous lake, or national/natural park reached as a day-trip whose presence the city name does NOT telegraph (e.g. Ljubljana → incl. Postojna Cave · Lake Bled; a Milan base → Lake Como; a US base → Grand Canyon or Yosemite). Format: <span class="dest-sub">incl. Place · Place</span> placed immediately after the dest-name span. Hard scope — natural features only: caves, lakes, canyons, waterfalls, geysers, fjords, parks, geological phenomena. NEVER obvious or man-made city sights — cathedrals, churches, museums, palaces, squares, ordinary landmarks — those are expected from the city name and adding them is drift. Gate — never autonomous: a crib does NOT add a subtitle on its own judgement. It parks a one-line proposal in Travel/To Do List/To_Do_List.md under 🪧 Index Subtitle Proposals and adds the dest-sub span only after owner approves. The .dest-sub CSS class lives inline in guides_index.html and is theme-safe (--muted / --fs-sub).
  • City added to Brain/Reference/Status Dots — guides_index.md master list as [ ] (want to go) in the correct country group, A→Z?
  • Predecessor card's data-guide-next and successor card's data-guide-prev updated to point to the new guide?
  • Banner counts (guides + countries) and the country section's guide count both updated?
  • The guide file's own toolbar-mount data-prev and data-next set to match its neighbours in the chain?
  • City pin added to the matching region map — Travel-Website/Trip-Essentials/Maps/Europe-Map.html, Travel-Website/Trip-Essentials/Maps/US-Map.html (North America), Travel-Website/Trip-Essentials/Maps/Asia-Map.html, Travel-Website/Trip-Essentials/Maps/Africa-Map.html, Travel-Website/Trip-Essentials/Maps/Oceania-Map.html, Travel-Website/Trip-Essentials/Maps/South-America-Map.html, or Travel-Website/Trip-Essentials/Maps/Caribbean-Map.html? Entry format in the PINS array: ['CityName', lon, lat, '../../Guides/City/file.html']. The ship gate scans all seven maps and hard-fails if no pin is found. Rule: Navigation.html § 5 step 5.
  • City added to the climate / Weather data so it appears in BOTH 🌤️ Weather tabs (By Climate = Climate-Finder.html, By City = Trip-Essentials/Weather.html) — both read window.TravelClimate, baked into assets/weather.js from assets/climate.json. After the map pin is in place, run python3 Brain/scripts/build_climate.py (it reads the map pins to know which cities to fetch), then python3 Brain/scripts/validate_climate_coverage.py (exit 0). The ship gate (_check_guide_in_climate) hard-fails if the city is missing from either source. Added 2026-06-15.
  • City added to the 📊 Travel Stats page — auto-generated from the index, so the ship gate rebuilds Travel-Stats.html (build_travel_stats.py) and hard-validates (validate_travel_stats.py); just ensure the index card is correct. Added 2026-06-15.
  • City added to the 🛡️ Safety Guide — Safety-Guide.html is hardcoded, so add the city's row by hand (US State Dept advisory level + official source link), one row per guides_index city. Ship gate validate_safety_guide.py hard-fails otherwise. Added 2026-06-15.
  • If the guide adds a new country: add it to the 💰 Currency Guide — append the country (with this city) to the COUNTRIES list in Brain/scripts/build_currency.py, then run python3 Brain/scripts/build_currency.py to regenerate. Ship gate _check_guide_in_currency hard-fails if the shipping city maps to no country. (Same-country guides need no currency change.) Added 2026-06-15.
